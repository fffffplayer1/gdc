@Luke:

 

Ah, dreams.  I believe that when your brain is tired and shut's down when you sleep, one of things you lose is the ability to discern between thought and reality.  However, your brain is still thinking away at things, but now thought is reality.

 

Also, when you're tired, your thought process kind of just jumps from one thing to another.  You don't make new thoughts.  Like this:

 

* * * * *

 

Bob yawned.  Now he was tired and he fell asleep.

 

Bob dreamed about saving the toothbrush from Poisair, a type of poisionous air.  So he took a piece of paper and turned in his essay on Social Studies.  Then he met his sister who turned into his best friend from school with his sister's pet cat who only likes his sister.  Then he went on a plane where there were a thousand people.

 

* * * * *

 

Disambiguation: Bob had to write a paper on Social Studies at school 12 hours before.  He also went on a field trip with his best friend from school where they learned about how exhaust gas can poison and kill thousands of trees.  His best friend from school is a girl and so is his sister.  And just before going to bed he brushed his teeth.  The plane came out of nowhere.

 

In some special cases you actually can make new thoughts while dreaming, and know that you're dreaming at the same time, so you can influence your dream and actually do some impossible things, like driving your favorite car when you are still too young to actually do so in real life.

 

I actually did that.
