Lots of interesting planning going on!

 

So................ yeah.

 

- I agree with thedude being in the story at least at its end, and I'll say a KOTOS story would not be complete without thedude!

 

- I don't think I'll be less active than I already am anytime soon, so I'll stay on the list!

 

- Perhaps thingguy had a KOTOS-independent backstory for Thingguy The First and Thingguy The Second.  Two Thingguys is a weird enough occurrence, I'm not sure if we should simply not try to explain it, or go ahead and make an explanation of our own.  I think, since it's part of the story and we are continueing the story, it is up to us to explain it in the way we figure out / create.

 

- @The map piece:

There could be other rogue groups, vigilantes, or ex-guards from Mathias' kingdom who may or may not have resisted thedude at first.  Most of them, of course, would have been defeated in the fifteen years but maybe some of them slipped into hiding and never surfaced again....

 

The largest resistance group would of course be the Knights of the Old Speech.  Yet Mathias still got his four Knights Kingdom knights together.

 

My ideas are, the map could point to other resistance groups, so it directly ties in to getting an army to fight Vladek.  It could also point, perhaps, to a piece of technology.  The grammar sword, which thedude (not Vladek, I thought it was Vladek in the prologue) wanted in the prologue.

 

Has Sir Shadow appeared yet in the story?  Perhaps, like a certain popular movie that became released in December 2015, they want to find Sir Shadow... or some other wizard.  Perhaps the ancient enchanter who cursed Militiregnum in the first place.  If they could get him to increase the anti-technology power of the curse, thedude would lose one of his greatest advantages.
