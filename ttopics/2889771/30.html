Ok this may seem weird, but...... I'm writing my own story with the knights of old speech in it. I have the prologue written down but I need the permission to use some fellow knights in the story.

The story will be named "Rage" and it follows Sir John Nicholas Cataclysm as he trys to bring down a gang led by a dark villain known as, "The Duke". I want to use at least five knights, they will not be doing much, but I do need them. I am writing this story while I'm inactive, this is the only chance I have of getting permission from you guys. I'm technically inactive right now, but today I have been able to find a gap of space to post a few lines. I will post the prologue to see if you guys like it.







Prologue,



Sir John Nicholas Cataclysm ran as fast as he could, dodging and jumping obstacles, trying to escape the sound of feet echoing behind him. He suddenly came up against a dead end in the dimly lighted alley. He drew his double-handed sword and waited for the attack he knew was coming. His blade glimmered in the dull light as the footsteps came nearer. Suddenly there was silence. Then a sound of unsheathing swords and daggers came to his ears. Sir John dove for the floor as three shurikens embedded themselves in the wall behind him.



Then they came. Out of the darkness stalked three figures, each wearing a black hoodie with the letter 'R' painted across the front. Each carried a dagger in one hand and a katana in the other. Their belts contained a magnetic clip holding five shurikens. Sir John jumped to his feet just in time to deflect a blow aimed for his head, then he attacked, thrusting his sword at the nearest assailant. But the assailant easily dodged and hurled his dagger at Sir John's head. Sir John ducked and swung his own sword, smashing one of them. There were two left.



Both of the assailants jumped at him, but Sir John dove under them and thrust his sword into the back of one, smashing him instantly. The other one backed up to the wall whilst pleading, "Mercy! I beg you to spare me!"



"That I shall do," Sir John replied. He walked up to the quivering form and raised his sword. He brought it down hilt-first on the head of his enemy, knocking him unconscious. Sir John sheathed his sword and took a small device from the motionless body. He pushed a button, and a screen lit up. A raspy voice asked, "Job done?"

Sir. John changed his voice and answered, "Yeah, boss, we got him."



"Well done," the voice said. Then Sir John turned off the device and walked down the alley.
