Hey, Talmid, guess what? I am going to start writing again. Not Skyward, though, even though that is probably my favorite plot I ever came up with. I am going to continue writing Set Sail. I just came up with most of it, because I have been up since 3. (In the MORNING. GRRRRR. It was actually quite fun, oddly. ) Here is the Prologue I wrote a LOOOONG time ago:


Set Sail! – The Story of a Buccaneer’s Life.
Set Sail! – Prologue:

Brandon Fletcher was walking out of the pub, dreaming about a life he would never have. Brandon lived down by the docks in a country far away from the Maelstrom. He was on his own from the start, after he ran away from an Orphanage when he was ten, Now he was twenty-one, and he wanted to join the Nexus Force. But more than that, he wanted to be a Buccaneer.
Brandon was walking down by the docks when he heard two people arguing.
“No! You can’t leave! I’m in charge here!” said one man, his voice dry and dusty.
“But I AM leaving, and there’s nothing you can do about it!” said another.
He saw both of them yelling at each other, with a few swear words thrown in.
“I’m leaving. Find more people to do your work.” One said, and walked off.
“That’s the fourth this week!” The old man said to himself.
“If you don’t mind me asking, what is your problem?” Brandon asked, walking up to him.
“Oh, not much. My crew is leavin’, thinking I can’t lead them because I’m getting old. What rubbish! One failed mission and it all goes down the drain.”
‘He DOES look pretty old…’ Brandon though.
“So you need more crew for you ship, otherwise you can’t sail again, for there are to many duties to be done with so little people,” Said Brandon, summing it up.
“Aye, mate. Say, you look like a strong fellow. Would you want to try to fill in for one of my men?” Asked the old man. Brandon’s heart leapt at the opportunity.
“Yes!” Brandon said immediately.
“Thank you for helping. You might be able to join my crew someday if you are successful enough! Oh, by the way, my name is Captain John,” he said, holding out his hand. Brandon shook it.
“We set sail at 7:00, so be here a bit before, or we’ll leave you here!” He said.
“Got that. I will be here!”
“And I here. Good day to you sir!”
“You too!”
And with that, Brandon walked away, feeling better than he ever had.
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Brandon walked back to his old, run-down house, and started packing the few things of value he owned. After he finished, he walked around town for a while until dark, then walked back to his house, and fell asleep almost instantly, his dreams landing upon the subject of buried treasure, and sailing the open seas, without a care in the world. He couldn’t wait.
