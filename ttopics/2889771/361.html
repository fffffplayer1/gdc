Hey guys, I've been thinking that I should draw a map of Morcia.


First off, what is Morcia?  I've been thinking that the Perpetual Doom series takes place on a continental landmass on a planet inside the LEGO Universe - either the landmass or the planet is called Morcia.  Morcia is also the name of the world in which Knights Kingdom II takes place.  Seeing as both Perpetual Doom and Knights Kingdom II share common attributes in names and theme, (King Mathias, Vladek, Morcia, and lots of Knightliness) we could tie them together.  It makes sense that Perpetual Doom takes place after Knights Kingdom II - but at the same time it also makes sense that Perpetual Doom would take place before Knights Kingdom II.

 

Now that I think of it, the simplest way to bypass these issues is to say that Perpetual Doom takes place in a parallel universe of the Knights Kingdom II world - Perpetual Doom shares the same important people and the same world as Knights Kingdom II, but the story is different in some ways or others.

 

Now that that's written, we know this information: If I made a map of Morcia, I'd probably have to include landmarks within Knights Kingdom II's world.  If I remember Knights Kingdom II's story well enough, we have King Mathias's castle here, a forest here, then a canyon down this path with a scary tower, and a wilderness down the other path.  At the end of the wilderness is the rogue Vladek's palace.

 

So I'll probably have to include those.  Okay, I could probably do that.  I don't remember the exact names, so I'll just have to call them "Wilderness" and "Forest" and whatever.

 

Then there are landmarks in Perpetual Doom.  There's King Mathias's castle, lots of roads, a big forest, lots of farmland and plains, that river separating the northern and southern hemispheres of the planet, and the unknown land of the Elephanters.  We described some geography inside the story, but not much.

 

 

 

 

 


Ah, I have a lot of work to do if I really am going to make this map.

“Why Sentinel? Why not Assembly? That’s the best faction,” Henry said proudly.

“Is not!” Said Alex, the 9-year-old Venture League lover.

“Is too!” Said Henry.

“Is not!” Said Alex.

“AWESOME!!!” Sally yelled, finishing his sentence for him.

“I was thinking more along the lines of horrifying…” Henry mumbled.

“No more interruptions! You ASKED me to tell this story to you!”
