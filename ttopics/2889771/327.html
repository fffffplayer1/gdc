* * * * *

Chapter 1: Beasts from Below:

 

The kingdom of Salinor has been ruled peacefully for centuries. The reason is because of one river: The River of the Gods. This river is a rainbow of colors, each color representing a certain god. This river is never blocked, never polluted in any way, for fear of the wrath of the Gods. And near the edge of the shoreline, the river plunges deeply downwards into a dark abyss, which leads straight to the Underworld. And down there, dark forces are stirring.

 

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

 

Alex Thatcher was walking along a dirt road, going down to the town to get some food for himself. He looked around at the beautiful morning, sun shining through the trees, birds flying about, and the fish swimming about in their pond. The town was still about a mile away, but he didn’t mind; he was enjoying his walk. Suddenly, the ground exploded beneath him. Dirt was flung all about, rocky shards slammed on the ground next to him. But that was not as terrifying as what he say next: A huge form, looming out of the dust, almost like a massive mole, but with sharp claws, and deep, black eyes. It sniffed around with its long nose, and dove back into the gaping hole it popped out of. Alex was terrified. He knew he couldn’t stay there; the thing might come back up! And if this beast was wondering around, he had to warn the town. He ran as quickly as he could to the town, not stopping till he made it there.

Chapter 1: Beasts from Below:

 

The kingdom of Salinor has been ruled peacefully for centuries. The reason is because of one river: The River of the Gods. This river is a rainbow of colors, each color representing a certain god. This river is never blocked, never polluted in any way, for fear of the wrath of the Gods. And near the edge of the shoreline, the river plunges deeply downwards into a dark abyss, which leads straight to the Underworld. And down there, dark forces are stirring.

 

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

 

Alex Thatcher was walking along a dirt road, going down to the town to get some food for himself. He looked around at the beautiful morning, sun shining through the trees, birds flying about, and the fish swimming about in their pond. The town was still about a mile away, but he didn’t mind; he was enjoying his walk. Suddenly, the ground exploded beneath him. Dirt was flung all about, rocky shards slammed on the ground next to him. But that was not as terrifying as what he say next: A huge form, looming out of the dust, almost like a massive mole, but with sharp claws, and deep, black eyes. It sniffed around with its long nose, and dove back into the gaping hole it popped out of. Alex was terrified. He knew he couldn’t stay there; the thing might come back up! And if this beast was wondering around, he had to warn the town. He ran as quickly as he could to the town, not stopping till he made it there.
