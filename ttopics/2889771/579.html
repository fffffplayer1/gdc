You have to realise that when there are many authors, if the story is not very well planned, it's difficult to always be consistent. You'll see that later when the number of active Knights and hence authors is diminished, it seems to lack of this problem (I think). The fact that chapters were spread apart, so that an author couldn't have easy access to the whole text did not help (thus someone could forget parts of the plot, possibly causing contradictions in their chapters).

As for Scattered Ashes, when Perpetual Doom was finished Thingguy proposed that the multiwriter nature of the series should turn to one different author writing each book, his starting with Scattered Ashes. However, at some point he got inactive and even though he reappeared later, he went inactive again. Thus, Scattered Ashes is unfinished. Yet, the Additional Manuscripts came to be, because of this, so that KotOS could write new material concerning the series.
