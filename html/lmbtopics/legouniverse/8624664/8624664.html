Hey there! This is Howls of Shadow, my LU story, rewritten. As I mentioned earlier, it's not terribly different from the orginal, but it does have a lot of other plot building once we really get into it. Here we go....

 

Prologue:

 

Maelstrom rules. Everything. Everyone. There’s no escaping them.

 

They don’t infect everyone like you might think, because they only have a small supply of the Maelstrom Core. Instead, they snatch you up when you do things wrong, never to be heard of again. It teaches everyone around you pretty fast about what not to do. You are to live your life by their rule. What they have to benefit from us? No one is sure. We don’t give them anything. So maybe their leader likes to watch our suffering.

 

Maybe he enjoys the pain on our faces. The clutched stomachs of little children at night, trying to convince themselves that tomorrow will be better. The fear engraved in our expressions. Maybe he doesn’t enjoy it. Maybe he’s completely indifferent, not even caring to look.

 

The Nexus Force is gone, and a good thing too. All they caused was war, terrible war….

 

War.

Chapter 1

 

My muscles freeze in place, locked with invisible keys called forward by fear. Leathery fingers trace up and down my neck, sharp claws slicing off the small hairs. I know who it is, but as warm, slippery drool seeps down my back, I can’t help but shudder.

 

“It’s been awhile,” a voice whispers quietly to me, breathing fiery hot breath on my ear.

 

“So it has,” I say, as had become my normal response to that phrase. It never mattered whether he had seen me the day before or it had been a month; his statement was always the same.

 

“She will…,” he says, voice dripping with hatred.

 

I cut him off in the middle; he seemed to only have couple of phrases he would repeat. “She will take the shadows. I know.”

 

“Fine,” he says curtly, surely disturbed by me. Several freezing cold claws dash up and down my body, running their sharp blades around my middle; they dance across the top of my head. “Have you yet to see me?”

 

I involuntarily gulp, the memories of every time he has visited me appearing boldly before my eyes. He is altered every time. A gray monster with green eyes and long teeth. A broken hero lying  smashed at my feet. A small orange tabby who transformed into a strange purple beast before my eyes. This time, however, is different.

 

His claws slowly slip off of me and he ambles to the side, his form sickening me. I stand before something familiar. All too familiar.

 

Me.

 

My light, wavy brown hair lay slightly over my shoulders. My sharp nose. My thin mouth; I think it’s quivering. My eyes seem sunken back, devoid of all color or emotion. My hands are decorated with claws, not something I really have, but a monster he has made of me.

I stare in horror, taking a few scrambling steps back. He steps forward, forcing himself into my face. I’m gaping at myself, as if staring directly into a mirror. I scream, as I always do in these fits of terror; it’s piercing and deafening, but I can’t help it. It escapes my lips before I can restrain myself.

 

The only thing that quiets me is that my copy, only inches away from my head, returns the petrifying noise in a shriek.

 

I turn away and grip my ears, trying to block out the horrifying noise. It doesn’t work, only muffles the sound of pure terror, the sound that had once come from me. I believe it might just go on forever, that I’ll smash there, clutching my head as he screams. But no, the voice suddenly cuts off and I’m sitting there collapsed on the floor, staring at nothing.

 

I sit there, my lips stuck together as if with glue. I’m afraid if I move them I might do something stupid, like cry. I bite my tongue as hot tears spill out; this isn’t the place for that kind of pathetic behavior.

 

I run my fingers across the cold, stony floor of my own little room. Why does he torment me? And only me? Is it because I’m a thief? I know what I do is terrible, that’s the reason I’m here, but it is necessary. Is it because I’m here?

 

I glance around, taking in the blank objects that crowd me. The uncovered bed, the filthy walls, the iron bars hanging in front of me. The air is hushed, but someone, somewhere in another cell, starts to sing. The notes are soft and sad, each section carried out with a quavering voice. Yet, for a weird reason, it’s calming. I feel myself sinking into sleep as the song continues with the deep voice of its singer.

 

“Speak before me,

Let yourself fly free,

Raise your head to me,

And free you shall be.”

 

If only I was free.

Chapter 2

 

“Wake up!” a voice calls urgently, and I feel hands under my back push me up. I jolt forward to a sitting position, wondering what the guards would possibly be doing inside my cell. I’m about to jump away, asking what they want, when I find I’m not staring into the face of my ugly guards.

 

Instead, it’s a boy who I estimate to be a year or two older than me. He’s kneeling on the ground, pulling his hands away. He has dark hair that swirls on his forehead, and grayish green eyes that stare into mine intently. Small flecks of light project into his captivating eyes from the candle he’s holding beside him. His expression is hard to read, so I try to imagine he’s simply confused.

 

“How did you get in here?” I stammer, gazing past him where the iron door is cracked open.

 

In response, the boy simply holds up a small silver key and flashes it in front of the candle. “Come on.” He stands and offers his hand out to me. “You do want out, right?”

 

Of course I do, you fool. But taking a stranger’s help? Wearily, I take his hand and allow him to lift me up. “Yes.”

 

“Then let’s get moving. Keep quiet.” He touches the door with his fingertips and slowly pushes it open far enough for both of us to fit through.

 

Suddenly, I’m overcome with excitement. It’s almost strange how elating it is to take the first few steps away from your prison, the only objects you have seen in a long time disappearing before you.

 

Together, we creep past other cells, where people watch us intently, but silently. They don’t dare give us away. Even if they’re not escaping themselves, they respect us enough to stay quiet.

“Wake up!” a voice calls urgently, and I feel hands under my back push me up. I jolt forward to a sitting position, wondering what the guards would possibly be doing inside my cell. I’m about to jump away, asking what they want, when I find I’m not staring into the face of my ugly guards.

 

Instead, it’s a boy who I estimate to be a year or two older than me. He’s kneeling on the ground, pulling his hands away. He has dark hair that swirls on his forehead, and grayish green eyes that stare into mine intently. Small flecks of light project into his captivating eyes from the candle he’s holding beside him. His expression is hard to read, so I try to imagine he’s simply confused.

 

“How did you get in here?” I stammer, gazing past him where the iron door is cracked open.

 

In response, the boy simply holds up a small silver key and flashes it in front of the candle. “Come on.” He stands and offers his hand out to me. “You do want out, right?”

 

Of course I do, you fool. But taking a stranger’s help? Wearily, I take his hand and allow him to lift me up. “Yes.”

 

“Then let’s get moving. Keep quiet.” He touches the door with his fingertips and slowly pushes it open far enough for both of us to fit through.

 

Suddenly, I’m overcome with excitement. It’s almost strange how elating it is to take the first few steps away from your prison, the only objects you have seen in a long time disappearing before you.

 

Together, we creep past other cells, where people watch us intently, but silently. They don’t dare give us away. Even if they’re not escaping themselves, they respect us enough to stay quiet.
