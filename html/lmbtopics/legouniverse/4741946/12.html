~The Imperial Seat

 

After returning to Icarus, the pirate found that the Insinuation was unable to pay him for his efforts, and finished the leader of the Insinuation off, before making a daring escape with the Baron!

With the leader of the Insinuation gone, what will happen next?

 

Xandrill folded the newspaper in half and set it on his pristine, smooth white desk. His fingers tapped against the table’s surface, slowly increasing in speed, his long, painted nails clicking strangely on the glass top.

 

With the leader of the Insinuation gone, what will happen next?

 

That one sentence replayed in his mind over and over again. The Insinuation was over, defeated. With its leader gone, it would crumble, and the Iron Law would regain the title of the Ascension.

The once great empire, with one, swift stroke by a known pirate outlaw, would be allowed to return.

Xandrill’s thin lips curled into a papery crescent, the slight smile radiating triumph.

He would once again resume his former role. His powerful seat.

After the last of the Insinuation was wiped out, Xandrill would be Emperor once again.

~The Maelstrom

 

The hologram fizzed to life before his glazed, artificial eyes. A man appeared over the holo-pad, his shoulders emboldened by the frilly, stunning gold epaulets that sat there like flashy gargoyles on a manor. His muscular chest was mostly covered by black, sclerite-like plates of gold-tipped armour, slightly obscured by a dark violet tabard, emblazoned unmistakably by what looked like a snake’s eye, peering from a bush of dangerous, strangling thorns- neatly yet unpretentiously, all in a deep scarlet ink.

His helmet wholly obscured his visage, as if pertaining to an altogether ulterior goal of sworn secrecy. A long, thin, black reflective visor stretched across the expanse from the edge of each eye, connecting at the glabella to make a curved slate of mirroring glass. A glowing red cross-hair danced across the visor in an almost playful manner, as if ecstatic at being the main instrument to target innocents.

And in the man’s knuckle-duster set hands was a long, crystalline purple, ceremonial staff, topped with a draping banner with the same insignia as was painted on his tabard. The staff was engraved with elegant filigree of gold and ruby, as to accentuate the man’s obvious high standing.

 

“Hail!” The man’s voice rumbled from his holographic avatar’s sealed helm in a commanding tone. “You are in the presence of the Imperial Guard.”

 

“I am conversing with a long-distance, three-dimensional mook. I am also incapable of feeling emotion, so there is no way you can taunt or ridicule. And no, my lack of empathy doesn’t mean that I’m a psychopath- it merely states that you are unable to create a machine that can properly connect with its makers.”

There was a tentative pause, in which Meyer Muckrake (For of course it was him speaking with the hologram) attempted an imitation of triumph, and the little projection stood, as statue-like as before.

 

“I was joking. Goodness, you don’t program me with emotion, but you do give me a sense of humour that no one seems to be able to appreciate. It’s complete and utter discrimination.”

 

The figure seemed to go a little less tense. “Ah." The holographic figure managed.

 

“And ‘Imperial Guard’?” Meyer added the barest tone of a scoff, pre-recorded from one of Vanda Darkflame’s cynical meltdowns, and vocally altered later on.

 

“Our Lord, the Great and Powerful Emperor Xandrill, has promoted us back into our former positions. You now answer to us.”

 

“Wonderful.” Meyer droned, adding a drop of sarcasm, for experimental effect. “On who’s authority?”

 

The figure paused, then, with a nod of his head, the hologram popped offline.

 

“Typical. Don’t know the answer of a question, so they wag their tails goodbye.”

 

“On my own, of course. No one has the power to object.” This voice didn’t carry half as much weight as the guard before, but its dark, manipulative undertone drove a chill through the currents beneath the waves.

Meyer Muckrake peered back down at the holo-pad. If he had been capable of showing emotion, he’d have probably jumped back in shock.

 

“My Lord.” Meyer tilted his head forward respectfully, and leaned forward on his ergonomic chair.

The hologram of Xandrill- recently crowned Emperor of the Ascension (By himself, of course) nodded in return.

“How may I be of service?” Meyer asked, and then, as an afterthought, added, “And what in the stars is on with that atrocious ‘topi’? How do you prevent getting cramps in your neck?”

 

“I don’t see a deer anywhere.”

 

“I meant topi as in the hat.”

 

“Oh, yes, of course.” The Emperor looked flustered, and scowled slightly, as if outraged at this little nudge of fun, probably considering it a threat to his throne. “It’s a ceremonial possession. Kings wear crowns, end of story. And for your last question, I don’t. King’s bear pain, end of another story.”

 

The crown was half a meter tall, plastered beautifully with dark scarlet velvet, peppered with gold dust. It's front rose up on a tall, curving, smooth golden plate, crested with a large, uncut shard of diamond. Curving golden spikes rose around the jewel-encrusted base of the helm like pristine and gallant antelope horns, enveloped and webbed in what looked like prayer beads.

He wore flowing, violet robes, lined with more dancing gold, curling and swivelling in the shape of lung dragons and naga. A large necklace of rudraksha hung from his long, thin neck, obviously stolen or bartered.

His fingers were laden with rings, his wrists with bangles, his arms and hands painted with thick, still-drying henna. His nails were long and polished into red.

“Ah.” Meyer Muckrake muttered lightly. “And why’d you call?”

 

“You shall refer to me as ‘your grace’, or some other high term.”

 

“Oh heathen king, lord of the setting sun, master of the growing flora, chief producer of plant-killing pesticides and world-wrecking machines of war-”

 

“As for what you can do to me, I need you to divert from your current course.”

 

Meyer blinked. “What for?”

 

“Make for the Imperial Seat. I shall personally speak with you and your company there.”

 

“I am sorry, your malevolence and future obesity, but we are on a rather important mission. If we halt our chase for a mere day, we shall lose our quarry.”

 

Xandrill’s hologram stared into Meyer’s photoreceptors. He seemed to have missed the insults, although Meyer sincerely doubted it. The man had a mind like the diamond on his head- sharp and interweaving, and harder than metal.

 

“Do I look like I care?”
