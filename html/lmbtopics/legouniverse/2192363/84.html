Chapter 19: Sometimes there aren’t just two sides in a battle Part 4

 

          The rock walls and ceiling at the narrow part started shifting. A slit appeared in the middle of the ceiling and the two parts of it started getting away from each other and going higher. Two more slits appeared on the walls, these ones vertical, opposite to each other. As the rocks shifted more and more Shadow thought they resembled two people getting up from a sort of sitting with the back curled position. The Magmaline started to mumble worriedly.

          As the two pieces of rock were getting up, they could clearly see that the one had magma parts while the other had crystals. In the end, they had two huge rock creatures before them glaring at each other. They resembled minifigures more than the Crystallines and Magmalines, but not as much as the Figlines. Compared to them, the Rocklines Maximus were little children. And they did not seem very happy.

          Now, the cave had been turned to a whole quite big one that fit the two tremendous figures standing tall. Meanwhile, some rocks had collapsed blocking the path behind them.

          “RUN!” Garutho commanded.

          The minifigures, then, astounded saw Garutho run between the huge creatures followed by the Magmaline. As the three of them started running, too, the Crystalline giant raised his arms slowly to push the Magmaline giant. As he hit the wall, pieces of the ceiling and walls detached from the rest and fell. The Magmaline looked down and grabbed the first thing it saw: the Draconus. The creature did not seem half as frightening trying to escape the hand of the giant. However, it did not get away and the giant launched it at the face of the other. Shadow even felt pity for it, as it hit hard on the Crystal giant’s face.

Chapter 20: The Lonely Islands Part 1

 

          Shadow set his rocket to follow Kate’s and looked at his arm to tend to it. It had been rendered useless –temporarily at least. Other than the wound from the laser his fake hand had also melted –in its bigger part– by that Rockling Troll.

          The young man spread some ointment over his wound, wrapped with a bandage, drank some Notion Potion and searched in his backpack to replace the destroyed hand. It was a good thing he was prepared for such an occasion and had a spare one. He felt quite better after that.

          “How are you two?” he asked the others through the rocket communicator.

          “I am mostly alright, but a laser scratched me at the waist!” Kate replied.

          “I am not in the best of conditions because a laser found me almost at the same spot where that rock hit me. But I can handle it!” Magical said and his voice revealed pain “What about you?”

          “I was hit on the back of my arm, but I bandaged it! Where should we go now?”

          “Let’s just…” Kate started saying, but then two rockets appeared behind them; they were the same minifigures chasing them.

          “What’s their problem?!” Magical complained.

          “They are a pain in the brick!” Shadow said, mostly to himself.

          The three of them started maneuvering their rockets to avoid the fire of their pursuers. Shadow turned the weapons he had installed on his rocket to fire behind him. However, the two minifigures were expert rocketeers and easily dodged his attacks. The others attacked, too, but the minifigures were indeed better at them at flying rockets. Shadow decided he officially hated rocket-flying even more.

Chapter 20: The Lonely Islands Part 2

 

          Shadow pressed a button for the rocket to continue firing on its own and started building some things inside his backpack. He couldn’t see the bricks, but a master builder does not need to see the bricks. They can feel what they are building.

          Shadow’s rocket was struck. It started moving less smoothly. Looking at his friends, he saw their rockets had similar problems. They were already damaged from the previous rocket chase. But Shadow noticed they were approaching another world.

          On the first sight, the chunk of Crux seemed to be covered only with water. However, with a second look you’d notice there was an island group in its centre. Shadow headed for it.

          They had just entered its gravity field when Shadow’s rocket was hit again and he lost control of it, as it started making circles. Coincidentally, severe damage was also caused at the other rockets about at the same time. But Shadow finished what he was building.

          “Turn off your engines!” Shadow said to the others.

          “What?! Are you crazy? We will fall!” Magical hesitated.

          “Just do it!” Shadow ordered turning off his own.

          At the same time, he released a brick wall out of his backpack. It was bigger than the previous one and blocked the sight of the rocketeers while forcing them to change direction. In the meantime, he also released some rocket replicas sending them at other directions, but towards the world. That was meant to confuse their chasers since they wouldn’t know which of the falling rockets would be the right ones.

Chapter 20: The Lonely Islands Part 2

 

          Thankfully, their own rockets fell near one of the islands. Their rockets entered the dark water and it ‘consumed’ them as if it had been a greedy beast. Their weight brought them down, but the minifigures got out of them in time. Only Magical got out after the rocket joined the sea, but he managed to get out.

          The momentum with which they fell from the skies brought them quite deep, too. However, buoyancy, helped by the flapping of their arms and the kicking of their legs, brought them to the surface again. Physics is not always against you.

          Shadow gasped for air once he was out in the air. He saw Kate’s head a few metres away from his panting like he did. Magical, having gone deeper, came out last and his face was red. He breathed in the sweet air and almost relaxed as the feeling that his lungs would burst subsided. They started swimming towards the coast.

          While from upwards it didn’t seem like a long distance, Shadow realised they weren’t that close to the island. It certainly was not a distance the average summer beach-visitor would go from the beach. Yet, it didn’t seem impossible to reach it –as long as they did not freeze to smashing; the waters were quite cold.

          They did not talk, saving their powers for swimming. None of them was a very good swimmer, not swimming often either back in Yorkanton or in the Nimbus System. Only Kate seemed to be doing a tad better, but it was not an important difference and it might have been because the boys were injured.

          Shadow started blaming himself for not making sure to bring a boat or pieces for one with him in his adventures before they’d even covered half the distance. How could he have forgotten that in all of his months in the Nexus Force?! Many of the worlds of the Nimbus System had sea, but Shadow had just not thought of it. Now, he regretted that very much.

Chapter 20: The Lonely Islands Part 4

 

          To make things even worse, after they had covered about half the distance –already feeling exhausted– one light appeared on the dark sky. It was moving and it was closer than the stars. Coming closer, it got bigger and then they realised it was a helicopter. A searchlight was lighting the water searching for something –probably them.

          “Dive!” said Kate and they all did at once after taking a deep breath.

          The light of the searchlight soon appeared above them, though through the water they couldn’t make out the helicopter itself. The light moved around the area in which they were. Shadow hoped it would soon move on to somewhere else, because he could feel his lungs starting to get uncomfortable asking for new air.

          The light finally left, but Shadow motioned for them to stay there for a bit. The helicopter was still close and it wasn’t wise to get out immediately. Shadow’s head started spinning as his lungs’ request for oxygen became more and more urgent. They soon started to go upwards.

          They got to the surface again and started coughing through their panting. Shadow wanted to stop swimming and relax. However, he knew that if he did the freezing water would overcome him. He started swimming towards the coast again trying not to think of what would happen when the helicopter would get back.

          “Shadow!” Kate called his name with a worried tone.

          The minifigure looked back and saw Kate pointing to Magical. He had stayed behind and had his eyes closed, not moving.

          “Oh no! Magical, wake up!” Shadow tried to bellow at him, but with a tired voice while changing direction splashing towards him.

          “I just want to rest a little and continue…” Magical said with a voice indicating he was on the verge of lethargy “after the break!”

Chapter 20: The Lonely Islands Part 5

 

          “No, Magical! You must not give in to it! The water…!” Shadow told him.

          Magical did not respond. He seemed to have fainted. His legs came up as if he was lying on the water. Shadow did not want to do this, because it could reveal their location to their enemy. By now, he had reached Magical and Kate was next to him, too. He reached in his backpack, which fortunately was waterproof and grabbed his spare rocket. He started pressing buttons on it before it was back to its normal size. Meanwhile, Kate tried to wake Magical. He opened his eyes slightly, weakly.

          “Grab on!” Shadow said.

          Magical, despite his state, gripped on the rocket, which was now almost at its full size and starting to bring them down. Shadow placed his hands over Magical’s to make sure he wouldn’t let go and Kate having grabbed on, too, he pressed one more button.

          The rocket shot through the water. In the beginning, it brought them a little below the surface, but Shadow had set it to bring them back up. Meanwhile, Kate managed to get on the seat of the pilot, so as to make sure it wouldn’t get too high or too deep.

          “Hang on, Magical! A little more!” Shadow whispered his friend.

          The rocket was much faster and Shadow’s mood changed for the better as he saw the coast coming to them in a much more reasonable speed. They had hopes again. And indeed it wasn’t many minutes before Kate made the rocket slow down and stop, falling to the bottom, which was not deep anymore though.

          Kate and Shadow dragged themselves and Magical to the less wet part of the sand beach and lay down panting despite the danger of being found.

Chapter 20: The Lonely Islands Part 6

 

          “Worst… idea… ever!” Magical remarked through his panting in spite of his condition and then fainted again.

          “It looks like we’re in the Lonely Islands of Batheia!” Kate said from her spot.

          “Ah yeah! I’d heard the Venture League was looking for this place!” Shadow replied.

          Shadow lay for a little bit more and then spoke “We should better get up!”

          Even though his body protested, he stood up and looked around. There was no one around… or was there? While he turned his back towards the sea he got a glimpse of something moving. He looked over his back and realised why he hadn’t noticed it. It was a crocodile. But not any crocodile: a Maelstrom Crocodile!

          “Kate! Careful!” he warned the girl while grabbing his crossbow.

          He fired an arrow, which hit the crocodile, but did not harm it. The beast’s flails were harder than a normal crocodile’s and it was bigger, too. The Maelstrom Being opened its huge mouth full of sharp teeth threateningly and started walking towards them. It was indeed fast.

          “Don’t let it bite you! It won’t let go!” advised Kate.

          They walked away from the brute while hoping it wouldn’t notice Magical. They launched arrows towards it, but they did not penetrate the flails. As it got closer, Shadow threw a bunch of bricks joined together into its half-opened mouth, hoping that that might stop it. However, the beast crashed the bricks with its teeth.

Chapter 20: The Lonely Islands Part 7

 

          Shadow turned his weapon into an ice saw again and tried to hit the crocodile, which had come closer now, on the head. He was successful, but this did not stop it. It opened its mouth in order to bite. Kate was quick and fired an arrow into its wide open mouth. However, she was not quick enough and it had time to close its jaws around Shadow’s leg.

          The arrow, though, managed to harm the Maelstromling and it let go. It started thrashing about in pain. Kate then grabbed her dagger and finished it with it.

          “We had better get going! There might be more!” Shadow said approaching Magical “Let alone the…”

          He had just then noticed the approaching light of a helicopter coming from the side of the sea. Only seconds after that, they found themselves being surrounded. A team of ten minifigures with blue uniform clothes and rifles pointed at them was circling them. At the next moment the searchlight of the helicopter found them and blinded the two minifigures. One of the minifigures spoke.

          “If you move, we will smash you! You have intruded the Lonely Islands Crux Chunk illegally! Vincent, handcuff them!”. . .
