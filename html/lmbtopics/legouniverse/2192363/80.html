Chapter 10: The lost girl’s story Part 1

 

          Strange Odd Shadow was still smirking when he reached the Nexus Tower. He had sent off Magical to search properties as well, for the time being, so that he could talk with Nirwell. This discussion could be of utmost importance and shouldn’t be delayed.

          According to the information the nurse who had called him gave him, the girl had decided that she liked Strange Odd Shadow and wanted to speak to him and not so much with Dr. Moswar, the psychiatrist. Shadow could imagine the doctor raging on this and he smiled. He almost felt the urge to laugh. He had a little, when he first heard the news.

          Meanwhile, he was already getting reports on the property search. He had made some sort of online list thingy that was only accessible by the FFFFF members. Someone could log in at any time and change the state of a property from “Unknown” to “Ruined/Pseudo-Infected” or “Normal”. And also any one of them could log in and see the state of the already visited properties. So far, Brick, Adam, Kate, Sarah, Lemon and Jean had all found ruined properties. Just as Shadow had imagined, the problem was far wider than it seemed in the beginning.

          Shadow enjoyed his walk till the level where the Mental/Psychic Diseases and Stuff wing of the Nexus Hospital was. By that time, he’d learnt the route like the back of his hand –both fake and real. Once he was at Nirwell’s room, he knocked on the door cheerfully. The people outside had been quite reduced, but there were still some people. Someone told him to come in and Shadow did.

Chapter 10: The lost girl’s story Part 2

 

          The young man found Dr. Moswar, Vlad and of course Nirwell in. Vlad had become something like a protector or a bodyguard for Nirwell. He was one of the people that she felt the safest with and the most insecure without. Shadow did not know whether she really needed protection from anything, but he didn’t argue if that made the girl happy.

          Shadow waved a greeting at Vlad, smiled facetiously at the doctor and then turned to Nirwell.

          “Miss Nirwell! So happy to see you! I’m glad you asked for me! How are you feeling today?” he greeted.

          “Hello, Dr. Shadow!” Nirwell replied with a somewhat insecure smile “I am fine! My injuries hurt less!”

          “But I am no doctor!” Shadow laughed “I have neither studied medicine nor gotten a doctorate! Would you like to have a walk or stay here?”

          “Let’s walk!”

          “After you!” Shadow pointed to the door.

          Leaving, he waved at Vlad again and winked at Moswar. As they got out, they first talked about unimportant things –but not for the weather, that’s too cliché-ish– and only started really talking when they felt comfortable in the loneliness of a huge crowd.

          “You remember about the property I told you?” Nirwell asked, most probably rhetorically.

          “Yes indeed! I even visited it! Found a suspicious man there! But he got away!” Shadow responded.

          “Good! Well, in that property more people were expecting me!” the blonde girl started.

Chapter 10: The lost girl’s story Part 3

 

          After many nightmares, many of which she was afraid to recall, Nirwell woke up in some dark, humid place, probably a cell. She didn’t know how much time had passed, but she supposed it was more than a day. For some time, she was left to her delusions and with hindsight, Nirwell would have preferred it to have stayed at that. But those were not the intentions of her captors.

          At some point, something opened up and light entered the room blinding the girl. In the instance before the door –because it probably was a door that had opened– closed, Nirwell saw two wicked cold eyes watching her. The voice that spoke afterwards was just as cruel as the eyes. He told her that her alliance with the Nexus Force had been wrong and that now she was marred. Described now, this might not seem as something too terrible, but his words were far more persuasive. And the seeds of doubt of one’s choices can drive one mad alone. But this is not where it ended. The man told her that perhaps if she talked she’d almost be redeemed. At first, Nirwell did not want to speak, but then came the torturing. At that point of the story, Nirwell’s voice cracked and she did not want to speak of the tortures. Shadow did not push her, but he assumed that her injuries and the infection of her right leg had occurred at that point.

          As this continued and it was certainly many days that it did, the girl’s resistance cracked. She spoke and told the man many things. But the thing is she didn’t know what he wanted her to tell. So, no matter what she said the tortures continued. And Nirwell was trying to find out what she was doing wrong and what there was to say.

Chapter 10: The lost girl’s story Part 4

 

          And so passed the time, with the only measure of it being the meals she was brought (which she was sure weren’t even coming twice a day, but any time the people wanted) and the times the man came to torture her. Throughout the whole process, she was given some other substance that made everything blurry; even senses other than sight were affected and thinking was harder. Only the sense of pain was acuter than ever –and perhaps of warmth and cold, too.

          After that, Nirwell got through something just as bad, if not more. They got her out of the cell and led her through dark hallways to some open bright place. It was also loud, but with the fuzziness of everything she couldn’t understand where she was. They called to her to fight and she realised there was something in front of her –not something, but someone. Nirwell did not realise much what was happening. With clumsy moves and her weak legs barely able to bear her she swung a weapon she had just been given. She thought the same was done by the other person. And then suddenly the other person was smashed. And Nirwell watched their bricks fall to the ground. And her mind was clearer now, but not as clear.

          Upon the realisation of what she had just done, Nirwell screamed her lungs out and fell to her knees. She couldn’t bear what had just happened. Someone urged her with a lance to get up again, but Nirwell continued mourning the fate of the person she didn’t know, but had just been smashed by her own hands. And then someone else passed by her and she heard a voice in her ear: “Fight them! Don’t give up!”

Chapter 10: The lost girl’s story Part 5

 

          The voice sounded tormented, but proud. Nirwell looked at who had just passed and saw a boy with skin of ebony. He was looking at her with eyes that sparked. He was a captive, too, but he was free. And he had just freed Nirwell as well.

          The girl got up decisively. She was pushed by metal and found herself in front of some other minifigure. The situation had not changed and the smashed were still smashed. But Nirwell had changed and she understood this was neither the place nor the time for mourning.

          “Play it dead!” she had whispered to the other person as she attacked with her sword.

          “What?!” the minifigure asked confusedly as they blocked.

          “They might spare you, if they think you are not alive!” she explained.

          The person understood. They pretended to fight for a bit and then the other fell down supposedly by her weapon. Nirwell tried that with three more people. It seemed to work. But in the end, it didn’t.

          Soon, Nirwell was dragged back to her cell. There she was told that her scheme had been discovered! One of the three had been smashed in the process of hunting them down, but the two others were captured again. Now, they were going to be tortured because of her. They moved Nirwell to another room where the two minifigures had been put. They had her watch them be punished. And this was a torment of itself without her own torturing that followed.

          After a new time period in her cell, but also in the room of the other men, too, she was brought to the bright place again. She wasn’t sure what she was going to do. She didn’t want to smash people, but could she let herself be smashed instead? But then she remembered the boy and its eyes and what he had said. And she took her decision.

Chapter 10: The lost girl’s story Part 6

 

          When she was put in front of another minifigure, she looked the woman in the eyes and poked her sword into the ground. She felt the acute pain of the blade next. The feeling of her pieces being torn from each other was realer than ever. Only the time she had been smashed in the Great Battle was like it. And Nirwell expected that to be the end.

          But it was not! And it was after that that Nirwell assumed she had that ability concerning smashing. She woke up in an unholy place full of skeletons and scattered pieces of minifigures. It seemed like a cavern. It took her time to find her way out, because it also was labyrinthine. But when she did, she found something that was lucky to the point of suspicion. A Portalling happened to be there. Portallings are a kind of Stromlings that has the ability to create portals to pass from place to place. Nirwell knew from experience that those portals could also be used by others if you jumped in before they closed. And when the Portalling created a vortex, which was quite soon, Nirwell was ready and jump she did.

          That got her out in Perith City. The creature was nowhere to be seen. Yet, it was soon forgotten due to the frightfulness of the place she had ended up to. All that happened through confusion and fear and Nirwell’s mind was never really clear until she woke up in the Nexus Hospital.

          And that was the story that Nirwell spake to Shadow that day. And Shadow having heard it was sitting long contemplating it, for it was most concerning.

          “Thank you, Nirwell! For telling me all that! It has been most helpful! And I’m sure it wasn’t easy for you to say it. But I hope you are feeling better now!”

          Nirwell smiled. She did feel better. Shadow soon led her back to her room. But as Shadow left, he was still contemplating on the story. . .

Chapter 11: The searching man searches Part 1

 

          Namandel’s rocket landed on the Nimbus Isle property of that girl, Nirwell. Shadow had added her properties to the list as well. Namandel walked in it. By now, he was familiar with the sight of a ruined property; Scattered bricks all over, with Maelstrom aura covering a lot of its surface. So, Shadow was right to connect the two cases.

          Namandel looked around the property for the sign of anything that could be a clue. He didn’t seem to find anything. But then he noticed some movement in a nearby tree. It was growing on a rock and it was higher than he was. And as he looked and came nearer, a flock of birds came out of it and started flying.

          The birds made a V formation and they seemed to fly away. But then to Namandel’s surprise they turned back. And it looked like they were heading for him. As they came nearer and nearer, Namandel grabbed his spear and shield. They could be aggressive. And then he noticed the purple stains on them. They were infected! That certainly meant a fight.

          The birds reached him and he felt their sharp beaks cutting him and biting him. He attacked with his spear and upon the formation’s collision he smashed one or two. Only when they drew back to strike again, did he realise what kind of birds they were.

          “Ducks?! Really? Ducks?!” Namandel exclaimed.

          Due to his being quite big, he was an easy target for the Maelstrom Ducks, while his weapon was not suitable for attacking a flock of birds. That’s why in the next attack he was even more hurt while he still only smashed two.

          At the third strike, Namandel fell down. How could these ducks be beating him?

          “I will not be smashed by stupid ducks!!!” Namandel shouted and his voice would have echoed loud and clear in a long distance, had they been in a whole planet.

Chapter 11: The searching man searches Part 2

 

          He let his shield and put his hand inside his backpack. The ducks were already turning back to strike for the fourth time and perhaps the last one, so Namandel grabbed the first thing he found and got it out… It was a hot dog!

          “A hot dog! Really?!” Namandel complained to his luck.

          Namandel quickly put his hand inside the backpack again in search of something else. But the ducks were coming. And then with the rush of adrenaline Shadow remembered what the hot dog was.

          He put his spear down and grabbed the hot dog again and tossed it in his mouth. He was still munching it when the first beak bit his stomach. And then he opened his mouth…

          Fire was spit all over the ducks who were gathering over him to attack. Burning ducks started flapping away, scattering all over the property, but eventually dropping on the ground and smashing. The few of them that were still alive fled for fear of a similar fate. Namandel continued lying on the ground trying to catch his breath.

 

                                           ~~~***---***~~~

 

          Brick was approaching the launch pad of yet another destroyed property. He couldn’t understand why anyone would do that! It really made no sense to him that anyone would want to smash everything in a property.

          However, as he reached the launch pad he heard a crash! Turning around he saw a smashed rocket in the inside of the property. It caught fire, but Brick ran towards it to help whoever had crashed. He found Adam!

          Brick dragged his friend away from the fire. The minifigure coughed and looked at the person who was helping him.

          “Brick! You saved me! Thank you!” he said.

          “You sound surprised!” Brick replied smiling.
