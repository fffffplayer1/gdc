From now on, first person means Hazel or Ryous. I am no longer doing either of them in third person, or anyone else in first person.

Chapter 2
Valiance Commander Hide Raxon stood in the doorway of an abandoned building. He wore Rank 3 Hero gear, not nearly as powerful as LordBrockTree’s but still packing a punch. He hoped he wouldn’t need to use it, though. His tracking sense told him the minifigure he was looking for was in the building. That same tracking sense was why he was a member of the Valiance in the first place. He used to be a member of the Venture League, but he found he liked the Hero gear better than the Scout, which was too much like the Daredevil for his taste. He sensed his quarry was in the shadowy corner of the room.
“I don’t like visitors.”
“Are you Zatorak?”
The minifigure walked into the light. He was wearing black armor Hide Raxon had never seen before. An intense aura of maelstrom radiated from him, far stronger than even Butterscorch.
“I’ve got a proposition for you. You see this?”
Raxon showed the minifig he assumed was Zatorak a gold badge in the shape of a lion. Other than its shape and coloration, it was the same as the badges minifigures obtain when they join a faction.
“I see it.”
“This is the proof,” Raxon paused to calm himself down, “Of the creation of a new faction.”
Zatorak seemed surprised by this, but Raxon couldn’t tell for sure.
“Aren’t four factions bad enough?”
“This faction is different. It’s for minifigures LIKE YOU.”
Raxon had a feeling Zatorak was considering his options. He knew that he had been sent to recruit Zatorak because of his skill at finding those who don’t want to be found, but he suspected that the Leader might have a bone to pick with him. This, of course, was not the case, but Raxon was a very frightened minifig. Zatorak’s answer was exactly what his file suggested it would be:
“No.”
Raxon headed for the exit, feeling dejected. Then he remembered another detail. “The Faction Leader is the Queen of Valia.”
That got Zatorak’s attention. For the first time he actually seemed to care about what Raxon was saying. “Tell you what, Magger: I will think about it… AFTER I talk to her in person. When the Queen gets up and comes here herself, then I will consider. No sooner.”
*************************************************************************************
“Hazel!”
I woke up in my bed in Nexus Tower. The other four Faction Leaders were standing over me, looking concerned. I groggily tried to sit up. It was Duke who had yelled my name. When I first became a Faction Leader about a month ago that would have surprised me, but I learned that Duke is just hard-headed, not heartless.
Ugh. My head was throbbing. “Hazel,” Overbuild said, “What exactly happened? You just went and fainted.”
“I-I-I-” I suppose I was going to have to tell them eventually. But that didn’t mean I’d have to tell them ALL of it.
“I had a vision.”
This surprised them. “Of the future?” Overbuild asked.
“No. Of the past. But past events before me.”
Before the other Leaders had looked concerned. Now they were distinctly worried. Duke left, as did Overbuild. Vanda apparently decided to break the tension, as she said, “Is that your laptop?”
“WAS my laptop is more realistic.”
“We have a great tech support person. Hold on…”
I sat back and waited for the tech support person to arrive.

Part 3: Tech Support
As I waited, I wondered if there was anything to eat nearby. There was a jar of pudding on my bedside table. I didn’t like pudding much, and didn’t see why anyone would want a whole jar of the stuff. Five minutes later my stomach overruled my dislike and I picked up the jar and a spoon near it. Suddenly the jar was yanked out of my hands by a mechanical arm, which screeched, “PUDDING!”
The arm retracted back behind the door, and a female voice yelled, “Stop it, Mech! Pudding is bad for you!”
“PUDDING!!!!!”
A girl stepped through the door, her trenchcoat completely covered in pudding. She wore goggle with orange eyepieces lenses over her eyes. The arm that had grabbed my pudding(not that I wanted it anyways) was attached to her, as were three more like it. The arms were flailing around with the jug, spraying everything with pudding.
“Someone call tech support?” The girl asked.
“Yes.”
“I’m Gwen Octavius. Call me G. This is Mechanical Extending Chordate Harness. Call him M.E.C.H.”
“Hazel. Hazel Sprite. My laptop…”
G walked over to my laptop, and screamed in rage. “WHAT HAVE YOU DONE TO IT?!?! THE POOR LAPTOP!!
“Relax, it’s just a laptop.”
She looked as though she was going to tear me apart. “Just a laptop? JUST a laptop?!?!?MACHINES HAVE FEELINGS TOO, YOU KNOW!!!!”
I was officially confused by now. This girl, G, talked about my laptop as if it was an infant that had fallen down. “They do?” I asked, almost certain that this was the wrong thing to say.
“YES, THEY DO! WHAT DID YOU DO TO IT?!?!?”
“I let Hael borrow it.”
If it was possible for G to get even more mad, she did now. “STORM!!!!! THE BANE OF TECHNOLOGY!!!!! HOLD ON, TECHNOLOGY, YOUR TECHNOPATH WILL SAVE YOU!!!!!”
She rushed out of the room, and I felt sorry for Hael when she found him.

“So you shall. But the need is dire. I shall supply you with new armor, a new name, and a new team to command.”
In a flash of maelstrom energy three other minifigures were teleported into the cloud. Golden Boiling Storm knew all of them, as they were all former agents, before the N.F.I.A. was ruined by the Ultimate Race Incident. The telepath known as Crossfire, the rock creature called Hardplace, and the Darkitect’s operative Zipja, who had made short work of the Commanders. Each was enveloped in separate tornados of maelstrom, as was Storm.

“Zipja. The cunning. You shall be Nemesis. Sabotage creations with your trickery.”

Zipja became more goblin-like, looking less and less like a minifigure, and gained a chain with a buzz-saw at the end as his weapon.

“Rockor Hardplace. The strong. You shall take the name of Outburst. Destroy creations with your strength.”

Hardplace grew spikes, and turned purple as Zipja had done. His weapon was a massive hammer.

“Raven. The emotionless. You shall retain your title of Crossfire. Turn minifigures’ ideas against them with your telepathy.”

Raven’s plastic split into hundreds of scars, which radiated maelstrom. She too became purple. Elbow blades grew out of her arms for use as weapons.

“Golden Boiling Storm. The dark. You shall become my Dreadnought. Lead my Reapers. Destroy minifigures before they can think of ideas.”

The battle with Solid Frozen Warrior had blinded Storm, but now he found he could see again, using the maelstrom. He sent molecular particles of maelstrom energy out of his useless eyes, and they reported to him an exact view of the world around him. He looked down at his hands and saw that he was holding a two-handed rifle. He shot a burst at a nearby brick and was pleased by the results. “Ordinary maelstrom just separates or corrupts bricks,” he said to himself, “This gun deletes them from existence. It disintegrates plastic.”
“GO, MY REAPERS, MY SLAYERS OF IMAGINATION. COMPLETE THE TASK I HAVE GIVEN YOU. DESTROY LORDBROCKTREE.”
