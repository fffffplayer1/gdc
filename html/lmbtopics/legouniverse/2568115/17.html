“Hael, how soon till we depart?”
“A few days, Ryous. We needs ta get this ship running again.”
“You mean we’re journeying to Crux on a ship that doesn’t work?”
“Aye. It was cheap.”

“Ryous! Where ye be goin’?”
“To find some answers.”


The bazaar was packed with people, and I started finding it hard to maneuver around them. The last thing we needed was some reporter trying to publicize our trip. I was so lost in thought I almost ran into a kid messing with a jetpack.
“You know how to work that thing, kid?”
“I’m learning. My name’s Epsilon Starcracker.”
Epsilon Starcracker. For some reason I felt that he would become important in a time of crisis. I continued my errand, reaching my destination: the All-Purpose Data Bank. The A.P.D.B. was where all records were kept. ALL records. I was certain I would find what I was looking for there.
“May I help you?”The administrator asked.
“Yes, I’d like to see the death record on the late Baron Typhonus.”
“Are you the new Baron Typhonus?”
“No.”
“Well then, I’m sorry but I cannot-”
I lifted my fedora just high enough for the administrator to see my eyes.
“On second thought, just once wouldn’t hurt… Ah, here you are.”
I read the data and nearly swore. The late Baron Typhonus, the uncle that the current Baron had boasted about receiving his title from, had died of poisoning. I had no idea that Typhonus would go that far. Melodramatic as it was, I had no idea what he was capable of.

Part 8: Hunted
Until now, today had been a rather boring day for LordBrockTree.
He had been sitting at an outdoor café, enjoying a cup of coffee. He was just about to meet with a very important individual when they attacked. Maelstrom wasn’t like this. Maelstrom wasn’t so…sudden.
One of the four stood in the ruins of the café. Brock had heard about these before; this was a golem. A particularly nasty-looking golem at that. He charged at the golem and was about to slash with Verminbane when the ground exploded under him, knocking him back and taking out a chunk of his armor. The four attackers surrounded him and the masked one he assumed was their leader(the cape was a hint) spoke.
“LordBrockTree. I don’t think we’ve been formally introduced. I am Dreadnought. And these,” He pointed to each of his teammates in turn, “Are Crossfire, Outburst, and Nemesis. We are The Reapers.”
LordBrockTree recognized that voice. “Golden Boiling Storm!”
“Not quite. I WAS Golden Boiling Storm. Now I am Dreadnought, servant of Typhonus. The master commands your death.”
As one the Reapers leapt towards him. He tried to damage Dreadnought, and was instead hit by Nemesis’ strange chain weapon. He then went for Nemesis, but was knocked down by Outburst’s hammer. Before he could even get up he was bombarded with a mental blast that shattered his consciousness.

Part 8: Hunted

Until now, today had been a rather boring day for LordBrockTree.

He had been sitting at an outdoor café, enjoying a cup of coffee. He was just about to meet with a very important individual when they attacked. Maelstrom wasn’t like this. Maelstrom wasn’t so…sudden.

One of the four stood in the ruins of the café. Brock had heard about these before; this was a golem. A particularly nasty-looking golem at that. He charged at the golem and was about to slash with Verminbane when the ground exploded under him, knocking him back and taking out a chunk of his armor. The four attackers surrounded him and the masked one he assumed was their leader(the cape was a hint) spoke.

“LordBrockTree. I don’t think we’ve been formally introduced. I am Dreadnought. And these,” He pointed to each of his teammates in turn, “Are Crossfire, Outburst, and Nemesis. We are The Reapers.”

LordBrockTree recognized that voice. “Golden Boiling Storm!”

“Not quite. I WAS Golden Boiling Storm. Now I am Dreadnought, servant of Typhonus. The master commands your death.”

As one the Reapers leapt towards him. He tried to damage Dreadnought, and was instead hit by Nemesis’ strange chain weapon. He then went for Nemesis, but was knocked down by Outburst’s hammer. Before he could even get up he was bombarded with a mental blast that shattered his consciousness.
