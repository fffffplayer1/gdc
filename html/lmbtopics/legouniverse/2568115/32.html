Part 22: The Hazards of the Spy Gear

Zatorak knew that sneaking in to listen to the Faction Leaders’ meeting was a bad idea. He remembered from previous visits to Nexus Tower what would happen if he got caught. Now, Zatorak was usually a lot more cautious than this. Usually. However, he had a bad feeling-the maelstrom that was a part of him did not like whatever was in there.

He had to give it to the Spy gear: Somehow the Nexus Force had managed to create armor that was light as a feather, nearly indestructible, AND had a working cloaking device. In addition to hiding him from sight, the armor also completely silenced his footsteps. If there was one piece of armor he would use for sneaking around, it was- well, actually it was his Rishno gear. However, that was just out of sentimentality towards the armor he designed himself as opposed to something the Nexus Force made.

‘Oh, right,’ he reminded himself, ‘you’re a member of the Nexus Force now too. How did Hazel talk me into that one?’

The one thing that perplexed him was the communicator. Zatorak didn’t know how it had gotten in his pocket or what it might do, such as blow up in his hands or track his every movement. Knowing the Nexus Force, it could be either one. However, despite his paranoia Zatorak was somewhat sure who was responsible for it; it was of the same design as the communicators he and Hazel used to use back when they were partners. That was before the only argument Zatorak ever regretted.

He took the communicator back out of his pocket. Yes, it was a TX12 Espionage-grade ‘Wormhole’ two-way communicator. Those aren’t easy to find. Surely she wouldn’t be as sentimental as to…

There it was, in his own handwriting. ZATORAK. She had kept it all these years. The signal he was receiving was the one they had referred to as the Don’t-Make-the-Same-Mistake-I-Made signal. In essence: the signal is sent out to all machines previously linked the sender when the user loses consciousness. Fortunately, the machines were able to distinguish between unconscious and merely asleep.

‘Alright,’ Zatorak thought as he climbed up the ventilation shaft that led to Hazel’s office, ‘It’s showtime.’

Part 23:Rising Threat
I floated about while my grandfather enjoyed having control of my brain. You know, I never actually met my grandfather until now. I never thought that when I did meet him it would be through a hole in the time-stream.
So, when I say ‘I am floating about’, what do I mean? I can’t mean in my body, because my body is currently in use by someone who has been dead for almost a decade. I am startlingly calm about this. It’s very funny that I can say that. That I can just go ahead and say, ‘I’m being very calm considering that I’ve been taken over by a ghost’.
Yes. This whole situation is HILARIOUS.
Amazing: in this form, I can go into reality, except as a variety of ghost, which means that: 1; I can fly, and fly through walls, 2; everyone else cannot see or hear me, and 3; I can read everyone else’s mind. This could be the most fun I’ve had in weeks…
Say, is that Frezija? The girl can’t stay away from my general vicinity, can she? Zatorak is with her as well… and he’s giving her a headache. Some of us, like Zatorak, can’t help being tied to maelstrom. And Frezija cannot stand maelstrom. How does the girl think she can do field work? The main threat of the maelstrom at the moment is the Warnoglee, and those things can smash all but the most hardened veterans, AND they must be either a team of a Sentinel, a Venture-Leaguer, an Assemblyman, and a Paradox agent, or a single member of the Valiance. Frezija’s Valiance, but only barely.
Oh. She’s just decided NOT to steal my Daredevil armor so that she can actually fight. Maybe she isn’t like me, after all. And Zatorak… is thinking that I’ve been in there too long and that something must be wrong. Oh, if only he knew… He’s also found his TX12Wormhole. I wonder if he knows how much trouble I had to go through in order to get that thing…
And suddenly I was gone. I felt myself re-form on the deck of the LIONHEART. Rover took it to respond to a distress signal from LordBrockTree… He appeared to have with him SolidFrozenWarrior, Hide Raxon, the chocolate obsessed Barbossa, LordBrockTree, meaning that the mission was successful, and… PRIMAL! He’s alive!
‘Of course he’s alive, Hazel,” I told myself, “Nothing can smash a monkey.”
Wait, what was that on the horizon? Barbossa was staring at something-

I am not seeing this. It’s a legend. A story. It doesn’t actually…
‘Valiance was a legend too. Did that make IT any less real?’
They could never get away in time with the LIONHEART. I doubt even the MKIII could outrace that thing. Or defeat it. They don’t stand a chance! Even the five of us Faction Leaders couldn’t stop it. It’s an ancient war machine built for nothing but destruction, and destruction it is good at. Who or what has any hope against the ENTIZONE?
“Grandfather!” I screamed at my body, “We need to go!”
