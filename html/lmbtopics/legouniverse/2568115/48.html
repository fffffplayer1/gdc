Part 32: Uncontrollable Wild-Rider

 

Storm broke the silence. “You know, direct evil doesn’t seem to pay anything. I’m thinking of another career that would suit me more.”

“Hopefully one that wouldn’t involve shooting at me?” I asked, three parts sarcasm and one part hopefulness. I asked this while my flareguns were still pointed at him, and his rifle at me.

Storm laughed his evil laugh. It seemed to come naturally to him. “Actually, this new one would allow me to hunt you every waking second of your miserable existence. I’m thinking of giving Bounty Hunting a try. That would be a real challenge.”

I didn’t bother trying to respond directly. “Do you remember when we met, Storm? As I remember it, you tried to smash me.”

He grinned shark teeth. “And it wasn’t the last time, either. I’m going to keep trying, you know.”

“Exactly. But has there been a single time where I have actively tried to smash you? I don’t plan on starting now. So I’m giving you one chance to leave before I start firing.”

“You’d spare me?” he said in mock surprise, “You really are one of them, aren’t you?”

I shook my head, slowly and carefully, holding my flareguns up as a warning. “You’re as wrong as they are, Storm. I’m obviously not like you, but- I’m not a hero, either.”

There were a few things that, even in a time of crisis, I still prided myself on. One of them was making an entrance.

It is possible to land a free-fall without injuring oneself using my Daredevil armor.

The dramatic execution of this fact was a technique I had perfected as an outlaw. I had several practiced formations- the Sky Kick, the Speed Slam, the Falcon Dive, and my favorite, the Impact Crater, named for the shape in the ground it creates when performed perfectly.

I looked below me to aim my landing. Perfect aim was essential. I didn’t want to accidentally demolish any important buildings. Which was when I noticed the obstruction in my path. Rover had steered the Lionheart right below me in an effort to catch me. I rethought my technique options; I couldn’t do an Impact Crater without ripping the Lionheart to shreds.

**********************************************************************************

“Rikerone!”

Rover and LordBrockTree rushed to my side.

“You okay?” Rover asked me.

I smiled. “You’re actually serious, aren’t you? You haven’t even seen a Sky Kick. That was just a Speed Slam.

Brocktree was looking upwards at the falling soon-to-be-wreckage. “And Golden Boiling Storm?”

“I decided to give him a chance to survive, which is obviously more than he would have given me. He has no way off the ship, other than being there when it crashes. If he survives, which I highly suspect he will, I want a rematch. How soon can the other Leaders get here, or vice-versa?”

Brocktree raised an eyebrow. “Pretty quickly, given the two of us calling them. But… why?”

“There’s something that needs to be said and done,” I answered, rather reluctantly, “And certain people need to be there to see it.”

*************************

 

“You’ll accept my resignation?”

 

“Resignation, Hazel?” Duke’s words were tinged with suspicion. “Why?”

 

“Because,” I said, turning to face him, “Of the Vortex of the Past. Because Ryous was wrong.”

 

Silence.

 

“The day Ryous smashed,” I began, “He did not perish. His life-force was contained inside the Imagination Nexus. When you retrieved the Nexus, you unwittingly retrieved Ryous as well.  When I summoned the true Valiance, it sent out a shockwave of Imagination. That reawakened Ryous, and he moved from the Nexus into me as soon as the opportunity presented itself. You were wrong; I wasn’t going back in time during my dreams. Time travel is impossible. The reason I saw them from Ryous’ perspective- and the reason his consciousness took control during the dreams- was that they were Ryous’ memories, being the first things that resurfaced as he slowly regained consciousness. As his consciousness was remerging, bits and pieces of his personality began vying for control- he was trying to remember who he was. As a result, I started acting, thinking, differently, influenced by these resurfacing personality traits. But Ryous was wrong.”

 

This was the tricky part. I had figured that since the fusion was mainly caused by my magic, magic would un-fuse us. The only problem was, I had no idea how. 

Fortunately, I didn’t need to. I felt the now-familiar sensation of the world blurring around me…

I awoke almost instantly, but I found it fitting. This entire business began with my blackouts, so it might as well end with them too. This time, however, two people had woken up again.

 

I only found out later how it had happened. Due to originally being separated by the Vortex of the Past, when the Vortex was broken, Ryous’ molecules rushed across galaxies to re-form themselves. It was exactly the same as if he had been teleported, only with a delayed re-formation. Ryous wasn’t alive again; rather, he had never died in the first place.

 

I had last seen my grandfather when I was five years old. To say my memories of him are faint would be an understatement. Yet he was exactly as I remembered him. Ryous was the image of a Sheriff of old, complete with a brown leather jacket and his signature hat, a wide-brimmed fedora, giving the appearance of a cross between a fedora and the traditional wide hat of the olden sheriff.

 

His first words upon reawakening were “I had a feeling you’d figure it out in the end. I knew the Vortex of the Past was no match for the Sprite of the Future.”

“Ryous!”

“Partially, yes, Duke,” I answered. I then stood to address my grandfather.

I sighed. That was that, then. “I had a feeling you’d say that. You’re wrong, Ryous. You’re not the first, but you’re wrong.”

“Hazel?”

 

“Ryous,” I began, “Everyone- You made me faction leader because you thought that I was a leader. But you’re wrong. I am not a leader. I am a wild-rider. I am untamable. I am uncontrollable. I am not Hazel Sprite, queen of Valia. I am Rikerone.”

 

“It’s my name, you know,” I continued, “It doesn’t mean ‘Chosen One’. It means what I say it means. And I say it means ‘The Uncontrollable Wild-Rider’.”

 

“Is that why you’re resigning?” Ryous spoke in a harsh tone, “To make a point?”

 

“Almost. I’m also resigning because it would be very hard for a faction to run with a missing leader.”

 

“What?”

 

“I can’t stay here any longer,” I continued, “My place is on the wind. That’s the only way I’ll ever be free.” I held my hand up to feel the rushing current already in motion. “West.”

 

I looked around me, at my colleagues; the faction leaders, and my friends, Rover and LordBrockTree. I knew I’d have to say goodbye.

 

“This is it, then,” I said at last, trying not to let any signs of emotion show, “Wish me well. Maybe someday…maybe someday we’ll meet again.”

 

And with that, I was gone. I didn’t have time to think about what I was leaving behind. I couldn’t look back. But I knew the wind and I knew the road. At least I wouldn’t be alone.

I hit the ground running. I needed the freedom of the sky, and to seek adventure somewhere far, far away.

Epilogue:

 

All of those who had seen the events of the ENTIZONE were gathered in Nexus Tower, just as they had been when Hazel had announced the creation of the fifth faction, what seemed like a year ago. They included the Leaders, Rover, LordBrockTree, the members of the Valiance who had been accompanying Rover on the Lionheart, Primal, and Ryous.

 

Vanda was the first to speak. “We were wrong. You were right this time, Duke.”

 

To Duke Exeter’s credit, he did not gloat as others might. Instead, he only sighed. “Just one thing: did anyone see this coming?”

 

“I did.”

 

The room turned toward LordBrockTree.

 

“Rover and I,” he continued, “Are the only ones who knew her before the fifth faction was formed. You never really knew her. If you had, you would have known that the one most important thing about Rikerone is the edge in her eyes. This isn’t anything new. It’s just what she’s been trying to prove to us the whole time. She embodies something, and it isn’t Valiance. Rikerone is the free spirit. The uncontrollable wild-rider.”

 

No one had anything to say to that.

 

“There is still the problem,” Overbuild broke the silence, “Of the leadership of the Valiance.”

 

All eyes turned to Ryous.

 

“Having been in the Nexus,” he said, not getting up from his seat, “I am reluctant to join any organization called the Nexus Force. However, I will take up temporary leadership of the Valiance until a more suitable candidate can be found.”

 

“And Rikerone?” Rover asked.

 

“We’ll meet her again someday,” LordBrockTree said, “I’m sure of that. She’s got the world ahead of her now, and we might just see her in the sky.”

They were all thinking the same thing…

 

 

 Fly with the speed of the winds, Wild-Rider.

Prologue:

 

Deep underground…

 

The laboratory had closed for the night. Two figures, however, remained. One of them was called Carlton. The other one was simply ‘Doctor’.

 

“Doctor!” Carlton stuttered out, “T-t-terrible news!”

 

“Oh?”

 

“They’ve found us- the Nexus Force shut down Lab B!”

 

“That, Carlton,” the Doctor stated- there was no other word for it- “Is what distinguishes Lab A from Lab B.”

 

“But, Doctor, don’t you worry that-”

 

“No, I don’t, Carlton. The fact that the Nexus Force found Lab B is not nearly as surprising as the fact that it took them so long. Lab B were Stromshapers. The amount of maelstrom they used should have drawn the Nexus like a magnet long ago. But not us, Carlton- we are Shadowshapers.”

 

“You mean your project, sir?” Carlton asked.

 

“Yes. I think I’ve found it this time. This one is perfect.”

 

“But sir, you rejected the original test subject-”

 

“With good reason,” the Doctor interrupted, “That one was flawed. This one is not. This is not a strombringer, Carlton- this is a stormbringer.”

Part 1: The Rising Storm

It happened that Hide Raxon did not like guard duty. Either you had to stay in one place for extended periods of time or you were roaming dark halls where anything could lurk. Neither of those situations was preferable to his old job. Was that a mouse or an intruder? A security alarm or lunch break?

 

By now, though, Raxon had resigned himself to the belief that no one was even going to try to break in. Guard duty that had once bored him out of his skull was looking strangely appealing. At worst, he could sit back and play chess with the door. Mind you, the door had never won a single game.

 

He thought, for a second, that he had heard a slight clang. Even if he knew he had heard it, he would have given it no heed. After all, odd noises were common in the PWG. That was almost what the place was for.

 

 He turned back to his chess game. The door was still making its move. He wished the door would just make up its mind. He couldn’t be bothered by strange noises now.

 

Which was, of course, exactly why he never knew what hit him. All he knew was that the impact knocked the door’s queen into his bishop in a formation known as checkmate.

“There’s been a robbery at the PWG.”

 

Ryous Vortex, Leader of the Valiance, raised an eyebrow. He had assigned the guards himself.

 

“What was stolen?” Vanda asked.

 

“That’s the problem,” LordBrockTree replied, “We never finished digging through that place. They could have stolen anything without us knowing.”

 

“Then how do we know?”

 

“We know,” LordBrockTree said, “Because they had to knock out the guard on duty first. He came and warned me.”

 

“That’s the third one this month…” Ryous muttered to himself. He had never wanted this job. He had spent most of his life fighting against having this job. When he’d finally agreed he’d told them it was only temporary. But they never found a replacement. So here he was.

 

“I have an idea.”

 

The room turned toward Rover.

 

“Use a thief to catch a thief,” he continued when he had their attention.

 

“Your opinion is noted,” Duke said, “But it’s been two years and no one’s even seen her. I don’t even know if she’s still alive.”

 

“Oh, she’s alive all right. The alternative would never be fun enough for her.”
