Part 9: Counterattack

Dreadnought smiled as LordBrockTree hit the floor. That was almost too easy.

“Is he still breathing, Crossfire?”

“Barely, sir.”

“HANDS UP!”

Members of the Valiance, all riding aboard a pirate ship with a lion’s head on the front. Dreadnought remembered that ship. It was the LIONHEART. There was a Hero he knew as the old combat trainer for the N.F.I.A., Hide Raxon. SolidFrozenWarrior, the destroyer of his master plan, was there as well, apparently having taken to the Spy kit. There was also a Thinker Dreadnought didn’t recognize, and at the front was the captain of the LIONHEART, CheerfulPowerRover.

“By whose authority? Or, rather, what are you going to do? Take ‘em, Reapers!”

As one he and his team leaped aboard the ship, and began dueling with the Valiance. As he ducked a swing of Rover’s massive Rank 5 cutlass,(Hazel had allowed Rover to keep his Buccaneer kit when he transfered) he saw Outburst flailing his hammer around, trying to hit SolidFrozenWarrior, who had activated the stealth cloak ability of his Spy gear. A glimpse in the other direction revealed Crossfire locking her elbow blades with Raxon’s sword. Nemesis was wrapping the Thinker up in his slicing chain. Strange though it was, Dreadnought couldn’t help thinking that something, or someone, was missing.

Due to circumstances, Dreadnought did not remember, as he was not there, what had happened the day Draconius betrayed his teammates. Draco had been about to smash Rikerone when Agent Prime stopped him. Enraged, Draconius shot Primal into the atmosphere. Although no one knew it, Draco had shot him into space. It had taken months, but he was coming back. A reentry to earth is impossible for an organic creature to survive without sufficient armor, usually the outer hull of a rocket.



Monkeys are surprisingly resilient creatures.



And Agent Prime was mad.

Dreadnought was hit by an enraged monkey from the sky. He barely lived to tell the tale.

Part 10
Zatorak sighed as he sheathed his weapon. He didn’t want to hit her. Hazel Sprite was one of the few people in the universe who didn’t deserve to be hit, at least from Zatorak’s perspective. When they realized there could be no more negotiations they charged at each other, but before either of them could land a blow she just fainted. Zatorak knew that Hazel wasn’t the type to faint like that. He lay her on one of the former barracks. As he did, he heard her talking in her sleep. At least, he assumed it was her. It sounded like the voice of an old man. He heard fragments...
“You mean we’re journeying…doesn’t work…some answers…you know how to…record of the late…Typhonus!”
Zatorak felt something cold and hard at his back. It felt like the barrel of a gun. “Who are you?”
“Name’s Draco Typhonus. I would like you to step away from the girl. Can’t risk you blocking my shot.”
“You’re going to smash her?”
Draco grinned maniacally. “That’s the plan. Not going to move? Then I’ll smash you first.”
He fired, having absolutely no effect on Zatorak.
“What are you?????”
Zatorak turned to face his attacker and lifted him by the neck, choking him. He whispered in his ear,
“That’s what you get when you mess with the Maelstromifig.”
“Don’t.”
He turned. Hazel had woken up. “Don’t smash him,” she clarified. Zatorak was about to say something about Draco trying to smash her when he noticed her expression. It said, ‘He’s tried to smash me before but I’m trying to be civil and it’s not easy and I would appreciate it if you would listen to me before I lose my temper’. Zatorak shrugged, and threw Draconius into the wall, knocking him out.
“You just had to do that, didn’t you?”
“What?”
“Male lead wants to kill villain but female lead refuses? This is too much like a story.”
She laughed, the first time he had seen her do so since she left her parents. “Yeah,” she said, “I guess so. But who says YOU’RE the hero?”
They both laughed now. “So,” Zatorak said, “Does this faction have cool capes?”
“Yup. Gold. Does this mean…”
He looked at her, and she took a gold badge out of her pocket, tossing it to him. When he put it on, Zatorak, for the first time, got a sense of purpose. ‘This is what I’m meant to be’, he thought, ‘this is what I’m meant to do.’
“We’re quite a bit away from the Valiance vendor, so I guess this is on the house… Hero, Scout, Thinker or Spy?”
Zatorak considered the gear in front of him. The Hero gear was powerful, just like the Sentinels… The Scout was less powerful, but had abilities suitable for traveling long distances, good for if he needed to make a break for it… ‘No.’ he decided, ‘I’m done running.’ The Thinker was the least powerful, but could make powerful leviathans… Then there was the Spy. It was connected to the maelstrom, like he was… but did he really want to get even closer to it? It was most streamlined, the most agile of the kits… good for evasion while staying in the fight. He knew his choice.
“I choose the Spy.”
Hazel handed him a Rank 3 Spy kit, which according to her was the equivalent of the Rank 4 of a kit from any other faction. The armor felt stronger than anything Zatorak had used before. It wasn’t black, like the Space Marauder, it was actually a very dark blue that blends into night better than black does. And the cape… he loved the cape. It wrapped around him like a cloak, responding to his thoughts alone. It was gold when he wanted it to be, blending into the environment when he wanted it too as well. He tried activating it’s ability, and found that it made him completely invisible. The helmet ability allowed him to see through walls and extended his hearing, while the twin dark daggers… they could hurt if thrust in the right place. He was ready.

---Rikerone the Wild-Rider---
