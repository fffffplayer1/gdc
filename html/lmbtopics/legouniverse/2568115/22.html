Part 12
“They’re gone,” LordBrockTree said, having recently been revived.

“We’d better get moving; they might come back. Rover, can you turn this thing around?”

“Can do, Barbossa.”

Barbossa opened his mouth to speak, but before he could say anything LordBrockTree said, “Before you ask: no, Barbossa, I don’t have any chocolate.”
Rover relaxed next to LordBrockTree, using the prow of the LIONHEART as a footrest. “Ahhhh…” he said, “Just like old times, right, Broccoli? You, me, Solid, the LIONHEART…”
“As I recall, you didn’t get the LIONHEART until those times were nearly over. And what have I said about calling me Broccoli?”
“Did you say we liked it?”
Brock faced Rover and looked at him with a disapproving glance. “You’ve been getting more and more cocky without Rikerone yelling at you all the time,” he said while gazing out to starboard. There was something there…
“ROVER! TURN THIS THING AROUND IMMEDIATELY!”
Rover didn’t even ask what it was. If Broccoli raised his voice like that, it was a good idea to do what he said.
************************************************************************************
Barbossa was in the middle of a book about the History of Chocolate when the ship turned. It flew out of his hands and was caught by a bird. Barbossa was mad about that. He had just been about to find out what the most delicious kind of chocolate bar was when that happened. So far he had gone through 983,854,721 books about chocolate, and yet something always happened right before he read the part where they tell you what the most delicious kind of chocolate bar is. To find the most delicious kind of chocolate bar… that was his lifelong goal. He believed he would fulfill his purpose of existence when he found it.
There was a speck in the distance. Barbossa grabbed a telescope and was quite surprised by what he saw. He saw a fearsome mecha, painted silver and black and easily the size of Nexus Tower. This was why LordBrockTree had ordered them to turn around. And it was gaining on them.
