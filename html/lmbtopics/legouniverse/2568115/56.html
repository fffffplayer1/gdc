I've got something even better: I finished what I was working on- a preview for a potential prequel!

 

As far as its neighbors were aware, 2181996 Thrill Drive was unoccupied. This often left them wondering why it was so well maintained.

 

 2181996 Thrill Drive was, as the neighbors had correctly assumed, owned by a real estate agency. The agency, however, rented it to a single tenant. This tenant would enter at night, usually through the windows. They had given her a key, of course, but she preferred entering with stealth.

 

Among the tenants the owners had hosted over the years, she was certainly the most unusual. They had been initially taken aback to meet a thirteen-year-old girl living on her own; especially one who wanted to rent a house and wouldn’t tell them her name. She’d even offered to pay double their intended rent. After hurried consideration, they had agreed.

 

Now, two years later, they still did not know her name, but they enjoyed her monthly visits to the agency. Other than her lack of any apparent means of supporting herself yet seemingly infinite cash, she was, they thought, growing up to be a fine young woman; beautiful, witty, vivacious, and extremely clever.

 

Did they ever wonder about her? About how she never told them anything about herself, even when she played Chess with them on weekends? About how she demanded silence, but paid the thrice the rent without flinching? Of course they did. But it was not a concern to them. They had grown fond of their secretive tenant, and truly would never care who she was. After all, they reasoned, if everyone who had piles of money and didn’t want their picture taken was a criminal, society was in trouble.

“Sergeant? Sir?”

 

Sergeant Draconius of the NFPD bolted awake, his hand reaching for the holster of his regulation-pistol. He was a relatively young sergeant, but his former youthful exuberance had faded from the serious nature of his rank.

His hand relaxed when he saw that the speaker was one of the four constables in his district, Constable Wing. Wing’s worried head had poked through his door.

 

“I was just resting my eyes,” he said to no one in particular.

 

“Sir,” Wing said, “The news from Corporal Forester has just come in, and it’s not good, sir. The Royal Inc. Bank has been completely cleaned out, sir.”

 

“Witnesses?”

 

“It was empty at the time, sir. Night shift. The teller on-site was closing it up when he heard the skylight break. Our mystery thief left this, sir.”

 

Draconius fiddled with the quarter dollar Wing tossed to him, his fingers running over the valleys carved into its formerly smooth surface. They formed two letters.

 

“HS again?”      

                                                                          

Wing coughed with worry. “It appears so, sir. This makes her total twenty-three, now.”

HS. Draconius had only known those letters to have a meaning other than the usual for a few months, but even that had been enough. They had never caught her, though Draconius had tried nine times so far. Half of him wished HS would leave for someone else’s precinct where he didn’t have to deal with her. The other half wanted to catch her himself, if only to recover his pride.

 

Among everything else she was, there was one thing Draco had to admire about her. HS was, and always would be, a thief, but she was a thief with honor. She stole only from the rich- and banks, of course- and more impressive: as far as the NFPD was aware, she had never, in her entire career, taken a life. That didn’t mean she wouldn’t fight- she’d defend herself if officers were on her tail, viciously, as Draco had discovered himself. But she shot to disable, never to smash. He had to respect that.

 

In a way, it would have been easier for Draconius if she had been a murderer. If she had been maniacal, he could have just taken her town and hauled her off to an asylum. He had dealt with murderers and madmen. They were easy. HS was something else.

 

She was perfectly sane. What kind of sane woman chooses a life as an outlaw, Draconius didn’t know, but he had a hunch.

It had come to him after the eighth time. He had almost caught up to her, confident in having cornered her on a roof. There were helicopters in the air that would keep her where she was. There were extra officers on the ground in case of anything unpredictable. It had seemed foolproof.

 

She had seen that she was trapped. But she hadn’t surrendered herself. Instead, she had tossed a coin to Draconius; with the same HS design as the one he held now- blew a kiss to him, taken a running start… and leaped.

 

She had grabbed onto the landing gear of one of the helicopters and used it to swing herself onto an adjacent roof before anyone knew what was happening. Draco had seen her wave to him as she disappeared in the maze of alleys below.

 

The next morning he’d searched the databases for teenagers, last name S.

 

He hadn’t found anything; the only teenage girls with the initials HS had been Harriett Stanton and Hazel Sprite. Both lived in Valia. So that was a dead end. His lack of results did nothing to deter him, however; if Draco had any one quality to be defined by, it was his relentlessness.

 

Draconius would never rest until he had found the Queen of Thieves and brought her to justice once and for all.
