Part 5
The Commander looked up from his paperwork to see a five-year-old boy riding a motorbike. He was not surprised. HappyDoodle42 knew to come back to him if Riker ever left him alone. The Commander was the one who informed Riker of her parents’ deaths, and he had helped the two of them, occasionally giving them gear and supplies when they needed them. He was the only person Rikerone trusted to take care of Happy in her place.
“Happy! It’s good to see you!”
“Riker went to race.”
There was only one race Happy could mean. She had entered in The Ultimate Race. The Commander knew he had to get her out of there. Riker didn’t know that the maelstrom were running it, and if she had one weakness it was that she froze up whenever faced with a spiderling, because of the way her parents died. Also, he had just sent Agent Draconius to investigate the race, and if she recognized him it would blow his cover.
Get me Agent Storm,” He said into the intercom. “I’ve got a mission for him.”
The Commander sat back, sipped a cup of coffee, gave HappyDoodle42 a chocolate bar, and waited for the Space Marauder to arrive.

Part 6
King led Rikerone and Cheerful Power Rover to a food court. “See,” Rover said, “I wasn’t lion. King is the best guide there is.”
This did not improve Rikerone’s already sour mood. “If you make one more pun like that, Rover…”
“Say, how about some food?”
Rikerone brightened up. “Sure,” she whispered, “If you can distract the guy then I can sneak over and…”
“I have a better idea. Let’s BUY some food. I’ll pay.”
Five minutes later he returned with two burgers and fries. As they were eating, he said, “There now, doesn’t that feel better than stealing the food?”
“Can’t say I think your wallet agrees with you.”
Rover sighed. “Is that what it’s about? Or is asking for a couple of cheeseburgers the same as asking for help?”
Rikerone gave him a glare and turned away. Rover looked at her and realized his guess was dead right. She’d been looking after herself without any help, and didn’t quite know how to react to receiving it. She’d been stealing for so long that she didn’t see that there was another way. Rover was contemplating this when he saw the Marauder. In the corner of the room stood a being completely clad in armor so black it seemed to suck up all light, with both hands converted into long-barreled cannons, colossal missile launchers build into his shoulderpads, and a helmet that invoked terror. A name tag on his chest said Golden Boiling Storm. Rover was looking at a Rank 4 Space Marauder.
He turned to warn her of this, but when he turned back Golden Boiling Storm was gone. Rikerone was looking at him like he was crazy when a spider landed in front of her. She let out a sharp scream and fell over in her seat, her face white with terror. Rover, meanwhile, casually picked up the spider and examined it.
“A Blue-Striped Drop Spider! They’re quite rare in this area. Completely harmless, unless you mind them falling on you. And look, this is almost twice as big as they usually get!” He turned to Riker. “Rikerone?”

“T-t-t-that’s a s-s-s-spider! I H-h-h-hate s-s-spiders!”
“I don’t see why, they’re very interesting creatures. Why...
His rambling was interrupted by Rikerone shooting the spider with her flareguns, almost taking his hand with it. “What did you do that for!?” He yelled.
Rikerone had regained her composure and her expression now could be chiseled. “You don’t understand,” She said, her voice a glacier, “Those things killed my parents.”
“Well unless it was a Black Widow I don’t see…”
“Maelstrom corrupted ones. Spiderlings. What were you going to say to me before it fell?”
“Oh, nothing really,” Rover said, his eyes fixed on the spot where the Space Marauder had been.

Part 7: The Marauder’s Hunt

Golden Boiling Storm was a patient minifig. His position in the N.F.I.A. was simple. Sometimes agents went rogue. When that happened, who could stop them? They’d been given so much training and equipment… when these happened Golden Boiling Storm was called for. He was their Extraction and Elimination expert. His missions were to defeat the rogue agent, rescue the captured agent, and destroy the foe that could not be smashed by ordinary means. He also disposed of double agents working for the other side. Storm was the perfect person for the job, as he never had any flickering of doubt, never hesitated. He knew his duty, and it was up to the other agents to make sure he had no need to do it. At the moment he was shadowing the target his Commander wanted him to apprehend. He had been very specific about her not being harmed. The worst Storm could do was knock her out. He noticed a connected minifig on his radar. Agent Draconius, the boss’s son. He thought he was so honorable just because he was a Sentinel. Storm didn’t form friendships or grudges by principle, but still he would like nothing more than to show that shiny Ranger exactly what honor was. He noticed on his radar that the target had left her companion. He smiled, although he felt no pleasure. The hunt was on.

Draconius was disappointed. He had successfully sneaked around the P. of W.G. and found absolutely nothing of interest. Draconius saw that Storm was staring as only Storm could stare at a table to his right… Not her! Why here? Why now? Draconius and Rikerone had a special relationship: he had tried to arrest her forty-nine times to date. They had an ongoing game of Cat and Mouse. Draconius was just trying to keep her from committing crimes, but Rikerone saw at as the most fun sport you could possibly think up. Draco decided to take his focus away from Riker for now. Maelstrom, he thought to himself, the maelstrom was the key here. He could try for round fifty at another time. As for the maelstrom, he had been sneaking around the Race for hours, and hadn’t seen a single trace of it. No Stromlings, no Mechs, not even any corrupted bricks lying around. It occurred to Draco that he should have asked how the N.F.I.A. got the footage.
“Ook Eek Eek!”
Draco to this to mean, “I am done eating. We should go investigate more and stay out of the Space Marauder’s way.”

Draco was good at sneaking around. In fact, he had had his Space Ranger gear painted dark grey, which was almost as good as black at camouflage and, unlike black, it didn’t make people think you were up to something. Of course, anyone who knew Draco could tell you that he was ALWAYS up to something. It was his job. He stopped to look at the announcement on the screens. The first qualification race was starting, and no one he knew was in it. He turned on, but saw something else. A door was right next to the largest screen. When he turned to face it, it was gone. A clever trick, but a useless one against Draconius: the door could only be seen from a certain angle. Primal, sitting on his shoulder, nodded, and together they opened the door.
