The Ultimate Race


The ultimate race: how could I resist?
That’s what I thought when I saw the poster advertising a racing tournament titled “The Ultimate Race.”My name is Rikerone. It’s not a very good name; I got it used. At least, it’s what people say when they want to get my attention. It translates into Public Enemy Number One. I’m a Daredevil. Rank 5. What’s that you say? There is no Rank 5? There is now. There were two problems with me entering the tournament: the first was that I’d be under arrest as soon as I walked in the door. However, people who know me (and those who just know my name and get the general idea) know that I NEVER walk if I can make an entrance. And for some reason crashing a motorcycle through a skylight generally makes an entrance. The other problem was my partner. HappyDoodle42 would likely object to another window entrance. Happy is optimistic, though. True, he’s just about useless with a gun, but give the boy even just a basic shortsword and suddenly minifigs are falling. So that was my plan for entering “The Ultimate Race.” It’s a pity that I hadn’t thought about what would happen afterwards. How could I have known?

Part 1

“I wanna hear some music, Riker!”
I sighed. “HappyDoodle42, I do not know how many times I have to tell you this, but my bike does NOT have a stereo. Besides, if it did, it wouldn’t have any music you’d like. Now, are we going to do this?”
“No! No! Nooooooooooooooooooooooo!!!!!”
Five exclamation marks. He was getting stubborn. It was time to use persuasion. “Tell you what, Happy,” I said, just barely keeping my patience, “If you come, I’ll steal you a chocolate bar.”
This did not help. “Stealing is wrong,” HappyDoodle42 persisted. I had been trying to work on that for some time. The main problem with having a 5-year-old as a partner other than the annoyance was that he had a ridiculous sense of righteousness. He even objected to my entering private places uninvited. In other words, he would like nothing more than to ruin my reputation. I couldn’t do anything about him, though. He’s my little brother and I’m the only family he’s got. “Happy, stealing is only bad if it is done by other people.”
“Mama says stealing is very bad!”
That was the other problem. He wouldn’t accept the fact that our parents had been dead for two years now. It didn’t really matter to me. I had run away from them when I was 12, and only learned of my brother’s existence two years ago. They were killed by an ambush of the Maelstrom. They had been some of the specialized guards of the Queen of our region. A strike force of Spiderlings attacked them in the mountains. No survivors. Apparently the Queen had been killed by the Darkitect himself. There were rumors that she hadn’t been killed and was his prisoner.
We had almost reached our destination. I switched the bike to its highest gear… and leaped.

Part 2: Enter Draconius

“Do you know what you are looking at, Agent?”
“It looks like a group of robots are building something,” Agent Draconius of the N.F.I.A. (Nexus Force Intelligence Agency) responded.
“It’s the preparations for the racing tournament referred to as “The Ultimate Race.” Now, look near the lower-left corner of the screen. What does that look like to you?”
Draco realized with a jolt what it was. “That’s a Stromling! Then those robots…”
“Are modified Corrupted Mechs, yes. Your mission is to infiltrate the race and find out why there are Stromlings running it. We can give you one partner. Agent Crossfire? Agent Hardplace?”
“Agent Prime. I’m going to need his skills.”
“Agent Prime? Surely not Agent Prime!”
Draconius gave the Commander a hard stare. The Commander suddenly agreed with his choice of Agent Prime. Draconius liked his job. The N.F.I.A. had the common sense to give him the interesting assignments. He also liked Agent Prime, despite him having (temporarily) smashed two other agents when he didn’t like the food.
“Oh, and one other thing, Agent.”
Draconius turned.
“The tournament is going to be a magnet for the toughest mercenaries around. Watch yourself.”
Draco nodded, and left to find Agent Prime.

Part 3: The King and His Human
Rikerone hit the skylight feet first and flipped onto the ground. She was about to raise her flareguns (with custom fireball setting) and demand to be qualified for the first race when a voice behind her said,
“Is that a Daredevil Kit? You’re Venture League too? Did you know that kit can survive a temperature of over 1,000,000 degrees?”
Riker turned and saw a Buccaneer, one hand petting a Lion Pet and the other resting on a Broadsider. If there was one thing Riker didn’t like in her faction, it was having to deal with pirates. “Who are you!?” she snapped, aiming one of her flareguns at him.
“Oh, come on! You wouldn’t dare shoot me! Name’s Cheerful Power Rover, call me Rover.”
Riker was not amused. Not only did he not react when she aimed at him, he had made fun of her! She was not one to appreciate “dare” puns. Especially since it wasn‘t much of a pun. “Tell me why I shouldn’t shoot you!”
“Because you look like you need a guide, that’s why! And nobody guides better than King here, right boy?
Riker gave up. “Fine, you can tag along. I’m Rikerone. Riker for short.”
And with that, King bounded off, with Cheerful Power Rover and Rikerone chasing after him.

Part 4: Monkey Buisness

“Good morning, Agent Prime.”
The monkey stopped slamming a pathetic minifig who had dared to try to feed it bananas into the pile of said bananas and turned toward his best friend. “Ook Ook Eeek Eeek!”
Draconius shook his head. “We’ve got a new mission,” He said, “And put the poor man down, okay? He didn’t know you only like oranges.”
As they were leaving the cafeteria, Draco reflected upon the situation. There wasn’t much he knew about this mission. So they had seen Stromlings preparing “The Ultimate Race.” What did that mean? Technically all it meant was that Stromlings were taking an interest in construction. But Draco had been in the intelligence business long enough to know to NEVER assume that a Stromling is doing something just because it likes to. What could the Baron gain by holding a racing tournament? Draco’s thoughts were interrupted by several ‘Ooks’ from Primal.
“I see them, Primal.” Dark Ronin, millions of them, were gathering on the hill outside the building. Draco and Primal were the only agents outside. Draco knew that even a Faction Leader couldn’t smash all of them alone without being overwhelmed. However, they weren’t heading for the Hexagon. Instead, they were attempting to enter the building near it, the L.O.H.S.E.T.S.N.B.T.B.A.B.T.P.S. (Location Of Highly Sensitive Equipment That Should Not Be Touched By Anyone But The Paradox Scientists. The N.F.I.A. just thought of it as the Place of Wise Guys) They were unable to get near it, however, as waves of maelstrom energy kept radiating from the building and disintegrating Ronin. From Draco’s perspective, it just kept getting weirder and weirder. First the Maelstrom held a racing tournament, then they tried to enter the P. of W.G., then the P. of W. G. started using a never-before-seen superweapon against them. Oh, Draco had seen Maelstrom weaponry before; he had just never seen it on that scale. Normally Sentinels did not like sneaking around, which was what made Draconius, a Rank 3 Space Ranger, so useful to the N.F.I.A. “The Ultimate Race” could wait. Something was going on in the P. of W.G., and Draco was going to find out what.
