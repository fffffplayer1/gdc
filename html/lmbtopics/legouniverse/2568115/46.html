Part 30: The Core of the ENTIZONE

Two down, two to go. If I had heard Outburst right, he was the third of the Reapers. Now, if fate really did have it that I was fighting them in the reverse order they identified themselves by, there were only the second and first left. It seemed unlikely, but in reality…well, that’s the exact kind of thing that’s likely to happen to me. Fate and I have a special connection. I mean, taking even a quick look at my past experiences will tell anyone that much. So the question is: why me? Was it just another part of this ‘chosen one’ thing?

I’d have to think it over later. Right now, my goal was to find the bridge and stop this thing, even if I had to tear the entire ship apart.

Brugal was very irritated. Logic had told him that the intruder wouldn’t get far, but experience told him something else. Specifically, experience told him that one intruder, one valiant hero, always, ALWAYS, came out victorious.

“Dreadnought,” he growled, “Two of your squad have fallen to this warrior already. Yet, do we have any visual of the intruder?” 

Dreadnought let out a low grunt. “If it actually matters, yes, we do. We should be getting something from the security cameras any minute now. After all, it’s not as if I spent all of the last two days getting them installed.

Brugal turned to the monitor, keenly aware of Dreadnought’s sarcasm. He had always had to deal with constant insubordination- why would it stop when he had the Reapers under his command?

The cameras showed a female minifigure in green and gray armor. Brugal didn’t recognize her, but Dreadnought tensed  immediately.

“This one’s mine.”

Brugal was very surprised by this, that Dreadnought would have a personal grudge against the intruder. “You’re forgetting,” he said, “Crossfire is still out there. She’ll probably deal with this warrior long before you have a chance.”

“No,” Dreadnought shook his head in startling certainty, “If that’s who I think it is…she won’t.”

I had quite suddenly stumbled upon what would probably be my best opportunity in this ship: the engine room. A quick wrench in the works here would disable the entire ship. You know, I would have thought they’d make legendary superweapons harder to break. It’s as though this thing will tip over if I tap it.

Of course, I couldn’t help noticing the other being who was already in the engine room. Crossfire floated as she had done previously when she had stolen Valiance from me during the Ultimate Race tournament, except now she was covered in armor that mimicked the armor I had seen on Nemesis and Outburst.

“So, how’re you doing, Crossfire?” I asked in my most amiable voice possible,(which was actually very hard to do) “I thought I obliterated your mind when you tried to read mine.”

Crossfire’s eyes flashed with hatred at the recollection of that incident. She spoke with a voice that seethed with cold anger. “I’ll confess, it was…difficult. Your will was far more powerful than I had calculated. This time, however, I am prepared. As second of the Reapers, I challenge you…”

I had been preparing to rush at Crossfire as she spoke that last sentence, but was stopped in mid leap and, to my intense dismay, telekinetically thrown against a wall. I was still recovering from the force of the blow when Crossfire activated her powers again, lifting me into the air. I began talking in the hope that it would distract her.

“So, Crossfire, I’ve been wondering about something. Why is this ship so… flimsy?”

It took about five-and-a-half seconds before she registered the significance of what I had just said.

“You didn’t! You couldn’t have destroyed the-” she screamed.

 That distraction was what I needed to make my move.  The punch I threw actually had a lot more power than I thought it would, and it sent her flying. Right into the main engine. The ENTIZONE really was fragile, if Crossfire crashing into it was all it took to destroy the most vital piece of equipment in the entire ship. Even more surprising was the fact that Crossfire went and got up again. I go through all of the trouble of launching a telepath into the core of a legendary superweapon, and she doesn’t stay down!

Of course, the next thing that happened was me being slammed through the wall. But in my defense, this was a moment where one combatant rushed at the other while the other was rushing towards the first- resulting in both being slammed in one direction. It was something that is supposed to only happen in movies, however, I did not have any time to reflect on that point. The main reason for that was that we crashed all the way out of the front of the ship.

I twisted around in freefall to look back at the ENTIZONE, and realized something: the section of the ship we had come out of, and therefore the engine room, was where the heart would be if the ENTIZONE was a minifigure. That meant that, most likely, the bridge would be located at the ‘brain’.

Crossfire had made one critical mistake, other than causing even more damage to an already half-destroyed superweapon, in launching both of us out of here. She thought she could just hover away and I’d be smashed upon landing. There was one thing, however, which she had not considered.

Two months ago, during the Ultimate Race Incident- which of course was a ridiculous name; I had asked to have it changed some time before- I had learned that I can use my magical abilities to form solid objects, although those objects usually didn’t last very long. The process required a  level of concentration I do not normally find and can only be done during periods of stress, such as freefalling while in battle with a giant mecha and a telepath. The interesting thing about the process is that there must be an existing blueprint, so to speak, of the object I am trying to create, which is why I ended up re-summoning Valiance instead of creating any other sword that could possibly exist. Again, fate playing with me.

I decided not to be overly specific, and focused my power towards building or summoning a method of flight. Even so, what I got surprised me. The sheer improbability of the object  in question being chosen as a method of flight was beyond belief. If there was any evidence of actual intervention at the dice, this was it.

It was my bike. My favorite bike. The most amazing vehicle in the known universe, if I do say so myself. It goes from zero to sixty in point-three seconds, has a top speed of over three hundred miles per hour, handles like a dream,  has dual bladed cannons, does not need to follow any speed limits, and can ‘drive’ on thin air. Needless to say, it is the motorcycle to end all motorcycles.

Valiance.             

I flew up to the ‘brain’ and entered through the viewport at the front, which was wide open for some reason. The moment I touched the floor of the ship,  Valiance disappeared. I might have needed it…

 

Inside the bridge were two figures. One of them was a hulking, monstrous beast, that I realized with a jolt was a Warnoglee  standing on its hind legs. The other one…I’d be lying if I said I didn’t recognize the other one.

“You know,” I said, putting on a casual voice, “When I saw that Crossfire was the second of the Reapers, somehow I knew who the first was.”

My greatest enemy, the only being I could call my nemesis, Golden Boiling Storm, smiled.  “I made a wager with Brugal here. He was willing to bet on his ENTIZONE being so powerful that you would never make it here. It’s a pity he doesn’t know you like we do, Rikeroné.”

“What are you talking about, Dreadnought?” the creature referred to as Brugal growled, “Destroy her already!”

 

“So,” I said, putting my hands on the holsters of my flareguns, “Hello, climax.”

 

“So who’s that talking? The Outlaw or the Sheriff?”

I faltered, and Storm used that chance. He pointed the shadowy rifle  he was holding at me- and I knew I’d never be able to evade his shot. Which is why what he did surprised me even more than it otherwise would have. He aimed…

But not at me. He turned back to me, and I was once again confronted with the cold, expressionless stare of his helmet.

“I thought he’d never shut up,” He said. It was classic Storm, through and through.

I realized what he meant. I had enough experience with him. “You were always going to betray Brugal, weren’t you? You just waited for me to get here. Why?”

“I’m surprised you can’t answer your own question…Sheriff.”  He smirked at my apparent ignorance, then continued, “I bet your new friends tried to explain things to you. Here’s one thing I’m willing to bet that they didn’t explain:  There’s a phenomenon that can occur due to prolonged exposure to magic. If two beings become magically linked, it’s possible for them to merge into one being, with the traits of both. The process is known to be linked to time travel, which is how it gets its name.”

I couldn’t see his face under his helmet, but I knew his mouth had formed a hideous fusion of a cocky smile and a sneer. “They call it the Vortex of the Past.”
