Part 17: Riker’s Darkest Secret

Tom Sprite had been a simple man. He had led an easy life with his wife, Rachel, and his daughter, Hazel. No one would have ever guessed that he was the King of Valia, a kingdom with close ties to the Nexus Force. All valiant weapons, including Valiance itself, came from their forges. The Sprites had all been Summoners, and magic ran so deep inside them that they used it without realizing it. The weather responded to their emotions, becoming clear when they were cheerful, raining when depressed, and thunderstorming in times of rage.
One day he came home to find Hazel gone. He found the crown that was his badge of office but he never wore on her bed. He died without ever knowing why she had run away. Five years later, he and his wife were traveling to Nexus Tower to discuss the proper use of Valiance when the Spiderlings attacked. They fought alongside the Royal Guard, but there were too many of them. Tom dueled with Typhonus himself, ending in Tom’s death. The Darkitect then did the same to Rachel. However, his goal had been to corrupt Valiance, not knowing that the ultimate weapon had been given by the Faction Leaders to their advisor, LordBrockTree.

Crossfire’s telepathy was overloaded by one being having three minds, three sets of memories. It was staggering. They’re her ghosts, she realized, Hazel never got over never saying goodbye to them. She took care of HappyDoodle42 for her own atonement as well as his health. Further memories squeezed through, the strain bringing Crossfire to her knees.

Holding a Summoner Staff and not connecting to it. Being pushed around by two Buccaneers and having an intense dislike of them ever since. Finding the crown and discovering that her life had already been chosen for her. Stealing first to survive, then simply for sport. Hearing the news that her parents had been killed. Feeling immense sorrow yet never forgiving them for trying to choose her destiny. Being told she had a brother. Forty-nine attempted arrests by Draconius, each one more inventive than the one before it. Bringing her Daredevil armor to Rank 5 by use of the magic within her. Finding “The Ultimate Race.” Meeting Rover and LordBrockTree. Losing it at Rover for judging her without knowing her. Being hunted by Golden Boiling Storm. Witnessing LordBrockTree’s death. Touching Valiance with a righteous cause in her mind. Her darkest secret: knowing that she was meant to rule her people and doing nothing about it.

Crossfire couldn’t take any more. She was nearly emotionless, making her an effective agent. Reading the mind of one with so much emotion… she realized before she blacked out that Valiance would have been useless to her.

---Rikerone the Wild-Rider---

Part 16
The Rank 3 Knight oversaw two workers moving a barrier to the entrance of the experimental part of “The Ultimate Race”.
“Move it little to the left!” he said. He suddenly heard engines. Loud engines. He turned just in time to see a girl riding a green skull bike heading towards him. That same girl crashed the bike through the barrier. Actually, she didn’t even seem to notice it was there. He yelled after her,
“You rabble-rousing young punk! I’ll straighten you out yet!”
“Many have tried that. I’m one of them.”
“Who are you?” the knight asked.
“Draconius. Duncan Draconius.”
“I’m ChampionVictoryPixel.”
“Bronson Jarl’s son?”
“Pre-pare ta get smashed!!!!!”
Draconius left, as people often do when hit on the head with a Power Jouster. Five exclamation marks. Whoa.
Rikerone was in the process of enjoying her new bike when she lost it. To be specific, she had just been yanked off of it by an invisible force. A female Inventor was levitating her bike above her, and looked at Riker haughtily.
Hey, I know you,” Riker said, “You’re Raven, right?”
“I am called that,” the mysterious girl responded, “But to most I am known as Crossfire.”
“What did you do?”
“You mean this?” Crossfire tapped her helmet. “Rank 4. Telekinesis. Enjoying using the ultimate weapon, were you?”
“The WHAT?”
“Valiance. Don’t tell me you didn’t know what this is!” She thrust back her head and laughed into the sky. Riker was angry about Crossfire having taken her bike, frustrated because she couldn’t get it back, and enraged from Crossfire laughing at her. And as Rover will tell you, NEVER make the Queen of Thieves that mad. Rikerone had a closely guarded secret, one that even Happy didn’t know about. Crossfire was too busy laughing to notice the clouds gathering behind her, just as they had when Golden Boiling Storm walked onto the racetrack. She had been afraid then. These, however, were storm clouds. Rikerone thrust her head to the sky. It was time to make it rain.

Part 18: The Edge in Her Eyes
In third round of qualifications for The Ultimate Race participants use vehicles they have brought with them instead of stock cars. CheerfulPowerRover sat uneasily in the seat of his Hinterlander, which, like all of the cars to be used in this race, had been modified for flight instead of ground racing. Riker should have been here by now. It wasn’t like her to be late for something like this. They were racing the Paradox Power Punishers again. Rover had seen LordBrockTree get smashed, and he was ready to launch Storm into the stratosphere. One of Storm’s Sorcerers drove up to him. He recognized him.
“Bro! How ya been?”
“Good,” the Sorcerer, Rover’s brother SolidFrozenWarrior, said.
“We’re gonna be racing against each other?”
“Guess so.”
The microphone blared the beginning of the race:
“Welcome, ladies and gentlemen, to the third round of The Ultimate Race! In this race, the team whose racer is in first wins, regardless of the positions of the other racers! To prevent any more misfortunes like in the last race, a specially chosen referee will be hovering over the track at all times. And with that, here’s ten time Nexus Cup champion, CHAMPIONVICTORYPIXEL!!!!!”
ChampionVictoryPixel. Champion was in racing what LordBrockTree was in combat: a Living Legend. (With two capital “L”s) Rover was irritated that he couldn’t do anything to Storm while Champion was watching. He tried hovering in the air a bit to test the new controls, and found it surprisingly easy.
Rikerone walked up next to him. “Where’s Valiance?” He asked her.
“I lost it. How did you-”
“It was carved onto the side of the bike. I asked your pal Draco about it.”
“I was about to say, ‘How did you get a Hinterlander to fly?’ ”
“That, Riker, is called “regulations.” It’s required for this race. So, how are you going to race without the MKII?”
“Like this.”
She activated her shoulderpad ability, which at Rank 5 functions as a jetpack. Rover noticed something different about her. She stood straighter than she did before, and had a new edge in her eyes. She looked as though she had just come out of an apocalypse as the only one standing. Rover didn’t know what had happened to her. He just knew that she wouldn’t be scared of spiders anymore.

Part 19: The Rock and the Shadow
The Commander sat at his desk, as he had done so many times before. There were two agents in front of the desk. One was essentially a pile of rocks held together in a vaguely human shape by ancient energy, the other one clad in black from head to toe, twirling a plasma dagger.
“So, let me see if I’ve got this straight: You want me to work with a rock?” the black warrior asked.
“I PREFER ‘GOLEM’.”
“Yes, Agent Darkslice. That is exactly what I want. Agent Hardplace?”
“YES?”
“Show the common courtesy of introducing yourself to our new member.”
“I’M ROCKOR HARDPLACE. I’M A GOLEM.”
“Zipja. A minifig.” He turned to the Commander. “I didn’t exactly agree on ‘Agent Darkslice’, you know.”
“What would you have preferred? ‘Agent Ninja’?”
“Darkslice is fine then. But I don’t take orders from you, Suul.”
“You dare…”
“I work directly for the boss. You know how it works. Each one of the five Commanders gets to command one group of agents, but in the end, they all answer to Orion. If you want to take up the matter with him, that’s fine.”
The Commander hesitated. No one knew Orion’s real name, or what he looks like behind the legendary Paradox Spy kit he uses. The only thing anyone knew about Orion was that Draconius is his son.
“I’m sure we can work something out. Orion is dissatisfied with my operations?”
“You could say that.”
