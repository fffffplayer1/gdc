Since the prologue and Part 1 were in first person, the last chapter and the epilogue will be in first person as well.

Part 25: The Fifth Faction
I stood in Nexus Tower, in front of the Imagination Nexus. LordBrockTree stood beside me, and the faction leaders in front of me. All were listening for what I was about to say.
“Two years ago,” I began, not letting the others see my unease, “My parents were killed traveling here. They were going to speak with you about allowing Valia a place in your society, after all of the help we have given you. Today, I have the opportunity to finish what they started.”
The Faction Leaders looked at each other. They had no idea what I was going to suggest. I continued my speech.
“Now, throughout the history of the Nexus Force, there have always been minifigures who have much more potential than most other recruits. These minifigures often never fulfill their potential, as they cannot obtain the gear or be sent on the missions necessary to do so.” She tensed up. They’d hate this part, but it had to be done. “I am suggesting a fifth faction.”
All of the Leaders were in shock, with the exception of Hael Storm, who seemed quite relaxed about the idea and said, “Arrr, an’ I thought ye were just another one o’ me swabbies.”
They started arguing with one another, until I was forced to shout, “HEAR ME!” They all turned at that. I silently rebuked myself. That was the voice my father used whenever he tried to get someone to do something. I continued now that I had their attention, “This faction will be different from the others. We will not be available to new recruits; rather, minifigures will be able to transfer to it from other factions. But, only when we have selected them. We will have four faction kits: the Hero, Thinker, Scout, and Spy.”
Vanda Darkflame whispered, “The legendary kits.” I knew what the first words would be from Duke Exeter, but that didn’t make them any easier to take. “I cannot allow this,” he said with a face of iron. To my surprise, the other Faction Leaders started to agree with my idea. Exeter noticed this too, and said, “Am I the only one here who remembers Ryous?”
“Arrr, a long time ago, that was. Don’t go insulting Ryous, Duke, he was my friend.”
“Hael,” Dr.Overbuild said, “Did you just say a sentence that wasn’t covered in pirate dialect?” Storm did not answer this.
“Valia needs a place here, sir. It’s either this or we break off trade.”
That stopped Duke’s stone face. He apparently decided he didn’t have a choice. He did, I just didn’t let him see it.
“Fine. What are you going to call yourselves?”
“The Valiance. A legendary name used for a new purpose.”
“You’d be the Knights of Valia, would you? Kid, war is more than knights in shining armor.”
“I know, sir.”
He looked into my eyes, and I do not know what he saw there. But whatever it was, it scared him, though he tried not to show it. I was sure now that my plan would pass. There was just one more thing to do. “LordBrockTree,” I said, turning to him. He had been unusually silent for the entire meeting.
“Will you be the fifth Faction Leader?”
“No.”
I was stunned. I started wracking my brain for a response when he put a hand on my shoulder. He said, in a low voice I had never heard him use before, “It’s not my place, your majesty.”
He turned to the Faction Leaders. “Are we unanimous?”
One by one they nodded. Satisfied, he turned back to me and said, “Hail, Hazel Sprite, Leader of the Valiance!”
It was a horrible moment.

Part 24: The Face of Orion
Part 1
Usually the RUTCARVER MKII is somewhat intimidating. When its machine guns start firing, the target should at least respond. Yet Orion did not. He seemed slightly amused. He reached out with his right hand, and a tendril of maelstrom energy struck Riker, knocking over the MKII. The LIONHEART appeared behind her, and Rover unleashed a blast of his Rank 5 cannon. Again, no effect. Rikerone was getting tired of invincible foes. What was with this guy? She knew he was Draco’s father, but unlike Draco, Orion appeared to have maelstrom completely at his disposal. She charged at him, but was knocked back by another chaotic tentacle. Orion laughed, and removed his helmet.

Rover now knew where he had seen Draco’s face before.
Draconius was the son of the Darkitect.
Part 2
Golden Boiling Storm knew he had to act while the Baron was distracted with the girl. He stood at the center of the cloud of maelstrom, holding up one of the Valiances that LordBrockTree had obtained. At his touch it turned into five shadow orbs, which circled around him, drawing power from the maelstrom… There was a flash of energy, and one of the orbs froze solid and fell to the ground, shattering. Storm turned to see his second in command holding his own Valiance.
“Solid Frozen Warrior. So you are the one who disrupts my plan. So be it.”
Part 3
“Get out of here, Rover!”Rikerone yelled above the roar of the storm.
“What about you?”
“I’m going to try to stop Typhonus!”
“AND HOW TO YOU PLAN TO DO THAT?”
Another blast of maelstrom knocked Hazel down. She realized that the Baron was right. She didn’t know how to defeat him. The Darkitect thickened the storm, tossing Rover and the LIONHEART out of it. Rikerone was trapped in the center of the storm. She charged at the Baron, but he swatted her aside. He disappeared in an instant, reappearing behind her and knocking her back down as she was getting up again. She lacked weapons, but she wasn’t helpless. She hit the Darkitect with a roundhouse kick, but his corruption had made him as hard as steel and Riker only hurt herself. “I need a weapon,” she thought to herself.”
A weapon…
She had upgraded gear before, that was no problem. But this… she was trying to create something out of thin air. She didn’t have time to think about whether or not it was possible. She just had to do it. She focused the magic inside of her as she had done so many times before, but this time, instead of letting it flow she compressed it together, gave it physical shape in her hand. What she held was a golden sword, long and slender, with strange runes on the side like Verminbane. She swung the sword, and found it was so sharp it cut through time and space, leaving a hole in the dimension. She pointed the tip of the sword, and a beam of pure imagination shot from it, disintegrating the maelstrom it hit.
This was the true Valiance. The Sword of Imagination.
She charged at the Darkitect with it, then… she thrust it behind her, and heard a satisfying scream.
Part 4
Rover was aboard the LIONHEART arguing with one of the Faction Leaders.
“Sir, the Darkitect himself is in that cloud. If we strike now, we can destroy him. We have to get in there and rescue Riker!”
“If your friend is battling Typhonus, she’s as good as dead,” Duke Exeter’s gaze was firm, his decision absolute, “We can’t afford a frontal assault against him.”
“Look- wait, what’s that?” He pointed to the cloud of maelstrom, which was going out of control, slowly expanding… just out of it… a legendary bike, ridden by a Daredevil.
Rover, LordBrockTree, SolidFrozenWarrior, and the Faction Leaders all yelled in unison,
“RIKERONE!!!!!”
Part 5
She rode like a storm, but the maelstrom surge was relentless. The MARKII responded easily to Riker’s touch, but it could not outrun the maelstrom. It was a race. It was an epic race for survival.

The ultimate race.

Just as the maelstrom was about to reach her, she and the bike along with her started to glow with a golden light. Her now useless Daredevil armor was replaced by golden armor and cape which shone with light, charged by pure imagination. Her headgear as well… “I’m wearing a crown, aren’t I?” she thought to herself, too busy steering the RUTCARVER to check. “I always hated crowns.” She knew what she had to do. It was time to press the special button.
She reached for the button on the controls that showed an aura of lightning around the MKII. “ It’s now or maelstrom, and I don't want to be a stromling.” The MKII began to use the imagination that was emanating from her armor to shift the RUTCARVER into a much faster form. The machine guns retracted into the hull, her seat leaned back so that she was almost lying down, the engines began to hum much louder than before. The entire bike transformed itself. The maelstrom could overtake the MKII. But they could not catch the RUTCARVER MKIII. She realized, though, that to STOP the maelstrom cloud, something more was required. She was reluctant… but who wouldn’t be? It had so much power… The person who wouldn’t be reluctant to give it up would be the person with no need for power. That was who she had to try to be. She tossed Valiance, the true Valiance, behind her, into the cloud of maelstrom. The clash of energies this resulted in evaporated the maelstrom and shattered the sword. She was no longer powered by it and so stopped glowing, but she retained the armor. She took the crown off of her head and was relieved to find that it was just a band of gold with a red jewel, nothing (very) fancy. She put it back on and parked the MARKIII. Everyone could see that the Queen of Valia had returned.

The Knights of Valia Book Two: I do not have a title for this book yet, instead I will use the best one someone thinks of using the prologue.

Prologue
Legends tell of four explorers who journeyed through the universe to find the Imagination Nexus. Those legends are true. But they left out one part: there were FIVE.

I stood at the doorway to my home. I straightened my gray beard and put on my fedora. It helped to make an entrance. I mounted my horse and rode to the meeting point. It was a spaceship dock. I didn’t like the things; they used unsavory fuel and generally didn’t allow horses on them. The star cruiser we were going to be flying on was in front of me. It was larger than most, with a sizable viewport and a red color scheme. I read the name on the side: VENTURE KOI. This was the ship of the minifigure who descended the ramp to meet me.
“Ryous! Ye came after all!”
“Hello, old friend,” I said to Hael Storm. We went back a long way, and he had invited me on this journey. “Am I the last one here?”
“Nay, we be waitin’ fer one more.”
I dismounted and we walked into the KOI. Inside were a blond, serious-looking man wearing blue knight armor and a man whose face was mostly obscured by the goggled helmet he had on. The second man had four metal arms extending from his back, one of which was holding a chainsaw. “Did you build those yourself?” I asked him.
“You are correct. These are technological appendages of my own design. I am Dr. Overbuild,” he said, extending an “appendage”.
“Ryous Vortex,” I replied, shaking the metal arm. The knight introduced himself as Duke Exeter. I turned back to Storm. “So, what new discovery are you bringing us to investigate? You obviously found something.”
“Arrr,” Storm said, a look of pure glee on his face, “A great treasure. I found me map…” He paused, savoring the moment, “to the planet Crux!”
“Crux.” I was not prepared for that. “I’ll need my students.”
“Nay, Ryous, we be goin’ on this trip alone. Jus’ us five.”
“Five? There are four of us…”
Then the final explorer walked up the ramp. “Typhonus.” I said, barely containing my rage at seeing him here.
“Ah, Vortex. I see Hael has coaxed you away from your private army.”
*************************************************************************************

I woke up from my dream. I was once again a seventeen-year-old Faction Leader. It had been so real though… I decided not to tell anyone about the dream. However, I needed to know more about Ryous Vortex. There was only one person I could ask.

Epilogue
I sat at the top of Nexus Tower, gazing in the direction of my homeland, alone with my thoughts. Did I do the right thing? How will I do what is to come? My thoughts were interrupted by Vanda Darkflame walking toward me.
“You ask much of us, Hazel.”
“You sound like Exeter.”
She smirked at that. “That was the idea. Listen, I know you’re in the right here. But Duke is going to take longer to see that than anyone else.”
She sat down next to me, gazing as I did, and continued, “If Duke has one flaw, it’s that he doesn’t really adjust to new ideas very well. He’s as stubborn as Hael is money-obsessed. But then,” she shrugged, “I suppose that’s what make Sentinels such powerful fighters. He didn’t like the idea of making you a faction, much less the special conditions for your faction. Also, he thinks you’re too young to lead it.”
“Yeah, I suppose I am. I asked LordBrockTree to do it…”
“The reason LordBrockTree refused was that he saw the spark of leadership inside of you. Remember how you commanded during the Warnoglee attack? Those kinds of qualities are what make Faction Leaders. Look, I know you’re a little young, but so was I when I formed the Paradox. Just don’t let Duke’s attitude get to you.”
“Thanks, Miss Darkflame, but… Storm escaped.”
“Please, we’re all equals, remember? Call me Vanda. As for Storm… we’ll be ready.”
I walked back into the tower with Vanda. I will sit with them as an equal… we will sit as equals… and I will build my faction as they have done. And someday, Golden Boiling Storm and I will meet again. And when that happens, I will look into his eyes. And he will see a not a teenage girl, not a thieving princess, but a Faction Leader.
END BOOK ONE
