Evil Has Risen


Darkness was still here, even if it were day light somewhere else. Which meant Shauna wasn’t even close to her destination because she was sure there was light there. Even if Thorn had his own life issues, it didn’t mean he had to react to her in that same way. His life wasn’t even of her concern, but she was still curious about it. What she didn’t understand though, was why he couldn’t give her the answers she asked. Was someone controlling him or did he have a master and couldn’t tell?

Shauna though of herself as the girl without the normal life. Everything that had happened to her was awkward in ways that she couldn’t even believe. And with her father being one of the most powerful villains of LEGO Universe, that made her also powerful, yet not evil. She didn’t even know how to use her power. Maybe that was her father’s intention so she wouldn’t know a thing, but it was only a possibility.
Thorn had fallen asleep- his head against the window. Shauna wished she was snuggled in her blankets, her body shivering from the cold.

Through the misty forest, Shauna noticed an eerie glow. Her vision seemed to be doing pranks on her, because it was very blurry. The glow seemed to be purple and red which didn’t seem normal to her. Shauna shook Thorn’s unmoving body.
“What?” Thorn asked irritated. His eyes opened drowsily.
“Look through the window.”
“I don’t even see a thing.” Thorn said, about to drift into a deep sleep.
“There’s this red and purple glow outside and I don’t even know what it is.”
His eyes grew huge- he seemed to have trouble of falling back to sleep again. “That’s bad. That’s really bad. We’re in danger! We may be smashed for eternity!” Thorn cried sarcastically, but then his body became afraid- the braveness in him left harshly.
“What do you mean?” Shauna asked nervously.
“You can’t get away from these creatures. Once someone’s their target, the prey doesn’t escape. We all thought they were extinct, but I guess not. When it is day, they seem very nice yet not, but the night is when they are very bad. This creature seemed to have gotten what he wanted: evilness forever.”
“What do you mean? You said they were good during the day.”
“Butterscotch is the only one whom could have made this all this happen. There’s this time, when they are about to become good again, but if the dragon smashes them a second before the change, they will be smashed and rebuilt into evil.”
“Seems gruesome to me.” Shauna gave a disgusted look.
Thorn just nodded sadly. “Good bye, universe. I will never see you again.” He blew his nose into a handkerchief.
“Are you kidding me? You’re already giving up to the world? I thought you were better then that. I guess I was wrong about you.” Shauna closed her eyes and shook her head with grieve.
“This is it, Shauna. That thing will smash us both. Good bye, kind girl, good bye.”
She rolled her eyes. “I guess I have to save the universe since you won’t do it. I’d rather try to save our lives then just sit here in the car with no hope.”
Thorn didn’t say anything.

Evil May Win...

There had never been a time when Shauna did know the answer to her problems in a quick motion as if it were the easiest thing in the entire world. Everything seemed harder for her to do, yet others would already know the answer in their head. She didn’t know her destiny. And maybe for some time, it was just sitting by the window sill looking out into darkness being what she described as idle. Yet that time had changed and she didn’t even know why she was even alive at this point. Was it to fight the monster and be smashed or smash. If that was the case, then she would have expected to have already been smashed a long time ago. Would this entire world even care if she had never come to this world at all? Would it really matter if Thorn had never met her, or her father the same? What would this universe be like if she had never made a trace of being here? She figured the world would be different, but minifigures just lived until they’re smashed. The only way to really make a difference in this world was if you were famous. Maybe this world would still be the same if she was smashed. Well, whatever. She couldn’t make sense of the question anyway. Taking her foot off the gas petal, she moved her foot to the brakes. Then, she opened the car door and jumped out. She had almost forgotten how could it was out there. Her eyes looked up. She could see the creature clearly this time. His eyes looked at her hungrily. He smiled an evil grin. Once they made eye contact, Shauna almost shuddered with fear and pain. She saw images of minifigures being smashed by him! Then, she saw an image she could never forgive. Her father, the Darkitect. He yelled evilly as he drained the energy out of innocent figures into his own. Tears began to trickle down her cheeks, as she was paralyzed with sadness. The monster lunged forward, moving like an ape. Thorn had somehow appeared beside her.
“Shauna, don’t look at the creature directly! Shauna!” Thorn cried, shaking the girl’s shoulders.
She wouldn’t move.
“Please, Shauna! STOP!” Tears welled up inside Thorn’s heart as he tried to stop her from looking through the monster’s eyes.
He stood in front of her face now, trying to block the creature from her sight. It seemed to have worked because then she stood up slowly from the horrible images the monster had showed her. She had trouble walking and Thorn helped her for support.
“Let’s hide behind the car for protection.” Thorn said. Once, they were behind the vehicle, he peeked, trying to see the monster from where he was at. The evil clown was already here looking much bigger then his normal size. His mighty arms flipped the entire car over, the two helpless minifigures now sat in the snow with no protection what so ever.
