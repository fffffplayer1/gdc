Shauna knew that she and Thorn probably wouldn’t make it, but she still had hope. Shakily, her hand wobbled to pull an arrow out of the quiver. She grabbed the bow and stuck the arrow to the string and pulled back. It was almost hard for her to even hold the bow to the air. Shauna aimed right at the center of the creature and let go of the string once she was almost sure she had gotten her target. The arrow went right into his stomach, but he pulled it out madly with one hand.
“Run!” Thorn yelled.
Shauna followed him as he made a path through the snow with quick movements.
“We’ll never make it!” She cried as the monster began to gain on them.
“Try another arrow!”
Shauna plucked the arrow and did the same thing as last time, but this time she aimed for his leg.
He ripped the arrow out of his leg and charged faster with a face of revenge. Shauna didn’t know what to do now, but just stand there as the creature hurled his way toward her. In seconds, Thorn grabbed Shauna’s shoulders and leaped into the snow just in time before the monster dug his hands into snow instead. “Come on, Shauna. We’ve got to get out of the snow!” He said helping her up. The clown turned his evil eyes on Thorn and ran like an ape toward him. “Go, GO!” Thorn cried to Shauna, pushing her forward.

Secrets Are Not To Be Told

Shauna’s legs went numb and she fell over in the snow. The clown was charging straight at them like a bull.
Thorn pulled her up and yelled, “We must go! We will be smashed if your legs don’t move now!”
“I can’t move!” Shauna told Thorn trying not to get frustrated.
They both couldn’t think right at the moment because, all of this commotion was going on-them worried about being smashed.
The clown – who really looked like a wild ape with arms that were flapping around evilly, grabbed Thorn with his bare sharp teeth and shook his head back and forth. He seemed to want to fling him out of sight, yet he didn’t. Instead, he decided to bite his leg which Thorn had wiggling around trying to escape the monster’s grasp.
Shauna had made a packed solid snowball and through it at the creature’s eye. Irritated, he dropped Thorn, holding his eye with his beastly hands.
Shauna grabbed Thorn’s wrist and pulled him out of the snow.
“And when did your legs not become numb?” He asked.
Shauna had no time to answer as they both scurried through the snow.

“Too bad we don’t have that car right now.”
“We would have been smashed by now if we were in that car!” Thorn muttered.
“Yes, but its easier travel then this!” The girl cried pointing down at her feet.
Thorn rolled his eyes. “You need to get in shape anyway.”
Shauna scowled him.
She looked behind her shoulder. The clown wasn’t there, but she knew something was wrong. He would have been here by now. That monster is really fast. A shadow was all that was needed for Shauna to turn her head in that direction. The Clown. He seemed to be even faster in his normal form then an ape. He ran right beside them, yet he was covered from the trees-and it was hard to notice him.
“We’ve got company.” She whispered to Thorn.
The Clown’s eyes blazed in her direction, but she kept her eyes on the trail. He knows.

The Follower


The creature knew her deadliest secret, and there for they were in much danger. It would be more than just a quick smash. First, would be the long years of torture- the host being the clown himself- only then they face the punishment of being smashed after that. Shauna was afraid to tell Thorn of what she knew…


“Is there some way to get away from this clown?” Shauna asked.
Thorn shrugged his shoulders in reply. “I had an opportunity once to find out more about them, but I was lazy at the time so I didn’t read it. And I thought they were extinct.”
“What do you mean? They had a book that you could read?”
“No. It was only a one chance thing. I don’t know where they put the scroll now.”
Shauna sighed with annoyance and frustration. “There must be at least a spell or magical weapon or something to make this creature go away.”
Thorn was silent.
Out of the corner of her eye, Shauna checked to see if the clown was still running at the same pace as she. He was gone.
“Uh…um…Thorn, the creature is gone.”
He still stayed silent.
