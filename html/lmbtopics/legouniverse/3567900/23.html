Shauna growled. “I will never follow you however hard you try.”

“Oh, but I fear you must. Or endure the pain!” The Darkitect laughed, raising his arms.

Shauna’s heart (she really didn’t have one) throbbed in pain as if one had dumped a bucket of fire onto her chest. She gritted her teeth with a snarl. “I really hate you.”

“Join me…”

“Never.”

Shauna roared. The pain was too intense…





The girl sobbed as her eyes awaked. She stood in the darkness of the dungeon, shoulders aching, ancient manacles digging into her wrists as she tried to go back to sleep. The chains prevented her from lying down. And to think that it all happened by her father, himself.

Two pairs of fiery glowing eyes glared at her.

“Who comes here?” She asked weakly.

No response.

The room she was in last wasn’t near as dark as this one. She huddled in the corner, whimpering in the cold. She was afraid, afraid of the world, of this universe. She bent her head down with grief.
