Chapter 5:  Lies of the Universe: Part 1
>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>
     I slowly woke up to a steady rumble of engines.  Where was I?  I opened my eyes.  Well, that question was easy to answer. I was strapped into a chair, one in many rows of seats, each holding a soldier ready for battle, dressed in the grey rock-coloured uniform suitable for fighting on the planet Forsythe.  Wait, what?  'I thought that we we're going to concentrate our forces on the vital planet Tanis,' I thought.  I could feel a metal chip pressed into the skin at the base of my skull, which meant someone at control was monitoring my thoughts.  A few seconds later a rush of information flooded into my head, and I felt like I had been a black and white picture that someone had suddenly coloured-in.  I had my answers.  The Falsohk are attempting to fool the rebels into bringing their own armies onto Forsythe so that we can launch a massive ship attack at Tanis, wiping out their main defenses.  Then, before they have time to build them back up again, we send in all our troops.  Tanis will be ours, and the rebels will be stuck on Forsythe with nowhere to go. . .Cont.

   . . .'But what about the civilians?' I thought back.  'We are just leaving them on Droninc to fend for themselves?'  I waited, expecting a reply, but none came.  I had my answer.
    "Entering Forsythian atmosphere; please remain seated," said a voice, echoing out of the headsets inside the soldiers battle helmets.  I looked around warily, wondering who was commandeering this legion.  "Disembarkment in five minutes, prepare for landing," said the voice.  I checked myself over, making sure my weapons were all in place.  GQ Laser blaster, Accented laser pike, disc-shaped shield attached to my right arm; they were all there.  A small screen flicked on on the arm of my seat. READY FOR DISEMBARKMENT? it read.  I hit the small green button underneath signifying I was, and it blinked off.  Once all the soldiers had done this, the sides of the room flipped down and and we all ran out into the cold, grey-blue air of the Forsythian dusk.
>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>

Chapter 5: Lies of the Universe: Part 2
>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>
     The planet Forsythe is covered in rocks, ice, and water.  Not the place you would pick for a vacation.   This planet spins unusually fast;  a day is only about 6 hours long.
     The line of soldiers hiked over the uneven terrain toward a small Falsohk outpost; here was where they would stay for the night and receive their orders in the morning.  I was in the middle of the line; fourteen men in front of me and eighteen behind.  We could see plumes of steam rising from the outpost; the hot water under the ground was how they heated the bunks for the men.  A lone wolf-like creature topped one of the blue rock ridges ahead of the caravan and stared at them all, it's yellow eyes unblinking.  Then it darted in through a crevice and was gone. . .Cont.

   . . .I watched as the creature disappeared, and I sighed and bit down hard on my water nozzle.  A squirt of water burst out into my mouth, flowing down my throat.  I thought of the rebels, of the little girl whose diary I had read.  Captain Fenloe had always said they were the bad guys, the rebel scum and all that, but I had had this feeling ever since I had read it that something was wrong.  Then I thought of my family.  The rebels had killed them, killed them all, and the Falsohk had saved me.  I didn't know what I should, what I could, believe.  I shook my head, trying to clear away my thoughts.  I was Falsohk now, nothing I could do about that.  That was how things had ended up.  And since I was Falsohk, the Falsohk was the good side.  If I had been a rebel, the rebels would be the good side.  Really, there wasn't any good or bad side, just two groups fighting.  And I was in one of them.

Chapter 5: Part 3: Lies of the Universe
>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>
     I walked alone through the outpost, weaving my way through the small tents and buildings.  Most of the other soldiers were over at the bunks; I ought to be there too, but at the moment I just didn't feel like sleeping.  It was dusk, but it would be light again in a few hours.  The short days made it hard to sleep, and so did a head full of questions.  I had a head full of questions.  I also had no one to answer them.  No one could answer the questions tumbling round inside my brain.  The only thing I could do with them was try to forget them.  As I past by a small bar, I looked inside and saw Fenloe and a group of other generals downing drinks, laughing and talking.  It was against the regulations for soldiers to drink before battle; I guessed that that didn't apply to the men in charge.  The night was getting cold; the sun was almost done setting.  That only took about twenty minutes here.  I turned and began walking back to the bunks; I should get some rest tonight. . . Cont.

    . . .Suddenly, out of the corner of my eye, I saw a small child, alone, huddled by the side of a building, trying to hide in the shadow of the building.
     "What are you doing over there?"  I asked, walking slowly toward the child.
He shrank back, a look of complete and total fear on his face.
     "D-Don't hurt me," the kid mumbled, closing his eyes.
     "Why would I hurt you?"  I asked, holding my hand out, almost as if this little boy was a animal I was trying to befriend.
     "Don't s-s-hoot at me," the boy said, looking back up at me with terror looming in his eyes.
     "I won't hurt you," I said, "why would I hurt you?"
     "B-Because you're one of the bad ones."
     "Bad who?"
     "Bad ones.  You're one of the bad people.  You smashed my pa."  A tear slid down the boys dirty face, and he pulled his ripped, stained coat tightly around him.  I looked into the boys face, and I realized that I had absolutely nothing to say to him.  Nothing.

Chapter 5: Part 4: Lies of the Universe
>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>

The next morning, I led a group of troops out of the outpost and toward a small village where about a hundred rebels were supposedly camped.  According to the instructions given to  us as we stood outside the bunkhouse in the grey, wet, early-morning weather, we would camp outside the city just across the river from the rebels for the night before attacking it by surprise early the next morning.  We would quickly clear out the rebels and take the outpost.  Once we were done, we would wait there as the Falsohk transported supplies before we moved on to the next rebel village.  We would continue like that from village to village until the rebels started moving their troops in.  Once they had a substantial amount of troops on Forsythe, we would pull out as quick as possible and dump as many men as possible on Tanis before they had a chance to react.  Boom, pow, we win, they lose, and everything's rainbows, fireworks and happy trees.  That's how Crilo Thenos, one of the men in charge of the mission described it.  That's how it would be, hopefully.  For some people.  For the Falsohk. . .Cont.

Chapter 5: Part 5: Lies of the Universe
>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>
      I walked slowly through the rows of tents set up in a special circular pattern.  Tired, sweaty soldiers sat around, in circles and by themselves, eating provisions and talking softly.  The sun was setting quickly to the west, glinting off visors and armor.  Some soldiers already had small fires going, and the flames cast eerie shadows over the tents.  I walked slowly back to my tent, set off apart from the soldiers tents with the other officers of higher ranks.  Fenloe and Crilos were sitting around a fire of their own; discussing the battle of tomorrow quietly.  I walked past them and into my own tent, fastening the door tightly.  I took off my armor and cape, leaving them on the sand next to my bed.  I lay back on my bed, not bothering to pull up the covers.  I probably wouldn't be able to sleep anyway.  I lay there awake, listening to the other officers talking.  Talking about tomorrow.  Tomorrow we would storm the rebel encampment; we would defeat them all.  We would charge through, taking lives.  The smashed bodies would litter the ground; that didn't matter.  It was for the better, for a better cause.  The civilians would be safe, now that the rebels were taken care of.  There would be peace.  Peace would reign.  That's what I thought, at least.  That's what I wanted to think.  Tonight would be a sleepless night.
>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>

Chapter 5: Part 6: Lies of the Universe
>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>

     I lay awake, listening to the creatures of the cold rocky wasteland of the planet Forsythe scrabbling outside my tent. The questions in my head tumbled around; sleep was  impossible.  I had to get them answered!  I was tired of the biased information aboard the Romulus, I wanted the truth.  I didn't know who had it, but I was going to find it.  I wanted to hear the battle from the other side; from the rebels.  I threw the thin sheet off of me and stood up next to my bed.  I pulled on my uniform and cape, leaving my weapons behind.  Silently I pulled open the flap of my private tent and crept out into the cold, Forsythian night.  The cold sand of the desert flew about and stung my face, rattling softly against my metal legs and arms.  I pulled the face-guard around my neck up to shield myself, covering everything but my eyes.  I knew that just over the river was the Rebels camp, and I would be sure to find a sentry out alone.  I only hoped he would let me speak to him.
>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>>
