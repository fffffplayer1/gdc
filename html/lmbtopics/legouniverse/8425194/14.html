‎05-14-2015 10:32 AM

A burst of laser whizzed past Takilia's head as she brought her horse around to face a mini-figure who seemed to be going crazy with an imagination turret. That thing seemed unbeatable! Its lasers blasted upon the ground in explosions, singing grass and smashing stromlings as easily. Percy, always a daredevil, had tried several times to sneak up and attack the turret, only to go lunging back as it swung around to face him.

"Forget it, Percy." Joni called. "You're never gonna get it."

"I beg to defer!" Percy replied. "Whatever that means."

"Differ," Joni corrected, turning to Takilia. "It's your call. Do you want to stay here and wait 'till Mr. Fancy Gun leaves or try someplace else?"

Takilia didn't give it too much thought. "Let's keep trying," she suggested. Takilia had grown to enjoy the thrill of battle. It was heavily preferable to fight an immortal enemy on the battlefield than to trugle with her own conflicting thoughts in her own room.

I've become a mess, Takilia mused silently. And yet, it didn't bother her- she finally felt like she was someplace she belonged.

And that worried her. After all, she was first and foremost a minifig, not a stromling. Right?

A thought struck her, a revolutionary one that she had never even considered during her journey, for the reason that it seemed just too perposterous.

Maybe she didn't have to be one or the other. Maybe minifigures and stromlings, regardless of appearance and side, were just, in the long run, people. Maybe the object that had divided her and confused her could be used to unite two warring nations.

It was a revolutionary, perposteruous idea. And yet, maybe it was a possible one.

 

Joni came around to her. "Percy says he has a plan," she said. "Percy?"

"Thank you," Percy said modestly. "When I say 'three', we charge at the turret and try to destroy it."

Joni rolled her eyes. "Isn't that basically what we're doing?"

"Well, yes," Percy said. "But we're doing it all at the same time, so it's cooler."

‎09-10-2015 11:11 AM

Joni sighed. "Somehow I don't think that's going to work."

"Seriously? It's a great idea!"

Joni turned to Takilia. "What do you think?"

"I think... I think we should have some lunch."

 

"So, Takilia," Percy asked in between bites of a strange Maelstrom dish that she was hesitant to try. "How was your first time horseback riding?"

"It was... interesting, to say the least." She poked at what looked like a big purple glob of maelstrom jell-o between two slabs of bread.

"I've been thinking... how would you like to try dragon riding?"

"I think she's already done that," Joni pointed out.

"Yes, but no. She's never flown a dragon into the midst of battle."

"Since when do you use the word midst? Since when can you pronounce the word midst?"

"Does it really matter?"

"No, not really."

"Actually... I have something else to do."

They stared at her. "Without us?" Joni asked, shocked.

"Well, no." Takilia said. "But... yes."

They stared.

"You're kidding, right? We've done everything together since we met you."

"I know guys, but... there's something I should have done earlier."

"Without us."

"Without you."

There was an awkward silence.

"Okay. then..." Joni said. "Come on, Percy."

"Can I finish my sandwich?"

Joni sighed. "Okay."

 

Takilia hated to see her friends leave. She had half a mind to call them back, to tell them everything... but no. There was no time for that.

With one last glance around at the life she could have kept, Takilia marched over to a cave.

In a few hours Exeter's fleet would be attacking the hidden Stromling city of Perrivore V. She could meet up with him, tell him all she had learned, and he would accept her again, change her back into the minifigure she'd been.

And then she would finally have peace.

Exeter glared out of the viewport at the swirling maelstrom mass that he had identified as a Stromling outpost. Once the army got in there, a battle greater than any other would take place. His forces would invade its capital, break into the building, and discover the Darkitect's location.

The only problem was getting through the vortex.

Overbuild was furiously punching something into his computer. Then he looked up at Duke.

"It all comes out the same no matter how I compute it. There's no way to get through that vortex without severe side effects. I suggest-"

He broke off at a glare from Exeter. "This is the greatest campaign we have ever launched. I will not allow mere odds to stand in my way. Yes, there may be casusalties. Is that a large price to pay for order?"

His voice rose to where he was almost shouting. "You were there, Exeter! You saw what he did to the Nexus- to pure imagination! If we stand down, Chaos will rule in the galaxy! We shall never have peace! I will not slow down until we have peace and safety. Order!"

Exeter and Vanda exchanged nervous glances. "Sir, methinks you should lie down," Hael suggested quietly.

Exeter turned to him, ready to unleash his flaming wrath-

And all of them noticed the Stromling that had suddenly appeared in their midst.

Overbuild ducked for cover behind his computer station. Hael pulled out his cutlas and held it out for defense. Exeter shouted horrible things in his anger.

"SMASH IT! DESTROY IT NOW!"

"Wait!" Vanda shouted. She held up a hand, and the command station was quiet. "Listen."

The stromling paused a moment, and spoke. "My tame is Takilia TheAwesome. Just a month ago, I was an ordinary minifigure like you. I was transformed into a stromling, and spent several weeks undercover.

"I've come to you with important information that will change our world."

 

DUN! DUN! DUN!
