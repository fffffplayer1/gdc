Last chapter part before Christmas! I hope you enjoy it, and have a Merry Christmas!

 

Percy and Joni traveled slowly through the wormhole. They were uncharacteristically silent. Flashes of Blue and Purple flew past them. This was the longest portal they had ever traveled through- and neither of them knew what they would find at the exit.

It was scary!

“Joni,” Percy said, finally. “When we get to the other side…”

“If there is another side,” Joni pointed out.

“Right, assuming there is another side,” he corrected himself, “We have to be ready for anything. The portal may not even have been left by Takilia. She could have been kidnapped by pirates, who used this portal to get back to her base!”

“What’s your point?” Joni asked, trying to act casual. Now that he had brought this up, she found her mind drifting through hundreds of unpleasant possibilities. “It’s too late to turn back now.”

Percy took a deep breath. “No matter what happens… no matter what happens at the end of this portal… will you be with me?”

Joni was almost tempted to make a joke or something to cheer him up. She hadn’t seen Percy look so serious before…

But she pushed the thought away. The time for joking had passed. Their friend was missing- and most certainly in danger. Now was the time for heroism.

“Yes, Percy,” Joni said somberly. “To the death.”

The two of them materialized inside an empty hallway. Percy felt the floor rocking beneath his feet and knew they were on a spaceship. Joni heard noises coming from a room and knew there were minifigures nearby. Both realized they were in danger, and made not a sound.

Percy gestured frantically, and the two of them ducked into a nearby closet to discuss.

“Well, now what?” Joni whispered. Every now and then they could hear the click of footsteps outside the door.

“I’m out of ideas,” Percy said glumly. “For some reason I thought there would be a big sign that said ‘Takilia is that way’.”

Joni frowned. She gazed around the small closet. “You’re good with mops, right? Could we use one of these somehow?’

Percy gave her a quizzical look. “I’m good with mops when you need them for cleaning. What did you have in mind.”

Joni grinned and threw him a large jacket, an oversized helmet, and a bucket of water. “Disguise.”

‎01-01-2016 10:52 AM

 

Takilia awoke inside an odd, glowing, spherical cage. Where there could have been a window there was only a charcoal drawing of space.

There was no door, fake or otherwise.

Takilia scrambled to her feet, feeling the floor rock beneath her. She felt like a hamster inside its cage.

There was one other person inside the cell: Vanda Darkflame. Takilia hoped that there were no hard feelings, considering that she had inadvertently caused Vanda to be stuffed into a cell. Takilia took a long look around to try to find the way out. The only possibility was a metal panel… but it didn’t look like it was going anywhere.

Takilia felt desperate, and pushed on it with all her might. But in a cell like this, even hope was shown to be a delusion.

She banged on the walls of the cell, and felt tears coming to her eyes.

“There’s no way out.”

She spun around, startled by the sudden noise. Vanda was awake and sitting on the floor. “I watched Overbuild design these cells. They can only be opened and shut by the robot arms- and when one of those enters here, there’s no getting around it.”

Takilia sighed. She didn’t have the strength to answer. There was no way out-

She paused. What was she thinking?

Takilia felt around eagerly for the pyramid, but as she looked on the floor to see if it had dropped, she had no luck. Exeter had already taken the pyramid. “So that’s it, then.” Takilia said sadly. “The minifigures will completely destroy the stromlings. We’ve lost.”
