Takilia dropped down on the upper room of the inn. Everything was so weird here, like something out of a Dr. Suess book. Yet it also seemed stange and inviting, almost cheery. Not at all like how she had expected it.

Provided in the room was bookcase. That was weird, because Takilia expected that Stromlings wouldn't know how to read.

It was raining outside, but it wasn't raining water. Big globs of Maelstrom energy pounded on the window from above and below and from left to right.

Even the rain can't stay in the same direction, she thought darkly. Outside, the skeleton red-eyed sticky-uppered-hair Stromlings were doing normal things like normal minifigures. Didn't they realize that they were on the wrong side?

She tossed and turned in her bed, the blankets chaotically messing themselves up whenever she touched them.

Takilia grumbled at the steady, bright light coming from the fixtures above her. The room was glowing in the middle of the night.

After a few hours of trying to sleep, Takilia finally tried to call room service. "You left the light on in here," she snapped. "How do I turn it off?"

"Oh," a voice answered her. "You can't turn it off, but feel free to turn on the darklight."

"The what?" Takilia asked. But there was no reply.

Takilia noticed something that looked like a nightlight in the corner, and she flipped it on. It showered the room in a steady haze of darkness.

"Everything is so mixed up here," she muttered.

Eventually, with the help of the darklight, Takilia drifted off to sleep.

‎01-09-2014 10:22 AM

Two men were marching through a garden. No, not just men- Mythrans. Dark Mythrans.

They seemed to be looking for something. Something to complete their device.

One of them waved a hand, and the other huddled over.

They picked up a triangle-shaped object.

"We've found it," one of them whispered. "The third triforce."

In a delicate opperation, the three triforces were combined into a pyramid.

The pyramid glowed Dark Red with Maelstrom energy.

A burst of energy zapped across the sky and lit some nearby grass on fire. The two men spun around.

"Not now," the younger one said angrily. "Not after all we've been through."

The two started running as more energy blasts shattered through the sky. A burst just missed the older one, and he fell down.

The younger one spun around, and knelt by the older. "Rabbi! Are you hurt?"

The older one groaned. "Go on without me. Take the triforce pyramid... to the Dark Mythran capital."

"I'm not going to leave you, Rabbi!" The younger insisted.

The older gave a faint smile. "You don't have to call me that anymore."

The younger nodded. "I will... Alemicla."

A starship touched down in the grass, and three men- and one lady- stormed out.

"Well," one of them said. "Look what we have here."

Alemicla gulped. "You can't take it!" He shouted. "I will not allow you Nexus Forcemen to corrupt it!"

One of them stepped into the light. It was Duke Exeter. "On the contrary. I will take that Pyramid."

He leaned closer to the two Dark Mythrans. "And you two shall be destroyed."

 

Takilia opened her eyes to a scream. A quick glance revealed that she was still in her bed, and still in the Maelstrom Vortex City.

"That was a weird dream," she muttered, rubbing the sleep out of her eyes.

Then she ammended, "But not as weird as this place."

‎01-11-2014 10:46 AM

As Takilia headed out through the streets, her thoughts were on one thing and one thing only. The pyramid.

Where did it come from? How did it infect her? Why did she have the weird dream? Where was it now?

Takilia thought about going back to the hospital to ask a few questions, but she didn't think that would go well, considering how they had parted.

Takilia slumped on a bench in the center of town, and walked the skeletal figures walk around, chatting, trading and acticing basically like purple minfigs.

Takilia's sensitive ears picked up a shrill note. Instinctively, she brought her hands up to her ears to muffle the sound. What was that?

She looked up angrilly, noticing a Stromling who was busy sweeping the ground with a mop. While whistling.

Takilia tried to ignore him.

She closed her eyes, and tried to return to her thoughts. She was so close to finding the truth. The pyramid. Maybe the people from her dream had created the pyramid, and when they were captured, had left the pyramid behind in Avant Gardens. But why did Duke Exeter want it? Were those other people the other faction leaders?

The Stromling whistled harder, and Takilia growled at him. She noticed some other people evacuating the area.

As the Stromling began surfing on his mop, Takilia stood up angrilly. "What's wrong with you?!" She asked.

He gave her a goofy grin. "Oh, the usual," he said, attempting a pole vault with the mop. He failed, and fell flat on his face. "I'm a Daredevil Pirate!"

Takilia rolled her eyes. Stromlings were so weird. "That's nice........."

‎01-17-2014 11:32 PM

Deep in a floating space platform, two men and a woman carefully steered the spacecraft around the orbit of Planet M. The first man's mouth was open in a toothless grin as he muttered to his parrot, "We're going to beat the Maelstrom out of those Stromlings... I can feel it." To which the parrot squaked back to him, "Hael so smart! SQUAK! Hael so smart!"

By a computer consle, another man quickly punched in coordinates into his computer with all six of his arms- two metalic, two flesh. His goggles glowed red with excitement as he noticed, through the viewscreen, a small purple haze surrounding the planet.

"This is it, Hael," he said, excitedly. "The readings here are extremely high with Maelstrom!"

"Calm down, Overbuild," the lady retorted. She climbed up from her small hole to get a better view. "We have to wait for Duke before we do anything."

"Um, yes, of course," Overbuild said hastilly.

Near the back of the bridge, a door opened up, and a knightly dressed man strode into the room. His blonde warrior's hair drooped over his earless head.

"I think we've found it," Duke said, his voice not revealing any of the excitement that Overbuild was sure he was feeling. "This is probably the Maelstrom base."

He advanced towards the front consle. "Summon the fleet. We must crush these Chaotic Beings once and for all. We shall leave no Stromling alive."

His eyes flashed, perhaps recallign some long-forgotten memory. "Not a single Stromling."

‎01-18-2014 11:00 AM

Takilia was heading back to the inn- which somehow teleported away from the Darkitect's castle and was now sitting on top of a large fountain- when the sound of sirens reached her ears.

BWEEEP...bwoop...BWEEP...bwoop...

"What's going on?" She called over the siren to a nearby Stromling.

"That's the battle siren. It means that some sort of planet controlled by the Maelstrom is under attack, and they need emergency reinforcemens."

Takilia frowned. "Will we get promotions?"

The man snorted. "Kid, if you helped to protect this vortex from being discovered, you would be promoted so high that someone could tell you where the Darkitect's castle is."

"I thought it was right there," Takilia said, pointing towards a castle.

"Naw," the man said, shaing his head. "There's one of those in every vortex. It's more of a vacation home."

Takilia nodded, sort of understanding, sort of not. But one thing was certain- if she wanted this plan to succeed, she needed to help out in this battle.

"Hop on over to the Vortex Pad,"  the man said, running off.

Takilia nodded and walked up to a fenced off area.

"Halt!" One guard Stromling said, blocking her way. "What are you doing?"

"I need to help defend. Didn't you hear the siren?"

The guard shook his head. "Look, that's great and all, but you can't go into a hostile area without backup. It's the regulations."

"But, I-"

But the guard was already doing something else.

Takilia muttered something angrilly and started walking away.

After she had taken about six steps, she felt a nudge on her side.

"Hey," a voice said. Takilia turned. It was the Daredevil Pirate. "I overheard you talking with the guy. I'm not on a crew, either. Would you like to partner up with me?"

Takilia blinked. "...No." She said, trying to walk away.

But the Pirate was presistent. "My name's Percy," he said, running to catch up with her. "I think we could do it."

"Leave me alone," Takilia said angrilly, hurrying up ahead.

‎01-19-2014 11:31 AM

But the Pirate would not leave her alone. "Where you from?" He asked, jogging at an easy pace. Takilia ignored him, looking for a place to hide where she could be safe from this-

She looked at Percy, trying to come up with a good word to describe him.

-Thing.

"Look, Percy," Takilia said, staring at him. "I don't want your help."

"Why not?" Percy asked, giving her a huge grin.

"Because..."

"They don't let you fight if you don't have backup."

"But... but... but..."

Before Takilia could finish her motorboat imitation, Percy shrugged. "Suit yourself."

And then he started walking away very slowly, peering behind his shoulder every couple steps.

Various scenes passed through Takilia's head. And, since she was half Stromling, the scenes were a little chaotic. She imagined having to fight minfigures with a sword while Percy was at her side playing loud music and hitting her with a mop. She imagined traveling through a vortex while Percy's mouth was moving up and down and up and down and shooting out words faster than a machine gun...

And then another scene passed through her mind. The vision of being the greatest minifigure ever, going down in history as the one who discovered the Darkitect's REAL base (not to mention all his fifty or so vacation homes).

"All right," Takilia sighed, talking to Percy. "I guess I'll be on a crew with you."

"Yay!" Percy said, rushing up to her. "And i've already figured out the perfect name! The Bacon Bosses! You like it?"
