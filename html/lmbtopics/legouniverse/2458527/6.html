Chap 4.3
Crux Prime. We had arrived.We had landed not at Sentinel Point Zeta, but instead at Aura Mar. Bob the told us what to do:
Bob: Guys, We'll need to fly out into those Maelstrom clouds in the distance, there you will find the Darkitect's Lair.
I looked out into the distance. I could see nothing but a purple sky extending onward into darkness. I suddenly looked behind me and... there was a mad mob of minifigures chasing us! We jumped onto some floating platforms, and we found ourselves on Butterscorch's platform. We had lost them. The minifigs were gone. And then Butterscorch turned around, hittting us with her big tail, and we were all sent flying into the air. When we landed, we were in an area I had never been to, it was peaceful, and quiet. We all just laid down on our backs to recover from the large amount of action we had just encountered. And then it hit me. We were on the other side of Rivendark Canyon. I'd remembered what I once read on a Nexus Force Plague. It said that there werre strong traces of imagination there. And then, another surprise, I noticed a blue transparent minifigure standing in front of me. It was a Mythran...

"Hello," said the Mythran, "I am BrickMania the Mythran. I see you are all on a dangerous journey. I believe I can help. Here, take this."
He handed me a weapon. But it wasn't just any weapon, it was a weapon so rare, that many thought it a myth. It was a Golden Uber Hammer. Created out of concentrated blue, red, green, and the ultra rare yellow imaginite, it had the power to destroy any enemy ever smashed by the Nexus Force. But of course, no one had ever smashed the Darkitect. I felt a sickly trying to hold the weapon, because I was Maelstrom, and it was imagination.
Me: Thank you, Mythran, but how did you know-
BrickMania: Mythrans know everything.
Me and SuperSentinel: Ohhhhh!
BrickMania: You'd best be on your way, good luck! Oh, and I almost forgot, take these, all of you.
The Mythran gave us all Jetpacks, another rare item. And then he simply disappeared. We few onward to the heart of the Maelstrom, as we neared the violet vortex, Bob said that now he had to leave, and we all said goodbye to him. And onward into the vortex we went...

Chapter 5 - Vortex of Chaos
There we were. Me, SuperSentinel, Fullo Mee, and Relly Strange were all standing on one of the many small chunks that led into the dark, shadowy, and worst of all, chaotic vortex. The chunks were not too small, but they were about moderate-sized, about half the size of Butterscorch's platform. They seemed to extend onward as a pathway to the heart of the Maelstrom. We all took off our faction gear to avoid any suspicion. The further we went, the darker and more purple the skies around us became. It was then that we faced our first obstacle, a Stromling guard.
Guard: Who are you? Why are you coming into the lair of the Darkitect?
Me: Uhh, we are um... the Darkitect called us! He, um, wanted to speak to us about something!
Fullo: Uh, yeah, that's it!
Guard: Hmmm. How do I know you're not Nexus Force recruits in a holographical disguise? Let me see your arms.
We all showed him our arms. Of, course we were infected, so it wasn't a hologram.
Guard: Hmmm. They're purple, they feel like stromling arms, ok, you may pass.
We all went onward. I had a feeling that considering how smart that guard was compared to other Maelstrom creatures, there would be more in store for us than a few easily smashed enemies or dumb guards. I suddenly felt the urge to turn back and betray Bob. I knew this feeling. But it was stronger, more urging. I tried to resist it, but I barely could, for the feeling was stronger every time. And I wasn't the only one, Relly, as we all expected wastrying to turn back so much we were almost dragging him. SuperSentinel was walking slower and slower and almost going backwards as we advanced, and Fullo was the only one who kept us moving onward, but even he had those dark feelings. We finally pushed back the feeling and continued on, going faster than before. Then, in the middle of this chaotic, dangerous, and scary world, there was something that, if I wasn't on the most dangerous mission ever, would have made me laugh. We saw a stromling that said, in his stromling tone, "You look like your quick on your feet! Wanna do a foot race to the Darkitect's Lair?" Relly really wanted to say yes, and, as it was not going to be an obstacle in our journey, we all agreed, and went on the race...
