Chapter 1.4 (supposedly).

The moon was obscured by a cloud bank. Suddenly, Eddie and Nash couldn't tell between their shadows and the Unknown. Any second the Mist would set in and they'd be eaten or worse. As they waited tensely for the inevitable, Nash's hand absentmindedly drifted to his pocket. He reached in and pulled out his pocketknife. He couldn't remember how he'd gotten it, only that is was his. A small LED light was attached. He turned it on. In front of him stood the Unknown he'd seen earlier, skeletal form aglow with its eerie light all of a sudden. Its fangs were bared, its claws poised to attack. Nash stood ramrod straight as the repulsive creature hissed, shielding its eye holes from the harsh light. The sound was a ragged, raw noise, like a whistle with a sore throat. Eddie turned behind Nash, saw the Unknown, and the twnety others behind it. He kicked the embers left over from their meager fire, which they had started to see the diary page. The wood shards flared up, and one of the flaming darts struck the lead Unknown in the face, right between the eyes. It howled and took a lurching step back. Nash could tell the predators weren't used to walking on their hind legs. In retaliation, though, the beast reared back and moved lightning fast with its claws, faster than Nash or Eddie could follow. A rapid blur could be seen, and then it smote Nash on the side of his head. NAsh's vision went blurry. He rocked on his heels dizzily, then fell backward. He was dimly aware of something wet and warm running down his face from where the Unknown had clawed him. He stood up shakily, and grabbed a stick from the ground. He saw that his brother had done the same, and was fighting the Unknown off with a burning stump of a branch. Nash dipped his stick in the hot remnants of the bonfire, and once it was flaming, he swung drunkenly at the enemies before him. It wasn't expected to hit, and surely it wouldn't given his condition. By medical standards, he shouldn't be standing. But nonetheless, the light from the fire (as it had doen many times) seared the Unknown's sight lines, and it howled once more and bolted. The rest of is pack followed. Nash could see clearer now. Eddie was panting a few feet away., The moon was shining again. For his next trick, Nash promptly lost his breakfast.
