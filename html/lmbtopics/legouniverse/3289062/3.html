Survival of the Fittest- Chapter 1.2 (Eddie)

"Nash, you stay here. I'm gonna go see if there's some food or something around here." Eddie Aldervan turned away from the ledge's beautiful view and ran inside the forest of Slikt. There were trees all around him; Why did this planet have to be so... wild? Eddie walked through the trees, searching for some food, or sticks, or a shelter, or- "What?" Eddie looked down, and before him was- "A shadow? Wait..." Luckily, he had a chicken leg in his pocket. He threw it in the shadow, and suddenly, the leg was tossed out, stripped to the bone. The shadow had eaten it. ''Oh..." The shadow had moved. Moved towards Eddie. "AHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH!" He screamed. Next to him were some sticks, leaves, wire, and... a pack of food? No time for questions, he thought. The Unknown shadow, as Eddie called it, was right behind him. He yelled, and started running back to the ledge. "Eddie?!" Yelped Nash, when Aldervan reached the campsite. Suddenly, the Unknown shadow dissappeared. "WHAT? WHAT'S WRONG EDDIE?!" "Uh, nothing." Eddie walked around, thinking. "Shadows..." He muttered. Nash looked unsettled. "Nash, I want you to stay absolutely still." "Why?" "You have two shadows." Eddie was right. Two shadows loomed behind Nash. "Why should I stay still...?" He asked. "There is a very dangerous creature behind you. If you twitch, you'll die."

"What in the world was that?" Nash croaked, the smile disappearing from his face. Eddie shook his head. "I think we just saw what an Unknown really looks like. Check this out." he opened the book and showed Nash his page. There was writing, and a pencil sketch. It read:



Beneath the text, an incerdibly terrifying picture of the exact form the writer described leered at them, its mouth open in a silent howl. It looked sad and dishevled from his bumpy ride, but the fourteen-year-old threw it into the shadows nearby anyway. As it cleared the streams of moonlight breaking through the trees, a pool of which the bros were standing in, a scuttling sound could be heard. Nash and Eddie froze. They weren't alone. When the chunk of meat hit the ground, all of it vanished except a string of gristle and an awkward bone. The Unknown were closer than they'd thought. "Eddie?" said Nash. Neither of them dared move. "What is it?" his brother answered. "If we get eaten-" "Don't think about it." Nash gulped and turned back to the bone. Their moonlight beam was smaller now. The Unknown were closing in on all sides.
