Chapter 9 Part 4:

 

"Ah... good times..." Harold grinned, leaning back on the bench.

 

"The rest of you doing the trials?" Kraken asked, attempting to partake in conversation.

 

"My team is." Harold said. "I'm doing the intelligence test, though I'm kinda upset I couldn't do the strength test."

 

"Don't brag." Will scolded him.

 

"Sorry."

 

"We're not." Said Walaby. "Our team didn't want to... why did yours, for that matter?"

 

"For fun." Harold shrugged. "Something to relieve the monotony. We'll probably try and get into the faction tournament as well."

 

"We were forced to by the headmaster." Revealed Ruby, a sly grin on her face. "He wanted to keep our team out of trouble after an... interesting first day."

 

"Wait, were you one of the teams involved in that fight on the first day?" Joy gaped at the trio.

 

"Yeah." Kraken smiled. "Just... sticking up for someone else and all that."

 

"Besides, those other jerks were Sentinel as well." Pointed out Ruby. "We didn't want to get a bad name, did we?"

 

"Well... you guys might have as a TEAM rather than a faction." Walaby said. "I'm pretty sure fight's already, like, spread into school lore."

 

There was a short silence.

 

"Well then." Kraken said. "That's problematic."

Chapter 10 Part 1:

 

"Well, here we go!" Ruby declared with a flourish to her teammates. "Best seats in the house!"

 

"I wouldn't know about that..." Plue fretted as the trio took their seats in the stadium, and Kraken would have to agree. The stadium itself was a very impressive structure, with seats climbing high into the sky and slowly lowering into an arena for combatants. It was similar to a coliseum in structure, and the fighting stage was made out of a simple metal floor. However, Intrepid's teammates were sitting at the very front of the stadium, with only a transparent energy shield seperating them from whatever action was going to take place.

 

"I hope this thing holds up." Kraken frowned, tapping the shield cautiously.

 

"I'm sure it'll be fine." Ruby said, though Kraken noticed she was looking slightly uncomfortable as she said this.

 

"Are you ok?" Kraken ventured.

 

"Yes, I'm fine." The redhead confirmed, looking more sure of herself as she spoke. Kraken shrugged and nestled into his seat.

 

A loud voice announced over the seaker system, "Hello, and welcome to the first round of the Nexus Force Academy Prowess Trials!"

 

"Try saying that three times with a mouthful of marbles." Kraken smirked. Plue giggled and Ruby shrugged with a grin when they heard him.

 

"Today, we will be testing canidate's aptitude and skill on the battlefield - broadcast for YOUR entertainment!" The announcer continued, and large plasma screens flickered into life, revealing the arena.

 

Ruby coughed into her fist - a cough that sounded suspiciously like the phrase "Sell-out".

 

"The trial our prowess canidates have today is organised combat! Groups of training enemies will spawn strategically throughout the arena, and the competitors will have to fight them off with a weapon of their choice! We have a grand total of twenty-five competitors fighting today, so be sure to give them all the support you can!"

 

"So one tenth of the teams in the school are participating..." Ruby murmered. "Interesting..."

"There are one thousand students here, and we have been gathered into teams of four - so, two hundred and fifty teams. Twenty five applicants means there will be twenty five teams in total, which is a tenth of two hundred and fifty." Plue filled in.

 

"Never mind." Kraken grimaced. "When's Intrepid rocking up?"

 

"I think there's a schedule around here somewhere..." Plue began looking around her seat. "You guys sure you aren't sitting on one?"

 

"Here you go." Kraken held his out, stoic.

 

"Thanks." Plue took the program and examined it, with Ruby being as polite as she could whilst peering over her shoulder. "Looks like Intrepid's last in line."

 

"Hah!" Kraken chuckled. "I guess the bonehead's happy with being the final act."

 

"You guys talking about your friend?" A voice behind the trio asked. Turning, Kraken saw Joy and Walaby claiming seats behind them.

 

"Oh, hey guys! Yeah, Intrepid has a flair for the dramatic." Plue explained.

 

"He was certainly taking his training seriously." Joy smirked as she recalled. "From what I've seen of him, the gym is like a second home to the guy."

 

"Well, no-one can say he lacks intensity." Ruby admitted.

 

"He might give Flynn a challange." Walaby agreed.

 

"Who?" Kraken queried. Walaby indicated to the program Plue was holding.

 

"The guy who's going first is a friend of ours." He said. "Lets just say... he's pretty good."

 

"Define PRETTY good." Challenged Kraken.

 

"You'll see soon enough." Joy said. "Looks like we're starting."

 

There was an almighty groan, and the metal floor of the stadium descended below the ground, leaving the middle of the coliseum with nought but a gaping hole. Several moments passed before it returned, except completely altered. Instead of a plain metallic floor, the battleground was now covered in what looked like ruins.

 

"The heck?" Gasped Walaby, as the crowd roared it's approval.

 

"I guess it's for variation." Kraken reasoned. "To keep things interesting?"
