Chapter 8 Part 2:

 

"Don't let the tip of the sword fall low. It give your opponent an opening." Kraken told Plue, who obediently raised her blade.

 

"Shift your legs slightly wider apart." He continued to command. "Make sure that your body is balanced evenly with the sword as your center of gravity. When in a fight, you must put as much of your weight behind the sword as you can without overbalancing yourself. It's all about finding a perfect balance, if you will.

 

"Okay..."

 

"And for goodness' sake, don't look at your sword as you hold it." Kraken ordered her suddenly. "How are you supposed to defend and attack if you're staring gormlessly at your own blade?"

 

"I have a quick question." Plue said.

 

"Fire away." Kraken shrugged.

 

"Do I have to use a REAL sword?!" Plue asked, her arms wobbling as she struggled to keep holding the large metal blade.

 

"Of course." Kraken declared. "Time is not on our side, so we're going to have to skip using any sort of training sword. Remember, this is stuff that you're technically already supposed to know. I think it's fair to say-"

 

Abruptly, the doors of the gym flew open and Intrepid sprinted in, skidding to a halt when he saw the two of them there.

 

"Oh, hey guys." He smirked. "I'm not interrupting anything, am I?"

 

"Nope." Kraken folded his arms. "We were practicing swordplay."

 

"Well, don't stop on my account." Intrepid shrugged as he grabbed onto a pair of straps hanging from the ceiling and began doing pull-ups. "I just need to train for my finesse trial."

 

"What will you have to do?" Plue asked.

 

"Finesse is just a fancy word for skill." Intrepid informed her. "I'll probably have to fight a whole bunch of training dummies with a stick or something."

 

"Do you fancy your chances?" Kraken asked him. Intrepid stopped doing pull-ups and looked at him, wiping his forehead as he did so.

 

"I'll have you know that, though I say so myself, I'm pretty darn good with a staff. If they let us choose our weapons, there's no doubt I'd be gunning for one of those."

Chapter 8 Part 3:

 

"A staff, of all weapons?" Plue queried. "Seems pretty uninteresting."

 

"That's why I like it." Intrepid explained. "A staff is both unassuming and versatile. If I really wanted to, I could pretend I needed a stick to walk around with and then suprise people by smacking them with it."

 

"That would be... interesting." She admitted. 

 

"That's a polite way of putting it." Kraken snarked.

 

"Well, what would YOU do if you were super skilled with a staff?" Intrepid proposed. Kraken thought for a moment, then shrugged.

 

"I'd not use my skills to attack unsuspecting innocents."

 

"You're Mr Self-rightous, you are." Intrepid told him, ceasing pullups and supporting himself on the wall, out of breath and energy. "You somehow manage to run the gannet of being disgustingly full of respect and yet have no respect at all."

 

"I reserve my respect for those I believe deserve it." Kraken told him, remaining steadfast. "It's very difficult to earn my respect once you know me personally."

 

"You toff." Intrepid scoffed. "I'll earn that respect of yours - just you watch."

 

"And how do you propose you'll do that?" Kraken asked him.

 

"By kicking whatever butt needs to be kicked during my challenge." Intrepid revealed.

 

"Well, we'll see what happens, won't we?" Kraken remarked.

 

"Exactly." Intrepid nodded.

 

"Well, good luck, anyhow." Kraken shrugged, holding out a hand. Intrepid shuffled towards him, trepidacious.

 

"How do I know this isn't a joke?" He asked, glancing warily at the hand.

 

"Just take it, you wuss." Kraken snapped. Intrepid obediently clasped onto Kraken's hand and shook it firmly.

 

"Thanks man." Intrepid grinned.

 

Just that moment, the doors to the polished wooden gym swung open again to reveal a boy and a girl in assembly clothing. The boy had hair the colour of sand and stormy grey eyes, while the girl's hair was purple, presumably as a result of dye. Her eyes were peculiar - one eye was the colour of the sun, whilst the other was a vivid emerald green.

Chapter 8 Part 4:

 

"Oh." The girl said as she saw Kraken, Plue and Intrepid talking and working. "We're not interrupting anything, am I?"

 

"Hey, that's what I said!" Intrepid grinned.

 

"No, we're good. It's not like this gym is ours." Kraken pointed out, trying to retain formality.

 

"Great to hear!" The boy flashed the trio a thumbs up. 

 

"I'm Joy, and the blonde is Walaby." The girl introduced.

 

"Pleasure to meet ya!" Intrepid declared. "You can call me Intrepid."

 

"For some reason he prefers an adjective to his own name." Kraken deadpanned in the background. "I'm Kraken, and that's Plue."

 

"Now why would you tell them that?" Intrepid whined.

 

"Oh, don't worry about it." Joy assured him. 

 

"Yeah, from the sounds of it you got to pick your own." Walaby pouted. "I should be so lucky."

 

"No need to fret, Wal." Joy told him. "So, what have you guys been doing in here anyway?"

 

"We've been training." Kraken declared bruesquely.

 

"Intrepid's taking part in the fitness trial in a few weeks, and I'd just like to improve." Plue explained.

 

"The fitness trial?" Joy and Walaby glanced at each other before as one turning to Intrepid.

 

"Good luck." Walaby shrugged.

 

"Huh?" Intrepid raised an eyebrow and sauntered over to the pair.

 

"A friend of ours is also taking part on behalf of the Venture League." Joy explained. "And... how do I put this... he's VERY good."

 

"Don't underestimate me." Intrepid told them. "I'm sure I can take him."

 

"Intrepid, don't get ahead of yourself." Plue reminded him. "We don't know what the trial is yet, it might not be person verses person."

 

"You know what I mean." Intrepid snapped.

 

"Ugh." Kraken groaned. "We should probably head back to our dorm."

 

"Yeah, I'm kinda out of energy." Plue confessed before stumbling.

 

"I guess I'll hang snake with these guys." Intrepid said. "Catch you later." 

 

"Yeah, hope to see you again!" Joy said, waving as Kraken and Plue walked out before turning to Intrepid.

 

"Come on handsome," Kraken heard her say, "let's see if you're as good as you say."

Chapter 9 Part 1:

 

"Tomorrow, eh?" Ruby grimaced, her voice almost lost amongst the natter of the mess hall.s

 

The weeks had flown by, and Kraken was suprised by how quickly routine had fallen into place. His teammates, too, seemed to be less and less annoying with each passing second, and he had made the aqaintance of several of his peers, such as Joy and Walaby.

 

"All we need is a Flynn and a Harold..." He muttered.

 

"Huh?" The sound came from Plue, who had paused with a spoon of food centimeters from her mouth.

 

"Nothing." Kraken dismissed his words. Ruby's eyebrow rose, but the girl shrugged and continued to speak.

 

"As I was saying... is the finesse challange really tomorrow?" 

 

"Yep." Plue nodded. "I had to convince Intrepid to not skive classes so he could train."

 

"He's taking this way too seriously." Kraken mused.

 

"I'd say that's in his nature." Ruby smiled.

 

"True." Kraken nodded, and continued to eat.

 

"Hey Plue!" A voice suddenly echoed across the clamour, and the trio ceased what they were doing to stare at the voice.

 

Two boys carrying trays laden with food purposely made their way over to the tabel that dorm 7 minus one were sitting. They both wore expressions of recognition and delight on their faces as they hastened over.

 

"Plue! You really ARE here!" One of them exclaimed as they sat down at the table. The speaker had light brown, eternally messy hair and an almost square face with eyes the colour of the ocean. The other boy had a mop of hair the colour of wet sand partially covered by a beige fedora hat. His amber eyes held a far less eccentric but no less warm joy.

 

"We didn't know that you had joined up along with us." The fedora-toting boy said, his calm and slightly monotonous voice a contrast to the deep yet vibrant voice of the other.

 

Ruby's brow rose once again.

 

"Friends of yours?" She asked.

 

"Indeed we are!" Brown hair dipped his head formally. "I'm Harold, and this is Will. Any friend of Plue's is a friend of ours, don't worry!"

 

Plue flushed crimson and turned away.
