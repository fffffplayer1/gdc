Chapter 12 Part 2:

 

Song Used: Mortal Sin (Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood OST)

 

"Recon for what? Who are you?" Kraken pressed, pointing an accusing finger at the figure, as they slowly walked past Kraken and ahead in the hallway.

 

"I'll spare you the details." They declared. "All I want to do... is ask you some questions."

 

"Is that so?" Kraken's finger twitched, and he instinctively moved his hand towards his waist before realising that he didn't have a sword by his side. "On what subject?"

 

"Dangarnon Village." They said simply.

 

The effect of the name was instantanious. Kraken's eyes widened, his teeth ground, and his fists clenched.

 

"W-what gave you the impression that I knew anything about that? I-"

 

"It's been three years." The figure interrupted Kraken, lifting their head to stare into Kraken's eyes with their own. "I'm curious as to how much you might or might not have forgotten."

 

"I haven't forgotten a THING." Spat Kraken, all pretence gone. "As a matter of fact, I remember it all too well."

 

"If that's the case, do you remember-"

 

"KRAKEN!"

 

The aforementioned young man turned his head, horrified, to see Ruby standing behind him, watching the pair as they conversed.

 

"Who is this guy?" She hissed, glaring at the figure. "And what's he doing here?"

 

"Ruby... no..." Murmered Kraken, whilst the figure remained emotionless.

 

"So... you've made friends, have you?" They said. "I take it that they don't know who you are?"

 

In a flash, the person was right behind Kraken, breathing into his ear.

 

"What you've DONE?!" They emphasised.

 

Kraken wheeled around, lashing out with his fist, but his assailant sprung backwards. 

 

"I haven't done anything!" He protested. "Who ARE you?"

 

"Someone who has been informed of your deeds, and who wants to take revenge on the behalf of the parties involved." The figure declared. "However, I must resist such an urge - our work is far from complete."

 

"What do you mean by "our"?" Demanded Ruby, stepping forward. "How many of you are there?"

Chapter 12 Part 3:

 

Song Used: Mortal Sin (Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood OST)

 

The figure's eyes held a look of disdain as they glanced towards Ruby.

 

"I do not want you here. Leave."

 

"What happens if I don't?" Ruby challenged, crouching down and raising her arms.

 

"Then I shall persuade you." The figure fell into a similar fighting stance. Kraken felt that he should say something, but before he could someone else interrupted.

 

"Number six! What do you think you're doing!"

 

*end soundtrack*

 

Instantly, the masked figure straightened, and when they spoke again, there was suprise in their voice.

 

"N-number one?" They gasped. "What are you doing here?!"

 

"I was checking on you to make sure that something like this hadn't happened." Another cloaked figure stepped forward. Whilst the attire was the same for the most part, their cloak and mask had purple highlights instead of red. "Six, why can't you restrain yourself for one lousy - oh." They paused when they caught sight of Kraken. "This the guy you've been going on about."

 

Number six nodded. "Yes sir. This is Kraken Rayne."

 

Number one studied Kraken through the mask, and Kraken saw that his eyes has black sclera and indego irises.

 

"Pleasure's all mine, Rayne. I've heard a great deal about both your family and you... I have to admit, I imagined you slightly taller."

 

"I appreciate the sentiment." Kraken's voice remained strong as the teen regained his composure. "Sorry to disappoint you - but then, I wasn't expecting you to exist, so I'd reckon we're even."

 

"Agreed. I hope you can forgive my wayward companion." One turned to Six. "We were supposed to be carrying out this operation in secret... still, no use crying over spilt beverage."

 

"What kind of operation are you planning, anyway?" Ruby interjected. One turned to her.

 

"Nothing that concerns any of you. You can report if you want, but there's really no point. You won't find anything."

 

"One!" Six hissed. "Don't tell them so much!"

Chapter 12 Part 4:

 

Number One shrugged as his comrade berated him.

 

"Six, you worry too much. I doubt master will disapprove of my actions at present... we have nothing to fear."

 

"What about the fact that we're willing to stop whatever you're planning?" Shot Ruby.

 

"Yeah, who's your "master", anyway? What's the meta game? Who do you think you are?" Kraken presented the questions to them one after the other. One laughed behind the mask.

 

"You two are very curious, aren't you? Well, whilst I can't reveal everything - that's something you will doubtlessly have to figure out on your own - there is something I CAN tell you. inform anyone in a higher position than you - your teachers, officers, your headmaster - about any of this, and you'll pay dearly. In fact, don't tell anyone about this. And don't think I won't know if you spill the beans, because I will."

 

"You're a coward." Spat Kraken. "Staying in the shadows like this. What are you after?"

"All in good time, Rayne." Number One waved, before snapping his fingers. In the hallway behind them appeared a swirling violet vortex. "But for now, I belive we need to leave. Enjoy your day, children!" With that, he stepped through the portal. Number Six turned to look at Kraken, who was struck by the now completely unfiltered anger in their eyes.

 

"Don't think that we won't have our discussion." They said. "You'd better be ready, because the next time you're alone, I'm coming, And we're going to have a long talk about everything that's happened."

 

"I'll keep that in mind." Kraken glowered at him, folding his arms. "Right now, you should probably take off before anyone else comes along."

 

Each person stared into each other's eyes for a while longer, before Number Six stepped through the portal after Nubmer One. The thing then evapourated, leaving Ruby and Kraken alone in the hallway.

 

Neither of them spoke for a good long while.

 

"Any idea what that was about?" Ruby eventually asked. Kraken shook his head.

 

"I wish I knew, Ruby. I wish I knew."

Chapter 13 Part 1:

 

It may have seemed unprofessional to anybody else, but Phyrra was woken from her slumber by a run-of-the-mill alarm clock going off. Despite the stakes, she still slapped the device and rolled over, mumbling to herself. And it was only when her husband, Conway, gave her a tentative poke did she sit up and blink.

 

"Is it time to go already?" She mumbled as she rose and began to dress.

 

"You know it is." Conway scolded her, though gently. "You've been stressing out about this for months - you couldn't forget that you had to go today if you wanted to."

 

"Believe me, I want to." Phyrra groaned, remaining casual. "But I've got to do my job, right? I kinda want to retire, and settle down."

 

Conway snorted through his nose. "The Force wouldn't let you retire - not while you're still fit and whole."

 

"Good point." Admitted Phyrra. "I'll just have to keep at it until I'm old and grey, right?"

 

"Until the war's over." Corrected Conway.

 

"You make it sound like it's lasted for ages. A few years isn't so long. Besides, it's not that big a difference. I slew dangerous creatures all the time - the only difference is the aesthetic and the whole hive mind aspect."

 

"I wish you hadn't made such a big name for yourself. Then perhaps the force wouldn't have hired you."

 

"And then where would we be? Before that, we were struggling to get by. Now we have a safe place to live for us and our family - who could want more?"

 

"A bunch of people."

 

"But not us, right?" Phyrra put the last remnants of her armour on and turned to Conway.

 

"Well, I can't speak for you, but I know I'M happy." Her husband confirmed. Phyrra smiled.

 

"That's all I needed to hear. Come on, I'd best be off."

 

The pair walked out into the corridor and towards the front door, still conversing.

 

"And you're sure you don't want to say goodbye to the children before you go?" Conway asked. "I'm sure they'd appreciate a chance to say goodbye."

 

"I'm afraid that if I looked at them, I wouldn't be able to make myself leave." Replied Phyrra.

Chapter 13 Part 2:

 

Song Used: One Is All, All Is One (Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood OST)

 

"But I think you should at least-" Conway paused mid-sentence, gasping. Phyrra turned to follow his gaze and saw two of her children standing in the hallway.

 

"I-Ivy! Norik! What are you two doing up so early!" She exclaimed, pulling off an air of carefreeness.

 

"Well... we heard you guys get up, and we wanted to see what was happening." Ivy admitted, fooling around with a lock of her jet black hair. "Besides, you can't leave yet."

 

"Oh? Why not?"

 

"Norik wants to show you something." Ivy gave Norik a tentative shove, and the young child stumbled forward - he clutched something in his hand.

 

"Huh? What is it, sweetie?" Phyrra inquired softly. Her youngest child looked at the ground and swayed on his feet.

 

"Come on, Norik, honey, don't be shy... show me what you've done."

 

"I...I... ta-da!" With a flourish, Norik revealed that the object in his hands was a crumpled piece of paper. Phyrra and Conway peered at the art for a closer inspection. On the paper were a pair of crudely drawn stick figures, one significantly taller than the other. Both of them had goofy large smiles on their faces, and around the drawing were the words "mommy is my hero".

 

"I drew it all last night." Norik declared with a cheeky grin. "When I grow up, I want to be just like you so we can go on adventures together!"

 

Without saying a word, Phyrra envloped both of her children in a hug.

 

"Mommy?"

"Mom?"

 

"Don't worry about a thing." Phyrra managed to choke. "We'll always be together, all five of us."

 

"Is that a super special promise?" Ivy managed to ask, half-joking.

 

"Of course." Phyrra said, greatful for the way that hugs hid faces. "I'll be with you for as long as you want me."

 

Norik laughed. "Silly mommy! We'd never not want you!"

 

"I know, baby. I know." Phyrra acknowledged. "Have I told you that I love you today?"

 

"Um... no. It's really early." Ivy pointed out.

 

"Well, then... kids, I love you." 

 

"We love you too, mommy!"

Chapter 13 Part 3:

 

"Glad to hear that." Phyrra smiled through her tears. "Will you promise to treat your dad and each other well while I'm gone?"

 

"You've got it, mom. Don't worry about us." Ivy reassured her mother.

 

"Okay." Phyrra rose, hiding her face from her children. "I'll see you later, Conway."

 

"Good luck." Murmered Conway as he embraced his wife.

 

"Thank you." Phyrra turned and almost fled out of the door, but managed to restrain herself  and walked casually down the front path, waiting for the sound of her family shutting the door behind her to run out of sight of the house. Eventually she reached such a location, and sat down on the ground, breathing heavily.

 

"Hello mother." A voice to her left said. Turning around, she saw her oldest son step out of the bushes.

 

"Oh, hello Kraken." She greeted the boy. "I guess you're awake too."

 

"I figured you'd try to leave without saying goodbye." The eight-year old admitted, rubbing the back of his head. 

 

"Well, you're not wrong - just bad luck that your siblings have sensitive ears."

 

Kraken laughed, and Phyrra smiled.

 

"I know I've said this before, but you're very mature for your age." She informed him.

 

"Well, that's life, I guess. I just see things in a different light to everybody else."

 

"Make sure that you still appreciate the viewpoints of others, Kraken Rayne." Phyrra scolded. "When I get back, I don't want to hear any horror stories of your insensitivity, understand?"

 

"Got it." Kraken shrugged. "But you have to admit, those two can be annoying sometimes."

 

"You mean your siblings? I refuse to belive you know the meaning of the word "annoying" until you've been a parent. All my children - yes, even YOU - have been so annoying sometimes I've wished I could just press a button and mute you."

 

"Much appreciated."
