A Star Alone

Chapter 1

I am a warrior, and I need no one. I stand alone. At least thats what I thought until that fateful day.

My name is Vinara. I am a level three paradox sorcerer with a Doomslicer. I have no friends, and I never team. I am alone, and I like it that way. I am one of the greatest fighters for the Nexus Force, and I have completed all the missions. But enough of that, here is my tale:

The ground rumbled beneath my feet, as a storm of purple blotted out the sun. I turned to the other minifigures; they were huddled against walls, trying to hide as best they could from fear itself. I looked back at the storm, and closed my eyes, trying to block it all out of my mind. Then I heard the cry ‘to arms brave heroes, to arms!’ from the faction leaders. It was the final battle, the battle to decide to fate of our world. It like the flip of a coin; one side darkness, one side light. I waved my hand and my Doomslicer appeared in it. With another flick of my wrist, I disappeared down to the surface of Crux. Crux looked even worse than it had before. Instead of occasionally seeing purple, it was now the black streaks of charred earth that were sparingly visible. The Maelstrom had almost completely eaten away at Crux Prime. Soon it would move to the other places, crushing all imagination that was left in the universe. I readied my weapon, preparing for whatever was to come next. I glanced at the other minifigures around me; they all tried to look brave, but were really trembling with fear behind their acts. An eerie silence hung in the air as the storm came closer. Then suddenly, in the blink of an eye, the storm was right in front of me. I could see into it, and what I saw scared me so much, that I wanted to hide away with everyone else. Because behind the storm I saw... Nothing. No hordes of charging enemies, no big Maelstrom beast; just an empty plane of terror. As everyone around me saw this, they tried to escape; to hide from the darkness. But they all knew inside, that no one can run from the Maelstorm.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Chapter 1, tell me what you think! There will be a new part every week (I hope).

Embrace the Illusions

Chapter 11, The Ghost Orb

“Endless... Crux Prime is endless.” I muttered to myself. While there may have been no sun, Crux Prime was still very humid. The ED had been walking for over a week now, toward where we thought the Maelstorm was headed. So far, we hadn’t come across any other bases, but I figured we wouldn’t. Our food source was waning, and there was nothing to eat or drink in sight. As we trudged across the gravel-like surface, the heat made us all see things. The only one that seemed immune to anything was Dashboard, but that was to be expected since he was a ghost. The first thing I saw was a white boat, similar to the one the mythrans used. It seemed to be sailing on the sand, but when we came closer, it disappeared. Sharpie got a bit excited when he saw a huge ball of lightning floating in the air; sadly, it turned out to be nothing.

It seemed to take eons to walk across crux. With our supplies lower then before, everyone had their turn at complaining. Nerves were frayed, and the whole group seemed on edge; it was easy to lash out at anyone.
“This is kinda boring,” Melian said one day “I was expecting to be battling something, not trudging across a desert.” “Well-” Brocktree started to reply, but I cut him off; this time Melian had annoyed me too much. “So sorry to hear that,” I said sarcastically “ Did you ever think we might need to find the Maelstorm before we could battle it?” “Maybe we could have just used the rocket launchers back at the IFB, instead of running off and trying to look like heroes.” She snapped back. All I could do was glare at her, because I knew she was right; we had made a silly mistake. Neither of us said another word until the sky started to get darker than before. After deciding where to camp, we all dropped our backpacks, and started setting up. Dinner consisted of Sandbrick Soup, and some Hard-Tack Bread. I ate my dinner in a hurry, and walked over to the front of the camp; it was my turn to guard. Guarding was the most boring job, because nothing ever tried to attack. I just sat there for half an hour.... An hour... Two hours... It never seemed to end. By now everyone else had retired to their tents, and were fast asleep. I yawned and glanced around; nothing seemed out of place. Then suddenly something strange caught my eye. A white orb, like the one I had seen back in the cave, was hovering several meters in front of me. This of course piqued my curiosity, and I walked over to it. Reaching out my hand, I tried to touch the orb, but it floated away before I could. It stopped a short distance away from me, almost like it was waiting for me to follow. I broke into a run, and chased the orb for what felt like a long time. Suddenly it stopped, causing me to stumble with the abruptness. Looking up, I saw something quite startling and out of place.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The mini-screen 'beeped', and an arrow appeared, pointing the way to the encampment. I turned around to face the launch pad, and pulled out my Doomslicer, Darklied. In one swift movement, I stabbed it into the surface, creating a jagged crack. I then dragged Darklied though the ground, which was surprisingly easy, since the ground was solid rock. Dragging the blade through the ground, I headed back toward the encampment. As I continued walk the sky started to change, the darkness turning into a lighter shade of shadowy purple. About an hour later, I was standing on a sand dune that overlooked the ED's campground. Melian was already awake and waiting for me.
