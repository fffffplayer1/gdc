Down in the residential area of Nimbus Station, a ways away from the fighting, a grizzled man in a black overcoat watches the colorful proceedings agasint a backdrop of the setting sun.  “Back at it again, so soon. They’ll never stop. Never cease beating the life out of each other, until one gives up. But it’s been so many years, I doubt they will stop anytime soon.”

 

“Yes, you’ve paraphrased that at least three times today. We understand.” A black haired youth rolls out from under a battered old car. Once, it might have been a majestic sight, rolling down the boulevard, but today it is scratched, dented, the windshield has a crack, the and bumper is off balance.

 

“But I dwell on it. One can only hypothesise about how many poor souls have been run afoul by such destructive wars, and who’s next to get the short end of the stick.”

 

“Coming from you? That’d be hilarious, if not so grim. Pass me the 1/4th?” The youth points to an open toolbox on a nearby table.

 

“The irony is not lost on me.” He fumbles around in the toolbox. “Which one is that again?”

 

“The littlest blue one. It’s also labeled.”


“You know that I know that you know that I don’t know about these things.” He extracts the 1/4th wrench, and squints at the faded white lettering on the side. “So it is. Catch.” The man tosses the tool to the youth under the car, who catches it without looking, and rolls back under the car to continue repairs without a word.
