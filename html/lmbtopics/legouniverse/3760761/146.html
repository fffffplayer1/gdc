DragonFire-1 wrote:

     Bane is not just claiming. He is.

It's a matter of perspective, my dear Watson. I've been called the baddest of the bad, but I've not lifted a finger against anyone in months.  I think Krill's a great guy, providing a vital service. What defines 'good' and 'evil', anymore, anyway? One might say that darkness is bad. But Darkness is Not Evil, darkness is a thing, a state of being. And Bane, who says he fights for good, has used 'the powers of darkness'.

Or you might say that one's alignment is determined by their actions. But my character has been, up until very recently, not been acting under his own will. So do his actions, from before, when he was not acting of his own will, count? And Bane has done many things commonly declared as 'evil', but he denies and justifies them as acts of 'good'.

Or perhaps the things you use, your tools, your weapons, are what defines your alignment. In that case, my character uses Dark Imagination, which one might say, is obviously evil and dark and scary. To which I would reply, it's about as 'evil' as fire. Nothing about it, by itself, is evil. What it has done, though, has affected lots for the worse. Which is to say nothing about what it might do in the future, or anyone who might control it. It's powerful, and has great ability to take, or to give. I could use fire to burn something down, or to heat homes and provide power. I could use DI to create an army of minions, and powerful weapons. Or I could use it to effectively heal wounds and create wonderful technology. DI is little more than a very advanced tool. Who's using it, and who's being used, remains to be seen.

 

But what makes someone 'good' or 'evil'? If you were to ask me, I would tell you I think there is no morality meter, no way to tell at a glance, but what they want to do, and the consequences thereof, should be looked at before anything else.

 

(Deep rant over, carry on.)
