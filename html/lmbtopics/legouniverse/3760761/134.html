Shipmaster, damage report!

 

Clone: Sir, Oblivion's reappearance is a problem due to the concussive forces involved; Harmony is leaking atmosphere and its fuel cell is about to go critical, Dawn's primary cannons are down and the ship is rapidly losing shield systems, but Freedom was able to escape damage by being above the atmosphere. The best tactical option is to use the ship as a weapon now; Harmony won't make it back to dry dock safely.

 

Got it. Harmony, all crew, abandon ship. Get yourselves out of there.

 

*Pods, gunships, and every single flight-capable transport or jetpack-equipped object proceeds to begin dropping or flying towards the Balancer defensive perimeter surrounding the Tower from the failing ship, as well as my team of Spartans and myself with jump packs leaping off Oblivion.*

 

*The Harmony suddenly powers up its FTL drive by a remote control, heading straight for the Oblivion at near-light speeds with a shield hardened to nearly maximum power.*

 

May our vessel be remembered. Everyone, get clear.

 

*The ship proceeds to crash at these speeds into the Oblivion, right before its critical reactor explodes with a force of unimaginable magnitude, as the entire area is bathed in a blindingly bright flash.*

 

All units, fire at will. Bring that ship down now! Citadel Actual, fire the Imagination cannons. We will have our vengeance for the loss of a warship of such majesty.

-Storm

OoC:

P.S. That was an antimatter reactor. Normally, a small portion of its power is enough to fuel the whole craft and its exemplary shields, but detonating the whole reactor at once is a power that is so great that Bane has forbidden its use in a warhead unless Omega itself is threatened. In other words, if a large section of the hull of the Oblivion is not smoking ash and the shields are not down after such an attack, I would say the Oblivion is more powerful than is fair.
