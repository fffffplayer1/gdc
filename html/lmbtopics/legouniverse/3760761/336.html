As Sparrowhawk slumped to the floor again, the Crystal Roses throughout the tower ceased their onslaught. With remarkable finesse, the turned instead to fixing up the damage they had caused. Quickly, injuries were mended, walls rebuilt, and general order restored.

 

That is, if you could call tattooing advertisements of various musical talents onto foreheads and the addition of rock band posters to the hallowed halls of Nexus Tower "order"...

 

Dantès smiled. Her work was done here. She split off into several alternates and beckoned the Crystal Roses back to herself. The formed a rough phonebox-sized rectangular prism on the floor in front of her.

 

"I thank you all for your time and effort. It is time I am off now!"

 

"Quite right, me. Adventures await."

 

Her hair leaps out into fiery red curls as a tuxedo settles onto her person and a walking stick leaps to her hand. The alternates slowly file into the box, each one grinning wildly and vanishing back into her original personage.

 

"If anyone needs me, I'll be heading home! Give me a ring. Ya'll know my number now, right?"

 

With that, the brief reign of terror ends. The Crystal Rose construct blasts off through the roof, leaving behind a large crater on the floor that looked like a rough approximation of David Bowie.

OOC: 

 

Explaination for Dantés, eh?

 

The character of Dantés was an experiment for me, really. In my roleplaying past, I've always used reasonable good guys and gals. For once, I decided to blur the lines a bit and play a truly insane character!

 

Dantés was a test subject and researcher from the ancient Brick-Tech Arms and Armaments. Thanks to an experiment gone wrong, she had her collective consciousness spread over several universes. The Dantés you saw played here was simply one of many forced into a single body.

 

Before Sparrowhawk provided the "trigger event" that allowed her to pull her collective consciousnesses together, Dantés was constantly swapping between selves, as it were. Think of it as having a thousand or so different versions of you living in one head, all thinking at the same time, all trying to do what they want to do. Rather painful sounding, no?

 

After Sparrowhawk tells Dantés that she will never get home to Brick-Tech, all of her various selves within her head are given enough motivation through their anger and sorrow to work together for once. In having this happen, Dantés is able to pull alternate versions of herself out from different universes to fight with her. These alternates each have their own powers. As you saw in the fight, we had nearly omnipotent angels, musicians, telelkinetics, among others!

 

Now that Sparrowhawk is smashed (even be it temporarily), the multiple Dantés are off running about through this Universe. What does that mean for the future? I have no idea! I'd like to bring Dantés back at some point. We shall see what that entails...

 

Thanks for reading!

 

Cheers,
