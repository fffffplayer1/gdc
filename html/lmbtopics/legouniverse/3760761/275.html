Ooc: The neutrality declaration was OoC? Sorry, I must have missed that; I thought that post was an extension of the first one's IC section. My mistake.

 

I am pleased, though, that my suspicion of an illusion was correct. While it was not a bomb, I had assumed that it would be; otherwise, why send an illusion to do a minifigure's work? Now that I think about it, a scientist is the perfect counter to an Illusionist; because they are skeptics by nature, they do not trust what they see without proof, and thus no illusion can become real enough to harm them. They can be frightened by the illusion, but not harmed as easily. Speaking of which, what can pierce an Illusion? Is there any instrument or weapon that can make it easy for the user to detect a fake image or sensation? If not, then it could be argued that it is somewhat overpowered; what if an Illusionist were to simply put an entire HQ to sleep? Or, even worse, what if he/she tricked everyone into thinking their ships were hostiles? What exactly are the limits of an illusion?

-Bane
