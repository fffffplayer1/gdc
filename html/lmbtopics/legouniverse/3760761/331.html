‎02-22-2015 01:02 PM

In the Paradox labs, presumably a lower level than the chaos happening in the tower, a DI Vortex pops into existence in front of two startled Paradox sentries. It deposits a labcoated woman directly in front of their feet. 

 

The two guards look at the woman, then at each other, then at the woman again.

 

"What in Crux's na-"

 

Before the guard could finish his idiom, the Vortex deposited more people.

 

A longcoat-wearing person with long spiky brown hair and a small beard floated out, completely oblivious of the guards, heading straight for the work stations at the far end of the room. Then came a cloaked fellow wearing runic armor and a long staff stepped over the edge of the portal, followed by a cheery man wearing a rather extravagent feathered hat and had a glowing mark on his forehead. Following that came fifteen soldiers armed to the teeth with outlandish weaponry, who proceeded to disarm the two guards. Then there was the entourage of horrifying creatures made of flesh and metal, and a gaggle of little metal spheres. All of these newcomers stepped over the first woman who still lay sprawled on the floor. 

 

The guards were so awestruck that it didnt register that this was a serious security breach. Then they realized they had been disarmed, their grenades, rifles, and any other weapon taken by the soldiers. 

 

Realizing the attempt was futile, they turned to the first man who had come through the Vortex, Sparrowhawk.

 

"You... you can't really be in... here." they said half-heartedly.

 

Instantly the memory of Sparrowhawk's exchange with Akia was slammed, not very gently, into the guards minds.

 

"Yes I can. Now unless you have something useful to offer me, go help your buddies outside. Looks like their getting their bricks handed to them out there. Go, shoo. Hov, tell your guys to give them their stuff back."

 

Sparrowhawk said all this while floating various machine bits in front of his face and assembling them.

 

"Oh, and bring me the PAHIAD. We should probably fix that first."  

~Sparrowhawk
Outbound Flight of the Swords of Light
SoL Historian

Sparrowhawk whirls his head around as the Dantès' begin screaming. He takes into account the seven identical women chasing him, and begins a calculation. 

 

As the electrical impulses flash rapid-fire back and forth in his head, kept from burning out by a stasis field of DI, he begins to reach a conclusion. Brick-Tech Arms and Armanments product descriptions #197 and #201 flash through his head, as does what he already knew about Dantès, and his knowledge of psychic connections and multiversal physics. 

 

The conclusion he reached was a rather nasty one. 

 

He creates an expanding cylindrical DI Construct around his body and wills it foward. It slams into the seven assailants, buying him some time to begin preparing a coup de grâce. As he does this, he springs up in the air, and red blades form on his wrists, slightly warping the space around them. 

 

"Okay then. If I'm right, which I usually am, come get me."

Dantès dispels the alternates as the DI wall slams into them. Before they vanish, each alternate slams the wall with magnificent force, somehow sending a reverberation through the construct and shattering it. The resulting blast sweeps any near it off their feet. Anything not bolted down is hurtled through the walls like rocks through glass. Not impeded, Original!Dantès proceeds with her assault.

 

"We are she who is named Dantès. My feet shall walk the width of the Blessed Earth once more. My mission is your end through my own beginning. Ascending, we shall rally against the Enemy. They shall rise up. And the Enemy shall fall. I am Dantès. I RISE."

 

The rampant screaming increases as the first Dantès continues hurtling towards the now armed Sparrowhawk. Her own blades materialize as her countenance shifts dramatically to a battle scared and far grimmer looking one.

 

An enormous claymore flicks forward, perilously thrumming through the very air as though the blade possessed a life all its own. Dantès let loose another bellow as she hurled the sword; it broke loose into hundreds of impossibly smaller fragments, each seeking its way towards their unfortunate target.

 

She splits herself into a dozen new alternates, each armed with another sword. They take up fighting stances and spread out, wary of another wall attack.

 

"WE ARE." They chant, their voices warbling together into a cacophony of dissonance, "WE ARE the symmetry. WE ARE the dancers upon the edge of forever and now. Alone, WE ARE many. An army unto ourselves. WE ARE the Angels of Dance. Angels of Music. Angels of Destruction. Amen and amen."
