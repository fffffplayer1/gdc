Bucky takes a seat overlooking the galaxy map, and watches the invasion unfold.

 

The outer outposts fall, one by one, their representative lights blinking out.

 

The interesting thing is that no transmissions come out of the outposts before contact is lost. From this, it can be inferred that they’re getting destroyed before they realize anything, or all outgoing transmissions are being blocked.

 

Long range energy scanners can detect large masses of what can only be spaceships of some kind, but they are not like anything you’ve ever seen before. You only detect small, quick moving ships. Nothing larger than a tractor trailer. But the scanners are taken out before long too.

 

An hour passes, and despite all measures taken, nothing can be done. All bases outside of a certain radius of NT go dark. At last, they seem to back off.

 

Bucky approaches Rex.

 

“This stage of their plan has gone off without a hitch, it seems. We are now surrounded on all sides by a force we cannot hope to fight. I recommend that you get in touch with anyone you think could be helpful, be they ally or enemy. It will take more than what you’ve got. They’re not going to come for us here for a while yet, I’m sure.”

Meanwhile, in uncharted space:

 

*The Defiant, the Peregrine, and the Hawkeye are at a rezendous point and a meeting between the Rangers and the Paradox is going on.*

 

"So the ruse worked?"

 

"It went well. I expect that my disappearance will cause no lack of courage among the enemies of the Nexus Force."

 

"Do you really think that faking the disappearance of the Defiant was the best way to go about doing this?"

 

"It was about as good as a chance as I was going to get."

 

*Scott leans in and pauses for a moment...*

 

"Now that the Maelstrom is gone and the Void is in a total state of disrepair, a massive power vacuum has appeared. That's why it was critical that I disappeared. With me gone, every single anti-NF organization will begin operating more frequently."

 

A blast door opens up, and a calm though concerned looking commander walks in.

 

"I think you need to see this... Right away."

 

The commander activates the holographic map.

 

*Scott observes for a few moments, slightly panicked.*

 

"There goes the asteroid base at Montas, the minefield at Tyre..."

 

Eris puts her helmet back on and stands up.

 

"Well. It looks like you certainly drew your enemies out."

 

"Barcha. Those clones of Barcha. This has to be related to them. Wait a second, they've stopped. They've stopped just outside the orbit of the Ninjago Monastery."

 

Everyone goes to battlestations, though the cloaking device is not disabled.

 

"Well then. What now, Scott?"

OOCIn response to DunamisKid)

 

I'm not sure all of you quite understand what I'm trying to do here. I'll explain it.

 

Since RP has been getting dull, I’ve decided to go and organize another plot to spice things up. In this case, I’m using the word plot to describe the sort of planned story we’re putting into effect here. Because that’s just it. Roleplay is about story, and we’re really low on that around here.

 

So we’ve created these ships. This race, this invading force. And set them on the roleplay here, in these last few posts of mine. The idea is that through the creation of challenges, you lot will have to use your noggin to solve them, and come up with some creative story type stuff. That would be a step up from the pointless puttering about that tends to happen.

 

So it sort of defeats the point if you just sort of create a nameless, random guy with a huge fleet that can, and has held back the invading forces.

 

I do realize that I created a huge fleet and did take out a lot of NF outposts without actually battling. I realize it sounds kind of hypocritical of me to say this. But I think I’m justified with the whole story thing. Turbo, who’s responsible for those outposts, was down with it. He said before hand that he was cool with the whole story thing, and if he had any objections, I would revert my moves.

 

However, just to sort of do what you did, without any foreshadowing or asking, just magic in a random fleet, I don’t think that’s okay. I’d like to ask you to revert those moves, and I think others will agree. It sort of derails everything we’ve prepared, and if everything is to be derailed, I’d at least like a little bit of effort put into it, you know?

 

So I’m going to go ahead and ignore your last move, DunamisKid, concerning the mysterious arrival of ‘several fleets of ships’. I hope you understand.
