Chapter 3, Part 1.

Trayor growled as he blasted a stromling to bits. It had happened recently. The plan had gone all wrong.
Bam!
He shot his dual rocket launcher at an admiral, but it ducked and it hit a horseman off his ride instead. The horse galloped away somewhere else.
It had started when they were in the cave. They had been waiting behind the barricade when some of the soldeirs got hungry.
"Okay, um..." Shadowshocker, their weapons specialist, had said. "Anyone got food?"
He was a rank 2 Buccaneer who was great at modifying things, particularly machinery.
Some of the people had donated some hot dogs, apples, and drumsticks, but there hadn't been enough for everyone. So, since they knew that Triks had found Heimlich Stewblaster on his way to Zeta Point, Traylor thought they could ask him to pick up some food.
Traylor had picked up his walkie-talkie with Triks. But when he hit the on button, a piano had flown from nowhere and crashed in to a pirate.
"Wrong one," Traylor said, rmembering he had brought the one he had gotten from Mr. Ree. Then he picked up the right one.
"Triks," he said. "Come in! Triks!"
"Read you loud and clear," said Triks. His voice sounded so... ordinary. There was nothing particular about it, really.
"Good. We need you to stop by Stewblaster's on the way back because--"
"Hang on, I need to do--Arrghh!" Triks screamed.
"Triks! Come in!" Traylor yelled into the walkie talkie. But then there was nothing but a buzzing sound.
Just then, the barricade gave way, crushing a few of the soldiers, including Shadowshocker, as the Maelstrom poured in. The recruits screamed till they began to run out of breath.
"Somebody help them! The rest of us will hold off the Maelstrom!" Traylor shouted. A few recrouits began to lift the pieces off of the trapped soldiers.
And that's how Traylor got here, fighting for his life.

Chapter 3, Part 3.

Traylor knew that they had to get out of here and find Rith. He aimed his blaster and shot a ronin, who fell back and died instantly.
Then he turned around and saw some horsemen carrying his men away as prisoners.
Traylor was about to attack the horsemen and get the soldiers back, but then he thought of a better idea.
He ran over to Ry.
"Get the team to slip out of here and follow the horsemen who took some of the guys prisoner!" Traylor shouted to her. She nodded and told everyone, one by one. Soon Traylor and Ry were the only ones left.
"Let's go," he said.
They followed the horsemen for about half an hour. It was boring and tiring. The seven men they had left were starving and sleepy.
"Are we almost done?" whined Volketor, a rank 1 Summoner.
"i have know idea--wait! I see something!" Traylor cried. Everyone scrambled to their feet and ran over.
"What is that thing?" asked Trollo, a rank 2 Samurai.
"I think, it's...oh, gosh no..." Traylor gasped.
Some, including Ry, stared in disbelief. Others hung their heads in discouragement.
They were looking at the Darkitect's new, better base: the Maelstrom infected Nexus Tower.
The minifigs had lost their most important base to the Maelstrom.

Chapter 4, Part 1.

Traylor slowly turned around to face the others.
"We have lost," said Nitewatch, a Mantis ninja with a black hood and pants.
"We have only lost this battle," Traylor said encouragingly. "We haven't lost the war."
"Then what do we do now?" cried Trollo. "We're outnumbered, have no base, no food, little supplies, and little men!"
"You guys can all give up, then!" yelled Traylor furiously. "I don't care! But I'm not gonna sit here and watch them enjoy themselves. Other teams can give up, for all I care, but my team will fight until we rot!"
The men exchanged looks among themselves. After much whispering, a rank 3 Sorcerer named Zolzar stepped up to Traylor.
"I am leaving this stupid, suicidal team," he said quietly but audibly. "Any others who would like to join me can." And he turned around and left the way he came.
Trollo was the first to follow, then Volketor, then everyone else. All except Ry.
Traylor turned around as the last team member left. He sighed as he looked up at the Maelstrom Tower. Chunks of it were missing. It was colored black and purple.
"How do we get out of here?" Ry asked him. "Our team's gone, so we can't fight the Darkitect or anything."
"The only way to Nimbus is through the launch pad in the old Nexus Tower. We can sneak in and fly to Nimbus," Traylor said.
"Oh yeah, I meant to ask you something," Ry said.
"What?"
"It's been bothering me since we started. Where's Rith?"

* * *

Rith trudged on through the bottom of Caldera Mar. The Maelstrom was being temporarily held back from here, but he still couldn't help but to feel he was walking into a deadly trap.
Rith spotted a bouncer and ran over to it. He just wanted to get out of here. He bounced on it and it sent him upwards into the place where Tralyor and his team were.
He looked around and saw that there was only a sigle stromling here.
Good, he thought. They must have cleared out the rest.
He ran towards the dropship crash without the stromling noticing. He sneaked around it and went in the cave to find...nothing. They had left.
Rith moaned and looked outside. The stromling was directing something down form the sky. It looked like bone cages. They weren't Maelstrom.
Rith watched as they crashed into the ground. One hit the dropship hard. It flew away from the cave, revealling him.
Then out of the flying cages came hundreds of skeletons. Some had pick axes, some had blasters, and some had bone axes. They all landed and the stromling left.
The ones with pick axes began working by swing ing at the rock surrounding him. But it didn't take long for their overseer to find him.
He saw Rith and shouted a command in Skelenese. Two miners quickly grabbed him and brought him over. Rith knew it was hopeless and didn't even struggle.
"Well, well, well," the overseer said. "Look what we have here."

Chapter 4, Part 2.

The Darkitect sat at his throne in the Maelstrom core. Nexus Tower was captured, but he wondered what he should do with the imagination. He had decided to hold back from infecting it and sent his mechs to study it. So far every one of them had burned up into purple flames because it had been to strong for them. The Darkitect too felt pain whenever he neared it. His throne was jst far enough to where the core didn't damage him. He sat here thinking about it.
He needed to study it to see if, instead of making more Maelstrom, he could also make stronger ones. Yes, that would make him unconquerable.
The skeleton overseer, by the name of Nuckal, casually walked in.
"My minions have arrived," he hissed to the Darkitect.
"Good," the Darkitect said.
"Where's the weapons?" Nuckal asked. His head was far bigger than the average skeleton's, so he had trouble looking up. He usually looked at the Darkitect's feet because of the Darkitect's size. He was five times the size of a normal minifigure.
"I will get them soon."
"You and I both know that we skeletons are only working for you for those...weapons," Nuckal said, obviously not wanting the Darkitect to know why he wanted the weapon.
"You will get your weapons."
"We need them now."
"Do you want them at all?" the Darkitect threatened. "You and your minions are nothing but filthy bounty hunters."
"You and your minions are scum," Nuckal spat bravely. "Do you want our help or not?"
"I will get your help, but will you get your weapons?"
The Darkitect made a signal to his stromlings and they grabbed hold of Nuckal's arms and legs. Nuckal lashed out, kicking and flailing his arms away, freeing him from their grasps.
Nuckal saw no reason for killing, but drew his bone ax anyways and sliced them both in half with a single, precise blow.
He turned to face the Darkitect. The Darkitect smiled crazily and simply kicked him with his oversized foot.
Nuckal landed painfully on the hard floor and skidded extremely close to the imagination.
The Darkitect laughed, but then realized that the imagination wasn't affecting Nuckal.
"You skeletons are immune to the affects of imagination?"
"Well, we never knew imagination had affects, so yeah, I guess," said Nuckal getting up slowly. "Why?"
"I will give you twice as powerful weapons if you could do me a favor."
A grim smile appeared on Nuckal's pale white face. "Nuckal at your service."

Chapter 4, Part 3.

An admiral walked on to a launch pad area to take a break from working. He sighed and stopped. Working was very hard. He had to practice his abilities every hour from five in the morning to eight at night. He had just finished and was enjoying the cool night on Crux Prime.
Bam!
The admiral fell back and died. Traylor, who was hiding behind the launch pad to Avant Gardens for the Maelstrom (for their deploy rockets) with Ry, had shot him with his blaster.
They quietly went over to the fallen admiral. Ry drew her sword and chopped the admiral's chain hand off. Ry looked at Traylor for instructions on what to do next.
"There's a launch pad to Nimbus Station right there," Traylor whispered and pointed to the launch pad on their left. "We can use the hook to grapple over there."
"This'll be something different," Ry said, "after how we got up here through the air vent."
She found the trigger on the hook and shot it at the railing around the launch pad. The hook caught it.
Ry looked at Traylor and hopped over the rail for this launch pad and swung down below the Nimbus Station one. The grapple stayed put.
Ry climbed up and hopped on the launch pad. Then she pulled the grapple up and swung it at the Avant Gardens launch pad, where Traylor was. Once again, the hook fastened itself on the railing.
Now, with Ry holding the other end, there was a tight rope. Sort of. Traylor climbed on andswung down beneath it. He began scooting himself from one end to the other while holding on underneath.
As he neared the Nimbus pad, he heard a little crack. Then he heard another, which was a little bit louder.
Crunch!
The chain couldn't hold Traylor any longer. It snapped right between where Traylor's hands were.
Traylor was now holding both pieces of the chain. But Ry couldn't hold Traylor for long, and she accidentally let go.
"Whoa!" Traylor said, a little bit loudly, as he swung back towards the Avant Gardens launch pad. He was now hanging just as Ry had been, but only under this pad. Traylor began to climb up back to where he started, but then the railing gave way. The bolts holding it to the pad were torn out, Traylor found himself falling towards the black, uncaring rocks around Maelstrom Tower.

Chapter 4, Part 4.

Zolzar shivered. The cave that Traylor's former team was living in temporarily was ice cold. Bugs crawled on the walls everywhere, and bit some of the men.
Overall, this cave was dumb.
Zolzar remembered when he had left Traylor's team. What had he gotten himself and the rest of these guys in to?
Zolzar sighed. He felt like an idiot. He had to do something.
Trollo went over to him.
"I should never have followed you," he said angrily. "This is just as bad as that fool Traylor's team."
"Shoulda neva left Trayla alone," muttered Fitz, a rank 3 Daredevil. "He was only tryin' to help the 'Force."
Zolzar stood up. "I was being stupid. I have led you all into this mess."
"What do we do now, fathead?" groaned Volketor.
Zolzar looked at them all. "We're heading back."
Trollo shot him an angry glance. "You guys are just as bad as him."
"If you don't want to go, then stay here," Zolzar said calmly. "Come on, guys. It's time we go back."

* * *

Traylor was still falling after five minutes. This doesn't seem like a lot, but it does when you're plunging to your death.
Traylor was falling close to the side of the Tower. Then he remembered: his sword!
About two years ago, when Traylor was thirteen, he wa given his first sword. It was just a simple Basic Shortsword, but he treasured it because it once saved his life from a stromling. But he was a terrible swordsman and hadn't used it for a long time. Instead, he became a marksman, and he was skilled with any gun.
But once again, the sword would save his life. He took it out of his backpack and watched as its silver blade gleamed. Then he stuck it into the side of the Tower. He continued to slip down a little, but the sword stuck in there and stopped him about six feet off the ground. Traylor let out a sigh of relief while he was hanging there. But his joy was short lived.
A skeleton had been guarding the perimeter of the Tower. He had seen Traylor hanging there and began using his pick ax to climb up to Traylor's level.
Traylor didn't noticed him until he was at his feet. He kicked hard with his foot at the skeleton, but it barely flinched.
What are these guys? A new Maelstrom menace? Traylor wondered. Once again, he lashed out with his foot, this time smacking the skeleton in the face. But the blow hurt Traylor more than the skeleton. Traylor's foot hurt like fire. Then he made a mental note: Never kick a skeleton!
The skeleton grabbed his leg and tore him away from his sword. Traylor screamed and plummeted towards the ground.
There was a hard thwack! as Traylor hit the ground head first, and he blacked out.

Chapter 5, Part 1.

Traylor woke up dazed and confused. His vision was cloudy and blurry. He looked around the room he was in. It was pitch black except for a tiny, glowing, bright blue crystal shard.
Traylor went over to it and touched it. It was an imagination shard. Then it dissolved into liquid and began to cover him completely. Soon he was a glowing, bright blue figure.
Then he began to create something. It was forming. A little bit of this, then he added that, and then...
Whack!
Traylor opened his eyes for real. He had been dreaming. A stromling had woken him up with a vicious punch to the gut.
Traylor moaned and realized where he was. It was a black prison cell. His arms ans legs were chained to the wall, but the chains were long and he could move around.
A stromling was in his cell. He looked at Traylor with shallow, yellow eyes. It then took out a key and unlocked him from his chains.
Traylor was a daring kind of guy, and once he was free, he bolted for the open cell door. But before he got there, he was eletrocuted. Electricity flowed through him to every part of his body, and he crumpled to the ground screaming, and was barely concious.
He looked at the stromling, who held a remote control in his hand. It was black and had two buttons. One was fat and red. The other was blue and was labled cleverly: ON/OFF.
The stromling pointed at Traylor's neck, and Traylor saw that a collar was placed around it. It must shock him whenever the stromling hit the red button.
Traylor groaned and got up. The stromling beckoned him to follow and went outside of the cell and down the hall.
Traylor looked at the other prisoners. They all sat in gloomy silence. Not even one minifig dared to look up at Traylor.
Traylor continued down the hall until they reached a door. The stromling turned a looked fearfully at Traylor. Then it tried painfully to make words, but gibberish came out.
"What?" Traylor said.
The stromling tried again.
"Be careful. I am sorry."
The stromling then opened the door. It led into the old Nexus core. Everything was black and purple. All except one thing.
The imagination still flowed through the center untouched.
And in the corner of the Core sat the Darkitect.
The stromling led Traylor to the Darkitect. Once again, it made words, but just barely.
"Master," the stromling rasped. "I have brought you the prisoner."
"The one that we found hanging on the side of the Tower?" the Darkitect bellowed.
"Yes."
The Darkitect looked at Traylor. "I thought for sure it would be a Venture League."
"This is him, Master. I am sure of it."
"You may not recognize him," the Darkitect said as if he was merely being friendly, "but that is your former scout."
"Triks," Traylor said. "No..."
The Darkitect laughed. "That is not Triks. He was a Maelstrom from the start. He sounded differently because I gave him a voice changer. Why do you always think he was in a box?"
"Tray...lor," the stromling said.
"Rith..." Traylor breathed.
"I am sorry," Rith the stromling said. "I tried, sir, I really...did."

Chapter 5, Part 2.

Traylor's head swam. Rith... Triks... And then it hit him.
"What the build did you need Triks for?" he shouted at the Darkitect.
"He was the one who got into Nexus Tower and shut down the security and radar systems."
Then there was a creak, and Traylor turned around. There stood a Stromling that was holding a box disguise.
"Triks, you dirty little, brick-brained, cursed idiot!" Traylor screamed at Triks furiously. Traylor walked up to Triks and threw a punch at Triks. His punch connected with Triks' eye, and with a painful growl, Triks fell to the ground. He immediatly got back up again, with an ugly, black eye.
Triks hissed and swung at Traylor with his sword. Traylor dodged them all and lashed out with his leg. It smacked triks in the jaw, and he yelped and collapsed.
Traylor looked back at the Darkitect. "Why didn't you stop me?"
"You will learn why soon," the Darkitect said coolly. "Right now is not a good time."
Traylor looked at Rith. "Why?"
Rith shrugged. then the Darkitect seemed to change his mind.
"All right, I will tell you," he said. "We are trying to run a test for a new Maelstrom machine that absorbs the skills of a minifigure and can then use them itself. Your scout here was the first subject. It didn't turn out."
"And I'm the next one?"
"Yes. That is why I haven't killed a few of you."
The Darkitect looked at Rith. "Take hem to the Test Lab."
Rith looked solemn. He nodded and told Traylor to follow him. Traylor silently nodded and followed behind him. There was nothing he could do. The Darkitect would use him to create powerful, new minions and then turn him into a Maelstrom.

Chapter 5, Part 3.

The stromlings held a few wires to Traylor's face, the ends touching him. He was in the lab, about to have his abilities sucked right out of him.
A stromling growled at another one to start the process. It nodded and pulled a lever up slowly.
Traylor suddenly felt a surge of electricity flow into him. He screamed and tried to break away from the cuffs that bounded him to the chair he was in. Then it stopped quickly.
Traylor thought it was over. But then another surge electrocuted him again. Traylor looked to his right to see a jar filling up with blue liquid every time he was shocked. It must be his skills or something like that. How could they make it into a liquid, though? Stromlings, after all, were idiots.
After another surge, the jar filled up. Traylor felt all of his strength draining away. He felt tired and weak.
The stromlings pulled the wires away from his face, but they left him cuffed to the chair. Then Traylor saw something strange come into the lab: a skeleton. It walked over to him and said:
"The Darkitect has told me to tell you that he wants you to watch the creation of the new mech."
Traylor groaned. This day had gone from bad to worse. Now it was probably going to go to worse than worse.
The skeleton laughed and walked away. The stromlings then brought out a huge, hulking, purple robot. It was shaped like a cross between a minifigure and that monster that guards Ravencloud Gate at Forbidden Valley.
The stromling then opened the jar of liquid and began pouring it into the robot's mouth. Traylor moaned.
Then the robot dissolved into a shower of blue sparks. The stromlings gasped with surprise.
Then a blue snaked slithered out of the pile of sparks from the robot. The stromlings hissed and ran away in fear.

Chapter 5, Part 4.

The snake slithered over to Traylor. It glowed blue everywhere except for its eyes, which dimly glowed yellow.
The snake slithered over to Traylor and lifted off his helmet. Then it crawled into his mouth unexpectedly. traylor gasped for air as it blocked his windpipe and he couldn't breathe, but once it was done, he instantly felt his strength returning to him. In fact, he felt stronger than before.
Despite the fact that he had his skills back, he still couldn't beak the cuffs. After three minutes of trying, he gave up. He was still trapped.
