Chapter 9, Part 2.

The henchman (who Traylor noticed was dressed in regular Engineer gear) attempted to hit the Great One with a vicious roundhouse kick (OOC: GO CHUCK NORRIS!), but the Great One simply dodged. In return, the Great One grabbed the henchman's arm, and swung him around in circles. Finally, he let go, sending the henchman flying at Traylor.
The henchman smashed into Traylor. With a shout of pain, Traylor was thrown off his feet and into the dirt.
The rest of the henchmen began a firing sequence at Traylor and his team. They all spread out, each member hiding behind a tree, large boulder, etc.
Traylor pulled out a little basic dagger.
"This'll have to do," he muttered. He peeked out from behind the boulder he hid by, and threw the dagger at the henchmen. It hit one right in the chest, and he screamed as he dropped his weapon and fell backwards.
Traylor looked at Golden. "Can you hear me?" Traylor called out. Golden nodded.
"Good!" Traylor said. "Do you have any idea where we should go next?"
"The only opening in the mountain I can see from here is cave over there!" Golden said as he pointed at it.
"I-I think that's where I came out of from the core!" Traylor said. "We'd better head up there!"
"What about the team?"
"We'd risk getting shot just going for them! Just go and hope they catch on!"
With that, Traylor bolted up the mountainside and sprinted for the cave. Golden was right behind him, firing at the henchmen with his Ranger blaster.
Traylor dove in the cavity which was the mouth of the cave, followed by Golden. They were safe...sort of.
"C'mon," Traylor whispered to Golden. "We can't just sit here. I don't think we have a lot of time."

*******************************************************************************************************

The construction workers had already begun the task of rebuilding the wreckage of what once was the bridge that extended over Nimbus Falls.
"...and that part went under there for support," one of the supervisors said to one of the workers. "But maybe we should-"
"What the stud is that?" the worker interrupted as he pointed out some movement in the dirt by the lake at the end of the waterfall.
"Maybe you should go check it out," the supervisor said.
When the worker had reached the bottom of the plateau which the water ran off of, the movement in the dirt revealed itself. A minifig burst out of the dirt, breathing heavily as he spat out a mouthful of dirt.
"What the brick are you doing?" the worker asked, puzzled.
"That, my friend," he said, "is none of your business. Now, do you happen to have a gun I could borrow?"

Brickface.... The suspense is building!

Chapter 10, Part 2.

Traylor felt his face grow red hot at the sight. Trollo merely laughed.
"Minions! Attack!" Trollo shouted.
Traylor shouted his war cry, and the minigifures charged. Instantly, people began throwing The Big One!s, holding the Maelstrom at bay temporarily. Then some people set Boom Traps around the groups that the minifigs split into to help protect themselves. The Spinjitzu ninjas lured the skeletons over to the other side of Nimbus, where they fought a fierce battle. There were bright flashes and tornadoes everywhere.
Traylor fired his Wormholer. It spat bullets into he air and into stromlings. He was careful not to hit any of his fellow minifigs.
A stromling leapt at him from the top of a tree. Traylor saw it just in time to jab his chainsaw at it before it could land and attack him. The stromling fell on the chainsaw, and died. Traylor shook it off of his Wormholer.
Then he felt a huge, cold hand grab him.
"Fool," Trollo laughed as he picked him up. "You will never defeat me. And your troops stand no chance against me without their leader!"
Trollo threw Traylor at the infected Maelstrom crystal. Traylor knew that anyone who touched it was immediately infected. He screamed.
And then he hit the crystal. He suddenly felt ooze begin to climb over him. He knew it was over. The Maelstrom was infecting him.
He heard Ry scream something before the ooze covered him entirely.
He should have died instantly and become infected. But he didn't. Suddenly, there was a bright blue flash that disintegrated the ooze instantly, and the crystal- along with Traylor- were sucked into it. Then it stopped.
Trollo gasped. Everyone looked to see what happened when they got the chance.
The crystal was no longer infected. It shone cleanly with Imagination.
"No!" Trollo said in disbelief.
Traylor stood in front of it. But he looked different. He was beginning to glow brighter and brighter blue, until it was hard to make out his features.
"The Warrior of Imagination," everyone heard Vanda breathe. Everyone stared in awe as the Warrior faced the Maelstrom.

I hold no shame in saying that when I read the end of this part, I started humming the Lego Universe Theme.

Chapter 10, Part 1.

Traylor and Golden landed on the glowing blue core.
"After him!" barked the Great One. One by one, his henchmen jumped into the hole, each grabbing his gun.
Golden saw them coming. He turned to Traylor.
"Do something!" he shouted.
Traylor actually didn't know what to do. Well, I'm standing on a bunch of imagination, he though. So...
Traylor imagined of something that could stop the henchmen. Anything useful could work. What should he imagine in particular?
Suddenly, he felt a weird, indescribable sensation in his body. He opened his eyes, and saw that Golden was staring at him wildly.
Traylor looked down at himself to see that he was glowing blue again. He was becoming the Warrior of Imagination again.
Traylor looked at the henchmen, now with their pistols aimed at him. Traylor raised his arm, and a patch of imagination grew up into a wall three times taller than him. The bullets fired from the minions pinged off of the wall.
"What the building block-?" Golden sputtered. Traylor ignored him and used his imagination power to shove the wall at the people. The wall slammed into them like a bowling ball hitting pins.
Traylor could hear the Great One from above.
"No!" the Great One said. He himself hopped down into the hole. He dragged Ry and Zolzar down with him, forgetting about the rebellious henchman.
When Traylor saw him, he turned around.
"Give me them!" he shouted.
The Great One didn't respond, but instead reached down to the ground and touched the core. Suddenly, black and purple colors began to flood the core from where he touched it.
"No!" Golden said. He drew a sword and threw it at the Great One with all of his might.
But the Great One was prepared. He grabbed the handle of the sword as the blade came a fraction of an inch away from his head.
Already Traylor could see the Great One's cloak turning black and dark purple.
"The Maelstrom shall arise!" he said. "I have the power to kill you now..."
Suddenly, a huge fist shot up out of the infected section of the core, and grabbed Traylor. It began squeezing him so tightly, he started to breath shallower and shallower.
"...and that I shall!" the Great One finished, chuckling menacingly to himself.
