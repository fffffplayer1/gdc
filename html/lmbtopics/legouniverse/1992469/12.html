Chapter 6, Part 1.

The Ranger's name turned out to be Golden. Traylor found out after he became a member of their team.
"So where're we headed?" Golden asked.
"Nimbus Falls," Traylor said. "That's what it says. I think I remember going there on the way back from the core..."
"Looks like we're about there..." Ry muttered.
The falls came into view. It was massive. It had to be at least two brickball fields long (for those of you who don't know what brickball is, it's minifig football. Of course, you probably figured that out).
"That's it," Traylor said. "It has to be."
"Wow," Ry said, amazed.
"We're pulling up to the station now," Brickface said.
The tram slowly began to stop. Traylor got out first, wanting to get as far away from the evidence of the gunfight there earlier.
"Ah..." he sighed. "So peaceful..."
"Whatever," Golden said.

Chapter 6, Part 2.

Traylor stood at the edge of the plateau which the waterfall poured off of.
"What do we do now?" Ry asked.
"Well, there's supposed to be a rock that's shaped like a crocodile, which leads to some not-named river that we sail down to the next place we go to. And the rock is right over... there."
The rock made an ugly, craggy crocodile shape, but it was still a crocodile. Ish thing.
"Well, I can't help it but to feel like this is some sort of trap," Brickface said.
"What choice do we have?" Traylor said. "We have to go."

* * *

"They're coming, sir."
These words had been spoken to the Great One by Lubjack again. They were at the area just passed the "not named" river, which was Brick Rapids.
"Our scouts have spotted them by Nimbus Falls," he continued.
"Who?"
"The ones that freed Brickface," Lubjack said. "I saw him among them. There are four, not including Brickface.
"So what have you done to stop them?" the Great One asked.
"I told the scouts to deal with them."
"Good. However, we'd better hurry. What is the next destination?"
Lubjack told him.
"We'd best be on our way."
