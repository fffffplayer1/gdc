You guys wanted the sequel (and I did too), so here it is! Look at the title at the top of the post to see its name.

Chapter 1, Part 1.

A minifigure stood outside of a building. It was huge, grey, and relatively uninteresting.
The minifigure wore a black suit with tie. He also had a cape on, making his whole outfit look a bit off. His hair was neat and combed.
The building was, although uninteresting, the key to the minifig's most prized possesion (next to imagination): The Core Map. The Core map was the only map that could lay out the one and only path down to the hollow core of Crux, where the Imagination Nexus was held. It was the most powerful thing in the Universe.
Ever since the Warrior of Imagination had healed Crux and put it there, the only other way to get close to imagination was to go to the hole it produced in the surface of Crux, where one beam of Imagination shot out. But it wasn't nearly as powerful as the main core.
And the Warrior had made a map of the route to take to the core so he could find his way back if he needed to go there.

Chapter 2, Part 1.

After the battle with Trollo and the Maelstrom, things had been pretty calm around the area for Traylor. Lately, he'd been practicing his swordfighting with Ry and Zolzar.
Clang!
Traylor swung his metal stick at Ry, who lifted her shield up to block it. Then she brought her stick around to hit Traylor in the side. Traylor fell on the ground, for the third time this practice.
The reason they used metal sticks instead of real swords is because of one particular incident that involved Traylor's head almost being chopped off. After that, they decided to use sticks, no matter how lame they were.
Zolzar had been speculating on the sidelines.
"Why is it that Ry always wins?" he chuckled. Traylor frowned and got up.
"I'll get back to you on that."
"When?"
"After I win a fight," Traylor said. Zolzar didn't reply, but merely looked at the construction going on around the place.
They were at the new, under-construction Nexus Tower.
Even though the Maelstrom had been destroyed, there were still threats out there to be stopped, so the Nexus Force was allowed to stick around. But since the Maelstrom had ruined the old Nexus Tower, they tore it down and were using its uninfected bricks to help build the new one.
A few levels had been complete, so Ry, Zolzar, and Traylor had been practicing on one of them.
They continued to practice for a few minutes, until a minifig came running in.
"Sir," he said between pants, "someone's stolen the map."
They all became alert at this.
"The Core Map?" Traylor said. Of course it was, he thought. What else could it be?
The minifig nodded.
"Who stole it?"
"Well, we don't really know."

Chapter 2, Part 2.

"The guy you're looking for is Grenwell Brickface," said the officer. Traylor, Ry, and Zolzar were at the Nexus Prison. If the thief was captured, he would be here.
"He broke into the map facility to get the map," the officer continued. "We searched him after we caught him, but he didn't have it on him."
"That's all we need to know," Traylor said. "Take me to his cell."
"Whatever, pal," the police officer said nonchalantly. He got out of the chair that he had been sitting in and led them into a room full of prisons.
The minifigures there looked tired, hungry, and wanted to get out of there. They looked at Traylor sadly, and Traylor would've felt sorry for them- if they hadn't do whatever they did to deserve this.
The officer led them to a cell with a minifig in it, who was oddly dressed in a dusty, black suit and cape.
"Ladies and Gentlemen," the officer said, "Grenwell Brickface."
"What do you want?" he said bluntly.
"What were you going to do with the map if you actually stole it?" Traylor said. He said it in a very I-want-answers-buddy tone.
"Why should I tell you?" he retorted.
"Because we want to," Traylor replied nastily. "The rest is none of your business."
"Well, you'll never get it back, now that my team has it."
Traylor noticed that he said, "my team." So he had been working with someone.
"Who's your team?" he said.
Brickface looked frustrated with himself, now that he realized that he gave that away.
"It doesn't matter! I won't tell you anything! Once my team takes over the Core, they'll come back for me!"
This was going almost no where.

Chapter 2, Part 3.

Traylor knew that this was going to take some work. But then he had an idea.
"How do you know that your team is coming back for you?" he asked.
"I was pretty high up there in rank," Brickface said. "They'll need me to get the- I mean whatever they want."
"But once they get whatever they want," Zolzar chimed in, "they won't need you to help them."
Brickface was still confident.
"Even if they do, I'm not telling you anything."
"Why not?" Traylor said.
"Why should I? There's only one thing that if you do, I'll help you," Brickface said, with a grim smile.
"What? No!" Traylor said.
"We have to be somewhat reasonable," Ry said. "What is it that you want?"
"I want out of here."
"What?" Traylor yelled. "Like we can do that!"
"We may have to," Zolzar said. "What's the cost to get him out?"
"How should I know?" Ry said. "Probably a good couple million."
"We don't have that money!" Traylor said.
"Then maybe there's another way."
"Do you want my help or not?" Brickface said, still smiling. "Get me out of here, or you don't get your information."

Chapter 3, Part 1.

The police officer was standing at his usual position, guarding the prison cells. Not a single prisoner could escape when he was on duty. Well, not that any really tried. He actually didn't know that one of their most dangerous criminals had gotten away.
He never actually knew that until about an hour later. He decided to walk down the hall of prisons to intimidate the prisoners when he noticed that the lock to Grenwell Brickface's prison door was broken, and the door was wide open, leading into an empty cell.
The officer gasped and clumsily ran to tell the boss what had happened.

* * *

Did he want to do it? No. Did he think Brickface deserved it? Absolutely not. Did Traylor need the information about Brickface's team? Yes.
And that's why he, Ry, and Zolzar had to break him out. It was their only chance.
They were interrogating Brickface at Traylor's house. His house was pretty small, and made of stone. It was kind of crude, but it worked.
"What's your team?" Traylor started out. It was a simple question, but if Brickface was telling the truth, then they'd be getting somewhere.
They were lucky. Brickface had apparently abandoned all loyalty to his team.
"Their official name is Black Core Squadron, for a reason that I'll say later, but most people refer to them as the Dark Force," Brickface said.
"What's their plan?" Traylor asked bluntly.
"Well, they want to corrupt the Imagination Nexus to recreate the Maelstrom."
"Why?"
"They claim that they're doing it for a better Crux, but we all knew that wasn't the truth. Our leader wants the Nexus for himself, but he was saying that anyone who joined him could help him rule. That's why I joined.
"I was a bit power-hungry back then, about three years ago, but once I joined, I immediately realized what was going on. I wanted to quit, but anyone who quit was killed. We all thought that was a joke, until a few people did quit.
"So I was forced to fight for them for three years. But I saw my chance out once you guys came to my cell. I didn't really believe they'd come back. I just wanted to make sure that it seemed like I knew the information which you guys wanted, which I do know," Brickface finished.
"So they had you steal the map?" Traylor said.
"Yes."
"Well, I don't remember the way back, so we're kinda stuck," Traylor sighed hopelessly.
"Not all hope is lost, my friend," Brickface said. "It just so happens that I have an almost perfect memory."
