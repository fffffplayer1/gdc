Prologue
The Darkitect sat in his dark, gloomy secret lair. The walls of the cave he lived in were cold and jagged, but the inside itself was warm from the lit torches in front of his throne. However, they had deliberately been put at an angle to prevent them from showing his face.

A stromling ran in.
"Master! They have drawn away from Crux Prime, as you thought they would!" he said.

"Yes, Master." The stromling ran away.
The Darkitect yelled out.
"Flogmore!"
An admiral entered the room.
"You called, Master?"
"Get the troops ready. Our attack on Nexus Tower begins now!"

Chapter 1, Part 1

Vanda Darkflame, the mysterious ninja leader of the Paradox, rummaged through books and tablets in her office. She was looking for a piece of paper...
"Aha!" she cried when she had found it. It was yellowed slightly on the edges and had several creases in it from being bent and squished under the books.
She read it quietly to herself:

"When the Universe is threatened by a menace, the Maelstrom, only the Warrior of Imagination can stop their leader and restore peace to even the far reaches of Lego Universe."

Vanda let out a deep sigh. She had been the one to write this down. Her vision in the Shadow Orb had told her of this, and she wrote it down because of its seeming importance. But she had told no one of this.
However, she realized that the Nexus Force needed this "warrior of imagination" now more than ever. The Maelstrom had taken over Gnarled Forest and was overwhelming the soldiers and ninjas in Forbidden Valley. The Nexus Force was losing.
Vanda walked out of her office to gather the other Faction leaders together. They had to talk about this.
* * *
"Vanda," said Duke Exeter, the Sentinel leader, "you and everyone else here knows perfectly well that I don't believe in ghost stories and propheies and visions and whatnot!"
"Duke, what choice do we have?" Vanda replied.
"She's gotta point," agreed Hael Storm, the Venture League captain.
"I think so too," Doctor Overbuild said, "but how can we find him? What should we do?"
"We aren't doing anything about this 'warrior'!" Duke said. "It's nothing but a folk's tale! Lie! Whatever you want to call it."
"The Shadow Orb doesn't lie, Duke," Vanda said.
"Like I haven't heard that before!" Duke snapped. "The Shadow Orb isn't real!"
"Duke, at this moment, soldiers are getting smashed and the Maelstrom is gaining power," Vanda said. "Don't you think the least we could do for them is to end this war as soon as possible?"
Duke turned away from her and sighed. He had to do something. He had to end this war. He had to.
"Fine, Vanda, I will go with you this once. But it had better be worth it."

Chapter 1, Part 2

Traylor whacked the dummy stromling with all of his might. The head popped off and it tipped over, so the machine immediatly put down a hammer stromling dummy. Traylor punched this one several times before its arms fell off. The machine up above put down another. Traylor began to throw punches at it, but before he could finish it, his time was up.
He exited the Sentinel Combat Challenge with a point total of 36. "Not bad, for using just my bare hands," he thought to himself.
Traylor was a Paradox Space Marauder and wore rank one gear with a rank two rocket launcher and shoulder pads. When his helmet was off, he had black, spikey hair that stuck up all over the place.
Traylor walked to the Seninel War Room. He took the vent up to Duke Exeter, who was having a conversation with a rank three Space Marauder while overviewing a map of Crux Prime.
"The Maelstrom's being overtaken on Crux Prime, but why would it suddenly have taken Gnarled Forest and Forbidden Valley?" the Marauder said.
"It probably took advantage of the fact that most of our troops were being sent to Crux Prime," Duke replied, thinking. Then he noticed that Traylor was there.
"If you need something, could it wait till later? I'm in the middle of an important conversation."
"Actually, it had to do with the conversation," Traylor said. "Why not just get everybody off Forbidden Valley and let them take it, and then take over Crux?"
Duke looked at him as if he was crazy; the Marauder just shook his head and said, "War doesn't work like that, kid."
"Then how does it work?" Traylor said back. "War is basically a bunch of people coming out and fighting over stuff."
Duke looked mad, and the Marauder said to him, "Sir, I'll handle this."
He hopped down into the center of the War Room and beckoned Traylor to follow. Once he was down there, the Marauder said, "War is how we solve important business. Obviously."
"Why have men go and die for it, then?" Traylor said. "There has to be another way."
"Kid, why in the name of imagination would you join the Nexus Force if you don't understand what war even means?" said the Marauder, outraged. "Men are being smashed for the people they care about, and you sit here and say, 'Why do that?'"
"Whatever,... um... uh...," said Traylor, trying to find out his name.
The Marauder knew what he was doing.
"Shadowgun," he said irritably.
"...Shadowgun," Traylor finished. Then he left. The War Room.
But then he heard the alarm sound. WHOOP! WHOOP!
"Nexus Tower is under attack!" cried a voice through the speaker.
Quickly, Traylor ran to the nearest dropship, and stepped inside, along with other recruits.
"Destination: Crux Prime," read the terminal.

Traylor looked around at the squad he was with a. He didn't know anybody's name, though he had seen at least two of them before. But then he spyed Shadowgun.
Traylor scowled. Shadowgun knew nothing. He was just another, ordinary recruit. HE didn't know anything about war.
Bam!
The ship was hit with a ball of fire. The alarm sounded. Powers cried, "It's a dragon! Our wing's hit! Abandon ship!"
Boom!
The ship was hit again. A minifig jumped off of the ship and into a battlefield below. Then everyone else began to follow his lead.
When it was Traylor's turn to jump, he saw the dragon, flying in circles as the ship fell to the ground. Traylor figured it was now or never, and jumped.
The dragon began to notice the minifigs who jumped down from the ship. Deciding that the ship had taken enough damage, the dragon threw balls of fire at the minifigs. Some of them were hit by them and caught on fire. Their shrieks echoed in Traylors ears.
The ship hit the ground first, exploding into flames. Pieces of metal, some on fire, flew away from the crash site.
Traylor hit the ground with a thud. His helmet fell off and rolled away. He got up and looked around.
The dragon had decided to stop, and had flown away, but the crash had caught the attention of every other Maelstrom villian in the battlefield.
Curses.

Chapter 2, Part 1.

Immediatly, someone cried out, "Charge!", and all of the minifig rushed at the Maelstrom. Swords swung and clashed. Guns and blasters fired. People fell on both sides.
A dark ronin went up to Traylor and swung at him with its sword. Traylor backed up and it missed him.
Traylor put his hand at his side to draw his blaster, but only to find it had been crushed from the landing. His rocket launcher had been too.
The ronin saw that Traylor was weaponless, and went up to Traylor fearlessly. He aimed his sword at his chest. He was about to strike when Traylor Punched him in the face. The ronin dropped his sword and put his ghastly hands over his face. Traylor got up and kicked the ronin in the chest. The ronin flew back a couple feet, then looked angrily at Traylor. He picked up his sword and threw it at Traylor.
Traylor screamed and ducked, and the sword fell to the ground behind him. He got back up and grabbed the ronin's arm. Then he began to swing him in circles, getting closer to the edge of Crux Prime. The ronin looked fearful but simply did nothing except let Traylor swing him.
Finally, Traylor let go, sending the ronin over the edge of the planet into the deep black space. It hissed until it disappeared into the distance.
Traylor, feeling proud about beating a ronin with his bare hands, turned around to find three stromlings facing him, slowly advancing.
He kicked the first one in the face, and it reeled back and fell on the ground. The second one, however, took his sword arm and swung it at Traylor.
He almost dodged. The sword hit his cheek, leaving a deep gash. He got back up and swept the stromling's feet out from under him. He toppled to the ground face-first.
The first one got up and punched Traylor in the side of the neck with his regular hand. Tryalor sputtered as he tried to recover, then grabbed the stromling by the throat and threw him into the second one, who had been trying to get up.
The third stromling swung his sword at Traylor several times, but Traylor barely dodged. Then the stromling kicked Traylor, and he fell back, his head over the side of the edge. The stromling put his bony foot on Traylor's body.
The stromling wasn't bright, but it didn't take a genius to realize that there was another option to smash this minifig than his sword. With his foot, he began scooting Traylor over the edge.

Chapter 2, Part 2.

Shink!
"Rrraaaggghhrrraaaggaa...!" the stromling moaned. It collapsed to the ground, revealling a sword protruding from its back. Behind its body was a rank three Seninel knight.
"You okay?" she asked. Traylor was surprised to find out it was a girl. But, she had still saved his life.
"Uh, yeah. I'm fine," he mumbled.
The girl nodded.
"The name's Ry," she said bluntly. "Yours?"
"Traylor."
She nodded again.
"See the crash?" she said a moment later.
"How could I not?" Traylor replied.
"There's a cave behind it. The soldiers're taking cover in it. We need to get there."
"Okay." Traylor didn't like to be told what to do, but he was in no position to question her.
He and Ry ran toward the wreck. Ry ran faster than him and reached the crash first. Traylor was almost there when he felt a big, meaty hand grab him.
He was lifted into the air and came face-to-face with an ape. Traylor screamed. The ape threw him further away from the ship, and closer to the horde of Maelstrom attacking it. A horseman went foward to finish him off, but the ape glared at him solemnly; he wanted to have this one.
The ape lifted his burly fist to strike Traylor, but it never hit him.
A Space Marauder had come over. He grabbed Traylor by the legs and threw him closer to the crash. Then he took the ape's punch. His head snapped back, and he flew towards the crash beside Traylor.
Traylor's fear turned to anger; he would avenge this Marauder's death. he got up and charged towards the ape.
The ape was ready for him, but he hadn't expected Traylor to come up and grab his face. Traylor began punching his eyes, irritating the ape. The ape roared and ran clumsily around, trying to shake him off. Traylor held on. Then the ape tried to pull him off, but Traylor swung left and right, dodging the ape's grabs.
The ape teetered towards the edge of the planet. Traylor saw this and jumped off, and the ape met the same fate as the ronin.
Traylor ran to the fallen Space Marauder.
"Hey, kid," he said.
It was none other than Shadowgun.

Chapter 2, Part 3.

Traylor dragged the barely concious Shadowgun to the cave. Ry met him at the entrance.
"Like playing hero, huh?" she said. Traylor ignored her and went deeper into the cave than the rest of the soldiers.
Traylor borrowed Shadowgun's spark thrower to light up the cave.
"Why did you do that?" Traylor asked him.
"Because," Shadowgun said weakly, "everyone deserves a second chance."
"Second chance for what?" Traylor continued.
"War," Shadowgun answered. There was a cold silence between the two of them.
"You have to fight for what's right," Shadowgun continued. "What you fight for is fun. That's how people get killed. Or learn the hard way...
"You see no point in war. Why do it, then?"
"Why does everyone else?" Traylor retorted.
"Because they're willing to protect their people," Shadowgun said.
Traylor realized something then: it was true. Shadowgun was dying here because he wanted to protect Traylor, give him a chance to protect the other people. If he just walked away from this mess, or died doing something stupid, Shadowgun's sacrifice would mean nothing.
Traylor looked at Shadowgun.
"You're right."
Traylor couldn't tell if Shadowgun was smiling or not because his helmet blocked his face, but he was willing to bet that he was.
"Good to know," Shadowgun said. "Now, take my armor, kid."
"What?" Traylor said.
"I would like you to use it more than I would when some Maelstrom guy comes and infects me," Shadowgun reasoned. "Take it."
"I will," Traylor said, "later."
"Okay, whatever," Shadowgun said. Then he coughed sharply several times and fell back. Traylor ran over to him.
"Before I die, kid," Shadowgun began, "who are you?"
"Traylor," he said.
"I don't know a lot about you, kid," Shadowgun said, "but one day it'll be you who does it."
Then Shadowgun's body went slack, and he finally died.

Chapter 3, Part 1.

"'...One day it'll be you who does it,'"
Shadowgun's words rang through Traylor's head. What did he mean? Does what?
Traylor took off Shadowgun's armor and buried him deep in the cave. Traylor looked at the armor. He didn't think that wearing a fallen soldier's armor showed honor, but Shadowgun had wanted him to.
Traylor took off his old armor and put on Shadowgun's. Traylor had never felt so powerful. He felt like he could do anything.
Traylor walked slowly to the mouth of the cave. The soldiers had built a barricade to protect themselves. Ry stood by it.
Traylor went up to her and said, "Ry."
She jumped a little.
"Where'd you get the armor?'
"It's not important," Traylor said, clearly ending that matter. "We need to find us a scout."
"Why do we need a scout?" she asked.
"We need to find out where we are and find a way to Zeta Point," Traylor said. "We have to get out of here."
Ry nodded and went over to the small crowd of about ten soldiers.
"We're gonna need a scout or two to get out of here, so let's take a vote," she said. Most of the people knew each other, so it wasn't hard to get one. In the end, there was a tie between two men named Rith and Triks.
Triks was quiet and always seemed to wear a Sentinel box disguise for no apparent reason, but he made a good spy and could escape and find places quickly.
Rith was sneaky and quick. He always wore a Bat Lord outfit with rank 3 Assembly engineer shoulderpads, giving him the Personal Fortress ability.
"Alright," Traylor said. "We have our scouts. Time for the plan."
