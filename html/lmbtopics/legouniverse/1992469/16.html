Chapter 8, Part 1.

Traylor splashed into the bone-chilling water. Man, if he stayed in there too long, he would FREEZE to death. He wondered if Brickface was even still ALIVE.
Traylor looked around. There wasn't a sign of movement.
What was that? thought Traylor. It was a movement in the water. Traylor swam as fast as he could toward the movement.
"Lurgleg!" Traylor made a sound. It was supposed to be "Yes!" but words never sound right underwater.
It was Brickface, lying unconscious on the lake bed. Traylor wanted to swim down and grab him, but suddenly he felt his lungs about to burst. He quickly swam up to the surface of the lake.
Traylor splashed out of the water, gasping for air.
"Anything yet?" Ry said. She was fine now.
"Yeah," Traylor said briefly. Then he went back down.
He took a second to find Brickface. Once he found Brickface, he started to swim towards him, but the lower he went in the lake, the harder it was to swim downwards.
Almost there... got him! Traylor thought. He grabbed Brickface's hand and pulled him off of the lake floor. Brickface was pulled along like a dead snake as Traylor brought him up to the surface.
Traylor made it! He gasped for air once again and dragged Brickface onto the nearest part of the shore, where Ry, and now Zolzar, were.
Traylor plopped Brickface on the shore.
"Got...him..." Traylor panted. Immediately, Ry and Zolzar began to pump the water that he had swallowed and breathed.
"C'mon, c'mon," Ry said.
"I don't think it's working," Zolzar said flatly.
"Leave it to Zolzar to ruin your positive attitude," Traylor said after regaining his breath. Zolzar just looked at him.
It was no use. Brickface wasn't coming to.
"Is he... dead?" Ry said. She seemed afraid to say the word, which was unlike her.
Traylor sighed. "I think he is."

Oh no! Poor Brickface. R.I.P.

Chapter 8, Part 2.

Traylor, Ry, Zolzar, and Golden (who recently surfaced, and who could hold his breath for a very long time) buried Brickface with a thin layer of dirt. They didn't want to leave Brickface's body out, but they didn't have much time either.
Traylor sighed. "Well, now what?"
"We follow the map, I guess," Ry said, obviously depressed about Brickface's death.
"The map is wet and soggy, not to mention useless," Zolzar said.
"Dang it!" Traylor shouted. "No map and no person with photographic memory! How much worse can this get?"
"Don't jinx us or something!" Golden teased.
"Oh, shut up!" Traylor snapped.
"Wait!" Ry said. "I remember something! See that mountain in the distance?"
"...Yeah," Traylor said as he looked for the mountain.
"That was where the map ended, which means the core's there!"
"So we'll just be taking a shortcut!" Golden said excitedly. "We're back in the game!"
"We'd better go," Traylor said. "Don't want to lose time celebrating."
The team stepped into the forest and headed toward the mountain where the core entrance lay.

Chapter 9, Part 1.

They were at the mountain. It extended far into the sky, towering over Traylor and his group.
"I guess we move, then," Traylor said. Then he heard a voice behind him.
"You!"
They turned around to see a squad of armed minifigures with a hooded one in front. The hooded one appeared unarmed.
"How on CRUX did you beat us here?" he said. "We were well ahead of you!"
"We took a shortcut," Golden said.
"Of course..." the hooded one's voice trailed off. "If Brickface told you anything about his past, you've probably heard from him about the Dark Force."
"Yeah," Traylor said.
"I am the Great One, as I preferred to be known as," the hooded minifig said.
"I've heard about you from Brickface," Traylor said.
"Speaking of him, where is he?"
"Dead," Traylor said. "He died on the way here."
"Shame," said the Great One, although it was obvious that he didn't miss him at all. "Anyways, to get back to what we were talking about, I am going to need you gentlemen to step aside."
"Don't be stupid. Since when would we do that?" Ry spoke up.
"Since this is for the good of the Universe," the Great One said.
"How could it be that this is 'for the good of the Universe'?" Zolzar said.
"Your planet needs a ruler. I intend to become it and give this place the unity that it needs!"
"It's fine on it's own!" Traylor said.
"But look at how much more we can accomplish as one nation than several cities!"
"We're not out for conquest!"
"Just step away from them, Master," one of the Great One's henchmen said. The other's looked in shock (some even gasped) at the henchmen's daring move. Only the brave (or the stupid) would challenge or tell their leader what to do.
The Great One turned to him.
"You're next for that henchman! Right when I'm done dealing with these imbeciles..."

Chapter 9, Part 2.

The henchman (who Traylor noticed was dressed in regular Engineer gear) attempted to hit the Great One with a vicious roundhouse kick (OOC: GO CHUCK NORRIS!), but the Great One simply dodged. In return, the Great One grabbed the henchman's arm, and swung him around in circles. Finally, he let go, sending the henchman flying at Traylor.
The henchman smashed into Traylor. With a shout of pain, Traylor was thrown off his feet and into the dirt.
The rest of the henchmen began a firing sequence at Traylor and his team. They all spread out, each member hiding behind a tree, large boulder, etc.
Traylor pulled out a little basic dagger.
"This'll have to do," he muttered. He peeked out from behind the boulder he hid by, and threw the dagger at the henchmen. It hit one right in the chest, and he screamed as he dropped his weapon and fell backwards.
Traylor looked at Golden. "Can you hear me?" Traylor called out. Golden nodded.
"Good!" Traylor said. "Do you have any idea where we should go next?"
"The only opening in the mountain I can see from here is cave over there!" Golden said as he pointed at it.
"I-I think that's where I came out of from the core!" Traylor said. "We'd better head up there!"
"What about the team?"
"We'd risk getting shot just going for them! Just go and hope they catch on!"
With that, Traylor bolted up the mountainside and sprinted for the cave. Golden was right behind him, firing at the henchmen with his Ranger blaster.
Traylor dove in the cavity which was the mouth of the cave, followed by Golden. They were safe...sort of.
"C'mon," Traylor whispered to Golden. "We can't just sit here. I don't think we have a lot of time."

*******************************************************************************************************

The construction workers had already begun the task of rebuilding the wreckage of what once was the bridge that extended over Nimbus Falls.
"...and that part went under there for support," one of the supervisors said to one of the workers. "But maybe we should-"
"What the stud is that?" the worker interrupted as he pointed out some movement in the dirt by the lake at the end of the waterfall.
"Maybe you should go check it out," the supervisor said.
When the worker had reached the bottom of the plateau which the water ran off of, the movement in the dirt revealed itself. A minifig burst out of the dirt, breathing heavily as he spat out a mouthful of dirt.
"What the brick are you doing?" the worker asked, puzzled.
"That, my friend," he said, "is none of your business. Now, do you happen to have a gun I could borrow?"

Chapter 9, Part 3.

The sound of gunfire faded into the distance as Traylor and Golden crept farther and farther into the dark and musty cave.
"Are we at the core yet?" Golden asked.
"No," Traylor said flatly.
"How about now?"
"No."
"Now?"
"No."
"How about NOW?"
"No."
"Come ON!"
"Shut up."
They continued deeper into the cave. Traylor was beginning to worry if this really WAS the cave he had exited from. After all, there could have been other caves in the mountain.
That's when they saw a dim, tiny, blue light in the distance.
"Are we there now?" Golden asked again.
"You're like a kid in a car!" Traylor snapped. "But yeah, I guess we are."
They crept closer to the tiny light. It was coming from a hole in the ground. The hole was about the size of a car tire.
They looked into it. Below was a small ball of glowing, blue Imagination. It was small because they were so far away from it.
"Would it be safe to hop in?" Golden said.
"I...don't really know," Traylor admitted.
"Hey!" said someone behind them. They spun around to see the entire Dark Force, with the Great One in the front.
"You think you can stop us?" the Great One said. "Think again! We have your friend and that pesky henchman, and we aren't afraid to kill them if we have to!"
Traylor looked at the Great One. Then he looked at Golden. They appeared to be thinking the same thing.
"Jump!" Traylor bellowed as he jumped into the hole, followed almost immediately by Golden.

*******************************************************************************************************

Brickface wasn't the best tracker ever, but he could get the job done. He had been carefully following the footprints from Nimbus Falls to the mountain.
"I hope this is the place," Brickface said. He searched around for a sign of tracks. There were a few leading into the cave.
Brickface mustered up his courage, drew his borrowed gun from the worker, and stepped inside the cave.
