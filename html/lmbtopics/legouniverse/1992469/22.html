Chapter 12, Part 2.

Shadowshocker brought the dropship to a halt on what appeared to be part of Nimbus City. The Maelstrom had abandoned the city after their previous attack on it to focus on more important matters, so the people repopulated it. The people were still walking through the city, but they still appeared to be shaken up from what happened.
"Do you think that the Faction leaders are here right now?" Traylor said to Fritz as they hopped out of the dropship.
"Hopefully," answered Fritz. "I really wanna get outta this mess."
Traylor couldn't blame him at all. The Maelstrom was only getting stronger, and he was on a serious time crunch.
It took them about a good hour and a half to find the Faction leaders. They were huddled in an alley between the buildings, talking.
"What should we do?" Vanda Darkflame said. "The Maelstrom's getting more powerful as we speak, and we can only sit there and watch it!"
"We can do more than sit there," Doc Overbuild said. "We can launch an assault at the Maelstrom."
"How can we do that?" roared Duke Exeter. "They'll only crush us like they have been for the last three battles! We haven't won a single battle yet."
"We need Maelstrom intel," said Overbuild. "Not to mention Imagination."
"There isn't any left," said a voice that neither Exeter, Overbuild, or Hael Storm recognized. Everyone turned to see Traylor and his crew.
"The Maelstrom's infected it all," Traylor continued.
"How do you know?" said Exeter.
"I've seen the damage."
"Okay, do you know anything about the Maelstrom that we don't know already?" Overbuild asked.
"Well, they have a fortress on a chunk of Crux that I've been in, and I happen to know their leader."
"Do ya know the fortress good?" Hael Storm said.
"Decently," replied Traylor.
"Before we go into any more details about the plan," Exeter interrupted, "who exactly are you?"
"Didn't remember him from last time, huh?" Vanda said. "Remember Traylor, the Warrior of Imagination?"

Chapter 13, Part 1.

The Maelstrom Fortress was well built and stocked with a huge surplus of troops. Ry made it so it was easily defended but hard to attack. There were always at least ten guards along each wall, every place was easily accessible, and it was hard to map out without being there for long periods of time.
Ry was in the center of the fortress, talking with some of her minions. The infected, Carnegie, Zolzar, Brickface, and Golden were all there, not to mention the Commander Stromling that had helped participate in Traylor's infection and a few others.
"I have gathered you all here today to discuss our next move," Ry began. "If we don't move quickly, we'll give the enemy too much time to think about their next move."
"Now that Crux is broken apart, it's easier to maintain each of our stations," Zolzar hissed.
"Yes, but that makes it harder for us to attack the enemy," replied Ry.
"Not if we can develop some new invention that could take care of the problem," said Draccus, one of the Stromlings who also was part of Traylor's infection. "As the lead scientist, I have been thinking of an invention which could attach itself to the bottom of a mass of Crux, then disperse the Stromling army over the entire fragment. If more troops are needed, we can teleport more there with a teleporter I'm working on also."
"Good idea," the Commander said, "but we still need something so we can act now."
"The Commander's right," said Carnegie, who was still Ry's second in command. "We need to start cutting off enemy access to some of it's stronger holds, like Nexus."
"I think we need to go after the one who's been more of a thorn in our side than anyone else," Golden said. "Traylor and his friends."
"Should we really focus half of our army on one minifigure?" said Brickface.
"This one minifigure is the one who destroyed the Maelstrom twice already, and is probably aiming to do it again."
"We've pretty much already defeated him after his brother here took him down in the forest," said Brickface.
"I'm not so sure," Carnegie rasped. "I've heard that he's been sighted in the forest after the battle, and that he's somewhere else by now."
"Bricks!" the Commander muttered.
Suddenly, the alarm for the fortress went off.
"They're attacking!" Ry shouted. "Move!"

Chapter 13, Part 2.

Traylor wasn't sure if this was a good idea. The Nexus Force and Shadowgun Squadron had joined together and launched an attack on the Maelstrom Headquarters, but Traylor had a nasty feeling that this wasn't going to go over well. Sure, the Maelstrom would be totally off guard, but the mission could still be a costly one. The plan was to fly in on twenty dropships and take down the fortress from there.
Not to mention the fact that he knew he was going to face Ry in the fortress. Ry had always been a much better swordsman - or in this case, swordswoman - than him, and though he was armed with a Space Marauder Rank 3 Rocket Launcher and Pistol, he was dreading the encounter.
The dropship turned hard to the side as it probably dodged something. He had a feeling that the ship was now in range of the Maelstrom's Anit-Air turrets and under fire. Traylor prayed hard that they would make it.
He and the rest of the Shadowgun Squadron had piled into one dropship, while everyone else under the Nexus Force had scattered into others. If the dropship he was in went down, there would definitely be no more Shadowgun Squadron.
"We're nearing the base!" said the dropship pilot Alkor. "Get ready for deployment!"
Traylor felt the dropship slowing down and landing on something. The doors slid open, revealing the fortress directly in front of them.
Traylor was in all of his normal Paradox Space Marauder Rank 3 armor already except for his helmet. As everyone else bolted out of the ship, Traylor grabbed his helmet. He stared at it for a few seconds.
It was one of the pieces of armor he had gotten from Shadowgun, the Paradox warrior that Traylor knew little about. It was scratched, dented, and worn, but Traylor wouldn't trade this armor for a brand new set.
Traylor put on his helmet, grabbed his guns and jumped out of the ship just as the entire Stromling army flooded out of the fortress's door.
This was going to be a long battle.

Chapter 13, Part 3.

Traylor couldn't hold out much longer. Fighting Stromlings really wore him out, but he had to continue. He had already infiltrated the base and the throne room door was in sight.
He shot another Stromling before it could even get near him, blew up a horde of more Stromlings with his rocket launcher, then used his bombs to clear the hall to the throne room door.
Traylor didn't know if the door was unlocked or not, but he shot the door down anyways. He charged into the throne room recklessly, only to be confronted by hundreds of Stromlings.
And he was all alone.
Traylot immediately started blasting, bombing, and fist-fighting the Stromling horde, but he was definitely overwhelmed. He smashed one Stromling's jaw with the nose of his blaster, and took it out by blasting it while it was stunned. Another Stromling closed in on him, but he banged the bottom of his gun on its head. A dull crack! was heard, and the Stromling fell.
A third managed to sneak up behind Traylor and grab his neck. The Stromling squeezed hard and started to choke Traylor hard. Traylor tried to break free, but the more the Stromling choked him, the weaker he got.
Just as he was about to give in, the Stromling choking him released his grasp. Traylor gasped for air as he turned around. The Stromling was dead on the floor, with Rith standing over it.
"Rith!" said Traylor.
"Don't mention it," Rith said with a grin.
"Stop!" screamed a voice that echoed throughout the room. It was a girl's voice, and although she was coming from behind Traylor, he knew who it was.
Traylor turned around to see Ry, or Preciponus, command her army to stop attacking. Although the orders weren't for them, the Nexus Force that had entered the building stopped as well.
"If any of you Nexus Force members make another move, I'll have this one's head for you!" said Preciponus. She pulled up a girl who was bounded and gagged tightly, and was powerless against Preciponus. This one act stunned Traylor more than anyone. Was it really her? His ideas against Preciponus had been apparently wrong.
The hostage was none other than Ry herself.

Chapter 14, Part 1.

"What?" Traylor muttered in shock. Was it really Ry? He wasn't really sure what to think.
"Why so surprised, Traylor?" Preciponus smiled grimly. "Ry never took over the Maelstrom. You just thought she did."
"But I saw her turn into the Maelstrom ruler!" said Traylor.
"It wasn't her who took over the Maelstrom. I did. You see, you're probably confused because I'm Ry's twin sister, Preciponus, which is actually my real name."
"T-twin?" stuttered Traylor. Ry had never told him she had a twin. But then again, Traylor never told her he had a little brother.
"Yes," Preciponus said. "I had always lived in my sister's shadow, since she was always the brave one, fighting for the Nexus Force, the heroine everybody loved, blah, blah, blah. Well, I was tired of it.
"But then Ry came home after her second adventure with you, Traylor, and she told me about how this wacko called the Great One almost brought back the Maelstrom. That gave me an idea. What if I could bring back the Maelstrom? I certainly would be more known than my sister ever would be."
"You did this for fame?" Traylor gasped. "You fool!"
"Or am I one?" replied Preciponus. "Now it is my sister who lives in my shadow! I have gotten what I wanted: rule of the universe, and to be known by all!"
"You haven't won yet!" he yelled.
"I already have. You and your friends are trapped. I could simply call on my infinite army of Stromlings to destroy you all at will. Nothing can stop me!"

* * *

Below the battle at the Maelstrom Fortress, at the center of the remains of Crux, was the Maelstrom-infected core of Crux. Its power was immense, and it was glowing purple and black, showing that it belonged to the Maelstrom.
But in that purple in black, something strange was happening. A tiny speck of brilliant blue popped up on the surface of the core. Then another, then another, then another, etc. The Maelstrom core was beginning to get overrun by tiny blue specks. The more that appeared, the less Maelstrom color there was.
Imagination was taking over the core. The Maelstrom's colors were soon wiped off the face of the core, leaving it to glow a brilliant blue once again. The core was now pure Imagination, making Preciponus's words about an "infinite Stromling army" hollow.
But the Maelstrom was through yet. All of the core had been reverted back to Imagination except for in one, little spot, where the Maelstrom remained.

Chapter 14, Part 2.

Traylor knew what he had to do. He and his teammates were surrounded, outnumbered, and overwhelmed, but they had to keep fighting.
But on the other hand, they didn't stand a chance. Was it really worth dying?
Traylor looked up into the sky. The throne room he was in didn't have a ceiling, so he could see the sky clearly. Up in the sky was a constellation of something Traylor really didn't want to see: one half. Traylor had half a day left to destroy the Maelstrom.
He couldn't have fought for one and a half days, though. He had only been there for a few hours.
The Maelstrom must be messing with the days, Traylor thought. I wonder if it even has that power. But something's not right.
Whatever it was, he knew he didn't have long to defeat the Maelstrom before being sent back to the core.
He looked down at his feet, hanging his head in shame.
"Ready to give up?" Preciponus jeered.
Traylor was about to reply, but then he noticed something out of the corner of his eye. His hand was glowing blue under his glove. His power was returning.
He looked at his other hand. That was blue as well. He was at full power.
Traylor looked up at Preciponus, smiling under his helmet. "Nope."
Preciponus stopped grinning. "So be it." As she said this, her Stromling army started to charge towards Traylor and his teammates.
"They're comin'!" cried Fritz.
Traylor knew what to do. He used his power to make an Imagination bomb in the middle of the Stromlings. It blew up almost instantly after it was made, doing severe damage to them.
Traylor then dropped his guns and raised his hand at another Stromling group. He made a solid brick just over the group, which fell to the ground on them, crushing them.
Stromling after Stromling, the army was being demolished by Traylor. And no one knew this more than the horrified Preciponus. She tried to call on more Stromlings, but nothing would happen.
Preciponus grew scared. Very scared. She grabbed Ry and started running away just as Traylor finished the last Stromling in the room.
"Scared?" Traylor shouted to her. A wall of Imagination shot up just in front of the exit to the throne room. "Stay here and fight, coward!"
"No!" screamed Preciponus. Then she wondered something. Should she use her secret weapon?
That was an easy decision.
"Brax!" she screamed at the top of her lungs.
Brax? thought Traylor. He's me! I got rid of him. What's he doing alive?
Did I confuse you in our last conversation? said a voice in the back of his mind. It was that voice that called itself fear.
By showing up, I warned you about Brax, just in a weird way, the voice said.
Weird indeed, thought Traylor. I had no idea that you were there to warn me.
Well, this is what I warned you about.
A giant purple hand burst through the floor in front of Traylor, grabbed him, and pulled him under the fortress. Traylor screamed as he saw what the hand was a part of.
It was a humongous Brax, which came from the speck of Maelstrom that remained on the core. And Traylor knew things were gonna get nasty, as fighting yourself is never easy.

Chapter 15, Part 1.

"Hello, me," Brax said grimly. "Long time no see."
"Brax?" muttered Traylor. "You died when I died!"
"Not so," he replied. "You see, when a Stromling dies, he is sent back to the Maelstrom core, where then he can be called on again later. Since you had a Stromling body, you were brought back to the core, too. How you got normal again I'll never know."
Traylor didn't answer, but instead caused heavy chains to shoot up from the core and wrap around Brax.
"You think this will stop me?" he laughed. He broke the chains an instant later. Traylor stared in amazement at this. How Brax was so huge and powerful confused him, but he needed to be stopped, that was for sure.
"Now," said Brax, "I'm gonna let you go for a little so I can have some more fun with you and your friends."
He pulled the arm he had Traylor in back and threw Traylor with all of his might at the Maelstrom fortress.
Traylor screamed in pain as he was thrown through the bottom of it and burst through the floor of the fortress. After breaking through the floor, he didn't fly up much higher before he crashed to the ground.
He was in the throne room again, and all of the Shadowgun Squadron ran over to help him.
"Traylor!" Rith shouted.
"I'm...fine..." Traylor mumbled weakly.
"What is that thing?" said Volketor.
"It's...Brax," he answered. "The Stromling version of me."
"What are you talking about?" Shadowshocker asked.
"It's complicated. But we need to stop him."
"You can't stop him!" shouted someone who Traylor really didn't want to see at the moment. Preciponus stepped out of the shadows with an unbound Ry being dragged at her side.
"There's a way," Traylor said as he got up from off of the floor, "and I'll find it."
"There's only one way to stop him, and that's through me," she said as she drew a sword. "And that's not happening." She pulled her weak sister up and held her over the hole in the floor Traylor had made. "Move and I'll drop her."
"No!"
Preciponus didn't reply, but her crazy grin grew a little bit wider.

Chapter 15, Part 2.

Traylor had no clue what to do. He couldn't attack Preciponus, or she'd give Ry to Brax. But he couldn't surrender.
Even now he knew that his time here was running out before he would be sent back to the core.
Maybe he had to do what he never wanted to do. He looked at Preciponus.
"Put her down," Traylor said, not so much in a demanding tone as a hopeless one, "because I surrender."
The Shadowgun Squadron and the Nexus Force soldiers solemnly set down their weapons. It was obvious that they were surprised at Traylor's surrender. The battle that could end it all, and he surrendered?
But he did have a point. Brax was pretty much unbeatable, and with a hostage, things would not be pretty if they acted.
Preciponus smiled jeeringly. "By all means, I accept your surrender, Traylor. But you see, I don't like competition, and it's always been a competition for me to beat my sister at the fame game. And I've heard that the best way to get rid of the competition is to get rid of the competitors."
Preciponus dropped Ry into the hole in the floor. Almost immediately, Brax's claw shot up and grabbed her in midair.
"Ry!" he screamed.
"Wait!" taunted Preciponus. "You said you surrendered."
"I told you to keep her alive!"
"Oh, so why should I listen to you?"
Traylor created a sword that formed in his hands out of thin air. It glowed a brilliant blue, showing that it was from pure Imagination.
"So tell me this, Preciponus: if you don't have to listen to me, why should I listen to you?"
Ry screamed from down below, and Traylor knew he didn't have a lot of time to save her.
"Let the battle begin again."

Chapter 15, Part 3.

Traylor swung his sword at Preciponus, but she ducked with ease and cleanly attempted to cut his legs in half. He jumped out of the way just in time.
She's good with a sword, Traylor thought. Like her sister. Traylor knew he couldn't beat her with a sword, so he dropped it and formed a Space Marauder Blaster in his hand. He fired a few rounds at Preciponus, but she dodged with great ease.
I'm gonna have to try a different method of attack, he thought. She's probably most vulnerable in hand-to-hand combat, but I can't do that as long as she has a sword.
Then he got an idea.
"See you," said Traylor as he jumped through the hole in the floor.
"Where're you going?" Preciponus jeered. "I was just getting started! It's not time to run away yet, coward!"
Traylor heard her words but didn't reply as he saw the scene happening below the fortress. Brax was dangling Ry by her foot. Apparently, he liked to have fun with his prey.
Traylor landed on the Imagination core without Brax's notice. Let's see, he thought. Brax is huge, and I'm small enough to get kicked away if he noticed me. I need to get bigger.
As the Warrior of Imagination, this was no problem for him. He instantly started to grow and grow, getting Brax's attention when he was about the same size as him.
"If you move, I'll throw her out there into space," Brax said.
"How many times have I been used as a threat now?" yelled Ry sarcastically.
Traylor knew what to do. He was close to Brax, so he swiftly grabbed the arm Brax was holding Ry in and grabbed Ry out of his hands.
"Go see your sister up there!" Traylor told Ry. He gave her a sword he thought of and gently tossed her up into the fortress. He didn't want to throw her like Brax had thrown him, but the fortress was also too far away to simply set her in.
Brax broke free of Traylor's grip and belted Traylor in the side of the face. He grunted in pain, but returned the favor with an uppercut.
Brax still had his gun arm, so he used that to knock Traylor back, then tried to shoot him, but Traylor kicked him before he fired, messing up his aim. The shot soared into space.
"I guess I'll have to do this the hard way," said Brax, not amusingly. Traylor was almost afraid to find out what he meant by that.

* * *

When Ry had landed in the Maelstrom fortress, she was met by the Shadowgun Squadron, who were fighting her sister.
"Well, well, well," Preciponus said in the middle of the fight. "If it isn't my own sister."
"Stop fighting her!" Ry barked at the rest of the soldiers. Although hesitant and unsure of the reason, they did as they were told.
"I think I had better handle this battle alone," she explained. The minifigs nodded, but stayed behind just in case they were needed.
"Precip," said Ry, "why did you do all this?"
"I figured it was time for a change, Ry," Preciponus said. "You were always more known than me, and now I'm more so than you."
"Was it worth it?" Ry replied as she tightened her grip on her sword of Imagination Traylor gave her.
Preciponus stared Ry in the eye and grinned. "I think it was."
Ry didn't say anything, but swung the sword at her sister. Preciponus parried, then kicked her sister back.
Ry tried again by sweeping the sword low to the ground at her sister's feet, but she jumped over it just in time.
"You don't have to do this, Ry," she said. "You don't have to fight me, you know."
Ry didn't reply.
"If you give up, you could save your own life," Preciponus said as she jabbed the sword at Ry, who pushed the tip away.
"I think your life is the one that's gonna need saving," she retorted. With that, she smacked her sister on the side of the face with the flat side of her sword. Her sister moaned and went down on the ground, conscious but hurt.
"It's over, Precip," Ry said.
"Maybe for me," Preciponus said as she sat up on the ground, "but if I go down, Traylor's going down too."

Chapter 16, Part 1.

Traylor and Brax fought each other hard, but they were at a stalemate. They were too evenly matched to finish each other off. When Traylor would gain the upper hand, Brax would suddenly come back, and vice versa.
By the time Ry had defeated Preciponus, Traylor and Brax were each on their last legs. And with no end to the battle in sight, each of the minifig's attack was weaker each time.
Then Traylor saw something he didn't want to see: an injured and infected Carnegie climbing up on to Brax's shoulder. He probably got up using his grappling hook hand.
Carnegie! thought Traylor. Great. Now I have to fight him, too. Perfect.
But Brax didn't seem to notice him on his shoulder. It was hard for Carnegie to walk on Brax's shoulder because he was moving a lot, which almost caused Carnegie to fall several times.
Carnegie managed to stumble towards Brax's head. He raised his regular hand above his head, revealing the sword in his hand, and stabbed it into Brax's neck.
Brax hadn't noticed Carnegie, but he did notice that. He roared in pain, more like an animal than a person. He grabbed the side of his neck in pain, but he felt Carnegie as he did so.
Brax angrily pulled Carnegie off of his shoulder.
"I thought I smelled a rat," he said. He pulled his arm back past his head, and threw Carnegie at the Maelstrom fortress.
"Carnegie!" Traylor shouted. How could Brax do this to his own brother who, although Traylor didn't know how, turned against the Maelstrom and attempted to destroy Brax.
Brax has gone too far, Traylor thought. He formed a sword in his hand, and, before Brax could react, Traylor stuck the sword into him.
Brax screamed and doubled over in pain, then crumpled to the ground, wheezing. But he wasn't dying; he was only weak.
"You're not dead," Traylor breathed with a hint of anger in his voice.
"I'll never be dead...as long as...Preciponus's got it."

"If I told you...I would be letting you win," Brax said, "and why...would I ever do that?
