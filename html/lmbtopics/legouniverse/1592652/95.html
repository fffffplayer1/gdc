New Universe Year Five Location- Nexus City: Civilian Metropolis Matt I felt the wind rushing past me as I fell, watching the top of the skyscraper quickly move backwards, my mask just barely staying on. My cloak flapped up in the air, and I could barely see what was above me through the ever-constant rain. I managed to spin over some, and I recognized Jackson’s office window. I just passed the thirtieth floor. Grabbing my cloak, I quickly drew into it, trying not to let any body parts out. I watched the road below grow ever closer, and I pulled in my head, bracing for impact. Impact came early. A truck that I hadn’t seen through the rain sped across the road, and I slammed into the top with a sickening thud. I felt the fibers of the cloak underneath me spinning, working to use its protective properties to keep from being too hurt. It bounced me into the air, and I landed back on top of the truck, as it had reached a stoplight. Breathing heavily, I stood up, shaking myself off. I had several bruises, and one cut on my left thigh. This cloak really did work well. Then I groaned when I saw the rip in the side of the cloak. I had no idea what material was used to make it, or how to repair it. I almost took off my mask, but then realized I was still in public, even though no one walked the streets. Then I remembered the creature that had been on top of the skyscraper. Quickly, I scanned the tops of the buildings, searching for the monster I’d battled. I had no idea what it was, but it wasn’t friendly. Or non-lethal. Or small. There was nothing there but the rain, still coming down. I sighed, and turned to jump off the top of the truck. Suddenly, the creature slammed into me. I rolled backwards, the hood of the cloak protecting my head as it slammed into the front of the truck. The creature dug its claws into the top of the truck, screeching to a halt and quietly snarling at my limp figure. Weakly, I got up, trying to regain my strength. I pulled out my wave knife and readied a throwing knife in my other hand. Silently, I stared the creature in the eyes. The creature stared back, the unnerving rumbling in the back of its throat ever constant. Suddenly, it stopped growling. I tensed up. It jumped across the truck in one bound, lunging for my midsection. Quickly, I rolled underneath it, slicing upwards as I did to try to injure the strange animal. The slash missed, and the animal sailed off the top of the truck, digging into the side of a nearby building with its claws. It quickly clawed its way up the building as I jumped up from where I was, silently watching it run. Then it turned around, facing the ground. I squinted my eyes as it jumped towards the truck. A blast of air suddenly encircled the creature, and it burst forward, slamming into me and ripping right through the material of the truck.
