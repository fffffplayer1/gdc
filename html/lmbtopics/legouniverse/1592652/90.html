The Beginning- The Fall of Typhonus: Chapter 3.9
New Universe Year Seven
Location- Ninjago Monastery: Headquarters of the Spinjitsu Ninja

Taken by surprise, Cyclone was throne backwards by the force of the figure’s shield slamming into him. He flew across the Monastery and tumbled in the grass before sliding to a halt and wearily looking upwards.
Now that the creature was out of the shadows, Cyclone could see it more easily. It looked almost exactly like a Rank Three Knight, except everything blue was now purple, it was glowing purple, some of the armor was ripped and you could see a skeleton underneath, and it was purely emanating chaos.
It held up a glowing purple sword and pointed it at Cyclone, tilting its head.
“Voltaic?” Cyclone said hesitantly. “Is that… you?”
The creature nodded again. Then it charged. Again.
Cyclone quickly sidestepped. “You’re not Voltaic, are you?”
The creature nodded, and charged. Cyclone ducked underneath its outstretched arm, slicing upwards with his katana. The sharpened blade cut clean through the corrupted armor, and left a deep cut in the Stromling’s arm.
Flipping upright, Cyclone spun around to see the Stromlings growling at its new injury.
He grinned. Now it was time to finish this fight.
Cyclone drew a portion of Imagination from his body, and mentally spread it throughout his body, until he was glowing. The blue glow quickly turned purple, and the purple gas absorbed the molecules making up his body.
Then the gas shot forward, releasing Cyclone about a foot away from the Stromlings. He flew out and cut clean through the creature, landing on the other side while it exploded behind him.
All this happened in about one second.
Cyclone frowned. Normally after performing the Shinobi Rush, he was exhilarated. But this time, he just felt… weird.
He brushed himself off, and picked his shovel back up, continuing to dig.

Voltaic saw the walls around him shudder, seeming to pulse. Almost as if they were alive.
He frowned at them. What was it with those walls that made him so nervous?
They pulsated again, giving off a faint breathing noise. This place seemed alive… Could that be possible? Maelstrom chasms… alive?
He shook his head.
“I’m scaring myself,” he murmured, standing up. “Time to look for a way out.”

The Demon King Chronicles- The Ruins of Nexus Tower: Excerpt
New Universe Year Two Hundred Forty-Two
Location- Nexus Tower: Ruins of the Nexus Force

Vanda quickly jumped back, startled by the decaying skeleton of one of Hael’s old parrots on the ground. The bones were completely free of skin; they were decaying because the skeleton was so old. But it seemed as if the skin had been eaten off… And she knew Jake wouldn’t eat a dead parrot’s skin.
“Metru?” she called out, still startled. Still half-asleep, Metru stumbled into the hallway of Nexus Tower, disconnected wires strewn all around the area, and walls crumbling, yet still stable enough to hold up the two floors above them.
“What?” he asked. He sounded more awake than before, which was good.
“Look at this parrot. What to you think?” Vanda asked. Metru shook himself awake, and leaned down to look at the dead bird.
“It’s kinda creepy.”
“Yeah, I got that already,” Vanda said sarcastically. “But, look here. See how the-“
Vanda stopped in the middle of her sentence and doubled over, falling to the ground, while grabbing her stomach. Metru reacted quickly quickly, and bent down to help her up, while she let go of her stomach.
“It still hurts?” he asked, concern in his eyes.
She nodded, regaining her breath while she felt the stitches that held together the wound in her stomach. With Metru’s help, she stood up again, shakily.
More serious now, Metru knelt down, concentrating on the parrot lying on the ground. “Weird,” he murmured. “It’s almost as if the skin was eaten off… Vanda! Where did you put Jake’s journal?”
Vanda was lying against the wall. She pointed back down the corridor, and Metru rushed down that way, coming back a few seconds later holding the old, tattered remains of Duke Jake Exeter’s journal.
He flipped through the pages, quickly skimming the crazed writings of an old man, and the detailed drawings that Jake had had all the time in the world to draw. He stopped when he came to a page with a picture a parrot’s skeleton, no skin on them at all. He began to read aloud the writing on the page.
“’Today is the tenth year since everything fell apart. Unfortunately, this means that the battery on Nexus Tower went down yesterday, as it hasn’t been officially used in ten years. Hopefully there nothing dangerous being held back by anything electronic. But I’m beginning to doubt that.’
“’The other day I found this. All bones, no skin. Poor old parrot; he had kept me company these ten lonely years. But I found it disturbing that one day, he’s an elderly bird, and the next day, a complete skeleton. Strange…’”
Metru frowned at the paper. He quickly flipped ahead several pages, coming to a page that had a drawing of something that looked like a floating, young girl. Except her eyes were completely white, and she had an expressionless, emotionless face. He began to read again, while Vanda listened, now interested.
“’It’s been a month since the electricity shut down, and there’s no denying it. Something walks these halls with me. I heard it singing one night, sleeping on my makeshift bed, and of course I was interested; I haven’t heard another living soul speak in ten years.’
“’I walked around a bit, following the voice. I came to floor one hundred sixty-three, the vault floor. It seemed that all the vault doors were held electronically, as every single one was open. Though the majority were empty, there was one I knew had never been emptied. Vault thirteen.’
“’The old space pirate, whose name I shall not say, for my hatred of him is too deep, had kept something dangerous in vault thirteen. I saw the vault open, and I can only assume that whatever I heard singing was the creature that was inside the wretched vault. I caught a glimpse of it last night, and I’ve never seen something so frightening and innocent at the same time. Its eyes were completely white, and it looked like it was eight or nine years old, with flowing white hear. It had pale skin, and was wearing a beautiful dress.’
“’But it floated above the ground, and I could see fangs in its mouth. Fangs with parrot skin still on them. I’ve deemed it the Siren, as it always sings. Though the singing is not magically enchanting, it is quite lovely, and I would advise anyone reading this to stay away from it all costs. If you ever hear this song, run.’”
“’Round we go, the world is spinning.
When it stops, it’s just beginning.
Sun comes up, we live and we cry.
Sun goes down-‘”
“And then we all die…”
“How did you know the song, Vanda?” Metru asked, looking up from the book. Vanda was shaking, and her eyes were wide.
“I didn’t sing that.”

New Universe Year Seven
Location- Unknown

“You’ve gone among them now, have you John? Pretending to be one of their injured? This seems like a coward’s move.”
“Not a coward’s move. This is a coward’s game. All the brave recruits down there? They’ll never be players. They have no idea what’s really going on.”
“If they did, do you think they’d be down there?”
“That depends. What do you say is really going on?”
“A petty feud between two old friends.”
“I don’t see how a war of this size is petty.”
“I meant me and you.”
“Did you, now?”
“When did it happen?”
“When did what happen?”
“When did you go bad all of a sudden? I used to know you as the nice old man who helped out at my favorite amusement park. You were always nice to everyone. But then…”
“I’ve always been liked this.”
“No, you haven’t! I’ve known for years, and this is not you. You are different. There is another, greater man inside. You built great inventions, brought amazing things from all sorts of time periods!”
“You don’t get it! My meddling with time is what completely destroyed the area! I wanted to get out of there!”
“No you didn’t. Something else wanted you to get out of there. The question is what…”
“You think I’m possessed!?”
“Either that or you’re a complete, utter mastermind. Which I doubt.”
“I don’t doubt the fact that I am a mastermind.”
“And I don’t doubt the fact that you’re not. The compromise would be right in the middle, so at half of a mastermind. Masterminds have wit, as they’re smart. So, this makes you a half-wit.”
“Must you turn everything against me?
“I’ll stop when you’re no longer turning on me.”
“I have stopped. I’m not turning; I’ve turned.”
“Then turn back, would you?”
“I’ve almost reached my goal, and you want me to turn back? Just how crazy have gone, cooped up in here all day?”
“I was already crazy. I think I’m a bit more sane than usual.”
“The Truths will make me strong.”
“One who is able to turn his back from something like the Truths doesn’t need the strength it will give. He would obviously be strong enough to do what he knows is right.”
“Doing what’s hard and doing what’s right aren’t always the same thing.”
“Most of the time, they are.”
