Chapter 20 Part 1:

 

Harold skated along the floor, sword drawn, directly towards Duke Exeter, who smashed the maelstrom minifigure to the side before spinning around and knocking him to the ground with one strike. However, Harold leapt back up into the air, and threw a fireball at Duke as he came back down. Duke blocked the projectile with his shield and swiped again at Harold, who teleported away and rematerialized behind Duke, prepared to attack him from behind. However, Duke flipped over the sword and kicked Harold in the head, before blasting him to the opposite side of the room with another burst of imagination.

 

Harold, instead of rising straight away, took time clambering to his feet, and stood there, swaying as if there was a breeze. Suddenly a wave of energy flooded the room, causing all thought present to almost fall over, or at least stumble.

 

"Oh no..." Will murmered as the group regained their balance.

 

Suddenly Harold charged, even faster than before. Minifigures stood in his way to protect Duke, but in vain.

 

He Ploughed through Dillip and Stu, effortlessly tossed Jacen away, side-slammed Vector and threw Gaia directly into Rose and Shadow. Kaylori wrapped her tail around his leg, but he just pulled her towards him before knocking her down. Will raised his pistol and stepped in front of Duke, only to be blasted backwards by a bolt of lightning. Duke raised his shield and defelected Harold's attacks repeatedly. Webbing, lasers and fire were all blocked. But then, Harold phased into the floor and reappeared behind Duke, before grabbing him and tossing him into the nearest wall. Before anything else could happen, there was an almighty explosion, and the room was revealed to be, once again, directly next to open space. The wall opened up, and sucked several items and minifigures out of the room before any emergency shield activated.

 

Including Duke Exeter.

 

Oh Noes.

Chapter 20 Part 2:

 

All throughout the room, there was silence. No-one could begin to fathom what their friend had just done, what this mean for the Nexus Force. The demise of the leader of the Sentinel Faction and the Nexus Force as a whole would take a long time to rectify, and... the cause was Harold.

 

"What HAVE you done?" Someone said, Will didn't know who. Everyone else, even the maelstrom minifigure himself, was lost for words. The fire faded from Harold's eyes, and when he looked back at Will, Will could see that his friend was back in control of himself. But at a terrible price.

 

"I... I didn't..." Harold choked. But Will knew what he was saying. "It wasn't me. I didn't do it. Please." 

 

Will was trying to figure out, whilst everyone else was shell shocked, where the explosion had come from, when suddenly there was a gunshot and Harold went down, cotamose before he even hit the floor. Startled, Will turned to see Doctor Overbuild holding a gun in his hands.

 

"Merely a tranquilizer." He declared. "Now, what's going on here?"

 

"Duke Exeter. He... he's gone..."

 

Overbuild visibly paled, in a performance so convincing Will almost believed it himself.

 

"Duke? No... no, no... not Duke. Please not Duke."

Overbuild shook, then appeared to regain his composure. "Tell me exactly what happened."

Hey. Hey, look. SNEAK PEAK 2!

 

Skye was slow to fire, and she found herself pinned down by the metallic being. The thing was several feet tall, noticeably taller than her, with spikes all over its hunched back and a frightening staff in its hand. The front of the armour, in the place where a head would normally be, split open to reveal a creature that seemed to be in control, some kind of slug or snake. The creature screeched in her face, until Sabriel fired several shots at it. The bullets ricocheted off the armour, but the creature was at least distracted, allowing Skye to slip out of its grasp and knock it backwards with a blast of electricity. She turned round just in time to see Sabriel get picked up by another of the creatures, helpless.

 

Suddenly, in what Skye had to consider being one of the stupidest actions she had ever had the misfortune of witnessing, Gaia threw herself on top of the creature, straddling it and hanging onto its neck for dear life. Sabriel dropped from the being’s grip and emptied both of her pistols into its midsection. The creature, although not physically hurt, was forced to retreat as the motion of so many bullets hitting it doubtlessly caused it to be nauseated – Skye had experienced such a feeling herself, and she found it hard to not feel sympathy for the revolting thing.

 

Suddenly, another one sprang from nowhere and fired a green ray of energy from its staff. It was about to hit Gaia, but Skye sprung in front of her new friend at the last possible moment, and was instead blasted. Instantly she experienced a pain like no other she had felt before. Not different in that it was the most agonizing or excruciating experience of her life, far from that. But it made her feel so cataclysmically ill, like her abdomen was dissolving, that when she hit the ground she was in no way ready to rise to her feet again.
