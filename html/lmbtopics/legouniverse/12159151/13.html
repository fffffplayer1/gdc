Chapter 18 Part 4:

 

"I hope you know what you're doing." Remarked Duke. He had summoned the entire sentinel faction to his aid - not wanting to take a chance, even though he was still dubious about the whole story. He had also contacted Hael Storm, who had sent a token force of Venture League soldiers. Duke didn't try Assembly or Paradox. Will had explained the whole thing to him, and the last thing Duke wanted was to risk unloyal troops. Right now he was busy shouting orders at the troops as they entered the teleporter one by one.

 

"Okay, your turn Rick! No pushing! Send Damian over there, I'll talk to him later! Stirling, Peregrine is NOT your tackle bag! Jess, WHAT are you doing with that? Yes that way, how many teleporters do you see?"

 

Eventually, the soldiers of the Nexus Force made it through the teleporter, and lastly the group entered, arriving back inside the cursed place. Instantly, Will recived a message via his communicator, a piece of tech the entire Nexus Force carried. Answering, he heard Rose's voice on the other end.

 

"Rose? You're alive!" He exclaimed, getting funny looks from several minifigures around him.

 

"Not for much longer!" He heard her yell on the other side, almost muffled by the sound of chaos. "I hope you brought backup?" 

 

"The entire sentinel faction."

 

"It'll have to do. Just get down here quickly, REALLY BAD things are going down. You know where we are?"

 

"Recieving co-ordinates now."

 

"Good. Now hurry up, please!"

 

Will switched off the communicator, and began sprinting down the corridor. Behind him, he could hear the sounds of the sentinels and Ventures following his lead.

 

After finally arriving at the co-ordinates, with Kaylori and Duke Exeter at his side, Will threw open the doors.

Hey? You guys want a sneak peak of another story? You don't? Too bad.

 

“Hello? Hello? Is this the PI, Skye Veil?” The caller said. Skye analysed the voice quickly. It was a female voice, rough, and unanxious. Skye had the feeling she wasn’t just some worried citizen turning to the only help they could find, like most of her clients were. She supressed her anxiety at this new client’s attitude, and answered the call.

 

“Yeah, that’s me.” She confirmed.

 

“Great.” Replied the caller. “Listen, I need you to do me a favour.”

 

Skye rolled her teeth. Great. Less than thirty seconds into the call and already she was wishing it was over.

 

“Listen, I’m a PI, not a charity worker. I don’t do favours.”

 

The caller, whoever she was, chuckled; not the reaction that Skye had been expecting.

 

“Pretty confident for a fifteen-year old, aren’t you?”

 

Skye felt her blood run cold.

 

“How do you know my age?”

 

“You’re not the only one who can snoop around and get away with it.”

 

Skye folded her arms, fear giving way to the return of exasperation, albeit slightly less mild than previously.

 

“Alright, not bad. But just because you know a thing or two about me doesn’t make me eager to help you, I hope you realise that?”

“Oh, I do. That’s why our payment is also quite unconventional.”

 

“Payment?” Skye leaned forward in her seat. “I hate to sound shallow, but the fact is that NOW you’re talking my language!”

 

“Glad to hear that.” Remarked the caller, with a hint of callousness in her voice. Skye decided to, despite her indignation, neglect that fact. This was her first paying client in almost two months, and the intrigue of how the woman already knew more about Skye than visa-versa intrigued her, dragging her in more efficiently than a top-of-the-line fishing rod.

 

“So what’s the case?” 

The Maelstrom Minifigure, Knight Of The Old Speech, Time Lord.

Chapter 19 Part 1:

 

Will only needed to take a single look to tell just how bad things had become inside the room. Whilst the rest of the minifigures cowered or were trapped under debris, Harold and the Darkitecht were having a showdown in the center of the room. Both were harnessing the entire maelstrom spectrum and using in against their opponent. In a few moments alone Will saw mist, fire, webs and teleportation all used. The two sides seemed to be evenly matched - neither could land a single blow on the other, as they were both using phasing and teleportation to escape being hit.

 

The bad news? Will could sense, could feel, that the Darkitecht was holding back. This was nothing compared to what he and other minifigures had felt when they were close to the heart of the maelstrom, for instance on the Venture Explorer. The Darkitecht was clearly toying with his friend, waiting till he tired and then dealing the finishing blow. Harold, on the other hand, was quickly expending a lot of his energy, and wouldn't last much longer.

 

Thankfully, not everyone was as awestruck and paralysed as he was. Duke Exeter stepped forward and roared, 

 

"Concentrate fire on the Darkitecht!"

 

Instantly the room was lit up as the Sentinels and Ventures opened fire on the lord of chaos. The Darkitecht saw the projectiles aimed at him, and instantly seemed to fold in on himself and disappear. 

 

"Cease fire! Cease fire!" Yelled Duke, but some of his soldiers had by this point become overexited and hyper, and with the Darkitecht gone instead aimed their weapons at Harold. The lasers and bullets flew towards the maelstrom minifigure, who randomly waved his arms and either dissolved or deflected the shots. 

 

"STOP!" Roared Will, and at last the firing ceased.

Chapter 19 Part 3:

 

Harold, instead of replying, pounded the ground with his fist, causing a shockwave to ripple across the room. Some stumbled - others fell.

 

Will bit his lip and struggled to control his fear as his friend seemed, in metaphorical terms, to suddenly tower over everyone else in the room. Whilst no match for the Darkitecht, he was still the extremely powerful at this particular moment, and he didn't seem to be in control of himself either. All Will saw, when he looked into Harold's eyes, was malevolance and hate, so strong he felt as if he could see them in his friend's eyes.

 

"What happened, do you think?" Kaylori muttered, both of them keeping their gaze on Harold in case he did anything.

 

Will shrugged. "I don't know. Perhaps the fight with the Darkitecht threw him out of whack-"

 

Harold suddenly slumped to his knees and groaned, practically on fire with indeigo flames as this point. He looked up, and Will could see his expression soften.

 

"Will?" He gasped. "What's... I don't know..."

 

"Shush." Will hissed. "It's ok, right Duke?"

 

Duke nodded. "I think the crisis has passed." Duke turned on his headset and began giving orders, starting to unposition the snipers stationed around the room. Duke was no fool, and knew what he had been getting himself into, so the second they had entered the room he had sent groups of sentinels around the room, and the upper levels in particular, to act as a safety protocol. Now that it looked like Harold had calmed down to an extent where he would be willing to co-operate, Duke began acting and calling his troops back down.

 

However, one trooper remained. He was a rookie, Private Gunnerby, and things didn't look ok from where he was stationed. He intended to obey Duke's order, but things looked suspicious. He could see that maelstrom minifigure full of power, the others cowering before him. However, what happened next wasn't entirely his fault. He couldn't have predicted it.

 

Gunnerby aimed and fired the sniper rifle.

 

Directly at Harold.

The bullet whizzed through the air, heading directly for Harold's head. It looked like it was going to hit him, but at the last moment Plue leapt in front of him. The bullet smacked into her and the jolt sent her flying off askew, landing awkwardly on the ground like a broken toy.

As Gunnerby was about the fall into the lasers, Kaylori leapt up with all the strength in her legs, making a gargantuan leap and catching Gunnerby, before grabbing onto the wall. Harold ceased his fire as Kaylori leapt daintily to the ground, and the surrounding Nexus Force troops cheered at seeing Gunnerby saved.

 

Harold then turned back to Will, and Will blanched as he saw the weakness in Harold's eyes had completely gone, replaced with confusion and hate.

 

"You did this." He rasped.

 

"What?" Gasped Will, waving his hands frantically as he focused on the sight of Kaylori kneeling over Plue, along with the other misfits. "No, no, no, I didn't do this Harold!"

 

"You lied to me." Harold continued.

 

"You shot at me."

 

"I didn't shoot at you! We can still sort this out, I know we can-"

 

"I KNOW." Roared the Maelstrom Minifigure. "THAT YOU'RE MY ENEMIES! ALL OF YOU!"

 

The troopers, having relazed, quickly raised their weapons again.

 

"Nononononono..." Will moaned as he stood between his super-powered best friend and over a quarter of the Nexus Force.

 

Duke stalked forward, raising his legendary sword as he did so.

 

"Concede." He challanged.

 

"I WILL NOT!" Yelled Harold, rushing forward seemingly at the speed of light with his sword drawn.

 

Unbeleavably, Duke parried the blow, and retorted in kind by swinging his shield at Harold fluidly, who batted away and shot lighting out of his sword, which Duke countered with a burst of imagination from his own.

 

"I guess this is why they describe Duke Exeter as "legendary" in the faction briefings." Muttered Kaylori from Will's side.
