C.B.

I've never actually played Portal, but I know that pretty much you're this guy who runs around and can teleport, and there's this other guy who wants to kill you. I just want to know if you actually get killed in the famous "part where he kills you". Also, Portal is the source of the false cake. I know that was really helpful.

TheGuardianABT, Alex003ABT, JoeSchmoe

That, my friend is the most basic description of portal in duh world.

You play as Chell, a test subject at Aperture Laboratories for the Quantum Tunneling device, otherwise known as the portal gun. Under the instruction of a tyrannical AI dubbed GLaDOS(genetic lifeform and disc operating system) you complete tests that defy the laws of physics, and escape when GLaDOS attempts to kill you. She tries to lure you back with the promise of cake, and you find her chamber for the final confrontation. You kill her, but are dragged back to aperture by another AI.

In the sequel, you awake a good 12 years later(still as Chell) Out of a statis hybernation thing. You attempt to escape with Wheatley, a personality core, and some how end up near the ruins of GLaDOS' chamber. Wheatley finds the main breaker room, and, in an attempt to turn the lights on to find the escape pod switch, reactivates GLaDOS, who crushes Wheatley and sends you down into the incinerator to retrieve the dual portal device. You find that Wheatley somehow survives the crushing and breaks you out. You destroy her nuerotoxin and turret manufacturing and end up in her chamber again via vaccuum tube. GLaDOS attempts to kill you, but weaponless, the lab's computer interprets her vain attempts into the fact that she is corrupted and you transfer wheatley and GLaDOS' cores, putting Wheatley in control of the facility. Corrupt with power, he turns GLaDOS into a potato battery (in this form, some call her potatOS) and banishes you to the old aperture labs, where you learn about the history of apeture and how GLaDOS came to be. You befriend GLaDOS and make your way up to the Wheatley controlled labs. He puts you through simple tests. He then traps you and tried to kill you in vain. He leads you to his chamber and tries to kill you there, but defeat him by sending him into space via portal and put GLaDOS in control of the facility again. She lets you go, claiming to not need you anymore after building atlas and p-body, the main characters of the co-op campain. As GLaDOS sends you up the lift to the surface, turrets empty of bullets us the gas provided by the shooting of the nonexistent bullets sing a song. The game ends as you find yourself in the middle of a field. GLaDOS sings "want you gone" in the credits.

I feel like a nerd, knowing all of this stuff O_O
