*At an unprecedented location, Karunax's entire body lurches as color floods back into his eyes.*

 

"Hrrrggh!!!"

 

Hold still! Your Antidermis still needs time to regenerate...

 

*Karunax jerks again, his eyes flashing around every which way.*

 

"What... ?? Who is that?!"

 

Karunax, shut up. Inside voice.

 

You?! What did I say abou-... ?!

 

I just saved your goshdarn life, pal. After that stupid stunt you pulled... you're just lucky I work fast. Now please, relax and let me work.

 

I can't move...

 

Yeah, getting to that.

 

Where am I?

 

I've no idea where we are. Not yet, anyway. Initiating restorative subroutines. Calm down, but keep talking; I need you to focus on something.

 

Uuurrgh...

 

So what the heck is wrong with that guy that you fought? Isn't he a Toa? A hero?

 

Pffft.

 

Don't Toa have a code or something?

 

They're supposed to, but Jalkron stopped caring, and he got a couple proxies to think the same way.

 

The way they acted was... shocking. Wicked, I'd say. D'you really think you could beat them on your own?

 

It didn't matter. Had I won, it would've only been a bonus.

 

Why're they so deviant?

 

They were consumed by hatred for what the Brotherhood did, stood for, and intended to do. We made them what they are; into something the Matoran and even other Toa fear. And I count that as a victory.

 

Yeah, well, you're not going to see many more victories if you keep acting with such bravado.

 

It was necessary. I just needed to be assured of something, and I ended up being assured of two things.

 

And what might those be?

 

One: that my mission's still worth living for--I was too close to giving up. You may find it difficult to understand, but regardless of my defeat and near-destruction, I actually felt alive for the first time in forever.

And two: that the good old days are long past. It's been long coming, but I now understand just how obsolete I've become to the world. My might was once unparalleled and feared, but now I cannot even touch my opponents most of the time. I'm in dire need of an update.

BlueBugaboo wrote:

 

Nevertheless, a throne awaits him at the center of the city. Slowly, proudly, the Makuta seats himself upon it. He had noted the scuffed buildings, shabby streets, hunkered citizens.  A mantra, bouncing and spinning about, one that he would have to teach to his new people. The Light is within us all. From Light, Hope. From Hope, Courage. From Courage, Peace. Such is the way of the Enlightened.

The makuta stands, and more closely surveys his surroundings.  The buildings are sparse and bare, volcanic ash scattered through unkept streets.  A poor foundation.  He gestures, and a group of agori and matoran form up behind him.  He marches up the slope of the volcano, scanning the terrain.  Here and there, he pauses, evaluating the surroundings, but none pass muster.  Finally, he emerges into the crater itself, and here he finally stops.  Motionless at the peak, he stares vacantly across the broad, lava-filled basin.  Then, he slowly brings his hands together, a look of intense concentration on his face.  As he slowly spreads them apart, a flickering cube of radiance forms between his palms.  it flashes in a multitude of color, never settling for a singular answer.  It splits into threads of shining color, each one its own hue, each weaving ever smaller designs.  Slowly a picture becomes clear.  An impossibly intricate city, suspended between his hands.  Then, with a flourish, he flings his hands apart entirely, and the image is sent spinning out over the crater.  As it travels, the threads still connected to the makuta are drawn taut, and it warps, spreading out as it travels.  Finally, it settles across the entire crater, an immense city, eerily ephemeral.  For the first time, the makuta finally speaks.

 

"Behold, the dream is cast.  The rest is yours."

 

With sudden, frantic energy, the villagers go to work, beginning the bare groundwork of the city before them.
