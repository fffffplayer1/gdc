Prolouge Chapter 1:

 

Jack Falcon sat on his seat in the Nimbus Station diner, staring at the colourful scenery and good company and whishing he was somewhere else. He had been assigned by his employers to watch the area and wait for a contact. But he couldn't get sucked into the interesting enviroment like everybody else. He found himself slipping into a stupor, waiting for this whole thing to be over. It had taken him almost fifteen minuites to get INTO the diner, owing to the large amounts of security outside. He muttered crossly to himself.

 

"Even though the maelstrom wars ended 20 years ago, the Nexus Alliance is still paranoid about the maelstrom returning." 

 

The Nexus Alliance was a government that had been set up from the renmants of the Nexus Force once the war was over. As governments went, they weren't too shabby, but the one thing that nobody appreciated was the level of security around every area of higher class than a house made of mud. The only reason Jack had been able to get into the diner so quickly was because his employers, the Alliance themselves, had given him special clearance. Appareantly this contact was very important to them, like Jack really cared. All he wanted was to get this over with, collect his pay, and go home. 

 

At this point, a man stepped into the club door. There was very little to be noticed from his apearance- most of his form was covered by a long black coat that went down to his ankles. His eyes were covered by a pair of dark anti-flash glasses, and the only outstanding feature on the man was the short brown hair that contradicted the colour scheme of his clothing. 

 

The man stepped into the diner, and looked around for a few seconds before purposly making his way towards Jack. As he grew closer, Jack observed the stranger's long legs and dark jeans, now visible as the man had opened his trench coat on arrival. The stranger grabbed a chair and placed it down beside Jack. Leaning forward, Jack sighed and prepared to do buisness.

Prolouge Chapter 2:

 

The stranger sat down, and leaned over to Jack.

 

"Circuit." The stranger said.

 

This had been the agreed codeword between Jack and the client. Jack nodded and, from his pocket, withdrew a bag containing what would normally be money, but today was specially filled with imaginite gems. Their emerald glow illuminated the two men's faces, and the client briefly smiled. In response, he reached into his coat with a gloved hand, and pulled out a bag containing something relatively small, yet heavy. The pair exchanged packages and shook hands. The client nodded.

 

"Pleasure doing business with you." The client grinned. 

 

Jack nodded his appreciation for the client's politeness, and then pulled out a gun from a hidden compartmant in the diner. At the same time, various other undercover agents for the Alliance whipped out weapons. It was easy to tell who was a real civillian, because on seeing the firearms they screamed and fled from the diner. Jack motioned with the gun and the stranger raised his arms in the air. 

 

"I trust," said Jack, "You'll understand that the Nexus Alliance can't risk anyone knowing about their affairs, so I've been ordered to terminate you."

 

"Oh I understand." Said the client. "That's why I wouldn't reccomend you do this."

 

"Fool." Spat Jack, as one agent walked up behind the client with a sword raised. The client turned round just in time to catch the downswinging blade on his arm. But instead of smashing, bleeding or even showing signs of pain, an eerie metal clang was heard, and the client grinned a dark grin. 

 

In contrast, Jack's face fell. "What's the matter with you?" He murmered. But the client grabbed the agent's throat with the same arm that had taken the blow, and lifted him clean off the ground before turning to Jack.

 

"You wouldn't want to know." Declared the client, before impossibly throwing the agent across the room. The agent slammed into a wall with bone crushing force, and Jack realised he was in deep, deep trouble.

Prolouge Chapter 3:

 

Jack shot four bullets at the client, hoping for at least one to make their mark. But he was disappointed. One ricoched off the client's strange arm, two missed, and one was about to hit the client's eye when it seemed to veer a 70 degree angle in the other direction, and Jack could have sworn he saw a strange purple flash. 

 

The remaining agents (five of them, not including Jack) exchanged nervous looks. The client snapped his fingers, and a purple mist started to fog up the room. Immediatly things started to go from bad for worse for the agents, as the fog thickened until Jack could barely see a few inches in front of his face. Suddenly a groan of pain came from his right. than another from his left. This continued for a few minuites until there were no more screams. 

 

"Agents! Respond!" Jack ordered. "Give your unit identification and describe your location!" This might not have been the best idea, but in all fairness Jack was panicking. This meeting had not gone to plan. 

 

There was no reply.

 

The smoke suddenly and quickly receded, to show Jack he was indeed the only one left. The mysterious client was standing in the middle of the room, with the last strands of purple mist clinging to him. Around him lay the smashed pieces of the agents. 

 

"Wh... what did you do?!" Jack exclaimed, caution and fear giving way to hysteria.

 

The client shrugged nochalantly. "What you were planning to do to me. I was simply returning the favour."

 

Suddenly the client sped towards Jack with an unrealistic speed. Anticipating the blow, Jack raised his arms in front of him. The blow knocked him backwards, but the client was already swinging at him with the other hand. This time Jack managed to dodge, and saw that the blow had, behind him, smashed into the bar and had sent wood chips firing. 

 

"WHAT ARE YOU?!" Jack shrieked.

 

"The destoryer of corruption. And you, my friend, are corrupt."

 

Jack prepared to fight for his life.

Prolouge Chapter 4:

 

The client raised his arm again, and  fired LIGHTNING from his hand. It arced to the side as the client aimed it directly at Jack. Jack, in response, dived under the beam and fired again at the client's face. All the bullets, however, were destroyed or redirected by the electricity. Jack tried to dive behind the counter, but the lightning smashed into said counter and sent Jack flying. Jack landed on the floor, dazed, as the client walked over and pressed a foot onto his chest, meaning he couldn't rise. Jack desperatly tried to pull himself up, and in doing so grabbed the client's arm and pulled the glove clean off by accident. 

 

When Jack raised his eyes, he couldn't believe what he saw.

 

The whole of the client's arm was a machine. Beneath a metal coating, Jack could see wires, circuits, and moving parts. Jack suddenly realised where the blasts of electricity had come from. He also noticed that there was a broken bottle on a nearby table. But there was little time to dwell on that. The client reached down with the bionic arm, grabbed Jack by the throat and lifted him into the air. 

 

"You work for the Nexus Alliance, don't you?" The client hissed. Jack glared at him.

 

"I work for whoever pays." He choked, whilst reaching for the bottle. The client noticed a second before Jack acted, but it was too late. Jack yelled, and smashed the bottle onto the side of the client's face. Glass flew, but no blood. When Jack looked again, he realised all he had done was dislodge the client's anti-flash glasses, and revealed the client's eyes. Jack gasped.

 

One of the client's eyes, the right, was a deep, malevolent purple. It screamed evil, and darkness. The other eye was an electric blue, and surrounded by metal and circuit instead of flesh.

 

"You shouldn't have done that." The client declared, and raised his bionic arm. He leaned close to Jack as the arm powered up. "This will show the the Alliance that... I'm coming for them."

 

The arm fired again, and Jack felt no more.

Chapter 1 part 1:

 

It was raining heavily as Solara walked down the busy street, trying to avoid bumping into anyone and looking down at the same time. The raindrops caressed her face as they trickled down from her blonde hair, towards her chin, and eventually fell to the ground. She repeatedly blinked, the reflex action protecting her Imaginite-coloured eyes from the rain. Sighing greyly to herself, she turned a corner to see her place of work. Only, something was wrong.

 

The diner that she normally worked in looked damaged. The exterior seemed alright, but through the windows Solara could see smashed furniture and... were those minifigure pieces? Repulsed yet intrigued, she pushed past the small crowd that had gathered, only to find that was as far as she was to go. Police officers from the Nexus Alliance were holding the crowd back, denying all news reporters and curious bystanders access to the scene. Behind them, Solara could see bricks and pieces of minifigure being loaded into cars and driven away. 

 

"Guess the Nexus Alliance really want the evidence to be removed." Solara thought to herself. She approached an officer.

 

"Excuse me." She said, getting t he officer's intention. "Could I go in please? I'm an employee and I'd like to look at this for myself."

 

"Sorry Ma'am." Replied the officer politely, yet firmly. "Can't allow anyone in. There were casulties."

 

"I'm aware of that officer, and I'm sure I could handle myself. What if I promise not to touch anything?" She asked sarcastically. The officer briefly chuckled to himself, then regained his composure. 

 

"Still wouldn't be able to let you in, Ma'am." He replied gravely. "Government buisness. Top secret."

 

"Alright," condeded Solara, "thank you." She walked away. But she wasn't giving up. She had a curious streak, and if it was the last thing she did she would find another way in.

 

 

 

What do you guys think? My first piece of writing with a female protagonist! Woot!

Chapter 1 Part 2:

 

As Solara walked away from the diner, she studied her surroundings. Upon noticing an alleyway between the diner and another building, she ran into it, hoping to find a way up. She was in luck. A drainage pipe led all the way up the building. Solara hauled herself up and onto the roof, taking great care not to get spotted to a bystander or a policeman. Clambering on the wet slates, she nearly lost her nerve; but repressed those feelings of self-preservation, a burning curiosity and thirst for excitement driving her on. 

 

After sliding around for a bit with nothing happening, Solara eventually noticed an opening in the roof. It hadn't been there the previous day, and looked ragged as if it had been blasted into existance (Note: it had) but it would suffice. She peered over the edge in an attempt to view the scene. Now that she had a clear view, she could see that things were just as bad as she had glimpsed earlier. There were scattered minifigure pieces, broken tables and chairs, drinks across the floor, and a smashed bottle in one corner of the room next to a minifigure that looked like he had been fried. (Hi Jack ) But, as Solara peered in closer, the damaged roof gave way beneath her feet, and she quickly plummeted to the ground. However, her arm reflexively grabbed a support rail in the roof as she fell, so now she hung over the scene by her arms, which were quickly tiring.

 

By some outrageous miracle no police officers had noticed her, but Solara decided not to think about the consequences if she was found here without permission. Suddenly, a hooded figure seemed to materialise out of nowhere. The person was dressed almost entirely in black robes, and a purple mist hung about him. Not willing to question a potential saviour, Solara held out her hand for him to take. 

 

Suddenly, the figure raised an arm and cut clean through the beam. Before Solara had time to wonder how this was possible and why someone would do this, she fell to the ground.

Chapter 1 Part 3:

 

Solara didn't have time to scream before she slammed into the ground. She heard a crunch, but couldn't tell if it came from the floor or limbs. After attempting to move, she decided floor. Nothing felt broken, and she could still move everything. Shakily standing up, she looked around the diner. Thankfully the door was closed, so the police hadn't seen her, but they might have heard here. She had little time. The chaos looked even worse up close, and it was all she could do to not close her eyes. 

 

Abruptly, something caught her attention. Reaching behind the bar, she pulled out what looked like Imaginite, only it glowed a much brighter and stronger glow. It was like a lighthouse compared to a lightbulb. Shielding her eyes, Solara looked around for something to carry it with. She noticed one of the smashed minifigures had a bag nearby him. A satchel, like the one worn by Indiana Jones or used to carry gas masks.

 

"Sorry." Solara murmered before grabbing the satchel-her satchel-and stuffing the Imaginite into it. Suddenly, she heard a faint murmer in the air. She twisted round wildly, and saw the same hooded figure who had cut the support beam standing in the middle of the diner.

 

"You!" Solara exclaimed. "You jerk! Why would you do that?" The hooded figure gave no answer, but instead raised an arm and pointed a finger at the satchel Solara was now carrying. It didn't take long for the penny to drop.

 

"Oh... I see." She declared. "You want the imaginite, don't you?"

 

The whispering increased and the figure gave a slow, stiff nod.

 

"Well, sorry," Solara rolled her eyes, "But I tend not to give potentially valuble objects to idiots who endanger my life."

 

The hooded figure gave the smallest shrug, as if to say "ah well." Then it suddenly charged right at her, giving her no time to react.

 

She raised her hands in front of her face, but knew it was futile.

 

There was a flash of lightning.

Chapter 1 Part 4:

 

The electric bolt arced across the room, smashing into the hooded figure and sending it flying into a wall. Solara looked in awe at her savior, a tall man in a black coat with brown hair dark anti-flash glasses. () He extended an arm which, on close scrutiny, seemed to be made entirely of metal. 

 

"Come with me." He said.

 

Solara had no idea of what was happening, but she'd take cyborg over Mr hood any day. At that precise moment, however, the door was kicked open and a squad of Nexus Alliance policemen entered the room.

 

"There he is!" Someone yelled. "Open fire!" The diner was abruptly filled with bullets. The cyborg grabbed Solara and in one fluid movement flung her behind the bar, before aiming his arm at the police and returning fire with more electric blasts. At the same time, the figure picked itself up from the blast, and started attacking the policemen. Confident the officers would be distracted for a while, the cyborg leaned over the bar to check on Solora. 

 

"Are you alright?" He asked.

 

Solara breathlessly nodded. He pulled her up, and glanced at the satchel at her waist. She cautiously looked at the satchel, then back at the cyborg. He grinned slightly.

 

"Don't worry, I'm not planning on taking it. However, for as long as you have it you will be a wanted minifigure." 

 

Solara looked at the imaginite again. She could stop it all now... and just keep living as normal. But there was her curiosity again. Here was a chance to do SOMETHING, and make a difference for once... for better or worse. She looked back at the cyborg.

 

"Wanted minifigure it is then." She stated. The cyborg nodded, as if to respect her desicion. 

 

"Ok. We need to move. Now." He said. She nodded. The cyborg reached into one of the pockets of his coat, and pulled out a strange looking spherical device.

 

"Hold still." He ordered, before throwing the device down at their feet. The next moment, Solara found herself on a grassy hill, with the cyborg beside her. There was now no going back.
