Chapter Two – Part Four

 

The elder scowled, his face scrunching up into millions of wrinkles, like an apple sat too long to dry. Rory’s hand involuntarily shriveled up into a fist, his anger flowing wildly through him. The dumb creature had just laughed like it didn’t matter; it was all just a big joke. He was working with the beasts that killed Angus. To his surprise, the elder made himself a fist and clouted him on the side of the head for laughing instead of answering.

 

The force of the blow knocked the creature over, which Rory decided might be a goblin, but he wasn’t exactly sure, and he thumped to the ground, unable to pull his face out of the dust since his body was bound. The goblin snorted and used his shoulder to rub his ear casually. “I’m not sure what in the world you want.”

 

The people suddenly started shouts of rage, all giving different answers, several of them repeating what the elder had said. Even Rory found himself whispering an answer. To stop the terror. To bring Angus back.

 

The creature’s ears started to lay back, like he was irritated with the noises. He waited for their rude, demanding cries to stop before speaking. “I still can’t give you any of that!” He shifted, trying to get himself into a less vulnerable position, but failed and sank back to the ground.

 

The elder shook his head violently, and he seemed to consider hitting the goblin again. “Put it away!” At first Rory stared, not sure what he meant by that, but two young men apparently did, and seized the goblin up, grabbing him by his shoulders roughly and dragging him away.

 

Rory watched grimly, his stomach boiling with questions. He knew something. Something in that little laugh tickled Rory’s mind. He knew where those witches were, and he was going to find out. He had run from battle. And now to make up for it, he was going to go looking for one. For Angus.

Chapter Two – Part Five

 

Rory sat in the shadows, legs crossed in the dusty rocks. Above him towered a large tree, its leaves dull and brown even though it wasn’t winter. A cool breeze swirled around him in a content, easy pace, but it couldn’t calm his nerves.

 

His legs were sore and stiff from sitting in that same position all day. He thought he should get up, walk around a bit to keep them from getting locked up and tight, but he never did.

 

Daylight faded slowly, his heart never stopping to slow down. Once the sun sank below the horizon, with just streaks of orange striking the sky, most of the villagers retreated to their homes and he finally stood up, stretching his back.

 

He hadn’t seen where they had taken that goblin, but really in such a small town, it shouldn’t be that hard. Should it? Back when he was smaller, he remembered being in a crowd of people watching someone being dragged into a barred building with strong brick walls, but now as he went to looking, every building here looked exactly the same. Wooden walls. Straw or thatch roof. Large round door with a streak of metal in the middle for support.

 

In distress, Rory tramped past another building. His footsteps became louder and louder the more frantic he became. This tiny town had no ends; it just kept going and going, and it started to freak him out. Head covered in slimy sweat and palms clammy, he finally mustered up the strength to ask for help. Truthfully, he wasn’t sure why he waited until dark to find this goblin guy, but he just felt like it should be secret.

 

He crept up to a random house, close to the old dead tree he had been sitting under. Gingerly, he tapped on the door, squeezing his eyes shut; hoping some maid or something would be sweet and kindheartedly give him the way without questions.
