Chapter One – Part Ten

 

In a flourish of green leaves, a flurry of movement acted like a whirlwind on the earth. Plants leaped out of the way as quickly as they could spring and the men followed in pursuit, dashing out of the way like scared mice. When they jumped back, they then had to jump forward, for they had trapped themselves in the forest between both of the raging dragon beasts.

 

Their dark green scales waved together in a pattern that matched the leaves, and standing together, towering tall, they stood as an undefeatable force. Angus stepped forward, and the rest of the group followed, raising their swords in defiance to the inevitable. The dragons had not expected this, thinking to encounter a group of shriveling cowardly knights that they could destroy without second thought. But this group was different. They had watched their elder die for them, and they were not to go unheard, even though they knew it was the end.

 

The soldiers, dignified and strong, let out a savage cry and challenged the beasts. All but Rory. Rory stood there, legs quavering underneath him, sword slowly drooping to the ground as he let out a small, helpless cry of fright.

 

They were far too big, there was no way they could even attempt to harm these giant creatures. Tears swiftly ran down his cheeks, collecting in a puddle at his chin. His heart pounded against his chest and thumped in his head, erasing all but thoughts of fear, and [smash.]

The dragons slashed with their sharp, talon-like claws as they worked as a team to scatter the minifigs apart.

 

Rory couldn’t watch; he stood there, eyes down to the ground in a childish dreamlike fashion. He caught a small glimpse of his brother falling to the ground and rolling, dodging a mighty tail slap. Then with a huge, bellowing roar, the dragons released their flames.

 

The heat sizzled away his salty tears, but it was too much. He slowly took one step back, then yet another. Within seconds he found himself madly fleeing through the forest, only stopping once to listen for more war cries from the scouts.

 

There were none.

Chapter Two – Part One

 

The air was damp, but sweet smelling, like plants. Rory bolted awake, arms dug into a pokey fistful of hay. His eyes were wide open, mindlessly absorbed on a blank space ahead. It was all so true. The dream was just history, a replay of a day’s events.

 

I ran away. Left my fellows to die. Rory reluctantly sat up from his makeshift bed of straw, and focused on pulling wheat-colored strands out of his hair instead of crying. Why had he gotten so scared? What if he could have done something?

 

Suddenly, the clops of metal against rock pulled him to his feet. Those were a knight’s footsteps, the light metal of their shoes against the hard ground. Confused, he glanced around quickly, hoping to find the window he had gotten in from. It was just down the hall, and it was flooded with sparkling light, which had meant everyone was awake and he had slept too long. He had been hoping he could wake himself up at dawn before whoever owned this stable arrived, but that was too late.

 

He never realized he had picked a royal stable. That would be the only place a knight would be. The sound of footsteps got closer, along with a second pair. Suddenly both knights started laughing boisterously, and Rory quickly pulled hay over himself, hoping that he wouldn’t be found.

“Yeah, I thought we were making a dent in their forces, but now I’m not sure. Why, just yesterday ore of the king’s scout groups was attacked,” One said, strolling past the slightly moving hay pile.

 

“Eek, I wonder by which clan.” The other one said, making a faint bouncing sound.

 

“The dragons to the south, I think. At least that would make the most sense, since they were in that region. The west ones are the worst; they are the most loyal to those witches. We’ve rid of the north ones though.”

 

“Ya, I heard one soldier made it from the attack.”

 

Rory, despite himself and his situation, allowed a little tip of a smile, knowing that it was probably him they were talking about.

 

“He must’ve fought pretty hard.” The knights then started moving down, picking out their horses. Rory’s cheeks turned a funny shade of red. Like they knew he hadn’t fought. Like they knew he was there.

Chapter Two – Part Two

 

Rory pulled himself up and staggered out of the large stable, tripping over bricks as he stumbled through the small window. The outside was overflowing with green, lush plants, topped with bright pink and purple wild flowers. It seemed strangely under kept for a royal’s stable, but he wasn’t about to question it.

 

He was slightly intrigued at the knight’s talk, yet terrified all the same. North, south, east, and west clans. Why had no one told him, he was in the ranks and should have been informed!  He knew that the creatures, such as the dragons and the other beasts that roamed, were controlled by witches to do their bidding, but why would different clans matter?

 

He snatched up one of the colorful little flowers and carefully inspected one of its feathery petals. Something so delicate and easily smashed, yet so wonderful. So lovely one minute, then gone the next. Rory mashed the plant in his fingertips, rubbing the oily sensation across his hand. He let the broken, brutalized blossom fall into the wind, and float gently away.

 

His eyes traced the flower, but his mind was locked on Angus. Angus had been so…caring. Then to be gone, crushed in an instant, it was...it was….

 

Rory couldn’t think of a strong enough word. He tromped out of the flower patch and away from the stable, hurrying into the nearest town below their hill-set barn. He arrived as soon as he feet could possibly deliver him there, bounding into the bustling town for no particular reason, feeling as if he searched then he might find someone to hold him close as Angus once did.

 

But as he arrived, scuffing the dusty ground noisily, a crowd of people were gathered in the center of the village, surrounded by buildings and houses, all angrily shouting at someone in the middle of the group.

 

Someone not human.
