Name: Caidor

Species: Toa (Explained in backstory)

Masks:

Great Kanohi Garai (Mask of Gravity

Great Kanohi Pakari (Mask of Strength)

Great Kanohi Xaoc (Russian for chaos)(Chaos Aura; When active, things backfire, go haywire, etc.)

Great Kanohi Interitus (Latin for destruction)(Gravity Storm; User is put in temporary stasis while massive gravity whirlwind is summoned up then explodes outward.)

Element: Shadow, Plasma

Weapon: Fists.

Armor: Black and orange, short-medium spikes on shoulders

Background: Caidor was once an ordinary Su-matoran like all his friends and family. Their village was a small one, sheltered from outside forces and evils. It was a well kept secret known only to them and the mountain on which they lived. Caidor was, as the Turaga said one day, fated for something bigger and far nobler than his tiny village, or even this island, could offer him. If only he’d had the chance to see that day properly fulfilled...

Backstory continued: (@ mods, this went through a while ago on page 473, for your convenience.)

 

...The Makuta’s rahi overran that village the very same day, destroying, trampling, and killing. The Turaga was killed in front of Caidor’s eyes by a swarm of Manas. The Makutas soon entered the village, and offered the survivors a choice; hand over the matoran whos power they had sensed, or they would be taken back with the Makuta, one by one, until someone gave him over. Caidor’s best friend, Motog, was the second to be dragged, screaming, away from him. He gave himself up after that. The Makuta forced an infected Kanohi on him. The next time he emerged from under that mask, not even Motog would have recognized him. Caidor was completely emerged in the ways of the Makuta, a shadow toa, all light and previous memories gone from his new body. He relished in spreading the shadow of Makuta over the new villages, tormenting them with a hateful spite, the same way that he’d been treated when he was a simple Su-matoran. He rose quickly in the ranks of The Brotherhood on his island, both through diplomatic and “less savory” ways. Soon he was at the top of the heap, king of the hill. That’s when he decided to leave his puny island behind and go seek some bigger fish. When he was gone, all that was left of the island was burning trees, cracked ground, and the limp corpses of the matoran who were once his friends, black, charred, and utterly devoid of life...
