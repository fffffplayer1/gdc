keplers wrote:

Is there such a thing as anti-matter lightning? I wouldn't imagine it is, as lightning isn't composed of matter.

NERD ATTACK!!!

 

Antimatter has some very interesting properties. There are antimatter versions of electrons, protons, and neutrons. Put, say, 1 antimatter proton and 1 pistron (antimatter electron) together, and you get a antihydrogen atom. Those have been made. They (scientists) have also made antihelium atoms. These antimatter atoms seem exactly the saem as the real atoms, as in they react the same way. Antimatter iron would rust, just like real iron. Antimatter hydrogen will burn like mad when heated and exposed to oxygen. Antimatter steel is very stong. (Iron, steel, etc have not actually been created).

 

So, in essence, antimatter is the same as matter, except for one thing.

 

When matter and antimatter collide, they both destroy each other. Then, all the energy that was in the matter is released, causing a gigantic explosion.

 

Phew.

 

*Breathes in*

 

So yes, keplers, antimatter lighting is possible. Just use pistrons. Lightning is just electrons after all, and pistrons will make the same effect.

 

But... as soon as a pistron hits another atom is will be destroyed, creating an explosion in the process.

 

 

Summary:

 

Yes. Keplers, you can have antimatter lightning.
