Just like the LUers(Swords of Light; not much difference) who tried to apply real life physics to Star Wars in the Star Wars: Clone Wars Roleplay. What you both didn't/don't understand is that it's a different universe entirely with laws of its own. For my Star Wars example, yes, our universe and the Star Wars one is very similar and close in the laws of physics that rule each universe, but the slight differences can have great effects in how things are done. Sure, we can use our physics to argue and compare since they are so close, but can't actually bind Star Wars by them. The same is with Bionicle, except on a grander scale. You just can't assume that since gravity seems to work the same there as here that the Bionicle Universe follows every law here. Bionicle has its own unique laws of physics, quite different from ours. You can't truely explain how a Toa can manipulate the element he/she controls, you can come close for some parts, but a majority of it can't be explained under our laws. It just like trying to explain how the Great Beings created working guts, then fused them to metal, and have a working, breathing, thinking, living creature like a Matoran. Just as we don't know every single law that rules our universe, we'll probably never know every law that affects the Bionicle Universe. That's why use what little we know to base off of how to creature our characters and other fanon stuff and try to stick so close to canon.

When in doubt, read the instructions.
