This is a story about funny stuff. Like Sensei Wu eating a pile of rubber duckz because he forgot to have breakfast. Or Nexus Naomi tap dancing on a rocket steakboard. Or disco tacos. You'll see soon enough.

So sit back in your seat, relax, and prepare to enjoy...

Episode 1: Steak and Tacos

Once upon a time, there was a guy named CompletelyDignifiedSteak. He preferred to be called by his last name, because, let's be honest, what kind of a name is Completely? It's a completely weird name is what it is. But then again, so is Steak. But at least Steak isn't an adjective.

Anyways, Steak was a Nexus Force Soldier. No one particularly impressive, just a Rank 2 Space Marauder with a fondness for quicksicles. Whenever he wasn't battling, Steak wore a No Pirates tee shirt and some jeans. His favorite food (other than quicksicles) was Yuki-Yaki, and he often used a deadpan voice when comedy was involved. Steak also had a property, a property with a house on it that was shaped like a giant steak. He considered this his best creation, and he was right, for the Steakhouse (as it was called) was his only creation, as Steak preferred battling to building. Lastly, he enjoyed videogaming, cooking, and collecting jawbreakers.

Also once upon a time, there was a guy named TheTalkingTaco. He preferred to be called by his last name, because, let's be honest, what kind of a name is The? It's the weirdest name you've probably ever heard is what it is. But then again, so is Taco. But at least Taco sounds funny.

Anyways, Taco was a Nexus Force Soldier. No one particularly impressive, just a Rank 2 Daredevil with a fondness for comedy. Whenever he wasn't battling, Taco wore whatever he found in his inventory, with no regards as to color or emblem. His favorite food was sushi, and he often would joke about any subject that came to mind. He even claimed to have a joke for every occasion. Taco had many properties, but his favorite was his medieval castle, as constructed from modules. He'd added a whole bunch of other things onto the modules, such as a few turrets, a huge spire in the middle, and a garden around the castle, for Taco was quite the avid builder. Lastly, he enjoyed videogaming, cooking, and being the world's first DJ comedian.

I'm a magical pony flying through the sky on a magical journey for you and me!

And now that I've thoroughly bored the audience with what is a essentially an essay on Steak and Taco, it's time to kick of the story with some wordwise eloquence from Taco.

"I'm bored."

Steak and Taco were in the Steakhouse, both sitting in bar chairs, which were part of a bar, which was in the house's well-stocked kitchen. The place was woodsy and had a sort of log-cabin theme to it; the cabinets were oak, as was the floor, and wooden beams crisscrossed the ceiling. It reminded one of an actual steakhouse, or what everyone thinks an actual steakhouse ought to be like. As in, without all the peanut shells on the floor.

I hate those things.

But anyways, it was a nice degree in the Steakhouse, it smelled nice in the Steakhouse, and everything looked nice in the Steakhouse, save Taco, who was dressed in a Mosaic Jester Hat, a Black Soccer Shirt, and Comfy Jeans. And, as most people know, when you are in a nice house where everything is perfectly in order, it's hard to not be bored if there's nothing to occupy your time with. And in this case, there wasn't.

"Well, I'm bored too," Steak replied, sipping some lemonade from a cup in his hand.

"Then what do you wanna do?" Taco said, after a pause.

"I dunno, maybe fight some Maelstrom?"

"Sure, why not."

They both got out of their chairs and went into the main hallway. It looked like any normal hallway in a steakhouse, and that was pretty much all you could say about it, unless you paid particular attention to the way that that one painting of Cowboy Bob was slightly askew. If you paid particularly close attention to it, you might have noticed a tiny, tiny switch in one corner of the painting, which Steak now activated by righting the painting. His armor promptly fell on him from a suddenly revealed hatch on the ceiling, slipping on to his body nicely. Taco simply equipped his own battle gear the normal way, and together, the two of them strolled out of the hallway and over to the property's launch pad.

‎03-02-2014 02:01 PM

Steak pulled out his Starbase rocket, while Taco pulled out a classic blue one, and together, they blasted off to Nimbus Station.

When they landed, the duo walked over to to the middle of Nimbus Station to see if there were any good deals, like every other time they went over to the place. And, like every other time they went over to the place, there was a ridiculous racket.

"SELLING SOLID METAL LEGS FOR 6,500!"

"20 NOTION POTIONS FOR 1,000!"

"RARE DROPS, GOOD DEALS! RARE DROPS GOOD DEALS! ASK FOR RARE YOU GET GOOD DEAL!"

And, like every other time they went over to the place, there were no good deals. So they moved on to the Forbidden Valley launch pad, but not before equipping the ninja hoods they'd both earned awhile ago; Steak's was black, while Taco's was white. And then, together, they both blasted off for Forbidden Valley, their choice grinding world.

Upon arrival, both of them walked through the shiny purple gate thingy of awesomeness (Taco, as always, tried to scare the guards somehow; and, as always, he failed at this), over the annoyingly drop happy rock updraft thingies, and up to the top of Cavalry Hill, where a few others were grinding.

"Let's go!" Taco said, equipping a stack of Firecrackers and running into battle. As he charged, however, he tripped on a loose stone, falling flat on his face. The Firecracker he was holding in his hand hit the ground as well, and thanks to the weird Nexus Force trigger mechanism that the thing was equipped with, the firecracker was set off. Taco was thrown into the air from the resulting explosion, and he promptly fell onto the head of a Ronin, who began madly running around like a spooked horse. After a second, Taco realized what had just happened, and began to clobber the Ronin upside the helmet with another Firecracker. This only made the creature buck and scrabble more, though, and that made Taco lose his grip on the entire steak-sorry, stack-of Firecrackers, which promptly blew up the moment they hit the ground.

The Ronin smashed almost instantly, while Taco was throw into the air again, but much higher this time. Thanks to weird Nexus Force explosive thingies, he didn't take any damage from all the explosions, but he did take damage from the resulting fall onto a Ronin Horseman. The poor Ronin got squished in a Daredevil-Saddle sandwich, though his being smashed was not in vain, for it produced a shiny faction token. However, the shininess of the token was lost of Taco in that moment, for the horse bucked him off, and he landed on his back in the mud of Cavalry Hill.

Total silence, but only for a moment, for at that moment, the other people began to laugh.

Now, if Taco wasn't Taco, he would've been sorely embarrassed. But when you're the world's first DJ Comedian, you bow and grin during events like these, even when your friend begins to drag you away from your adoring crowd and over to a safer place.

"Smooth, Taco. Real smooth," Steak deadpanned, smirking.

"I improvised," Taco replied as Steak let go of him.

"And got your butt soaked in Maelstrom," Steak said, passing Taco a red notion potion. The latter blanched and poured the contents of the vial all over his pants, thoroughly soaking them. After a moment, Taco said, "Okay then, let's see you do better."

"Can do," Steak replied, pulling out an Xpresso. He then proceeded to charge in the exacting same manner as Taco, except he avoided the same rock that had tripped his friend. He then proceeded to drink the Xpresso; however, when he used the typical Nexus Force drinking method of throwing your head back and chugging the entire drink in a few gulps, reality ensued, and the piping hot Xpresso (how is it that those things always stay warm?) splattered all over his face. As a direct result of this, Steak screamed and began running around in circle, occasionally hopping into the air a little bit. A few nearby Ronin just stared at him, confused, before shrugging and resuming their attack on the other players.
