OOC: @airdude: Nah, no betrayal. There was, like, NOBODY here when I started this HQ. So few that I had to play the parts of Villain and Hero. That's why this is here. I'm going to continue, and all Chaos plots and plans are not known to any of my other characters, and vice-versa

 

As in response to your questions

1. It breaks down regular matter, anti-matter, etc. However, plasma is intangible to it, and electricity and light are absorbed by it. So, lasers are useless.
2. Depending on the intensity of the chaos energy, it can destroy a different amount of things. For instance, the biggest thing a Chaosling (kinda like a stromling) could destroy would be a Minifigure. The biggest thing a Chaos tank could destroy would be one story of a building. However, a Chaos Starship could take out a whole starship. And their leader, My character, Pure Chaos himself, can destroy ANYTHING. However, there are a few limitations set up so that he can't always do that. I'll get to that in the rest of this post.

3. No, Gravity is something that was created long before either the Great Creator or Chaos ever existed. It primarily came from the first universe, something far far away from the LEGO Universe, and that force of Gravity bled through the universes, into eachother.

4. Chaos is half of Maelstrom. The other half is imagination. If there's more imagination than Chaos, or an equal amount of both, Maelstrom is created.

5. Without either Imagination shielding or this new element I will get to in a second, yes. It is completely impossible to engage Chaos in battle. However, my Assembly character, Aron5, has re-created the only thing to destroy chaos for good. But it's still in development, so it'll onlt destroy small amounts of Chaos. Once Aron gets this thing finished, it will be able to.

6. Information is a lot like plasma to it, completely intangible, so no. But all sorts of sound waves, light waves, Gamma rays even, are absorbed by it.

 

Hope this helps!

IC: -After a few days of testing and lots of missed paintballs, Cerise finally perfects the design for the Chaos-Void .35 Caliber Bullet MKI. It would take a lot longer to make other Chaos-Void bullets, but at least he had one version ready to be mass-produced.-

-At the moment, Cerise was inside the same room where he had first tried to put a bullet into a normal old rifle. He'd seriously upgraded the room by then, and it had become a kind of workshop. He had a lathe, a table saw, a smelter, a rail on the ceiling (those were always useful, one way or another), some other big things, tons of smaller equipment besides, and four tables to hold all of it on. All of the tools were made of Void, so he could work with regular matter and Chaos both.-

-At the moment, he was trying to melt some Chaos into a malleable form in the smelter, while working on a microchip of Void and matter with robotic arms in the corner. Lines of molten lead were pressed into place with infinite care, while near unobservable things such as resistors, logic gates and even smaller, simple microchips were fused onto the cold, hard surface of the non-conducting Void base plate. Throughout the process, Cerise watched very carefully with a microscope as a tiny city of circuitry pulled itself together in an immensely satisfying, poetic feat of microscopic engineering. After about an hour, the microchip was ready, and Cerise took a new piece of Void the size of a thumbnail an pushed it into the exact same place, watching as the process repeated itself, but in a different fashion that produced a different chip. And then he did it again. And again. Twenty six times in a row, though it would have been twenty five if that one arm hadn't broken down mid-production.-
