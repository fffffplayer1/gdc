OOC: Yes to both questions. Here's a full description

The Chaos HQ now stands on a lone planet orbiting dangerously fast around a three-star system. there is a large asteroid field, and the danger is that many incoming ships, and outgoing ships, only have a small window in which to get out of the system, for about 4 hours. This System keeps the HQ well protected.

 

The Planet itself looks like a complete sandy lifeless desert, but Apocalypse's city stands at the northernmost pole, made of red bricks on the outside, but reinforced with Titanium on the inside. Beneath it is an expansive cavern, filled with farms to provide food for the soldiers, as well as weapon barracks, Star ship docks, and Manufacturing areas. Many of the chaos creatures, huge and Dragon-like, were converted to Brick-based forms, and are now housed in cages in their section of the cave. This cave is ever expanding, as Apocalypse has drilling machines drilling through the rock underneath the planet, hollowing out and taking the deep underwater springs and draining their water, bringing it back to the main HQ. As all of Apocalypse's Army have become lifeforms, they now need to sustain themselves.

 

This star system is far from the Nexus itself, but close enough for an intersetllar drive to get a ship there quickly.

 

~Apocalypse~

Unfortunately, I don't think you quite understand the difficulty of what you are tying to do.  In terms of in character knowledge, you have no idea that these shards are even there.  Unfortunately, no conventional methods of detection would be able to find them, as they are inert, and not putting out energy.  Other means of detection would prove equally impossible to use.  Finding micrometer long shards from miles away through a thick atmosphere is not a thing you can do.  It would take a truly astounding amount of luck to find them if you were standing three meters away.  Their base materials are pretty standard (it would read as quartz, not a "foreign material"), and doing a molecular analysis would actually require you to have a shard to examine in the first place.

 

  I ask that you please cease playing based on things you could only know out of character.  Your character is free to investigate them by sending something or someone up to examine more closely and/or realistically.  Additionally, you have no reason (In character) to suspect that they are even slightly dangerous.  Please rewind to the point of the crash, and either continue on in an unsuspecting manner, or look more carefully.  There are other reasons this is not workable (or sensible, considering that bringing a planet that close to the sun would strip away its magnetosphere and then boil off the outer layers of the atmosphere, which would permanantly render the exterior fo the planet utterly uninhabitable.), but those reasons are not particularly relevant at this moment.

OoC:

 

Barcha, it is actually reasonable for the Chaos HQ to be suspicious of your shards. Keep in mind that Barcha as a character was never really trusted; why would his successor be any different?

 

As for the star, we could assume they were controlled, like a fusion reactor. Their gravity would be there, but any sufficiently advanced civilization capable of bending spacetime for FTL travel, among other chores, could surely figure out how to contain the heat of a small star by bending space or simply using magnets to channel the ionized, heated air into a protective bubble that would isolate the star from the atmosphere and thus prevent thermal energy transfer.

 

Speaking of gravity, that is how they could find your shards. There should not normally be any of that crystal in the atmosphere, so the slightly increased gravity due to the density of the crystal (which has more mass per unit of volume than air or any biological source) would be small but noticeable to an advanced civilization. Searching the sky for such a gravitational anomaly, while impossible with modern technology, is perfectly feasible if there is no upper limit on technological achievement. Besides, the Chaos HQ may have other abilities I am not aware of, so they may have alternate solutions.

 

Finally, this is a Roleplay. Physics is fun here, but putting limitations on the potential capabilities of a fantasy civilization with various groups capable of wielding magical abilities is not. Sometimes, story should trump what modern science says. Perhaps, eventually, humans will be able to do many things we have mentioned here on this forum anyway, so why worry about it? If it was an important storyline bit, I get it, but try to do that while respecting established abilities of people on here.

-Bane

I shall address your points in order.

 

Yes. They have every right to be suspicious.  Turbobuild does have some familiarity with my technology.  Enough to identify that it is mine, although nothing more.

 

The second point is more or less irrelevant at this point, because I am presenting the case that detecting the threat as simply as they did is impossible.  And therefore, taking such draconian defense measures against something so easily replaced seems silly, if not outright wasteful.

 

The third point is where I need to elaborate a bit more.   Let me do some basic math for you.  A singular shard is smaller than these given dimensions, because they are not perfect rectangular prisms.  So, if we envision a singular crystal as a rectangular prism measuring one micrometer on its longest side, and 100 nanometers ( .1 micrometers) on the other two sides, that makes the volume of a singular crystal 1 x 10^ -20 cubic meters,  (or .000000000000000000001 cu. m.)  If we convert that to cubic centimeters, we get 1 x 10^ -14 cubic centimeters.  Cubic centimeters convert directly into mililiters, so we have a volume of 1 x 10^-14 mL.  Converting to a unit with a name, it amounts to 10 attoliters.  It would take more than a million of these crystals to fill the volume of a singular human cell.  Detection of such small, inert, and generally  undetectable artifacts would be outright impossible with modern technology.  I do, in fact, assume a generally higher level of scientific understanding among the various factions.
