BIONICHARGE wrote:

No.

Jammers for keplers only:

Jammers
Just to give you some background on this: remember when I said that the point of Kapronan's creation was so that none of the Hauden had to be sacrificed?  Well... I've decided that I don't really want to sacrifice Kapronan either!  My current thoughts are that the Ignika saved the Toa's spirit at the last moment as 'thanks' for being the mask's guardian and bearer, transporting it into an alternate dimension to keep him safe and creating a new body for him identical in shape to his original Toa form (before he was mutated by the Makuta) and wearing the Ignika of the alternate universe (which would give him his new powers).  He would then go on to realise what was happening in the main roleplay universe and rejoin the Hauden for the Battle of Bara Magna.

Now, I've always been slightly irritated by Kapronan being a Toa of Stone and Shadow in a Toa Team making up the entirety of the secondary Elements, not counting Light or Shadow - something about upsetting the inherent symmetry of the group.  However, by introducing a Toa of Life that balance would maintained.  Of course I could put Kapronan back in with his original Elemental ability but if wouldn't feel... right, somehow.

Now, a couple things to bear in mind: firstly, Kapronan is only a normal Toa, unlike the Hauden Nuva, so he would only have standard-level Life powers, and, secondly, his powers would come from the Ignika itself - I wouldn't give him any other Kanohi, and this lack of Kanohi powers would, I hope, balance the inherently powerful nature of his other powers. It's almost like giving a character without an Element or any Kanohi a more extreme version of the Garai.

I do hope that you consider this for what it is, not what the impression of the one power that Kapronan would possess makes it seem to be.

Reply:

Jammers
Let's test that assumption, just for a second.

Looking at BioSector01 on the Ignika page, we have a helpful list of powers that Life is shown to encompass.  Here it is with my own notes in bold:

  • The mask can appear in many different forms.  Easily achievable via Mahiki.
  • The mask drew Vezon to itself by influencing him.  Psionics can accomplish this.
  • The mask created a physical manifestation of itself to test Matoro in Dark Destiny.  Matatu can do the same.
  • In City of the Lost, the Kanohi Ignika leaked Life energy in the form of air.  Toa of Air.  Need I say more?
  • The mask transformed the Toa Inika into the Toa Mahri in Prisoners of the Pit; the mask can undo the effects of Energized Protodermis as seen when it devolved Tahu in Journey's End; the mask enlarged a Gadunka via leaked Life energy in Prisoners of the Pit; Toa Ignika devolved Icarax in Shadows in the Sky.  Mutation Rhotuka.
  • The mask changed Dekar into a duplicate of Hydraxon in Prisoners of the Pit.  Mutation Rhotuka combined with Psionics for mental wipe.
  • The mask turned Matoro into life-force energy, and used him and its powers together to resurrect Mata Nui in Downfall.  Original power - but highly case-specific and literally self-destructive.
  • The mask crafted a body for itself in Shadows of the Sky.  Practically any matter-based Element and an Iden will let the user do this.
  • Toa Ignika accelerated the aging of two of the Toa Nuva and Av-Matoran in Shadows in the Sky. It has since reversed their ages back to what they should be.  Original power, though similar effects can be accomplished with a Mask of Mutation and slower ones with a Mask of Aging.
  • Mata Nui used the Ignika to give five Glatorian Elemental powers in The Legend Reborn.  This one is OP.  I promise that I won't use it.

    Looking at that... Life doesn't seem too powerful.
