My thoughts on this are relatively simple: devices and items that utilise things that are canon are fine, but entirely fanon devices and items are more questionable and should, at the very least, be thoroughly checked and thematically matched with the basis of the roleplay before use.  This is why I have been generally sticking to utilising things that are seen in canon, like using the Matoran method of Kanoka forging to create the various abilities and weapons of the Defenders of Atero Nui.  The Intangibility Cutting Beam Generator is a good example of such a weapon, projecting a beam of Intangibility energy that has been modified with Accuracy to only turn the things that it is touching intangible.  Elsewhere, Emulation Kanoka have been used to copy the morally-neutral Stasis Field ability, allowing the Defenders' infantry to project Stasis Fields. 

 

For that reason, I'm voting against these Protosteel variants/alternatives/enhancements.  Generally they have no basis in canon whatsoever and are purely a way to gain a lead in an arms race.  I'm actually not displeased by the arms race itself, but it should, if it is present, be based on the ingenuity of the user.  This, indeed, is sometimes how military technology advances: somebody thinks of an idea for a weapon based on their knowledge of how the world works and presents it to an army, which then deploys the idea in physical form on the battlefield.  One notable example would be Archimedes of Syracuse's inventions aiding the defence of his city against the Romans.  That's how we ought to be thinking: not 'What new material can I dream up to turn the tide of battle?'; instead, 'How can I use what already exists in a new way to turn the tide of battle?'

 

As such, if you want super-strong Protosteel, I'd recommend enhancing normal Protosteel by forging it with Regeneration, Strength and possibly Fate Kanoka.
