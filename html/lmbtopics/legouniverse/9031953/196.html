90% of the Xian empire is based on a single peice of possibly fanon tech I developed waaaayyyy before Interceptors were even invented.

 

And Brickme invented the energy tech game. I just took it in a different direction.


I'll own up to the fact that I was the leading influence in the rise of armies, but I can definately state that at that time, I had no idea that what i was doing would encourage armies themselves to become so massive. My primary driving force was always simply to try and give NPC's a chance at sucessfully fighting player characters. However, I will admit that as I expanded my NPC use, I did so on the basis of a what I have come to view as a a faulty idea. 
It has been mentioned that I invented the energy tech. This is, in my opinion, one of the greatest reasons our NPC's are so powerful. In cannon, powers, abilities, and energies are given only to specific characters, masks, or species. This selection acts to limit the power that any one unit/being can possess, and also encourages units/beings to work together in order to accomplish tasks that the abilities of one being would be unable to accomplish. In the roleplay, I operated under the notion that energies could be isolated, examined, and then reproduced so as to allow them to be given to other units (such as vehicles and the like). This notion immediately shatters the careful dispersion of powers between specified beings, and masks, and allows a NPC to suddenly become almost as powerful as a main, character.
I have since found the above idea doubtful, as it ruins the balance of power in the RP. Toa, and other experienced main characters are cannonically seen as more powerful than lesser minions (such as Rahkshi, most rahi, Bohrok, visorak, exo toa to name a few). Because of this, most users at the time I joined the roleplay used NPC's as little more than cannon fodder.

   Along with being rid of mass armies, I would like for us to return to canon-based technology and characters. This will prevent us from returning to the situation we are in or any other difficult scenarios. It will also bring balance to the scales of power that every user collectively has.

 

NPCs:

  • All non-personal characters should be related to canon armies. Exo-toa, Fohrok, Kraata/rahkshi, Manas, Mana Ko, Maxilios robots, Visorak, Energy hounds, Rahi, and Vahki or slightly modified versions of the listed will be allowed as well as some fanon NPCs.
  • An NPC is to be weaker then an RPC. But if a user skillfully and strategically manages to beat an RPC with their non personal characters, it should be accepted and not put down as OP. Such cases will be rare though.
  • There will be a limit as to haw many NPCs you may have to attack, and a slightly larger limit if they are for HQ defence.
  • When you post a fanon NPC's profile, it must have a "quantity" section as well, high-numbers will be lowered depending on how powerful the NPC is.

RPCs:

  • Characters will only have the powers their species had in canon.
  • ...with the exception of one personal trait, such as "faster then most toa" or "highly skilled in his kraata power of rahi control." Karunax's trait is his super strength, Charix's trait is his masterful control of shadow.
  • Toa have a limit of six kanohi, with other species having a limit of two. This rule can be case specific if an RPC's abilities are limited to the use of kanohi.

Other:

  • Kanoka-tech that does the same as a kanohi must be used by someone who could naturally use a kanohi of that power.
  • A skakdi striking himself with a Hau disk will not create a shield around him, only the basic 8 powers kanoka possess can produce their effects onto a target.

 

Any additions or (slight) alterations?
