Axolc scrutinized the lone Jester from afar.  Even with the advanced capabilities the mysterious warrior boasted, it didn’t seem fitting that he would flaunt them like this, attempting to take on all three of his adversaries at once, after they had already repelled more than five legions of his comrades.  Something was up.

 

The warrior raised his staff, and ice shot out from it like an arrow, hitting the center of the metallic skeleton that generated the outer membrane, and shattered it.

 

The outer membrane faded, revealing an army large enough to conquer the entire Valley of Life three times over.

 

While the trio was still in shock, the commander marched towards them, his army following in prefect sync behind him.  He stopped halfway to their defenses, and as he did, the metal skin covering his face lifted, revealing a face Axolc didn’t recognize.

 

He did, however, hear a sharp intake of breath from his left.

 

He turned just in time to see Reyna yank the remote detonator from Praxer’s hand in a panic and slam the button.  With a roar, the explosives activated, sending the barricade walls careening across the floor, straight into where the outer membrane had been, screeching horribly all the way, and taking half the army with it.

 

The commander was still standing there when the smoke cleared, the barricade split in two where he had passed through it, the edges still glowing a frozen-blue hue.
