‎10-21-2012 12:48 PM

As if on cue, their luck started to turn sour. The chariot carrying the commander suddenly cruised around the bend in front of them and raced towards them. Their formation stopped to present themselves, and the Major almost felt his heart stop with them. If they got caught now, it would be game over.

“How goes the search, captain?” the commander called.

“Same as it has the past hour, sir. No sign of the infiltrators.”

“No? …Well, keep me posted then. Oh, and one more thing. I am personally providing an ample reward for the first squad to find them, so if you could do me a favor and spread the word around, I’d really appreciate it.”

The squad captain smiled. “Sir, killing a Jester is a pleasure; the gratitude of High Lord Zanjor is an honor. Either one of those is a reward in and of its own right. We could never ask you of anything more.”

The man riding the chariot, whom the Major now assumed was this “Great Lord Zanjor”, smiled back, and said, “You flatter me, but that’s enough of that. A reward will be provided, but no one will get it if we all keep standing around here! Let’s get back to work.”

And with that, he turned his battle chariot and started to continue on his way. Just when he had almost passed by, he paused, and stared straight at the Major. The Major felt his heart freeze again, as the Great Lord stopped his chariot again, and started moving back towards them. The Major was pleading to every great being he could think of when the chariot finally stopped and the rider finally spoke.

“SOLDIER!!” he barked, “STAND AT ATTENTION!!”

The Major quickly corrected his stance and muttered, “Yessir.” He was practically paralyzed with fear, so this was no easy task.

However, once this was done, it seemed to satisfy the Great Lord. “Don’t let me catch you slouching in line again!” he added as an afterthought, and then promptly turned back around and rode out of sight.
