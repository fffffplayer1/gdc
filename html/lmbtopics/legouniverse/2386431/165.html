***

 

The Major and Jonathon trudged on through the desert, growing weaker every minute.  They were not equipped for such a journey, and the conditions they faced reminded them of this relentlessly. 

 

They had set off several days ago, heading straight eastward, marking their direction by the rising sun each morning.  They had made little progress, due to both the heat of the day and the sheer size of the barren landscape.

 

The Major always kept an emergency supply of water and rations, but these were insufficient, meant for only one person’s consumption, and not for long-term indulgence where such things would be sorely needed.  If they all but starved and dehydrated themselves, these would run out by the end of the week.

 

“The desert… is like Scylla and Charybdis,” the Major said with a groan.

 

Jonathon lifted his sweat-caked face with a look of confusion and fatigue.

 

“Just a reference to certain folklore,” the Major explained, “The desert is a choice of two dooms: the blistering heat of the day, or the subzero cold of the night.”  He looked to Jon and gave a weak smile.  “I would have us travel by night, but we need the morning sun to guide our path, or else we’d tire out going in a pointless circle.  And we need to put every ounce of energy to good use.”

 

Jonathon nodded, and they continued their march across the sand.  The sun and their own exhaustion hung over them, beating them, trying to push them back with every step they took.  The smallest steps felt like miles, the hours like eternities, and, though neither would admit it, their journey appeared absolutely pointless, and there was no way they would make it to the other side of the desert without a miracle. 

 

And miracles were as scarce as water in the desert.

I am not very sure Homer's epics should be called folklore!

Just wondering, out of all of the characters you've created, who is your favorite?

Sorry for not answering sooner!  That's a tough one...   There's a little something in every character that I really like, even in the ones like Malorus.   Of course I love all the main characters: Axolc makes for fun dialouge, especially around people who aren't used to him like Praxer or Reyna.  The Major of course was the one who started it all.  Jonathon is a very special character to me, and in some ways I think one of my most well-thought out ones; he seems a little one-dimensional at first, but every now and then he really surprises you in bewildering ways. 

 

But you know, I think a lot of my favorite characters are the ones you mostly just see on the sides: Thoki is always a pleasure, and I had a lot of fun writing about Alkara and Zanjor.  For some reason I enjoy writing about these even more than the main characters.  Perhaps it's because they help bring the story to life.  Or maybe they're just a pleasant break from the main quintet.  In any event, all my characters are special to me, each in their own unique way.
