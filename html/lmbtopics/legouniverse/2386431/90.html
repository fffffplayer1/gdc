Chapter 55


The Major woke up to a sound he wished he could forget.

“GUD FRENDS, UNCLE THOKI IS HURTS!!! WHY DID YUS TRY TO DECEEVES ME??!!!”

Since he was not yet fully awake, the Major’s response was remarkably less than eloquently put.

“Whah?-” he stammered, trying to regain his senses. After shaking himself awake, he realized what Thoki was taking about.

The crafty little Bone was holding the two disguises they had relinquished last night, and from his tone he obviously thought they had stolen them from his wares. And if that was the case….

…Actually, come to think about it, that might not be a bad thing. “You misunderstand,” the Major explained, shaking Jonathon awake, “We were just looking at these last night, and brought them up here to try them on and see if they fit.”

“OOOOOHHHH,” Thoki replied in -relief? It was hard to tell- and quickly tried to regain lost ground. “Okays. Wud yus like to buys dems?”

Now fully awake, the Major had to think fast. If he said yes, the Bone would almost certainly overprice them, but if he said no, then all their work last night would be for nothing and they’d have to start over. Think, think, think…

Then he had an idea so crazy it might just work.

“What, after you accuse us of trying to make off with them?!” The Major feigned shock, “After you accuse us of acting like petty thieves?! We would NEVER try to rip you off like that!!” [No, the Irony was not lost on him] “I’ve half a mind to leave this building and never come back again!”

This got Thoki’s attention. Apparently the Major and his friends were the first customers he had had in a long time. “NOES PLEESE DON’TS!! Heres: Thokis will gives yus teh wares at a bargaans! Yus can has dem boths for jest 100,000 cruxibles!”

The Major thought back to his first conversation with Idagget, and realized that was probably not a ‘discount’ price at all. His instincts and his knowledge of Thoki agreed. “One thousand,” he replied, “Remember, we can always take our business elsewhere.”

“Okays okays! How about fifties?”

“One,”

“Twenties!”

“One,”

“Dees ees not nagoshiashuns! My number ees changing!”

“Alright fine,” the Major replied, “I’ll give you a small rarity worth at least 12,000 for both, but that’s my final offer. Frankly, after what you tried to pull, you ought to be paying us.” The irony of their conversation was building up by the second. Surprisingly though, this demand seemed to work.

“Okays!” Thoki agreed, “Uncle Thoki weel accept dis offur eef he likes wat he sees! Now shows Uncle Thoki dees payment!”

The Major slowly reached into his bag and pulled out a light green gem. He held it up for Thoki to see. “Will this do?” he asked rhetorically.

Thoki was completely mesmerized by the gem. “Yeeeeeeeeeeees” he slowly replied, and then, without hesitation, he dropped the disguises and snatched up the gem. He held it in his palm and gazed at it, and then ran down the stairs in this manner, muttering something along the lines of “my precious”.

With the diabolical little Bone gone, the Major gave a quiet sigh of relief. Realizing his ploy had worked, he couldn’t help but laugh. He looked over to Jonathon, and the boy gave him a queer look. The Major realized he had forgotten to explain what he had just done.

“With any luck,” he said with a wink, “We’ll be out of here before Thoki realize the gem I just gave him is a fake.”

Realization dawned on the boy’s face, and he put his hand over his mouth as if to stifle a laugh. The Major had just beaten the con man at his own game, and in a most obnoxious way, too.

When they had finished laughing, the Major promptly picked up their new disguises off the floor. He gave one to Jonathon, and then went into one of the bedrooms to try on his. It fit perfectly, which was fortunate seeing as it was their only excuse for bringing them up to their living room. When he came out, he found that Jonathon had already donned his own and was testing out the new equipment. The Major thought this was a good idea, and took a look at his own gear.

There was really no distinction between the uniforms other than who was wearing them; they were pretty much identical. Each one was composed of a combination of flexible blue and black chainmail and armor plating. They both came with helmets which, to the Major’s relief, covered their faces. Their shields were fairly basic, as well as the staffs they carried in hand and the swords that hung from their belts. The last thing the Major noted was that the staffs did not entirely match their color scheme, leading him to belief that they were reserved for the nightly patrol and did not actually belong to the soldiers they had acquired them from. This made some sense, since the army they had faced in the Everjade Hills had used swords in general, and only their commander had actually possessed a staff during the battle. Staffs were apparently reserved for the higher-ranking officers and nightly patrols, for whatever reason.

After making sure their equipment was all together, the Major picked up the bag that held their original gear, and led the way downstairs. They didn’t stop to say goodbye to Thoki, although the way he was staring at that fake gem, he probably wouldn’t have noticed them anyways. They were able to proceed out the door without any trouble.

However, once they were outside was another story entirely.

**********************************************************************************************

“RED ALERT!! RED ALERT!!” yelled a red-garbed Bat Lord from atop a moving battle chariot. He was using a sort of megaphone made of animal bone to amplify his voice, which was unnecessary, because was already shouting at the top of his lungs. “THERE HAS BEEN A MOSAIC JESTER INFESTATION!! SEARCH EVERY HOUSE AND AREST ANYONE WEARING UNIFORMS OF OUR FALLEN COMRADES!!! RED ALERT!! RED ALERT-”

Great. Either the two patrollers they had caught last night had already been missed or had managed to get free and report what they had seen. Now the people were on their guard, and their disguises were as good as useless. Moreover, the Major noted, not one soldier on the street carried a staff, so those would be dead giveaways. They would have to leave them behind, if they were ever going to get through this deathtrap. Hmm…

The Major looked around the streets. They were already flooded with soldiers, entering and exiting houses, shuffling around, and all-in-all it was too chaotic to make out any one person or thing in the crowd. That would have to be their only hope.

The Major bent down next to Jonathon. “Do as I do,” he muttered, “And walk with purpose.”

That said, the Major straightened up, put on his best military stance, and marched along the street. He didn’t look behind him, though he could tell from the pattern of his footsteps that Jonathon was doing the same. Together, they naturally fell in line with one squad of the searching soldiers. Then, at the Major’s beckoning, they quietly broke formation and slipped away into a different squad, all the while making their way down the street towards the castle. The Major couldn’t believe their luck; they still hadn’t been noticed! Their little ploy was actually working! It was a miracle!

The Major gave a loud sigh of relief, and then suddenly drew it back, worrying he’d be heard. He looked around at the rest of the squad, hoping he hadn’t been noticed. His fears were quickly realized when he saw the soldier next to him staring at him.

But then, to his amazement, the soldier just smiled and said, “It’s alright buddy. You can relax. He’s gone now.”

“Ugh,” another said, “That guy just gives me the creeps. Did you see him on that chariot like that? At this rate, he’s going to start thinking he’s the king!”

“What, you think he doesn’t already?” another piped in. “I mean, two patrollers check in late last night, and he has to turn it into a huge Jester conspiracy! Either he wants to turn this into one of his oh-so-amazing ‘war stories’ or he’s gone paranoid.”

“I’m guessing both.”

“You’re probably right then. He’s just full of himself isn’t he?”

“That’s enough!” the squad commander interrupted. “I don’t like that guy anymore than you do, but we have a job to do. Let’s not waste anymore of our time gossiping on his account; he’s simply not worth it.”

With that, the squad marched off, with the Major and Jonathon following at the rear. By that time the Major had decided that they had just come way too close, and it might actually be better to try the old-fashioned way at the soonest possible instance. They stuck with the squad they were currently in until the Major saw an opportunity and ducked into an alleyway with Jonathon close behind.

When they were out of sight, the Major beckoned for Jonathon to get comfortable. They’d be waiting a while before they’d have a chance to make their move.

Nightfall was still a long ways off.
