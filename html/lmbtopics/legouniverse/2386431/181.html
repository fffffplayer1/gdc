Chapter 74 - Part 3

‎03-09-2014 11:57 AM

Reyna closed her eyes and held her breath.  Of all her days in danger, of living on the edge, this… was not how she expected she would go out… or how she wanted.

 

Suddenly she felt the wall scrape against her arm, and she opened her eyes in surprise.  To her even greater shock, she realized that Axolc was still dragging them, even though the water had extinguished his Fig-on-Fire!  Now he was half running, have swimming down the ventilation shaft, carrying them, while holding his breath!!

 

Then Reyna saw a light.  It was coming from up ahead, at the end of the tunnel.  At first she was relieved, thinking it signified salvation, but then she realize the light was not an open point source, but rather, was grated, meaning there was something blocking it.  And that meant that their exit was closed.  Brick! 

 

And then Reyna ran out of breath.  She groped her face and throat, trying desperately to stop herself from trying to take a new one.  If she opened her mouth, it was the end! Just when she thought she was done for, Axolc changed his pattern, slowly jumping up and extending his legs in front of him.  Since his speed was hindered by the water, it took Reyna a second to realize what he was doing.  When she did, at first she thought he had started way too early, because the exit was still a few yards away.  But then she realized that the current itself was carrying them towards their goal, and that he had timed his movement perfectly.  They were almost there…

 

Suddenly Reyna heard a sharp gasp, and looked to see.  Praxer had let go and opened his mouth, and Reyna watched in horror as the water rushed in and filled it.  She opened her mouth to scream his name, but the moment she did the water rushed in her too, filling her mouth and nostrils and rapidly seeping into her lungs.

Chapter 74 - Part 4

‎03-09-2014 11:59 AM

Then she heard a clang, and saw a flash of light before falling several feet onto a cold, sterile floor.  She lay there a moment, then huffed and gagged, and hurled out a quarter of a gallon of water, adding it to the little pool they had created.  She was alive.  They were free!!

 

She sat up, and shot her head from side to side.  They were in a medium-sized room, much like the one they had been captured in.  Axolc had collapsed on the ground, still breathing, but exhausted from the strain of saving their lives.  The ventilation grate was floating in the pool on the ground, which was spreading out into the room rather than piling up, as the stream pouring from the shaft gradually lost vigor.  Praxer was-

 

PRAXER!!!!

 

Reyna ran over to his side and flipped him onto his back.  He wasn’t breathing.

 

Oh no.

 

Reyna pushed down on his chest, and water slowly trickled out of his mouth.  She removed the pressure, and air went in.  She had never actually performed this procedure on a live subject before, but there was no time for anything else.

 

She pushed again.  More water came out.  She released her hold.  More air went in.  Back and forth, back and forth.  Water out, oxygen in.  She continued pumping for several minutes.

 

Suddenly she heard a sharp cough and a gasp, and then an extended sharp intake of breath.  Her gaze wheeled to Praxer’s face.

 

“Well…” Axolc coughed, “He’ll be disappointed to hear that you didn’t have to use CPR.”

 

“Oh, shut up, Axolc!” Reyna screamed in a whisper, lifting up Praxer’s head with one hand and resting the other against his cheek.

Chapter 74 - Part 5

‎03-09-2014 12:00 PM

After a brief moment, Praxer gagged.  Reyna leaned forward in a silent prayer, as he slowly exhaled… and inhaled… and exhaled…

 

Reyna’s face lit up as she realized his breathing was approaching normal levels.

 

“YOU’RE ALIVE!!!” she gasped in joy, choking on her own tears.

 

“Barely…” Praxer wheezed.  “But I wouldn’t be if it weren’t for-”

 

“Oh, save it!” the Daredevil mumble, getting back up on his feet.  “I heard enough cliché speeches of gratitude in those movies you watched every Saturday night back home!  Besides, you’d have done the same.”

 

Praxer smiled weakly and said no more, but his gaze did not leave their savior for several minutes.  Or at least, it felt like minutes; in reality it was probably mere seconds, since they were still on their adrenaline high.

 

Reyna was, to be perfectly honest, a little bit jealous of the attention Axolc was getting.  She knew it was unfair to feel that way, since he had just saved their lives, but… after almost losing him, she wished she could have had some time with just her and Praxer.  For the first time, she fully grasped just how much she cared for him. 

 

“Let’s go,” she eventually said, “Before they realize we escaped.”

 

“Oh, I think they already know…” Praxer groaned, pointing to a corner of the room.  Reyna looked in the indicated direction, and cursed.  A security camera was staring straight at them.

 

“Let’s hurry!!” Axolc suggested, sounding a bit on-edge.  He ran across the room, and flung open the door.  The hallway was empty, so they must have put some good distance on their pursuers.  With any luck, it would stay that way.

 

Reyna and Praxer followed Axolc, and the three of them resumed their mad dash down the hall.
