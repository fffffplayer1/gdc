Ok, since it's almost time for me to start filling character requests, I have a few announcements I'd like to share. First o all, anyone who would like to re-post their bio should feel welcome to. (I don't want to shift through almost 60 pages any more than you do ) But either way, I'm going to go back and review some of them now... But first, there are some things I'd like to touch on...

Because my characters are constantly evolving, I would like everyone to make note: I RESERVE THE RIGHT TO MODIFY CHARACTERS AS NEEDED FOR STORY PURPOSES. Now, this doesn't mean I'm going to do any dramatic changes I know the character's creator would not like, such as gender switching (Although, a few females would be nice...). If you list your character as a male Sentinel, I'm not going to turn it into a female Assembly. You know me; I'm nicer than that. But what it does mean is that I'm allowed to make slight alterations when something seems out of place. This is, of course, something that's built on the trust between me and my readers, but I'm hoping all of you trust me enough to accept this.

Also, for future reference, it's a good idea NOT TO ELABORATE on how your character met the main characters, what his/her childhood was like, why he is where he is today or whenever he is, etc, etc, etc. I'm much more willing to accepted requests when they DON'T include descriptionsof dramatic twists in the plot; that way I'm much more flexible when I write (and seriously, I come up with too many of those on my own ). When I see a long description of such a thing, I sorta find it... annoying, for lack of a better term. But don't worry, I'll come up with PLENTY of twists in plot for the characters. (I haven't done a bad job so far, have I? ) Plus, being able to come up with character origins myself allows me to add characters in more smoothly, and make them seem like a more natural part of the story. To put it simply, you tell me about the character, and I'll tell you about how he got there.

Basically, the new(ish) condition for submitting a character is that, in doing so, you must be willing to let me take the reigns and lead the character off into the sunset on my own.


And now, time for me to do some digging for characters... I'm not looking forward to this.
