Red pressed her forehead against the cool, hard bark of a tree, letting her long hair tumble into her face. Everything Leomaris does has a reason. Right? She flipped around to look at the tall dragon and sank to the ground, resting her cheek on her knee. “I don’t know.”

 

Alan stared at her, trying to puzzle out who had told her everything. Who would tell a little girl to wander out here to a strange creature and not know what to do? He started to open his mouth, but then clamped it firmly shut, thinking better of it.

 

Red slowly unsheathed her sword, watching light glimmer over it mesmerizingly. She rubbed the base of her thumb over the icy gem, knowing that Leomaris wouldn’t let her in unless necessary. If it zapped his strength to bring her, then she would have to wait.

 

A quiet hush settled over the air, encasing everyone, including Ruby. She stood there, eyes slightly downcast, wings lowered and almost folded.

 

“I wonder if we should just go to it.” Red set down her sword and laid her hands neatly in her lap. “No harm I guess.”

 

“The guards would think otherwise. Besides, what friend told you to come here?”

 

Red softly closed her eyes, and leaned back. Leomaris had been something secret for her, but no more. Everything about Leomaris suddenly came spilling out, as if she could no longer hold the mysterious information.

Chapter 96

 

Almost everyone listened, with the exception of Senkara who sort of just hung around. Even Theron was quiet. Ruby laid herself down, using her wings to cover her back from the cold air to listen. She had to constantly fight off Red Lightning though; he was young and bold, constantly tempting the dragon by snorting, dashing up to her, and trying to pound her with his hooves. Alena and Mist stood nearby, calmed and grazing, but Wisdom’s Whisper was a lengthy ways away, carefully studying the beast.

 

By the time Red was done, Senkara had scaled a tree, and was now hanging limply from it, long asleep. Theron and Alan were both awake, and listening, but drowsy. Ruby had her eyes closed, her chest moving slightly in and out with each slow, ponderous breath. She looked like it, but she wasn’t asleep, for her head would perk up at the descriptions of the dragons and such.

 

At the end, Red simply sighed, and laid herself down on the ground, leaving the three who were awake to themselves.

 

Within the next two hours, the sun poked it’s head over the group rousing them to the morning. Alan was already up and moving, trying to wake the others. After several failed attempts, he snatched up Theron’s gun, clutched his ears, and fired a single shot into the sky.
