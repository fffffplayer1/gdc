Book One: The Virus Spreads
Chapter 2
23rd November 2028
7:53 am
Nexus City

Zedez didn’t recognise the place. Then again, why should he? He’d never been very far into Nexus City. He lived on a Nimbus Rock property not in the city. The blue arrow on his radar was pointing north-east. He began to head in that direction.
‘Unidentified personal nearby.’
Zedez almost jumped. The voice had come from right next to his ear. After a few seconds, he worked out that this bio-armour had a built in early warning system. It really was impressive. Some people were nearby: possibly enemies. He approached the door of a house. There were voices coming from the inside. He slowly looked inside making sure they couldn’t see him. Two people stood inside the house. Defiantly rebels. They wore hoodies with the hoods pulled up and held sub machine guns.
‘Your idea is stupid. We can’t just wait for Imfen to leave his office.’
‘But we can’t just storm the office either.’
‘Shut up. I’m going outside now.’
He walked out a back door of the building. There was one rebel left. He went into another room. Zedez was surprised to see that the door was unlocked. He walked inside. The room that the rebel had gone into was a kitchen. The rebel was making food on the counter. Zedez’s foot clanged against the wall. He cursed silently. The rebel turned around and began to come out of the room. Zedez needed a way out of this.
‘Cloak engaging.’ Said the bio-armour’s voice.
Zedez was surrounded by a bubble of blue energy and then his disappeared from sight completely. The rebel looked round. He saw nothing. He shrugged and went back into the kitchen. Zedez silently walked into the kitchen and smacked the rebel over the head. He fell to the floor. Zedez dragged the body into the living room and put in on the sofa. Now just one more to deal with. He was heading to the back door when something caught his eye. A box marked ‘ammunition’. Zedez open the box and searched for some pistol ammo magazines. He picked up three. On his lower back there were three more magnetic holsters. He attached the ammo clips to them. I unholstered his pistol and loaded a fourth magazine into it. His visor scanned the gun and indicated that it now had twenty bullets.

He headed for the back door. He opened it and cloaked himself. He walked out slowly and found the other rebel sat at a table drinking coffee. He thought about the best way to attack him. About thirty seconds later the bio-armour spoke.
‘Low energy.’
Zedez ran to the end of the garden and dived over a small wall. He became visible as he was standing on the wall. The rebel saw him and fired a stream of bullets in his direction. Zedez dived behind the wall. The shots continued. Zedez needed a way out. The rebel stopped firing. Zedez looked over slowly to find the rebel reloading. Zedez quickly aimed his pistol and fired three shots into the rebel. That was the threat of those two rebels over.
‘Radio transmission with Dr. Imfen incoming.’
Zedez heard a voice in the ear piece of his helmet.
‘Prophet, where are you? I need back up over here! Bring those Nexus Force troops or we’ll be in a lot of trouble.’
Zedez had to reach Imfen as quickly as possible.

Book One: The Virus Spreads
Chapter 3
23rd November 2028
8:09 am
Nexus City

Sergeant Zedez ran through the streets. He’d been spotted by three rebels. He looked round seeing that they held heavy machine guns.
‘Aw come on!’ he thought in exasperation.
He turned and fired a dozen bullets. One of the rebels took a shot to the shoulder and fell back. That just left two. The rebels returned fire. He dived to the side and into a house. Once inside he engaged invisibility. The two rebels caught up and looked inside.
‘Nothing.’ Said one of them.
‘You guys found anything?’ five more rebels had come up to the other two. Zedez almost swore. There were now seven to face. He hid behind a sofa and deactivated his cloak.
‘I’m sure I saw him go into this house.’
‘Yeah, me too.’
‘OK. You three check the house. The rest of us will go down the street. Regroup in five minutes.’
Zedez checked his armour energy. It was three quarters full and was slowly recharging.
‘There’s something behind that sofa.’
Zedez readied his gun. One of the rebels looked down at him.
‘IT’S PROPHET!’
Zedez sent a bullet into his face. The other two opened fire on the sofa. It stood no chance against their machine guns. The sofa was ripped apart.
‘The Prophet is defiantly dead. There’s no way he could survived that.’
But Zedez rose and fired at the two rebels. He had discovered another ability of his armour: defence mode. It maximised his defence levels to make him virtually indestructible. He charged out into the street, still in defence mode. He fired more shots into more rebels. He quickly dived behind a wall, ejected the empty ammo clip, loaded a new one and continued firing.
‘This is Rebel Unit Four. We need immediate backup here! The Prophet is invincible.’ One of the rebels yelled into a radio transmitter.
Zedez checked his energy levels: there were at half way now. Strange, defence mode seemed to use energy less often than stealth mode. He shrugged and continued killing rebels. Occasionally stopping to reload. There were hardly any left now. Zedez fired his last three bullets as the APC arrived.
‘Low energy.’
Zedez deactivated defence mode. He ran behind another wall and the APC opened fire on him. Bullets smashed into the wall but Zedez was safe- for now. One rebel troop came running up to the wall to try and shoot Zedez. But Zedez was ready for it. He grabbed the gun and ripped it out of the rebel’s hands. He swung his foot round into the rebel’s face. He searched the pockets of the rebel’s unconscious body and found what he was looking for: a grenade.
Zedez pulled out the pin and threw it at the APC. He yelled in alarm. He and thrown the PIN not the actual GRENADE. He quickly hurled the grenade away. It bounced along the floor and landed next the APC. Next second there was an explosion which threw the APC five metres into the air and into a shop window.
‘Prophet, seriously, man hurry up!’
Zedez groaned. Dr. Imfen was really starting to annoy him.

Book One: The Virus Spreads
Chapter 4
23rd November 2028
1:03 pm
Nexus Tower

Capt. Arran, Assembly engineer put his head against the door in an attempt to hear what Duke Exeter was shouting about.
‘WHAT DO YOU MEAN THEY’VE DISAPPEARED?!’ Duke was screaming.
‘Sir, I mean that they have gone missing somewhere.’ Came another voice
‘I’ve called for Captain Arran. His squad should be able to help find them.’
Arran recognised that voice as Dr. Overbuild’s. The door opened and Arran fell with it.
‘What are you doing?!’ shouted Duke.
‘I was... uh...’
‘Arran.’ Said Overbuild, saving him from thinking of an excuse. ‘You’re here.’
‘Get your squad, Captain. I have a mission for you.’ Said Duke, walking out of the room.
Ten minutes later Overbuild, Exeter, Arran and the other five members of his squad were sat around a meeting table.
‘OK. Let’s get on with this.’ Said Duke. ‘This morning at about six o’clock, I sent a team of Nexus Force troops to help Dr. Imfen and his group of fighters.’
‘Dr. Imfen is a fool.’ Muttered a Shinobi.
‘As I was saying. I sent some troops to help them. The team was in Avant Gardens at the time. I sent a submarine to collect them and take them to Nexus City.’
‘We all know that the South of Nexus City has been evacuated.’ Overbuild took over. ‘Because of the rebel force who had decided to fight against us, we cleared out the whole of the Southern city area which is where the rebels’ main base is.’
‘The team I sent was named Vamor Squad. They were supposed to contact us as soon as they arrived in the city at about six-forty. It has been hours since they were supposed to have contacted us.’ Duke continued.
‘As we speak, Hunter Squad is searching the seas for any information on their whereabouts. You-’ Overbuild pointed to Arran. ‘And your team, Tech Squad will search the streets. We want radio updates every half hour.’
‘Everyone with us?’ asked Duke.
‘Uh yeah.’ Said Private Kandem, a Knight who had been playing cards with Private Osllo, another Knight.
‘Can you go over it again?’ asked Sergeant Tarip who had dismantled his Daredevil’s flare gun and was trying to put it back together.
‘Don’t worry, sir. I’ve got it. I’ll tell them on the way.’
‘Good. You leave in two hours at ten past three. The survivors from Vamor Squad then they’ll have wait for just a bit longer.’
‘If there are any.’ Pointed out Tarip.
‘Yes.’ Agreed Overbuild. ‘If there are any.’
