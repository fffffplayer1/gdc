Book Two: The Four Kriken
Chapter 22
Xeryon’s Memory 2
19th February 2010
6:47pm
The Nexus Hotel

“So, how did Verarak manage to get us into the biggest and most expensive room of this hotel?”
The speaker was a minifigure in full battle armour holding a two handed battle axe. His name was Vorox and he was one of my most elite assassins.
“No idea but I think it was something to do with Duke’s Exeter’s bank account.” Replied Terraz, another assassin of mine.
He wore black camouflage with military body armour and a helmet. Slung over his shoulder was an assault rifle.
“And why this hotel? He could have chosen any hotel near to our target’s house but it had to be this one.” I said.
I was in his usual gear: white hooded cloak and body armour. With my usual weapons: sword and rifle.
“I just thought this hotel looked good.” Verarak said, coming in.
Like me he had a hooded cloak with the exception of it being red and black. And he carried a dagger.
Together, we were a team who regularly carried out missions together.
Vorox laughed. “Because you thought it was good?”
“Hey, guys I know why he chose this one now.” Terraz was looking out the window. “That girl, what’s her name? Maria. She’s in the restaurant down the street.
“That’s not the reason!” Protested Verarak.
“Time we were off. Our target’s out there.” I pointed outside.
I opened the balcony door and hurled myself off the balcony. Vorox followed me. We landed in the middle of the street. A second later, Terraz landed next to us and Verarak dropped down too. Before we could go anywhere several people surrounded us. Each was holding a weapon and they looked as though they wanted to smash us.

Book Two: The Four Kriken
Chapter 23
26th December 2028
8:55am
Helicopter

Xeryon awoke after another vision of his past. The crystal stopped glowing.
“Xeryon, you OK?” Asked Tarip. “You suddenly fell asleep and we had to drag in here.”
“Yeah I’m good.” Xeryon replied. “I saw my past.”
“I told you the crystal gives you weird dreams.” Arran said.
Xeryon looked around. He was in the helicopter with Arran and Tarip. Also inside were the three faction leaders. Duke was complaining about having to share a helicopter with three “annoying people”. Namely Xeryon, Arran and Tarip.
“Here we are.” Shouted the pilot.
Arran looked out of the window. The surface of the familiar red planet was quickly approaching. The helicopter landed and the doors opened. Everyone got out. A helicopter landed nearby, from which Dextron, Exceedra and Zedez got out, followed by Yakow.
Eventually everyone had got out of the helicopters.
“Alright, listen up!” Duke shouted. “We’re going to search the whole of this planet for the Kriken’s main base and we’re going to destroy it.”
Several people shouted in delight.
“I want Zedez, Xeryon, Exceedra, Dextron, Arran, Tarip and Yakow to do another mission for me. Alight troops, set up camp.”
He walked over to the group of friends.
“You know your mission. Find the Kriken keys.”
Duke signalled and two knights came forward. They handed Tarip and Zedez two backpacks.
“Camping equipment and supplies.” Duke told them. “Captain Arran, I’m putting you in command of this mission. Good luck.”
He turned and walked away.
“So, where do we start?” Asked Yakow.

Book Two: The Four Kriken
Chapter 24
28th December 2028
6:51pm
Krikeya

Two days on Krikeya hadn’t been very eventful. The group had come across a few stray Kriken but nothing else. They’d set up camp three hours away from where they had landed. They were near a small forest. There were only three small tents in the backpacks so some of the group had to sleep on the ground in sleeping bags.
Xeryon and Dextron were attempting to make a camp fire from a few pieces of wood from the forest.
“It’s no use.” Yakow said. He was sitting nearby. “Kriken wood is different to your wood. It can’t be used to make a fire.”
“Great!” Xeryon hurled several pieces away. “How much food we got?”
Yakow looked in one of the backpacks.
“Twelve bottles of water, five loafs of bread, a few packets of ham and cheese and six bottles of something.”
Yakow held out a bottle of blue liquid.
“Notion potion.” Said Dextron.
“What?”
“It’s an imagination drink, supposed to heal injuries.” Informed Xeryon. “Well, according to Duke anyway. I’ve never used it before. What supplies have we got?”
Yakow looked back into the back.
“A couple of handguns and a few clips of ammunition, a few grenades, a box of medical supplies.” Yakow opened the box. “Bandages, syringes, and medicine.”
“I reckon it’ll last us about two to three days.” Dextron said.
“Let’s hope we find the temple soon then. They others should be back soon. We’ll see if they found anything while on patrol.”
