Book One: The Virus Spreads
Chapter 6
23rd November 2028
3:49 pm
Nexus City

Arran fired. The missile blasted through the window and hit the side of the helicopter. The helicopter was ripped apart into four pieces. The pieces were hurled away in different directions.
‘YEAH!’ yelled Kandem. ‘TAKE THAT YOU FOOLS!’
‘They didn’t even see that coming.’ Quan celebrated.
Just as they were starting to relax, Corporal Fern ran over.
‘They’re coming from round the back.’ He said urgently.
‘They’ll get no further.’ Said Tarip, pulling out his flare guns.
‘Tech Squad! Defend this building!’ shouted Arran.
‘Yes, Captain.’ Osllo saluted. He may act stupid a lot of the time, but Osllo was always serious in battles.
The first rebel ran inside- only to be shot down by Tarip. Three more ran in, two carrying knives and one holding a machine gun. Arran dealt with the gunner as Kandem and Osllo engaged the others in a sword fight. Rebels continued to come in. After a few minutes they started using the front door too.
‘We can’t hold out forever!’ shouted Quan, dodging a knife and thrusting his katanas forward.
‘You’re right! But what can we do?’ Replied Arran, slamming his wrench down on a rebel’s head.
Tarip flipped round, shot a rebel, ducked a blade and kicked upwards. He turned again and fired point blank at a rebel who was standing next to him.
‘Any way we can get back up?’ He asked.
‘Fern had the radio transmitter.’ Said Quan. ‘Fern get over here!’
Fern ran forwards. On the way towards them he shot a rebel and hurled a grenade out the window.
‘Yeah? What is it?’
‘The radio transmitter. You got it?’
‘Aw, man! I left it on the plane.’
Quan cursed.
‘Well that’s not good.’ Said Arran. ‘But we have to keep holding out. Osllo and Kandem need help.
He wasn’t kidding. Osllo’s sword had been broken apart and he was running around dodging knifes and bullets. Kandem had picked up a gun and was behind cover, trying to find a moment to look round and shoot.
Fern, Tarip and Arran readied their guns. Quan gathered up a few shurikens.
‘Now.’
Quan threw the shurikens as the others opened fire. The rebels were killed instantly.
‘Nice work, team.’ Arran commented, kicking a body with his foot.
Their victory didn’t last long though. A window upstairs smashed.
‘I’ll check that.’ Said Kandem, running up the staircase.
There was a yell and Kandem was thrown down the stairs.
‘WHAT THE NEXUS?!’ shouted Fern.
A strange creature came down the stairs. Its whole body was bright red and tough looking. One hand was a giant crab claw. Three blue eyes were positioned in the middle of its face. It had no mouth.
‘It’s...’
‘A KRIKEN!’ yelled Fern.
The Kriken leaned down over Kandem’s downed body.
‘No!’ It was Osllo. He charged forward and kicked the Kriken in the face.
The Kriken looked at him. Then it picked him up and threw him into Quan. Fern and Tarip opened fire, aiming for the Kriken’s head. The shots had to effect what so ever. It punched Fern with incredible strength, sending him to the floor. It turned round and swung his foot, sweeping Tarip’s feet away from him. It turned on Arran next.
Arran raised his gun, only to have it knocked out of his hands. The Kriken threw him to the floor. It stood over him with its claw in his face.
‘Go on. Kill me.’
The Kriken looked at him. It raised its claw. Arran closed his eyes. This was the end. The claw came down. There was an inhuman scream. Arran opened his eyes. The Kriken was surrounded by purple energy. Not just any purple energy. It was Maelstrom. Something he hadn’t seen since he was a kid. The Maelstrom seemed to be draining the Kriken of its strength. The Kriken screamed.
‘Now, Xeryon!’ yelled a voice. There was a gunshot and the Kriken collapsed.
Arran looked up. Two people stood in front of the dead Kriken. One wore a black hooded cloak and a sword in a sheath on his back. Maelstrom energy curled in his hands. Arran recognised him as Elite. The Darkitect’s only son. Sixteen years ago Elite had defeated his father and destroyed the Maelstrom entirely.
‘Elite.’ Said Arran.
‘Yes. It’s me. Ever thought you’d meet the one who destroyed the Maelstrom?’
‘I doubt he ever did.’ Said the second person.
He wore a white cloak and body armour. He held a rifle and had a sword in a sheath at his side.
‘Xeryon? But aren’t you an enemy of the Nexus Force.’
‘I was.’ Replied Xeryon. ‘But I’ve changed. Noticed my helmet?’
Arran looked up. Xeryon was wearing a Rank Three Sentinel Samurai helmet.
‘Wow. I can’t believe I’m actually meeting you two.’
‘Look, I know you want to talk. But now is NOT the time.’ Said Elite.
‘He’s right. That crashed helicopter will attract a big crowd.’ Xeryon told him.
‘C’mon let’s get out of here. Wake up your squad.’ Said Elite who had noticed the downed soldiers.

A couple of minutes later everyone was ready. They gathered up all the supplies they could find and walked out into the street.

Book One: The Virus Spreads
Chapter 7
23rd November 2028
4:28 pm
Roads

‘We stop here to rest.’ Said Elite, pointing at a house.
They had been walking along the roads for a while. Arran looked over to where Elite was pointing. It was a farmhouse in good condition.
‘Are you sure that somebody isn’t living there?’
‘Well, when we found it, it was empty. If anyone goes in there without me they’ll be smashed by the nest of Dark Spiderlings I left in the back garden.’
Elite, Xeryon and Arran and the rest of his squad squad walked through a field and to the front door of the house.
‘I think I’ve lost the key somewhere.’
‘Never mind.’ Said Xeryon. He smashed his foot against the door. It crashed open.
‘Nearly fifty years old and I can still execute a perfect kick.’
Everyone quickly went inside as Elite shut the door.
‘There’s food through the other room. I’ll get some sleeping bags for us all. We’ll camp here for a few days.’ Said Xeryon, heading upstairs.
‘Tomorrow we’ll make a plan.’ Arran said.

24th November 2028
2:07 am
Farmhouse

Tarip woke up in his sleeping bag. He was hungry. He got up and walked through to the kitchen. He opened a cupboard and got out a pizza. He opened the oven and put in the pizza. He then turned on the oven and waited while the pizza cooked. While he waited, he looked out the window. He could see something strange in the distance. Bright lights. He assumed that they were just streetlights in the Northern city area. But then he saw the spaceship. It approached the lights and landed.
‘Guys, wake up!’ Shouted Tarip.
‘What is it?’ Asked Xeryon.
Elite rubbed his eyes.
‘Why are you waking us up this early?’
‘This had better be good, Tarip.’ Said Arran.
‘Outside! There’s Kriken activity.’
Hearing the word ‘Kriken’, Elite was instantly awake.
‘Where?’
‘In the distance. I saw a spaceship land near some lights.’
They looked out the window.
‘Over there.’ Tarip pointed out.
‘But that’s...’
‘The Nexus City Power Station.’ Finished Xeryon.
‘If the Kriken are around there, then it can’t be good.’
‘Come on. We’re going up there.’

24th November 2028
2:19 am
Power Station

They had crowded around the electric fence surrounding the power station.
‘There’s something going on in there alright.’ Said Kandem, looking through some binoculars.
‘Look over there.’
Four Kriken were walking about, quite clearly guarding the place.
‘We’ll take out those guards first.’ Said Xeryon, attaching a silencer to his rifle. Tarip brought out his own rifle.
‘How do we kill them?’ He asked. ‘When we attacked them back in that house, our shots had no effect.’
‘Aim for the left of where the heart would be.’ Explained Elite. ‘If they’re tough looking then you’ll have to weaken them first.’
Xeryon took out two extremely quickly. Tarip aimed and shot a third. The third was armoured.
‘My turn.’ Said Elite, running forwards.
He gathered energy and waved his hand at the gates. Maelstrom energy hit the gates and flung them off their hinges. He charged at the Kriken. Before the alien could react, he created a giant hand made up of Maelstrom and pulled it into the air. The Kriken’s energy left it and was sent to Elite instead. The Kriken began screaming. Elite dropped it to the ground, unsheathed his sword and drove it into the Kriken.
The others ran through the area where the gates once were.
‘Inside here.’ Quan had found a door leading inside.
They filed inside, slamming the door behind them. They were in a security room full of TVs.
‘Look there.’ Fern was pointing at a TV.
Elite looked at it. There was a minifigure in futuristic armour walking towards the power station.
‘The Prophet.’ Said Elite.
