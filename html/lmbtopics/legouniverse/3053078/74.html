Chapter 7

Part 2

 

“Argh! Help!”

Qartal got up to see one of the Assembly Grunts hanging out of the door. He and Kraken went over and pulled him in.

“Thank you.” The grunt said. “We’ve lost the missiles though.”

“Bricks.” Kraken cursed.

One of the other grunts spoke up. “I can try and hit the pilot of a helicopter with this rifle.”

“Go for it.” Kraken nodded.

The grunt took aim and fired three times. The helicopter dropped out of the sky and into the water below.

“Nice shooting.” His friend congratulated him.

“And look, the other helicopter is retreating.” Qartal said.

“Good now’s our chance, let’s do this.” Kraken said, hurling himself from the dropship.

Qartal and the Assembly grunts followed him. Qartal felt the air rush past him as he fell and then the hard wood of the deck as he landed. He heard a groan behind him and saw two grunts dragging their injured friend behind an unmanned cannon. Kraken had already brought out his Wormholer and had shot down four rebels who were on the deck. He focused his fire on the quarter deck where Colonel Vexvol was firing his pistol. Vexvol took cover as Kraken stopped shooting. 

“He’s trapped. Let’s go get him.”

Qartal drew a dagger which was his last remaining weapon. The two reached the quarter deck with one of the grunts behind them.

“He’s gone! What the brick is this?” Kraken shouted.

“He’s there!” Came a shout.

The injured grunt was pointing at the mast. Vexvol was climbing it.

“Oh, you’re for it now.”  Kraken said, taking aim with his Wormholer.

“No.” Said Qartal. “We can’t shoot him; we need to bring him in to find out why he did this.”

“I guess you’re right. You climb after him, my armour is too heavy.”

Chapter 7

Part 3

 

Qartal ran to the side of the ship and pulled himself up one of the ropes. He began climbing his way up. Vexvol took aim and fired. It barely missed him and Vexvol took aim again. By some miracle he was out of ammunition and he hurled the gun at Qartal. Qartal dodged and it landed in the sea. He continued climbing and reached the crow’s nest. Vexvol was already there.

“Why did you do this, Colonel?” Qartal asked.

Vexvol shrugged. “The rebels offered me a lot of money.”  And with that, he drew his sword and lunged.

Qartal dodged and Vexvol ran past him. Going too fast to stop himself, he tripped straight over the edge. Qartal reached out a hand to save him but it was too late. Vexvol had fallen straight onto the deck.

Alien Vengeance

Chapter 8

Prisoner

 

Zedez rolled over as he awakened. He sat up and surveyed his surroundings. He was in pitch black room. Zedez activated his heat vision and took another look round. The room was ridiculously small, just big enough to hold one person. Zedez knew it was only thanks to his armour that he was still alive. This room certainly contained no oxygen. But Zedez didn’t need oxygen nor food or water. His armour did everything. In time, it had even healed his neck wounds. Zedez took another look at his prison. One wall was a slightly lighter shade of grey than the other three.

The only thing I’ll die of here is boredom. Zedez thought. I think it’s time to leave.

He got up and used his armour to enhance the strength in his legs and slammed a foot into the wall that was a lighter grey. The wall smashed instantly and revealed a brightly lit corridor.

I hope they have home insurance. Zedez thought, going invisible.

Keeping his back to the walls, Zedez moved down the corridor. He froze momentarily as three creatures ran past. They were yellow lizard/scorpion like things. As the creatures went past, Zedez uncloaked, giving his armour a few seconds to gather its energy before cloaking again. He continued down the corridor and up a set of stairs. He had just reached the top  when his armour failed him again as it had done in Gnarled Forest.

“Did you really think it would be this easy?” Laughed a voice.

There was a flash of purple and Zedez fell unconscious. His escape had failed.
