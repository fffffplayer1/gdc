Book Three: Poison and Shadows
Chapter 17
29th June 2035
8:18pm
Nexus City Beach

Vaduz and Arran had met up with the others at the beach.
“So, this map will lead us to the staff.” Said Arran.
“Once we’ve found it, we can destroy both the staff and the sword. Then we can get back here and defeat the Masters.” Xeryon said.
“What does the map say?” Asked Akaras.
“It tells us to go across the sea where we will find an island. The staff is on the island in a building there.”
“Right! Let’s take the helicopter.” Said Xeno.
“I don’t think so!” Came a shout.
Several people stood behind them. They wore black ninja gear and had katanas either sheathed on their back or in their hands.
“The Shadow Master’s men.” Realised Alena.
“Yes. And we’re about to destroy you.”
They charged. In seconds, the team was in combat with them. Xeno tried running to the helicopter but found his way blocked by two ninjas. They swung at him. Xeno blocked both the attacks and stabbed back at his attackers. But they blocked too.
“We could take one of them.” Xeryon said to Alena. He pointed to a few speedboats tided to the pier.
“Yes. Vaduz, get one of those started up.”
Vaduz jumped onto the boat, followed by a ninja. Xeryon found himself faced against three ninjas. He swung his sword and then pulled back at the last second. The ninja had raised his sword to block and Xeryon used this advantage to slash at the ninja’s undefended area. As the ninja collapsed, Xeryon threw his final throwing knife at another ninja. That left one ninja. He swung his sword quickly three times. Xeryon blocked two strikes but the third one grazed his arm. Ignoring the pain, Xeryon kicked the ninja in the stomach and stabbed him.
Four more ninjas arrived. Xeryon pulled out his pistol and shot one. Then the other three got within melee combat range and Xeryon was forced to sword fight again. One ninja repeatedly slashed forward, forcing Xeryon to fall back. The ninja managed to sweep Xeryon’s legs and he fell to the ground. The ninja stood over him triumphantly, his sword raised. Then he collapsed. Alena had thrown her dagger at him. She ran over and collected it again as Xeryon stood up. The next ninja swung, Alena countered, knocking him down. The remaining ninja ran.
“Thanks.” Xeryon said.
Before Alena could answer there was a shout.
“YEAH! AND THAT’S WHY YOU DON’T MESS WITH US!”
Akaras and Xeno stood next to a group of ninja bodies. Arran and Zedez were nearby with even more.
“Hey guys let’s go!” Called Vaduz.
He threw his attacker into the sea and everyone got onto the boat.
“Nearly there now. We’ve almost got the staff.”

Book Three: Poison and Shadows
Chapter 18
29th June 2035
8:47pm
The Seas

The Island came into view after a bit. It was a volcano surrounded by palm trees.
“Probably dormant.” Said Vaduz.
“You sure?” Asked Arran. “Because I have no wish to be on an island with a mountain throwing lava at me.”
Eventually they reached the island. Vaduz parked the boat at the island’s small beach and the team got out. There was two boats already there.
“Got the cutlass?” Xeryon said to Akaras.
Akaras showed them that it was hidden inside his cloak.
“Good. Let’s go.”
They walked through a forest of palm trees for a while before reaching the centre of the island and the bottom of the volcano. There was a building here. Well, half a building. It was made of grey and black stone.
“What have we got here?” Asked a voice.
Xeryon turned round. The team was surrounded by green robed soldiers and ninjas. Many had swords but some were armed with rifles and crossbows. Two soldiers parted to let the two Masters through. The Poison Master held something dark green in his hands. It was a cross between a spear and a scythe. It was the staff they’d been looking for. The scythe blade glowed slightly.
“Ah. So nice of you to bring us the sword as well. Hand it over or we will shoot you. ”
Akaras had no choice. He pulled out the sword and threw it to the ground. A soldier ran forward and picked it up. He handed it to the Shadow Master.
“At last.” He said. “The weapons are ours.”
“Now to bring back the Kriken! They will give us power!”
“Hang on a second.” Said Xeryon. “I’d like to know: was it you who sent my clone after me?”
“Yes. We know you would cause us trouble and we had to get rid of you. Now enough talking. Let’s do this.”
The Masters stepped inside the building.
“NO!”
Akaras had had enough. He wasn’t going to let this happen. He and Xeno charged forwards. Akaras was drawing his sword and something hit him. It was a shadow. It smacked into him, smashing him. Xeno tried to turn around but was stopped in his tracks. A green cloud had surrounded him. He collapsed, the cloud weakening him. He too was dead.
The Poison Master spoke. “A demonstration for you. Showing you the powers of the Shadow Sword and the Poison Staff.”
“Piece of brick!” Arran yelled at him.
“Silence! Unless you want to be next.”
There was two shapes embedded into the walls. The two Masters placed the weapons in the shapes. It was a perfect fit. The ground began shaking and two blasts of green and black shot up into the sky. They hit each other and a vortex began, similar to the one that banished the Kriken.
“Guys,” Xeryon whispered. “On my signal, we attack.”
The shaking got worse. Xeryon picked his moment. He stepped forward, pulling out his pistol. He fired twice into the air.

Book Three: Poison and Shadows
Chapter 19
29th June 2035
8:51pm
Island

After firing, Xeryon ran, shooting a couple of soldiers as he did. He dived behind a large rock for cover. Alena and Arran did the same. Zedez turned invisible and disappeared somewhere and Vaduz used his Doomslicer to create a couple of energy blasts before he ran and ducked behind a tree.
A group of ninjas fired their rifles at the rock that Xeryon, Alena and Arran were using for cover. Xeryon put a new ammunition clip into his pistol and fired a couple of blind shots. Alena drew her blaster and fired a few herself.
“I’ve got this.” Arran said, getting out his Engineer’s magnet.
He waved it and several guns and came flying towards him. They became attached to his magnet, leaving the soldiers undefended so Alena and Xeryon could shoot them. Unfortunately, since the crossbows where made of wood, they hadn’t been caught in the magnetic field. But Zedez was already taking care of that. One by one, the archers went down by an invisible force. While all this was happening, the Poison Master had charged through the forest and left. Vaduz left the cover of the tree and charged over to the weapons.
“Guys I need some time to get these out!” He yelled.
“No problem.”
Alena and Arran began fighting the remaining guards, with the help of Zedez. Xeryon ran at the Shadow Master. Xeryon put his weapons away.
“Let’s make it a test of skill.” He told the Master.
“If that is what you wish.” He replied, putting away his own sword.
The two struck fighting stances and the Master lunged. Xeryon blocked and pushed him back. He swung with two of his own punches, each one blocked by the Shadow Master. Xeryon lashed out with his foot. The Master caught his leg and threw him to the ground. Xeryon quickly got up as the Master punched again. This time Xeryon countered, getting in a punch of his own.
“Xeryon what are you doing?” Yelled Vaduz.
“Buying you some time.”
Xeryon kicked the Shadow Master and swung a few more punches. Then he grabbed the Master’s arms and threw him to the floor. Xeryon crouched beside his fallen opponent.
“How?” Asked the Shadow Master.
“Goodbye.” Xeryon brought his dagger down on the Shadow Master.
By now the vortex was spinning faster and the rumbling was louder. Then it happened. A Kriken jumped through the vortex.
“Sorry! But you’re going back!” Shouted Alena.
She ran at the Kriken, slashed it with her dagger and kicked it back into the vortex.
“Vaduz hurry up! The vortex is causing the volcano to erupt.”
She was right. Smoke was coming from it. Arran shot the last couple of guards.
“That’s them all. But the Poison Master ran.” He yelled above the rumbling.
“I can’t do it!” Vaduz shouted.
“Let me try.” Zedez said, coming over.
He grabbed the staff. He struggled for a few seconds and then he pulled it out. He did the same with the sword. The rumbling stopped and the volcano calmed.
“Nice one Zedez.” Called Arran.
“It was my armour... Not me.”
“Come on guys, the Poison Master!” Alena said.
