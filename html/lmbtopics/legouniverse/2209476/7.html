" Yea." I said, sheathing my golden dagger.

" Doesn't matter." I said. We walked to where Levi watched Kel'ina being taken away. I followed them to where they threw her in a cell.

" Ha nice try Dorkitect." I said, " I ain't moving."
" Fine." Darkitect said, " I'll just wait for your attention span to run out" With that he disappeared.
" Why did I ever agree to help that fool." I muttered.
" You what?" Kel'ina said.
" Don't ask." I said. Kel'ina and I ended up staring at each other for about an hour in silences. Rose came down to us and broke the silence.
" Diana. Why are you STILL down here?" Rose blinked.
" Just leave us alone." I said, not removing my gaze from Kel'ina.
" Yea go find that kid Leafy." Kel'ina muttered.
" Its Levi." I corrected.
" I don't care. " Kel'ina Said, " How did you get the dagger back?"
" I jumped off the tree grabbed it and flew back up " I said sheepishly.
" Har Har." Kel'ina said. Rose blinked and left us and Another hour at staring at each other went past. I started into her eyes. both of us hated each other yet in some way I had some feelings for her. Finally, Vanda made me go to my room. Muttering, I left Kel'ina, With a promise that I would be back. . . . .

Vanda led me into a chamber and shut the door. She pushed me into a chair, which restrained my arms. She shined a harsh light on me. "Okay" she said. "Who are you and where did you come from?" I chuckled. "Whouldnt you like to know!" The light grew brighter, so I had to squint my eyes. "This isnt a joke" she said "I wanna know why you are hunting Diana." We sat in silence for a few minutes, staring intently into each others eyes. "Fine" said Vanda, standing up. "You wanna be siletn, fine. Im leaving. I hope you like going without dinner for a while. If you get hungry, just speak up. We can work something out." I knew what she meant: she getsffed info and I get fed food. She walked out the door. I heared a series of locks go into place. Right after she left, Garmadon appeared. "YOU!" I screamed. "My day has been ugly enough, and now I have to look at you!" He raised a hand and mocked me with an offended look. "Harsh words have harsh meanings" he said. "Besides, I wanted to discuss youre..eh.. failure. Why was that?" To be honest, I didnt know. I should have easily beaten her. "It was you wasnt it" I questioned. "He made another mock expression, this time looking innocent, like a child. "Me" he said, pointing at himself. "How could you say such a thing. Im hurt. Nah, Im just kidding. It was TOTALLY ME! I cast a weakening spell on you! Ta ta!" And with that he vanished.

I sat in the cell for about an hour. Then, a suge of power went through me. Garmadon's spell had worn off. I willed the metal in the restraints to open. I hopped out of the chair. I looked up, and on the ceiling was my escape: a vent shaft.

The shaft got out at what appeared to be the Command Room of the tower. All of its workers were easily dealt with. As I wanted to strike them down with the Blade of Dark TOmmorrows, I remembered it was no longer in my possesion. But what I also remebered was that two people had touched it: Vanda and (who else but) Diana. And no matter how brief their touch was, the posion would set in. But I remebered another thing: one of the two people who knows how to cure the posion is in this tower. His knowlege combined with the tech of NT and the Nexus itself, he might cure it in time. This must have been a hailstorm of ideas, because I got another one. Actually two
1) He would need all of the tech at full power to cure it
2) The Nexus Core Protector ran on electricity.
I began to press buttons, remove wires and put the elsewhere, and sometimes destroy them. A hologram of a girl, Nexus Naomi, popped up. "INTRUDER, INTRUDER" she chorused. "WHAT DO YOU THINK YOUA ARE DOING?"
"Shutting off all power in the tower" I replied. "It will be much easier to retreive my sword and hunt my prey when its pitch black. We lightning ninjas can see ANYTHING in the dark. All equipment, intecoms, weapons, defenses, teleporters, systems, computers and holograms will be shut down." i said, reaching for a lever. "Oh, Naomi, that means you too sweetie!" I pulled the switch, and everything went off.

It had been about a week since Diana had died. Well, that wasn't fun. "No, I am not leaving this room!" I said, resisting the tugs and pulls of Cole, Sensei Wu, Kai, Jay and Zane. I was holding onto the bed pole, and I am pretty sure that Cole nearly broke his leg. "Rose, we have to go to Crux!" Kai said, pulling harder. I knew he was still hurt, but I didn't want to leave, surprisingly enough. I missed Diana... she was my best friend, and other than Jazmin and Kai, the only one who understood me for who I am, not the strong and balanced Spinjitzu student. "Diana would want you to," Kai said. I sighed. "You had to say that, didn't you?" I said, and let go. We started walking to the launchpad. "Alright, let's go," Sensei said. We flew off towards Crux Prime.

It was about ten hours later when we got there, and my legs were numb. We landed at Sentinel Point Zeta, and I had the pleasure of crushing a stromling. "We need to get to the ruins," Kai said. We were unidentifiable with the hoods on. Levi had his on, too, because we didn't want to be noticed. I smashed a pirate, then kept walking. I held the Swords of Fire, and Jay held the Numbchucks of Lightning. Apparently Diana had dropped them. Cole held the Sycthe of Quakes, and Levi had the shurikens of ice. "You guys can smash the skeletons... we have to talk to the new Ninja master here," Kai said. Jay, Cole, Zane and Kai walked up to the ninja and started talking. Levi shrugged. "Fine with me," he said plainly. I slapped him. "Levi, my best friend just died, and I'm not in the brightest of moods.... don't anger me." He laughed at my remark. Since Diana was gone, he felt a little more confident around me. "Don't tell me you were afraid of Diana," I remarked, swinging the knife around. Levi nodded. "That girl scared me to the underworld, Rosy." I turned sharply to him. "Levi, do not call me Rosy!" I demanded loudly. A lot of people turned to us, and I looked at them back, returning their stares. "Oh, just keep to yourself!" I said. They nodded, and turned back to the skeletons they had been fighting, which also had turned to us. "Fine, your majesty," he mumbled. I glared at him. "What was that?"
