Chapter 2

In the mist of red flickering flames, strange blue gas, and flying pieces of scorching hot metal, the two ships flew forwards and then down, trying to unlock the two tail ends. Metal scraped off the hull as the two locked together and savagely trying to get away.

Deshal started to back away, but fear clogged his veins and seized his muscles. It was going to hit him, mow him over like grass.

Suddenly, one of the ships opened up, almost completely splitting down the middle. In a flash of yellow, several small figures slung out, scattering across the sky. The other ship also released small ships, probably given up on trying to keep the ships from crashing.

The abandoned cruisers kept speeding towards Deshal. He covered his face as if to shield it, even though it wouldn’t do anything.

At the last second, one of the small fighters made a loop and returned to Deshal, it hovered infront of his face and a hatch opened. A hand stretched out and seized Deshal by his jacket collar.

Chapter 3

Snatched up into the ship, Deshal struggled against whoever was holding him. “Let go!” He cried, and was instantly thrown to the flooring of the vehicle.

“Fine,” A deep voice said gruffly.

Deshal whirled to find whoever had grabbed him, but there was no one in sight, just the empty back cargo section. He cautiously stood up and balanced himself against the churning of the ship. He brushed his hair out of his face and tried to see if there was at least a window or a crack to look out with, but there wasn’t any. He felt trapped in a moving metal dungeon. But this dungeon did at least keep him from getting crushed.

Deshal wandered in small circles to keep himself warm from the chilly metal below. It seeped through his shoes and crept up his spine, freezing him all over.

Within thirty minutes or so of smooth flying, a small hatch opened in the cargo section. It hummed and pulled over to the side, revealing the front cockpit of the ship. It was ladled with buttons and beeping gadgets, but only a single pilot sat there in a stiff yellow chair.

“So you’re a recruit?” He asked, keeping his head forward.

“Umm….” Deshal mumbled.

“Great,” the pilot replied as if he had just answered yes, “Welcome to Kreativity Kingdom. Spelled with a K. Don’t mistake it.”

Chapter 4

Deshal didn’t have anything to do but nod as the pilot grabbed him roughly by the shoulder and squished him into an equally as stiff dull yellow chair.

“Just hold on.”

This was quite the confusing man considering there was nothing to hold onto besides himself. Deshal gazed ahead, peering out the front windshield when he finally realized what he was looking at. Space. Stars twirled around him as the pilot slowly twisted the ship to the side in a dizzying pattern of moves.

“Do you know what happened back there?” Deshal asked, his eyes locked onto the swirling murkiness in front of him.

“Not really. All we know is something happened to a pilot in one of our ships and he crashed into us. It doesn’t seem like that weird gas had any ill effects though. Not sure where it came from.” The pilot then pulled the ship into a tight left turn, heading towards a large body of mass.

Deshal’s eyes widened as he approached a large, dusty brown planet. Patches of green dotted it, surrounded by very small pools of blue and pockets of scarlet.
And sure enough, the pilot was determined to land here.

Chapter 4

Deshal didn’t have anything to do but nod as the pilot grabbed him roughly by the shoulder and squished him into an equally as stiff dull yellow chair.

“Just hold on.”

This was quite the confusing man considering there was nothing to hold onto besides himself. Deshal gazed ahead, peering out the front windshield when he finally realized what he was looking at. Space. Stars twirled around him as the pilot slowly twisted the ship to the side in a dizzying pattern of moves.

“Do you know what happened back there?” Deshal asked, his eyes locked onto the swirling murkiness in front of him.

“Not really. All we know is something happened to a pilot in one of our ships and he crashed into us. It doesn’t seem like that weird gas had any ill effects though. Not sure where it came from.” The pilot then pulled the ship into a tight left turn, heading towards a large body of mass.

Deshal’s eyes widened as he approached a large, dusty brown planet. Patches of green dotted it, surrounded by very small pools of blue and pockets of scarlet.
And sure enough, the pilot was determined to land here.
