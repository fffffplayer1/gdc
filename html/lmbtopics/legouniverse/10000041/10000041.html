‎06-02-2014 04:22 PM

I could feel the power

 

that was the first thing that I thought to myself, sitting in silence.

 

Something's happening to me

 

it was a gut feeling. like how you feel you're going to be sick after a vegan-style all-you-can-eat buffet, or you really don't want to go to school/work today. a dead certainty, like "I'm just going to give an advanced warning of kicking the bucket  or something but not allow you to do anything because watching you panic is fun."

 

my breath grew more and more rapid, and I could feel my heart smashing around inside my chest. was termination the only answer? did I deserve this? what had I done? what was going on? and did I have to die in these terrible clothes? 

 

I turned to the other person in my cell. he had been blinded, poor soul, as the gaurds seemed to never stop tormenting us. they took it to far with him though. 

 

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 

Apex looked up. he saw the other person, Lux. his glow seemed to be brighter then any others he had seen before. all the gaurds had their own light, of course. but this man was something different. he was like a moon to their stars. but a purple, cloudy tendril was envloping him and the tip of the cloud was writhing around his head-trying to find an entrance into his brain.

 

Lux's light, the gaurd's dim glow, the light of the person outside the window with explosives. but that was all he saw. because everything else was total, utter, blackness. he could only see people.

 

he heard the hissing, of course. he heard and saw the gaurds running from the area. but he didn't see the explosion. and, although he was in the midst of it, it didn't harm him. he had long since gone beyond physical pain. but he felt a forceful arm grip his shoulders, and drag him out of the blown wall of the cell. he turned around, but his mind was starting to fracture again

 

"No. don't let me forget."

Lux was up and running very quickly. sharp reflexes and years of training meant that the explosion had not harmed him, and when the mysterious figure dragged Apex from the blown cell, Lux sprinted up to them and grabbed Apex's other arm. together, they dragged him away and over a hill. When Lux paused for breath, having not prepared himself for such a daring escape, the stranger took a hood off to reveal long, shimmering blonde hair and eyes the colour of imaginite. as Lux leaned in closer, he noticed that her clothes seemed to conceal several weapons, from ninja stars to flash bangs. there was a katana strapped to her back, and some kind of rifle in her free hand. noticing the presense of Lux properly for the first time, she held out a partially gloved hand.

 

"I'm Izora." She said, in a brief and to the point fashion. 

 

"Lux Ralters." Lux replied. suddenly, there was shouting and the brief smile that had appeared on Izora's face vanished, to be replaced with a dark look.

 

"Can you shoot?" without waiting for a reply, she tossed the rifle to Lux. Lux caught it neatly and loaded, using the pump action to push the cartriges into position. 

 

"Well? Can you?" Izora repeated. Lux prepped the rifle against his shoulder and took aim. he noticed several figures running towards them, unmistacably gaurds from the prison. there was a blast from the rifle and one of the gaurds flew into several pieces.

 

"I think that answers your question." Lux said, and grinned. Izora's face flashed into an unmistakable smile for a second-then snapped back to normal. 

 

"Good. now keep at it! I'll handle any of them who get to close." Lux once again eyed the girl's weapon stash and decided not to argue. instead, he fires several more shots into the night. most of the gaurds were smashed and those that remained fled back to the safety of the prison. 

 

"Alright." said Lux. "Now,why did you break me out?" Izora shook her head.

 

"it wasn't you I was breaking out." she pointed at Apex. 

 

"it was him."

for a brief moment Lux was too stunned to reply. what did Izora want with Apex? he seemed to be more then a bit crackers, and had muttered nought but insane nonsence all the time.

 

noticing the questioning look on Lux's face, Izora leaned foward. 

 

"He is the last of his kind." She revealed.

 

Lux glanced once again at Apex, who was sitting motionless. "Kind of the crazy?" He asked.

 

Izora shook her head impatiently. "Not funny."

 

"It was a genuine question!"

 

Izora folded her arms. "It's a long story."

 

"Oh, but I adore stories."

 

"Fine. a long time ago, after the defeat of the maelstrom, minifigures started to mutate and evolve. they developed specific powers, and it spread very quickly. eventually, there were enough evolved minifigs to form different peoples, or tribes, of mutants, whilst the normal minifigs let them be and got on with their own lives."

"out of all the mutant tribes, the greatest were the seers. the seers could speak with the divine forces, gods, the creators. they looked down upon the rest of the nimbus system and judged them inferior. as a result, they swore never to intefere in their affairs. only to watch, observe, and learn."

"The trouble was, regular minifigs were jealous of mutants, and thier powers. their main target were the seers, whom, in all honesty, were arrogant and proud to be themselves. as a result, there was a war. and the seers lost. their mountain city was laid waste too, and all the seers were killed. but the normal minifigs suffered as well. out of the hundred thousand soldiers who went up the mountain, only a few hundred came back down, and most of them had gone mad. we thought that was the end." 

 

Izora pointed at Apex. "he is a seer."

 

"I see." murmered Lux. then he turned to Izora. "Are you evolved? a mutant?" 

 

She smiled properly for the first time that night. "Yep. my tribe is the ender tribe. we are artistic geniuses, and can sense danger from some ways away." 

 

She looked down, at her feet. "We also control some aspects of magic."

"Magic?" Lux asked, half exited and half dubious. "What sort of magic?"

 

"Brews and infusions. potions. that sort of thing." Izora explained.

 

"Oh."

 

"Now help me drag this guy to my operatives." Lux raised an eyebrow.

 

"I'm sorry, what?"

 

"Operatives. I have other teammates surrounding the area, waiting for my signal to blow that prison sky high."

 

"Smashing everyone inside?" Lux questioned. Izora hesitated for a moment, then stared at him straight in the eye. 

 

"Yes."

 

"Is Apex really worth that much? all powerful mutant and all, he's in CRAZYTOWN. he's taken up a permenant residence there, he's got an eye twitch, he never speaks.... I don't see how he can be of any practical use to anyone."  Izora nodded in agreement.

 

"Not practical use, no. but if we can extract his memories and abilities, and impliment them into a member of our tribe-"

 

"And what's going to happen to him?" Snapped Lux. "Are you planning on leaving the last of the seers an empty shell, a souless hole of a minifigure?" 

 

"Pretty much." Izora admitted. 

 

Lux stopped walking, and pulled Apex from Izora's grasp. "That is a fate no-one should ever have to suffer." Lux declared, feeling his anger rising.

 

"He should not." Agreed Izora. "But it is the only way." 

 

"To do what?"

 

Instead of answering, Izora cupped her hands and whistled hard. She then looked at Lux. "Now my troops are here. the situation is no longer in your hands, Ralters." 

 

But there was no-one showing up.

 

A hint of puzzlement crossed Izora's face, and she whistled again, then a third time. still no responce.

 

"Er... either your troops are the most stealty figs I've ever seen, or they're not coming."

 

Izora shook her head, her blonde hair cascading down her face. 

 

"They're loyal, and wouldn't abandon a cause so easily."

 

Feeling the situation rise beyond her control, Izora hurriedly grabbed at Apex. But Lux's weathered hands were stronger, and he shooker her off, running for the trees.
