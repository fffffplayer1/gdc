Chapter 11: I am (probably not) the Chosen One

‎09-21-2013 06:21 PM

*Flashback*

Chao pointed his finger at me. “And you’re probably the only one that can save the universe from him.”

 

*Flashback*

 

 


I stood dazed at Chao's words. Me? The only person who can defeat the Baron? Nonsense!

 

I scoffed at Chao's words.

 

"If you think I'm the 'chosen one', you're wrong. I bet that with all of your high tech Nexus-Force equipment, the factions can easily squash the Baron and still have time to make a sandwich." I said.

 

Was the ghost of a smile forming on Chao's lips, or was I just imagining it?

 

"Well, even with all of our 'high tech Nexus Force equipment," Chao replied, "We can't even leave a scratch on the Baron's ugly face. You see, the only thing that can defeat the Baron comes in the form of this."

 

Chao grabbed a dusty old notecard and read it to me.

 

"The Maelstrom can not be put out by light, or anything heavenly at all. Only the darkest Imaginiation user can topple the Maelstrom and save the Universe."

 

Now I understood why I was the chosen one. As the darkest user of Imagination, only I had the power to smash the Baron.

I grabbed Chao's shoulders and looked him in the eye intently,

 

"Get me the fastest rocket to Crux Prime." I said.

 

"Actually," Chao responded, "You'll be escorted to the Baron's command ship,"

 

Chao looked around to see if there were any volunteers.

 

"and be dropped off. Once you're in his command ship, don't stop to rescue any minifigs who have been infected."

 

I raised my mouth open to protest, but Chao cut me off.

 

"I know it'll seem immoral to not rescue anybody in danger, but remember that this might be the only chance you can even fight the Baron.", he said.

 

I silently agreed. Before I could even back out of this mission, I was led by a column of armored minifigs to my rocket. Sure enough, there were many eager minifigures to escort me.

 

As I stepped into the rocket, Chao said to me, "Good luck soldier."

 

The rocket lifted off, and I took what might be one of my last looks at Avant Garden.

 

END OF PART I.

‎09-24-2013 05:56 PM

Thoughts raced in my head as I stared out into the skies without any facial expressions. It was the voice of my pilot that jolted me back to reality.

 

"Hey darkling!", he said. I wished people would stop calling me that.

 

"What?", I rasped.

 

"You ready to land?", he said with a grin. I didn't understand while he was grinning until I realized that he opened the doors below me.

 

Let's just say falling through the skies at high speeds is not a fun task.

 

I screamed at the top of my lungs, then tried to make sure that I wasn't squashed into a pancake on impact. With the help of Imagination, I landed on my feet on the Baron's personal command ship with a thud.My dizziness faded and I jumped up into the air and threw my Maelstrom katanas.They acted as individuals, slicing any stromlings to studs and protecting me from harm.Soon, my katanas returned to me and all that was left of the stromlings was purple dust. I sprinted towards a hangar,and that's when I realized something was wrong.

 

The ship that had escorted me was shot down by two turrets and spiraled out of control, crashing on the surface of Crux Prime. The two turrets then pointed their guns at me.

 

Suddenly, they fired.I zigzagged across the surface of the ship,dodging every shell. The stromlings that were manning the turrets looked surprised, as if they thought nothing could get past them. They sat dazed for a while, while a growing ball of Imagination was forming in my hands. Once the stromlings regained their composure, I launched the ball of Imagination towards them.

 

The turrets exploded in fantastic colors.After a few minutes, they became smoldering ruins. I walked towards the hangar door and ripped it apart with my mind. Then, I walked inside.


"It's showtime.", I said with a smile.

The nine most terrifying words in the English language are, ‘I’m from the government and I’m here to help.

Chapter 13: Sixty Seconds, Part 1

‎09-28-2013 12:24 AM

The air reeked of musty bricks and worn-out weapons. I held my nose, and very carefully, tried not to throw up at the putrid odor. Random noises could be heard in the distance, and occasionally, I glanced over my back to see if anything was following me.

 

I walked towards a door, sat down, and meditated. Using a crushing gesture with my fists, the door flung itself open and crumpled itself into a ball of scrap metal. My eyes fluttered open as I surveyed the area around me. Astonished stromlings with gaping mouths were holding peanut-butter sandwiches in one hand and weapons in the other.

 

Don't even ask me how stromlings can eat peanut butter.

 

At least one stromling had the sense to react correctly. He raised his club in one hand, and shouted.

 

"Charge!", he screamed.

 

In a few seconds, stromlings flung themselves at me, with a colorful variety of weapons in the hands and sandwiches in their pockets. I blocked a swipe from a club, and parried. The stromling stagged back, and I pushed him as far as I could with my Imagination powers. Turns out it was more than enough, as he was propelled into the side of the wall, creating a crater.

 

It was evident that I couldn't hold this many enemies, I thought. Deflecting a slash from a sword and a jab from a spear, I implanted my katanas into the ground. A shield was formed around me, and the stromlings, being the silly creatures they are, kept attacking the shield. Meanwhile, I calmly sat down, then levitated. All I saw was red before the shield exploded, leaving me unharmed and all of the stromlings smashed to studs. There was only one being left though.

 

He was dressed in pure black, and it was clear to me that he was a high ranking soldier. Walking towards me, he unsheathed his partisan and pointed towards me. Before I could whip out my katanas, he implanted a device onto the ground. I recognized that. It was a bomb that exploded within sixty seconds.

 

 

Only a minute remaining before it goes off.

 

 

Only sixty seconds.

 

 

The nine most terrifying words in the English language are, ‘I’m from the government and I’m here to help.

Chapter 13: Sixty Seconds, Part 2

‎10-01-2013 11:29 PM

I would've been left staring at the bomb if it weren't for the being slicing his partisan towards me. My impulses took over and I blocked the incoming blow. I then thrusted fowards with my katanas, but this being was fast. He anticipated the blow and kicked me in the face.

 

WHACK!

 

The kick sent me flying across the room. I would've kept going if it weren't for the wall that stood in place. With a throbbing head, I stood up slowly, only to see the being lunging towards me with his partisan.

 

Luckily, my katanas reacted when I couldn't. They flew out of my hands by themselves, and engaged the being. Occupied with my katanas, the being slashed and parried with lightning speed. Speaking of lightning, there was a growing ball of one forming in my hands. When the being finally disabled the katanas, he looked towards me.

 

"Have a nice day!", I said playfully.

 

A metallic smell pervaded the air. The being pointed his partisan towards me, and charged.

 

Closer, and closer, and then...

 

My ball of imagination lightning flew out of my hands and hit the being. It exploded on impact, pushing him into the wall with such force that the wall bent around him, as if it were made out of dough. Before I could grab my katanas, he looked at me and spoke.

 

"Too late, Dimensio.", he said laconically.

 

Air rushed in as a hole was made in the wall, and the dark being was sucked outside. I put my katanas into my sheath, before I remembered what the being had said.

 

Too late, Dimensio.

 

Too late? Late for what? Then I remembered...

 

The bomb that was standing in the center of the room suddenly roared.


Three!

 

Two!

 

One!

 

BOOM!

 

The floor was torn apart by the explosion, and the flames were coming closer.

 

I ran up the stairs, dodging the flames as best as I could. Finally I came across a balcony. With nowhere to go and the flames approaching, I jumped towards the surface of Crux Prime.

 

I closed my eyes as I braced for impact.

Chapter 14: The Crux of the Problem, Part 1

‎10-08-2013 10:55 PM

I wake up, only to find myself stuck in a pool of purple tar. My hands go to wipe the goo off my face, when my muscles stiffen. How long have I been out, anyways? Slowly, and one step at a time, I step out of the pool and grip my katanas tightly. They've somehow changed from dark purple to rose red, but I have no idea how they've changed colors.

 

I walk. Run. Slow down a little bit.

 

Then, suddenly a cry.

 

"Hey stromlings!", a fearless voice shouted. "Come and get us!"

 

That voice originated from a Sentinel knight, who was accompanied by many other knights and samurais. This caught the attention of every stromling nearby. Mechs, spiders, and ronin charged towards the Sentinels.

 

Volley after volley, samurai fire flaming arrows into the ranks of the stromlings, causing mass confusion. Still, the stromlings regained their composure quickly and attacked the shield wall directly.

 

A sentinel knight sacrificed himself to a energy bolt to save his comrades. A squad of samurai held down a group of ronin, and intense close combat was soon underway. The shield wall was weakening fast.

 

Soon, the stromlings started attacking me. As a mech fired its energy bolt, I dodged and ran forward as it was reloading. I threw out my katanas to cut off its shield and ran up its body. Ripping out its circuitry board, I subdued the mech, and now made it work for me. I made it aim towards a group of ronin heading to smash some Sentinels.

 

BOOM!

 

The ronin were blasted to studs, and all that was left when the dust cleared were some burnt helmets and armor. After a few minutes of sabotaging and smashing, every stromling was defeated.

 

The sentinel knights stood in awe, lifting their burnt visors to take a good look at me. They straightened their armor, and put their weapons at their sides.

 

I laughed. “I do this on a regular basis.” I said.

 

While I was laughing, I failed to notice a Sentinel was charging at me.

 

I still don’t know why he would do this when my katana smashes him.

Chapter 14: The Crux of the Problem, Part 2

Options
  • Mark as New
  • Bookmark
  • Subscribe to RSS Feed
  • Highlight

  • REPORT POST

‎10-08-2013 11:24 PM

It’s that quick. My arm thrusts out with my katana in its grasp, and smashes the knight. His armor and shield fall to the ground with a clang.

 

The looks on the faces of the Sentinels are mixed. Some are still in awe, others sad, some even crying.

 

But the looks all turn into one kind.

 

Anger.

 

“Get him!”, screamed a samurai.

 

I find myself suddenly parrying blows and deflecting arrows from the people I once called my allies. The clash of metal on metal continuously rings in my ears. What happened to the Sentinels anyways? Have they turned on their fellow factions?

 

It’s this thought that causes me to be disoriented. My whirlwind of parrying comes to a stop, as I get knocked squarely in the face with a spearhead. Luckily, my reflexes are fast enough to keep me alive. I backflip, and throw my katanas at the incoming knights. The katanas spring into action, and start engaging the knights.

 

Then I see I’m weaponless. A group of samurai have encircled me, and the first one charges. I hold my palm out, expecting some kind of Imagination to flicker. There was a lot more. A boom from a cannon could be heard as the samurai flies out of sight. I look at my hands, and wisps of smoke are coming off them. That boom wasn’t from a cannon. It was from my own hands!

 

I’m using this new skill to defend myself against the onslaught of samurai. Scores of them are flying in the air, and then smashing into bricks and studs after I fire blast after blast of Imagination.

 

“We need him alive for the war with Paradox!”, shouts one samurai, before I throw a ball of Imagination fire at him.

 

“Who?”, I ask.

 

Note to self: People can’t answer questions if they’re on fire.

 

It’s not long before all of the samurai have either fainted or have been smashed. There’s only two Sentinels left on the battlefield. And only I will be standing soon.
