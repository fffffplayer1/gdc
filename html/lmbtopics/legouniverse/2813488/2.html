Chapter 3: Out of the Ordinary, and into the Strange
“So let me get this straight,” said Chao as we drove off. “You’re a Maelstrom Darkling, that can use Imagination in a really weird way, and you hate the Darkitect, or Baron, to you.”
I nodded.
“Well then!”, he said. “This proves I’m really going insane.”
I thought about the Baron, or the Darkitect, which was the name that these minifigures gave him. I wondered if he was still searching for me. Or what’s left of me anyways.
It was a couple of long, and arduous hours until we finally reached the launch pad to Nexus Tower. The rocket opened with a sort-of dramatic flair. Chao and I stepped into the rocket.
“A word of advice,”, Chao said. “Just don’t make Vanda mad, or you’ll end up like Genie over there.” Chao pointed to a minifigure with no eyes, no arms. He grinned at us. I gulped.
The rocket door closed with a SWISH, and we were propelled towards Nexus Tower.

I don’t know why, but I got scared the moment we arrived at the doors of Nexus
Tower.
It was strange…because…everything was dead silent. The usual hum of the Imagination Tanks were gone. Trades between minifigures were no longer here. Talking was absolutely a prohibition, as it seemed.
I grasped my Katanas as I advanced with Chao. Nothing seemed the same at Nexus Tower. Or so it seemed…
A growl could be heard in the distance. “Did you hear that?”, asked Chao.
Suddenly, a strange creature jumped towards me. It bared its fangs like a barbarian.
“SSSS!!”, it growled, as it pounced over me.
It was close, but not close enough. Imagination lightning burst out my palms, as I was electrocuting the creature. I kept standing and moving forward, still blasting the creature with lightning, until it finally succumbed with a whine, as it lay along the corridor.
Stromlings were all circling around us, with blasters designed to fire us all down. I wouldn’t let that happen. I stood back to back with Chao as he grabbed his energy pistol, and I unsheathed my katanas once again.
“Let’s do this.”, I said. Chao nodded.
We charged with a fury that was unequaled. I slashed stromlings into bare studs, with my katanas. Several of them tried to knock me out cold from the behind, but alas, they failed miserably. I grasped a stromling’s head, and sent arcs of lightning through his body, while the other lightning arcs lashed out at multiple. Once they looked like they couldn’t take anymore, I lift them up with my Imagination, and thrust them out into the surface of the world Nexus Tower was on, if there ever was one.
I looked towards Chao.
He looked like he was in deep trouble. Chao had multiple, flowing scars from his face from battle. He got out two blasters once his main gun was out of Imagination. Ten stromlings came towards him. Chao looked sideways, and shot like a madman. Stromlings were reduced to mere purple bricks, as they were ripped apart by Imagination, into studs. But still, there were many, too many more stromlings left to be smashed.

I decided to end this all.

I became one with Imagination, as I slowly made a field of Imagination. First, the weapons of the stromlings were absorbed by my field. Stromlings started to begin circling around me, helpless in mid-air. Chao looked at me in awe. I saw pure red in my eyes, and I unleashed my fury. I let loose a scream, to be heard miles away. Walls and doors were blasted straight open, as Imagination raced across Nexus Tower. Stromlings were blasted with a tremendous amount of Imagination. They were stripped down to bricks, studs, until they were finally transformed into dust. One stromling was slowly dismantled, as Chao looked in relief. As the stromling turned around, he was into bricks, and then into purple matter. And then finally into dust. When I finally finished my fury, I stepped down onto the ground, and Chao looked at me.

“That. Was. So. Bricking. Awesome!”, he said.
I shrugged, as the wind blew the remains of the stromlings away.
Then suddenly, clanks and creaks could be heard in the distance. Some kind of machine was coming towards us. And we were ready.
Chao grabbed his cleaver, while I stood furious and resolute, lightning brimming from my palm.
The nine most terrifying words in the English language are, ‘I’m from the government and I’m here to help.

Chapter 4: A Cruel Twist
I grabbed my katanas from the ground, and charged them with lightning. It didn’t faze me one bit. Chao grabbed his pistols, hesitant to shoot at the approaching thing. Or machine.
The machine revealed itself. But it wasn’t just one. No, it was plenty of others. It banged its claws and shield against the ground.
And then, something went wrong. The machine charged towards us, and with its baton, swiped Chao offbalance and smacked him down under Nexus Tower, where the surface of the planet was. The machine then looked at me. It threw fireballs and shards of ice at me, but none could unbalance me. I slid under the machine, and shocked it with a ball of Imagination.
That kind of petrified it a bit. I came out the other side, feeling furious. I lifted the machine, still shocked, into the air. I grasped and tightened my palm. The machine started crumbling into a ball, until finally, it was the way I wanted it to be. I thrust the machine away, with the flick of my hand, and pushed a ball of imagination towards it. I walked away, as the ruins of the machine exploded in the air, creating one huge crack in the wall.

I ran towards the room where the faction leaders met. I had to know what was going on in Nexus Tower.
When I arrived, the room was dark. Pitch-Black. I starting searching where the leaders where. Something was there, and the footsteps of it could be heard. I turned, and a blade almost hit me. I deflected it, and shook the curtain behind me with intense Imagination. Out of the dust, came a stromling, completely fried to the smash. He slided down on the ground. I sighed.
I advanced slowly into the room, knowing everything could go wrong in a second. Suddenly, a flickered. The room was turned into a brightly-lit place. I unsheathed my katanas, believing masses of stromlings would come after me. But no. It was something far worse.
“Welcome back Minion!”, said a familiar voice. A voice that seemed much too familiar.
I whipped around. It was the Baron.
I was in a rage. I grabbed the Baron, and pushed him into layers and layers of walls. Each time I broke a wall, I became even more furious. We then reached the bedmetal. I stepped back, and broke out my Katanas.
I pointed them towards the Baron.
“Where are they?”, I snarled. “Where are the faction leaders?”
“Oh you’ll meet them sometime.”, the Baron said. “Sometime very soon…”
The Baron cackled, and before I could strike him down, he vanished into shadows.
I cursed. “Dang it!”, I shouted. “Almost had him!”
I suddenly remembered I had Chao’s radio transmission in my pocket.
I tuned in, and I got voice.
“Is this Chao?”, someone said. I recognized the voice. It was Turbo Velocity. “No it ain’t.”, I said. I looked back at the holes I made in the walls. “We’ve got a lot of work to do…."
