â01-23-2016 04:31 PM

Original
     A massive metal staff with four perpendicular prongs at one end. The prongs are capable of firing a colorless beam of energy that only affects organic matter and passes right through all inorganic forms of matter, including other forms of energy. The beam breaks the known laws of physics by bridging the physical and digital realms, forcefully transforming victims' bodies and essences/minds/souls into raw data which is then stored inside the staff's vast hardrive. The data is fully manipulatable by the user, who can reconstruct it in any way he likes and expel it back into the physical realm at a whim.

 

     Assimilated organic matter can be used to create any physical object. Assimilated minds essentially become a form of artificial intelligence, which can be programmed however the user wishes. The reprogrammed mind data can be merged with the reconstructed matter data to create living bodies once expelled back into the physical realm. The Personas primarily use the staff this way to create their legions of Datai. All assimilated data can also be forcefully trapped back inside the staff for storage, allowing the user to repair any faulty coding that may appear over time, such as when the occasional Datai begins to remember its human self (as was common in earlier assimilations, like Robert Shay).

     

     A secondary function utilizes a severely modified and downsized human slipspace device, allowing the user to crudely slice open gateways in the fabric of space. These portals can lead to anywhere within the user's current dimension, or to anywhere within a different dimension entirely, granting instant travel to another location. This use creates an unsettling noise akin to countless glass panes shattering simultaneously, so it's not sneaky whatsoever.

     The staff is only able to be wielded by A.I.s classified as "smart" or above, due to its technopathic demands. The one exception is that Datai are capable of using it.


     It and all of its stored data, including The Confirmer, was destroyed by Gamma Division agent Kraatix in the Hero Factory universe.


     Higher-ranking Datai are equipped with the assimilation beam as standard issue.

 

Pain's Colossus's Staff
     Pain briefly created a super-sized Composer Staff to fit in the hands of his gargantuan Colossus body. It was much cruder than the original, but still included all the same functions. It was destroyed along with Pain in the energy storm that resulted from the activation of the Codrex in the Matoran Universe

 

Hardlight-manufactured
     Since the perfection of Hardlight technology, the Personas can potentially create as many completely functional Composer Staves as they want. Appearance and size can vary infinitely, and at this time these can only be wielded by the Hardlight-controlling Personas. It is worthy to note that most of these may not include the dimension traveling ability due to redundancy, but those that do would most likely be installed with modern warp drives. Additionally, like all hyper-advanced technology, they would take a significantly longer time to conjure than static or mechanically operated objects.

 

Data-manufactured

     Some models of Datai are equipped with the original blueprints and knowledge needed to construct a fully-functional Staff from the data particles of their own bodies. This has only ever been seen in use once, by the late Cynderklaw-based Datai model in the Chima universe.

Warp Drives (1)

â01-24-2016 04:43 PM

     With the Personas' rapid technological evolution and the even faster increase in number of Datai, the clunky and inefficient portal technology integrated into The Composer's Staff simply couldn't satisfy the high demands of quick transportation for them all. After extensive research, development, and perfecting, the Personas finally developed a far superior alternative via warp drive technology, which allows the user to instantaneously travel from one location to another. This action can be done within or between dimensions, allowing immediate travel between universes if desired. The travel time is indeed instant, being slightly faster than the time it takes to blink. Unfortunately, the space/time disturbance of the warp still gives off a rather bright and deliberate flash of blue light, making it unsuitable for stealth.


     The warp drives are, in actuality, powerful miniature time machines. Instead of forcefully passing through the "folds" of the fabric of space to shorten long distance travel, they use time travel to move so much more smoothly through the planes of existence. However, they are not time machines in the familiar sense, and cannot travel to the past and future. In basic terms, rather than moving up (future) and down (past) on the y-axis, the warp drives allow the user to move side to side on the x-axis (present); essentially to any pinpoint location within a single instant of time in the present, permitting such fluid and immediate movement.


     It is fully possible for the warp drive technology to be transformed into full-on time travel, unlocking access to the y-axis. However none of the Personas have a use, let alone an interest, for such ability. Yet.

Software
     The warp drive tech is available as software for the Personas and each Datai. Every Datai is equipped with a warp drive written into its coding, and the Personas can willfully install or uninstall it within their own processors. Other inorganic minds could potentially download it as well, should any Persona grant them access.

 

Hardware
     The warp drive tech can also be made into hardware as small devices that can be used by anyone, including organic beings. These handheld devices are somehow able to link to the user's will, and can be operated exactly like the software version. It is speculated that the warp drives simply connect to inorganic minds, and respond to the electric signals of neural activity in the brain for organic minds.
     

Warp drives are able to be created with Hardlight, but like all hyper-advanced technology, they take significantly longer to conjure than static or mechanically operated objects.
