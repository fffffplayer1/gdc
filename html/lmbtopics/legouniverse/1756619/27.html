Chapter 4 >>>

BerserkFusionDude, or Fusion, as he was know to his friends, sat down at a table, after grabbing his dinner meal.
On his left was Hurricane, who was deep in conversation about battle plans with Ninewells, and on Fusion's left was EmperorFred, who was munching on a biscuit while reading a book.
Fusion looked up, and tapped Fred on the shoulder.
'Who?" Fred said, not looking up from his book.
"Fusion. I was just wondering what gear you would want to choose when Numb Chuck asks us."
Hurricane had heard, and turned around, along with Ninewells.
"Well, I sort of wanted to look like Percy Jackson..." Fred said.
Golden, who was sitting across the table, picked up Fred's book, and glanced at the cover. It was the first book of the Percy Jackson series. He then set the book back down, after reading his favorite part.
Everyone started to discuss what they wanted to have for their gear. Fusion, however, had his own thoughts.
"I didn't really like any of the weapons we trained with," he said. "I was more interested in learning... well.. magic."
"Kenjin the Wise did mention that magic does exist," Milligan, who had joined the conversation, said.
"Exactly, you are correct."
Everyone turned around, and found themselves staring at Kenjin the Wise.
"How does the magic work?" Fusion immediately asked.
"I do not know. Only Mythrans, First Builders, and those who came before them know the secrets of magic. The only problem is this: Mythrans who support humans have all been imprisoned by the Nexus Force, the First Builders and the other Mythrans would kill any of us on sight, and those who came before them are on the edges of the Universe, and many of them would kill you on sight too," Kenjin said.
"So, I guess I don't have a hope," Fusion said, dejectedly.
"That is not true. There is one Mythran who will be able to help you."
"Who is that?"
"He is a native of our world, and went to yours for a short time to help the Lego Company. The reason he is not imprisoned is because he was able to fight off the Nexus Force Troops sent to capture him. His name is Inventrius."

Wow, that's exactly like me, reading a book and not paying any attention to what anyone's saying ! Anyways, here's what could have happened to me

When I first started playing LEGO Universe during the giant Maelstrom attack, I was in Pet Cove, taking pictures for the Best Faction Leader screenshot contest. Most of the newbies there were smashed instantly, but about a dozen newer players, a few semi-strong ones, a couple of other rank threes and I made it to the top of the lighthouse, where we were safe from the Maelstrom. Eventually, dragons came. We were able to hold them off for a little while, but eventually, they broke through our defenses. At that same time, however, we all entered the game. Because of this, we were able to actually go inside of the lighthouse. Inside, I found a gray owl that looked strangely like the one that my minifig that I was holding in real life had, was obedient to me, and answered to the name "Owluke" [the same thing I call Demigod Chewy's owl]... As the Maelstrom lay siege to the lighthouse, we were trapped there, and we couldn't use our in-game since there were no launchpads, so I built my rocket for the Rocket Building contest, the Flying Fred Rocket, out of spare parts in the lighthouse, and flew to Forbidden Valley to get help.

Here are two ideas for future things that you could make me do, if you feel like it and can fit them in.

1: A mission to rescue the people in Pet Cove

2: There could be a cave or temple or something in Forbidden Valley or some other planet that either (a) Numb Chuck sends everyone into for the final stage of their training or (b) just I go to. This cave/temple thingy could be like that one cave in Star Wars on Dagobah that Yoda sends Luke into, in that it creates different illusions, some of which are real and solid. When I get to the end, I find a helmet, armor, and the sword and shield that transform from art supplies, which all look exactly like the ones that Demigod Chewy has.

Also, the sword is made from a metal that I made up called Olympian Silver, the Shield is painted Olympian Silver, the armor is plain steel with some Olympian Silver plating, and the helmet is made from a mixture of Olympian Silver, steel, and Obsidian.

Actually, I made up an alternate universe today where minifigs had to escape from their galaxy eons ago, and they journeyed through deep space for dozens of generations. Over this time, they mentally regressed, becoming somewhat like Rabbids in terms of average intelligence and attention span, if anyone has ever played one of those video games. The first to reach a new galaxy was a ship containing minifigs that kept their original intelligence but were mutated into horrible monstrosities with deformed skin [known to some as flesh-colored minifigs], and they became bitter and evil. The new galaxy was populated by clone-brand figures, devoted to utterly imagination-less law and order, and the fleshies joined them. However, the yellow figs still carried imagination with them, and they mercilessly smashed all who opposed it, driving the few survivors far beyond the outer rim.

Now, it isn't about good versus evil. It's about order versus chaos, and order, I'm glad to say, is fighting a losing battle. This is a universe of parodies and paradoxes, parachutes and parakeets, paratroopers and paralegals! The minifigs may seem like peaceful little fellows, but that smiling face contains the heart of a violent, merciless warrior. Now, you may be thinking that it is because of some deep-seated grudge against life itself, but the truth is, these minifigs just love blowing each other up, and love being blown up themselves almost as much, because they realize just how awesome it is. They exist to spread destruction, awesomeness, and humor, and wars are always being fought over whatever seems to be the funniest possible cause. The minifigs share a proud heritage of fighting in primordial toy boxes, where they weren't subject to the mundane laws of logic, physics, and continuity, so why should they be now? Furthermore, the entire universe is like a crazy, pun-filled parody of the universe [for example, did you know that the great Master Yo-yo created a strange type of energy called the "fork" out of mini-chlorines, and that its dark side is called the "spork"] It is said in ancient legend that somewhere within this universe exists a mythical place known only as the Amazing Brick Source [ABS, like the type of plastic that LEGO uses], where all LEGO pieces are made. Due to the massive amount of Imagination that it produces, great cities can be built in mere days. However, they're usually destroyed just as quickly, and if, for some reason, the enemy fails to do so, the minifigs of the city will generally get bored quickly and smash it themselves so that they can build something else. Because of this great imaginative force, the most valuable things are bricks. Some minifigs are part of great empires spanning dozens of planets, while others eke out an existence on forsaken pieces of rock in space salvaging for bricks and often resorting to "rainbow-ism" for their creations. Due to their incredibly short attention-span, there are very few civilians in this universe. The minifgs are generally only satisfied when doing things such as fighting to the death, discovering ancient treasures, and building incredible fortresses, powerful starships, and weapons of mass destruction. And now... I've been holding it back... !!!
