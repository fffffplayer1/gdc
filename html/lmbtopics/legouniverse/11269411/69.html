‎04-30-2016 02:20 PM

Panting heavily, Austin ran down the tunnel. It was so dark he could hardly see anything, and as a result kept tripping over shovels and pickaxes. Despite all that he continued running as fast as he could.

 

It seemed almost an eternity before he reached the giant trapdoor. With his sword still unsheathed in one of his hands, he climbed out of the mine, trying to catch breath as he did so. Refusing to stop to rest, he ran through the door that led into the main tower.

 

A cluster of Paradox Rogues was in the hallway and turned towards him as he emerged. Austin was angry, though not surprised, to find more of thedude’s minions here. He held his sword up and attacked, and soon defeated them. Spurred on by this victory, he raced to the end of the hallway and paused. Where was Lady Charlotte most likely to be? Austin glanced at the door leading to the dining room, then to the door to the stairs, breathing heavily. He wasn’t even sure if it was a mealtime right now, and Charlotte now rarely ate in the dining room anyway. Deciding that she was more likely to be upstairs studying, he barged through the door and took two steps at a time, only to run into more Paradox Rogues at the top. He smashed two of them before they could draw their blasters, but the third Rogue managed to get a shot off. It grazed Austin’s leg, making him grunt in pain even as he struck down his attacker.

 

Limping, Austin made his way toward the next stairs until he was in another hallway. By now the enemy had been alerted of his presence; he heard the synchronized approach of a dozen troops as they came around the corner and sighted him. Unsuccessfully trying to ignore the pain in his leg, he thrust his sword at the nearest Rogue.

Even as he defeated the first one, reinforcements came from other doors, and more came from behind him- apparently they had gotten past Grongringro and Peregrine in the mines and followed Austin upstairs. They engaged him on both sides, and while the narrow hallway worked in his favor, there was no way he could defeat so many foes.

 

A part of him knew it was lost cause. He had been doing hard labor for over a week, had just run almost a mile, and was outnumbered by enemies with superior gear. He was not going to survive.

 

A frightening light shone in his eyes, and he gripped his sword more tightly. He growled, a sound that grew into a roar of adrenaline.

 

“So be it!” he cried, and thrust himself at them savagely. He fought as hard as he could; he’d always been the best swordsman out of his brothers. For every blow he received he made sure he delivered two more. But his strength started to quaver, his wounds proving too much as they multiplied. He tried to feel at peace with this end- after all, they had always known their scheme was a long shot. He failed to block one strike, then another, and another.

 

“Let it… never be said,” he gasped, something warm dripping from his mouth, “that the Silverstines… died as cowards….”

 

A final shot rang out as he fell to the stained floor.

"HEY! I'm a good runner too!" Peragrine hollered over his shoulder after Austin, who was already gone. Then he dove out of the way of a crushing blow from the creepy lady attacking him.


"Whoa. Even the ladies around here are strong!" he thought to himself.

Peragrine kept moving. He was too fast for her. "For the moment, anyway. When's she gonna try something new?" he mused, as he hopped away from another bone-crushing blow.

 

The Paradox Sorceress abruptly spoke. Her eyes dimly lit purple in the shadowy mine. "Your- You're so BRIGHT! How did I not notice that before? So full of Energy! I MUST have you for myself!" She quickly slung her Doomslicer on her back, and began muttering words under her breath while doing elaborate hand motions in Perry's direction, as if she was doing a kata.

 

"Oh, now that's not creepy at all!" said Perry, who saw his chance, and lined up and fired his flareguns.

 

The shots were true...but not enough.

In the short span of time it took for the projectiles to reach the Sorceress's outstretched hands, a dark- darker than the mine around it- portal opened between her hands, and Peragrine's projectiles were swallowed up. The woman behind it gave a small gasp... she staggered, but held herself upright. Her eyes were very bright now, and the small portal in her hands began giving off miniature bolts of violet lightning.

 

Peragrine, despite himself, was somewhat impressed. "Gee. That's a cute little maelstrom vortex you've got there."

 

Menaya Kull smiled. "You have no idea" she thought. She saw her incantation doing its work. The bright pulsating lifeform of this boy was being channeled into the Portal, directly into her inner self, boosting her mystic prowess. A shame that quite a good bit of it would have to be used to heal the internal flaregun wounds... But it would still be a large net gain.

 

Peragrine aimed his flareguns again, intending to not hit the vortex this time, but as he aimed, he suddenly felt... very... relaxed...

 

His arms drooped, and his eyes sagged. Then he shook himself and said, "Wait a minute! Is this a Jedi mind trick?"

 

Menaya Kull winced. "How???" The boy had regained his lost energy in what she saw as a large flash of florescent green light. He was regenerating it at an alarming rate! She smiled. He was a living spring of energy! Now she had to capture him. Ending the portal enchantment, she simply pointed a hand at Peragrine and said, "Yirdmoi"

 

Immediately, Perry's eyes flickered...

 

And he dreamt.

a month ago - last edited a month ago by LEGOModerationTeam

Foul, muddy liquid dripped from the stone ceiling, falling on Lady Charlotte's lap. She was sitting in a dark cell, her hands chained to the wall behind her. She had been given a scratchy, plain dress, and her gray-streaked hair hun around her head in a tangled shroud. There was the steady sound of feet on stone stairs as someone descended into the toxic-smelling darkness. Lord Vladek appeared from the stairwell, a triumphant swagger in his steps. He came and leaned against the bars of the Lady's cell.

 

"Welcome to the stronghold of the King, Miss Charlotte," he taunted her. She looked up at him, unflinching even as she recognized her sister's destroyer.

 

"Strong enough words do not exist," she said with a chilling lack of emotion, "that I can say that could express the hatred I have for you. I will not waste my breath on it."

 

Vladek smiled meanly.

 

"Must be hard for you. That was a very clever scheme you had going there." He sneered, "Yet your plan failed, Charlotte, and we smashed the fugitives you were hiding."

 

She watched him evenly. "Not all of them," she objected coolly. "I heard several got away from your supposed Super Sentinel." He eyes narrowed slightly. "See? You can't smash everyone, Vladek."

 

"I can smash you," he replied chillingly. He stroked his chin and looked at her sitting in the cold chains, as if considering it. "But even now you don't understand me. I don't live to smash everyone, like you think. Though if I did not have more important things to do, I would easily hunt and destroy all the fools who fled Lord Brocktree." He grinned maliciously. "As easily as I destroyed your sister."

 

Lady Charlotte tightened her jaw and steadily held Vladek's gaze. When she offered no retort, the dark knight laughed and spun on his heels, striding out of the dungeon haughtily. When he was gone Charlotte's eyes softened painfully. She exhaled, and let her head hang back down.
