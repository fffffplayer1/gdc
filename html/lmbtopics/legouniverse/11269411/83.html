39


  Sir Talmid watched the sun set as he made his way to a mountaintop pass.  Along the way he found Ember, who joined him at his side.  They walked in silence, but the knight's mind was active - he was playing with time, an amount of which he did not know if he had.  He hoped he had enough time for one, final, plan.


  When they reached the mountaintop, a small, open mesa overlooking the Athelas Mounts, with the great plain of Aquila in the northern horizon and a hint of Morcia to the south, Sir Talmid had his plan sorted out, and he turned to Ember.  He knelt to her height and put his hands on her shoulders assuringly.


  "The gist of this is, we're turning ourselves over to thedude." Sir Talmid told her.  "But I shant forsake you.  So, I need you to create an illusion of yourself.  Create the illusion of that purple smoke, as well, rising around the mountain.  Put the illusion of yourself up here, next to me, and keep it up for as long as you can.  You can do it."


  The girl did not stir, but as Sir Talmid watched, a field of the disabling gas, an illusion of it, appeared behind her.  He turned to watch her handiwork.  It surrounded the mountaintop, fading at the higher altitudes, but creeping over the rocks as well, across the ground and around their feet.


  And then, when Sir Talmid turned back to Ember and his hands, that rested on air, dropped to his sides, he realized that for all of this time he had been staring at an illusion as well.

39 part 2

 

  Sir Talmid turned around to face Ember, who he had sensed behind him.  Instead of Ember, he found himself facing a young woman standing at the edge of the mesa.  Her attire was simple and practical, providing good coverage for an all-weather traveler, with many pockets and pouches.  She had short, cropped, practical-lengthed hair, golden in color, but her face was of nobody he ever recognized.  She was about as he was, taller and older than Ember had appeared.  But of course, this was Ember as well.


  "So this is your true appearance." Sir Talmid said.  Ember nodded, and for the first time Sir Talmid heard her speak.


  "I have been searching for you, Killian, for a long time." she said, addressing him by his first name.


  Sir Talmid's eyebrows raised.  "And who told you that?  I'm well known in these parts, but only as 'Sir Talmid'."


  "We have mutual friends." Ember said.  "And that's why I'm here, to deliver a message from one."


  "Well," Sir Talmid said, "you've certainly waited awhile to mention it."


  "I believe events are meant have certain outcomes," Ember explained.  "I've had to wait for the right time, when my actions will not affect what will come to pass."


  "...And that is?" Sir Talmid asked.


  Ember smiled.  "That's for you to find out.  But I think you already know it."


  Sir Talmid threw his arms in air and sighed.  If what Ember just told him was true, and it was, then she had not been, nor would ever be any help at all.  "And what of your family in Morcia?" he asked.  "They're an illusion as well, I imagine."


  "They were real," she said, "but they were not mine, and are as clueless as you have been.  I should be well forgotten by now."

 

  Sir Talmid could think of nothing else he wanted to get from this conversation.  He turned away and stared over the edge of the mountain, upset, in thought.
