Chapter 1:
DATE: 4207, THREE YEARS AFTER THE BATTLE OF NEXUS TOWER

Duke Exeter breathed in the cold, thin air of Crux Prime. He immediately heaved it back out as a sigh.
Three years- That was how long ago it had been since the Maelstrom had been defeated. He had missed the fight. It wasn’t that the past few years had been bad. The pirate vs. ninja conflict had been resolved. Duke had been getting to know Vanda. Imagination, its full power restored, was once again bringing creations to life(under the supervision of the Mythrans). There had even been certain thrills- Who could forget the heist at the U-coin mint, or the time that one minifigure for some reason imagined a giant beetle too close to Imagination Nexus?

But nothing compared to the battles the Maelstrom had brought.

He occasionally went out for a walk on Crux Prime. It reminded him of the battles that had been fought. Even after three years, Crux’s surface had remained relatively unchanged. That was just the way Duke had wanted it to be. He walked over to the edge of Aura Mar. Stepping onto the bridge that had been built a couple of years ago, he began to cross to the other side, the one that had simply been named The Temple. The reason for that was that this was the location of the Temple of the First Builders, or as was referred to by the natives, the Nexus Ka.
This place also brought back nostalgia. Thirteen years ago, he, Albert, Hael, and Typhonus had first descended upon Crux. The temple back then was a glorious sight. But now…
The glow of the Imaginite walls had faded to a dull, marbly white. Pock marks from incoming space debris had peppered the temple. The most obvious damage was the three massive cracks etching their way across the floor. They all protruded from the same opening Imagination beamed up through, a sad reminder of the loss of one of his friends. Yet it also reminded him of the beginning of his their adventures. He sighed as he ran his fingers over the dented walls. There had been repair efforts by the Assembly, but the Mythrans would rather have had kept it as it was. This was a sacred site to them; they didn’t want anyone, not even their most trusted, tampering with it.
Suddenly Duke caught a bright orange glow out of the corner of his eye. He was sure it hadn’t been there a few seconds ago. Drawing his sword, he cautiously moved towards the light. He didn’t like mysterious glowing objects. He turned the corner and found a swirling, orange and red portal. The second he recognized what it was, a black shape tumbled out of it, and the portal disappeared. It landed as a black mass of cloth on the porous floor. The figure seemed to rest a few seconds, then heaved itself up with a staff. The staff was like twisted iron, crude and crooked. Mounted on top of it was a flaming, orange orb. Duke realized even from behind it was some sort of person as it raised itself up, moving as if it was recovering from an intense duel. “Sifa rae, Riovix.” The person uttered. “Sifa rae.” His voice sounded hoarse and choked. Duke gathered enough courage to speak to him, “Um, excuse me, sir? Do you need-“
The figure snapped back up to its proper stature- About six mini-feet –and spun around to face him. Duke felt his heart skip a beat.
