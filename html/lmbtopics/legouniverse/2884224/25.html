Chapter 9 part 2:

Bob marched through the dense jungle of Gnarled Forest, Golden Hammer in one hand, and a sword which he used to cut through the undergrowth in the other. Keep strong, Riovix, He assured himself as he swept his way through some bushes. If you could do this once, you can do it again.

The smell of sulfur reached his nostrils. He assumed he was getting close. In fact, it only took him a couple more hacks to clear his way to Brig Rock- The base of The Hooded One.

Large towers strewn about the clearing billowed smoke, which Bob assumed produced the sulfuric smell he had smelt earlier. Tents covered the jungle floor, while more stations were suspended by the surrounding trees and the smokestacks. Mactarus’ army moved hastily about, bringing in felled trees and discarded aircraft. “Mactarus,” Riovix breathed, “What have you done?”

After cautiously looking around, Riovix snuck his way into the camp. The Toshik seemed to be around every corner. Nevertheless, he managed to reach the center of the camp, which was where he figured Mactarus was.

In the middle of it all was a tent, much larger than the others. Grey flags flittered through the air, marked with the Urah- the Mythran symbol of darkness and corruption, as well as the Toshik’s trademark. Two large stone golems stood watch at the entrance. Perhaps if you made your way around the back, Thought Riovix as he slunk his way around, you might be able to get past the guards, slip in from underneath the tent, and talk with Mactarus. Quietly he snuck his way to the back, but only halfway around, he ran into a massive Burn Beetle, carrying a load of logs within its three mandibles. The monster dropped its cargo and shrieked. Riovix heard the two golems shout and crash into each other in panic and confusion. Riovix drew his hammer and with a few quick whacks felled the beast.

 

But that would be the least of his problems.

This is more of a continuation to Chapter 9. So... Yeah. On with the chapter.

 

Chapter 9 part 3:

Eventually the Golems caught up, clenching their massive fists. A trumpet sounded the alarm as Bob crushed one Golem’s chin with his hammer. The other attempted to catch him up like a firefly, but he counterattacked by pulling the Golem down by his arm. An angry yelling resounded from the side of the tent, and just moments later a mob of evil-looking minifigure-like figures rounded the corner.

They had large orange eyes and skin so pale it looked white. Many of them had whitish-grey facial hair. One had a long, braided beard and a pirate captain’s hat and uniform. In his hand was a long, grey sword. He yelled something in the ancient Mythran language. The minifigures charged. Bob had dropped the Golem’s arm while he was looking at these eerily familiar characters, and now he had cornered Bob. The minifigures grinned, considering their prey helpless. Bob braced for impact.

“SI!!!” Yelled a hoarse voice. (“Si” was the Mythran word for “Stop.”) Bob cracked open his eyelids and saw Mactarus himself, standing to the side of the crowd. He shouted some more words of rebuke, and the party slunk away. The Hooded One looked at Bob. “Riovix?”

“Mactarus,” Said Bob, slipping his hammer behind his back. Mactarus nodded towards his tent. “Come. We’ll talk privately.”
