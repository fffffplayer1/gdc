Chapter 2:
The figure’s body was a skeleton. A black-as-night skeleton.

He was draped in a black hood and cape. His flaming eyes were the only thing that showed from the shadow of his hood. The figure scrutinized Duke. “Who are you?” He demanded. “What are you doing here?” Duke swallowed. “I-I’m Duke Exeter, leader of the… Sentinels and the Nexus Force.” He drew his sword. “Now, who- or should I say what –are you?” The figure answered, “That is of no business of yours. What are you doing here? How did you get here?” He pointed his staff at Duke. The sulfuric stench was like none he had smelled before. Nevertheless, the threat of the staff made him bolder. “Why don’t you go check your calendar?” He said sarcastically, doubting the figure even had one. “If you really don’t know, you’re in the year 4207. And just to let you know,”-At this point Duke raised his sword-“Nobody threatens Duke Exeter.” Apparently the figure didn’t like being scolded. “If you knew who I was, minifigure…” He paused. “The Nexus. Where is the Nexus?!” Duke pointed out towards Nexus Tower. The mysterious minifigure turned the direction of Duke’s finger. “You… You have the Nexus?” He whispered hoarsely. Duke nodded. “Yes, we do. Why do you want to-“ He stopped as the figure slowly turned towards him. “My kin… They gave you minifigures our heritage?!” He said angrily.

“Heritage?” Said Duke. “Wait… You can’t possibly mean…”

The figure swung his staff at Duke, humming as it sailed through the air. “The fools!!” He screeched. “They trusted YOU with the Nexus?! Where are they? What are they doing?!” He swung his staff again, but this time Duke blocked it with his sword. “I spent ten years of plotting… Barely escape the fools with my life… And I travel to the future, for a second try… And what do I find?... A simple minifigure, claiming the Nexus is his?!” The infuriated figure kicked Duke’s sword out of his hand. Before the leader of the Sentinels had time to react, the figure had him by the throat, suspending him in the air. “And by the way, Exeter, I don’t think I recall answering your question, ‘who am I’.” He squeezed Duke’s throat harder. He was beginning to feel dizzy. The figure answered, “I, minifigure, am Mactarus… though you might know me better as… The Hooded One.”

Duke blacked out.


Not the best, but a chapter nonetheless.

Chapter 3 part 1:
“Hael, are you sure he’s not…Not… D… D…” The voice was concerned. Duke, just coming out of unconsciousness, thought he recognized it.
“Blimey, quit yer worryin’! He’s only passed out!” Came the voice’s response. As Duke’s vision cleared, he was able to recognize the source of the voices as Overbuild and Hael. “See, he’s coming out of it already,” Said the pirate, gesturing towards him. “Oh, thank the Mythrans!!” Cried Overbuild in relief. Hael ignored him as he walked over to Duke. “What happened to yeh? We found yeh lyin’ unconscious over by them ruins!” At that moment, Vanda walked in. “Is he awake- Oh. Never mind.” She said. “I knew he shouldn’t have gone out by himself!” Fretted Overbuild. “There’s robbers and bounty hunters of all kinds about! The least he can do is get some bodyguards for himself!” Vanda sat on the opposite side of the bed Hael was sitting on. “What happened?” She asked. Duke felt his throat. “I… I was ambushed,” He said.
“Ambushed!!” Cried Overbuild. He looked like HE was going to pass out. “Who did it?!” Demanded Hael, leaping off his bed in fury. Duke was feeling well enough now to get off the bed himself. “It was a hooded… Thing,” Duke responded. He wasn’t sure what to make of the character he saw last night. “A- A black skeleton. That was what he was. Flaming eyes. Oh, and he also had a staff.” Overbuild was becoming more and more worried by the second. “Black skeletons with flaming eyes, knocking minifigures unconscious! What is Nimbus System coming to?!” Duke continued, “He told me his name, but I passed out just as he said it.” He turned towards the other Faction Leaders. Vanda was concerned, and yet excited –Duke saw it in her eyes. She always enjoyed a challenge, especially since the Maelstrom had been eradicated. Hael was furious that someone, or rather something, had dared laid a hand on his long-time friend and companion, and Overbuild was pacing around the room as if the universe was going to end. “I think we’ll need to have a meeting.” Duke announced.

Chapter 3 part 2:
They met two hours later in the Conference Room of Nexus Tower. Aside from Duke, Vanda, Hael and Overbuild, several other figures had been summoned.
Beck Strongheart, Master Fong, Captain Jack Knife and Rusty Steele stood to represent the faction commandoes. Beck had a brilliantly quick mind, and the best soldier Duke had in his infantry. Master Fong, Vanda’s foster father, was wise and quick to understanding. Jack rivaled even Hael at the sword, and Rusty was always at the head of all of Overbuild’s construction efforts. Along with them were several other famous names, including Shival, the chief of the Mythrans, himself, and Bob, who always tagged with him. Duke saw fit to begin as he stood up. “Attention everyone… Good. Now, first off I’d like to thank you all for coming here-“
“Get tah the point already!!” Hollered Jack impatiently. Duke looked at him irritably. “I’m getting to it.” Duke felt like Jack was a constant bother every time he was near him. “Anyway, naturally I’ve assembled you all for a reason. Me, Vanda, Hael and Overbuild all believe that a new force of evil is beginning to rise before us.” Murmurs of whispered conversation filled the room. “We came to this possibility just two hours before, after I recovered my consciousness after being ambushed last-“
The Conference Room went off like a bomb. “Yah brought us here ‘cuz yeh were attacked?!” Shouted Jack in annoyance. Beck stood up in fury. “You mean to tell me, sir, that somebody dared to ambush you?! Tell me who he was; By Nexus, I’ll give him what he deserves…!” Fong went into worrying, and Vanda had to assure him he was OK. Rusty absently pulled an unfinished blueprint from his Backpack to work on, and Shival and Bob merely glanced around awkwardly. The rest of the room was ablaze, and Hael had to fire his square gun to get them all to quiet down, unfortunately shattering a light fixture doing so. “Thank you, Hael. Anyway, the description: Hood and cape, skeletal body, all black. Iron staff, with an orange orb on the end, flaming eyes. Said his name was… Mactarus? Yes, Mactaurus. That was it.” Suddenly Vanda spoke up. “Duke, I thought you said you couldn’t remember his name.” Duke turned towards her, “Well, you see, I just now remembered. He said it just as I fell un-“ Suddenly he found Shival by his side. He didn’t exactly like how Mythrans got around like that, being there one moment and here the next. But Shival’s face had urgency written all over it.
He had decided to assume his minifigure form for this occasion. His gray hair had been pulled back and braided behind his creased face. He wore an Imagination Blue cape around his neck, as well as an Imagination Blue everything else. “Exeter,” He began, “Did you say the figure’s name was… Mactarus?” His voice was as concerned as his face. “Well, yes, that was his name… Is it of concern?” Shival nodded. “Grave concern.” The room fell into an uneasy silence. Duke happened to glance at Bob. His smile hadn’t just faltered; It had flat-out turned upside down. Shival spoke again, “We knew this… Mactarus.” He paused. “And no one knew him better than Bob.” Bob stood up. “We know you’re confused, and we understand why. There’s something you don’t know about me. My real name isn’t Bob- It’s Riovix. And I’m a Mythran.”
