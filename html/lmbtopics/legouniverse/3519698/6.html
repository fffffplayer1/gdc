Chapter 8

Bailey stirs uneasily in her sleep. Although she wishes she could rest some more, something is commanding her to wake.

The puppy eases her head out from under Cesca, and stands to her full height, which isn’t all that tall. She yawns once, but continues with an uncontrollable second one. Night has captured the sky, holding it as its prisoner until the sun returns to save it. The moon hangs suspended in air, dauntingly above. The stars must have taken a bite or two out of it, because a whole section is missing, and now it’s just a crescent. However, it provides barely enough light for Bailey to see sufficiently.

She sits down and wonders what could have woke her up. Truthfully it could have just been the grass ruffling for all she knew.

Just then, as if on some strange cue, the plants wildly rustle in front of Bailey, but in no other spot. She flattens her ears and stands up again. The waving plants part, and a bizarre body strides out of the trail.

It’s a dog, no doubt about it. Its long almond eyes stare at Bailey, each slightly slanting down his face. His coat is rough and short, reflecting the moons light with golden waves. The fur is patchy, the look of an older dog, resulting in sections of white fur.

He takes a step forward, towering high above Bailey. He must be at least twice the size. His back legs and neck are muscular, but the rest of him seems skinny and frail.

Bailey springs forwards, tail flying back and forth. This is a stranger, but any dog is a friend. The puppy barks once trying to convince the dog to play.

This dog doesn’t want to play.

He stares angrily at the playful wiggly pup, and starts to growl.

Chapter 9

Bailey stops in her tracks, three legs already bounding forward. She teeters on one leg for a second then, like a cartoon, she tips over to the ground. She pulls herself up, never taking her eyes off of the rather tall, irritated creature.

“Why would you do that?” Bailey barks, lowering her tail and bringing her ears forward. She slightly cocks her head to add the finishing touch. She then softens her eyes, her most innocent look.

The dog takes a step forward, the scarce muscles he has jerking unevenly under his skin. He ponders for a moment before deciding to ignore this imprudent question. He slowly takes another stride forward, bringing his paw way up before going to take that step.

Bailey scampers backwards, tail cowardly tucked between her legs. She quivers at the sight. The golden hound starts to up its appearance. He raises his lips, letting white teeth shine nastily at Bailey.

Bailey whines, which wakes Cesca.

The girl slowly sits up, yawning as she does. “Bailey?” She mutters, wiping her eyes with her fists like a little kid. She then sees the hound, his stance bent over, like he is about to bound towards them. “Hi, doggie,” She coos, trying to calm him, “Stay right there.” Her face pales as he advances, leaping into a full on dash.

Cesca turns, blindly running away from the hound. Bailey starts to follow, but she knows she is beat. The hound’s legs are long and thing, stretching way out underneath him as he runs. The puppy’s small, slightly uncoordinated legs won’t beat that.

The puppy trips, falling face first into the rough dirt. The dog starts to slow himself, but topples over Bailey and lands on the ground himself.

Chapter 8

Bailey stirs uneasily in her sleep. Although she wishes she could rest some more, something is commanding her to wake.

The puppy eases her head out from under Cesca, and stands to her full height, which isn’t all that tall. She yawns once, but continues with an uncontrollable second one. Night has captured the sky, holding it as its prisoner until the sun returns to save it. The moon hangs suspended in air, dauntingly above. The stars must have taken a bite or two out of it, because a whole section is missing, and now it’s just a crescent. However, it provides barely enough light for Bailey to see sufficiently.

She sits down and wonders what could have woke her up. Truthfully it could have just been the grass ruffling for all she knew.

Just then, as if on some strange cue, the plants wildly rustle in front of Bailey, but in no other spot. She flattens her ears and stands up again. The waving plants part, and a bizarre body strides out of the trail.

It’s a dog, no doubt about it. Its long almond eyes stare at Bailey, each slightly slanting down his face. His coat is rough and short, reflecting the moons light with golden waves. The fur is patchy, the look of an older dog, resulting in sections of white fur.

He takes a step forward, towering high above Bailey. He must be at least twice the size. His back legs and neck are muscular, but the rest of him seems skinny and frail.

Bailey springs forwards, tail flying back and forth. This is a stranger, but any dog is a friend. The puppy barks once trying to convince the dog to play.

This dog doesn’t want to play.

He stares angrily at the playful wiggly pup, and starts to growl.

Chapter 9

Bailey stops in her tracks, three legs already bounding forward. She teeters on one leg for a second then, like a cartoon, she tips over to the ground. She pulls herself up, never taking her eyes off of the rather tall, irritated creature.

“Why would you do that?” Bailey barks, lowering her tail and bringing her ears forward. She slightly cocks her head to add the finishing touch. She then softens her eyes, her most innocent look.

The dog takes a step forward, the scarce muscles he has jerking unevenly under his skin. He ponders for a moment before deciding to ignore this imprudent question. He slowly takes another stride forward, bringing his paw way up before going to take that step.

Bailey scampers backwards, tail cowardly tucked between her legs. She quivers at the sight. The golden hound starts to up its appearance. He raises his lips, letting white teeth shine nastily at Bailey.

Bailey whines, which wakes Cesca.

The girl slowly sits up, yawning as she does. “Bailey?” She mutters, wiping her eyes with her fists like a little kid. She then sees the hound, his stance bent over, like he is about to bound towards them. “Hi, doggie,” She coos, trying to calm him, “Stay right there.” Her face pales as he advances, leaping into a full on dash.

Cesca turns, blindly running away from the hound. Bailey starts to follow, but she knows she is beat. The hound’s legs are long and thing, stretching way out underneath him as he runs. The puppy’s small, slightly uncoordinated legs won’t beat that.

The puppy trips, falling face first into the rough dirt. The dog starts to slow himself, but topples over Bailey and lands on the ground himself.
