Chapter 5

Cesca gulps loudly next to Bailey, who is panting heavily under the hot bed. No one has ever heard Emma scream so crossly, it was daunting, and terrifying.

Bailey whines noisily, someone is yelling back at Emma. She can’t understand any of it, but it sure isn’t good. The puppy knows she is supposed to stay there but….

Bailey squirms out from under the bed, and dashes out of the room, brushing past the nightlight and continuing to the front door. The man standing at the door is leaning heavily against the bricks on the wall of the porch. He is wearing some sort of black robes. Most importantly, he is menacingly pointing a fuming finger at Emma.

Bailey’s fur immediately starts to rise on her neck and shoulders, followed by her entire back, and all the way down her tail. It’s a threatening sight, until she starts to growl. Then it’s a plain nasty sight.

Or so Bailey thought.

The man laughs cruelly, and this time points his finger at Bailey, the puppy who doesn’t realize her growl isn’t intimidating. It’s cute and weak.

He then ignores the snarling pooch, and turns back to Emma. “You lied,” He says accusingly and chuckles deeply.

Just then Cesca rounds the corner, her face pale, staring straight into the eyes of the man. She scowls and marches over to the cloaked figure. “We didn’t do anything.” She then knocks the pointing finger away from her mother.
“You doubly lied.” The man declares, curling his hands into fists.

Suddenly Emma is a blur, hands whirling out the sides as she lunges forward. She slams into the guy, knocking him down, but doubling over with him. “Run! Cesca, go! Bailey…keep her safe.”

Cesca pauses, but Bailey knocks her forward, only taking instinct on the few words she understood. The girl follows, Bailey trots down the street outside their house, looking back. The man has a hold of Emma. But the woman waves them on, tears brimming in her eyes. She blinks them away.
