Brocktree's thoughts soared with his rocket. He pondered all that had happened recently.

<br/>The death of his friends, Shival's story... it was almost too much to comprehend. Suddenly, his thoughts turned to the... thing he had tripped over. He unzipped his backpack, and quickly pulled it out. His first impression was dust. Literally. The oblong device was COVERED in the stuff. He quickly brushed it all off, and held the device in front of him.

<br/>

<br/>It looked irreplaceable. From top to bottom, it had thousands of miniature glowing blue wires, twisting and turning as they strove for existence. However, there was only one button. Large, with a green sheen to it, it's very presence spoke silent words:

<br/>"PPPPUUUUSSSSHHHH MMMMEEEE!"

<br/>

<br/>Brocktree always liked pushing buttons. Such a fascinating experience. There was always the possibility that a bomb would go off, or set off an alarm, but... they were buttons! Who cared?

<br/>

<br/>So, without a moment's hesitiation, he pressed the large button.

<br/>

<br/>Instantly, something that looked like a round window opened up in front of him. Where before there was massive space, with a smattering of stars, now there was a glimpse; a glimpse into another world. It was thickly wooded, with several mountain ranges shooting into the sky. It looked like it was... an island. A sea stretched out for miles and miles around. And he thought he saw things that looked like.... ants moving about on the coast. But he could see nothing more. In an instant, his rocket had moved through the whole, and was plummeting down towards the coast. Brocktree groaned. Of all the times to be out of gas.

<br/>

<br/>*******************************************

<br/>

<br/>5 years prior

<br/>

<br/>The lone minifigure was instantly teleported to the other world. All of the other minifigures were milling about, trying to find out where they were.

<br/>

<br/>They were on an island. An island on a world that had never even been dreamed of in any of the minifigures wildest imaginations.

<br/>

<br/>*******************************************

<br/>

<br/>5 years later

<br/>

<br/>The same minifigure watched as what looked like a hole in the sky opened up, and a flaming object fly through it.
<br/>Brocktree and I have something in common.....we both love pushing buttons!LOL,Waros! I love this!
