"Did we have to go this way?" asked Luke, blowing a cobweb out of his face.

They were crawling through a small tunnel that Brocktree had found on the blue screen. It was extremely cramped, and none of them were enjoying it, not to mention absolutely filthy. They were also exhausted because of two reasons: it's hard to crawl on your hands and knees for 30 minutes, and the entire thing was sloping upwards.

Finally, they turned a corner and popped into a much larger hallway, and Brocktree turned around. The other Minithrans stopped, and looked at him.
"Guys, I think we're almost there. However, I'm starting to get suspicious. This has been too easy ("He calls crawling for 30 minutes easy?" Rover muttered), and I'm starting to suspect that Shival let us escape back there."

"Why do you think that?" responded Selia.

"We were unconscious for 5 years. That gave Shival plenty of time to find out everything about this tower, and about us. He probably knows about these tunnels, and he knows that someone could get through them. Why aren't they guarded? Look, the point is, be on your guard. If we're expecting something, then we'll be ready for it when it comes."

He turned around, and headed up (it was still sloping up) the hall.

They roved through passageways for what seemed like an eternity, and with each step they took, Brocktree got more and more nervous. Why wasn't anything happening? As his nerves grew shaky, so did his guard, which is why he didn't see a small, nearly invisible wire stretched across the end of another passageway. Brocktree had gotten a little ahead of the group, and he tripped on the wire. Instantly, he heard a strange clicking sound.

"JUMP, JUMP!" He screamed at the others.

It was too late. Before Brocktree's eyes, the entire floor collapsed beneath the rest of the group. They fell, and he lost sight of them before they hit bottom

‎05-09-2012 02:42 PM

Brocktree let out a scream of pure despair. They were smashed.
Brocktree's horror transformed to something different.
Anger.
Anger towards Shival.
Anger towards the Mythrans.
He was going to make them pay.
If it was the last thing he did.

Brocktree stood up and clenched his fists. He knew Shival was nearby. He was going to find him.

He drew Verminbane... and began to walk. Brocktree made his way through the Tower, constantly going upwards. A couple hours later, he arrived at a metal grate on the floor beneath him. He peered beneath him, and seeing that no one was their, he quietly slipped through it. Brocktree landed lightly on his feet, and began looking around for an official looking door. At last, he spotted it: a tall, imposing looking pair of iron doors, with an advanced lock system on the front. Brocktree snorted. Like that was supposed to keep out intruders? Well, it might. But it wasn't going to keep out him.

He thrust Verminbane deep into the iron, and slowly carved a circle in it. He completed the shape, and kicked the now separate piece of metal into the opposite room. He followed it in.

Brocktree was within a massive hallway, filled with pillars of all shapes and sizes. A throne sat in the middle of it, facing the other direction. He grabbed it's edge, turned it to face him... and saw nothing. A deep, throaty laugh rang out, resounding around the room. Brocktree twirled around, but he couldn't see anyone. A second laugh, louder this time, rolled out. It came from the far end of the hallway, which was clothed in shadows. A single form stepped out of it....
Shival.

"Well well Brocktree, I see you finally made it. Do you like my holographic throne room?" He pressed a button, and the majestic pillars disappeared. He pressed it again, and the seemingly massive room shrunk to normal size, with Shival strangely much closer.

"Shival!" Brocktree gritted his teeth.

"Hmm? Oh I see. Are you angry that your friends are smashed? Why, whatever for?"
Shival smiled cruelly.

Brocktree roared in anger, and charged at Shival, rage clouding his judgement. Shival calmly sidestepped, and neatly tripped him. Brocktree scrambled to his feet, and held Verminbane at the ready.
Shival rolled his eyes.

"Oh, very droll. Take out the big bad sword and make the bad guy scared, is that it? Well two can play at that game."

Shival withdrew a glimmering steel longsword from a sheath hidden behind his back. He pointed it at Brocktree.
"En garde!"

Brocktree's eyes narrowed, and he and Shival began to circle, each looking for an opportunity to strike. Suddenly, Brocktree came at him. He twirled Verminbane over his head, and brought it sweeping downwards, in a blow that would have cleaved Shival in two. Shival's sword came quickly up, and blocked, knocking Brocktree back. Shival saw his chance, and began to batter the stunned Minithran with blows, all of which Brocktree barely avoided. He recovered his senses, and noticed that while attacking him, Shival left his right side uncovered. In between the blows, Brocktree swung his sword in a curving arc, and Shival barely blocked it. Now it was Brocktree's turn for the offensive. Using the superior strength and size of Verminbane, he hammered at Shival using sweeping blows. Seething with rage at the heartless murderer, he began to bellow at him.

"SHIVAL!" He brought his sword in a downward curve, but was stopped by the longsword.

"YOU!" He thrust his sword forward, and Shival jumped back only just in time.

"ARE!" The sword came at Shival from all sides

"GOING!" Shival began to sweat in fear.

"TO!" Brocktree's face was contorted in rage.

"PAY!!!!!!!!!" He brought the sword in an upward arc, knocking Shival's weapon far beyond his grasp. He kicked Shival into the ground, and held Verminbane at his throat.

"Before I decide what to do with you Shival, just tell me one thing. Why."

"You mean why did I smash the Minifigures? Why did I smash your friends?" spat out Shival.

"Yes. Why."

"Because it was the only way!"

"What only way?"

"The only way to get my revenge!!!! I was just a small child. It was my 4th birthday and my parents and I were celebrating together, when suddenly there was a large crash from the front of the house. I remember it like it was yesterday. My father's face paled, and he thrust me under the couch, and told me to stay there. I was an obedient child, and I did as told.
I heard heavy boots come treading in, and I heard two thuds. Then the front door slammed shut, and I came crawling out.
My parents were gone. I made my way to the window, and saw 4 Minifigures walking away, dragging my parents behind them. Tied up. That day I vowed revenge. Even at that young age, I knew where they were going. To the Mythran Mines! And you want to know something Brocktree?" He said the name with contempt. "Those Minifigures were the future fathers of each Faction Leader!"

Suddenly everything Shival had done became clear. A sweet, innocent boy, twisted by the foolish actions of four Minifigures.To be part of a race that later would be honored by his most hated enemies. To be so hurt within by want of revenge that he was willing to smash an entire species.

Brocktree wanted nothing more than to smash Shival. This person had smashed innocents. This person had smashed his friends. But even as he fingered his sword, a stronger emotion welled up in him.
Pity.
Pity for this poor creature.
He sheathed his sword.

"Tell me something Shival."

Shival stared up at Brocktree.

"Do you feel like you have accomplished your revenge? Do you feel like it was all worth it? Do you feel like you can finally rest?"

"No." whispered Shival.

"Then stop Shival! It's too late to bring back the Minifigures. But end your reign of tyranny!"

Shival was about to respond when a whistling sound pierced the air.
A bright red dart was suddenly in Shival's arm. He tried to speak once more. "Beware of...." and then he fell.
Shival was smashed.

Brocktree searched around the room, trying to find the one who had blown the dart. He fled the room as another whistling sound suddenly went out, and a dart clattered on the floor where he had just been standing. He made it back into the metal grate when an electronic voice sounded out:

"ATTENTION ALL PERSONNEL! NEXUS TOWER HAS BEEN SET TO SELF DESTRUCT. EVACUATE IMMEDIATELY. THIS IS NOT A DRILL."

Brocktree raced through the tunnels, but he knew he wouldn't make it in time. Then, suddenly, he felt the strange red sensation come over him. Instantly, time slowed down, and he sped up. In no time at all, he had reached his rocket and was pulling away from the tower. He noticed several transports full of Mythrans fleeing as well. He was well away from the tower when an ear-shattering boom sounded out behind him. And then Brocktree knew that Nexus Tower, the last icon of the Nexus Force and the life left behind him, had exploded.
