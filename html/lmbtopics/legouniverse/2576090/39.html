‎10-02-2012 03:58 AM

They were crouched underneath a giant tree/plant, looking at the entrance to a cave. After Grandrayda's revelation, the minifigures began to act out her plan.

The cave was massive, but there was a small hole at the top, and peering down into it you could just see a single shard of Zigantium near the back, but could see nothing behind it. The cave's mouth was large and gaping, and a steady stream of the strange beasts entered and left it.

So Brocktree, Grandrayda and James were preparing.

Suddenly, a strange bird call trilled through the air.

"That's the signal," whispered James. He then stood up, and placed a large horn to his mouth. He blew mightily into it, and a resounding boom echoed amongst the trees. The animals stopped, staring up at James, as he blew repeatedly into the horn.

And then a dozen Minifigure Rockets crashed into the roof of the cave (with no pilots).

A massive explosion literally lifted off the cave roof, and debris rained down into the horde of animals.

They were stunned for a brief time, before roaring out in rage and charging at James and the others.

James stood atop the hill, with Brocktree and Grandrayda crouched beside him. He whispered to himself.

"Just a second more...."

The beasts were almost upon them when suddenly, 50 Minifigures broke cover from beneath the trees and started blasting the beasts with spare guns. Summoners created gigantic, technicolored monsters with huge claws that tore into them. Shinobi's lured the animals close to them before vanishing in a puff of smoke. A few seconds later, flashing katanas reappeared behind the animals. Venture Leaguers laid into the creatures with flare-guns, scimitars, and whips. The Sentinels shielded the others from the attacks of the beasts. A mass slaughter occured. The beasts dropped like flies, and due to their teamwork, the Minifigures suffered few casualties. But they were vastly outnumbered. The beasts continued to pour out of the cave, and the Minifigures were few. Brocktree, Grandrayda and James had to move fast.

They ran about to the top of the cave, and stood above the small opening. Grandrayda withdrew a.... a time bomb?!?!?!

"I thought this was going to be stealthy!" hissed Brocktree.

"Don't sweat it buddy. These big monkeys have brains so small, they can only focus on one thing at a time. At the moment, they're focused on the confusing problem of their prey attacking them," she said with a wink.

She planted the bomb, and they hastened backwards a few feet.

Thankfully, the explosion was highly muffled. Some of the beasts still in the cave noticed a small amount of debris raining down, and some noticed the light at the cave's top was bigger, but Grandrayda had been right; they didn't care.

Grandrayda handed a pair of knives to both of them, and said,

"We'll have to crawl down the wall. These will help."

And then, they started down.

Inch by inch, they labored down the cave wall, stabbing their knives into clefts in the rock, careful not to let the beasts hear or see them, and then pushing them in to lower holds.

Brocktree landed softly on his feet at the bottom of the cave. They were behind the crowded mass of the beasts, and slowly tip-toed to the pedestal the Zigantium was mounted on. But.... how had the beasts made it's resting place so smooth? He shrugged off the feelings of doubt that suddenly arose in his mind. Moving forward, he lifted the Zigantium, and stuffed it into his backpack. A light blue glow surrounded him. James jumped, but then settled. For a moment, he had looked like.... a Mythran. But then, the blue glow faded, and Brocktree was left without it.

James beckoned to Brocktree. They were about to make their way out of the cave,when they heard a peculiar grating sound. They slowly turned, and saw that the Zigantium's resting place had started to rise. And then it reached it's peak. The grating noise stopped, and Brocktree noticed that all of the beasts had turned, and were staring at them.

The beasts turned as one, and fled out of the crowded cave.

In a few seconds, they were gone.

Outside, the fighting had been almost resolved. The massive numbers of the beasts were overpowering the Minifigures, and one by one they began to fall, when an ear piercing, mounstrous, keening wail tore the air. The advancing animals looked back at the cave entrance, and saw their fellow creatures fleeing. They sped away, leaving the Minifigures alone. A ragged cheer went up from the tired Minifigures, but Intrepid, who had been almost killed and was lieing in the dirt, sat up.

"What's happenining?" he whispered.

Inside the cave, Brocktree, Grandrayda, and James stared at the cave's rear.

The wall was rising, and slowly being revealed was a massive statue.

Four electrical spirals surrounded it, and suddenly they sent a massive force of electricity springing into the statue..

It groaned. And slowly, ever so slowly, a evil looking red light began to shine in it's eyes.

The statue stood up, and began to stomp towards the three Minifigures.

The Minifigures gathered around the cave's mouth, and stared inside.

The statue stopped, and stared at Brocktree.

And then a huge, shining laser erupted from it's mouth, slamming into James. He flew backwards, propelled by it's huge force. He lay against the wall, and a small moan escaped his lips.

"Grandrayda!" yelled Brocktree, "Take care of him!"

Grandrayda complied.

Brocktree faced the... thing, and drew his sword. Verminbane flashed in his hand.

Another laser sprang towards him, but Brocktree dodged it. He ran to the side of the statue, and prepared to cut into it with his sword, but it's gigantic stone hand crashed into his stomach. Brocktree doubled over, and groaned. The statue turned on him, and swung it's fist at him again. Brocktree avoided the punch, and managed to bring his sword into it's thick leg. It cut deep into it.

The statue looked at his leg, and then at Brocktree, who was panting heavily. And then, a rusty, grating voice came out of it.

"HEEAAAAALLLLLLL"

One of the electric spirals activated, and a bolt of electricity sliced into it's leg.

Brocktree gasped.

It was already healed.

"What.... how!?"

The statue suddenly leapt into the air, and came crashing down again. A shockwave ran through the ground, and knocked Brocktree over. It came stomping towards him.

A red, creeping mist made it's way into Brocktree's eyes. With incredible speed and strength, he leapt up, and brought his sword swinging towards the statue's head. But the statue's hand moved up through the air like a bolt of lightning, and he grabbed Brocktree by the waist, stopping him in mid-air. It brought Brocktree's head close to its mouth, and Brocktree stared as he saw the laser begin to form.

"No!" he shouted. It would not end like this.
He swung Verminbane over and over again at the creature's hand, until with a mighty slash (and just as the laser came out of it's mouth), it's hand separated from it's arm, and Brocktree fell to the floor.

And then it spoke again.

"HEEEEEAAAAAAALLLLLLLLL!"

And it's hand was suddenly reattached by the electricity.

Brocktree moaned, and the red mist fled from his eyes. He didn't think he could win this fight.

Epilogue
The machine tottered, and fell with a resounding thud.

It didn’t get up.

The Minifigures gathered around the lifeless body and cheered, before going to tend to their wounded. James, who had regained consciousness just in time to see Brocktree go flying, ran over to him, followed by Intrepid.

Brocktree lay against the side of the stone building. James could barely feel a pulse.
He was almost dead.

-----------------

The remote finally accepted the Zigantium replacement, and the button turned bright red.
James activated the portal, and once more it revealed Avant Gardens, but without flickering this time.

-----------------
