‎12-11-2012 03:57 AM

James let out a whoop, and hurried to get guards to help them.

 

But something that sounded like an explosion suddenly rocked the air.

 

James spun around, and stared up at the approaching rockets.

 

Out of nowhere, a black disc of a battleship hummed into view, blocking out the sun suddenly materialized; a weird humming came out of it. It was so massive it completely blocked out the sun as luminescent red light surrounded it in a fiery glow. It hovered around the miniscule rockets, and robotic arms whirred out of its base.

 

Each arm grabbed one of the rockets, and began to draw them into the body of the ominous ship. He heard a distant scream, and then the rockets were gone, and the holes that the arms had come out of clanged shut.

 

James ran to a launch pad, and put down his customized rocket. He sped into the air, disabled the auto-pilot, and zoomed toward the ship, on a direct-collision course. He neared the ship, when his rocket smashed into what seemed to be an invisible wall. His rocket hovered against it for a second before a massive burst of energy sent him surging back. He spun wildly out of control, ejecting from the rocket at the last second before it hit the ground in a fiery explosion. He fell to the dirt, rolled backwards and stared up at the ship. Something that sounded like a laugh echoed around the planet, and before his eyes the ship vanished.

 

Nimbus Station was as quiet as it had ever been as James stared at the sky.

Prologue

 

Brocktree opened his eyes wearily. His eyes swam around, trying to focus. He tilted his head to the right and saw his seven restored friends were lying on what looked like metal boxes.

 

A huge electric jolt rocked his body, and he arched his back in agony. He collapsed backwards, and felt himself hit metal.

 

A loud humming sound surrounded him, and he opened his eyes once more, taking in his surroundings. He was in a large room, with pipes twisting and turning all around it. Steam hissed out of vents in the floor, and scarlet red light illuminated the area.

 

A door slid open at the end, but before Brocktree could turn his head and look at it, another shock ran through his spine. He screamed in spite of himself, and he crashed back down. He stared up at the ceiling, when a transparent blue head looked down at him.

 

“S—“ Brocktree started to say, when the Mythran hit him so hard that he passed out once more.

Chapter 1

 

The Minifigures were preparing.

 

James had quickly gotten to work. He hadn’t seen the massive ship moving, but he had heard its humming sound lessening, but he knew what direction it had been going in.

 

But… it was going towards an uncharted area of Nimbus Space. Vessels that had gone there in the past had vanished mysteriously.

 

James didn’t care though. He organized the Minifigures, preparing them to head into the direction. The bustled around, preparing supplies. They weren’t going to try to keep Nimbus Station. Except for the few Minifigures stationed at Avant Gardens, everyone was leaving.

 

One by one, they set down their rockets and launched off. They disabled their auto-pilots, and hovered in flight formation. Joined by their comrades, the Minifigure entourage started to fly. They zoomed ahead, and Jamt said in a gruff voice, “It’s a trap!”

 

James looked at him quizzically, shook his head, sped to the head of the formation, and led the Minifigures away.

They hurtled forward into the starry sky.

 

(In case anyone is wondering, they went past Avant Gardens).
