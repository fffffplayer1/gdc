Prologue:

5 years earlier

Doctor Albert Overbuild's hands, which were normally extremely steady began to shake.
His hand was so unsteady that he had to try several times before he could find the strength to push the inter-com button.

"Get me Exeter. Now!"

*******************************************

5 minutes later, the four faction leaders stood staring at the inside of one of the Mythran Giveaway items.

"I got suspicious when I heard a very slight beeping coming from my second pair of arms," explained the doctor.

"The Mythrans. Why would they do this?" responded Vanda Darkflame, the leader of the Paradox.

"I... I don't know," responded Duke Exeter, a warrior, and leader of the Sentinels.

"I examined the insides of several others; hoping that it was just an experiment, or a mistake. Each item sent from the Mythrans has enough destructive capability in it to destroy a block of Nexus City! And what's more. All of them, mark me, ALL OF THEM, are set to explode," here, Albert took a deep breath. The other faction leaders leaned forward in horror.

"Tomorrow."

Exeter shot out of his seat.
"TOMORROW!" He frantically waved his hands around as he spoke. "We have to evacuate, we have to, we have to get out of here, we we can't st--"

Hael Storm, leader of the Venture League, tackled Duke and thrust him back into his chair.
"Keep yer wits about ye Exeter! Yew know as well I tha' none o' our transports could get out of the known Universe by tomorrow!"

"Then... then... we just sit here and wait? And out there," Duke's voice began to tremble. "Out there, there are tens of thousands of minifigures! Their all going to die?" His voice rose to a shout.

"Quiet!" commanded Albert. "I didn't say we would let them die."

Albert walked towards a nearby wall, and an electronic panel slid out. He entered a long string of numbers and letters, and the wall suddenly dissolved in front of them.

"Behold! My latest project. The T.E.L.E.P.O.R.T.! Able to send any minifigure to a location far, far away!"

Hael Storm's eyebrow raised as he saw the skeleton of a fish attached to a wire.

Albert saw his expression, looked behind his shoulder, and blushed.

"Sorry," he stuttered, "wrong wall panel."

He closed the panel, and went to ANOTHER electric panel, and entered the same code. He coughed, and said,

"Behold! The Teleport!"

This time he had gotten it right. In front of them stood a tall, circular metal device, with enough room for a large minifigure to pass through. Albert spoke as he moved around it, adjusting wires and connecting various plugs.

"The Teleport is a piece of (he blushed again, although this time purposely) pure genius! All a minifigure needs to do is step in, and he will be catapulted to another
world, who knows where!"

Vanda pointed out an obvious flaw.
"If nobody knows where, then how will they get back?"

Albert hemmed and hawed. "Well, I'm still working on that part. There isn't anything fool-proof yet, you see, but if the minifigures who go through take this remote control," he held up a VERY advanced looking piece of technology, "it SHOULD be able to reopen the portal and send them back here."

"Should. You said should. So basically you're saying that whoever goes through this risks being stranded on a deserted world, or worse, one inhabited by unfriendly people, with, if I know your inventions Albert, no way of getting back?" asked Exeter.

"Well.... yes. But, I am currently working on a safer way of returning, which is much better."

"We don't have time for it Albert! We need to get all of the minifigures we can here IMMEDIATELY!"
He responded.

"Yes, you're right. I'll go announce it."

Albert rushed out the door.... and was thrown back in by a huge, hulking brute of a Mythran.

Shival.

Albert adjusted his glasses and stared up at the Mythran.

"Shival, what are you doing here?" he said, in an attempt to sound nonchalant.

"So." he began, in his odd manor of speaking. "So. You found one of the bombs."

Albert's throat bobbed nervously.

"Bombs? UherIdidn'tfindanybombsbu--" His incomprehensible string of words was cut of short as Shival slammed his massive foot into Albert's face; knocking him unconscious.

"Albert!" screamed Vanda.

"Silence!" shouted Shival, turning on Vanda. "So, you thought that you would cheat my revenge, is that it? You thought you all would help the minifigures get away!"

A passing minifigure heard these words, and came and peaked in at the door.

"What were you going to do again? TELEPORTER? Let's here the exact words, shall we?" Shival pressed a button, and a tinny recording of Albert's voice came from somewhere in the room:

"Behold! My latest project. The T.E.L.E.P.O.R.T.! Able to send any minifigure to a location far, far away!"

"Well my dear leaders." His voice sunk to a menacing growl. "I don't think so." He pressed another button, and 4 darts, each with a tranquilizing agent, sunk into the leaders. They sank to the ground; unconscious.

The lone minifigure hid as Shival came out of the room, carrying the leaders bound and gagged. He thrust them into an elevator, stepped inside himself... and the doors slammed shut.
The figure's eyes bulged as he whispered words into a small pocket walkie-talkie that he always carried around with him.

Another 5 minutes later, exactly 50 minifigures were gathered in the room housing the teleporter.

Buzzes of conversations floated among them as they tried to decide what to do.

"Can't let Shival take leaders," "Gotta fight," "Get rid of items," "Escape,"

The Minifigure suddenly banged his fist.
"I'll tell you what we're gonna do! We're gonna get everyone out of here! We're going to go through that teleporter, and get out of here! Everyone, split up! Talk to friends, talk to strangers, talk to anyone! Tell them to come! Tell them what happened! And get them here! Stay away from Mythrans though, whatever you do! I don't know how many of them we can trust."

All of the minifigures headed out.

An hour later, a 1,000 minifigures flooded the room and the halls outside it, talking among themselves. The lone minifigure looked at them in despair.
So many, yet so few. What had happened to all the others?
Suddenly, a nearby TV screen flickered on, and a Minifigure reporter began to speak.

"Hey everyone! Jim Valowski here, and have I got news for YOU!" The Mythrans are hosting an all-night giveaway at Red Blocks! Anyone who's anyone is there! Hurry hurry hurry, while supplies last!"

The Minifigure bowed his head in despair. His entire life was falling apart. But they couldn't go after all the minifigures at Red Blocks. They were scattered across servers, and thousands of Mythrans would be patrolling. The Minifigure wept. He was about to make a hard, hard decision. And he knew it wasn't the right one. If Minifigure life went on, he would not be remembered kindly. He would be the Man who abandoned his fellow Figs, he would be the Man who ran away; the man everyone always hates. But he could see no other option.

His whispered words echoed like a shock-wave around the crowded area.
"Through the portal."

The other minifigures took up the words.
"Through the Portal! Through the Portal! Through the Portal!"
The figures flooded through. Soon, the lone figure was the only one left. He looked around him at everything that he knew. Everything that he loved. And then he turned... and stepped through the portal.

***************************************

And by mistake, he stepped in without the portal remote: The only thing that would ever allow them to return.

***************************************

5 years later

As Brocktree ran through the tunnels to get out of the exploding Tower, the strange red mist in his eyes, he tripped over a strange, remote shaped object. And without thinking, he shoved it into his backpack.

The mysterious shadowy figure would not have smiled had he known that Brocktree had picked up the portal remote.
