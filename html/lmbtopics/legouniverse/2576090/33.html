Brocktree's senses flared, and he twisted suddenly.

A huge iron spear flew through the air where his head had just been.
***********************************************************************************
5 Years Prior

The gathered minifigures screamed and dove for cover, as a gigantic animal dove from the tree cover, and landed in the middle of them. 4 Minifigures were crushed underneath it's paws.
***********************************************************************************
5 Years Later

Brocktree unsheathed his sword, and then nearly dropped it in astonishment.

The spear had come from the minifigures.
***********************************************************************************
5 Years Prior

The lone minifigure withdrew an Elite Long Barrel from his backpack, took aim, and fired.

The piercing lasers hit the animal directly in the forehead. It fell.

And it didn't get up.
***********************************************************************************
5 Years Later

Brocktree was confused. Why were they trying to kill him! They were Minifigures!
He dove to the ground, and 4 arrows whistled through the air above him.
***********************************************************************************
5 Years Prior

The minifigure blew the smoke from the top of his blaster. And then went closer to look at the animal.

It was huge. It looked like... like a deer. If you didn't count the foot long fangs, the poison dripping from the tips of the antlers, the spikes running along his back, and the huge, sharp bones protruding from it's knees.
***********************************************************************************
5 Years Later

Brocktree couldn't kill them. He knew that. What if they and the other minifigure were the only ones left (not that he condoned killing Minifigures in the first place)!

Brocktree rolled, and 5 arrows plunged into the body-shaped indent in the sand.

That was it.

He wasn't going to kill them.

But he was going to teach them a SERIOUS lesson.
***********************************************************************************
5 Years Prior

The lone minifigure had just walked away from the dead body when ANOTHER of the creatures leapt on top of it. It let loose a guttural screech, and prepared to launch itself at the minifigures, when 1,000 bullets from an Assembly Personal Fortress thudded into it. The beast toppled.

The minifigure groaned.

They had come to a very dangerous place.
***********************************************************************************
5 Years Later

Brocktree picked himself out of the sand (dodged another arrow) and without further pause, dashed towards the approaching minifigures.

Brocktree, still dodging arrows, hammered into the first minifigure with his shoulder. The minifigure flew backwards, until he collided with a nearby rock.
************************************************************************************
5 Years Prior

The Minifigures quickly built shelters.

They had to.

As they soon found out, the island was densely populated. But not by Minifigures.

By predators.
************************************************************************************
5 Years Later

Brocktree spun, avoiding an arrow, and heard the tell-tale sign of a sword coming out of a sheath. He brought his hand up, the hilt facing down, and brought it's jeweled pummel down on top of the nearest minifigure's head. She fell, immediately knocked unconscious. Brocktree brought the sword whistling in an arc around him, and felt a jolt as a sword clanged into it, barely blocked. He twisted, fended off another blow, and brought his foot straight into the minifigures stomach. Moving while the figure was still stunned, Brocktree sent his fist rocketing into the figure's head. He fell to the ground.

There were 2 minifigures remaining. Both of them had unsheathed well-made swords.
They attacked at the same time. Brocktree dodged one blade, knocked away the other, and prepared for their next attack.

Again they came; but this time, Brocktree wasn't defending. He kicked one of them away, and with a well-placed flick of the wrist, the other figure's sword was sent flying, till it came to a halt 20 feet away. While the minifigure was distracted, Brocktree brought his knee into his stomach. The figure doubled over in pain, and Brocktree again brought the sword-hilt down.

His foe crumpled to the sand, and he turned towards the last attacker.

This one had seen what Brocktree could do. He approached warily, and held his sword at a defending angle. Brocktree smiled. This one would be easy.

He swung his sword in a curving path towards the minifigure, which he easily blocked. What the minifigure did NOT block was Brocktree's fist following his blade as it curved, slamming into the minifigure's unguarded side (Brocktree swung one-handed). He staggered to the right, and Brocktree brought his sword slamming down on the other's. The minifigure (who wasn't exactly focusing) dropped his sword under the blow, and looked up in time to see a fist-shaped object speeding towards him.
He blacked out.
************************************************************************************
5 Years Prior

The hungry animals were only too willing to attack the minifigures, and they quickly learned the art of survival. Once they had gotten suitable protection around their colony, some of the more hot-headed minifigures decided that they wanted to wipe out the beasts. Track them to their lair, and exterminate them.

But the minifigure knew better.

They wouldn't be able to destroy them.

There were too many, and they were too strong.

No.

They would wait.

They would wait... but not forever.
************************************************************************************
5 Years Later

Brocktree stared at the minifigures, clustered together; all unconscious. He had made sure he hadn't hurt them too badly. They were all okay.

And as Brocktree stared, he began to come up with a plan.
