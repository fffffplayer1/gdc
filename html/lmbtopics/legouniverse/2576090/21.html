Chapter 15
Brocktree stared down into the pit, which was rapidly closing. Mardor had vanished from view, rapidly falling to his death. He stood up, and a single glistening tear dropped from his eyes, descending into the rift. He turned and looked at the others, who were still staring at the pit in a state of shock, and slowly walked towards them. Their eyes slowly moved up to him, and back down to the pit.
"We can mourn him later. Right now we need to get out of here before this field of disasters does something else."
He shepherded the Minithrans into the tunnel, and taking the lead, started off.
-------------------------------------------------------------
Clyde the Mythran was talking to his buddy Joe outside the metal door.

"So, you wanna get somethin' to eat Joe?"

"Sure, once the other guys get back with those Minithrans."

"Where do you want to go to? There's Shivalbucks, McShival, Shival Hut, Shivaloes, Shival King, Shival Fried Chicken, or Little Shival."

"We haven't been to Shivaloes in a while. How about there?"
"Sure! If they aren't back in 10 minutes, we'll head over."

Unfortunately for Clyde and Joe, their lunch plans were interrupted by Brocktree banging their heads together. He gave a low whistle to the other Minithrans, and they headed back into the main room at the front of the building. Brocktree peered out into the sunlight streaming through the door. He thought aloud. "Let's see.... if I remember rightly, then the Rocket Pad to Nexus Tower is right over there. I'll bet that there will be a clue as to the whereabouts of Shival there." He told the others his plan, and they set out in the direction of the Tower Launch Pad.

Brocktree breathed a sigh of relief. The launch pad was still there, and miraculously, there weren't any guards. He set out towards them with the others following, and in a very short while they were at the pad. Brocktree was startled. He had expected that there would at least be an alarm or something of the sort, but nothing had happened. Shival must be on the lax, he mused. Luckily, all of the minifigures had faction rockets which had survived the blast as well as the faction gear, and they prepared to launch. However, before they had set down the rockets, Sergeant pointed something out to them.

He walked behind the Launcher, bent down, and picked something up. When he was facing them, they could see that it was a complete set of Rank 3 Summoner gear with it's valiant. Sergeant put it on, and the moment he did so, the gear glowed bright blue, and a slight electrical buzz came out of it. It settled, and Sergeant tried it out. He swung the Valiant back and forth, trying to get a feel for the weapon, and then activated some of his abilities, testing them. He chuckled in delight.

"This is so much better than my Shinobi gear!" he said.

"I'm glad. But right now we need to get into space." Brocktree replied.

Sergeant took out his rocket and set it down on the launch pad, but before he could do anything else, a strange whirring filled the air.

"I don't remember rockets sounding like that...." said Justice, looking around her.

Suddenly, metal walls at least 12 feet tall (far beyond Minifigure jumping height) sprang up around them, enclosing them and the launch pad. A screen flickered on on the wall in front of them, and Shival's face appeared. Brocktree jumped, but he quickly flicked a small object below the screen.

"My my my, aren't you the warriors? Disarmed some of my best men, and you, little Brocktree, killed a lieutenant of mine." said Shival, "But harming my people won't get you anywhere Brocktree. I have millions of Mythrans at my disposal, willing to do my every command. And you know what? I believe that I want to end this right now. You are trapped, I have men right outside, and there is no way out."

Brocktree snorted. "Really Shival, I thought you were more intelligent than that. Did it even occur to you that we are right next to a rocket launcher?"

Shival's face contorted in rage. "Who forgot to move the Launch Pad!...................... Well Brocktree, it looks like you escape for now. But believe me, I will not rest until I have you captured."

Brocktree dashed forward, plucked the small object off of the wall, and ran back to the launch pad, calling out to the others to get on it.

They made it out just in time. As the last rocket took off, a massive explosion shook the ground below them. Brocktree sighed in relief, and looked around at the other Minithrans. All of them seemed to be doing okay, except.... Justice was staring straight ahead of her with sightless eyes. She wasn't even controlling the rocket.

"Justice? Are you okay?" Brocktree asked

There was no reply, but then a static hissing came from her, and Shival's voice spoke once more.

"Brock, Brock, Brock... Do you really think that I would just let you get away, free of charge? No. Justice Rivers has perished in the flames down below, and what you see before you is a solid hologram.

"No!!!!!!!!!"

"Yes, Brocktree, yes. And you know what? It was your fault. You were so relieved that you were able to get away, that you didn't notice that your little friend was missing. Maybe you could have saved her; who knows? But I do know one thing. You may have escaped, but you had to pay a toll."

The hologram disappeared, and Brocktree was left staring at empty space.
