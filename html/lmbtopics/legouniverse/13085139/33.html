"One thing at a time," replied Exceedra as he bashed the last of the Skitters, as he came to call them afterward.  "Maybe they're sensitive to distortions caused by cloaking fields?"

"Likely," said a voice from behind them.  Arianne had her rifle ready to fire when she saw two blue outlines in her visor.

"Let me guess," said Exceedra to Freya and Eliza.  "You found something about them?" he continued as he led the way toward the reactor.  "And yes, I'm forgetting about R&D."

"Yes...the results of hybrid projects," said Eliza.  "Specifically trained, like the Hunters, to hunt everything and anything."

"It would explain why they see us even though we're cloaked," replied Arianne as more Skitters charged down two adjacent hallways.  "How many do you think there are?!"

"Far too many," replied Freya as she conjured throwing knives of shadow and used them against the enemies.

"Last I counted was in the area of a thousand," continued Eliza as she fired a burst of imagination.  "Mostly due to some sort of new spawning method...some pools of some kind."

"I'm going to have to figure out how the heck a silencer can be applied to that thing," said Exceedra as he bashed and slashed at several Skitters and jerked his head to Eliza's launcher.

"All I know is that it works, and that's enough for me!"  The Alphas kept pushing toward the reactor room undeterred.

The hallway behind them now seemed alive, the Skitters like blood in an artery.  "As long as we live," said Arianne.

Meanwhile, Exceedra uncloaked and turned the power to auxiliary systems.  "It's not like the cloaking is helping anyway!" he said.  "Boost comms, scanners, and whatever else instead!"

"Might as well also take silencers off!" added Freya.

"Finally!" replied Arianne as she took the silencer off of her rifle, the bullets flying faster and harder almost immediately.  Eliza was also able to use actual rockets instead of a muffled imagination blast.

"Just keep moving backward!" said Exceedra.

"Take another step, and another step..."--Steven Curtis Chapman

"Backward to...the reactor?" asked Eliza as she shook her head and fell.

"Yes!  Where...else..." replied Exceedra as his sight and feeling blurred.

Arianne and Freya picked the unconcious minifigures up and dragged them backward, the Skitters suddenly stopping.  "What the brick is going on with these two?!" shouted Arianne as the four got in the reactor room.

Exceedra and Eliza did not realize they were safe for the moment.  Likewise, Arianne and Freya did not know that their friends were being confronted by the Darkitect.  All was black and purple for the unconcious Alphas.

"Exceedra Norell...Eliza Lupine...and the particular others," said the leader of the Maelstrom, identifiable only by his voice.  "You've reached the end of the line, I'm afraid."

Around the two, visions flashed of the fallen Baron's dream of the future.  Fallen worlds, slaved minifigures, and many other things appeared through the darkness and faded.

"Just a few questions, before I go," said the Darkitect.  "Why did you come here?  What was your intent?  And why is it that you, Exceedra, of all people, seem to be chosen to get in my way?"

It was the lead Praetorian who finally snapped out of his frozen, floating trance.  "Go back to your hole, Typhonus," said Exceedra.  "And get out of our heads."  Just as quickly, everything brightened.

He and Eliza woke up with a start, darkness receding from their eyes and feeling returned.  Arianne quickly hugged her boyfriend while Eliza was helped up by Freya.

"Wait, one thing," said Exceedra as he got up.  "Mariah was with you, Eliza...and Freya, you were with Chase.  Where are they?" he asked, looking around the room.

"Right here," said Chase with Mariah.  "Let's just say Freya's shadow walk is more potent than I thought."

"It put a strain on me for sure," replied Freya.  "But we're all safe here...even the Abomination is gone."

"It's not," said Exceedra as his eyes burned with blue fire.  He saw the Abomination in the core.

"What do you mean?  There's nothing in the central core!" retorted Arianne.

"You guys rely on technology too much," replied Exceedra as he kept walking forward.

"Well we can't exactly rely on Mythran powers either, boss," said Chase.  "We're not you."

Only Exceedra at the moment could see the Abomination in the central core.  In the other smaller ones to the sides, there were no minifigures.  "Prophet, any footage of the survivors?" he asked.

"Yes...from a week ago," replied Eliza.  "The last thing I saw of them on file was them being...infused into another Abomination.  I'm sorry, Chase...you're the last one," she said, turning to Chase.

The Daredevil only shook his head and cursed at Eliza, pushing her aside.  While the other Alphas shouted at their friend, he walked up to the central reactor glass container and punched it.

Glass flew everywhere, including onto a focused Exceedra who noted the Abomination's features.  Four arms with scythe-like appendages, bull-like legs, humanoid chest, and dark wings.

However, when Chase shattered the glass, Exceedra pulled up his shield.  "No, it's loose now you idiot!" he retorted, shaking glass off.  At the same time, the Abomination stepped out.

Of the head Exceedra could now see; it was humanoid but had a snake-like jaw and two bull-like horns.  The only familiar things he saw were intricate purple etchings in its dark skin.

"Children of the enemy, come to your grave and it will be fast," it said.  The voice was harsh like a lightning storm but colder than a blizzard's howl.  "May the work of my Master be finished."

The Abomination said that while looking right at Chase, who froze in place.  His hands were on his flare guns, but he did not pull them out.  Everyone else seemed entranced or held in place.

Not Exceedra however; he knew what everyone else felt though.  A cold hand over the chest, and a blackness pulling one in.  He, however, unleashed an arc of imagination at the creature.

Meanwhile, Exceedra was still boosting from spot to spot on the Abomination, using his sword to anchor himself.  However, he was soon forced off when the Abomination ran over to a wall.

"Brick this!" shouted the Praetorian as he dismounted a second before the creature slammed its left hip into the wall.  Exceedra recovered and boosted into a group of Hunters, knocking them over.

"This isn't getting any easier!" shouted Eliza as she fired a rocket at the same group.

"I know!" replied Exceedra as he boosted away to avoid the rocket and the Abomination.

"So what now?!" replied Mariah as she hooked onto the ceiling, almost out of reach.

"Fight to the last, I guess??" asked Arianne as she rolled under a Hunter, sniping it.

"No...keep them occupied!" replied Exceedra.

"Like we aren't?!" retorted Chase.

The beast was swiping its four scythes at Freya, Chase, and Eliza.  It kept missing, however, because it was too large and slow in the room.  Furthermore, there was nothing but Maelstrom creatures behind it.

At least, it used to; now there was Exceedra and significantly fewer enemies.  As he sailed toward the bulging spine of the Abomination, he could hear Hunters and Skitters shout at their field leader.

However, the monster did not turn around in time to swipe Exceedra into oblivion.  It did so only when the lead Praetorian had lodged his boot tacks and sword into the Abomination's back and spine.

"Get off of me, you filth!" shouted the creature.

"Go back to the Maelstrom," retorted Exceedra.

The Plainslander raised his sword again, imagination swirling around it, and unleashed an arc of imagination.  That arc stopped in the Abomination but began to cause a chain reaction in its body.

Shuddering and croaking, the monstrosity fell like a skyscraper, crushing several Skitters and Hunters underneath.  However, the five Alphas on the ground had long vacated the part of the room.

When Exceedra dismounted, the Abomination began to smoke and disintegrate.  Any other Maelstrom creatures still left turned tail and ran out of the now open room, leaving the Alphas alone.

"A victory for the ages, right?" said Arianne, clasping her hand on Exceedra's shoulder.

"Not quite yet...one last thing to do," said Chase, walking over to the body of the Abomination.

"A victory, yes...the closest one yet, but not the grandest either...not yet," replied Exceedra.

Behind them, Chase fired one shot from his flare gun before walking back to his squad.

"Okay...that's it then..." he said, looking down and kicking the floor.

"Not quite what you expected, is it?" replied Arianne.  "Revenge?"

"No...don't get me wrong, I feel great...but it can't bring them back."

"Nothing we do can or will," replied Freya as she hugged Chase.

"Right...let's get out of here," said Exceedra.

"Just give a moment for the secondary thrusters to charge!" replied Ben.

"What secondary thrusters?!" shouted Eliza, flak and fire going off by the Crusher.  "Don't tell me you made more modifications without me there!  If you did, you and I are going to have a long sit-down talk about this!"

As the Alphas looked on as the destroyed Darkmount, they all put their hands on Chase.  He had knelt near the cockpit and had his head lowered, shudders going down his back.

"You'll be fine, buddy," said Exceedra as the group looked on at the expanding debris and fire field.  Still, the Crusher drifted along, staying out of range and reach of all danger.

"I know I'll be fine," said the Daredevil as he rose.  It was not clear who stood up more straight upon seeing tears roll down his eyes.  "It's just...being there and...fighting the enemy..."

"Hey, it's all past, all right?" replied Exceedra as Freya went up and hugged Chase.  The normally stoic psychotic man dropped his head onto her shoulders as the Alphas looked on.

As the lead Praetorian looked off toward the field of space that was Darkmount, questions hit his mind.  How did the Darkitect contact me?  What was his purpose with his questions?

There were many more that also bombarded Exceedra's head as walked upstairs.  "Is anything clear or certain these days?  War...war always changes...men don't.  What is certain?"

"Not much," replied Arianne as she followed her boyfriend up.  "What is clear, however, is that we still have a job and duty to do...and we need a leader with a level head."

"What?  My head hasn't been level enough for you?" replied Exceedra with a slight shaking of the head.  His girlfriend came up and lightly punched him in the shoulder.

"By the way, I've got the crystals Kor'elos gave to us up top with Maxwell," said Ben.

As Exceedra and Arianne looked behind them, they saw the reclusive man at the holotable.  Behind him in the back were twelve crystals, three of green, blue, red, and orange each.

"How about the two of you get a room...please," said the Inventor apprentice.

"This is our room...you're in it," replied Exceedra as he pointed downstairs.  "So please do us the favor of getting out, or I shall invoke my leadership status and boot you into space."

Just as Maxwell opened his mouth and walked back, hands up and in front of him, Exceedra smiled.  "Oh lord!" shouted the Inventor as he relaxed.  "Now you're talking my style!"

"Again, I didn't realize you could talk in conversation," replied Arianne, causing the whole gunship to echo with laughter.  Maxwell simply fell on the holotable and convulsed in laughter.

"Ben, are..are we...clear?" asked Exceedra, still laughing a little bit.

"Yea...we are; no Maelstrom anywhere," replied the Engineer.

"Okay then...let's go home, Alphas," said the lead Praetorian.

"Another record-holding mission, it seems," said Eliza.

"What, we're counting now?" asked Chase.

"Yea, and have been for while," replied Freya.

As Exceedra walked up to the cockpit of Crusher and looked toward what was Darkmount, he began to feel warm.  As he looked on at the blue nebula forming in the destruction, Kor'elos' voice again echoed, but only in his head.

"The Darkitect knows more than for which you give him credit," said the voice while the other Alphas kept talking.  Ben turned Crusher around, heading to Crux Prime.  "Beware yourselves...and your leaders.  Don't trust yourself."

Kor'elos ceased again, this time not to be heard from again in the mind.  And, again, Exceedra was left with more questions than answers.  A plague that is settled in my mind, thought the young man as the Alphas left.

"Okay...who're the rounds on?" asked Chase as he started a game of rock, paper, scissors.  At the end, Jason was the one with the lowest score and was the one to buy the rounds.  "Sorry pal...looks like it's you...sorry!"

"Oh darn you all!" shouted the Inventor as Exceedra walked up.

"No rounds...this mission never existed, remember?" he said.

"Rrriiight..." said the group in unison as they lowered their heads.

"Still, next mission, the rounds are on you, Jason," continued Exceedra.

"Oh come on!" replied Jason as the Alphas laughed.

"Okay...lock it down...Ben, go faster," said Arianne.

Ben complied, engaging the cloak on Crusher as well.  "No sense in going places if we get stopped by the debris at the Maelstrom field's edge," he said as the Alphas took up stations.

"No sense indeed; good thought, Ben," said Exceedra as he and Arianne walked downstairs, hand in hand.  Maxwell stayed up, attending the battle room and securing the crystals further.

"Still...something's not right," said Freya as she, Eliza, Mariah, and Chase walked up.  Ben and Jason continued their work in the cockpit, watching for any threats and correcting flight course.

"No...nothing was right with this mission," said Exceedra as he turned to face the assembled Alphas.  "The enemy was too well prepared for us.  Add in the Abomination and the Darkitect..."

"Yes...it was as if they were waiting for us.  Add in how the fallen baron got to us and...well," said Eliza.

"It was as if we were meant to go there to be smashed and forgotten," said Freya.  "By our leaders..."

"We don't know the full story at all...not by a long shot," replied Exceedra.

"But we're closer than we were before," said Arianne.  "But Darkmount..."

"Just the tip of the iceberg, so to say," the lead Alpha said.  "I'm afraid we've been left with far more questions than answers.  But our enemy's capabilities are enhanced, and knowing that will let us prepare."

"Prepare for what, though?" asked Mariah.  "Isn't that the question?"

"One of the many 'the question's out there, unfortunately," continued Exceedra.  "Now how will we deal with reappearing in Nexus Tower?  Bar that for now...we still have some time to figure out things from Darkmount."

"Questions for which there are no answers, you mean," said Arianne.

"Yup.  So much for not wondering about the how and why of things..."

"The important part is we know of the new capabilities and tactics of our enemy, and his works," said Mariah.

"Yes...other questions can wait for now.  But that leaves me wondering...where're my visions?" asked Eliza.
