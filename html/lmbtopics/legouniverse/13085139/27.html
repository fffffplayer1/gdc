A Gray Christmas--Flashback Christmas special Part 3

‎12-27-2015 09:47 AM

Shortly afterward the two left their small house, decorated only by a few wreaths of mistletoe and an angel up top, a beautiful woman-like being out of the beliefs of humans calling themselves Christians.

Anyone who has more than one set of beliefs has to be deranged, thought the young man, reflecting on the legend that the disorderly humans were in reality the great First Builders.

Nonetheless, even looking at the angel decoration seemed to fill Exceedra with a hope and fire he had not found for a while.  "Maybe there's something to the belief after all," he said.

"Your father always said that it was a hollow belief and that there was nothing to it; he only believed for me," said Natasha as the two walked away.  "Now that belief is all that's keeping me together."

"Had I know you were that fragile I would have picked up more around the house mom," said Exceedra as he lugged around the two baskets, one for food and the other for presents.

Now that he looked down to check his grip, he realized that there was snow on the ground, and it was still falling.  "Oh!  That's why it's so dark at eight in the morning!" he said while jumping.

Up in the sky of Plainsland, a rare winter storm had formed and was sending down the small envoys of winter onto the Crux remnant and turned the sky gray and white, much like the ground.

Unfazed by his lack of appropriate clothing and shrugging the cold off, Exceedra trudged ahead, his mom close behind.  Through the town they went, wishing a merry Christmas to all.

"Queer lot, the Norells," said one man as Exceedra and Natasha walked by.  "Over their grief already, that is."

"Even at the celebration, Natasha seemed happy," said another, the two whispering to one another.

"I can hear you two, ya know," said Exceedra as he walked farther on, shutting the two men up.

"Grief holds us back from life and all that goes with it," added Natasha as she looked at the two men.

Meanwhle, Exceedra stared a thousand miles away while still walking on auto-pilot.

‎12-28-2015 09:41 AM

Christmas...what's the meaning of it in the first place? asked the young man to himself.

Shaking his head and looking back up, Exceedra continued walking along through the unusual snow on the ground.  Whatever the case, it's a chance to escape the sorrows of the times and to reflect on what we have....

Soon enough, eyes brighters and hearts lighter than when they left, the Norells arrived at the Krystal's house, a grand two-story mansion with an indoor shooting range and stable as well as the usual interior rooms.

The two realized they had stopped and were gawking at the place before long and resumed walking along the paved road leading from town to the mansion on the outskirts, with farms, pastures, and water wells.

"It never ceases to awe you, does it?" said a voice from a group of corn stalks nearby.

"And you never cease to try to scare us, Ben," said Exceedra back as Ben emerged.

"Ah well, I had to try!" replied the would-be Engineer as he and the would-be Praetorian shook hands and bro-hugged each other.  "Hey, you're looking better than normal...you too, Mrs. Norell."

"Thank you very much, you gentlemanly scholar," replied Natasha.  "Christmas always has a way of including many miracles and surprises, such as Exceedra finally feeling right for once."

"I think the weather also has something to do with it," added Exceedra.  "Days like these are always my favorite...which makes me think that, when I'm old and retired, I'll be living somewhere like Sylvania or Nimbus."

"And my friends and I will pursue you to get you back; through traps and snares and toils, we won't rest 'till you're back here with the rest of us sorry lads," said Ben as the three walked down the road to the mansion.

"Hey, where's the rest of the company?" asked Exceedra.

"Already inside; they can't handle the cold," Ben replied.

"I'm surprised you are," added Natasha, looking at Ben's short sleeve leather shirt, jeans, and boots.

"Eh...what's a little bit of cold to stop me and the Christmas cheer?!"

Within a few seconds of hastened walking on Ben's asking, the three arrived inside the white and black walled and windowed mansion, the inside floors covered with wood as were the walls and ceilings.

"Natasha, Exceedra!" shouted a voice from the main room in front of the small circular entrance room, where people could hang up their coats and boots.  "You made it!  Merry Christmas to both of you!"

"Merry Christmas to you too, Veronica!" replied Natasha as she and Arianne's mother hugged each other.  Veronica was much like Arianne was to be; dulled green eyes, blond and black hair, and overall beautiful.

"And you young man, why I'm surprised you're here!" said Veronica as she brushed a strand of her shoulder-length hair back over and made sure her flannel long sleeve and jeans were right.

"Well you know me; I'm where the party is," said Exceedra.

"Only because of the food," cut in Ben, delivering a truthful remark.

"You know, some days I wonder why you and I are friends even," replied the young Norell, shutting his friend right down.  "Anyway, I bet it's hectic in here...and it smells good!"

"Thank you, and yes it is hectic," replied Veronica, her red cheeks softening a little bit.  "Obviously you can tell.  Anyway, come on!  Put the presents and foods where they belong!"

After kicking off their boots and setting them down the three walked inside, depositing the presents under a large tree that lorded to the second story through a hole in the first's ceiling.

As Exceedra put the sweets along with the many other dishes in the adjacent kitchen, he realized Arianne was nowhere to be seen.  No, just leave me alone! she said last time they met.

The young man shook his head as he recalled the Plainsland celebrations for Gerald, her brother.  She was so shaken and distraught that, last he heard, she had locked herself in her room.

After setting the food on the marble kitchen counter top, he walked back into the main room to socialize, knowing not to immediately look for Arianne.

‎12-28-2015 03:31 PM

Eventually after much talking and laughter, the whole crowd of nearly half a hundred relatives and friends to the Krystals gathered around the large tree, unwrapping their presents.

Much screaming and cheering was heard echoing throughout the mansion.  Naught however was heard from Arianne, who was nowhere in the room nor anywhere to be seen.

As Exceedra looked at his gifts of new clothing, board and card games, training sword and shield, flute, cooking book, and Christmas money, his joy and warmness quickly dissipated.

After gathering his presents in a corner with his boots in a corner of the room, the young man asked around as to find Arianne's location and what she was up to.  He quickly learned from Veronica, her eyes still a dull green but now brighter and more open, that his childhood friend had run off into the fields to avoid any possible contact from anyone.

His mind set upon trying to get her out of the doldrums, Exceedra gathered up Arianne's presents in the two baskets that he and his mom had brought and then left the house, nearly forgetting to get his boots on before leaving the main room.  That would be a cold and painful walk, he thought as he pictured the scene and tightened his jaw and eyelids.

Soon he was off, the snow still falling and a wind now piercing his clothing even through a new jacket he received from the Chaffmans.  Neither his arms or his legs grew tired as he walked seemingly ever on in search of Arianne.

After asking around town, Exceedra learned that his friend had gone east after going into town toward a ravine that ran from Plainsland's only mountain and to a crater.  In that ravine was a river and some beautiful trees.

His heart rate and breathing picked up on hearing the news and he was off again, blundering through the snow so furiously a hunter could track him bereft of any aid.

Eventually, well past lunch, Exceedra came to the ravine and a fence a few yards away from it.  On it was Arianne all lonely, looking out toward the east.

The young man started walking up, his arms and legs now hurting, and he crashed against the fence.  "Tag...you're it," he said with ragged breaths.

"Why did you come looking for me?" asked Arianne, clad in a knit cap, gloves, flannel jacket, jeans, and thick boots.  "You should know, gentlemanly scholar that everyone makes you out to be, not to have looked for me."

"Well, I wanted to get these presents to you first off," Exceedra replied.  "And secondly, you're my friend and I want to help if at all possible."

Arianne then looked at him, her emrald eyes bloodshot and face dotted by snow.  "You can help me by bringing my brother back...or by leaving me alone for Crux's sake," Arianne said.  "Thanks for the gifts."

"You're welcome...but they're not all from me ya know.  Some of these are from your other friends and family, who sorely missed you these last few weeks and especially at the Christmas party back at your home."

"Well you can thank them all too when you go back."  Above and around them, the snow seemed to fall faster and in thicker sheets.  Exceedra got on the fence, shaking his head at her.

"I'm not leaving until you do; I can't leave a lady out in the snow."  Arianne again looked at him and shook her head.  "Call me thick skulled if you want, but I'm not going, and that's final."

"Awfully insistent, aren't you?  Just leave me alone!"

"Arianne...just please, listen to me, just this once."  Exceedra's would-be girlfriend turned her head away, and her hands gripped the fence even tighter.

"Just leave...there's nothing you can do to help me."

"Hard to argue with that.  But there's something I can say.  Look, I'll never know what it's like to have a sibling older than I and to look up at him...or her.  And I'll never know the pain of losing that sibling."

"And let me guess, you know something even more painful."

"And I wish I never did.  You know what?  Life hasn't been a cakewalk for me either!  And I don't have a great house and furnishings!  But I've managed and lived."

Still the snow fell and the wind picked up, stabbing the two with the cold weather.  "Easy for you because you've had hardship all your life though; you're technically cheating," said Arianne.

"Okay...you've got me there," said Exceedra after a chuckle and a grin.  "All I'm saying though is that, when you've got things to do in honor of a loved one, it's much easier to move on after that."

"Don't you dare bring Gerald up in this," said Arianne, shuddering as she said the name.

"I wasn't going to; you did, but I was going to use my father," replied Exceedra.  "Look...he wouldn't want me to wallow all the time; your brother wouldn't either, for sure.  Now please, come home."

Below in the ravine, the water gurgled on, bathing the many rocks in its bed.  "It all looks so peaceful and serene down there, doesn't it?" asked Arianne as she looked through tearing eyes at her smiling friend, his hand out to her.  "I can't tell you how many times I've been here, just even thinking about jumping down there...but I never could be...because..."

Cracked on the inside from the toils of life, Arianne broke down for the first time in months and nearly fell off of the fence, barely caught by Exceedra.  She leaned on him, burying her head in his jacket while he did his best to hold her stable and shield her from the weather.  "It'll be fine...I'll be there, just like I've always been," he said as soft as snow.

"Why...how do you keep on going through this...this stuff?  What drives you after you lose someone dear to you?"

"I didn't lose everything, and I go on because he'd want me to...and because I still have things and people to live for."

Not until later did he know the impact of those words on her as she looked up, her eyes brighter and her smile spreading and bringing a glow to her face.

"I'm feeling a bit cold out here all of a sudden."

"That's 'cause you're not a cold snow woman."

For a while the two stayed there at the fence holding each other, eventually leaving to go back to the mansion.

"Thanks for walking me back...and for everything back at the fence," said Arianne when the two arrived back through the light storm at twilight.  "I'll see you tomorrow I guess."

"Sure; what's the occasion?" asked Exceedra standing next to a relieved Natasha.

"I don't know; just talking I guess," replied Arianne as she stood with her parents.

"Thank you very much son," said her father, Nathan, a man of forty five years and graying short hairs on his head and face to show for it.  His sharp, brown eyes brightened along with a smile.  "It meant a lot to us what you did today."

"Sure thing; now where's my fair recompense?" replied Exceedra with a fake country accent put on for joke effect.  For a moment the Krystals stood with straight backs and slack jaws, but then they laughed with Arianne being first.

After a good night wished as well as a happy new year, the two familes parted ways for the time being.  It was the following day that the two started dating before courting.

 

Exceedra woke up from a sitting position on a couch in the Nexus Tower room that he and the Alphas had been assigned, his eyes heavy as bricks and a smile on his face.  "Dang good memory," he said to himself.

"What memory?" asked a dry, croaking voice next to him.  "Was it from our past back on Plainsland?"

"Yea...how did you know?" he asked to Arianne sitting next to him.  "Were you having the same thing?"

"Oddly enough, yes," replied Arianne, sitting with him and with a blanket wrapped around both of them.  "I guess we fell asleep watching the Christmas specials...again, like we used to back home.  Back there however..."

"We weren't on the same seat and under heavy surveilance," said Exceedra as the two chuckled.  "It's odd...five Christmases not celebrated in earnest...may this be the last one of the war."

"May it be indeed," replied Arianne.  "And like you said, we still having somethings; our friends, each other, freedom, and the Ultimate Gift.  Merry Christmas to you, love."

"Merry Christmas, darling."
