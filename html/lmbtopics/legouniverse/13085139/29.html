At the same moment, Chase said a word inappropriate even among adult minifigures.  "When did you know, Ghosty?" he barked at his leader, who promptly put his hand up to ward him off.

"I knew no later than the Four, Chase," replied Exceedra.  "We were going to tell you here."

"Well, that was great going boss," said Freya as the doors closed again.  "One less Alpha..."

"Yea I dare say we could use those," said Arianne as she inspected one of four female suits.  "But let me guess; we've got to carry special weapons that can go with us on this mission and be cloaked."

"You're half right," said Vanda as she moved to another rack, this time rotating it around herself while Exeter stood back.  On the rack were all of the weapons of the Alphas, modified for stealth missions; Exceedra's sword and shield were now collapsable, all guns had silencers, Freya's katana could fold, and everything had stealth material on it.

"I think Chase was the only one who really needed the upgrades," said Freya.  "If only there could be a silencer for his obnoxious mouth..."

Now that I've been moving through the story some more and have looked back on things, it's come to my attention that I've been slacking in one part and that's the backstory of the LEGO Universe.  Granted most readers here will know what I'm talking about but there are certainly a few out there who don't.  I would reference an outside source but alas I cannot in keeping with the MB guidelines.  For that reason I'll do what I can here and over the story to remedy my mistake, starting with the story of the start of the area and what was Crux.

 

In the beginning of the whole story of LU, four explorers set out to find the last source of imagination.  They were Hael, Exeter, Albert, and Baron Typhonus.  They found the said source on a square planet called Crux, originally inhabited by the First Builders (humans presumably but unclear even to today) and their Mythrans (transparent, blue, glowing minifigures).  However, Typhonus was deranged and made a chaotic creature with the original nexus which in turn dragged him in, corrupting the great source.  In that moment, Crux shattered into thousands of island-like remnants, some large and some small.  Exeter, Albert, and Hael survived and found a crystal from the imagination nexus, miraculously uninfected, and set about restrengthening imagination.  However, a purple, black, fiery Maelstrom formed from the ash and dust and was controlled by the mind of Typhonus, who had survived and was reincarnated as the Darkitect, a giant, dark figure of god-like power of chaos and evil.  A few to several dozen years in, thousands of minifigures had migrated to the area and were stuck in a war against the limitless Maelstrom and the spawn of it, who were spreading from remnant to remnant, always looking and ready for destruction.  That is where my story (or Exceedra's, however you look at it) begins...somewhat.

 

Next post will detail the Four faction leaders and what the factions are.

The Factions and their leaders (aka the Four):

 

Sentinels:  They are they warriors of the Nexus Force, the military force of the minifigures dedicated to protecting imagination.  Dangerous, tactical, inspiring, and protective, they look out for each other and the other factions.  There are the Knights, Samurai, and Space Rangers (classes), and the leader is Duke Exeter, the paragon of all values that the Sentinels value.  He is the one who led Hael, Albert, and Typhonus on the expedition to find the Nexus.

 

Assembly:  The builders--and repairers--of the Nexus Force.  They are virtual conduits of imagination, making and repairing things thought impossible, but they too are skilled fighters if it comes to it.  The Engineer, Inventor, and Summoner are the classes, and Dr. Albert Overbuild is their leader, perhaps the brightest, most creative and caring minifigure alive, though he can be a little...weird at times, especially with four additional robotic arms.

 

Paradox:  The special operations (and least trusted) group of the Nexus Force.  They have learned to use Maelstrom energy against their foes without adverse effects and dream of the day the Maelstrom defeats itself.  The Shinobi (a dark semi-ninja), Sorcerer, and Space Marauder (supersoldier) are the specializations, and the Paradox are led by Vanda Darkflame, a former apprentice to Typhonus.  She's reclusive but wise, and highly distrusted.

 

Venture League:  Basically the scouts and sappers of the Nexus Force.  They're carefree, aloof, and prefer to be off exploring or patrolling worlds, but are dangerous if engaged.  Their specializations are the Daredevil (parkour), Adventurer (getting to hard places), and Buccaneer.  Their leader is Hael Storm, a space pirate who went on the original expedition because he knew how to find a then-in self-exile Exeter.  He's also wanted and notorious.

 

And you wonder how the Nexus Force exists...

Exceedra sighed, looking at the floor for a moment before sending the other Alphas on ahead.  "As long as this apprentice-friend of yours doesn't make a mistake, he's welcome aboard...for now," he said.

"Thanks I guess," said Jason as his body relaxed and cheeks whitened.  "So...Crusher now?"

"Yes, and our mission," replied Exceedra as he grabbed Chase's gear and engaged cloak.

"Alphas, I'm waiting on you!  Where are you guys?" asked Chase, still somehow in comms.

"How's he on the comms anyway?" asked a cloaked Freya somewhere in front of Exceedra.

"That's what worries me...or maybe it's not Chase at all, just a good patch-up," said Eliza.

"We'll know once we're in the hangar," replied Exceedra as the group got on an elevator.

"Handy that these suits let us see each other while cloaked, or I'd be bumping into Eliza a bit too much," said Ben, with everyone else looking right at him immediately before laughing.  "What did I say?!  Seriously!?"

"Lock...lock it down Alphas," said Exceedra as the elevator doors opened to the hangar.

"Everyone geared up and ready at their posts?" asked Exceedra as he did a normal check up on his squad while he walked around, hands behind his back.  It was only now though that he was beginning to note everyone's size.

"All set in the cockpit," said Ben at five feet six inches and normal shoulder width for a man.

"Me too," said Jason, a couple inches taller than Ben, a bit wider at his stomach than normal.

"Everything's looking good," said Chase at six feet and almost as strong as Exceedra.

"Feels like Plainsland all over again," said Arianne as she huddled over in a corner.

"It'll be fine; I'll be there," said Exceedra as he kneeled down and wiped away her tears.

"Thanks," she replied as she stood tall, a couple of inches shorter than he and more sturdy than most women.

"What about you Freya?" Exceedra asked as he walked over to the Shinobi's station.

"All's good, especially now that Chase's on board," she replied, as sturdy as Arianne but shorter by three inches.

"Just waiting to get this done," said Mariah at five feet eleven inches and a slightly weaker frame than the other women.

"You're not the only one; drinks're on me by the way," said Eliza, five feet nine inches and a more slender body.

"Is this normal for pre-mission banter?" asked Maxwell over the comms while he tended the holotable.

"Pretty much pal," replied Jason as the Crusher continued on her course deep into Maelstrom territory.  Why now have I noticed those petty details?  And why is everyone looking more...humanoid? asked Exceedra to himself.

Outside, a cloud of Maelstrom loomed with an asteroid field, Crux remnants too small for life.  On them however were several enemy gun emplacements.  "Come on cloak...work for us," said Ben with his fingers crossed.

A few seconds later, the Crusher passed on through with no alarms or suspicious eyes cast in the Alphas' direction.  "Looks like it did the trick...now to get us to Darkmount, and our goal," said Chase.  "My goal..."
