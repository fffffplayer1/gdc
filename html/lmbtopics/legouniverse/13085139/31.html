‎02-01-2016 09:47 AM

Song used: The Ancients by TSFH

 

Deeper in did Exceedra and Arianne go until they came to a massive, dome-like cavern with crystals all around.  In the center however was a Mythran, someone far more amazing and ancient.

As they walked toward the mountain's center, the other two groups came from different tunnels.  "Exc!  Ari!  You made it!" shouted Eliza from across the way alongside Mariah, cloak disengaged.

"So did we you know!" replied Chase and Freya as the three groups joined.

"Please, quiet children," said the Mythran at the center of Mount Imagination.

When the six had rejoined, Exceedra kneeled down in respect as did the other Alphas.  "Thank you...for saving us," replied the Praetorian, eyes alight and mouth agape.

"I've fought these abominations off this long...I was surprised when I saw minifigures trotting about my remnant," replied the Mythran, his voice only barely identifiable as male.

"You've...been here...forever?" asked Chase, wobbling in his kneel.

"Since the remnant was formed, yes," replied the Mythran.

"Holy...so you've been fighting them for years on end and keeping them from infecting the whole remnant?" said Arianne, bracing onto Exceedra.  "But why?  What is there here?"

"For years I did fight, keeping the Maelstrom away.  When it hit, I kept fighting it, preventing them from wholly infecting Darkmount.  'Why?' you ask, and yet you see the evidence all around."

"The crystals?" said Freya, her eyes alight.  "What is so important about them?"

"Imagination, pure imagination at that.  If the Maelstrom were to get a hold of them, I and my fellow associates fear that the Darkitect's power would be increased exponentially."

Behind them, a table set with bread and water appeared.  "You've been through much, children," said the Mythran, rising along with the Alphas.  "Sit, and I'll have your friends in soon."

"Just one more question...are there Mythrans on all the remnants?" asked Exceedra.

"Now there's the interesting part...eat first," replied the Mythran.

"Take another step, and another step..."--Steven Curtis Chapman

The Alphas were all to happy to comply, sitting down at the rectangular oaken table with seating for ten on the circular rock pedestal.  The Mythran motioned for Exceedra to sit at one end of the table while he himself sat at the other end.

Soon after, a hole in the mountainside opened up, allowing for a cloaked Crusher to fly in and dock near the center of the cavern, the glow of the crystals in an opening through the circle seeming like a miniature imagination nexus.

"Woah...this is...wow!!!" said Ben as he disembarked, Jason behind him with his pistol drawn.

"Guns and weapons down fellas, we're guests here," said Exceedra, making the two jump.

"So this is where you guys have been?!" shouted Jason as he put his pistol away and motioned into the Crusher.  "We'd been searching for the rest of you for a long while, and then this hole just opened up..."

I convinced him and Ben to fly inside so I could get a better reading of the area.  That was also when I picked up all of your vital signs, texted Maxwell as he came out, still using a wristpad to text.  Presently, he looked up.

"Surprised much?  And what are your readings telling you?" asked Arianne.

"Forget my readings...I need to get out more...holy...wow!" shouted Maxwell.

"Hey!  He can talk!" said Mariah as she munched on a piece of bread.

"Apparently the lady lacks manners," he replied just as quickly.

The whole room, including the Mythran, started laughing while the hole in the mountain side closed up.  "All right, lock it down Alphas...we're still on hostile soil," said Exceedra.  "My good Mythran, please, tell us what you know."

"My name is Kor'elos, the watcher of Darkmount," said the Mythran as he conjured up ammunition for the Crusher.  "I have been here since its founding and will be here until its destruction.  And my vigil is not alone...not at all."

"So there are Mythrans on all the other remnants?" asked Arianne.

"Yes...every last one of them, but not for the same reasons."

"I'm willing to bet, however, that the infected remnants no longer have Mythrans," said Exceedra.

"I spoke the truth of what I just said...those Mythrans, however, are slaved to the Accursed One."

"That must be an awful fate for them..." replied Eliza.

"It is...worse than you know, even.  But enough of that."

Kor'elos looked at the Crusher, then at Exceedra, and finally to Chase.  "I know your plans to destroy this place, and I will help you, even if it means my temporary dispersion."

"Temporary?  So you mean you can't smash for good like the rest of us?" asked Jason.

"No...all Mythrans are reborn anew in time with the same memories and same personality.  If we are corrupted, our rebirth will cleanse us of whatever impurity lingers."

"However, it is a long and arduous process," finished Exceedra for Kor'elos.

"Exactly, but it's something I will endure if it means denying the Enemy this place.  Before you leave, take one crystal for each person among your group and four more."

"Because there will be four more to join our ranks?" asked Eliza.

"Exactly, young Eliza Lupine, daughter of the vales of Sylvania.  And that is what I have to say next; the Mythrans on each remnant impart a characteristic of theirs to everyone."

"So the Mythran there, whoever he or she is, gave me visions?"

"Yes, in much the same way the Mythran on Plainsland gave Arianne and Exceedra their determination...and...more?  Exceedra, are you glowing, or is the fight getting to me?"

Everyone looked at the lead Praetorian, and sure enough a soft blue glow came from the corners of his eyes and his hands.  "What...the...brick?  What is this, Kor'elos?"

"When you destroyed Plainsland...and yes I am aware of many things beyond my 'prison' here...the Mythran, Meraj as I knew her, must have imparted some of her essence..."

"Ugh...thanks for the thought of me having a 'goddess' inside of me..." said Exceedra, half fainting.

"Cheer up and shut up...there is much to discuss...quickly now!"

As Jason, Maxwell, and Ben reloaded the Crusher's guns and got out the materials they needed to make a Seismic Bomb, the rest of the Alphas gathered around Kor'elos.  "So what's your gameplan?" asked Arianne.

"Almost the same as yours," replied the Mythran as he hovered over the opening in the cavern's center.  "Build the bomb and destroy this place.  The almost part is due to the fact that I know the Maelstrom has captives..."

"Wait...all this time some of my people were still alive?!" said Chase, standing up only to almost fall down.

"Woah, easy Chase!" said Freya as she and Exceedra caught him and helped him sit down as he cried.

"All this time...some of my friends are alive..." said Psycho through sobs and snufflings.  "I might forgive Exeter..."

"Yes...and the Maelstrom has information you'll need to win.  You'll need to move quickly however," replied Kor'elos.

"Come Chase...like you said long ago, there's time to cry after the mission," said Freya.

"That was on Gnarled Forest...long time ago, right?" asked Chase as he got up with help.

"Only a few days I think, but it certainly feels much longer, doesn't it?" replied Exceedra.

"The Maelstrom is making its push solely because of your group, Exceedra," said Kor'elos.

"My squad?  But we're just nine of thousands of soldiers!  Why'd the Maelstrom give a dang about us?"

"Because of you, and who you are.  I'd say it here and now, but there isn't enough time.  Just get moving!"

Exceedra nodded and moved to the northeastern end of the cavern, toward the sole town on Darkmount.  "Ben, Maxwell, Jason, stay here and defend the bomb.  Everyone else, with me."

"Regroup here once your mission is complete and leave...I'll set off the bomb myself," said Kor'elos.  "A good end is it's own reward, after all.  Oh, and please head up on the mountain itself..."

"Let me guess, there's a jammer up there?" asked Arianne and Exceedra.

"Yes...and an annoying singing Stromling...destroy both, please..."

"Well...how do we get up there?" asked Chase.  "Not all of us can fly, and the face is unsafe."

In response, Kor'elos levitated Freya, Eliza, Chase, Mariah, Exceedra, and Arianne up through the mountain and to an outcropping far up inside and on the northeastern face.  "Why couldn't we have just walked up?" asked Arianne.

There is a Maelstrom shield in the way, keeping the jammer and the Stromling, who serves as a watcher, safe from any outside threat.  They won't expect an attack from inside the mountain however, replied the Mythran telepathically.

"Right, let's move Alphas!  We don't have all day," said Exceedra, leading the way forward.

In front of them, rocks dissipated much like they had before, expect for slower and more quietly so as to not tip the enemy off.  "Specter Actual, how's it going in there?" asked Arianne.

"Well so far, but the job just started; don't expect us to have anything big finished just yet!" replied Jason from the other side.  "And I think Terabyte has some more things to say before..."

...You get up there.  Yes I do, texted Maxwell.  The town has mist all over the ground and many defenders all about it.  I'd stick to the roofs if I were any of you, it's much safer and easier up there.

"Is there anything else we should know about?" asked Mariah as the group advanced.

About the town?  Well, there are minifigure vital signs coming from a shipyard on one of the smaller mountains to the east of town; I'd head there first.  Just expect some Hunters, Stromlings...

"The usual suspects, we got it.  Thank you; keep us apprised," said Exceedra.

Through the rocks now, they were beginning to hear a voice singing an annoying verse that repeated over and over.

Outside, a storm raged, thrashing sleet and lightning across the world.

"Definitely the Stromling.  Prophet?  Do us the honor of blowing the jammer sky high," said Exceedra.  "Elite, take the shield down.  And Psycho, feel free to boot our dear Cacophonix off the edge."

"Cacophonix?  Oh, the Stromling," said Arianne as the group approached the opened end.  "Clever actually..."

Slowly the group moved out of the tunnel and into the air once more, the Stromling with its back to them and still singing its annoying tune.  At once, all of the Alphas acted and gave quite a scare.

"Huh?!" shouted the Stromling halfway through his tune as the jammer and shield exploded and he jumped.

"Surprise!" shouted Chase as he ran up and kicked the Stromling over the edge of Mount Imagination.

"LLLAAA TTTAAA DDDEEE!!!" replied Cacophonix as he went flying and smashed on the rocks far below.

"No time to waste, let's go!" shouted Exceedra as he threw his shield down and used it to slide down the face of the mountain.  "Move Alphas, we don't have all day!"

"Your boyfriend knows how to make an entrance, that's for sure," said Mariah to Arianne as she went down, using her boosters like the others to descend the mountain.

"You don't even know the half of it," replied Arianne over the comms as she followed the group down the mountain, slowly catching up to the sliding Exceedra.

Below in the town of Paradise Valley, nothing stirred at first.  Within moments of the ceasing of Cacophonix's singing however, hundreds of Maelstrom minions emerged.

"Brick!" shouted Ben as he dodged a Maelstrom missile aimed at him and Eliza.  "Mechs and turrets, watch it lads!"

Soon, a Maelstrom bolt struck where Chase was a second ago.  "And more Hunters!" he said.  "Definitely watch it!"

Meanwhile, Exceedra continued sliding down Mount Imagination, deflecting any projectiles with his sword.  The shouting from below from Stromlings indicated their disbelief.

"You better watch out, you better not cry!  You better not yell I'll show you why; I'm coming to town!" he said.

He's a.... shall we say pitchy  Gaulish bard from the year 50 BC. He resides in a certain village that still holds out agains the Roman invaders, through the aid of a magic potion brewed by the druid Getafix, which gives them superhuman strength. He's a semi-minor character, with the running gag of his singing and his psaltery-strumming being so heavily out of tune, that he's often brained with a hammer by the local smithy Fulliautomatix.
