The Maelstrom attacked today...within minutes the world was lost.  I bet they would have had a tougher time if the world had multiple locations of interest.  But we had just one village; so no surprise we fell quickly.

I would have stayed alongside my love, Exceedra, if he hadn't kicked me into an evac shuttle.  But his sacrifice allowed me to live...allowed others to live.

Except my family and his mom.

Everyone I ever held dear is gone, of that I am certain.  Even if Exceedra had survived (forget about Kevin, he's got to be a traitor), there's no way he can escape now.  I've lost him...

We're on the evac shuttles en route to Nimbus Station.  Alot of the people seem ready to just give in, but not I.

I hate the Maelstrom so much, for taking everyone and everything away...

I'm going to join the Sentinels and make the Maelstrom pay.

 

We've arrived.  To my surprise, alot of the people around me have decided to join the Nexus Force.  Sure they're joining different factions, but they aren't as ready to surrender as I thought.

I asked one of my friends about her change of demeanor, and she just looked at me and said, "It's what your boyfriend would have done."  Perhaps Exceedra's sacrifice reaches farther than I thought...what I would give to see him alive.

I asked one of the men at the duty roster to post a message asking that, if Exceedra ever came back to us--to me--I'd be informed.

As is though, I doubt I'll see him again...but there have been miracles elsewhere, when everything seemed bleak.  Perhaps the same will happen to me?

I can't think like that all the time though.  I enlisted with the Sentinels today, and I've joined the Space Rangers.  I'll be seeing frontline action soon enough, just as soon as I prove my worth and help contain a Maelstrom outbreak on a world called Avant.

I won't let this world suffer Plainslands' fate, and I won't fail...for the lost's sake.

Exceedra and my family would ask that of me...well, would he?  I'm just so angry...

Blast it all to the lowest pits of existance!  Kevin and the Maelstrom have caused my world to fall...

Ah well, he's probably not long for existance, and at least I'm still alive!  I think I'm starting to go crazy if I think that being alive on Plainslands right now is a good thing...

Anyway, I"m writing this mostly for my sake, so I don't go completely insane, but also just in case somehow some minifigure comes back with friends and finds me.  My name is Exceedra Norell, and I am, or was, the last minifigure alive.  I stayed behind with a traitor--unknown to me at the time--called Kevin Farsight.  He was one of those friends who gets kept at the fringe of most things...perhaps that's what caused him to turn us all over to the Maelstrom?

Anyway, it's been a few weeks now, and I'm lucky to find all of these supplies still lying about.  I've got it all; a purifier, this journal, some clothes and military gear, and a good hiding spot.  Still, it's a small comfort.  What I really would like to know is if my friends and family, including my would-have-been betrothed Arianne, made it to safety.  Something tells me not all of them made it though; Kevin and I, as he prepared to drive his sword through me, thought we saw an explosion off in the distance.  Well...I thought, who knows if he saw it even?

Well, I can't dwell on it now.  This place has changed drastically; gone are any hints even of my old world.  Everything is changed, and it looks like some place right out of some horror movie...

It's taking all of my wits and skills just to stay alive right now.  I'm hardly getting the sleep I should, because every night I'm concerned some Stromling or something worse will find me...luckily it hasn't happened yet.

What's really concerning me is that I'm hearing whispers from people I knew, and they want me to avenge them however possible.

I know how and where to start, but I just need to get the right items needed to carry out the plan...long time...

We just evacuated Plainslands...one of the shuttles blew an engine and went way off course, toward the Maelstrom.  Arianne's family and Exceedra's mom were on board.

The people are looking to me now; I was alongside Exceedra near the end, fighting the enemy off while the people got away.  When the last shuttle was ready to leave, he stayed behind, along with a reluctant Kevin...I'm willing to bet that guy was a traitor, he didn't seem too happy about evacuating or fighting the Maelstrom.  Bah, what does it matter?

What did matter was when Arianne got kicked into the shuttle by Exceedra.  I never saw him so distraught but calm at the same time...

I hope he's somehow still alive and will one day get back to us.

Anyway, with the people looking to me, I've got to lead them.  But I'm not much of a leader...Exceedra would definately have qualified, and some people on the other shuttles may be a better choice than I as well.  Still, as is, I've told them that I intend to join the Nexus Force.  If they want to follow me down that road, they are more than welcome to.

Unlike Arianne, who intends to join to beat the Maelstrom, I intend to join to help people more than anything else.  For that reason, I'm probably going to join the Assembly.  They're the most caring and creative of the four factions, and are more oriented on defense, like I am.

That doesn't mean they aren't good fighters when it comes to it.  But vengeance is no way to go; if stories from across the universe are true, it just leads to ruin and more chaos.

The Maelstrom does enough of that!

First drop was today, when we landed in Avant.

After I enlisted in the Sentinels and got my Space Ranger equipment, I had to mill about Nimbus Station for a while before the dropship was supposed to arrive.  During my quaint little stay there, I was able to stroll through the gardens, stadium, the whole place.  Ben and some others tried to talk to me, but I wasn't going to hear their apologies and comforts anymore.  What's done is done, past is past, and yet to come is yet to come.

Maybe Exceedra is still out there, alive, but I wasn't about to wait around like a damsel in distress.  When our time finally came, I wound up stuck with three other newbies, each from a different faction.

Some Sentinel Space Ranger who called himself Epsilon led the mission.  Our job was simple; get down there, clear the Stromlings and Mechs away, and allow Paradox and Assembly to clean up the contanimation.

Simple was not what the job was.

Our dropship got shot at, but it never got it.  The evasive maneuvers threw us around the cabin though...my head still hurts...

Eventually, we got the green light to get out and down to the surface.  Once we were there, we had a hard time at it; Epsilon was stuck trying to protect the other three, who were complete novices and never faced down the Maelstrom before.  Meanwhile, the dropship was trying to get some shots off while still evading surface fire...

Okay, so maybe it was my first time in an actual fight, but I was around the Maelstrom and fought it, even if it was from behind an AA gun.  But anyway, later on I felt for the others; even though Exceedra and I had gone through combat maneuvers hundreds of times before, actually applying them was much harder, and getting used to the guns and designating orbital strikes made live much harder at that moment.

Thankfully though, the clean-up crew came along and actually assisted in the fight, Ben among them.  It was over.

I just wonder, would Exceedra approve of me now?

This first night is rough...

After we had helped the clean-up crew haul around their gadgets, we returned to Nimbus Station and to a nice, warm meal.  We also were formally inducted into the Nexus Force and to our respective factions.  It was a great celebration to be sure, but it was a cold one for me.

Exceedra wasn't there...he should have been.  More and more though, I find myself asking what would he say of my chosen path.  Am I doing the right thing?  Am I fighting for the right reasons?  I think I am, but why am I feeling disapointed, crestfallen, and unaccomplished?

Later, as I'm trying to fall asleep in the women's barracks here, I kept having nightmares.  I saw the faces of the Stromlings who were once minifigures...I saw the faces of my loved ones, lost to me.

Is this what they call post traumatic stress disorder?  I know I was doing the right thing, fighting to save others and liberate the Stromlings from their slavery to the Maelstrom, but it doesn't feel that way.

Now I'm just writing away, trying to fall back asleep and let some sleep medication kick in.  Obviously it hasn't yet...

One of the faces I saw was Exceedra's.  I remember all of the times we spent together, all of the times he stood up for me...

Now there's a man that the universe can't do without.

Still, I can't hold out hope that he's alive and that he'll one day come back.  I've got to move on.  In the morning I'm being assigned to some world that is a forest.  They called it Gnarled Forest, home to pirates; I'm definately going to keep my wallet secured and my pistol at the ready...

Before that though, the rest of the Plainslanders and I are going to erected a memorial here, on Nimbus Station, facing where Plainslands once was.  From there, we're off to our separate or conjoined paths.

The only one of us who won't be there who should be is Exceedra...I and the others could care less about Kevin, who doubtless was a traitor.

I feel tired now...just need to close my eyes...

It's been months now.  I don't have a watch or a calendar, but the time has really gone by and somehow I can keep telling it, even if the sun and moon are barely visible  Every day is much like the last; wake up, morning ritual, go out to ambush some Maelstrom minions, scavenge around, get back for dinner.

My little camp is improving though; it's got a wall, doors, carpeting, and some automated turrets.  It's not enough to withstand a dragon though...here's to hoping that never comes to pass!

But every night is also as rough as the last.  I can sleep easier than I used to months ago, true, but my sleep is interrupted by faces that I am likely never to see again...especially Arianne's.  Why do I feel like she's not the beautiful young lady I once knew?

What also keeps troubling me are the whispers I keep hearing.  I'm certain it isn't because of me going crazy, it's as if there are spirits of my friends and family still stuck here.  They still cry for vengeance, and to them I have this to say: it's best served via Tectonic Bomb.  But I'm still trying to get all of the pieces and the plans for that thing.

In the mean while, they'll have to be content with what I'm doing now.

 

The Maelstrom almost found my spot during the night.

It's been weeks since my last entry, but I feel like writing again, just something to pass the time.  Anyway, not much changed since the last entry, except for the fact that I almost got discovered, the whispers have gotten louder, and I'm still no closer to finishing a Tectonic Bomb...

But also is the fact that Arianne is the only face I see in my dreams now.  I still remember all of the other faces I've seen in my life, but hers is the only one to appear in my dreams...nightmares, whatever!

I cannot help but think she's joined the Nexus Force, has moved on, forgotten me, and is going down a dark path, one darker than even mine...

It makes me wonder...what's happening to me?  Nothing feels right about me anymore...

I've been at Nexus Tower for a few months now, and all I still have to say about it is wow.

At least now I know enough of all of the nooks and cranies to describe that tower as a labrynth.  Perhaps the main defenses are in the endless tunnels, service corridors, and hallways.

But anyway, it's so much fun to be off and about, learning how the tower functions, all of the important places, and so many other things.  To think I was off harvesting things when I could have been doing stuff like this!

Nowadays I'm making sure everything runs smoothly, no flaws develop, still learning tools of the trade, and enjoying some good times with some other Plainslanders and fellow Assembly folks.

Yet I can't shake the feeling that combat is going to come to us.  Crux Prime is under constant attack from many directions, most notably from the southeast, toward the Maelstrom.

I hope the day doesn't come, but until then, I'm happy to keep working away in this place.

 

It happened.  Of all things to happen, the Maelstrom actually came within range of the tower's defenses.

Normally those turrets would have ripped the army to shreds, but we Engineers were busy trying to repair them because Maelstrom Dragons, of all things, would keep rendering the turrets useless.  Add that to the fact that we kept being attacked by little imp/bird like Maelstrom minions, and it was like we had suddenly become short-order cooks!

Well, at any rate, we successfully repelled the invaders, though at great cost.  Alot of my fellow Plainslanders gave themselves to the cause in the greatest degree possible, and there's alot of repairs to make.

Once we're finished, Dr. Overbuild will give me a promotion to Engineer Rank 2, I'll get some combat training for just in case I have to use my wrench more than I already have to...

And I'll also make two trips when I get shore leave: one to visit Arianne and one to visit the monument, and add more names to the list of the lost...
