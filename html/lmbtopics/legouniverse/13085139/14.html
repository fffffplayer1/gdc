‎11-05-2015 04:15 PM

Song used: The Last Immortal by audiomachine

 

Exceedra continued firing his rifle when it went empty.  Slowly reloading with one hand, he kept talking to the minifigure.  "Well maybe I can turn that around!  Praetorian Ghost at your service."

The minifigure dropped his jaw several centimeters.  "Uh uh yea...I think the command center needs all data wiped, just in case this place is compromised.  Priority one for a Praetorian here, I think."

Patting the minifigure on the shoulder, Exceedra picked up his rifle once again and started firing, this time at some debris.  It fell down a few inches in front of the guard.  "Stay behind it," he said to the minifigure.

Darting out from behind cover, Ghost quickly brought his shield back to bear and started walking around back, all the while taking and giving fire.  I still like the sword better, he thought as he missed and had to reload.

He realized that he was out of magazines; he only had one spare, and that came in the rifle.  Tossing the gun aside, Exceedra drew his sword and ran full speed toward the back.

There, he found the Maelstrom was working on destroying power conduits.  Dashing up and forward with his jetpack, he then slammed down on an unsuspecting Mech that had been infected.

Just as quickly, he dashed forward to deal with more Stromling Soldiers, as what he called the Nexus Troopers infected in action.  It was too easy, even if they had guns, mainly because the bullets bounced off of his armor.

Soon, he found a door that was not sealed shut nor blocked off.  Instead, it was blown out, and the Maelstrom was pouring forces into it.  How on Crux could there be so many on a simple light frigate? asked Exceedra to himself.

Shaking his head, Ghost again went into the fray, startling and besting the Maelstrom at the door, however few there were.  Inside, there were far more, but they were taking cover from people on the inside.

Getting on general comms, Exceedra warned them that Praetorian Ghost was coming through and was mad.

"Take another step, and another step..."--Steven Curtis Chapman

Dashing from side to side and being careful to avoid friendly fire, he quickly eliminated the Maelstrom enemies in the corridor.  He quickly cleared the way, just in time for a group of Assembly Engineers and Sentinel Space Rangers to get outside and assess the damage dealt while also repairing turrets in the hallway.

Meanwhile, Exceedra walked into the command room of the base; there was not much to it, just some tracking screens, monitors, computers with data on them, and a holographic map.  Around that map, however, was a battle-scarred rank three Space Ranger and some man, apparently the person in charge.  The two of them were arguing.

"I'm telling you, this place needs to be glassed," said the Space Ranger, a female.  "All data needs to be expunged and wiped; Nexus Tower has the back-ups for the coordinates and your research."

"And what if it doesn't?" said the man.  "No, I need to put the data on a flash drive or two and keep it with me."

"And what if the Maelstrom gets to you and the data?  Then what?"

Shaking his head, Exceedra cut through some computers while the two continued arguing.  When the first computer exploded, the man ran over.  "What the brick are you doing?!" he shouted.

"I'm one of Exeter's Praetorians, I'm taking over command," Exceedra replied curtly, still slashing computers.

"Well it solves your problem Max, now that it's no longer yours," said the Space Ranger as Ghost destroyed the last computer, sparks showering from it.

"Relax anyway sir," said Exceedra.  "I just came from Nexus Tower and I can confirm there are back-ups."

Max, the man, exhaled a little bit but still looked tense.  "Well it won't help if we're all dead."

Looking at Exceedra, the Space Ranger spoke.  "You're a rank two and already one a Praetorian?  What's your name, Knight?"

"Ghost is what I'm known far and wide as," Exceedra replied.  Murmurs and excited chatter immediately broke out.

"Well!  Our problems are small now," said the woman.

"And why is that?" asked Exceedra.

"Even though this place is notorious for having bad communications due to solar winds and constantly deteriorating equipment...well, news reaches us in bits and pieces or by other minifigures," said the Space Ranger.

"Man...maybe we can save the station yet if you're anything of the legends we've been hearing," said Max.  "You already heard my name I bet; that Space Ranger there?  I call her Blizzard, but she goes by Arianne...Krystal was it?

Exceedra's heart raced; here she was, alive and well.  And even though she had joined the Nexus Force it only made Ghost prouder, but also sad at the same time; she would likely not be the same.  At least she moved on! he thought.

"There'll be time for proper introductions later," said Exceedra, shoving the emotions aside and forcing his heart to slow down.  "Right now there's stuff that needs doing, with the destruction of data being first...and completed."

"Well, we won't be able to do much without the atmospheric shield," said Arianne, her voice unrecognizable due to five years and her helmet's speech system.  "After that, we'll need our defenses, then our cloaking, then comms."

"So then why the heck are we sitting here?" replied Ghost.  "Blizzard, you know the way around this place better than I do; lead on."

"You got it sir," said Arianne in response, leading Exceedra back out the same way as the room was further fortified.

Immediately they encountered resistance by more Stromling Soldiers.  Bringing his shield back to bear, Ghost shielded himself and his soulmate while she returned fire.

"How long had they been here, and what else has been going on?" he asked.

"They had been here for just a few scant minutes," replied Arianne.  "The frigate was on patrol duty, and when it finally contacted us after several missed call-ins, I got suspicious.  Max said to think nothing of it..."

"And this is the result?"

"Yup, the great, blasted result of his stupidity."

"Not much farther; if the northern hall hadn't been blocked, we could have been there much faster," replied Arianne, the slower of the two due to constantly seeking cover and having to shoot at the enemies.

Exceedra was moving forward at a much faster rate, cutting enemies asunder and bashing them into either the walls of the command center or into each other.

Eventually he sighted the generator, resting in the center of the station and between the CC and some turrets and launch pads.  It was a cylindrical device, protruding on both sides and with dishes to boost its range.

"There it is!  Past a whole lot of Mechs and Soldiers," said Exceedra, boosting up, forward, then down on some Stromlings behind cover.  Frightenly smart for Maelstrom minions, the Praetorian thought.

"Yea, give me a moment," replied Arianne, just coming over some stairs.

While cutting down another Soldier and a Mech in one fell swoop, Exceedra looked around the place.  Honestly, I would have thought a place so important would be much more grand, he thought, looking at the command center, a couple of docking bays, the turrets, and some launch pads to the hidden, untouched worlds.

Snapping back to the present, he kicked a Chainsaw Stromling back into a Mech.  That was when Exceedra realized that the frigate now had a portal opened up in its starboard hangar, with minions constantly pouring out, from ground to air.  "Brick...not good," Arianne said beside him, having finally caught up and standing back to back with him.

"Every time, everywhere," he said.  "There's always a stinkin' portal."

"So who's the commander?" asked Arianne, and Ghost looked at her.  "Again, I hear some information!"

"Blazes if I know who or what the commander is," replied Exceedra as he hacked down a Hammer Stromling and Arianne blasted some incoming Banes.  "All that matters is getting that shield restabilized!"

Using his jetpack to boost over the enemies between him and the generator, Exceedra landed in front of it.  Blizzard followed close behind, using small rocket boosters on her greaves.

Looking at it, he shrugged his shoulders at Arianne.  "Figures it's up to me; please cover my can," she said.

"Thanks for covering for my lack of technical expertise!" he replied as he brought his shield to bear.

Sheathing his sword, he grabbed her pistol and aimed.  This time he was a better shot, taking out Mechs with one shot to the eye.  Arianne's shoulder turret also helped to cover them both.

Quickly reconnecting some wires, repairing some tubing, and redirecting some of the bent dishes topside, Blizzard stabilized part of the shield.  "Now for the bottom," she said, dropping through a maintenance access port.

Staying up top, Exceedra heard some buzzing on the comms.  Then a voice came through.

"Missed us?!" shouted Chase as Crusher flew in.

Shaking her head, Arianne walked toward the fire control systems, boosting over the hole.  For now, the Maelstrom had backed off for some reason.  "You get the feeling something bad is about to happen?" asked Chase.

"Yea...Prophet, sit-rep," said Exceedra.

"We're at the cloaking device, re-wiring as we speak," replied Freya for Eliza.

"How's ground traffic from up there?" asked Arianne, now in the Alphas' comms channel.

"A heck of a mess," replied Elite.  "Odds are you'd be somewhat safer in the sky...if it weren't for the Banes and the Stromlings with rifles.  What about the ship?"

"It's much too close to the station for the turrets to target," said Blizzard.  "If they target the frigate, the explosion will take the station with it.  Forget the portal's odd habit of turning into a black hole when the commanding thing dies."

"So in other words you need someone to board it and send it flying far enough away?" asked Exceedra.

"Yes, unfortunately for whoever gets volunteered...that probably means you, Praetorian," replied Arianne.

"It sure does; but hey, I'm fine with it.  Just get me a way to get on it."  At that moment, the turrets came back online and cloak was restored to full functionality.  Setting the turrets to aim at any enemy but the ship, Blizzard exited.

"By comparison I look like a beauty queen," said Chase.  Arianne was covered in ash and soot.

"By comparision skill-wise, you'll be in the grave before a second is even over," replied Blizzard, hand on her pistol and auto turret aimed.  Psycho backed up and apologized.
