‎09-22-2015 05:03 PM

Song used: Orinoco Flow by Enya

 

I've been on Gnarled Forest for a while now...six months I think?  I haven't bothered to count!

These pirates are polite and charming!  They allowed me to set up my camp in their own camp, and they always have fish cooking when I wake up.

Anyway, when I first arrived, I thought this would be a quiet post, but I was wrong.  In addition to protecting this place from the Maelstrom, I also have to play police and keep the pirates and ninjas from going at it too much.  Mostly, with regards to the second part, I have to send the ninjas home before they get caught by the pirates.

At least I'm not the only one here.  A few other Plainslanders are here with me, and some rookies from the Nexus Force.  We're spread throughout the world though; it's not like we're all stuck in the general area that alot of other minifigures frequent...thankfully.

And while the Maelstrom is present here, it hasn't turned into a full-blown battle yet.  So far, as soon as a pocket of Maelstrom shows up, all hands are on deck to clean the mess up.

Here, the Maelstrom manifests itself in the pirates, sometimes the plants, and the animals, but it's mostly just pirates whose luck ran out.  They're easy enough to deal with though; put a siren statue in front of them and they are rendered threatless while they look on in...rapt joy, to put lightly.

Anyway, I've dealt with enough problems to earn my promotion to the next rank of Space Ranger.  I'll have shore leave in a while, so when that comes I'll go get my gear.

But, the more I think about all of this, the more I realize that Exceedra would likely not agree with the life course I have chosen.  But, he would support me anyway, so that's enough for me.

In the meanwhile, the news that reaches my ears about my fellow survivors is bad and good all at once.  Plainslanders are now known for their heroic actions in battle, known for being very dangerous, and known for...

Let's just say that this war has enough departed heroes...

Song used: Lords of Lankhmar by audiomachine

 

About four years have passed since I last wrote in this little book.  I've learned just about everything I could around here.  I must have done hundreds of repairs and check-ups.  I got my Rank Two Engineer set.

In short, life is good but boring at the same time.

The one thing that really troubles me, however, is the fact that every time I go back to the Plainslands monument, I have to add more names to it, at least five a shore leave.  It's not even funny.  I, Arianne, and about a dozen others are all who remain of our world, though I'm not sure Arianne is even still among us.  She's been so occupied with Gnarled Forest and the increased Maelstrom attacks there that she's gone silent.  But, her vitals are still pinging on the Sentinel Overwatch set up there, so we know she's still fighting.

I've also given up all hope of Exceedra still being alive and one day returning to us...almost five years is impossible for survival on a Maelstrom world.

Arianne also thinks so, the last time we talked which was at least a year ago...how time changes everything.

 

Plainslands blew up today.

I was on shore leave, adding three other names to the monument when I saw and, after a few minutes' delay, felt and heard the blast.  There's no way that a Nexus team did that.

Exceedra actually had survived all of this time.  Now though...unless he somehow obtained a rocket or teleported away, he's gone.  Still though, at least it's one world that Maelstrom no longer has any hold on.  Besides, he must have done that thing a crippling blow.  Doubtless Plainslands had become a ground for the amassing of Maelstrom armies.

Anyway, I have little time to celebrate and no cause yet to add Exceedra's name to the list on the monument.  I and a few other Assembly people are being shipped off to Avant.  The Maelstrom is making a huge push there, and alot of the roads, power plants, fortifications, and buildings are ruined.

So long Nexus Tower, hello battlefields...

Song used: Lost Empire by audiomachine

 

Five years after I lost my world, family, and boyfriend, I saw my wish come true while fighting the Maelstrom on Gnarled Forest.

As I took a break from the fight for the world, I looked up toward Plainslands and saw it explode.  I guess Exceedra did survive these five years.  No more though...

I can't dwell on it forever though.  This world is so contested right now, the ninjas and the pirates have finally called a truce.  And yet, ninjas are still trapped in the brig the pirates have set up.  There's little we for them that we can do though; between that place and us is a horde of Maelstrom minions, and add to the fact that the brig is the site of where the Maelstrom portals and contanimation zones are, rescuing the ninjas is not worth it.

It was only a few weeks ago that my job was still the same as in the last time I wrote here.  Now though, we need reinforcements to help deal with the sudden influx of Maelstrom minions.

I haven't even had time to take shore leave or catch up on news of my fellow Plainslanders.  I can't even take the time to properly give Exceedra a funeral of some kind...five years of wistful thinking, now crushed, though honestly I had stopped waiting for him about a year ago.  I just accepted that, if he was still alive, escape was impossible.  His only remaining option would have been to go out in a blaze of glory and avenge us all.

He's done both, so hopefully he can rest easy...and somehow I still feel crushed, despite the fact that this was his only option.  But part of me says he's still alive...impossible, unless he found a teleporter or a rocket.

Anyway, the battle for this world continues.  I've still got Maelstrom to fight and defeat, a craving for its defeat that has lingered these five years.

If someone finds this, I'm likely gone.  Please give this to a Ben Chaffman; he'll understand.

And in the off-chance Exceedra Norell is alive and around, give this to him instead, and tell him that I loved him...

Song used: Red Warrior by audiomachine

 

Five years of having to do everything possible on this world just to survive.

Five years after I got betrayed...after we all got betrayed.

Five years since I last saw Arianne, my mom, or my friends face-to-face.

Now it's all coming to an end...probably.  I have a finished Tectonic Bomb with a timer for ten minutes, and I also have a rocket that, while in bad shape, will get me far enough away from Plainslands to save me from the brunt on the blast.

I doubt that this journal will survive, but if it does, then you now know of my account, if someone is reading this.  I've had to face Maelstrom minions in the thousands, and in the end I've always come out on top.  Today, I'm not sure if that is the case.

But, at the same time, I won't let them win this time either.  There have to be tens of thousands of Maelstrom minions here.  And besides, the planetary debris will fly in directions of other Maelstrom-controlled zones.  The colateral damage will be high and mighty for that cursed force of chaos.

Yet, I find myself regretful of many things that will likely never be resolved.  I can only hope that, for the Plainslanders alive and well, the destruction of our lost home brings some small amount of closure.  And also, I can only hope Arianne can or has forgiven me for not being there these last five years.

Anyway, time grows shorter still.  There's a window coming up and I have to take it.  This is likely the end of things.  To whoever is reading this, I have but one thing to ask of you...two things, actually, but it may just be one.

Please, if I am not around to do the things I wanted to do, then deliever this journal into Ben Chaffman's hands, if he's still around also.

If, on the other hand, you know that Arianne Krystal is alive and well, then hand this to her with my highest and warmest regards along with my love.

Also, tell her that, if she's joined the Nexus Force, that I approve and am proud of her.

Well, that's it I guess.  Exceedra out.

Song used: Cirsum Perficio by Enya

 

After an hour of preparations, Ben and Exceedra departed their fortress for Nimbus Station, though they left an AI called Roland in charge of keeping the place running smoothly.

"Nexus Station...err, Nimbus Station..." said Exceedra, fumbling over his speech skills...or lack thereof.

"Getting a little excited eh?" replied Ben.

"Well, when you spend five years just talking in your head, your actual talking skills tend to get a little neglected.  That, and names tend to slip your mind when all you can think about is survival."  All Ben could do then was just shut his mouth as he and Exceedra put their rockets on two of the four launching pads and blasted off.

 

After a few minutes' ride, the two landed at Nimbus Station.  To Exceedra, it seemed much the same way that Avant did, minus the Maelstrom.  He could tell that the threat of war had been here once, long ago; there were new trees growing, some ruins, and monuments.

Off to the right of the main path from the launch pad from Avant to Nimbus Station was one such monument, but it was not for the battle waged here years ago.

This monument was dedicated to Plainslands.  It was in the shape of a minifigure in a field reaping his harvest and fighting off Maelstrom at the same time.  On a large placard surrounding the front 180 degrees of the statue were the names of all the fallen minifigures from Plainslands.

"I knew few of us remained, but this is..."  Exceedra stopped, put his hands on the placard, and bowed his head.

"There are only a dozen of us left, but we are among the Nexus Force's most dangerous/beneficiary," replied Ben.  "Imagine that; simple farmers have become some of the most dangerous enemies for the Maelstrom out there."

"I don't need to imagine it, Ben.  I see the proof around us."  The stalwart survivor wiped a tear from his eye.  "Right, you said Nexus Jay was in charge of the whole recruiting effort here?"

"Yup, follow me...let's get you officially listed and inducted."

Song used: Freedom Fighters by TSFH

 

Walking down the long path, Exceedra and Ben saw children playing tag in the forest, while parents watched on.  Here, there was no war, not now at least.  But both of the Plainslanders knew from bitter experience that sooner or later war does come to even the most peaceful of places.  It was just a matter of how long war stayed.

When reaching the end of the road, where Exceedra could see Nimbus station for the first time, he felt a little nostalgic.  The main square was made up of some shops and apartments, vendors, minifigures trading and talking--all invoking memories of his lost home.

There, at the very end of the path, Nexus Jay, an old friend of the two Plainslanders, was shouting in a hoarse voice, recruiting new minifigures and telling them of all the options ahead of them.  When he saw Exceedra and Ben however, he stopped and almost dropped his clipboard and pen.

"Everyone behold a true hero: Exceedra Norell, aka Ghost aka The Gardener...and the destroyer of Plainslands after surviving on it for five years!"

Even in the square behind Nexus Jay, everything seemed to mute immediately.  The legend of a lone survivor on Plainslands was know, but to behold the person, who himself was already a legend, was something else entirely.

As Exceedra and Ben walked up, a roar of cheers and applause burst forward, somehow louder than the chatter of mere moments ago.

"I thought you were in pieces Exc!" said Jay, somehow finding his voice, as he and Exceedra bro-hugged.

"The Maelstrom found me a bit harder to dismantle than most," replied the legend.  "Hey, I'd talk if there were time, but these days there isn't so, just sign me up for the Sentinels."

"You got it my man, and besides, I've heard so much about you that talk would be kinda pointless.  Anyway it's good to see you again.  Take care out in the fields!" said Jay as he registered and inducted Exceedra into the Sentinels.  "Welcome to the war!"

Exceedra nodded his thanks as Jay kept working.

Song used: Enigmatic Soul by TSFH

 

The two Plainslanders walked to the Sentinel station in the main square while the normal hubbub of the place continued along, though the focus had changed.

The moment Exceedra walked along, other people were talking to him, asking him about his story, and if they could have an autograph or something.  The survivor only said a few words.

"When the war is over."

That conveyed his message loud and clear, and everyone backed off immediately.

"Not much of a friendly guy, are you?" asked Ben.

"Not as much as I used to be, no," replied Exceedra.  "Again, surviving for five years tends to jade you and make you harder than rock."

The two made their way to Xeno Blueblade, a former warrior and now vendor.  Showing him his entrance token into the Sentinels, Exceedra ordered a suit of Knight armor...and got a rank one kit that had no shield and no back and shoulder gear.  Xeno looked at the legend apologetically.

"No, I get it," responded Exceedra before the vendor had a chance to speak up.  "Operating procedure and with the whole supply and demand thing."

"Pretty much," replied Xeno.

"Ah well, I made it wearing worse," finished Exceedra as he and Ben walked away.  "Oh wait, do you have some kind of a Sentinel drink that I can give to Jay?  His throat is getting a little worn."

"Yea, and I'm surprised he didn't ask you; normally, he asks minifigures to get him a drink after he inducts them into the Nexus Force," replied Xeno as he tossed Exceedra such a drink.  The survivor nodded his thanks and raised the drink as he jogged back to Nexus.

"Hey, catch!" shouted Exceedra to Jay, who caught the drink just in time.

"Thanks, and how's the gear feeling?"

"Good enough, anything for me to do?"

"Well there's a gunship to Gnarled Forest taking off soon; you might want that ride!"  Exceedra nodded his head and ran off, almost bumping into a female rank one Shinobi from the Paradox.

"Watch it!" she shouted.

"Sorry ma'm!" replied Exceedra as he went to sign up for the job.
