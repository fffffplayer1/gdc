Song used: The Lion's Heart by audiomachine

 

Another long day on the job done, thought it did get a little harrowing toward the end of it.  After a short but seemingly lengthy shore leave, my squad and I were called upon to resolve a situation at the northeastern battlefield of Crux Prime, by the politicians no less.  However they likely only did it as a last resort as they badly botched it all...figures.

Little did we know (because our surveilance was jammed), but the Maelstrom had been off building ships with scrap parts and Maelstrom ore, all salvaged from the battlefields it seems.

We didn't know that at the beginning of the mission however, so we got sent in there in the dark and with several other failed attempts by others evident.  The politicians now have imagination on their hands...

After a tense fly-in and emergency landing, we fought our way through the cracked, rocky land before reaching a cave wherein we made a new combat rover, dubbed Crusher 2.  She's a beauty, that one...

Anyway, we were near an outpost and figured beforehand "Hey!  Let's go give them a hand and secure this region!"  And we did just that with flying colors; well, we lost one minifigure.

After we secured the outpost we went to clear some embattlements before moving onto a wall guarding an island with a Maelstrom storm over it...did I mention that was our original target?

So, we broke the wall and moved onto the island, where we discovered the truth of the matter after seeing a Maelstrom fleet first hand.  Almost immediately we were targeted, but we managed to escape and get through to the command ship (nasty looking thing by the way).  There, we set off a critical reaction in the ship, blowing it to kingdom come.

Because of our actions, the tower and imagination has been saved, albeit quite narrowly.  I'll be honest; if we lost Eliza I don't know what we'd do.  It's because of her we know where to go.

So now we're all just enjoying a good shore leave, including a new face--Mariah Dune.

The day is long over, but even into the night I find myself wondering many things: how come Stromlings now talk?  Why did they have a fleet?  Was Kevin hunting the remaining Plainslanders for a reason?  There are far too many questions for me to list here, but they are all formidable...

At the start of the day, we had been called upon to resolve a situation at the northeastern battlefield of Crux Prime, one which the politicians upstairs had already badly botched.

The situation called for us to investigate a Maelstrom wall erected in defense of a new island.  The reason why us and not surveillance?  The Maelstrom was jamming our devices.

Exceedra led us out there, but we had to make an emergency landing far away from the target, and we had to land in the middle of a swarming field.  Nonetheless, he led us through without much harm into a cave, where Eliza and I helped in construction of the Crusher 2.

After our new combat rover was all set, we drove our way through the enemy, eventually reaching an outpost in dire need of help.  We helped to secure long and well enough for the brass to decide to come back.

When it was all secured by them, we left to destroy three battlements in the area that had been knocking our birds out of the sky.  It was during that part that Exceedra saved our newest Alpha, Mariah Dune.

Once the battlements were destroyed, we regrouped on a cracked plateau overlooking the Maelstrom wall we had been sent to destroy and investigate what it was protecting.  That part went off without a hitch.

Then there was the part where the island was covered by ship parts and Maelstrom ore...and then it moved through a portal into a massive area with a whole dang fleet.  Thanks to Eliza, we survived and made it to the command ship.

Once on board, we set off to destroy it, Crusher having been crashed in a hangar...anyway, we succeeded in the mission and were celebrated as saviors at the tower...which I guess we are now.

Song used: The Ancients by TSFH

 

Well...another day at the office--or battlefield--done and gone.  Maelstrom wall smashed, battlefield secured, enemy fleet destroyed, praised as saviors of the universe (rightly so I guess)--you'd think my mind would be at ease.

No; instead I find myself wondering a great many things as is Eliza.  In fact, I think she's the only one other than I who's questioning the things that went on today; everyone else is probably happy to be off laughing and celebrating, including our newest Alpha Eliza Dune (remarkable woman by the way, really!).

But they're smart; I'll wager they also have plenty of questions on their minds but aren't willing to talk about them, not just yet at any rate.  Anyway, the mission went off badly, as the politicians had already botched it all up royally.

That's the first thing; the politicians are making moves that are detrimental to the Nexus Force, and they're messing up communications.  Whose side are they on?  Ours or the Maelstrom's?  At any rate they're not all shiny and good.

Then there's the fact that they sent us only as a last resort and (I confirmed this) tried to keep it all hush-hush, even going so far as to tell the Four not to tell us everything!

The second question: why do the Stromlings talk now?  Why not before?  It's a little unnerving, how the Maelstrom is improving itself.  But at the same time it's funny...

I'm laughing right now!  Some of the things those guys said were hilarious!  Such as "Run away from the enemy!" or "He's smashing us all!  Run!"  Funny, but unnerving.

And the last one that I'm willing to write about (as there are far too many questions in my mind): where on earth did the Maelstrom learn to build ships and whole dang fleets?

As far as we know, no blueprints or ships had fallen intact into enemy hands, so the Maelstrom couldn't have just copied it all and made cheap copies (which they weren't).

I'm concerned...our enemy is getting better.

And we're starting to slip and decline rapidly...

‎01-04-2016 12:14 PM

Song used: Legacy of the Lost by audiomachine

 

In his mind, Exceedra was still in the day prior, when he and the other Alphas just went on shore leave.  They put their gear in for repairs, changed out of their Nexus fatigues into more casual wear, and went off celebrating, talking, and laughing.  It was also coming up on his and Arianne's anniversary of dating, and Ben and Jason had at it.

"So...are you guys going to get married soon?" asked Jason.

"Yea, or are you just going to keep this up?" added Ben.

"I'll tell you what; if you keep at this, you won't know.  Clear?" asked Exceedra with a grin on his face.

"And I'll help him make sure you never know.  Are we both clear?" added Arianne, grinning as well.

Ben and Jason simply clamped their mouths after that, allowing the festivities to go on.  They also really integrated Mariah into the "family of misfits," as she later put it, and the other Alphas were regretting it except for Freya, whose wit and nerve rivaled the new addition.  But like all good things, it had to come to an end faster than it should have.

Toward the close of the day, Exceedra alone had been summoned to Exeter's office, where he was given an assignment for the following day.  That assignment was to track down the ship parts found with the Maelstrom.

"A word of warning...the trail, as far as Albert and I know, leads to Chase's old homeworld," added Exeter.  "You may wish to do your homework on it, Exceedra."

"Will do sir," replied the Praetorian before leaving on his own accord back to the Alphas' quarters.  Of course, he forgot after sitting down with Arianne and snuggling up while watching a movie based around the Mythrans.

It was not until waking up in the middle of the night on Christmas day that he remembered, and what he found was disturbing enough to recall his own experience.

That was all in the past day however.  As the six AM alarm went off in the Alphas' quarters, Exceedra snapped out of his reminiscing and went about relaying the day's orders.

"Everyone up and at it!" he shouted, sending everyone else sliding or rolling off of their bunks.  "Get those morning necessities out of the way and meet up at the general armory!"

"What?  Was our special gear repaired with all of the other stuff?" asked Mariah with a grin and wobble of the head.

"No; it's where we're meeting on Exeter's orders," replied Exceedra.  "Now come on!  He's waiting on us ya know!"

As he sat down and ate his sparse breakfast of wheat cereal, granola bar, and strawberries, the lead Praetorian pulled up all pertinent mission information on his data pad, keeping it below the kitchen bar so only he could read it.

"So what's the game plan today boss?" asked Freya as she and a few others sat down to eat their breakfast.

"You'll know when we get to the general armory," Exceedra replied, momentarily eyeing Chase next to Freya.

Within a few minutes, the whole group had finished and was out the door.  "Oh snap!  I just realized it's Christmas," said Jason.  "Well...merry Christmas and a happy new year!"

"Were it so," replied Arianne as the group jogged to the elevators, Exceedra still using his data pad.  "We'll have to do without presents this year...like we have all the other five."

"Hopefully it's the last," said Mariah.  "I know three years is nothing compared to what you guys have gone through but even so...it's enough I feel.  And hey, we've got each other."

"Something I think we're all reminded of and thankful for every day," replied Ben as the group filed into an elevator.  "And there are a lot of other things we have too."

"Only because some are willing to give all in return," added Exceedra as the elevator doors to the combat and tactical floor opened.  He led the group out to the left, following the white, blue-lit hallway for a few seconds before entering a  medium-sized rectangular room on the right.

"And we're meeting here...why?" asked Eliza.  "Sometimes I wish I had visions just to know things."

Behind them, the splitting door slammed back together while the lights dimmed and a table in the center rose up.  From around some racks, Vanda Darkflame and Duke Exeter emerged, both looking grimmer than usual.

"Sir, ma'm," said Exceedra as he nodded at both of them while his squad came to attention, and he spun around.  "Guys, how many times do they have to tell you to just listen, not standing in ceremony?"

"If they so choose," said Vanda as she fixed her purple-streaked black hair, "they can continue standing like that.  But we will discuss that later.  Eliza, you wanted to know why here?  Well, it's because there are two things in here."

"First off is the briefing on a mission we're not even letting the politicians know of, hence the secrecy, even to the point of turning off the camera feeds," said Exeter as he pointed to several spots in the room.

"Very black ops-ish sir," said Arianne.  "Why the absolute secrecy though?"

"Because we're concerned the politicians aren't ours anymore," said Vanda.

"So I'm not the only one with concerns," said Exceedra as he let a sigh of relief out.

"You never were; at least Eliza and I were beginning to wonder ourselves," said Ben.

"But what do you mean 'the politicians aren't ours anymore'?" asked Mariah.

"I think Vanda means they've been compromised," said Jason.

"The Inventor is right," said Exeter.  "And this mission cannot be detected.  What you are about to undertake is classified tier ten.  No one else knows except for Albert and Hael."

"Sounds pretty cool actually...and scary obviously," said Exceedra.  "So, the mission please."

"You are to take Crusher and head into Maelstrom territory," said Exeter, immediately drawing sharp breaths and wide eyes from the Alphas.  "To help you, she's got a new stealth drive."

"Thanks for the upgrade, but I prefer she gets left alone unless it's me," said Ben.

"Noted, Engineer Chaffman.  "Where you're headed is a bit tough...a Crux remnant, Darkmount."
