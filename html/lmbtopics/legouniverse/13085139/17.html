Song used: Guardians at the Gate-Bruno Cariou Remix

 

While Arianne held the line against a swath of Stromlings, infected Mechs, and Spiderlings, Exceedra opened fire on a cloud of Banes and a few dragons, sending them to the ground quickly.

That was when a group of new airborne enemy appeared; infected Drones.  They were mechanical, using one engine to thrust itself along and two small blade rotors to keep itself aloft.  They also had missiles and chain guns.

Furious at how someone was stupid enough to leave Drones for the enemy, Exceedra turned the turret on them, having a harder time than normal because they evaded so well.

Even they, however, could not evade well enough.  Soon the skies were temporarily cleared, and as Exceedra exited the turret to assist Arianne the three evacuation shuttles took off.

"Thanks alot you two, whoever you were," said a voice through comms.  "We'll remember this...and you."

"Don't worry, the two of us aren't gone to the grave just yet," replied Exceedra as he walked up to Arianne.

"But we will be soon," she replied, her guns smoking along with several enemies.

"Might as well make the most of it," said Ghost as he brought his shield and sword to bear.  He started to walk up the cliff alongside Blizzard, and the both of them saw something they did not like.

In the center, where the plaza was, a large device had been raised out of the ground.  It was causing the cloud of Maelstrom while emitting a jamming signal and thus creating a dark zone.

Around it, there were many minions of the Maelstrom, from the many variations of the Stromlings to the Banes and Drones above.  There was only one way out according to odds.

"This is how it normally is for you?" asked Arianne, checking her weapons.

"Always," replied Exceedra as he flicked his sword.  The two looked at each other for a few seconds before Blizzard's orbital strike designator beeped.  It had a signal from an IIPS.

"Cover me," said Ghost as he charged down the hill and into the fray.

"Take another step, and another step..."--Steven Curtis Chapman

Exceedra never questioned why and how things happened; he just saw them as part of the inevitable flow of life.  One sails down the grand river, from stream to stream, tributary to tributary.

As he charged down the hill, screaming at the Maelstrom occupying Nimbus Station, the same philosophy still clinged in his mind.  It was now that the enemies noted their only two enemies and charged in kind.

"Here it goes...to hoping this signal isn't false," said Arianne as she pointed her designator at places near Exceedra but not too near.  That way as he charged, he would have fewer enemies with which to deal.

For a few seconds that seemed like minutes, nothing happened.  Then parts of the dark sky lit up as three rounds pierced the veil the Maelstrom cloud had cast.  Arianne found herself cheering and jumping.

As the rounds hit near him, Exceedra kept charging.  Around him, he could see the imagination-infused blue flames and debris from the ground and unfortunate enemies fly by him.  He could also feel both, but it did not matter to him.

When he charged through the flames caused by one of the rounds, he found himself running toward the center of the plaza.  Exceedra turned on his jetpack, pulling a one-eighty in mid-air and looping back toward a stunned Hammer Stromling that never stood a chance.

Meanwhile Arianne was running down the hill, gun and turret blazing.  "Stay back on the hill!" shouted Exceedra.  "It's a last stand; no real need to be heroic about it!"

"And I'll be blasted if I am not at your side through to the end!" she replied, using her jetpack to boost to his side.  Standing back to back, the two looked at the Maelstrom enemies arrayed against them.  For the moment, their enemies stood unsure, looking at each other.

From the east and west, two Spider Queens scuttled, and the enemies looked at Exceedra and Arianne.  "Brick this all," they said in unison.  They then looked at each other and nodded.

"Journey's end."

"Take another step, and another step..."--Steven Curtis Chapman

Blizzard wasted no time on hers, using her jetpacks to get on top of her target and blast into it from above, opening several holes in its body.

Ghost took a lower approach, sliding through enemies and getting below his Spider Queen and thrusting his sword into its exposed belly.

Demoralized, the Maelstrom retreated for a little bit, and only a little bit.  It was long enough for Exceedra and Arianne to regroup at the same place, remnants of enemies littering the battlefield.

"Yes...and it was good to see you one last time," replied Blizzard.

As the Maelstrom closed, they readied themselves.

From behind again, Kevin materialized out of the sphere.  It was only Arianne's shout that warned her soulmate, and Exceedra turned around just in time to dodge and send Battlebourne across Kevin's chest.

The betrayer stood, eyes wide looking at the long wound on his chest, and then he started to stagger backward.  Ghost then finished the job, bashing Kevin with such impetus behind the shield that that was what did the betrayer in.

As the former Plainslander got hit by Exceedra's shield and was smashed, the many portals around Nimbus Station turned into black holes.  The Maelstrom on the Crux remnant were drawn in, just as they were about to defeat the Alphas.  Then, the portals closed, only unleashing a small shock wave.

The seven minifigures, tired and sore from battle, collapsed.  They either knelt or lay flat on the ground.

Only Exceedra however had the strength to push his sword into the ground and rest on it.  Today, he had saved another world and fully avenged his.  However, the Maelstrom cloud remained.

As the minutes ticked by, everyone and everything seemed frozen in time.  The cloud did nothing else other than just be there, and no sounds were heard but the ragged breathing of the Alphas after hours of battle.

"Never, ever do that again," said Chase, the first to speak after the victory.  His commrades grunted their agreement; they had been in conflict for longer, but they never fought as long nor as hard as they had that day.

"So, we're stuck here I guess," said Arianne, lying flat on her back near Exceedra.

"No...the skies are brightening," replied Ghost, lifting his head up.
