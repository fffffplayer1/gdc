‎05-04-2012 07:09 PM

Clay opened his eyes. As his surroundings came into focus, he began to remember what had happened.
"Brick it, Exavier," He said as he rememberd when I had grabbed his head, "What did he-AAAAAHHHHH!!!" He screamed as he saw that he was standing on nothing, just floating in a vast space of blue and purple. Lazy sparks from each color would drift toward each other and fizz on contact.
Clay gaped open mouthed, circling in place, not sure how he was even doing so. As he came back to is original position, he saw a Translucent blue minifig floating with him. He could see the awesome form of the minifig, but it was tainted, growing purple at the edge of its hands and feet, as well as a slight purple aura that was battling against his bright blue. The minifig lifted its head, since it was down, and eyes filled with pain beyond anyones imagintation showed there.
Clay attempted to speak to it. "Wh-what are you?"
He could hear a raspy voice inside his head, echoing with sorrows.
"I am a mythran, THE mythran. Imagination in a body."
Clays eyes filled with amazement, as he gazed upon something few ever had.
"You... you hurt me every day. You scratch at my hands, grinding away at them with your destruction. I can no longer feel my hands, or my feet."
He drifted closer, showing his hands to Clay.
"You see them? This is what happens every day, hour after hour, torture leading to torture."
Clay felt... odd. It was a feeling he rarely experienced, since he never had need to. It was guilt.
"But, I never hurt you..."
"YOU NEVER STOP!! THE PAIN, IT CONTINUES ON AND ON! EVEN BEFORE THE MAELSTROM CAME, YOUR KIND CONSTANTLY REJECTS THE WONDER AND BEAUTY IN FRONT OF YOUR NOSELESS FACES! iNSTEAD, YOU DRIVE AWAY CREATION, DESTROYING ME WITH EVERY ACT OF VIOLENCE!" The mythran was glowing red, and the purple started to eat at him. The pain brought him back to normal quickly, but now the purple had reached his wrists and ankles.
"Stop fighting, end destruction wtih creation..." The mythran said briefly, before Clay felt excruciating pain, and he was brought back to the real world.

Clay opened his eyes, for real this time, and saw me looking down at him. He scooted back a little, wary of me.
"What did you-"
"Do?" I cut him off, "I showed you why I wasn't a nut, why I am against fighting."
Clay shivered, even though it wasn't cold, and stared at me again.
"How? How did you show me... that."
I kneeled down to look him in the eye.
"I have a direct link to imagination." I told him, "I came into contact with the Imagination Nexus."
A puzzled look came to his face. "But, you've never been to the tower, right?"
I nodded. This brought further puzzlement to his face.
"Then when did you ever touch it? The only people that have ever touched it before it was put in the tower wa-aaa-aaa..." He began to stutter and babble as he looked at me.
"You're last name is... Typhonus?"
I nodded again. This sure was hard for him to work out.
"Th-th-then y-y-y-"
Daniel cut in from behind us, "Yeah, He's the Baron! Is it that hard to work out!? It took me maybe five minutes after I met the guy to figure this out!"
I turned around to look at him, it being my turn to look puzzled.
"I don't judge very hard. I understand you were going through something."
This brought a sigh of relief from my lips. At least Daniel was taking it fine. Brick, he had already taken it home and wrapped it up for uncle bob!
