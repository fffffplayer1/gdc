My Brother Under the Moon – Chapter Three

Torpedo and Clot jumped out of their rockets. They walked among the crowds of people in Nimbus Station and squinted at minifigure’s faces as they passed.

“Do you see him?” Torpedo whispered to Dapper Poetic Clot, otherwise known as Mawr Blaidd.

“No, but we better find him fast, it’s almost dark,” Mawr Blaidd whispered back.

For the past seven weeks, Torpedo and all the other Ware Wolves, besides Ci Enfwar, would go around from 8 A.M. to 5 P.M. having no idea that they were indestructible servants of the Maelstrom, and then Ci Enfwar would call them and they would run obediently to his side, where he would give them a task to carry out. Their new mission was to bite Nexus Jay and turn him to their side.

“He must be out,” said Mawr Blaidd, “running an errand or something.”

“We need to find him or Ci Enfwar will kill us,” said Ceunant Helaeth. He had already seen him lose his temper with Maint Llawn Ymaith, and had killed him an excruciatingly painful way.

“Where would he be if he wasn’t putting newbies in a Faction?” asked Mawr Blaidd.

“Dunno,” said Ceunant Helaeth, “let’s look around a bit.”

The two Ware Wolves walked around Nimbus Plaza, searching for Nexus Jay. A rank three buccaneer noticed that they were searching for something and called over to the two of them.

“Hey,” called the rank three buccaneer, “you looking for something?”

“Yeah,” Mawr Blaidd called back, “do you know where Nexus Jay is?”

“He’s in the third tower to the right,” said the buccaneer, “what do you need him for?”

“Uh… we want an autograph,” said Ceunant Helaeth. The buccaneer wasn’t convinced.

“Hey, wait a minute!” said the Buccaneer as Mwar Blaidd and Ceunant Helaeth walked over to the tower the buccaneer had pointed out. He grabbed Mawr Blaidd’s shoulder and reeled back in shock. His hand was covered in little purple electricity sparks and his brain was foggy.

Mawr Blaidd looked around to see if anyone was watching. When they weren’t, Mawr Blaidd grabbed the buccaneer’s throat and squeezed. The buccaneer gagged and fell to the ground. Mawr Blaidd took out an herb of some kind and stuffed it in the buccaneer’s mouth.

“What did you do to him?” asked Ceunant Helaeth.

“I infected him,” said Mawr Blaidd, “he won’t be as strong as other Ware Wolves, but he won’t expose us either.”

Ceunant Helaeth nodded his head and the Ware Wolves left the buccaneer propped up against the wall, while Maelstrom poison slowly worked away at him.


It was getting dark and Rover felt dizzy. He vaguely remembered something that didn’t seem right, but he couldn’t recall what it was. He stood up and instantly regretted it. His head swam and his vision blurred. He fell to the ground and two sentinel knights saw him fall. They rushed to his side and helped him up.

“Thank you,” said Rover, “I don’t know what’s wrong, but I’m awfully dizzy.”

“That’s okay,” said one of the knights, “I’m Silent Wavering Mustang, and this is my companion, Enormous Large Mammoth.”

The second knight nodded his head.

“We’re members of the Elite Sentinels, called Gorau,” said Mustang, and we’re trained to take on any Maelstrom menace imaginable efficiently, so it’s no problem helping you to stand.”

“Thanks again,” said Rover, “something doesn’t feel right, I just can’t name it.”

A gunshot was heard and the window to Nexus Jay’s apartment shattered. A trail of smoke was coming from the gap in the wall, and shadows flickered on the interior of Nexus Jay’s room.

A light clicked in Rover’s head and he remembered the two minifigures who had asked about Nexus Jay. He drew his sword Hesevalintes and charged to Nexus Jay’s apartment, the sentinel knights right behind him. He jumped into the elevator and zoomed up to Nexus Jay’s level. The three minifigures ran down the hallway and sharply turned into Nexus Jay’s room. They saw the Nexus Force recruiter backed into a corner and brandishing a heavy broadsword, keeping two enormous wolves from tearing him to shreds.

“Thank goodness!” cried Nexus Jay when he saw Rover, Mustang, and Mammoth, “these Ware Wolves are going to eat me!”

Rover gave a mighty battle cry and jumped on Ceunant Helaeth, driving his sword into the beast’s back. The wolf reared up and flung Rover off him, the stab wound healing instantly. Mwar Blaidd turned towards Rover and spoke in a deep, resounding voice.

“Welcome,” said the wolf.

Rover’s body started to prickle and his spine shifted and extended. His hands changed to paws and he grew fangs two inches long and poison injecting. Rover turned to Nexus Jay and flung his body at him. Nexus Jay swung the broadsword with immense strength, sending Rover flying through the air and land crashing on the floor.

Two bolts of imagination hit Rover’s body and gave him immense pain. He turned, snarling, to the two knights, who were brandishing their swords and running at him. Rover turned and snarled at the two knights. He vaguely saw Mawr Blaidd jump at Nexus Jay out of the corner of his eye and Nexus Jay thrust his sword forward. The blade penetrated Mawr Blaidd’s chest and he smashed.

My Brother Under the Moon-Chapter four

Everyone stood still and watched Mawr Blaidd’s pieces fall to the ground. An energy ball formed in the center of the pieces and the form of Dapper Poetic Clot appeared. Clot raised himself and looked around, bewildered.

“What happened?” he asked.

Ceunant Helaeth jumped at Clot and sunk his teeth into his arm, restarting the cycle. Clot vibrated and quaked for a few minutes, then turned back into Mawr Blaidd. They turned back to Nexus Jay and saw him leap out the window into a helicopter. The two knights jumped out the window after him, but only one made it. The other fell to the ground and smashed.

“Mammoth!” shouted Mustang as his friend fell to the ground.

Mammoth rebuilt on the window ledge and Rover leaped at him, sending him to the ground again. This time he didn’t smash, because he had already transformed into a wolf by then. The helicopter flew away, but Mustang’s cries of dismay were heard long after the vehicle disappeared.

The four wolves saw the helicopter disappear, but only two of them recognized the full solemnity of the helicopter’s disappearance. Ceunant Helaeth and Mawr Blaidd looked at each other. They knew that Ci Enfawr would kill them if they returned to him without Nexus Jay. They used that innate communication ability they have to tell each other that they were not returning to Ci Enfawr under any circumstances.

“So what do we do?” asked Ceunant Helaeth.

“Forbidden Valley,” said Mawr Blaidd, “many a Ware Wolf have hidden there.”

The two wolves nodded and ran off into the night. Rover and Mammoth hesitated for a few seconds, then followed. The four wolves ran through Nimbus Plaza and jumped into a transport freighter. Mawr Blaidd grabbed the controls and steered the freighter away from Nimbus Station.

The freighter broke through the atmosphere and made its way to Forbidden Valley. The freighter touched down in a depression that was filled with Dark Ronin. The Dark Ronin clustered around the freighter, poking their lances and katanas at it. The docking port opened and the four wolves exited the freighter. The Dark Ronin saw that they were not Nexus Force and continued with their usual drudgery.

The four wolves took in their surroundings. It was a small depression, with only a few Dark Ronin manning it. It seemed as if the mountains completely blocked off entrance, so it would make a great hiding place.

“It’s perfect,” said Mawr Blaidd, “we rest here.”

Rover and Mammoth looked at each other, confused. They could tell that Mawr Blaidd and Ceunant Helaeth were scared to death, but they didn’t know why.

“Mawr,” said Ceunant Helaeth.

“Don’t call me that,” said Mawr Blaidd, “we are no longer in the order of wolves, therefore we must get rid of our names.”

Torpedo nodded his head, understanding.

“Clot,” said Torpedo, “how can we live like this? There must be a way to get uninfected.”

“The nearest I can think of is by destroying Ci Enfawr,” said Clot, “but that is impossible in itself.”

“What about when you smashed?” asked Torpedo, “after you smashed you turned back into a minifigure.”

“I have a feeling that that was only for that night, and I would have returned the night after that,” he said.

Torpedo hung his head.

“Then I guess we’ll be stuck as Ware Wolves forever,” said Torpedo.

My Brother Under the Moon – Chapter Five

“Oh, give it up,” said Nexus Jay, irritated.

“Mammoth!” cried Mustang desolately, “Mammoth!”

Nexus Jay tried to keep his anger down, but it was rising too fast and too high. He couldn’t take it anymore. He walked over to the sobbing Mustang and slapped him hard in the face. Mustang staggered back and regained his composure.

“I’m sorry,” said Mustang, “it’s just that, Mammoth and I have been friends forever.”

“I’m sure there’s a way to disinfect him,” said Mammoth soothingly.

“How?” asked Mustang, “You saw what happened when we tried to fight them, they’re unstoppable, we can’t possibly restrain them long enough to disinfect them.”

“You don’t know that,” said Nexus Jay fiercely, “we can disinfect them.”

“You don’t know that either,” said Mustang, “He might be stuck a mindless slave of corruptness forever, and I might have to fight him. I could never fight him.”

Nexus Jay sighed inwardly. This was turning into a soap opera.

“Look,” said Nexus Jay, “everyone said that the Nexus would never be discovered, and look what’s happened, so I’m sure that we can disinfect a couple of dogs.”

But Mustang was not in the mood for common sense.

“Mammoth!” cried Mustang, “Mammoth!”

The co-pilot of the helicopter walked out into the cargo hold.

“We’ve reached the transport,” said he co-pilot.

Nexus Jay practically leaped out of the bay of the helicopter, and away from the sobbing minifigure. He ran up the ramp of the space transport and quickly found a room. He rushed inside, slamming the door shut behind him and locking the entry. He heard the muffled yelling and crying of Mustang as he stumbled past the door, on his way to his own room. Nexus Jay sighed with relief as he heard Mustang’s door slam shut. He made sure that everything was secure, then told the pilot of the transport to take off. The engines started up and the ship lifted off the ground.

Nexus Jay arranged for a substitute to fill in for him in Nimbus Station while he was at Nexus Tower, then stretched out on his bed and took a nap, while the faint sobs coming from Mustang’s room were suddenly silenced.
