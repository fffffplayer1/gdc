Having discussed their possible options, the private and lieutenant, along with Col. Darek Skychaser, have decided to attack the bridge of the L.S.S. Arrowell and retake the ship.

 

"If this doesn't work, we are most definitely going to wind up smashed," the private mentions softly as the group walks down a hall, blasters at the ready.  "And if it does work, we're going to be alone in space.  Typically, it requires at least a crew of 100 to fly and maintain one of these things, and we are only three minifigs."

 

"Eh, don't be so pessimistic," Darek tries to say optimistically, "I think we can each handle the workload 33 1/3 minifigs.  Besides, we're not taking this ship into battle or anything, we're just going to enter hyperspace and fly back to Nexus Tower.  No big deal, really."

 

"Sir, are we 100% certain they'll actually fall for our bluff about being ready to set off the self-destruct, even if we're still onboard?" asks the Lieutenant.

 

"But that's the catch.  We don't bluff, we use the real self-destruct."

 

The lieutenant and private stop in mid-stride, and stare with gaping mouths at their commanding officer, who they have begun to think is slightly off his rocker.

 

"Sigh... I know it's extremely dangerous, and carries a substantial chance of not even working, but if we let the enemy keep this ship, and gain the location of the Nexus Force, then we've failed in our job to protect the Universe.  Our job is to fight to the last minifig to protect peace in the Nimbus System, and we've got to do that."

 

*He turns and looks at them*

 

"But, I've got a contingency.  If this falls apart, you both take escape pods and get away while I set off the self-destruct.  If only takes one person to do that, and I'm not letting either of you get smashed unnecessarily.  You're both smart - the Nexus Force needs you."

 

*Darek starts moving forward again, but as he rounds a corner, the colonel runs into... one of them*
