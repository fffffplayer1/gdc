With a flicker, an image of barcha resolves itself onto the space above the Codex.  On all frequencies, Barcha speaks.

 

"Welcome, visitors, to my humble library.  I'm sure you have a great many questions about exactly who I am and what I'm doing."

 

His eyes glow gold for an instant, a grin stretching across his face.

 

"and I'm not going to answer any of them.  Look around you.  This world is becoming beautifully free.  Do you see the creatures in the sky?  That one is a bird, but only for an instant.  Next it's a ribbon.  Finally, it's the breeze whistling in your ear.  These are the inhabitants of a world on a razor's edge.  Their existence is only limited by their imagination.  Now embrace it!"

 

Chuckling, he dissapears.

 

 

OOC: to properly explain the phenomenon, the longer you spend in the field, the more you start to realize that your physical form is malleable.  You can, in fact change yourself into almost anything with merely a thought.  This is not a good thing.  Changing binds you to the field.  Once bound to it, you stop existing when you leave it  In other words, a major danger is presented.  An idle thought could turn you into something impossible.  While the benefits are obvious (e.g. immortality).  The cost is an eternal prison and probable erasure.

(Lord) Barcha, Piercing Spear of the SoL
