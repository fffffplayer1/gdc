‎04-21-2012 06:42 PM

“That’s it!” Exclaimed CaesarOctavius, and I imagined he was doing facepalm. “The Mythrans also implanted a virus that would show a video that would turn a player into a Stromling in the real world!”
“How exactly does that work?” Asked AstroCrusherAtom.
“I have no idea,” Caesar admitted, “Though it probably has something to do with Lego Friends.”
It really was all interesting and all, but by now, I wasn’t anywhere near the computer, because A STROMLING HAD JUST ENTERED MY ROOM. Another mystery was who it was, because I didn’t know anyone in my neighborhood that played Lego Universe. Maybe it had dragged itself halfway across town, just to smash me. Did that mean that they could also home into players or something? It didn’t really matter, because it was advancing, forcing me into a corner.
To buy me some time, I picked up the chair I had been sitting on, and hurled it across the room, which knocked the Stromling back momentarily. Using my time, I had another glance at the computer, and franticly typed “Help Stromling attack advice needed!”
Already the Stromling was getting back up. It was pretty fast, and seemed to have more than one health point.
Suddenly, Milligan was on. “I got one too, try a textbook or knife.”
Unfortunately, I was not in a kitchen, so I was knifeless, and, as I had tried to explain to my school teachers many times, I had no idea where my textbooks were. I was going to have to make do.
The Stromling was advancing again, swinging its claws. I picked up my pillow and used it as a shield. I also picked up my lamp and started to batter it on the head. Dropping the lamp, keeping the severely slashed pillow blocking the Stromling’s blows, I backed myself away to my Lego Shelf.
My Lego Shelf was where I kept all my old Legos. Mostly, there was old Star Wars, a lot of Bionicle, and a few other bits and pieces. In recent times, the dominating feature of it was the Lego Mindstorms set. My current project was a firing mechanism, which would fire small spheres at things. Unfortunately, it was currently broken after an incident involving my little sister, our dog, and badly placed pillow.
But I knew how I was going to stop it. I just had to figure out how to do it.

The next few minutes past in a blur. Basically, I knocked over my Lego shelf onto the Stromling's head, then continually hit it on the head with a copy of Lord of the Rings. I continued slamming the book up and down onto the floor long after the Stromling dissolved.
Suddenly, my mom walked into my room."What's happening here!" she asked.
I was about to answer when she turned into a Stromling.

I woke up. I had been having a nightmare. I cleaned up my room. This bit was very boring. (One of the reasons that the Nightfall Incident was so popular was because of the action. This, the faithful parody, will instead skip the action and head onto the boring bits.)
So, I picked up my shelf, rearranged all the stuff on it, picked up my pillow, picked up my lamp, hid everything that was broken in the closet, and went back onto the computer.
Soon, the boring bit was over, and the interesting bit began- I grabbed a mop and cleaned up the purple stains on my carpet.
Soon, I was hopping back onto the computer, where I typed this thing up, making it short so it would be easy to read.
"Hey, I just survived a Stromling Attack!" I said in the chat box, and realised how lame it made me sound.
"Well, we all seemed to have at least one attack", said SamuelGolderstein. "But that doesn't make sense.", he continued. "That means that about only half of us survived. But there are way less of us here then that."
"Maybe there are a few people still in this game,playing, avoiding Maelstrom. Maybe we can-" Milligan was cut off at that point. "Dragon Attack!" He typed as on last message before disconnecting. Suddenly it was just EmperorFred and I, alone at sea, floating on a rubber duck.
We weren't going to be alone much longer though...

Well, the Kraken seemed to be less like a Pirate's version, but more like a squid from one of the Atlantis sets, a fact supported by the Shark and Manta Men surrounding it.

Well, it might look cool, but it was definitely a unfair battle for two Minifigures against one. So, it was just the two of us, against the rest of the Maelstrom Armada and their Undersea Allies. Only a Deus Ex Machina could save us now.
So, really, it was a great time for a bunch of Minifigures with Jet Packs and Flame-throwers to randomly show up.

I woke up. I had been having a nightmare. I cleaned up my room. This bit was very boring. (One of the reasons that the Nightfall Incident was so popular was because of the action. This, the faithful parody, will instead skip the action and head onto the boring bits.)
So, I picked up my shelf, rearranged all the stuff on it, picked up my pillow, picked up my lamp, hid everything that was broken in the closet, and went back onto the computer.
Soon, the boring bit was over, and the interesting bit began- I grabbed a mop and cleaned up the purple stains on my carpet.
Soon, I was hopping back onto the computer, where I typed this thing up, making it short so it would be easy to read.
"Hey, I just survived a Stromling Attack!" I said in the chat box, and realised how lame it made me sound.
"Well, we all seemed to have at least one attack", said SamuelGolderstein. "But that doesn't make sense.", he continued. "That means that about only half of us survived. But there are way less of us here then that."
"Maybe there are a few people still in this game,playing, avoiding Maelstrom. Maybe we can-" Milligan was cut off at that point. "Dragon Attack!" He typed as on last message before disconnecting. Suddenly it was just EmperorFred and I, alone at sea, floating on a rubber duck.
We weren't going to be alone much longer though...
Axolc
XT

Of course, the dragon attack that they were experiencing in Nimbus Station was of Butterscorch, and another, and another, and another, and another. Of course, I didn't know this at the time, because I was in Gnarled Forest, floating along with EmperorFred on a rubber duck.

Suddenly, I saw something new. Before, my computer screen had shown an endless blue sea. But now, we had finally reached the end of it. Looking back, I could still see the Gnarled Forest coastline in he distance. Or rather, I could see the swarm of purple of the Maelstrom invasion there. It seemed that the duck wasn't the fastest water transport available. The Duck finally bumped into an invisible wall.
"So,uh, Fred, what do we do now?" I asked.
"Maybe we should just sit and wait", he replied.
Luckily, there was a small rock out near the border, so me and Fred could go and get off the duck for a while. "If there's a battle, I might need to summon something, and this most likely won't help." He explained.
"But what if we need to run?" I asked.
"Uh..." Apparently he hadn't thought about that.
Of course, we didn't have to wait long before something happened.
"How did I know this was going to happen!" I said.
We were in a ocean. And under that ocean, was the unfinished Atlantis world, with all of the guardians of it pointing their tridents at us.
But that wasn't the worst bit. The worst bit was the Kraken.
Axolc
XT

Well, the Kraken seemed to be less like a Pirate's version, but more like a squid from one of the Atlantis sets, a fact supported by the Shark and Manta Men surrounding it.
This at least explained one thing. The area we had entered was not a glitch world; but actually an unreleased area, dedicated to Atlantis.
Unfortunately, that meant that we hadn't gotten away from all Maelstrom enemies. Well, actually, they weren't technically Maelstrom at all. They looked almost exactly like their Minifigure counterparts, even though the actual squid was exempt from this, as it had a purplish glow surrounding it.
Me and Fred prepared to fight with our ruined gear. But it looked like a tough fight. Each Shark and Manta was armed with a trident, and there were about 10 of each. Then there was the Kraken itself. Each tentacle would probably be like fighting a Stromling Admiral.
Of course, it seemed like the Mythrans had been spawning more previously unseen enemies. It seemed logical that Pirates should get pirate ships, right? Well, it might look cool, but it was definitely a unfair battle for two Minifigures against one. And there was more than one. there were about four or so, each swarming with Stromling Pirates and Admirals. Luckily, there were no Apes. That would just be weird.
So, it was just the two of us, against the rest of the Maelstrom Armada and their Undersea Allies. Only a Deus Ex Machina could save us now.
So, really, it was a great time for a bunch of Minifigures with Jet Packs and Flame-throwers to randomly show up.

(Sorry for the re-post, but I made a spelling mistake.)
Jet-Packs are amazing things. They allow you to swoop through the air, go as high as you like. They are beautiful things, and, when taken from fiction by inventors and made in the real world, historians in the future will use then to demonstrate a shift into a new technological age, one where dreams and technology combine to make a great new society.
Another amazing thing is that in the previous paragraph, I did not use the word awesome at all, when "Jet-Packs? AWESOME!!!!" would send the exact same message.
Of course, in a two weeks or so the Mythrans were going to announce the closing of Lego Universe, and Jet-Packs were to become a common sight in the game. Even more obviously, I did not know that fact, so instead, the message I put into the Chat Box was "Jet-Packs? AWESOME!!!!"
And I also haven't even talked about the flame-throwers yet. They of course were useful in setting fire to the ships. But that didn't even matter. The rest of the chapter didn't matter after the inclusion of JET_PACKS! I was so excited (Done!) that I fainted.
