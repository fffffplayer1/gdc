“Wow”, breathed Prophet. Apparently the Mythrans had updated it since he had last come. But still, even I, without knowledge with what it had been like before, was stunned.
The first thing that caught my eye was the feature in the center, rising up high, dwarfing all other features of the crater. It rose up from a machine, nestled into a crack, so you barely noticed it was there. It rose, huge and sapphire, looking like the Imagination Nexus, only without its protective column taken away, so it spiraled up into the sky, swishing back and forth mysteriously, indistinguishable shapes swirling around, never fully formed, all the ideas that it had yet to be made real, thoughts of its own.
Far away, up away into the darkened sky, I could just make out a huge sphere, which rotated around, like an eyeball, never looking in the same place, searching around. Then as it turned towards me, I recoiled; it was actually literally, a giant eye. The iris was a slightly different shade, more azure than anything else. The most central area, the cornea, was a pure, sparkling diamond, with a small blue pulse racing around inside, directing the entire huge ball of an eye around. It seemed harmless enough, but for some reason, I felt a sense of malice coming off it, so I crouched down, and pulled Prophet down with me, until it had rotated away again. As soon as it moved away again, I dared to poke my head over the rim of the crater again, wondering what was happening.
My gaze continued down the side. It went down level by level, each cutting off suddenly like a giant flight of stairs. I looked around, and saw it was like that, each step continuing all the way around the circle, so it wasn’t actually stairs, more like an amphitheater. It wasn’t very deep, but went around for a very long way.
But the thing, when I looked that captivated me the most was what was in the center. And, as I looked around, I began to see what was happening here. Something terrible.

Back in the real world- well, the real fake world- or maybe like the world inside the world that happened to also be the world the story was in, but the world was fictional, and the story was not- back in the game, all of us were starting to fall asleep, some of us probably in the real world too. The fact was, even though Caesar was a rather good author, it really didn’t matter with description when you are typing it word by word into a chat box. I at least had been staying up half the night, waiting for him to finish. Occasionally, somebody would try to post a short comment to try and tell him to get on with it, but he just continued on, regardless. But now, even Milligan, who had an incredible amount of patience, was beginning to get tired. Any second now, my mom was going to walk into my room and ask me what I was still doing on the internet at- I checked my watch- 11pm.
It was StarchyStoicTurbine who finally broke the silence. “WHEN ARE YOU GOING TO FINISH!?” He typed in capital letters, his patience at end, like the rest of us.
“Yeah”, said EmperorFred, and left it at that.
Caesar got rather mad at that. “Do you realize how hard I’m trying to make this sound realistic? Well, if you want it fast, then I’ll tell it to you fast! Real fast!”
“If you are, then tell us already!” I said, amazed that this was the first time I had had any dialogue since chapter 4 or 5.
“Then” he said, and I imagined him taking a big breath, “The Mythrans are assembling a big army of licensed characters from the Lego video games, and they are going to use them to take over Lego Universe because they are tired of it being a kid’s game, but then they sent them all after us, and ProphetOfDoom was smashed by Jar Jar Binks-“
It seemed that EmperorFred was a fast reader, because he said straight away “Jar Jar Binks? Why him? Why didn’t they send somebody like the Joker or Boba Fett something if their point was that the game was too childish?”
“I didn’t really get it either” Said Caesar. “All that I know is that he had laser vision, and was breathing fire. Maybe it was some other Gungan?”
“It could be an overly complex reverse psychology move” Offered Milligan.
“Was there anything I forgot to mention?” Caesar asked himself.’ Let’s see: Giant army, licensed characters, Prophet, Jar Jar Binks- No, that’s all.”
That instant, a Stromling burst into my room.

“That’s it!” Exclaimed CaesarOctavius, and I imagined he was doing facepalm. “The Mythrans also implanted a virus that would show a video that would turn a player into a Stromling in the real world!”

“I have no idea,” Caesar admitted, “Though it probably has something to do with Lego Friends.”
It really was all interesting and all, but by now, I wasn’t anywhere near the computer, because A STROMLING HAD JUST ENTERED MY ROOM. Another mystery was who it was, because I didn’t know anyone in my neighborhood that played Lego Universe. Maybe it had dragged itself halfway across town, just to smash me. Did that mean that they could also home into players or something? It didn’t really matter, because it was advancing, forcing me into a corner.
To buy me some time, I picked up the chair I had been sitting on, and hurled it across the room, which knocked the Stromling back momentarily. Using my time, I had another glance at the computer, and franticly typed “Help Stromling attack advice needed!”
Already the Stromling was getting back up. It was pretty fast, and seemed to have more than one health point.
Suddenly, Milligan was on. “I got one too, try a textbook or knife.”
Unfortunately, I was not in a kitchen, so I was knifeless, and, as I had tried to explain to my school teachers many times, I had no idea where my textbooks were. I was going to have to make do.
The Stromling was advancing again, swinging its claws. I picked up my pillow and used it as a shield. I also picked up my lamp and started to batter it on the head. Dropping the lamp, keeping the severely slashed pillow blocking the Stromling’s blows, I backed myself away to my Lego Shelf.
My Lego Shelf was where I kept all my old Legos. Mostly, there was old Star Wars, a lot of Bionicle, and a few other bits and pieces. In recent times, the dominating feature of it was the Lego Mindstorms set. My current project was a firing mechanism, which would fire small spheres at things. Unfortunately, it was currently broken after an incident involving my little sister, our dog, and badly placed pillow.
But I knew how I was going to stop it. I just had to figure out how to do it.
