Chapter 4
The ancient elevator creaked and groaned as it inched its way toward the surface. I tapped my foot impatiently. I was so close to getting revenge on the Nexus Force I could taste it. Proud Duke Exeter, whose face was plastered all over the city proclaiming the glories of the Nexus Force would be in the public eye again very soon…In a casket. The rickety elevator finally shuddered to a halt at the surface, and I hopped out. It was nighttime in the new city, and the only people about were some sleepy night-shift faction vendors and a few drunken Venture League staggering back to the barracks. Perfect for stealthy travel. I leaped into a car parked nearby, hotwired it with the speed of experience, and took off for Nexus City. I flipped through the channels on the radio, and finding nothing worth listening to, settled in for the drive. A few hours later, the gleaming skyscrapers of Nexus City hove into view in the pre-dawn light. I took a small, little-used exit that led deep into the city. High above me, in transparent tubes, the city started to come to life, as the early-shift workers streamed to their cubicles and cash registers. The gleaming, towering city above was not for me. Here in an eerie twilight formed by the shadows of the skyscrapers, a maze of alleyways, service doors, and narrow, twisting roads, I made my way toward the crown jewel of Nexus City, Nexus Tower. The tower stood tall in the early morning light, a monument to what once was. Once a symbol of hope, it was now a symbol of oppression. As I contemplated what once was and is now lost, the engine sputtered and died. I glanced at the gas gauge and cursed. The tank must have had a leak, and now I had to walk the rest of the way. I had made it about halfway to the tower’s service entrance when I felt an arm around my neck, and a blade in my back. “Don’t move a muscle,” a voice growled.

Chapter 9
I awoke the next morning feeling oddly rested. So much more so, that I decided to explore the area a little. The cave would not serve for too long, as the Nexus Force was sure to be after me. With that in mind, I set out towards a large rock ridge about half a mile in front of me. My arm was protesting fiercely, but the pain was not debilitating, as it was yesterday. I trudged on towards the ridge, climbing steadily, if slowly. I gained the top and looked down at a huge, stomach-turning chasm. From underneath a heap of memories, understanding flooded my brain. I was looking at Rivendark Canyon, one of the few areas that remained unexplored by the Nexus Force. There were so many legends about Rivendark Canyon that it was hard to know what was real. What I did know, though, was that it was deep. Really, really, really deep. A faint shout drifted up from below, and I quickly pressed my body against the rocks. My injured arm banged against a rock in the process, and stars filled my vision as I struggled to remain conscious against the pain. When I regained control of my senses, I heard the bits and pieces of a conversation carried up to me by the howling wind.
“…want to go down there. I’ve heard so many…”
“…afraid? Those are just legends told to…”
“Ok, but if we… it’s on your...”
I cautiously peeked over the edge and saw two explorers, obviously creeped out by the prospect of going into a dark, deep canyon inhabited by who-knows-what. I resisted the urge to let out a ghastly shriek, and watched them inch their paranoid way down into the blackness. More bits and snatches floated up to me.
“…creeps!”
“…legends…”
“... not helping!”
At that moment both me and them were startled by a deep, sinister voice that seemed to come from the darkness of a tomb.
“Legend? Foolish mortals. I am no legend. SEE AND TREMBLE BEFORE MY POWER!!”
A huge blast of dark bluish-black energy soared out of the pit with a mighty roar. Or was the roar what caused the blast? A charred helmet I recognized as one of the explorer’s landed next to me. My body, ever alert to shifts in power, sensed a deep and dark force gathering strength in the black maw of the canyon below. I wanted to look away, but found my eyes glued to the scene by some subconscious force. As I watched, a dark being rose from the depths and floated towards the edge. A voice that I recognized as the one that preceded the blast came from the figure.
“Fools. They do not heed the warnings of their forerunners. Now I shall rule this universe. And they thought last time was hard. I am even more powerful now.”
The voice caused shivers of dread to go through my body. This being, whatever it was, was enormously powerful, and enormously evil. The malice radiating from it was terrifying, even to me, the ruthless assassin. It halted, and turned towards my position. I knew that a being of that power would destroy me instantly, so I took the boldest course of action. I rose to my feet, and called out in a slightly shaky voice, “Hail, mighty one. I sense your power, and wish to serve you in your conquest.” The being looked at me with glowing red eyes and spoke.
“You are bold for a mortal, and I sense great potential in you. I need such as you in my army. I accept your allegiance. Come. I am Emperor Zolmar.”
I slid down from the rocks, and followed Emperor Zolmar into the canyon, into the heart of his domain.

THE END. OR IS IT THE BEGINNING?
