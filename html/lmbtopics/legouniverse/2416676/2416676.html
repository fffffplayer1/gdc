Greetings, reader! I hope you enjoy my story! Presenting...

Cold Hard Steel

Chapter 1
I slid along the rooftops, melting into the shadows like a ghost. Below me, in one of the slums of Nimbus Station, the bar door burst open, spilling light, raucous laughter, and four rank three buccaneers who looked like they’d had one too many Notion Potions into the street. I viewed them with distaste as they called a slurred “Goodnight” to their friends and lurched down the alley, then glided along the eaves to a position in the alley in front of the buccaneers. As I looked at them, laughing and singing like they hadn’t a care in the world, the streetlight, which had previously been trying valiantly to stave off the darkness, sputtered and died. I smirked. This would be even easier in the darkness, not that four rank three buccaneers would be a problem for me, even under normal conditions. They were almost under me now, and I readied for action, drawing my sword from its sheath on my back. This would be a piece of cake. I had expected more from Hael Storm’s son, honestly. He had been as easy to follow as if he was giving me directions, and then he was so nice as to drink himself into a stupor. The group of pirates was underneath me now. I waited until they were just ahead, then dropped lightly into the alley below. Reagan Storm must have heard me, because he started to turn around, then tripped on a rock and fell. That saved his life, for the moment. Just as he fell, I brought my sword into a sweeping slice, right at neck level. His companions never knew what hit them. As they fell, I was already turning to Reagan. He fumbled for his pistol, but his reflexes were so slow that he had barely found it when I hit him. One stab. I sheathed my sword and jumped onto the roof, then melted into the shadows. A shout from the alley told me that a passerby had found the bodies. I cursed. That soon after smashing them, he could have seen me. I couldn’t afford any uncertainty. A throwing knife to the neck silenced him forever. I never liked smashing civilians, but sometimes it couldn’t be helped. A siren wailed behind me, and I knew it was time to go. I vanished into the shadows, working my way towards my lair. This was the way I rolled. Appear, smash, and vanish without a trace.

KrillMathias
The Iron Hand of the Swords of Light

Chapter 2
Outside, the sounds of traffic made their way through the apartment’s cardboard-thin walls. I relaxed on my couch, watching a documentary on the fall of the Maelstrom. The Nexus Force finally destroyed the Maelstrom at its heart, and the fringes collapsed. The overjoyed citizens made the faction leaders the rulers of the new Nexus Force. Big mistake. The faction leaders, bored and power-hungry, so oppressed the citizens that hardly a day went by without hearing of a new rebellion, then hearing on the news at night that it had been crushed by Nexus troops. As the narrator’s droning voice went on about the glories of the Nexus Force, I finally had enough and turned to the news. I never could stand watching state-sponsored documentaries. I knew the real story. I had been a proud and loyal Paradox member, always in the thick of the fight. When the Maelstrom was destroyed, no one had been more excited than me. Then the faction leaders revealed their true nature. I became thoroughly disgusted with the Nexus Force, and quit. I decided then and there that I would see the fall of the Nexus Force if it was the last thing I did. My thoughts were interrupted by a news anchor, who, with an expression of shock on his face, was saying, “This just in… One of the Nexus Force soldiers who were victims of a brutal murder in the slums of Nimbus Station has been confirmed to be Reagan Storm, son of Hael Storm. Authorities have not specified why he was in that part of town, and the name and description of the assassin are unknown at this time. More news as it comes in. Now in the build competition earlier today…” I turned off the TV and indulged in a satisfied smirk. Four less Nexus troops, and one heart-broken faction leader. Not bad for a night’s work.

Chapter 3
I strolled casually along the street. This was the dark side of Nimbus Station, the part that most people don’t see. The Nimbus Station that most minifigures know was actually built on top of the old NS. A smuggler’s den in its heyday, the old NS didn’t change its ways one bit. Here in the eternal darkness in the center of the city, crime was rampant, and you had to watch your back and your wallet, if you didn’t want to lose both. This was my home. I grew up in this city, watched it grow and fall. I saw the Assembly place the first brick on top of the old NS. One more reason I hated them. I was brought back to the present by a scream from an alleyway. I hardened my heart and walked on. This happened all the time, nothing unusual. I was headed to my old stomping grounds, the broken shell of the Brickmart factory. I had a pickup to make. The blackened hulk of the factory loomed out of the darkness ahead. Streetlights bathed the street behind me in an everlasting twilight as I made my way into the shadows of the factory. “Eternal darkness,” a voice called from behind a stack of boxes. “Is our lot,” I replied, as I picked my way over the rubble. “Hey, Krill, is that you?”
“Of course, brickhead, unless you’ve been telling all your pals the passcode,” I replied.
“I haven’t, just making sure. This factory gives me the willies!”
“Like darkness isn’t normal for you?”
“You know what I mean!”
“Yeah, sure. You have the package?”
“Got it right here. You know how hard it was to get this?”
“Just give me the package, you’ll get what’s coming to you.”
I had made my way over the rubble, and into the stack of crates where my contact was hiding.
“It’s right under that crate.”
“Just like you to put it somewhere I can’t keep an eye on you when I get it, then stab me in the back, and keep the gun and the money. You pull it out.”
“Ok, ok, don’t be hasty. Sheesh.”
Grumbling, he gingerly pulled a box out from under a crate. I opened the box, and pulled out a gleaming black sniper rifle.
“Looks like what I paid for. Now where’s the ammo?” I asked.
“Right under this crate. I’ll get it, don’t want you to get mad.”
As he leaned over to grab the ammo, I unsheathed my sword. He heard the slight ring, and instinctively put his hands up to protect himself. Precious lot of good that did him. In the old city, you had to be ruthless. He learned too late not to trust anyone, especially when money was involved. As I walked out of the factory with a free sniper rifle on my back, I looked up at the floor of the new city and shook my fist. Their time would come soon. I started out toward my lair again, with a little swagger in my steps. In a city that was bad to the bone, I prided myself on being the baddest of the bad.


KrillMathias
The Iron Hand of the Swords of Light
