Now with that out of the way...

Chapter 3

December 4, PE 5
“...the shipment arrive on time?” The voices floated down the hallway, drawing nearer as the sources approached. “Yes, the ship just passed Certay 3. They ran into a Void patrol vessel on the way, though. The captain is nervous that they may be being followed.”
“Dear Mythrans. If the Void found our base…”
“Don’t worry, Admiral, the captain is one of our best. I’m sure they shook that Void vessel.”
The speakers came around the turn, headed for the main hall. As they did, a soldier in gleaming steel armor came up alongside.
“Sir, the shipment just arrived. There’s someone you might want to talk to on board.”
“Thanks, soldier. Tell him and the captain to meet me at the main hall.”
“Yes sir!” The soldier ran away to inform the captain.
“Looks like they shook it, Admiral. The captain never would have come here if he thought he was followed.”
“I hope you’re right, Ferno. I hope you’re right.”

December 4, PE 5
PRED-362 “Darkfire”
“Captain, the vessel we were tracking is engaged in surface activity on Selitos 64-B. Type unknown, docking suspected. I can’t see for the cloud cover, do you want to launch a drone?”
“No, don’t want to let them know we were following them. Move out of intercept range and send a message to the Black Diamond. Tell Krill we found their base. We may just get promoted for this, lieutenant.”


December 4, PE 5
Ferno Firetype strode purposefully down the hallway. The ragtag collection of knights he had assembled was gaining strength rapidly, but still far inferior to the Void. This shipment that had just come in was carrying a load of weapons and troops that might just bring his unit up to fighting strength. He wanted to review the troops, but that would have to wait. The messenger had said an important person had been on that ship, and he wanted to find out who it was. Ferno opened the doors of the main hall and entered. The cathedral-like room soared above him, engulfing any noise in its stillness. A ceremonial hall built by mysterious beings long ago, it was still in pristine condition when they found the complex. At the far end, the captain was seated at a table, next to a robed figure. Ferno sat across from the robed figure. “Well, you didn’t come to this remote corner of the universe to admire the architecture. What did you want?” The robed figure smiled knowingly.
“As impatient as your name suggests. Fire has always been an impetuous element. But I suppose you don’t want to listen to a self-improvement talk, so I will be brief. I am the Archmage of the City of the Sun. I head an order of mages that has gained quite some power. We have heard of your struggle against the Void Empire, and come to offer our aid.”
“So you want to join us? I would welcome the help, but how do I know I can trust you?”
“You will have to trust a little longer. All will be made clear soon. Do you accept my offer?”
“I accept. Welcome to the Knight Order.”
