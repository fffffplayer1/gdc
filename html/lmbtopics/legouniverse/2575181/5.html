This one is for LEGO Universe.


Overbuild grappled with Sutoro. He called out to Vanda, "Didn't forget me, did you?!" And commanded his robot arm to slash Sutoro with its chainsaw.
Then Assembly warriors began pouring from the trees. Engineers, Summoners, Inventors, and all kinds of others. Then, from the collapsed dirt wall, Maelstorm began emerging. They were held on three small legs, creeping across the ground slowly. They had large, rounded bodies, and small heads. Three arms protruded it strange angles from their bodies, one a scythe, one a blaster, and another a wicked-sharp claw.
Then they all stopped collectively and began rolling. They popped into themselves like turtles, then threw themselves forward and began moving at an insane speed. An Engineer yelled and five others activated their Personal Fortresses. Machineguns began pelting the creatures, but the bullets bounced off their bodies. They unfolded and their blasters began firing, energy bolts aimed at the Fortresses. However their effort resulted similarly to the Engineers'.
The Summoners let Thwoks loose, which began tearing apart the black-clad Maelstorm figures. Then the Inventors created the Universe's largest bee swarm, stinging and poisoning the figures and the creatures.
Vanda stood up and ran past the three-legged creatures. They ignored her. She stopped at the dirt wall and scrabbled the dirt away, hoping to find one of her friends. She struck something hard and took away more dirt, and found Extreme lying there. His helmet was sparking, and his Wormholer lay, a twisted wreck, by his side. His armor was damaged and paint scraped off in whole patches, exposing dull gray metal. A few scattered bombs lay around his shoulderpads, and a pad was broken off, exposing a broken squad-missile launcher. His arm was twisted painfully.
"Extreme..." she said. "What have they done to you?"
There was a slight movement from him, and then his helmet shifted. Only part of his visor was glowing, but he spoke. "I... I..." He coughed. "I'll... be f-f-fine... I can... mak-make it... find... the others..."
He shifted his head in a tiny nod, and Vanda cleared away more of the dirt to let him have more space. "T-thanks..." he said, and Vanda moved off.
She searched more, and found Shade. His face was covered in dust, and his eye closed. His sleeves were torn, and his arm was twisted like Extreme's. His breath was shallow.
"Vanda...?" he asked.
"I'm here," she said. "What did you do to deserve this?"
"I... was a... a Guardian... Ap-apparent... ly... that was... a good rea-reason... for him... but go... I'll... onl-ly bring you... dan-nger... find Meyer..."
Vanda reluctantly went to search for the remaining two. After several minutes, she uncovered a Sorcerer hat. "Thenso," she said, searching more. She soon found him, buried in dirt. He had a black eye, and his shirt had a huge burn mark on it. His leg was at an unnatural angle.
"Your creative spark!" she said. "It's damaged."
Thenso opened his eyes. "I... know," he said, coughing. "I can... can repair it... my... self..."
"You need help. Asssembly's here."
"I don't... don't need Assembly... go now. Find... Meyer..."
Vanda nodded and moved off. She knew the tree had fallen on him. She thrust her hand toward it, and threw out a blast that exploded, melting it down and throwing wood shards everywhere. She soon uncovered Meyer.
His armor was dented in every spot, and Maelstrom spots were around his neck where Sutoro had held him. He showed no signs of movement, and his breathing was shaky and kept breaking.
"Why? Why did he do this to you?"
Then Meyer coughed and opened his eyes weakly. "Vanda..." His voice was low and barely audible. "G-go... he... he'll come and... fin-finish... me..."
"I'm not going," Vanda objected.
"Leave me... I've... I've had a good run... at least... least now you know... I've... I've always... loved you..." Meyer closed his eyes.
"No!" Vanda yelled. "I am not leaving you. You're coming with me."
Then a voice came from behind them. "I... second that."
Vanda turned around to see Thenso standing, clutching his arm.
"Thenso!" Vanda cried. "You fixed your spark!"
"I... told you I could do... it." He looked at Meyer. "What'd he... do to you? I never... liked the guy."
Meyer let out a tiny smile. "I'm... done... here... I can't... be saved now..."
"Doctor Overbuild's here," Vanda said.
"He... can't save... me..."
Then there was a victorious yell and a chain of explosions. Then Doctor Overbuild landed heavily beside them. "Sorry... I failed..."
Sutoro trudged toward them, holding only one katana. Behind him, the figures and creatures were destroying the Assembly forces. "I'm sorry, Doctor," he said mockingly. "Your experiment failed."

Swinging has katana casually, Sutoro walked over to them. Overbuild had one robot arm missing, a fresh, long scratch right across his chestplate, and looked battered in general. "My combat capabilities... are limited," he said apologetically to Thenso, Meyer and Vanda. "I held out... as long as I... could, but I can't... can't take him myself."
Vanda nodded, accepting his apology, as Sutoro spoke up again. "Stop with all your technological speak, Doctor. It does you no good here." He turned his attention to Meyer, Thenso and Vanda.
"Ah, the Sorcerer. I thought I toasted you. I've never liked you anyway, since I met you, which, by the way, was when you shot me."
"Hey, that wasn't my fault! You were the one infected at the time."
"And yet you don't shoot me now?"
"You never gave me a chance."
Ignoring Thenso, Sutoro turned to Vanda. "I believe I've already consulted you, however the others were unconscious at the time. Well, here it is again. I used to trust you. You were my leader, I would've easily placed my life in front of you like a shield. I was foolish back then. The Darkitect taught me that my life was precious. Others' lives - such as Stromlings and other mindless zombies - are disposable, to be thrown at your enemies in sheer numbers. Numbers... yes, the Darkitect taught me about those too. In my trials, I fought some Stromlings. I said they were useless, and then he sent out thirty. I was mauled. Numbers are everything. Alone, a Stromling is nothing, but sheer numbers make them powerful." He gestured toward the Tekuillien elites behind him. "These, however, are good. Even alone, they pose a challenge. In numbers..." He laughed, "...well, I call it 'interesting'."
He turned to Meyer, and his expression turned to rage. "You. You... I have much to say. I wouldn't say it in front of the lady here, of course*." Vanda glared at him. "But, I must compliment you on surviving my weakest blows. I threw you into a hard-packed wall which collapsed on you, then I dumped a tree on you. Most people are smashed from that effort. However, I can do much more. I've never really extended my power more than medium range, when I fought against ten Butterscorches in my trials. Ah, good times. Now, my life is boring. No challenges."
Meyer looked at Thenso, and understanding flowed through them. They formed a plan.
"Sutoro," Meyer spoke up suddenly
"Hmm?" Sutoro said, not even glancing at him.
"I propose a duel."
Sutoro laughed. "You think you can defeat me? In the state you're in? Even at full energy, you're no match for me. I -"
Suddenly something hit ihm over his head. He only had time to mutter, "What the heck..."
Then he collapsed, unconscious. Behind him was a Stromling in black clothing, with futuristic weapon in his hand. It said, "Don't overestimate yourself."
As he said it, his voice changed, and his appearance changed too. He became less and less infected, until he was just a normal minifigure - one who the other recognized. It was Yonder.
The three just stood - and sat, for Meyer - there, unbelieving.
"Hello," he said. "I followed you."
Then Thenso began laughing. He kept laughing for several seconds, until he said, "That was brilliant! Almost as good as when I blew him sideways on Crux Prime!"
Then Sutoro stirred. He leapt up, and, with pure rage, kicked Yonder in the chest. He flew backwards and landed in the dirt.
"Ouch," Thenso said. "Meyer. It's time..."
"Right," he said.
Sutoro yelled. "WHOEVER YOU ARE," he screamed at Yonder, "I WILL DEAL WITH YOU AFTER I'M DONE WITH THESE!"
He sounded ridiculous, yelling at an unconscious person in a calm tone. He turned to Meyer, Vanda and Thenso.
"Now," he said. "The duel?"
"Do you accept?" Meyer asked.
"Why, of course. Your end will be swift."
"I don't think so," Thenso said. "Meyer, now!"
Then they joined hands and Theno let his healing energy drain into Meyer. After a few seconds, Thenso fainted and fell to the ground. Meyer stood. He walked over to where Shade lay.
"Shade," he said.
"I'm... here," Shade said. "What's... your plan?"
"May I use your sword?"
"Wield it with honor."
"I will." Meyer picked up Shade's fallen longsword gratefully. He walked over to Sutoro.
"Alright," Meyer said. "I'm ready."
Sutoro grinned. "You're bound to fail."

* What I really mean is that it wouldn't be appropriate for the LMBs.
