His next strike on them had failed. Unfortunate, and they hadn't even tired Meyer. Meyer... he hated that name. It made him fume. The guard, stealing all the attention from Vanda, and he wwent green with envy when he found out Meyer had got Vanda's death mark. He would smash him soon.
***
Vanda, Thenso, Extreme and Shade continued through the forest. Thenso had repaired his harpoon gun overnight, and had it slung over his back. Extreme was checking all his laser grenades were on working order. Shade was figuring out what his maximum speed was with his healing leg. Vanda was thinking. About Meyer, and where he might be.
He might've actually obeyed her orders, or maybe she just shook him up so much he stayed back at the Tower. Or maybe he was following them.
Then... something, jumped out at them. It was black and purple, and had no visible face or defining features. It was just a blotch of purple and black, with strangely-shaped arms and legs.
It smashed into Vanda and claws snapped out from it's hands. The minifigures snapped into action. Shade jumped at it and lopped off it's hands, which began growing back. Thenso summoned a Maelstrom wind and blew it away from Vanda, and Extreme began blasting it to bits with his Wormholer. It evaporated.
Vanda jumped up. "Guys?" she said. "Behind you..."
They turned, and, only fifty meters away, was a charging army of the monsters.

Thenso began firing Maelstrom enegy balls at the monsters, blowing holes in the ground and sending them into the air. He infected the tiniest twigs of the trees, which then began to rain from the sky, spiking monsters.
Vanda unsheathed her katana and began to move forward, but then something grabbed her and she fell over. The next thing she knew, someone was dragging her away from the battle.
"Who are you?!" she demanded, trying to get out of the person's grip. "What are you doing?!"
"You know me," a familiar voice said. The person let go of Vanda and moved in front of her. The figure wore a cloak. He pushed back the hood and Vanda's eyes widened. The minifigure was Meyer.
Vanda jumped up and swung at him. He dodged and said, "Wait! Stop! I've been saving your life! I-"
He was cut off as Vanda swung again, just missing.
So Vanda attacked Meyer as they jumped aorund the trees, Meyer trying to persuade Vanda to stop. Extreme, Shade and Thenso battled the monsters.

Thenso, Extreme and Shade had cut down at least thirty of the monsters, but they just kept coming. Maelstrom splotches covered the ground. Trees were missing branches, which were impaled in the ground or in smashed monsters. Extreme was cutting through lines, and Shade was discovering his expertise with the longsword.
Meanwhile, Vanda was still attempting to smash Meyer. He was avoiding her attacks, and she was now firing energy blasts at him in an attempt to blow him up. Instead, she was destroying the forest and sending fireworks into the sky.
"Stop, please!" Meyer said, dodging another blast, which burst a tree into flames. "Can we talk about this before we're crushed by trees? Or blown up?"
Vanda just fired more blasts.
The battles went on for longer, longer - until they ran out of energy. Vanda was just too exhausted to keep making destruction, and fell down. Shade had tired from all the swordwork he was doing, and fell back behind Thenso and Extreme before he fell over.
Thenso was also tiring, in a similar to to Vanda, but before he too stopped, he called all the Maelstrom-infected branches he'd made and they went underground, pushing up the dirt until it made a five-meter wall in front of the monsters.
Extreme found his Wormholer running low on ammo, and it was growing heavy in his hands. He put it down and sat.
Meyer leapt from the trees and landed next to Vanda, who lashed out at him with her katana. Before she could slice him into pieces, Thenso flung the weapons from her hands.
"None of that," he said. "Did you even see what we were doing? We were battling an army of those things! And all you did was fight against each other, uselessly! Imagine what would've happened if Extreme, Shade and I hadn't been here? You would've been overrun long ago. You'd both be smashed!"
Vanda and Meyer looked down, feeling terrible.
"You cut them off," Extreme said, pointing at the wall of dirt.
"It won't last long," Thenso said. "They'll tear through it. I'm nearly out of energy. We're all tired. Vanda, Meyer, if you'd helped us, this would've been over and we'd be on our way by now! Why can't you just set aside your arguments?! This is a life or death situation!"
"Thenso," Meyer managed to say, "Vanda was trying to kill me. I would've helped, but if I stopped for a moment she would've roasted me alive."
"That's true," Thenso said, "but you should've improvised, negotiated-"
"Negotiated?" Meyer snarled. "I tried! I was trying the whole time! She wouldn't listen!"
Vanda's rage grew. She was being blamed for everything. It wasn't her fault, it was Meyer. He'd started all this. She finally popped. "It's all your fault!" she screamed at Meyer. "You've been following me, annoying me, fighting my battles, and I'VE JUST HAD ENOUGH!" She grabbed her katana and charged Meyer. He stood, showing no sign of defending himself, until she reached him. She knocked him over and swung her katana at his head.

He laughed. His plan had worked perfectly - he knew that Meyer couldn't resist coming to Vanda now. And then she'd tried to kill him while they left the others to tire fighting the useless army. And now he'd called them off, and they'd think the army would burst through any moments, run toward the city - and fall right into his trap.
At least, that was the originall plan. Now Meyer was going to get killed by her.
How ironic.
***
Time slowed down as the sword came toward Meyer. He thought, "My last thought is of her killing me. That's... horrible." He closed his eyes.
He waited a while. Then he realised he could feel his limbs, and the wind, and heard something. He opened an eye. He saw Vanda next to him, her katana lying next to her. She was sobbing.
The other three were standing around her, not really sure what to do. Meyer sat up. Vanda saw him. "I- I can't do it," she said. "I can't- I can't kill you..."
They sat for a while. A storm rolled in. The noise form behind the dirt wall faded. Rain came down.
Then Meyer said, "I couldn't kill you either."
The two minifigures looked at each other, and said at the same time, "I care too much."
Shade, Extreme and Thenso, freaked out, walked away to check on the dirt wall.
Meyer leaned forward, and kissed Vanda on the lips.

Sutoro ran through the forest. The elite Tekuillien group followed closely behind him on their hoverpads. Their angular heads gave them a menacing look, and they held a block and a curved sword in their hands.
Sutoro was maddened. Meyer had stolen her. Now it was time to end him.
***
There was a blast. Suddenly, another. A third.
Then a figure, in purple clothing and a rounded hat, with twin hook swords burst from the trees.
"Sutoro?" Thenso asked in wonder.
The fallen shinobi looked at him for one instant and opened his hand. A beam blasted out of it and hit the Sorcerer in the chest, throwing him into the dirt wall. Extreme raised his Wormholer, but Sutoro twisted his infected hand, and Extreme's arm twisted. He yelled in pain and fell to the ground.
Sutoro caught sight of the Guardian, Shade. The two instantly knew they were mortal enemies, created to do the opposite thing. Shade raised his sword. Sutoro unleashed a blast at Shade, but it was deflected off his sword. Sutoro scowled and twisted his arm, and Shade dropped the sword. Sutoro unleashed another blast, knocking Shade down.
The Tekuillien elites were circling Meyer and Vanda, torn from their kiss. Sutoro summoned a hoverpad and floated over to them and said, "Ah, well, isn't it the two who left me on the most dangerous place on Crux Prime to die. I saw you fly off, leaving me behind. Every days for months, I was taunted by the Tower's nexus so close, but impossibly far. The Stromlings taught me a valuable lesson: pain can be the path to realization. And I learned from it, and joined the Darkitect. And now I can finally be relased from the pain, by smashing you both."
"You couldn't," Vanda said.
Sutoro laughed. "Of course I could. What I can't do it believe that you went on to be happy together while I lay writhing. You didn't miss your vendor?"
"I was happy? With him?" Vanda asked in outrage. "I hated him! He always followed me, every single minute, fighting my battles, and never leaving me alone!"
"You are referring to your experience in past tense," Sutoro said. "Why has this changed? Now I am here? What is different? You don't believe he saved you, do you? Well, let me tell you, it is as true as I being able to smash you."
"It's not because of any of that. It's because... because..."
Sutoro laughed again. "No reason, eh? I highly doubt that. How about I have a second opinion..." He flicked his hand toward Meyer. He twisted his hand, and Meyer was thrown into the air and held there by his throat. "Tell me. Why does she not hate you now?"
"Because..." Meyer struggled for breath. "Because... I love her."
