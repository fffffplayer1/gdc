"Warm welcome," Meyer grumbled.
"Look at it this way," Coyote said. "If I weren't here, you'd be dead."
"You're not very optomistic."
"I'm as optomistic as I need to be."
They walked through the gaping gates into the city. Teknites were patrolling the area. Before the minifigures could do anything, the Stromlings each had one less eye. Coyote's rifle was smoking.
"Why do you always shoot them in the eye?" Thenso asked.
"It's my trademark," Coyote said. "Moving on. If I know Tekuillien patrol routes - and I do know them - this area won't be empty for long."
They moved forward until they reached a building. They went inside and shot down a lone Teknite.
"What are you doing here?" Coyote asked.
"We're here to save the Universe," Vanda said. "And we have two days to do it."
"What are you saving it from?"
"All this Maelstrom. The Darkitect is going to send it all at Nexus Tower in two days. Every last Teknite."
"Nexus Tower," Coyote said. "What's that?"
Everyone stared at him.
"You don't know about Nexus Tower?" Doctor Overbuild spoke up. "It's the home of the Imagination Nexus, the last shard of Pure Imagination."
"I'm sorry," Coyote said. "I don't know much about what you're saying."
"Why not?" Thenso said. "Surely you know something about the Nexus Force."
"I know little," Coyote said. "I was brought up by coyotes since a very young age. They don't care much for other issues in the Universe. They did tell me about the Maelstrom and the Darkitect, and told me to kill them on sight."
~ Brickmaster7946

"You were brought up by coyotes?" Extreme asked.
"Yes," Coyote said. "It is how I got my name."
"I would never have guessed," Meyer said sarcasticaly.
Vanda slapped him. "It makes no difference to us," she said forcefully, mainly to the other minifigures rather than Coyote. "Let's move on. We're saving the Universe here. No time to lose."
They worked their way to the top of the tower. There room had several Tekuillien workers on control panels. Coyote took aim, blowing a hole in one, where the laser continued to strike and smash another. Thenso used Dark Thunder, knocking the other down. Extreme blasted one and Overbuild threw an Imagination-infused brick at the last, incinerating it.
The room was cleared and they walked forward, inspecting the controls. Extreme went to the front of the room and found a red button.
"Hey, what does this do?" he asked. Then he pressed it.
Suddenly, the light flickered off. Every light. As in, the sun went out too. It was pitch-black.
"Extreme," Vanda said with barely contained anger. "What - did - you - do?"
"I, uh, pressed a red button?"
"Red button?" Thenso said. "Those are always bad."
Right on queue, small white lights flickered on. Everywhere. In the room, on the ground, in the other towers, in the... sky? And... on the trees. The trees!
"Those aren't real trees," Thenso said. "That's not a real sky... This isn't a planet. It's a space station."
~ Brickmaster7946
