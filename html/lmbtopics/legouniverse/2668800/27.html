Book 2 Chapter 1.3

The Queen’s attention was barely caught by their landing, but as Treem fired up the engines to leave, she turned over and her huge eyes focused on the dropship. “Get down!” screamed Reyna, who tackled Dwight from behind. Lex and Shay dove flat on the ground and all four of them crawled into a bush. Dwight pushed aside some shrubbery so he could see.

One of the Spider Queen’s upper cannons kicked back, and in a flash of light, a flaming missile, not only afire but seething with Maelstrom, rocketed toward the receding spacecraft. The Halberd weaved and spun, and the missile followed effortlessly until it lost its course and impacted on the ground with a spectacular explosion. The dropship escaped and flew off into the distance toward Avant Gardens. When the smoke cleared, the ground was scorched gray and Alpha Team was coughing miserably, trying to get smoke out of their systems.

Book 2 Chapter 1.4

“We have to fight that?” Shay whispered in utter disbelief. Dwight shuddered. The power of the Queen was evident now.

“I am calculating to make sure that if we make any mistakes larger than ripping our clothes we will be certainly smashed,” said Lex as he typed away on a miniature laptop. “Oh, look, guess what, guys? If we make any mistakes larger than ripping our clothes, we will be certainly smashed!” the Summoner proclaimed sarcastically.

Dwight wasn’t sure if his Shinobi Gear, even after going through its advancement procedure for Alpha Team duty, could handle that kind of incoming firepower. Reyna’s Knight Gear, maybe. Lex and Shay were seriously endangered, neither of their equipment being designed primarily for close combat. Shay’s Daredevil getup was protected a little more, but not enough.

Book 2, Chapter 1.5

A sudden voice behind them broke through their lament. “Alpha Team? No way! It’s really you!” A Sentinel cadet guard crawled through the bushes toward them.

“I’m Property Guard Vance Bulwark, at your service.” he gave a little bow, which was hard because they were all crouching. “When I saw the typhoon coming over and the Spider Queen entered the Property, I was the one who ran back and reported it. My fellow guards are unable to continue fighting, but I tried to send the Maelstrom away. It didn’t work.” He stuck out his left leg, which had a bandage wrapped around it that was stained slightly with a dash of purple.

“Spiderling bit me in the leg. Those mandibles they’ve got are sharper than they look.” The guard was wearing a Cadet helmet, sort of a crash helmet with two vanes swooping forward on either side of the chin. He had a black polarized visor that was positioned up so his face showed. He looked boyish, but older than even Lex. His armor was blue and light gray, and he dragged a Guardsman’s spear behind him.

“Dwight Lavamonster,” said Dwight, and shook Vance’s hand, which nearly resulted in them falling over. “We’re here on orders to smash the Spider Queen.”

“Can’t just let her go, can we?” Shay breathed exasperatedly. She hefted her Flarecannon and blasted aside the plants, leaping into the open and firing again and again at the Spiderlings. Lex summoned one of his creations, a baby stegosaurus, and climbed onto its back to ride into battle.

Dwight and Vance got up and followed Reyna into the fray. “Impressive,” said Vance. “Are you and she…”

“Not a good time!” yelled Dwight over the babbling guard. It was about then that the Spider Queen noticed them. And she was not happy with their intrusion.

A Really Good Time for Bug Spray

Every Spiderling on the property stopped and stared at them. The Alpha Team froze. For a peaceful moment, no one moved. Then, a strange noise came from the Spider Queen’s mandibles and one of her upper cannons glowed. A targeting reticle, located by Dwight’s HUD, lit up the ground beneath them.

“Mortar launch! Scatter!” he yelled. The group of minifigures leapt in all directions as the missile hit the ground where they’d been standing a second ago. And with that, the battle began.

Vance Bulwark hefted his spear and whacked a Spiderling with the blunt end, following up with a thrust to its underbelly. “The first smash of the fight,” he mused. He finally shut up when Reyna saved him from a second arachnid that was charging him from behind.

“We’ll hold off the nasties,” she cried. “The rest of you take the Spider Queen and we’ll join you in a minute!” She slashed at a trio of Spiderlings that got too close.

“No chance,” said Shay. “If we’re taking this bad girl, it’s together.” She primed her Flarecannon and fired a four-shot spread, clearing the area of Maelstrom save the Queen herself, who angrily hissed at Shay and scuttled forward, knocking the rifle out of her hands.

Lex summoned his ever-present Axe Mech and leaped on its shoulders. “Cease your violent involvement with our friend immediately!” he bellowed and smacked the Queen in the face with the flat of his scythe blade.

“Uh, my Lex filter isn’t too good, but I’m pretty sure he just said, ‘Leave her alone,’” Reyna remarked. The Spider Queen reeled back and shook her head confusedly, stunned by the Summoner’s attack.
