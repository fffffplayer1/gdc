Chapter 5.1
Shay
P.S. (This is the last chapter that intoduces a character.)

Shay Fuse ran as fast as she could. The big cat was after her, she was sure of it. A tiger had noticed her and thought she looked tasty, so now she was bolting through the trees and hoping it was slow. No dice. It was matching her speed, and she could feel its hot panting on the back of her neck.

She wheeled and fired a burst from her simple explorer’s flaregun. Its barking report echoed for miles around. The intense heart singed her ash blonde hair. The minifigures who wanted to tour the Exploration Safari Park all carried a flaregun, in case they got lost or were injured. Now she used it to put off an unwanted diner.

The tiger reared and hissed, backing away. Shay resumed running, checking behind when she’d gone about ten meters. The cat was following her a little hesitantly, more cautious now that it knew she had a weapon. Shay turned and began climbing a tree to gain some ground on her pursuer.

She was now up in the branches, where the tiger couldn’t see her, and also a good spot to look around and get her bearings. She continued climbing to the top, and surveyed the forest around her.

Shay’s grandfather had been in the Nexus Force, a Rank 3 Venture League Daredevil. He’d come home on leave only a couple of times after she was born, but she knew him well. Then, he’d gone MIA on Crux Prime and was never found. It’d been months now, but Shay wanted to join the Force and look for him. Worst-case scenario, he’d be dead, or infected. She hoped for neither.

Because of this, Shay hated the Maelstrom and everything to do with it. She even had a locket with a picture of a Stromling being smashed in it. She wouldn’t take any crap from some half-skeleton freak with a few purple clouds and a sword. But, back to the task at hand. Time to get out of here.

The Exploration Safari Park was an enclosed area that was a haven for wildlife and outdoorsmen and women alike. A minifigure wishing to roam around in forests and streams, away from civilization, could sign in at the Customer center, pay a small fee, and then they were given a pack with provisions and a flaregun and sent on their way.

Shay was a frequent visitor of the E-Park, but even then she hadn’t seen all of it. Right now she was in unfamiliar territory. She’d gone farther than ever and still wasn’t a little bit close to the edge. Some minifigures claimed that the E-Park’s land coverage rivaled that of Gnarled Forest, but Shay was skeptical. Gnarled Forest was its own planet, albeit a chunk of Crux.

Shay was in her late teens, living in an apartment near her parents’ house on Brick Island. She could take care of herself fine, and had been to the E-Park before, but she’d never encountered a tiger under these circumstances at those times.

The wildlife at the E-Park was monitored, but couldn’t really be controlled because of the expanse of land it covered. And the management wished to be humane, so just going in and smashing all of the animals was out of the question, Mythrans forbid. Who would want to do that, anyway?

Shay was supposed to be a Nexus Force Venture Leaguer, but her application, sent in weeks before, had gone unanswered as far as she could tell. She wondered how it was passing through. Nexus Tower was a long way away from Earth.

Suddenly, the air around her grew dark. She fell from the tree that was no longer there. Instead, she landed in a hard-floored room with no visible features except a glowing blue beam of light pulsating in the center. A voice whispered over the hum of the nexus, and it spoke troubling things.

“Adventurous and quick you are, but sometimes you explore too far. Don’t awake what should stay asleep, and your comrades’ safety you will keep. Fourth of four, finds the door.” Then it faded and she was in the tree once more. She sat a moment, contemplating what she’d heard. Then a birdcall startled her.

Chapter 5.2

Shay snapped back into reality and looked around. She could see the informational facility in the distance. It was far off, but close enough to pick out. The forest extended in all other directions, but she knew there was an end to it. So what to do? ‘Make for the Customer Center’, she decided. Better get started, then.

She dropped a couple of feet, and caught herself on a branch. So far, so good. Suddenly, the tiger burst from the surrounding trees, leaping up and swiping at Shay’s legs. Its claws left deep gashes in the bark of the tree inches away from her ankles.

Shay struggled to lift herself, but the attack had rattled her, broken her concentration, and she could not summon the strength to do so. She let her flaregun drop and brought her feet together as it fell, grabbing it.

Using her toes, she pulled the trigger, aiming the barrel straight downward. It was a good trick. The tiger yowled and sprang back, but only for a minute. It crouched and growled. Shay knew it was going to pounce. She braced herself for its claws ripping into her legs.

Something flew out of the trees and hit the tiger’s rump, sticking there. The big cat roared and shook it off, bounding into the undergrowth to nurse its wounds. Shay squinted to see what the object was. Huh? A letter opener?

Out of the blue, a souped-up Hyperstarter with four seats pulled into the clearing. A kid, maybe thirteen, was hanging out of the front, as if he’d thrown something from the vehicle. A girl of the same age was at the wheel, and two guys were in the back, one middle-aged, one college-age. The former was Duke Exeter.

Shay made an embarrassing squealing noise and dropped from the tree. She ran over. “Thanks for the save,” she said breathlessly. “Who threw the letter opener?”

The kid in front raised his hand. “I did,” he said sheepishly. Shay picked it up and handed it to him.

Duke laughed. “The kid’s good, no doubt. Paradox for him. Just give him some shuriken, tell him he’s a Shinobi, and the Baron will be next!” He picked up a notebook and scribbled in it with a pencil.

Shay was confused. “Wait, what?” Then she remembered: she’d sent out a Nexus Force application a week ago, but forgot after a while and today was recruiting day! How had they found her?

All in a second, the tiger leapt from the brush and charged growling at the car and Shay. She raised her flaregun, but there was no need. The girl in the driver’s seat flipped out of the car, grunted, and pulled off her door to use as a shield. She bashed the tiger with it, sending it yelping into the trees again.

Duke made some more notes in his book. “Shield bash. Good. Knight for her, maybe? Sentinel, definitely.” he mumbled to himself. Shay still didn’t understand what was going on. Then the tiger, a determined creature, returned, snapping the car door out of the girl’s hands and flinging it into the forest.

The second man in the back yelled and chucked a small pod of some sort at the tiger, which exploded in a puff on the ground. At first everyone looked at it weirdly, wondering what the point of it was, until a huge stegosaurus made of bricks rose out of it, bellowed, and stomped on the tiger, which smashed in a cloud of smoke, hopefully to rebuild somewhere else.

They all looked at the college guy. Duke made another note. “Assembly. Used big creation to fight. Summoner for him. Very nice.”
Shay lost her patience.

“Can someone tell me why you’re here, how you found me, and what this is all about?” Shay sputtered. The four minifigures stared at her. Duke spoke first.

“All right. I suppose you’re confused, so let’s start. You may want to sit down.” The girl who’d ripped off the door motioned to the seat she’d left open, so Shay sat.

They began with introductions. Dwight, Lex, Reyna-but then a story of questionable truth. It was preposterous, but Duke was a Faction Leader, and they’d just saved her, hadn’t they? And her vision… it had said something about comrades. And the blue light was familiar, and the voice and the rhymes-could it be real?

The inner explorer in Shay wanted in, so she said. “I’ll give it a try.”

Duke nodded. “Great. Venture League. Daredevil for you, then. I saw you with that flaregun. You’re a crack shot.” Shay grinned.

“Thanks,” she said. This was going to be fun. If they all made it out alive.

Chapter 6.1
The Warning

‘Duke’s private frigate is really nice,’ thought Dwight. They were aboard the latter heading for Nexus Tower. Its interior was padded and comfortable, fit for a king plus his family and dog, and could travel real fast. That’s why it would only take an hour to go to another galaxy altogether-and to its most remote planet, no less.

Right now, the other four minifigures on board were sitting on a leather couch in the main area, save the pilot, who was surrounded by soulless machines in the cockpit. The recruits and Duke were laughing and talking, and at one point Duke was chortling so hard that his Sentinel Super Soda came out of his nose.

Dwight considered joining them, but first he wanted to complete his letter to his mother and father. It went a little something like this:

Dear Mom and Dad,

I’m writing this from Mr. Exeter’s private ship on the way to the Crux galaxy. I am missing you both a lot. My teammates for the Alpha stream are nice and polite, and Duke isn’t as hardened and unfeeling as he wants people to think. But I digress.

I know you aren’t overjoyed about where I am, who I’m with, or what my destination is, but I’m doing what I want. And I still love you guys. I promise I’ll avoid trouble and I won’t get smashed, for you. I’ll write daily if I can.

Love,

Dwight

He finished typing now, and clicked send. There’d be a time delay of three or four minutes, and then the message would appear on the screen of his family computer where his parents were anxiously waiting at home. He swore to himself that he’d post at eleven twenty-six every morning, so it’d come by his house at eleven thirty, the time his mom always checked her e-mail.

He sighed, wiped the hot tears that had been running down his cheeks – against his will - off his face, and shut down the computer.
Then he walked over to the couch and sat, put his head in Duke’s lap, and fell asleep.

* * *

Duke looked down, surprised, and then turned his gaze upwards, to the other minifigures, helplessly. “Right when I have to take a bathroom break,” he whined. Lex and Reyna laughed. Shay spit out the milk she had in her mouth.

“You’re gonna make me choke!” she said, in between giggling fits. This only made the others laugh harder, except for Duke, who was trying to shake Dwight off without waking him up, twitching his legs repeatedly.

“Oh, for Pete’s sake,” he said. He picked Dwight up, held him high over his head, and rose from his seat. Then he put the sleeping thirteen-year–old back on the couch and ran to the lavatory. This, of course, provoked more laughing. It was amazing Dwight slept through it all.

* * *

Dwight was having a fitful sleep, not able to wake up as if he were buried under tons of blankets that he couldn’t get off of himself. He was sitting slumped up against a boulder on a bare cliff face, with no discernable surroundings whatsoever, except for a dark, starless atmosphere and shady mist below.

He got up, with effort, and trudged over to the edge of the cliff. It felt like his limbs were made of lead. When he looked down, he saw a cloud of purple Maelstrom seething with sick, poisoned energy racing up the sheer drop toward him. He fell back, crying out, looking for somewhere to run. He had no weapon, no armor.

He was frightened to discover that the boulder blocked of any escape route there could be. He backed up against it, shaking with fear while at the same time ashamed of his own cowardice.

The cloud had seeped over the lip of the cliff and was hovering near the edge. It paused, and then an enormous minifigure walked out of it, dressed in what might have once been a suit, top hat, and cape. It was holding a cane and would’ve looked jaunty if not for its face and the circumstances under which it approached him.

It head was purple and black, faintly glowing, like most of the flesh of Maelstrom creatures. Orange, catlike eyes and a garish smile topped off its salad of horror. Rats. Dwight’s stomach growled. Now he was making food analogies!

The man took one look at Dwight cowering in the corner and laughed. It was a terrible laugh, deep, rich, and chocolaty but at the same time high and cold. It made Dwight want to curl up in a ball and hide, but something kept him from doing just that. He decided he wouldn’t be humiliated by his fear in front of this titan. He paused, straightened, and stepped forward.

The giant looked amused. “Oh, are you going to fight me? Sorry. I’m on a tight schedule, so I’m only dropping by for a moment. Lots of innocents to assimilate, you know.” Its voice was like its laugh, evil and sinister, having the same terrifying effect.

It was obviously trying to make Dwight angry, so he did his best to keep a straight face. When the creature saw his expression had not changed, it seemed to decide not to push on. “Cat got your tongue? Oh, well. On to business, then. Baron Typhonus, at your service.” It gave a mock bow.

The Darkitect! This was the being that had caused so much pain and destruction. So many were lost because of the foolishness of this one man. In spite of himself, Dwight was infuriated at this…this thing.
He gathered his wits and pulled together enough courage to speak.

“You’ve got a lot of nerve bothering me now, you two-faced, bat-fouling, villainous swine. Your foolishness made hundreds of my kind die and become part of your pet abomination! Get out – I can’t stand to look at your idiotic mug!” Right as he yelled it, he regretted it. What if Typhonus got mad? But he only looked a little taken aback, like a child whose science project has gone wrong.

Then he laughed. It was insulting, but Dwight couldn’t blame him. Being told off by a dust bunny the size of your big toe wasn’t very daunting. Then he spoke. “I could defeat you for that, but it’d be too easy. So I’ll let you and your pitiful friends live, for now. My wishing you dead isn’t anything personal, Dwight; it’s your destiny that has to go. The destiny your Mythrans gave you. Think about that, Dwight.” Then he turned and disappeared.

Dwight stood there, dumbfounded, for a moment. Then he shook his head. The Mythrans had given him his destiny, but that wasn’t their fault. Typhonus was trying to turn him against his friends and what he believed in. It wouldn’t work.

Chapter 6.2

Dwight wanted very badly to smash the Darkitect, but to do that, he’d have to get stronger. And sticking with the Nexus Force, the Nexus, and his teammates would help him do just that. Dwight had wanted to be in the Force his whole life. The words of a crazy, twisted buffoon who infected the very embodiment of Imagination in his folly weren’t going to stop him now.

The cliff faded and he was lying on the couch in the ship again. The room was dark, and it seemed everyone had gone to bed. The only illumination was from the silent expanse of space and its stars, glowing sentries placed there to keep the dark coldness of the void away.

He got up and walked toward the floor-to-ceiling window and stared out into oblivion, considering what he’d seen and what he’d felt.
He was fantasizing about elaborate ways to destroy the Baron, most of which involved whole metric tons of dynamite, when a noise behind him caught his attention.

Duke was coming out of the living quarters and over to the window. He stood beside Dwight, following his gaze. “Not you, either, huh?” he asked knowingly.

“Nope.” said Dwight. Then it all spilled out. The letter to his parents, the dream, everything. He was so loose-lipped he was surprised he didn’t tell Duke his life story right then and there. When he was done, Duke’s eyeballs were the size of dinner plates.

“Sorry, that must be an awful lot to dump on you.” said Dwight in a small voice, having lost his fervor and adrenaline that came with the thrill of remembering his ordeal. But Duke only patted him on the back.

“The others are gonna be itching to hear this. Come on, we’re almost there. Once you see the planets in flyby, you never forget it.” He inched closer to the window, and Dwight followed, thankful for the distraction.

Then the inner core of the galaxy came into view. It was beautiful to rush by the floating chunks of rock. Dwight was proud that he could name them on sight. “Welcome to Crux,” said Duke.

Chapter 7.1
What Door?

“And you’re sure about this?” asked Vanda Darkflame inquisitively. She was wearing a red ninja gi and leggings, and two Maelstrom-charged katanas were on her back.

“Absolutely.” answered Dwight. He was in a briefing room with the Paradox leader and Duke, discussing his dream the night before. They’d arrived at Nexus Tower an hour ago, and it was huge and awesome. Everything was slick white metal, and the systems were always running. Dwight hadn’t gotten to see most of the Faction Bases, though.

The landing pad they’d touched down on was high up in the air, so high that you could see the enormous battle cruisers launching gunships down onto Crux Prime. The pad transitioned into the main area, where the Nexus Tower Artificial Intelligence, Nexus Naomi, was always watching and giving directions to new recruits. She’d moved on each one of the Alpha team to their Faction, except for Dwight. Which was why he was part of this conversation.

Vanda sighed. “The Darkitect feeds many riddles into your mind, young Dwight. Do not think the thoughts he wishes of you. They are unhealthy, and could turn you against your teammates. I am sure you don’t want to be manipulated, right?” She looked Dwight in the eye.

“Kind of a no-brainer, but no.” he replied. Vanda smirked. Duke coughed behind them.

“We’ll have to figure out how Typhonus has established a rapport with your mind,” she said. “The only way we know he could do that is if, one, he knows you personally, or two, your potential is great enough that he can touch your consciousness without knowing you, but simply by knowing OF you. I favor the second option. Duke?”

Exeter cleared his throat. “I think it’s two.”

Vanda stared at Dwight like she was trying to figure him out. “Then that means he’s definitely one of the four.” Dwight was a bit modest about all of the attention, and just sat quietly.

Duke raised a finger. “Vanda.”

“What?”

The Sentinel leader looked confused. “What about the door?”

Vanda nodded. “That remains to be found, yes.”

Dwight suddenly spoke. “In my first vision, I heard a voice tell me to seek the door. And Duke says the Mythran told you to go through the door to find the four. If my teammates and I are the four, where does the door come in?”

Duke slapped his own head. “Darn!”

The two others looked at him. “What is it?” asked Dwight.

“I just remembered! Shay told me she had a vision, too! It told her she’d be the one to find the door. Maybe she’s found it already!”

Vanda seemed to agree. “That does seem like a Venture League type of thing to do.”
