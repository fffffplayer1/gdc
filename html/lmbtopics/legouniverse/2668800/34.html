Book 2, Chapter 4.9

Dwight glared furiously at the being before him. The Baron glared back, but more amused then angrily, like Dwight was a mildly interesting insect on the ground that he had paused to wonder what it was before squashing it beneath his boot.

 

         “How are you, Dwight? I mean, really. It’s been so long since we last talked. And look! You’re all grown up! My, aren’t you a big boy now? Got the attention of my old apprentice, I see.” he gestured to the Paradox gear Dwight wore. Then he peered at the spear, which was strapped to Dwight’s back. “Would you look at that. Oh, she does like you. Made that for me?”

 

         Dwight swallowed and spoke for the first time since Typhonus had appeared. “Yes, it’s made to smash you specifically. Like it? Good, ‘cause you’ll be introduced to it soon.” He slid his hand slowly to the hilt for emphasis.

 

         The Darkitect laughed long and hard, even slapping his knee once or twice. ‘Oh, ha ha ha! You’re funny, Dwight. Really funny. If you weren’t who you are, then I’d keep you as a jester when I take over. But alas, your destiny prevents…” he shook his head in mock disappointment. “I don’t want to destroy you, Dwight. Really, I don’t. But neither of us can live while the other does.” he smirked. “So we’re surviving on borrowed time. You know what I mean?”
