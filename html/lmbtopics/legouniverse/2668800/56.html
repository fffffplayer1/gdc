Book 2, Chapter 10.1: Reassignment

Dwight half-limped over to the comm panel and presses the keys to respond. "We copy, Vanda. What've you got for us?"

"I can't say it here," she said. "This channek's open and can't be locked. Come to Nimbus Station. You can use the portal on the Starbase to get here. We'll brief you on your arrival. Vanda out." The panel blinked to signify the closing of the message.

Dwight turned to the others. "Looks like we're going on a trip. I don't like leaving the Shield behind, but it's not quick enough. We need the portal. Treem, could you stay behind and start repairs? I'm sure some of the Assembly guys will gladly help you." Treem nodded. "Okay, the rest of us will go. Doctor, if you please...."

"Certainly," said Overbuild. "I'll lead you to the portal straight away. Follow me." He started down the hallway. Alpha Team fell into step behind him. Lex and Dwight stood directly at his back, while Shay and Reyna were behind them, having a heated discussion about someting or other. Dwight rolled his shoulders back, feeling the familiar twinge of pain where the meteor had whacked him upside the head. 'I wonder how long it'll be before that stops," he thought.

"Over here!" the Doctor said. "Just around this corner! Feast your eyes on the smallest wormhole known to minifigurekind. It sits exactly in between this space station and Nimbus Plaza. The portal's twin is in Brick Annex, an offshoot of the Plaza itself, where you will need to go. Just step inside and you'll be whisked away!"

As they rounded the corner, Dwight saw a monolithic archway, built with all kinds of bricks. The technology required to harness the wormhole was massive, gray wires and capacitors poked out in between their orange shell casing. In the archway where empty space should have been was a shimmering film of bluish-white energy. 'That would be the entrance to the wormhole,' Dwight thought. "Well, step into it, go on!" Overbuild encouraged.

Chapter Whatevs, aka the chapter where the author admits he's a  liar.

Dwight stepped into the glowing white film within the archway and without warning was whisked off his feet, pulled every which way, and fliped countless times. 'I hope I don't throw up,' he thought disjointedly as he jacknifed through the air. Suddenly, he was thrown forward and hit grass. He looked up and almost gasped. Nimbus Station was beautiful. It was always night here, so close to the Crux Nebula, and stars glittered like sequins in the sky. The plants were green and lush, stone walkways connected artful buildings. Dwight got to his feet in an area known as Brick Annex, as a signpost proclaimed. It was right next to a small ocean, water scintillating in the starlight. Several rocket launchpads dotted the landscape, a patio in front of a skyscraper sat in front of thm. The building had the Assembly logo above its doorway. On the patio stood a wizard who was wearing robes of construction orange. "Ah, Alpha Team!" he boomed as he notcied Dwight and his teammates, who had similarly been flung out of the portal behind him. "Sorry I you can't stay and chat, but Nexus Jay is waiting for you with a special mission! Go right that way - it leads to Nimbus Plaza!" He gestured to a path leading between two grassy knolls. "Thanks," said Dwight. He walked across the patio toward the path. Unexpectedly, he heard a voice in his head yell, "I am a liar. I said there'd be a chapter last weekend and didn't post one because I was busy. I lied to my wonderful, faithful readers. I feel so bad about myself." Dwight pulled up and looked around. "What?" he said loudly. Shay popped up next to him. "What, what?" She demanded. Dwight looked at her. "You didn't hear that, did you?" Reyna appeared on the other side of him. "Hear what?" she asked. Dwight grabbed his head. "This is it. I've finally gone off my rocker. I'm going INSANE!"
