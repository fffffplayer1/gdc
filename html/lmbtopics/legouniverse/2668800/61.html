Chapter 11.11

“I let my guard down,” he said. “All of you, stay alert!” He glanced back and saw Reyna, Lex, and Shay fighting another group of Pirates, and had to flinch aside as and Admiral launched its hook at him. He wheeled around, glared at the zombie, and spread his swords, leaving his chest and open target.

The admiral took the bait and shot its grappling hook again, but Dwight jumped impossibly high, boosting himself up with his powers, and landed on the chain, balancing perfectly. He ran up the length of metal, dodging the Admiral’s frantic cannon blast, and leaped on it, slicing with his blades until it was only dust.

Another two Pirates charged him, and he swept his blades outward in a wide slash and caught them in the sides. When they grunted in surprise, he pulled his swords together, smashing them into each other. The two Stromlings disappeared in clouds of mist. Dwight’s actions left his back and sides open to attack, but Reyna, Lex, and Shay jumped in from all sides and reformatted to cover their commanding officer and to bring the team back together in one place.

As another Admiral flung itself at him, Dwight let it crash into the points of his swords and, thus trapped, Shay was able to finish it off with a blast from her weapon. With that, the cluster of enemies that had been stalking the Alpha Team seemed to be gone, and the other battle groups were all fighting Venture League scouts and a few non-infected pirates.

Chapter 11.12

Dwight took the brief respite as an opportunity to locate Renee Tombcrusher, the woman whom Toby had mentioned, and was rewarded when he saw her by the opposite end of the Ghash. It was a huge, at least five-foot deep rut in the ground. Fissures were carved into the stone as he had seen before, but rather than venting the earthy glow of magma, they spewed Maelstrom refuse like geysers.

The deepest and farthest removed part of the Ghash sported a huge skull chiseled into the rock; unlike the tiki head in the ravine it was newly and crudely shaped. On the ridge to the skull’s left, which was on Dwight’s right, a group of Venture Leaguers stood, mostly Adventurers and Daredevils in favor of their crossbows and flareguns. Dwight’s visor zoomed in and he could see that Renee was in command, her ponytail whipping as she turned her head left and right to better yell at her troops. She cradled a hook rifle of some sort, which seemed to have been cannibalized from an Admiral’s arm cannon, albeit purified of Maelstrom. She noticed the Alpha Team standing across from her, her eyes widened, and she beckoned to them hurriedly.

“About time you folks showed up,” she half-yelled as Dwight and the others approached. “Them Pirates shore made a mess of things. It’s a heckuva lot to clean up, and we could use some extra blades.” She pivoted on the ball of her foot and fired her weapon at an Admiral that was getting too close. The hook latched onto it, whirled the Stromling around, and sent it flying off into the canopy.

“How bad is it?” Shay asked.

Renee shrugged. “Soon as ‘bout a third of the crew got themselves infected by some messed-up treasure, the Maelstrom a-started to duplicate the Pirate and Admiral forms it’d acquired. There are over a coupla hundred on the planetoid, but more are comin’ every day.”

Chapter 11.13

Lex’s eyebrows knit themselves together. “They should outnumber all of us eight to one in this clearing.”

“They don’t?” Reyna sounded a little surprised.

“No,” Lex said. “From what I’ve studied of the Maelstrom’s field distribution, which is scattered and random at its most organized, there ought to be many, many more…” he swept his arms in an arc. “All around us right here.”

“Ah, well,” Renee Tombcrusher shrugged. “Like eighty percent of ‘em are up by Brig Rock anyway. Maybe there are just less over here.”

Lex’s frown deepened. “But that would still mean more should be here. I do not see why their forces would be holding back. If the Maelstrom’s numbers are as you say, they could take the island at any time.”

“Then we’re probably walking into a trap,” Dwight said grimly. “Whatever we need to do, let’s do it fast.”
