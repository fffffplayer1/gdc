Chapter 5.4

 

The Baron smiled. “I came to pay you all a little visit, of course. I talked to Dwight just now. Great kid you’ve got there. You must be such proud parents. But wait! You’re not his parents, are you? And neither are those others, who took him in before.” Seeing their expressions, he chuckled. “What’s wrong? Was it supposed to be secret? Well, his foster parents have their own secrets, don’t they?”

 

Vanda started. So he knew… She had to warn Alex. Typhonus would go after them as soon as possible. If he’d found them… Dwight would never be able to deal with his ‘parents’ being destroyed by the Baron.

 

Typhonus noticed her movement, and therefore her intention. “Ah, my disobedient apprentice. It is not I who should be branded a traitor.” He stepped forward. “You don’t have to be punished, Vanda. Arrogance and ambition can be forgiven, you know.”

 

Vanda grimaced and angled her katanas at the towering figure before her. “Arrogance and ambition were not my motivation,” she said coldly. “I ended my partnership with you for two reasons. The first is that I thought you were dead. The second is that you are insane. And I have not returned because you have nothing more to teach me.” She gestured to the observation deck window, which overlooked the Nexus Chamber. “As long as your blade is aimed at the Nexus and its people, I will work against you.”

 

Typhonus shrugged. “Alright, if you insist.” Then he raised his staff and fired six bolts of crackling bluish-purple energy across the room. They moved as if they were alive, ricocheting off the walls and making a beeline for the Faction Leaders.

Chapter 5.5

Vanda deflected the first pulse with her katanas, but a second later another one appeared, rushing toward her blind spot. In that instant, Duke sprang to one knee and threw his shield, an impressive feat since it was a two-inch thick slate of titanium, and it slammed into the Maelstrom projectile, dropping to the floor as the blast dissipated.

 

As Duke got up and ran to meet Vanda, Hael and Dr. Overbuild joined them. The Doctor opened a backpack and assembled a Nexus Force shield generator, which enveloped the group in a dome of green light. As the last four missiles impacted on the shield, Typhonus laughed uproariously.

 

“Pretty little fireworks display you’ve got there, but it won’t hold me for long. In the meantime, enjoy uninfected life while you can.” He stalked toward the shield and paced around it, searching for a weakness. The beams of energy flickered, beginning to fail due to the Baron’s chaotic nature, which disrupted all order around him.

 

Soon the shield would be gone, and the Council would be at Typhonus’s mercy. Not the most enchanting prospect. As Vanda glanced anxiously at the shield’s dimming light, something changed in the room. A presence descended and filled all empty space, but at the same time it felt voided. In the dark indigo fog, two yellow eyes materialized, flaming gateways to a fiery world.
