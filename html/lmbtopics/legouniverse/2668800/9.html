Chapter 11.1
Not So Fireproof

Dwight stood dumbfounded as the huge beast landed on the arena floor. It was enormous. Its eye was about the size of his fist and its teeth were like kitchen knives. It opened its mouth and a bellow of rage slipped forth from its throat. It was early and totally not under control. The team was only Rank Two when they should’ve been facing the dragon at Three. Even Lex’s mechs or the plasma turret couldn’t stand up to it.

It made a high-pitched sound and suddenly a rush of flames erupted from its maw. The dragon swung its head from side to side, covering the immediate area with fire jets. Dwight and his companions had to duck and weave to avoid being lacerated.

The beast stamped its feet and roared again, flattening its ears to its head like a cat. “To the bunker!” Dwight screamed at his friends. They ran, rolling to avoid tracer streams of flame the dragon shot from its mouth. Then Dwight heard a girl screaming. He turned to look. The worst had happened. Reyna… was hit.

She yelled in pain and clutched her ribs, where on the lower left side, she had a tear in her tunic where her armor had melted and had a red burn about six inches in diameter, if you could call it a circle. It was shiny with blood plasma, and Dwight could tell that if she didn’t get medical attention, she’d be… no, he wouldn’t think about it now. Save her first, worry later.

Shay was trying to help Reyna to her feet, but Reyna had gotten her foot stuck under a root, which had caused her to trip and become an easy target. Shay’s tugs became more frantic as the dragon took aim and the high-pitched noise started up again. Rather than stand by helplessly, Dwight acted.

It was crazy and potentially smash-inducing, but Dwight charged the dragon and thrust downward with his katanas, sinking them satisfyingly deep into its neck. The animal roared in pain and shook its head. Dwight jumped onto its back, pulled his swords out, and stabbed it over and over again, thinking, ‘Reyna hurt, Shay almost hurt, me and Lex in jeopardy, die dragon!’ He only thought of his injured friend as he drove his blades into the dragon’s back.

His rampage was stopped by the dragon turning its head around, biting him around the breastplate, and lifting him into the air in its mouth. Dwight gasped as its teeth drove the cold metal into his chest, bruising his torso. The pain was like tiny needles being wiggled and slowly driven into his sternum. The dragon again shook its head, flinging him across the arena. He smashed into the front gates of the bunker, slumping. The pain was excruciating, but he had to get up, had to help his friends.

He staggered to his feet, his swords were missing, his shuriken were useless against the dragon’s thick hide. Shay and Reyna were loose and running to the bunker. Lex was nowhere to be seen. Dwight walked toward the dragon, thinking about a power he’d seen in his book. If he could manipulate the Maelstrom lightning at the right time…

He ran at the dragon, yelling obscenities and at the last second jumping. While he was airborne over it, and the beast reared up to swipe at him, he called on his powers.

From the purple-indigo cloud above came a jagged bolt of lightning. It struck the dragon and the animal roared, weakened. It curled up in a defensive position. Dismayed that he had not smashed it, Dwight fell to Earth. Then Lex appeared.

The Summoner was riding in a race car, a three-seater with a platform on the back. The flat top had a plasma turret – the plasma turret – mounted on it and Shay was firing it from a seat behind it. Reyna, despite her raw injury, was driving, wincing at every turn of the wheel.

The car’s grill on the front was reinforced with spikes, so it made little difference to its passengers when they rammed it into the dragon’s flank. It roared and fell on its side. Shay popped off her flareguns, blinding it. And finally, Lex leaped out of the car and plunged his bladed staff into its soft underbelly, smashing it into Maelstrom waste. It was gone at last.

Reyna stayed in the car and Shay applied healing injections to her wound. Lex pulled his staff from the ground, where it was stuck, and walked over to Dwight. “Sorry I’m late,” he mused. “That was certainly invigorating.”

Dwight sat up, dazed, and scratched his head. “I thought we were supposed to fight the dragon on Level Thirty-five,” he said. Then he fainted.

Chapter 11.2

Dwight woke up to one of Notion’s Explorien Bots slapping his face. “I seem to be waking up injured a lot lately,” he grumbled and sat up. The rest of his team was sleeping soundly on cots. They were in the bunker. All was silent outside. Nothing moved for a second.

Notion’s bot sat with its back to Dwight. “I had a serious malfunction,” he said. He sounded like he was about to cry. “I failed. The samples escaped. Eoghan wouldn’t like that. Oh no.”

“But we won,” protested Dwight. “We beat the dragon. We’re still alive!” He waved his arms at Notion. “Are you listening to me? We. Are. Okay. You. Are. Not. A. Failure.” The Explorien unit looked back at him.

“Really?” Notion asked. “’Cause it seems a whole lot like I am. I let a premature sample in, and a dragon, no less, while you weren’t of qualifying Rank, my processors caused me to babble senselessly and I couldn’t intervene, and here you are out cold. I FAILED.”

Dwight drew back a fist and punched the Explorien bot in the face. The robot was sent flying backwards, but Dwight’s hand screamed in pain – or rather his mouth did – and he clutched it, cursing. “Shut up!” he yelled at Notion. “Robots aren’t supposed to have inferiority complexes! If I ever catch you talking about yourself that way again I’ll shut you down or so help me…” He stopped. Notion was staring at him, as were his teammates, who had woken up during the ruckus.

“Guess we solved the out-cold problem, then.” Dwight said weakly as he sat down hard on a chair. His flush of rage at the AI was gone, and he looked in Notion’s direction to apologize but the Explorien Bot was gone. He sighed. Another conundrum for his list.

“So, what do we do now?” cut in Reyna. She’d been healed nicely and her armor was good as new. Shay and Lex were in likewise condition.

“I guess we wait for Notion to say something about what happens next,” Dwight said in a hollow voice. His throat was sore from scolding the AI.

“What made you mouth off to him like that?” asked Shay inquisitively.

“He thinks he’s a ‘failure’ and started going all ‘low self-esteem’ on me. He’s never done that before. Apparently he’s really depressed about the dragon breaking in. It’s a wound to his personal pride.” he answered. For a moment, no one spoke.
Then a voice quietly began to speak over the intercom. “Dwight, thank you. Here are your Rank Three kits. There will be one last Level in a few minutes and then you’ll leave the Continuum… and I with you. Good luck.”

Small golden parachutes floated down from the sky and landed with a muffled thump outside the door. There were four of them, each marked with the Faction symbols. A few seconds later, a fifth came flying, resting in Dwight’s arms as he burst through the door into the open. He opened it hurriedly. There was a small paperback book inside. Its title was, “Class A1 Military Intelligence: User’s Manual.”

He set it carefully aside as he and his team eagerly opened their kits. Shay’s shoulderpads covered her chest now, and her helmet had extra spikes. Her flareguns were also double-barreled. Lex’s robes were heavier, his hood was inlaid with leather-backed mail, and his staff and orb were huge.

Reyna’s Knight gear was heavy-looking. A shield her own height, a sword almost matching that length, and armor too layered and numbered for words. They all looked very impressive.

Dwight’s new Shinobi equipment was amazing. His shoulder armor was made of steel plates of varying length chained together and draped over his frame. His gi now had plate armor as well, and his pants. The chain mail only showed over his joints to allow free movement.

His helmet was like a leather rice hat with circular metal plates layered on top, with a Maelstrom rebreather hanging from its brim. This would allow him to be around lots of infected air – which he was going to do fighting the Darkitect. The others had their own rebreathers built into their headpieces somewhere.

His katanas now took on a more radical shape, the tips of their blades curving downward – a falchion, he thought the sword style was called – which meant he had to heavily check his fighting method… again. Oh, brick.

The stone wall produced a dozen of each enemy they’d faced (excepting dragons, of course) and each of them took a stance. Shay squeezed the triggers of her flareguns and four high-explosive projectiles flew toward the Stromling mob. Dwight hurled kunai and shuriken at the enemy. Lex deployed his turret and mechs, his new suit preventing it from tiring him.

Reyna hefted her sword and charged the seething mass of skeletal creatures. Dwight followed, brandishing his katanas. Lex brought up his bladed staff and ran close behind. Shay brought up the rear at a distance, sending flaming projectiles into their midst, taking out a Maelstrom creature with each one. They were unstoppable.

The four minifigures cleaved, bashed, and fired their way through the melee. It was a grim battle, but in the end, they were sitting exhausted on the ground, their armor stained and dented, their weapons steaming, but totally unhurt. How far they had come from the recruits who couldn’t battle eight Stromlings without getting scratched up.

Notion appeared in one of his Exploriens. “I have repaired by emotion chip,” he announced proudly. “I shall not sustain any more defects such as the one I experienced earlier. Hurrah.” Dwight smiled at the bot’s deadpan voice trying to cheer.

“Are you ready to leave the Continuum?” the AI asked. Alpha Team exchanged glances.

“Well, I’m getting tired of the place.” remarked Shay. Everyone (other than Notion) laughed and agreed.

“Let’s go,” said Reyna.

“I wish to get my hands on bricks again soon,” added Lex wistfully.

“Okay, it’s unanimous. Let’s get outta here, Notion.” he looked at his manual. “Er – execute command EXIT//103. Authorization code: Delta Alpha Black Teal?” Eoghan’s code.

If Notion could smile, he sure did try. “It has been a long time since I heard that command code,” he said. Then the room shone white and their surroundings disappeared.
