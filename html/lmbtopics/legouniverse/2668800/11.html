Chapter 12.2

Later, in the Nexus Force Briefing Room, Alpha Team and their leaders sat at a table. Dwight sensed some important news was to come. He took deep breaths and adjusted his spear where it was mounted on his back. This was the moment he and his compatriots had been waiting for.

Duke pressed a button on a remote and pointed it at the far wall behind him. The wall flipped on an axis and the other side faced them. It was a plasma TV screen. Duke clicked another button and a green map appeared. It was strangely shaped, with little peninsulas and rounded edges.

“This is a gamma-ray map of Avant Gardens,” proclaimed Duke. “Recently, it was a Maelstrom-free planet that housed many temporary homes, a Sentinel outpost, and a Paradox refinery. A month ago, the refinery blew.” He clicked his remote and an image of a Spiderling appeared. “Paradox was in the process of studying these guys when one of them got ahold of some other Maelstrom samples. The result was…” he again pressed the button. “…the Spider Queen.”

A fearsome photograph came up, basically a Spiderling with longer legs, a thinner body, and multiple blasters on its thorax. It was more heavily built than others of its kind. “The ‘Queen’ immediately went out on a rampage, freeing herself and other samples to breed in the Spider Caves nearby, which we thought we’d cleaned out.

“Then she charged through the Sentinel camp, nearly demolished the Monument…” Duke showed them a slide of a huge stone statue of a minifigure in a red shirt and blue pants fighting a yet-unpainted Maelstrom creature. “The repairs cost us time and sweat. We then tracked her to a Block Yard Property – which is a Property just off the side of Avant Gardens – and she was creating a huge Maelstrom typhoon. We lost several guards in the fight, only to have them rebuilt at the base as gibbering mental patients. Some were infected.”

The screen now showed a circular grassy area, surrounded by boulders, with a view of Avant Gardens in the distance. Another click, and it was darkened by purplish-indigo clouds and swarming Spiderlings. Their Queen sat in the middle of it all.

Duke turned off the TV. “So, Alpha Team, you have been debriefed. Your first true mission in the field: cleanup in the Gardens and taking down the Spider Queen in the Block Yard. You’ll find your personal dropship waiting on landing pad 307. Good luck.” Dwight got up. His team followed suit.

“May the Nexus be with you,” said Vanda. Then, they exited the room and walked past the lobby. Nexus Naomi was waiting.

“Hi!” she belted out in her bubbly voice. “Landing pad 307 is to your left! You’ll find basic equipment packs inside the dropship, and your pilot will take off once you’re in. Talk to Notion, He’ll explain the rest.” The team thanked her and sped over to the landing pad.

The dropship was oddly shaped, an undercarriage that could hold four minifigures and a computer console was most of the bottom. The cockpit sat in front of it, a triangular viewport set upside-down, where the pilot would be located, half sitting, and half lying down vertically.

Two wings sat above the undercarriage, each with three large blue and black conical tubes - Imagination missiles - mounted underneath, ready for launch. Two jointed metal spires slanted out from the middle on either side, horizontal turbojets (a kind of disc with an antigravity field around it, which worked sort of like a propeller and was used for vertical takeoffs) on the end. The engine, at the back, had six rocket boosters and another turbojet. The dropships were always heavily equipped and carried squads into battle.

There was a minifigure in a blue pilot jumpsuit and helmet, his light plastoid breastplate and shinguards reflecting sunlight. He was sharpening a Super Dagger – the standard pilot’s sidearm – and sheathed it as they approached.

“Hey there,” he said. “I’m Wing Commander Treem, your pilot. This here’s my special girl…” he knocked on the dropship’s hull. “…the Halberd. She’s seen battle but that’ll suit you just fine.” he chuckled. “Better get rolling, then.”

“Hello,” Dwight introduced himself. “I’m Dwight Lavamonster, Chief Warrant Officer, and Alpha Team Leader.” Vanda had told him his exact rank earlier. It felt good to say it. “These are Markswoman Shay Fuse, Tech Marine Lex Craftyrhubarb, and Martial Arts Expert Reyna Hydroblast.”

“Pleasure,” replied Treem, shaking hands with each of them. “Now let’s get outta here. There’s a big storm coming.” A dark indigo cloud was rolling in from the east. Maelstrom reinforcements to Crux Prime, most likely. Ugh. Without a word, Treem climbed into the cockpit and closed the open windscreen. The team boarded the cabin and grabbed the hand straps hanging from the ceiling. Then, with a rush of gale-force wind, they took off.
