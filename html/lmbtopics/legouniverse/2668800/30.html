Book 2, Chapter 3.4

The roar of the Halberd’s engines thankfully pushed all sounds out of Dwight’s hearing, such as Shay’s soft sobbing, Reyna’s hitched breathing, Lex’s twiddling his thumbs, and the voices in his head telling him he’d failed Vance. Treem, from the cockpit, motioned to the cabin, and the team boarded.

No one spoke as the hatch closed and the turbojets were muffled. The ship took off and headed out of the atmosphere. The minifigures silently, collectively lamented Vance’s death.

Treem’s voice came over the intercom. “There’s a Cumulus comin’ our way. It must be the remains of Queeny’s typhoon. We’ve got minutes, maybe seconds. It’s closing in fast, so prepare for evasive action!”

There was a huge rocking within the ship, and the team grabbed onto the hand straps hanging from their cabin ceiling as the ship weaved and rolled to avoid the dangerous Maelstrom cloud. These formations traveled through space as Maelstrom air transportation, and could also spawn deadly aerial versions of most enemies. They sometimes lurked in the far reaches of star systems, waiting to ambush passing craft.

Dwight cursed as an impact could be felt on the hull. “We’re being boarded, Treem!” he broadcasted to the control cabin.

“I know, I know, lemme handle it,” the pilot griped. He flicked a switch, and an electrical charge of Imagination laced over the outside of the ship. On the outboard cameras, a Stromling with batlike wings and long, spindly blades for arms could be seen as it was thrown off of the hull, smoking. The Alpha Team cheered and high-fived. It was nice to have something to be happy about.

The roar of the Halberd’s engines thankfully pushed all sounds out of Dwight’s hearing, such as Shay’s soft sobbing, Reyna’s hitched breathing, Lex’s twiddling his thumbs, and the voices in his head telling him he’d failed Vance. Treem, from the cockpit, motioned to the cabin, and the team boarded.

No one spoke as the hatch closed and the turbojets were muffled. The ship took off and headed out of the atmosphere. The minifigures silently, collectively lamented Vance’s death.

Treem’s voice came over the intercom. “There’s a Cumulus comin’ our way. It must be the remains of Queeny’s typhoon. We’ve got minutes, maybe seconds. It’s closing in fast, so prepare for evasive action!”

There was a huge rocking within the ship, and the team grabbed onto the hand straps hanging from their cabin ceiling as the ship weaved and rolled to avoid the dangerous Maelstrom cloud. These formations traveled through space as Maelstrom air transportation, and could also spawn deadly aerial versions of most enemies. They sometimes lurked in the far reaches of star systems, waiting to ambush passing craft.

Dwight cursed as an impact could be felt on the hull. “We’re being boarded, Treem!” he broadcasted to the control cabin.

“I know, I know, lemme handle it,” the pilot griped. He flicked a switch, and an electrical charge of Imagination laced over the outside of the ship. On the outboard cameras, a Stromling with batlike wings and long, spindly blades for arms could be seen as it was thrown off of the hull, smoking. The Alpha Team cheered and high-fived. It was nice to have something to be happy about.

Book 2, Chapter 3.5

Then another impact sounded. And another. And another. “Dag!” Treem growled. “You guys’ll have to take care of them. My Imagination pulse is still recharging! They’ll cut through our instruments in a flash!”

Good. Maybe a big fight would cheer Dwight up. “Activate your rebreathers,” he said to his teammates. “If you haven’t already.” They all smirked slightly, and he knew they had. “Treem, open the doors.” The hatch slid open, and silence was all. Even the engines didn’t make a sound. What did, however, was the swarm of winged Stromlings buzzing toward them from the storming Maelstrom cloud. Lightning flashed and thunder boomed. The creatures hissed and dove in to attack.

“Long-range mode!” Dwight advised. “We can’t get close to them without leaving the ship!” He accessed his HUD and pressed a catch on his boot. A window came up and stated, MAGNETIZED. ‘I was wondering what that did,’ he mused. “Oh, and press the button on your shoes to magnetize the soles to the hull!” He yelled over his shoulder. While the team did as they were told, Dwight caught a hand strap, swung upwards, which was easy in zero gravity, and touched his boots to the hull directly above the cabin opening. ‘Focus on the local vertical,’ he reminded himself. He had been briefly instructed in null gravity combat but that was nothing compared to the actual experience. If he was having trouble, Lex was probably suffering from severe vertigo.

That theory was disproved when Lex appeared above him, having crawled up from the other side of the ship. “Greetings,” he said. “Let us bash these repulsive new enemies.” Dwight snickered inwardly and nodded.

“Yes, let’s,” he agreed. The two faced the mass of winged Stromlings. The lead flyer screeched and flew toward them. The swarm followed suit. Dwight and Lex raised their weapons and, for once, didn’t charge.

Book 2, Chapter 4.1: Nullgrav Combat and The Crash.

The winged Stromlings twirled into a dive and sped towards Dwight and Lex’s position. “Got anything?” Dwight broadcasted over the comm.

“I’m afraid not,” Lex answered. “None of the Creations at my disposal can handle null gravity. They aren’t built specifically to withstand space, you see.” He frowned.

When the first squadron of creatures reached the ship (Darkwings, Dwight had decided to christen them) they latched on with their three-toed clawed feet, tearing into the hull to stay anchored on the ship. Dwight and Lex plodded over with their magnetized boots, and slashed through the mob of Maelstrom servants easily.Dwight summoned enough strength to his legs to tackle a Darkwing to the ship, trying to examine it and find a weakness. Instead of learning anything, he got a wingtip in the eye. The Shinobi smashed it with frustration, drop-kicking it off the ship and stabbing it to finish it off.

Dwight’s HUD linked to his brain, and text rolled across his vision. *Their advantage: their speed. Our advantage: our strength. Their Weaknesses: frail body, obvious inexperience in close combat. Our Weaknesses: Limited mobility - our boots, movements slowed by zero gravity. Odds of victory: 2.37 defeat, to 2.63 victory. Outcome: Injuries at best.* He sighed. This was not going to be fun.
