Chapter 9.1
Prey and the Predator

Dwight was struck speechless. Duke and Vanda reeled. Hael gaped. Overbuild looked startled. Lex fainted. So did Shay. Reyna had to catch them, but she was still gawking. "M-my father?" Dwight couldn't believe what the computer had said.

*Yes. Did you ever wonder why you were adopted? Your true family are Mythrans. Nobody was to know until you were ready to know yourself, and here you are. Computers aren't made to keep secrets, you know.* Notion replied. It paused. *Dwight. Do you wish to enter the Continuum?*

"Hang on," said Dwight, regaining his composure. "I'm still trying to get over the fact that this guy is my dad. You mean the one who gave us those visions, is my father and the same one who built you as well?"

*Yes.*

"That's kinda freaky," said Reyna,trying to hold up Lex and Shay at once. Duke shook his head.

"Too much behind-the-scenes manipulation," he mumbled. Vanda and Hael nodded assent. Overbuild just looked mildly interested.

"Tell him to let you in, Dwight," the doctor said. "Let us see what happens."

Dwight wasn't sure i he wanted to just yet, and he was still kind of in shock over the recent revelation. 'I have a father!' he thought gloriously. It had a nice ring to it. All his life he'd felt wrong, out of place, and he'd hoped joining the Nexus Force would fill the emptiness. 'Turns out, it did," he chuckled inwardly as he looked backto Notion's console.

"Lemme into the Continuum," he said.

*Access granted. Hello, Alpha Team.* the AI said strangely. Before anyone could even think, the hatch behind the console was thrust forward. The opening shot out steam, and a metallic hiss announced the opening of the Door.

His father was a mythran?! The plot thickens...

Chapter 9.2

The hatch fell away, and the portal was revealed to be… nothing. A blank, endless void was outside of the door. Big whoop. Duke spoke first. “Is this the Mythrans’ idea of a joke?” he said indignantly. “Because we don’t have the time to-.”

Suddenly, Dwight felt a tugging sensation in his gut, a huge gravitational pull guiding him toward the door. The other Alpha recruits were experiencing it, too – Lex, Reyna, and Shay were quickly sucked into the door and disappeared. Winds howled as the suction became greater and greater.

As Dwight flew at the opening, he grabbed the frame of the hatch. Vanda ran over and grasped his hand. “You have to let go!” she yelled over the roaring wind from the hatch. “Succumb to the winds! It’s the only way you’ll make it in!”

Dwight looked at her. “Tell my parents I found my father!” he screamed back. Then he jerked his hand from hers and let go of the hatch.

* * *

He fell, long and slowly, until a gray mass came rushing toward him out of nowhere and he impacted on the ground. Everything went black, and he found himself in the room where he usually found the Mythran called Eoghan.

“Hello?” he said timorously. No answer came. He stood up and looked around. The Nexus, or whatever it was, was there, as well as the familiar dark surroundings. But then a light came on, the first he’d seen in this place.

The lamp illuminated a box sitting a few feet away from him. Dwight walked toward it, looked down. It had the Paradox symbol on it. ‘Weird,’ he thought. Cautiously, he began to open it. When he saw what was inside, he gave a small cry of delight. It was a Rank One Shinobi battle gear kit!

There was a book enclosed along with a gi and leggings, both lined with chain mail, a thick red and black hood, and a katana. All would be useful in his training. But how had they gotten to him? He was outside of time, right? Vanda couldn’t just send them, could she?

He picked up the book, which was entitled: Rank 1 Shinobi Guide. Opening it, he saw a small message, written in ink, on the first page.

Present for you – good luck with training!
E.S.

“E.S.?” he wondered aloud. Then it hit him – Eoghan Sarun. “Thanks, Dad.” he said to nobody in particular. After all, he was alone except for the darkness. A sudden thought came to him. ‘Guess dear ol’ dad doesn’t rhyme when he writes.’

Chapter 9.3

He awoke lying on a grassy plain in the Continuum, apparently. His four teammates were standing over him. “He’s awake!” whooped Lex with pure joy. They all high-fived.

“Guys, where are we?” Dwight asked. He tried to get up, but his body felt like lead. Reyna helped him into a sitting position.

“Easy, big guy. You had quite a fall. Scared us out of our wits when we landed on our feet and you came plummeting down like a rock. I don’t know if I should nurse you back to health or slap you.”

Shay laughed. Lex tried not to make it obvious that he was snickering. Dwight sighed. Same as always, his friends were insane. But, that was partially why he stuck with them, so he couldn’t complain.

“I have something that might help,” said a voice behind them. They all turned to see a minifigure in a white jumpsuit standing behind them. He had no hair, but a yellow light was blinking on his head.

“Who’re you?” asked Lex curiously. The minifigure blinked.

“Why, 6804 Creative Notion, of course. This is only one of my countless bodies, ‘Explorien Bots’, if you will. They allow me to traverse areas and transport sixty percent of my internal nodes elsewhere. So, although my current place of installment is Nexus Tower, I can still retain a large amount of my abilities portably. A stroke of genius on my builder’s part, I must say.” If robots could beam, that would be the expression the android directed at Dwight. Dwight smiled back. Already he was proud of his dad.

“Anyways, here’s a cup of Bancha tea and a Hardtack biscuit. That should get your Imagination and overall health back to full.” The Explorien Bot tossed a full teacup and what looked like a French Baguette to Dwight. “Go on. Eat up.”

Dwight gobbled the bread and chugged down the tea. All in a second, he was full of energy, better than he’d ever felt in his life. He kicked himself into a standing position. “Thanks, Notion,” he said.

“No problem,” said the bot. “My secondary directive, after all, is to protect you and help you when I’m needed.” Then the minifigure began fading into the background. “I have to go,” it said. “But I’ll be watching your progress. It might be wise to put on your battle gear now. I’ll give you a few hours to get a feel for it.” Then it vanished.

“I like his idea,” said Shay. So they all got out their kits, sat down, and started to read.

“Kind of anti-climatic, isn’t it?” whispered Reyna as they all fell silent to focus on their books. The others shushed her furiously, and then the learning commenced.

Lex was reading about the higher points of being a Summoner in Assembly. Reyna was learning how best to use a Knight’s gear to serve the Sentinels. And Shay was absorbing knowledge passed down by the first Venture League Daredevils. Dwight glanced at his Shinobi book. It was complicated, but he’d figure it out.

Chapter 1: Starting Up. Chapter 2: Swordsmanship of the Ninja. Chapter 3ual Wielding swords (higher Ranks only). Chapter 4: Knife and Shuriken throwing. And Chapter 5: Maelstrom abilities and handling. Piece of cake.

Hours passed. Everyone was in their gear practicing now, having thoroughly read their guidebooks. Shay’s Daredevil gear, as before, looked like a green and gray biker suit. Lex’s Summoner outfit consisted of a hood, robes, what looked like an orb to carry, and a staff. Reyna was in full Knight armor, though as she was Rank One it was a tad light, and she had a sword and shield. Dwight was getting used to the weight of his chain mail gi and pants.

Notion had provided them with targets in the form of Stromlings, ceramic minifigures to slice up with their swords, and stuffed leather bags for Lex’s creations to tear up. They all used at least one.

Dummies fell from encounters with Dwight’s and Reyna’s swords. Shay’s flaregun and Dwight’s shuriken made quick work of the targets. And Lex’s huge mech he’d learned to call forth tore the bags to shreds with dual axe-bladed hands. All in all, they were doing pretty well.

Then a voice boomed out of nowhere, probably over some type of intercom. It was Notion. “Okay, people. Now that you’ve gotten used to your gear, we can begin. What we’re beginning will be a one week period during which you will battle with samples of the Maelstrom we’ve collected over the years. They have formed a miniature intelligent form, which can spawn everything it infects. Keep in mind you will be fighting all areas of Maelstrom, from regular Stromlings to, eventually, a dragon. Please stand by as we release the samples and take you into the Survival arena.”

The training equipment disappeared. Nothing was left but the minifigures and their backpacks. After a beat, they were all in an enclosed, rectangular area, with grass flooring and trees, along with some rocks and a white metallic structure on one end. The building had a small bunker-like bottom story, with an open roof that sported a plasma turret and a Nexus Force flag.

“This bunker will be your main defense point,” the voice continued. “Inside you will find items that restore armor and health, as well as the two other Ranks in kits you will be able to access. You must be at level twenty of the “game” to use a Rank Two kit, and at level thirty to use Rank Three. When you reach level thirty-five, there will be only the dragon to defeat and then you can leave the Continuum. Good luck, and don’t lose the bunker!” With that, the transmission was cut off, and the opposite end of the arena, a stone wall, burst open and eight Stromlings entered the area.

They were minifigure-sized, with glowing red eyes and wet-looking purple skin. Their hair was blown back, if they had hair, and parts of their chests were open to show their ribs, black and burned from Maelstrom overexposure, as were one of each of their legs. Their left arms had blades instead of hands. ‘Two for each of us,’ thought Dwight.
