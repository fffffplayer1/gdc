Chapter 8:

 

Alsoir opens his mouth as if to say something, then shuts it. The auctioneer slams the hammer down upon his little table and proclaims,

"Sold to yon gentleman in the back!"

Talan stepped off the block and was roughly shoved along by two men. As he passed by Alsoir, he glared at the traitor. Alsoir gave the slightest hint of shame, then resumed his previous air.

 

The traitor congratulated Talan's master, contradicting the fact that they looked like enemies only minutes before. Talan concluded that they were competitors in the slave market but friends otherwise.

 

Talan was exchanged over to his new master, who introduced himself as Hugo.

"I hope you enjoy your stay at my estate, eh?" Hugo said, chuckling at his terrible humor.

They embarked on a carriage. As they rode through the city, Talan would have felt like a king if it were not for the fact that he was a slave and the handcuffs the bound his hands.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

It was nighttime when we find Talan stepping off the carriage onto the estate. He is shown his quarters, which was a Lilliputian mud hut with three other occupants. Just before his was roughly shoved inside, Talan catches a glimpse of rows of the same minute huts that stretch for an easy 100 yards. He lands on a tiny mat with a thin blanket, which he was obviously supposed to sleep on.

 

The door is shut, locked, and bolted.
