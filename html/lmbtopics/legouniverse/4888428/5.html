Here comes the next chapter!

 

Chapter 9:

 

A few minutes pass after Talan's escort walk away. Our view is of the mud hut, of which we are about to hear the occupants converse. After some time of absolute silence in the non-existent light, a barely audible whisper flew into the air.

"Who be ye?"

Talan rolls over until he was facing the origin of the noise.

"I'm Talan. You?"

"Cass. These here are my companions, Halem and Rosie."

Although no sound was to be heard from them at the moment, Talan remembers the glimpse he caught before he was rudely shoved in the hut.

"What is this place?" Talan wonders.

"Hush!"

Two pairs of footsteps tramp past the hut, obviously guards. A moment of tense, nervous silence pass before Cass answers Talan's question.

"'Tis a sugarcane plantation."

Talan interrupts,

"Sugarcane? Never heard of it."

"Shaal-whathisname brought it up here with his blasted army. An' he brought up a few slaves too. Us'ns be on o' them that came up."

Talan nodds understandingly. 

"Ye won't be havin' no fun here," Cass continued, "A real pain, be yon slavedriver Jaze. Quite impossible to understand the man too."

Halem put forth an interjection.

"Harvestin' be in two days. Mas'r Hugo'll be buyin' lots o' slaves for the season."

Chapter 10! Checkpoint!

 

Chapter 10:

 

It is deep into the night. Talan's conversation is long since finished, and silence lays over the tiny hut with the exception of the occasional guard, ensuring that no slaves could dig through the mud and escape. It is time that I should offer a slight explaination.

 

During the ride in the carrige, Talan unknowingly crossed the border between Amythra, where his home was, to Arridia, which lies south of Amythra. Talan is now in a land where sugar cane is nearly ripe. Shalhyaar's army's conquest began in the south of Arridia, in the capital. Sweeping northward through the vast southern desert into the milder climate of Amythra, Shalhyaar then advanced to Amythra's capital city, which lies near the south border and is the location of Talan's home. Shalhyaar still is conquesting the northern half, but without a king to lead them, the inhabitants remain almost helpless. From there Shalhyaar plans to swing northward into Swordic, the savage northern icelands, in a detailed invasion of what designs will be made known at other times.

 

It is nearly morning now, and we will soon see what events take place on Talan's first day in slavery...
