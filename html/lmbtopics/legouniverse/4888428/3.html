Chapter 6:

 

It is nighttime. Talan's hut is visible in the distance, protruding from the ground in an unmoving stance. The chirps of crickets mingle with the last cries of the evening birds. Behind us, all we can see is a vast plain of seemingly endless grass.

All this beauty is about to change.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------

Alsoir Jaimian stood at the King's door. He is posing to knock, but he hesitates. This would change history.

He tapped lightly on the solid oak. A voice echoed from the other side.

"Come in."

Alsoir stepped inside. He observed the marvelous chamber around him. The king's chamber was a work of art. There would never be a more royal luxury greater than living in these quarters.

The king sat on his bed. He held an air of authority, like one who deserved such a high position. The king was elderly, but he oversaw his domain with fine efficiency.

"My most trusted servant, what brings you here at this time of night?"

Alsoir felt a pain in his side. He was only doing this to gain more money.

But, he thought, I already have plenty of riches.

Alsoir shook himself from his thoughts. He wasn't doing this for money -he was doing this on Shalhyaar's orders.

The knife slithered out from underneath Alsoir Grey Jaimian's cloak.

A rumble like thunder rose from the distance. Shalhyaar's army had arrived.

Long chapter, but big turn in plot!

 

Chapter 7:

 

Alsoir Jaimian walked down the corridor, leaving the king's room, but taking the guilt of his terrible deed with him. We follow him, but as we walk, Shalhyaar's army is attacking the city. Small fires begin to spring up, flicking their flames as a serpent flickers its tongue. Screams of despair rent the air as houses burn to ashes.

It is a sad day for those who dwell near the king's palace.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

About 20 days later...

Talan is going to be sold at the slave market. He steps up on a small platform and observes the crowd in front of him. Two richly dressed man appear to have their eye on Talan. They seem like enemies. One is dark haired and quite tidy; he values cleanliness. The other has brown hair and blue eyes. He is not as strait as the first. The auctioneer announces the beginning of the auction, and the bidding begins.

"50!"

"100!"

"120!"

After a short time of bidding, the fight comes between the two men we saw earlier. The prices are becoming very high.

"700!"

"740!"

"745!"

The price is beginning to even out. Talan watches intently, wondering which one will be his new master. It is the brown-haired man's turn to bid.

"755!"

"760!"

"765!"

A sly grin appeared on the tidy man's face as he set the next bid.

"800!"

The brown haired man showed a look of dismay. Talan held his breath. The man up next to bid looked like a good master, yet he seemed to be overcome by the other man, who was now starting to seem a bit cruel. Indeed, he would turn out to be a very cruel master. Suddenly with a gasp, Talan recognized the brown-haired man.

 

It was Alsoir Jaimian.
