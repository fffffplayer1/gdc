Sigil snapped the memory off and pulled back into his own head. During that stream of words, that man had said several things of interest. He played the babble back from the beginning.

Yes, there they were. There were few things Sigil had run into that could be described as "odd panther-lizard things." Combined with the name he had slapped into his companion, well...

No need to skip ahead. Being polite was always the first step in any action. He stepped forwards and shook the hand of the man who had identified himself as Sparrowhawk.
"Pleased to meet you, Mr. Sparrowhawk! My name..."

He trailed off. Sparrowhawk had suddenly gone completely dull and vacant. Staring off into space, a relaxed hand, everything. With the exact same grin on his face.

Sigil peered past Sparrowhawk to the cloaked man and the woman in the tattered lab coat. "Does he do this often?"

Sparrow watches as Dantès runs around and falls over. Obviously irritated that what had seemed to be a simple objective when he had started his mission, bring back Hovawart, had turned into such a mess. 

 

With a fire in his eyes (quite litteraly, they glowed a faint red as he did this.), he whips his arms out like a conductor, grabbing Dantès with invisible hands and levitating her directly in front of him. 

 

"Listen, you crazy woman! Your home IS GONE. Brick-Tech was DESTROYED twenty years ago in the DRE war. That Vortex there leads to Nexus Tower, probably the closest thing to the altruism and ingenuity (Sparrowhawk almost gagged as he said these words.) of Brick-Tech that you can find. I have been jumping around dimensions for who knows how many weeks now! I am ready to finish this fight. So if you aren't going by yourself, then I guess you'll have to be persuaded."

 

Sparrowhawk hurled Dantès through the Vortex.

 

He turned to Hovawart, Sigil, and the assorted group of soldiers and constructs from the Pocket Space.

 

"Alright. Lets go save your world. Then maybe we can figure out who did THIS", he gestured around at Crux, "to mine."

There are points in a person's life that they will always remember. For Dantès, each one of her badly fragmented lives remembers this day, this hour, this minute, this second. In her carefully constructed insanity, derived from having so many millions of differing life events shift in and out of her present consciousness at once, one unifying event suddenly brought them all together.

Sparrowhawk.

Each life remembers him at once, and not one will ever forget what he had done to hers. All of hers. One thing become clear as she falls endlessly through the portal...
