Chapter three. Cave life part 1.

Jo-jo had invited Ryo to stay in the cave during Ryo's "quest". Ryo had brought stacks of food with him... more than enough to last the two of them for the two months. The two would also go out fishing and ACTUALLY caught some... a lot actually.

But Jo-jo was getting tired of all the tinned vegetables and fish. But one day, Ryo had just come back from a walk with an excited expression on his face. "Guess what!" he cried. Jo-jo lay back on his stony sofa, "What, did ja find your cousin or something?" "No," said Ryo, "but I found somethin' to stop your bored face that you have at ev'ry dinner." Jo-jo sat up. Just as he did, Ryo tossed him a bow and some arrows. "We're goin' huntin'." said Ryo triumphantly. Jo-jo smiled. He had no experience with bows and arrows, but he was willing to do anything for real LEGO meat. So they set out from the cave and Ryo showed him an old path up a hill by the coast. The hill had some woods on the top. The two went into the woods and walked on for a bit. Ryo turned to Jo-jo and whispered to him. "I saw a herd of zahuks just to the left of here." "Zahuks?" asked Jo-jo, all confused. "Are they EVEN, an animal?" Ryo laughed as he walked on, "'Course they are! What did you except, them to be a plant?" Jo-jo smiled and went on with him. "Well, though I'm a Nexus 'legend' as you say, there are some things don't know." he said. "No! Really?" said Ryo sarcasticly. They walked on for a second and then Ryo suddenly stopped in his tracks and whispered to Jo-jo. "Make no noise at all, what ever you do... we're here... at the zuhak clearin'."

Cave life part 2.

Jo-jo looked through a bush. sure enough, there were meaty looking animals. Jo-jo watched as Ryo took an arrow and placed it in his bow. Ryo took aim and fired. ZING! A zuhak fell. The rest of the heard scattered as Ryo and Jo-jo came into the clearing to drag their prize off.

While they were taking their new food home, going down the hill, the heard a loud shout. "STOP! THIEF'S!" Jo-jo and Ryo looked 'round to see an older minifig charging down the hill with a flintlock rifle in hand. "Must be the local game keeper!" Ryo yelped. Ryo, you was holding the zuhak, turned and flew down the hill, into the sand dunes below. Jo-jo ran after him. When Jo-jo caught up with Ryo, he got this response: "Don't follow me! I'll go back to the cave and you lure the game keeper away, then come back." That Ryo sure thought quick thought Jo-jo, as he went of to lure the G-K away.

When Jo-jo caught sight of the old G-K, he quickly spoke to himself... not out loud though...: "The trick is to catch his attention and to lure him without raising his suspicion that it's a trick... so no 'OY! Look at me!' or 'follow me, you weirdo!' OK boy?"

So Jo-jo ran out of the dunes and zigzagged across the grasslands and the mudflats and beaches. The G-K saw this and sped after him. Jo-jo ran and ran, but he was getting tired and he was running out of breath!

Cave life part 3.

Suddenly, Jo-jo tripped over a rock! He fell into the grass that was on top of a lonely sand dune. The game keeper ran past, into the forest nearby, thinking that Jo-jo had darted in there. Jo-jo climbed out the dune scratched and cut,(anyone who's been to a beach with sand dunes on it, will know how painfully sharp the grass is.) He was shaking all over, unsuccessfully trying to brush the sand off himself. "Well," he thought, "at least he missed me by far." He was right, the Game Keeper was still running, through the massive forest, looking for the nasty thief that had raided the hill woods.

Jo-jo later found his way back to the cave, where he found Ryo sitting by a fire, roasting the zuhak on a spit above it. "What took you so long, bud?" asked Ryo mockingly, "fallen into a giant sand-dune, get lost on the way home and fall into a gorse bush field?" "As a matter of fact... yes." chuckled Jo-jo, sitting down across from Ryo.

When the meat was cooked, Ryo took it off the spit, and seasoned it with salt, and cut it up and served "dinner".

And that's what life in the cave was like from then on, whenever Jo-jo got tired of what pathetic food Ryo had with him... they'd go pay a visit on the angry game keeper and steal their prize.

Ah... life in the cave was good.

Chapter four. People of the mountains.

One night, after dinner around the fire, just outside the cave, Jo-jo and Ryo were telling each other silly stories, when Jo-jo heard something. A very faint but regular BONG!... BONG!... BONG! "Ryo, ya' hear that?" asked Jo-jo. "Sure bud," said Ryo, "It sound like some tribal gathering." The two listened for a bit more and then stood up and walked towards where the sound came from. It was coming from the top of the tallest mountain in the area, and and the sky was orange above it, so there must be some sort of fire there.

"Do you think we should go up?" asked Jo-jo. "I thought you were a member of the Venture League!" laughed Ryo. Jo-jo smiled and the two walked up the zigzag path to the top of the mountain. The noise got louder, and louder, and louder.

Eventually, the two reached the top... they saw a central camp fire, with an old man beside it. Sitting on the ground on with the fire in-between them, were to also old minifigs. There were people dressed in red, people dressed in white and people in black silk robes... each of the three colour groups looking just like Forbidden Valley ninjas.

They seemed to be sneaking up on the to men who were sitting down.

People of the mountains part 2.

Jo-jo and Ryo quietly walked up to the old man by the fire. "What's going on?" they both asked. The old man wasn't shocked or angry with them for intruding, he faced them and said, "Only the greatest test of strength and stealth in the world... the challenge of the ultimate warrior." The two friends were fascinated by this man, he had a sparkle in his eyes and had a nice smile and spoke clearly, but with a hint of mischief in his voice.

Meanwhile, the three groups of ninjas had turned invisible. The two old men on the ground sat perfectly still, with no sign of worry in their faces. It was an extraordinary sight. Suddenly for what seemed to be for no reason what so ever, the two men jumped up, still in sitting poses, and used their staffs to whack the air below them. There was a loud "OW!" coming from beneath the two and three ninjas, two black, one red, uncloaked and ran away, rubbing what ever area they were whacked in.

Jo-jo and Ryo were filled with awe. "Is this training for the ninjas?" asked Jo-jo. The old man, rubbed his beard and looked back at them, "Yes it is good training for them, but the real training is for my two sons; Hikaru and Hoprosgra." "Hoprosgra?" asked Ryo, "Is that even a name?" The old man, smiled and spoke:"Yes, and no, it wasn't a name but I called my youngest son that, because I couldn't think of any more good names... I took the name from: Grass hopper."

Hoprosgra, was very fast with his staff and struck every ninja who dared come close. Hikaru on the other hand, got out a boom box and started break dancing. The ninjas couldn't resist dancing too, and while the boogied, Hikaru, whacked them away with blows from his staff. Both had proved to be great warriors, despite their age.

People of the mountains part 3.


"You have down well, my sons." said the old man, when Hikaru and Hoprosgra came over. "You're training is now complete... you are now ready to protect the people of the mountains from the pirates of the forest of doom." "I should have seen that, coming..." said Jo-jo to Ryo, "... if there's ninjas around, it's probably because of either a pirate or a samurai threat... and both those are fierce enemies." "I can just imagine." said Ryo, thinking of being ambushed by vicious pirates.

The beating ninjas looked dead, lying on the ground, so still... and it was weird when the sat up, stood up and started talking. Some of them had quite bad bruises, from the staff blows, but on most of the stronger ones, hardly even a mark! "I'm surprised that your staff hasn't broke." said a strong older ninja to Hoprosgra. Hikaru was talking to some ninjas and citizens about his favourite music. "I like rap and rock best... as well as hip-hop..." he kept saying things like that. He was obviously VERY popular with the people.

The old man, known just as "the sensei" was talking with Ryo and Jo-jo, about funny things that had happened in the mountains over the past 90 years (He was 94 years old) and the two best buds, Jo-jo and Ryo had a great night, the sensei invited them to a party, and they talked and shared silly stories, had drinks... them and all the people had a blast... you could hear the chatter and laughter from miles away.
