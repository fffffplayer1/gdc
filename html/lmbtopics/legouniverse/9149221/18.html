Chapter 216

Wyatt's thought-out plan was brought to light when Gene inquired, "Would I be correct to assume that you have several team initiatives in mind for locating Tyrobe?" "Yes, Wyatt said. "Good," said Penelope, satisfied. "I know how much you're focusing on this mission, Wyatt, but just make sure you don't let it overtake you so much," advised Peyton gently. Wyatt looked at her concerned eyes, knowing what she was referring to. "I know," he quietly replied. "Know about what?" Cooper asked, warily. "It was nothing big," Wyatt said to brush the matter away. Penelope pressed on, though. "No, what happened?" Corbin and Gene regarded Wyatt with fretful stares. He reluctantly revealed, "When Peyton and I were discussing the matter with Zlytann, there was an... event that happened. I just got dizzy, nauseous. It was small." "It wasn't that small, if you're trying to say that," said Peyton. "Are you okay, Wyatt?" Cooper asked, both worried and taken aback by this news. "Of course I am," replied Wyatt. "I'm perfectly fine."

"Wyatt is always like that, at least from the way I've seen him work at Dr. Folson's lab," interrupted Penelope. "He's a big workaholic, always burning the midnight oil." "You are taking up the role as our pillar of determination and our shield of defense," Corbin commented. Gene proudly added, "And the glue of our partnership." "Definitely," agreed Peyton, sending Wyatt a bright smile. The corners of his lips curved up in a forced manner in general to the team, but he was feeling uncomfortable having this topic centered around him. Luckily they all sensed his uneasy demeanor and backed away, even Penelope, the most aggressive one of the group. After their dinner was finished with Cooper letting out a loud burp and Penelope stating, "Was that necessary?" - to which he just goofily grinned - they all exchanged their some last words of non-serious chitter-chatter amongst each other as they walked to the elevator.

Chapter 220

The phone clicked off, and with each subsequent call, the doctor would not answer. This made Wyatt very concerned; he wondered as to what caused the connection to cut off. Xavier himself had made sure that the UCTs were outfitted with only the best receptors. Wyatt shook his head and put the smartphone down on the bedside table. Then Wyatt instantly started to feel a queasy feeling in his head, and his vision was becoming incredibly blurred. It was the exact same frightening feeling he was experiencing earlier with Zlytann and Peyton in the vault level. But strangely, it ended in just a few moments and Wyatt returned to his healthy state. "Why does this keep happening?" he murmured to himself in thought, although he thought he knew the answer. He felt like the stresses of this whole mission and controlling his Super Nex powers were getting to him. Wyatt was hoping to break new ground soon, not only in terms of conceiving a plan to find the protana and Nekotaros, but also keeping his Super Nex energy strung together with his soul.

Wyatt was rolling these thoughts over and over in his mind as he went to the bathroom to complete his nightly routine duties. Once he finished with everything, he went back into the living room and sat down on his bed. He quickly took a notepad and pen from his bedside table, writing down a list. He found that if he used this process for certain situations like this, it helped organize everything into very neat mental compartments. He was going into detail as he wrote down the activities they had completed up to now, such as infiltrating the Maelstrom base at Gnarled Forest, finding Quentin Medanar's cave, discovering the six Lunatark portal pieces, and so on. The last three items on the list were 1. Finding Nekotaros and bringing him back to Lunatark, 2. Finding the looted protana and bringing it all back to Lunatark, and 3. Traveling back to LEGO Universe safely with the protana in tow.

The smoke trails also clumped up together to form two shapes reminiscent of solid fists. He punched his fists around in the air, and the smoke fists followed his exact motions.
