 LEGO Universe story: Project Super Nex

 

Chapter 206

 

Adira led Gene and Corbin to an antechamber where one of the spherical elevator pods was located. They went in and Adira entered in a command into the keyboard, and the vehicle zipped away through the temple's elevator tunnel, until they found themselves back in the royal chamber at the temple's topmost level. She led the brothers to a hallway off to the left, where several doors were on either side, leading into different rooms. Adira explained, "This is where my family and I live. I'll show you to my room, where the items are. I have feeling you'll appreciate these deeply." She had a knowing smile as she walked towards the second door on the left. When she opened it, she waved for Corbin and Gene to walk in. Their eyes were filled with wonder once they saw the environment. The first things they noticed were the many magnificent sculptures that adorned the area. They were in many contorted shapes and were very eye-catching, as was the rest of the strangely shaped furniture. There was even a pink couch that had several cushions supported up in the air with sturdy rods.

 

Corbin noted, "This is certainly decorated with a whimsical taste of sorts." Adira smiled. "Yes, I have always had an eye for peculiar designs." Gene nodded with approval. "Yes, I think we are all unanimous with this style choice." Adira gestured to the tarps that were hanging from hooks in the ceiling. The edges of the covered objects poked out from under the tarp. "Here is what I needed to show you. I can make extra copies for your comrades once you leave Lunatark." Corbin and Gene exchanged puzzled, curious smiles. When Adira walked over to the tarps, she pulled on some thin strings that were holding up the protective covers. The pulled strings loosened the tarps, allowing them to sprawl all over the floor. The tarp had been covering what looked like prototype inventions and there were small, metal parts unkemptly lying around in toolboxes.

Chapter 207

 

This reminded the brothers of their own house, except their tinkering materials were always stored in extremely organized shelving units. Adira noted, "Please excuse the mess. Sometimes, I'm not quite sure what parts should be added to the machines." Gene and Corbin both had stunned grins on their faces, and Gene said with fascination, "So you're a tinkerer, too!" "Yes," Adira said, smiling. "I've always helped Oraysae and Zyltann with electronics, so I've learned a trick or two about engineering." Corbin asked, "So what do all of these items do?" "Here's one that I think will be very useful to you on your next journey," Adira said, gesturing to something that was slightly smaller than a car engine and it had a very smooth, clean look to it. There were no wires, pipes, or extra mechanical pieces sticking out of it. The rounded edges, the silver color, and the dark green light placed on the top, made it look futuristic.

 

Adira explained, "This is an engine that can be used with pollution-free energy. There are small crystals, called fleksna, inside the engine that are easily mined from deposits near the surface in Lunatark. When rubbed together, they cause electric discharges that can be magnified if exposed to certain types of metals. It is designed to run off of the electric discharges and is able to store a battery full of energy for over three months. It also can't be affected by the types of e-bombs that Tyrobe was using earlier today - the ones that shuts off devices - because of its thick coating." Gene asked, "Why would we need this for our next journey?" He thought he knew what the reply would be, and he was correct when Adira responded, "Well, if you want to track down Tyrobe so much, surely you'll want some good engine power with you." Corbin asked uncertainly, "Are you sure that you could get permission from the Lunatark council?" Adira answered sheepishly, "In a way, I am the council leader. So, yes."

Chapter 208

 

"Come, I want you to see this." Adira walked over to the device that was next to the fleksna engine. It was a pair of foggy gray gauntlets that had black claws protruding from each end of the gloves' five fingers. An array of lit-up crimson red veins were right underneath the surface, running along the hands and fingers, appearing to supply the claws with great power. Small yellow bulbs surrounded the wrist, the innards subtly jiggling with a gel-like liquid. Adira picked up the pair of clawed gloves and explained, "These are what I have dubbed 'Pierce-Paws'. Just pierce a three-feet thick slab of metal with one claw, and the liquid inside will dissolve it. This only works on metal, so it won't harm any of your comrades in case of an accident." Then Adira put down the gloves and picked up a pair of slim black goggles with light orange-tinted lenses. Structural pipes ran around the goggles's rims, and the pipes' ends weaved in and around the lenses.

 

"This is a Visitra," Adira said. "They track where your eyes are moving so they can find what target to catch. Then when you press this button" - Adira paused to point to a gray button on the goggles's right side - "It will eject plasma-energized ropes to snag your target." Corbin and Gene both nodded with approval. Corbin wandered over to a table, and picked up a hook with a dangly rope hanging from its end. "Be careful!" Adira warned. But Corbin already felt tiny needles prick in his fingers. He dropped the hook on the table and rubbed his fingers. Adira went over to a cabinet, getting out ointment and bandages to treat Corbin. Gene asked with concern, "Are you okay?" "I'll be okay," said Corbin. "I think." Gene smiled, then looked around. "Adira, your creations are made with the utmost precision and ingenuity!" "Thank you," she said, obviously appreciating the compliment, and she spent the rest of the afternoon showing the two brothers the rest of her creations.

Chapter 209

 

Night had fallen, and Wyatt and Peyton had just finished studying their Hastirs. The minifigures walked out and took a transportation pod back to Opare Temple, where the other minifigures were. Exhausted, their eyes and minds were weary from absorbing so much information from the documents. Nonetheless, they conversed all along the ride about all of the fascinating information they had learned. They were both excited to soon share them with the rest of the crew. When they reached the temple, they were both stunned to see how much successful restoration work there was. There were now multiple robots who were competently mending all of the structural injuries. There were less cracks and holes, and almost all of the columns and statues were being restored to their original glory. Wyatt and Peyton went inside the ground lobby to take the elevator pod to the guest room level.

 

The two minifigures stopped when they heard familiar voices yelling their names. They whipped their heads around to see Cooper and Penelope running down the hallway, trying to reach their friends. Wyatt exclaimed with relief, "Hey, guys, it's great to see you! How have you been?" Penelope replied restlessly, "We have a lot to tell you!" Cooper asked, "Wyatt, how come you weren't answering my calls? I called you five times!" He was obviously and humorously exasperated. "I was inside the Lybri, a huge library filled with Hastirs," Wyatt answered. "Must've not gotten any reception. What's up?" "And speaking of the Hastirs," Peyton interjected, "We have loads to share with you as well." Wyatt asked, "But what happened with you guys, though?" Cooper explained, "We talked to Oraysae and told him what you said in the voice message. About how we needed to find Tyrobe and get help from Lunatark, and all that jazz.
