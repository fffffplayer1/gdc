General Aurelius sat in his new armchair and sipped his tea. No, no, it was just Mr. Aurelius now. The war is over. It was over ten years ago. But it still bore its marks on Mr. Aurelius, in the form of an artificial leg and numerous scars. Sometimes he forgot. He would wake up to his alarm, and think it a siren warning of approaching danger. Strangers would bump into him on the street, and he would overreact.

 

But such is the price one pays. Former High General Aurelius was in charge of the Dark Ring Empire’s ground forces, back in the day. He was the best of the best, and many battles were won by his cunning and prowess. Privates would stand straight at the very mention of his name, and jokes would be made about how hard he worked his troops, but everyone knew he was the best General they’d ever meet.

 

Those days are over now, though. Today, Mr. Aurelius sat in his chair and took another sip. He turned his head, and looked out the window. He had a fantastic view of Piltover, from his home high in the clouds. He saw shining towers like his own, flying cars zipping around followed by colored streams. Far below, smaller buildings, and people walking along the streets.

 

He looked back at this place he called his home. A nice enough place, he supposed. Bucky had made it just for him and his son. The Aurelius banner hung on the wall above the mantle, providing a backdrop his ceremonial sword. The fire below crackled softly, and lit the walls. Various awards and medals were placed there, which only served to remind him of the past.

 

But to Aurelius, this pace was not his home. His home was the bridge of his command cruiser, in a bunker with his men, in the Ring’s war room, overseeing the latest offensive.

 

But all of those places are gone now.

 

And nothing could be done about it.

 

So he sat in his armchair and sipped his tea.

-BuckyKat, The Kingmaker

The teleportation anchor crackles to life, the runes glowing an intense green, before a tearing sound and a flash of light. Savos strides off of the platform, and heads for a visitor kiosk. He loots a map as he walks past it, towards the downtown area of piltover, deftly avoiding the increasing crowds of pedestrians, all the while intently studying the map. He hangs an abrupt right, nearly upsetting an unfortunate newspaper salesman, mutters an apology, and heads straight into the real estate office.

 

Once inside, he heads for the first lackey he lays eyes on. A quick flash of a card gets him ushered into the head agent's office, where several other flashes of cards, and a letter with Bucky's seal on it quickly obtain him the deeds to a nice plot of beachfront land. Several other trips and card flashes later, and he has a sporty new hovercar, a DI apprentice in tow, and a pair of stylish sunglasses.

 

When they arrive at the beach after a trip of several hours, Savos spreads a sheet of paper on the hood of the car and starts to draw up plans. The apprentice, relieved to be free of Savos singing along with the radio, goes and sits on the beach. After some time has passed, Savos finishes the plans, then takes his turn reclining on the beach, and watches his house rise from the earth.
