"I wouldn't dream of telling." Miranda sits down accross from Bucky and places a large leather briefcase on his desk. From within she draws a single scrap of paper and slides it over the polished wood surface.

 

"The DGS Firm does all sorts of business. I myself am from the more legal side of things. I don't normally do what I am about to do, but the Firm thought it best."

 

She flips over the document.

 

"I, Lieutenant Jean-Jacques Lafayette de Montagne, in being of sound mind and memory, do set for my final will and testament. These are my wishes to be carried out as I have stated forthwith.

 

My only desire be that my wife stands to inherit all that I own. Baring her own untimely end, she stands to receive my fortune and holdings upon my own demise.

 

If, however, she is unable to claim what is left to her due to her own passing away, I name Bucky Kat the sole heir of my possessions."

 

Below the writing stands the signatures of several witnesses as well as the date of signing. 

 

"Mr. Kat. It has been 15 years since either Lafayette or his wife have been seen. I brought to you only the meat of Lafayette's will. A clause states that if after a certain period of time he has dissappeared and his wife absent, you are to inherit everything. This is why I am here today. That time is up."

Bucky furrows his eyebrows.

“Impress me with the Firm’s capabilities? I have no proof that your Firm even exists, save for that paperwork, your word, and the location of some quartz.”

 

Bucky takes a framed photo from his desk and turns it so that Miranda can see. It pictures Bucky, many years ago, in a lab coat bearing the NF logo, with a group of similarly clad individuals, presumably his co-workers.. They are celebrating something held in Bucky’s hands, some small bit of technology, presumably of some importance.

 

“You should know something about me, though. I don’t work by myself. My accomplishments, my feats, my reputation, are not so much truly mine, as they are my allies’. I am not truly incredible. I am not a one man army. I am a leader.”

 

Bucky sets the photo back on the desk and turns his chair to gaze out the window behind him. The ocean now replaced with Piltover, sitting on the coast.

 

“I don’t know the first thing about keeping tabs on anything. But I know a guy, he goes by Talon. Little guy, short, kind of mousey. He worked as a spy for the Nexus Force, and was spying on the Grand Council from the inside. I found him out, and converted him for our cause. Today, he’s aged too much to do much actual spying, but he leads my intelligence department, and is likely listening in on this very conversation.”

 

Bucky swivels his chair back around, and looks Miranda in the eye.

“If you only want to work with me, I’m afraid you’ll be rather disappointed, because I’m not much.”

"Don't ask where this came from, but take it as truth. This is the book found by Lafayette during your time in the Codex. To his credit, he did well to write out the events of that expedition. In fact, it seems everything that transpired in that place was recorded. You are not a one man army, but you are what you said: a leader. Sure, collaboration is a fine thing, but you, as an individual, have influenced everything and everyone around you since the day you showed up. You show up and things happen. You show up and progress is made."

 

Miranda paces back and forth, clearly in her element as she speaks, never breaking eye contact.

 

"I'll call your attention to that list of items you have inherited. A metal pod was among them. That is something The Firm wants you to investigate. You will lead a team, you will open that pod, and you will figure out what is inside. That is it, plain and simple. But I will personally tell The Firm you are a dried up old codger unfit for anything more than standing as a figurehead if you refuse to see that you have done more than stand there and hold important things."

 

She stops with arms akimbo.

 

"I loved my sister and had the greatest respect and admiration for her husband. They both put their faith in you, Mr. Kat. I expect you to live up to their claims."

In the Piltover Public Museum, a croud of citizens are gathered around a display. The display is a large Vortex Generator from Ringworld, salvaged from the immediate wreckage. With a little work, the Giovannians have got it working, its spinning, zero-grav DI Vortex proving to be a nice attraction. 

 

Suddenly, to the pedestrian's surprise, a man comes flying out of the Vortex. He is holding a bulky rifle. He stands up, looks around, and adopts a smug grin. Dusting off his shoulders, he walks past the people, and out the door, his longcoat billowing dramatically. This man is, of course, Sparrowhawk.

 

As he walks into the ever-sunny Downtown Piltover, he lets out a sigh of releif. He was dead meat back there. Suddenly he remembered. Back There! Deady was still back there with an army of DRE warbots! He broke into a run, finding a Vortex Arch. Quickly keying in Bucky's office number, the Vortex fizzled to life, and Sparrowhawk ran in. 

 

Finding himself standing in front of Bucky and Miranda, He tries to put down the rifle. He just ends up dropping it with a crash.

 

"Oh. Uhhh, hi. I'm Ambris Raptorum, you can call me Ambris, or Sparrowhawk. Everyone else does. I usually look slightly better than this, its just that I was just attacked by 300 twenty year old homocidal warbots and their saxaphone playing insane leader. Thats kinda what I needed to talk to Bucky about. Bucky, do you have time, or are you busy?"
