Chapter 5 – Dust

 

I heard the control tower officer’s voice coming through my headphones. “This is Nexus Tower Flight Control. The Nexus Searcher has permission to land. Medical crews are waiting at the landing platform.” “Thanks, mate!” I exclaimed in relief. I looked back at the others. They seemed to be doing okay, but I wasn’t sure. I began to put my full concentration into piloting the ship. I was getting close to the platform now. “Okay, and If I can just put the landing gear into position…” I said as I clicked a button. Nothing happened. I tried the landing gear again. Nothing. “Oh, great! Now I’m trying to land a ship with no landing gear!” I moaned aloud. I heard my crewmembers groaning in the back. I took a deep breath. I had to help them. I racked my brain for an idea. Suddenly, one came to me. It was crazy, but it just might work. “This is the Nexus Searcher!” I called into my radio. “My landing gear isn’t working, so we’ll have to parachute out! I’m going to tie my crewmembers to myself, so be ready for four minifigures to drop out of the blue!” I heard the flight control telling me something, probably that I was loony, but I clicked off the radio. I jumped up from my seat and strapped a parachute to my back. I then tied my four comrades to myself. I struggled under their weight, but Idont Feelwell was getting better, and she helped me steady the others. I looked out the window, timing my jump. “What are we doing?” Veri moaned. “We’re going tandem skydiving, mates!” I said as I threw open the door and jumped out.

Chapter 6 – Steele

 

I stomped up to my bed and pulled out the drawer underneath. I grabbed my clothes and stuffed them into my backpack.

“I’ll show them! I’ll start from the beginning and work my way up! I’ll earn their respect!” I fumed.

I gathered the last of my things, and looked around. I pulled my pet Doberman, Dagger out of my pack. He barked and licked my hand.

“Come on, Dagger; let’s go smash something in Gnarled Forest!” I said, hoping that I could cheer myself up.

We rushed over to the launch pad to Nimbus Station. I pulled out my Startalon rocket, and we soared away to Nimbus Station. Once we landed, Dagger and I went to Nimbus Plaza, and then we launched away towards Gnarled Forest, enjoying our last day of freedom. We landed on an elevated platform, and crossed the Ravine. I led the way to the Elephant Escarpment, when we encountered a Stromling Ape.

“Take that!” I screamed as I slashed away at it with my Great Shortsword. I took out my anger on the evil creature, when it roared and began to retaliate. But, I wasn’t used to having weak Rank 1 armor.

“Ahh!” I screamed in pain as it hit me, hard. I tried to fight back, but it was no use. As black spots of pain danced before my eyes, it smashed me, and I passed out.

Chapter 7 – Dust

 

The wind ripped at my face, and my three friends were all moaning. It was great fun!

“AHH!” Sik screamed.

“This is almost as fun as doing it in old Down Under!” I shouted.

Suddenly, I noticed the landing platform approaching. I found my parachute cord, and pulled it, as hard as I could. We four jerked up, as the parachute opened.

“Ugh!” Veri moaned as we gently floated down to the platform.

“Watch out!” I yelled to the Nexus Force members below.

We landed on the pavement, and I unbuckled myself from the parachute. I stood up.

“Hello, mates!” The crowd of medics, flight control operatives, mechanics, and curious bystanders stared at me in silence.

“Well, these brave warriors need medical care, so I suggest you get to work.” I told the medics.

They snapped out of their shock and began loading my crewmembers onto stretchers, taking pulses, and doing whatever it is that they do.

They had carried my friends inside, and I was just about to pose for a photograph, when a mechanic pointed at the sky and shouted, “Look out!”

I turned my head and saw the Nexus Searcher dropping towards us.

“Run!” I yelled as everyone stampeded off the landing area. Just as soon as the last minifig made it off, the Searcher crashed into the platform in a spectacular explosion.
