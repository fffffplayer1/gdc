Silver’s POV: Any Town is a Ghost Town
James has really let this go to his head. I mean, I like superheroes just as much as anyone else, but this is going a little too far. He’s built some kind of comic-book base in an attempt to become one. I never really liked comic books because of one thing: Every single time, the villain kidnaps the hero’s friends/allies and the hero can’t do anything about it. Now, who is first in line for kidnapping? Unfortunately, the answer to that question is yours truly, Silver. In other words, me.
However, I am a ghost. They can’t touch me. That’s right, I’m a ghost. To be honest, I don’t really know what happened. One moment I’m inside a giant monster that’s attempting to destroy the universe, then it all goes dark and suddenly I’m flying through walls.
Being a ghost(or whatever I am) does have its benefits. For example, no one can see me if I don’t want them to. I’ll never need a doorknob. I can snoop like no one’s snooped before. With me, any town is a ghost town. Okay, I’m sorry. I didn’t mean to make a joke that bad…
Also, Halloween is much, much more fun. MUAHAHAHAHA!
The downsides: I can’t touch anything. I just go right through it. I need a cheeseburger, and cannot eat one. All of my senses have shut down except for hearing and sight. I can still talk to people, although I have no idea what me voice sounds like to them, they can understand me.
“Miss Silver?”
I floated downstairs to greet the approaching pirate, one of the crew I had inherited from my adoptive father, Lord S.
“James Harmony has gone out on another rooftop run. I think he said something about a ‘jackal’.”
Jackal, eh?
“A jackal, the animal, or is there some new ‘villain’ called The Jackal?”
“I think it’s a villain.”
He barely had time to look surprised before I swept past him and rushed through the door. When most people rush through a door, they open it. Me…
James had departed into a potentially hazardous situation without telling me. This superhero thing was getting out of hand; I needed to teach him a lesson.

James, you’ll never see me coming. No one ever does.

Jackal's POV: Awakenings
I staggered across the Avant Gardens landscape, struggling to regain control of my mind. I was trying to get back to Nexus City, where all my friends were.
I just got my mind back when a speedy, roof jumping shinobi, landed in front of me. This wasn't any ol' shinobi; this was one of the few that I myself trained. At the sight of each other we paused, stared at each other, and then looked at my back pack which was lying a couple meters away.
He began to dash at it while I, staying in my spot, unholstered my pistol, (Which was the only weapon I was wearing at the time.) and began to open fire at him. The shinobi managed to dodge three shots, while the other three hit their mark; squarely in the left shoulder.
"RAAAH!" he yowled in pain, but kept going on. I did way to good a job. Using a few bricks from the research center, I built a one use car, drived to the bag, picked it up, and drove to the launch area.
But he didn't give up, now he was after me. I immediately equipped my buccaneer gear, only I used my cutlass and pistol instead. My car went into a u-turn. I jumped out of the lousy excuse for scrap metal (after all, it is a one use car) and it went speeding toward my pursuer.
He yowled in surprise when the car went toward him. He jumped over it and ran at me. Readying his hook swords, he kicked me in the chest. He swung one sword at me, only to have it lock with my sword.
"What's your name kid?" I asked.
"James, James Harmony." he unlocked his sword and swung again, and again we locked.
"Didn't I train you?" I aimed my pistol at his leg.
"Yes you did. You did a good job to-" I fired my gun. It wasn't a fatal wound but it would have to do.
I ran toward the launch pad and flew to Nexus City.

Okay, I've finally finished my review!! And here it is:

Overall, this is not a great story. However, very few multi-writers are on the first try. So you’re doing okay. However, you should try to improve before the next book, or you may be sunk. I’ve decided to separate the review by author. So first off, here are the things all of you need to work on:

1) Communication. More than once a lack of proper communication has endangered this story. This is less of a story-writing tip, but it is just as essential, especially in a multi-writer.

2) Plan ahead, and take it slowly. This story so far has been all dramatic reveals with no buildup. A story with reveals should be at least 2/3 buildup and only 1/3 reveal, otherwise the reveal is ruined. Let me put that another way. You know the story of the tortoise and the hare? Every time you read it the Tortoise always wins! Be the tortoise, not the hare.

3) This story is a bit random, and not even in a funny way. Your action sequences are great, but more often than not they left me wondering who the characters were fighting and why. Random acts of violence are very bad. That will be the end of this story if it isn’t stopped soon. To keep this from happening, remember these six words: Who, what, when, where, why, how. Who does what? Why and how do they do it? When and where is it happening? ALL of these are essential, and so far I haven’t seen much of the ‘why’. ‘Why’ is probably the most essential of the six. Yes, Black Knight attacked James in the first chapter, but why? Without the why, or more specifically, the motivation, an adventure crumbles to merely a gaggle of blokes wandering about without a reason.

Now, on to the authors! Due to the 600 character limit, they will be in later posts.

Grieves:
Okay, there are two things you should know here. One, you remind me of Praxer: you’re the leader and you get angry. Sorry, that was bad, but still, there is a meaning to it. Just like Praxer, you must learn to control you’re anger, and just like Praxer, you must learn to be the leader your team wants you to be. This is an extremely important lesson to learn, and I can’t stress it enough.

The other thing is that you need to be more original. Seriously. Taking someone else’s story, changing a few of the names, and then calling it your own isn’t jut disliked- it’s plagiarism. Doing that is a lose-lose situation: if the reader knows the story you’re ripping off, it annoys them, but if they don’t, it’s that much harder to understand what’s happening. I have never read Warriors, so that part really left me wondering what the crux was going on. There is no benefit of using someone else’s work, but there is every benefit of making up your own!

There is, however, one exception to this rule. If you REFERENCE something as a humorous joke, it can be very effective. The moment based on the Prince’s Bride left me laughing, and that’s good. However, if the reference is not meant to make the reader laugh, it will lead to no good things. “Alfred” was the last straw, that was just going too far. You are very lucky that Ryous Vortex saved your butt by turning it into a joke. But if you think I’m favoring him, don’t worry; he has his share of problems too.

Grieves:
Okay, there are two things you should know here. One, you remind me of Praxer: you’re the leader and you get angry. Sorry, that was bad, but still, there is a meaning to it. Just like Praxer, you must learn to control you’re anger, and just like Praxer, you must learn to be the leader your team wants you to be. This is an extremely important lesson to learn, and I can’t stress it enough.

The other thing is that you need to be more original. Seriously. Taking someone else’s story, changing a few of the names, and then calling it your own isn’t jut disliked- it’s plagiarism. Doing that is a lose-lose situation: if the reader knows the story you’re ripping off, it annoys them, but if they don’t, it’s that much harder to understand what’s happening. I have never read Warriors, so that part really left me wondering what the crux was going on. There is no benefit of using someone else’s work, but there is every benefit of making up your own!

There is, however, one exception to this rule. If you REFERENCE something as a humorous joke, it can be very effective. The moment based on the Prince’s Bride left me laughing, and that’s good. However, if the reference is not meant to make the reader laugh, it will lead to no good things. “Alfred” was the last straw, that was just going too far. You are very lucky that Ryous Vortex saved your butt by turning it into a joke. But if you think I’m favoring him, don’t worry; he has his share of problems too.

Major , there NEED to be loose ends. This is three , maybe four , possibly six if the others agree with what I'm planning. All will be revealed in due time. Of course some call me mad and insane and I suppose they're right. But I hear the voices of the world. Some don't belive me. Some do. Your choice wether or not to belive the truth.
