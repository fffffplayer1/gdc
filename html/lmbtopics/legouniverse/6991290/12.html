A/N: I won't be using all italics from here on out.

Today was utterly exhausting.
     We began by Xeron waking me at dawn. It was quite dark, with a slight drizzle outside, and everything seemed still. Xeron handed me a pistol for my armament, then showed me how to climb down from our floor. It was a great deal harder than I had imagined it would be, and my arms quivered by the time my feet touched the Earth. Xeron (who, I may note, climbed down with ease) commented that I'd get used to the descent. I hoped he was right.
     After that, Xeron and I began trekking due north. I felt a tad timid, but my senses were also on high alert for Stromlings. We soon came across a large, concrete trench, which Xeron said was part of the city's water supply. We then walked parallel to the trench, until we came across a skyscraper that had fallen across the trench. We used this as a bridge, though not as a very stable one; several chunks of the building's walls fell off as we walked upon it. After that, Xeron took me farther north, past leveled, three-story strip malls and warehouses that were sans their roofs. There was a still, dead feel to the cold morning air.
     After a few more minutes, the landscape began to change. The buildings grew larger, and some sported cracked and leaning smokestacks. Graffiti appeared on larger scale. A noxious odor of chemicals and rot hung in the air, and sometimes, I believed I heard a distant clang of metal, as if some far-off, unseen ghost were still working away in this tormented place. Then I noticed that the sky had become an slightly lighter shade of black. Dawn was nearing.
     Soon, Xeron instructed me to stop. Confused, I did so. When I attempted to ask what was happening, he made me be quiet. The man looked suspicious.
"When I shoot, run to that building and wait for me," Xeron whispered, pointing at what looked like a chemical depot. He then raised a pistol of his own, fired it into the air, and ran away from me as fast as he could.

As soon as he did this, something began happening. I became aware of several distant sounds, none of which sounded pleasant in the least. I hesitated, wanting to ask Xeron just what he believed was doing, before abandoning that thought and running in the direction of the chemical depot. Upon closer inspection, I saw that it was a crumbling, white brick building with several metal vents and chutes coming out of its sides. One of the chutes was dripping some kind of gooey, pale blue substance onto the cracked sidewalk. And, upon my reaching of the building, I pushed myself into a narrow, one-way alley, and waited as the noises grew louder. After a few more seconds, I realized something: the things making the sounds were Stromlings.

Another shot was fired into the air. The noises briefly grew louder, before quieting down again. Then, the Stromlings raced in front of the alleyway's entrance. I hid behind a dented, overflowing dumpster upon their passing, so I did not see their horrible forms. But I cuold hear them well enough, and that was enough to make me involentarilly shudder.

Awhile after that, when the noise of the Stromling horde had faded and I had cautiously come out from the alley, I saw Xeron coming towards me. I wanted to ask him a very great deal of questions, but he silenced me the moment I opened my mouth. "Not until we're inside," he whispered. He then motioned for me to climb into the largest of the ducts, which looked like it could just barely accommodate my body. I cautiously obeyed his order, slowly pushing myself up the duct. It smelled very potently of something sulfurous. Once I climbed out of the duct, I found myself in a small room with various empty, rusted buckets in it. There was some sort of pump to my left.

"Help me through this, will you?" Xeron asked from inside the chute. I pulled him up with both my hands; he was heavier than I thought. Once he was inside, I turned to him and said, "What were you thinking?"

Xeron sighed, as if were a simpleton. "It was a diversion. I neded to get any nearby Stromlings out of the area. They'll swarm you in no time if you're nearby. Oh, and, in case you don't realize it, we were qualifying as 'nearby'. Is that a simple enough explaination for you?"

I simply nodded. No need to say anything further, I thought. However, I did not think it fair that Xeron expected me to know what he was doing. It was not as if I had recieved any real training.

"So, Lyn. I want to know something. Where are you from?" Xeron asked, looking about the room for the first time. "Paradox? The Low Ends? Up north, maybe?"

"I'm from the Ark, sir."

Xeron stopped, as if had been put on pause. "Where?"

"The First Ark. That is the first place I remember being, anyways."

"That's.. not possible."

I looked at him. "And yet I am here."

Xeron scrutinized my face for a second. He then told me, "Alright, Lyn, I'm going to say something, and I want you to say the first thing that comes to mind after I say it. Okay?"

"Well... Okay."

"Periodic identification request for number..."

"...001." I finished, almost automatically. I was surprised myself that anything had happened, but Xeron looked completely taken aback. He even recoiled. "You're Model One," he muttered. "You... You're..."

He then turned away with a haunted look in his eyes. He opened the door room's tarnished metal door and passed through the open doorway; I did the same. This landed us atop a balcony which looked over a large, half-underground warehouse. The cracked, concrete floor was painted in several colors, the window-frames were missing their windows, and vats, tables, and hulking metal contraptions littered the area. There was metal shelving on all sides, with hundreds of chemicals, powders, liquids, and compounds sitting on the stained shelves, though many of the materials had been spilled. A miniscule amount of light poured in from the broken windows, but otherwise, the place was almost pitch-black.

"What happened here?" I asked, as we began descending into the room via a small auxilairy staircase.

"I think it'd be more accurate to ask what happened everywhere," Xeron replied, reaching the bottom of the staircase. He began searching the shelves for anything of use.

"Alright, then... What happened everwhere?"

"It was a war. A very viscious one, in fact. See, these Stromling didn;t come from some random lab accident. They have been here for a very long time."

"How long of a time?"

"I dont know. I don't think anybody does."

Xeron picked up a canister labeled "Potassium Nitrate" before continuing. "Anyways, there was this orginization that was made to protect the public from these Stromlings. The Nexus Force, they were called. They were composed of four Factions: The Sentinels, the Assembly, Venture League, and Paradox, and each faction had a leader. They thought that this was a good idea. But war is a very stressful thing."

As Xeron ransacked the shelfes, he told me of what had become of everything. The Stromlings as a collective were growing stronger, getting better at what they did, and becoming harder and harder to smash. The faction leaders became more and more stressed, and tensions were becoming high. Eventually, a few small disputes turned into large arguements, with in turn escalated the tension even higher-so high, in fact, that war broke out among the factions. Soon, an all-out war was afoot, with not only the factions involved, but other smaller, minor groups attempting to otherthrow the factions, even annihilate them. And, due to the war, Stromlings were getting past the Nexus Force and into other, uncorrupted areas. Soon, all the Factions realized that they were close to the brink of destruction, but it was too late. Therefore, the factions took their greatest weapons and took out as much of the Maelstrom as possible. Before this, however, they built structures called Arks so that they could survive this wouldwide destruction.

‎04-26-2014 01:31 PM

The Nexus Force were forced to stay inside their Arks as the world around slowly recovered. But even their Arks failed, in one way or another. One was built deep underground; it lost its air supply twenty years from its being built. The surviving persons made their way up to the surface, where they founded the first settlement. This settlement was called Black Mountain, for the nearby terrian was a rocky flatland of black rock spires. I am told that that settlement is still thriving today.

Eventually, more and more of the Arks failed, and more and more people were driven into the world they had broken. New settlements were made, and with them came the old ideals of the Nexus Force's factions. Soon, dispute broke out among old Faction members. However, not wishing to cause another war, they decided to create territories for themselves to live in.

"The Assembly and Venture League had their teritories, but they ended up just disbanding and mixing into the rest of the crowd after awhile. The Paradox and the Sentinels are both still going strong, though. And for no reason other than to spite the other side."

Xeron looked up, then picked out one more canister, before putting the things he'd found into a backpack he was wearing. I myself looked up as well; the sky had turned much lighter since our arrival.

"We need to get going," Xeron said, heading back towards the balcony. "Stromlings are drawn to light; that's what keeps them outside during the day."

However, before we reached the stairs leading up to the balcony, Xeron stopped for a moment, and then turned around. I followed him, confused, up to a rusty metal ladder that lead to the roof.

"The streets are too risky right now. We'll take the rooftops. Tread gently."

We climbed up the ladder and up to a metal trapdoor that required a good deal of pushing to get open. After that, we began to head in the direction of the Tower. The sky was a dark shade of grey, but a few small holes in its cover revealed a grey dawn sky.
