‎08-29-2013 01:18 AM

"Now jus' put yer hand on that there rock, an' we'll go on," Roswell said, pointing to the wall in front of me. I had my doubts about the sanity of the Sentinel, but complied. And as I did, pain coursed through my hand. Blistering heat and shivering cold assaulted my palm, each one seeming to conspire together to cause extreme pain. With a distressed cry, my hand seemed to fly off the rock by itself. Roswell! What had he-

I turned around to see the man laughing, along with the rest of his squadron, and even Avi and Padrex. Versil was standing around, confused. And I was quite annoyed.

"Precisely what was the purpose of that?" I snapped. Roswell struggled to contain his laughter for a moment, and then addressed me. "Ya dumb Paradox never built a DNA scanner? Yer sure stupid, in that case."

"I am nothing of the sort!" I retaliated, but Roswell had gone back to laughing at me. "And for the last time, I am not affiliated with the Paradox Faction!"

 

After eleven more DNA scans (an interesting fact: apparently, DNA scans only cause pain the first time they are initiated-therefore, only Versil was subject to pain), the rock wall was revealed to actually be a sliding panel. I was amazed; it had seemed like the panel was a part of the cliff.

"Now 'fore ya go in there, I need you to promise me you ain't gonna pull any tricks," Roswell warned me. "That means no wrench, an' no lightin' guns, an' no gooey, somethin' or other guns."

Reluctantly, we handed our weapons over.

"An' in we go!" Roswell said, cheerfully hefting my wrench over his shoulder. His cohorts followed, and we found ourselves in a small room, with a futuristic train in front of us. What alarmed me was, placed on turrets on the walls, machine guns. Machine guns pointed at our faces.

"Password!" A female voice called. Roswell groaned. "Burger county!" He said plaintively. I might have heard him say something about idiotic passwords, but at least the machine guns were not pointed at us anymore.

I'm a magical pony flying through the sky on a magical journey for you and me!

‎09-03-2013 01:18 AM

Note: I now present to you, the character with the coolest pair of shades ever made... Sage.

The doors of the train slid open with a hiss, and we entered. No windows were visible from the interior or exterior, and the metal comprising the sides seemed to have been patched up very many times. Several empty, padded benches lined the walls, tattered, but still usable. We each proceeded to sit down on one. The doors then slid shut, sealing us inside.

"Why is no-one else inside the train?" I questioned Roswell. He grunted, yet again. "Too many questions, boy!" And just as he said this, I heard whistling.

In strode someone who can only be described as awesome, no matter how cliche the word may be.

The first thing one would notice was her sunglasses. Polished and semi-tinted, each lenses glowed softly with an electric blue, pulsing energy. Then you would notice the suit she wore. It was a navy blue, yet also slightly gray, and had lines of the same blue energy of her glasses crisscrossing itself. A belt on the suit carried two guns with more of the blue energy, ammunition, and a few strange cylindrical objects. Something akin to a motorcycle helmet, but more militaristic, completed the outfit. The whistling stopped as soon as the figure saw us.

"Roswell, who's this?" The figure asked, tranquil fury edging her voice like diamond-edged blades. From her voice I could tell she was female.

"Long story, Miss Sage." Roswell replied, grinning in an unfriendly manner. "Take 'em to Alpha."

For some reason, the woman's expression changed from disguised fury to slyness. "Yeah... That's a good idea."

By this point I was getting an unfortunate feeling in the pit of my stomach. This was most certainly going to end in an execution of some sort.

"Hold on a second, who's Alpha?" Padrex piped up from the seat across from me. The woman name Sage smiled nastily.

"You'll find out soon enough, Paradox."

By this point I was sure our story did not seem like truth to these people.

Note: Sorry about the latency of this chapter; I couldn't post last night for IRL reasons. Also a good thing to know: don't read this part yet; wait until I post the next part, and the part after that, then read both parts while listening to 'I AM NOT A MORON!' for best results.

After the alarming exchange, Sage walked out of our car through a previously unseen doorway-she must have entered that way-and evidently started our train. Beeping, whirring, and clacking could be heard, along with ominous rumbling that could not have made by anything but something gigantic. I saw it fit to try and stop worrying, but that task is near impossible when one would be in a situation such as ours. Were we on the verge of imprisonment? Or, worse, slavery? Or perhaps we were even about to be smashed? Tortured? I shivered at the exponentially increasing possibilities as the noises stopped.

Then the train began to move.

A huge crack sounded from nowhere, throwing me to the side like a slap from a giant. All of us shouted (or screamed annoyingly loud for comedic effect, in Padrex's case) as we nearly tumbled off our seats; a sound that could only have signified high speed resounded through the car. The train whooshed along an unseen pair of tracks, flinging us around as if we had entered a demented roller-coaster. I even believe that that was the entire purpose of the ride. We twisted and turned for what seemed to be about five minutes (but was probably only two), our nerves jangling the entire time. The worst part, however, was that we did not know what would come next; therefore, we could not brace ourselves for any of the insane ride's turns. We had just gone over a particularly steep hill when the ride lurched to a stop, throwing us into a heap at one end of the train. We managed to pull ourselves together before one wall of the train began to move, splitting apart in the middle to reveal the minifigure who, as the reader will soon see, was about to decide our fates.

Alpha.
