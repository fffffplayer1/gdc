Death’s Colleague- Prologue

It was a bitter day in Avant Gardens. The weather was cold and damp, and the few fresh recruits who had been left behind were huddled in a small bunch, shivering in fright.

An emergency in Nimbus Station had gone down, and the few superior recruits who stay in Avant Gardens flew to Nimbus Station to help out. They had left behind fifteen scared recruits who had just come off of the Venture Explorer to ward off the maelstrom at Avant Gardens. This was a foolish decision on their part, because when I say they just got off the Venture Explorer, I’m not exaggerating a winch. Two of them had already been smashed by a mech and the other thirteen had run and hid in a small crevice.

One of the thirteen heard something and turned to his neighbor.

“Hey!” he whispered, “did you hear something?”

“No,” said the other recruit, “did you?”

“I don’t know,” said the first one, “but I thought I heard the sound of wings flapping and heavy breathing.”

The second minifigure gulped and said in a hushed voice; “I’ve heard that they have dragons in the inner regions of the Nimbus system.”

The first recruit shivered.

“Dragons?” he said, “but there hasn’t been a dragon in Avant Gardens since the second age, and this is the fifth!”

“You’re right,” he said, “you must have just imagined it.”


Beck Strongheart was kneeling over his map when he heard the scream. He picked up his sword and rushed out of the Sentinel Base Camp.

“Hello!” yelled Beck, “is anyone there?”

“Beck! Over here!” said a voice.

Beck looked to his right and saw Epsilon Starcracker, who had evidently heard the scream as well. He ran over to where Epsilon was hovering and stopped dead in his tracks. What had been hidden by the lip in the ground was now made visible to him.

Thirteen smashed bodies lay on the ground in front of him. Some of them were in piles, while others had their pieces scattered all over the place. He walked among the fallen soldiers and examined their faces. The youngest could not be more than ten, and the oldest could not be more then sixteen.

“What happened here?” Beck asked Epsilon.

“I have no idea,” Epsilon replied sadly, “I was talking with Wisp Lee and heard a scream, I came over here as fast as I could, but all I saw a massive black form flying over there in the distance.”

Beck whipped out his communicator.

“This is Commander Strongheart to aerial nine,” he said, “did you notice a large dark mass flying over sector three a few minutes ago?”

“Negative,” said the pilot, “do you want us to set up an aerial search?”

“That would be appreciated,” Beck said. He closed his communicator and started checking the recruits for a sign of life. Epsilon soon joined him.

A few minutes later, Epsilon’s voice called out through the silence.

“Beck!” he called, “over here!”

Beck ran over to Epsilon and looked down at the recruit he was kneeling over. He was alive.

Death’s Colleague – Chapter One
(Some ten to fifteen years after the prologue)

Happy Silent Eclipse pulled the trigger on his Wormholer and a barrage of energy bolts slammed into the Spiderling. The Spiderling reared up on its hind legs and let out a horrible scream. Then it smashed.

Eclipse turned towards a second Spiderling and started shooting at it. The same thing that happened to its comrade happened to it and Eclipse repeated the process. Eventually the area was cleared of Spiderlings, but then Eclipse had a different problem.

Mechs fired energy blasts down upon him. He put on his jetpack and flew above the mech’s heads. He flew towards the nearest mech and started blasting him. The mech smashed and he moved onto the next one. He switched to his heroic composite axe and brought it down on the metal servant’s head.

His jetpack took a moment to reorder itself before Eclipse could take flight again, and in that moment a Hammer Stromling came up behind him and swung its obnoxiously large hammer at him. Eclipse gasped and fell down. He quickly got up and flew away from the ground, before the hammer stromling could finish what it started. He quickly reequipped his wormholer and opened fire upon a Spiderling. He failed to notice the energy blast that came from behind him until he was smashed.

He rebuilt in the middle of the field and a display in his mind showed his times.

“Not bad,” he said, “not bad at all.”

He decided to leave Avant Gardens survival and head over to the Spider Caves. There was the usual tingling sensation as he left the simulated Avant Gardens and appeared in the real Avant Gardens. He turned in his “survive for three minutes” mission to Beck Strongheart and headed over to the Spider Caves. He put on his Space Marauder kit and walked up to Melodie Foxtrot.

“Is there anything you need me to do?” asked Eclipse.

“Yes, there is,” said Melodie, “Those caves are becoming a bit crowded, would you mind clearing them out for me?”

“It would be my pleasure,” Eclipse said. He gave a mock bow and ran off into the caves.

He returned three minutes later.

“Spider caves cleared,” he said, “now how about a reward?”

“Here,” she said. She handed him a green gem.

“Well, that’ll be jolly swell,” said Eclipse.

He leaped into the pit between the Spider Caves and the Sentinel Base Camp. The Spiderlings, Mechs, and Stromlings rushed at him instantly, but instead of asking for mercy, he let his Wormholer do most of the talking. The last mech smashed and Eclipse ran up and over the ridge that was between him and the Base Camp.

He rushed through the gate and up to Klaus Zett.

“Hello, kid,” he called. Eclipse was twenty-eight. He hardly qualified as a kid, “would you like to buy or sell?”

“First I would like to sell this green gem,” he said, “but my notion potion supply is rather low.”

“Deal!” he said. They made their transactions and Eclipse ran off to go see Rusty Steele.

He was halfway there when a shot rang out. Eclipse turned and saw a massive rotary cannon sticking out through a hole in the wall of the tunnel. He rolled to avoid the laser barrage and aimed his Wormholer at the cannon. Except it wasn’t there. Eclipse ran out of the tunnel and looked in the skies, and saw a black form of some kind flying away. It was too far away to tell, but it looked like a starcruiser.

Starcruisers were incredibly rare, seeing as most people prefer rockets, but there was a huge advantage to having a starcruiser in a dogfight.

He shrugged his shoulders and continued on his way. The assassin was long gone by now, so there was no point in raising the alarm.

But what he didn’t know was that a black figure was watching him.

Death’s Colleague-Chapter two

“GO AWAY YOU BLASTED BIRDS!” Rusty Steele shouted abruptly, “now, what did you want?”

“I said,” Eclipse repeated, “do you have any spare laser transmitters?”

“Er… no,” said Rusty, “why?”

“I was thinking of building something and I need a laser transmitter,” said Eclipse.

“What were you thinking of building?” asked Rusty Steele.

“I was thinking of building a starcruiser, but I need a laser transmitter for the main exhaust.”

“Now it still beats me why you joined Paradox and not Assembly,” said Rusty.

Eclipse shrugged his shoulders.

“I greatly considered it.”

“Well,” said Rusty, “I don’t have any spares, but if you smash a laser cutter, you’re entitled to whatever bricks you want.”

“Thanks,” said Eclipse. He walked up to the nearest laser cutter and shot it with his pneumatic drill of blasting. It smashed and he scooped up all the bricks and put them in his backpack.

He put on his Mythran jetpack and flew up to Vector Longview on the road behind the finish line. Vector gave his usual “you feelin’ fast today?” and Eclipse walked by him. He reached the launch area and mounted his rocket on the launcher.

He zoomed out of Avant Gardens and his Block Yard property came into view. He started the landing process and looked down at his dashboard. That’s when he saw it.

It was just a tiny flicker, but it was enough to concern Eclipse. His censors had never been wrong before, and they said there was a rocket or spacecraft of some sort behind him. Or at least there was, for about a half a second. He switched on his cloaking device censors and they started warming up. Something was forming on the very edge of his readings when…

BAM!

Eclipse had totally forgotten about his Block yard property and he had hit the magnetic layer of the atmosphere at such an angle that it seemed as if he had broken through a rock wall. He got his rocket back in control and looked back at his censor screen. It was blank. His radar antenna had broken off.

He jumped out of his rocket and instead of stowing it in his backpack, disassembled it and put in the brick section of his backpack. He wouldn’t need it after today.

He took one last look at the skies and shrugged his shoulders. Maybe my censors are jumbled, he thought, although he still wasn’t convinced.

He walked over to his mailbox and looked inside.

“Nothing new,” he said to himself.

He then turned to his property and saw the whole reason he was here.

He ran the palm of his hand over Ilofrudd Tân, his almost complete starcruiser. He had gone through great pains to eliminate the main reason Starcruisers were so unpopular.

The main reason Starcruisers were so unpopular was because when taking off, it took a long time to gather enough energy to power the repulsors that move it around, so Eclipse had made two large weights that when dropped, would force air through holes in the bottom of the starcruiser, giving it a jump start. This had taken a long time to perfect, because he had to apply the right amount of armor so that the bottom of the starcruiser wouldn’t be forced outward due to air pressure, and little enough so that the bottom wouldn’t be overly heavy. He smiled to himself as he remembered the starcruiser blowing the bottom out and falling while Eclipse was still in the cockpit.

He noticed a couple of scratches in the black and grey paint and decided to repaint those spots. He headed to his supply closet and opened the door. He couldn’t find any black paint, so he decided to repaint those spots dark red, enough to show that he was in Paradox, but little enough to show that he built it, and it wasn’t a standard issue vehicle.

He repainted the spots and opened up the main thruster maintenance compartment. He fiddled with a few wires and put the laser transmitter in and reconnected the fuse to the combustor. He closed the maintenance door and stepped back to admire his work. He ran his fingers through his shaggy brown hair. He knew he was forgetting something, he just couldn’t think what.

Excitement strangled reason and Eclipse climbed into the cockpit of Ilofrudd Tân. He eagerly gripped the fight controls and entered the start-up code. The mounted weights dropped and air surged through the bottom of the starcruiser, pushing it off the ground and spinning the dials that send energy to the main reactor.

He let out and excited whoop as Ilofrudd Tân soared out of his Block Yard Property and into space. Then he remembered the rocket he thought he had seen before he crashed through the atmosphere of his Block Yard.

He extended his antenna and his Radar censors started charging up. They were not so much for finding the location of cloaked objects; they were more for finding out if there were cloaking devices around. His readings came back positive. There was a cloaked object somewhere near him.

He turned back to his Block Yard before whatever it was decided to attack him. He landed and jumped out of Ilofrudd Tân to look down the barrel of a rather scary-looking laser gun with an axe blade attached to the underside.

Death’s Colleague – Chapter Three

This man was a trained assassin, there was no doubt about it. He wore a black helmet with an equally black opaque visor, dark pants that look like they were infused with Imagination repellent, a jetpack like Eclipse had never seen before, and a dark blue armored jacket.

The assassin pointed his unique blaster rifle at Eclipse’s head.

“Now just stay calm and keep quiet or I’ll blow a hole in that metal head of yours,” said the assassin in a clearly altered voice.

Eclipse was rather insulted by the crack about the metal head. Only a quarter of his forehead was metal, and that was because of an incident that happened in Forbidden Valley, when a Dark Ronin’s lance nicked his head and tore the skin.

The assassin put his rifle in his left hand and pulled out a cylindrical tube of some sort. He pressed a button and blue electricity bolts flew around Eclipse’s body, entrapping him. The electricity bonds lifted him in the air and carried him over to the rocket pad of his Block Yard property. The hired gun pushed aside some branches and led him over to a starcruiser.

A cargo bay opened up in the back and the assassin led Eclipse over to the bay.

“Get inside,” said the Assassin.

Eclipse started to get in, but drew a S-J16 stun dagger and sunk it into the assassin’s back. The assassin gave a yelp of pain and slumped into the cargo bay. He wasn’t dead, just stunned, and he was probably going to wake up in less than two minutes. Eclipse took the rifle away from him and fused the cargo door shut. He ran back to Ilofrudd Tân and opened the cockpit.

“Weapons!” said Eclipse, “that’s what I forgot!”

He ran back to his little shed and grabbed a basic firecracker launcher and mounted it on the side.

“That’ll have to do,” said Eclipse.

He jumped into the cockpit and let the weights drop. Ilofrudd Tân lifted off and was soon zooming through the atmosphere. He soon saw a starcruiser on his radar panel, and it was gaining. Eclipse was afraid to put the thrusters at full thrust, because he wasn’t sure if the engines could take it.

Avant Gardens came into view and Eclipse thought that he could make it. Until a second starcruiser came into view and started to intercept him.

Eclipse pulled back on the flight yoke and he looped upwards. He changed directions and hurtled down. He suddenly leveled out and headed strait towards Avant Gardens. He hesitated for a moment and pushed forward on the acceleration lever his engines whirred noisily, but he was almost there. A few more yards and….

BAM!

A laser bolt hit his port engine and it died on him. The extra thrust from the starboard side sent Ilofrudd Tân spinning. Eclipse tried desperately to even out the thrust. It wasn’t working and the two other starcruisers were closing in on him. Eclipse cut all power and launched his firecracker launcher. The opposing force sent Eclipse backwards and through Avant Gardens’ atmosphere.
