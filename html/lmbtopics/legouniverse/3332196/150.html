~ Chapter 39 ~

Part 3

 

  A loud horn suddenly sounded, the warcry of thedude.  The Knights assumed sparse defensive positions on their side of the Ocean Strait, two Knights in each group, with King Mathias's army filling in the gaps between them, but numbers would not help them win this fight.  The Knights were few, as was the King's army.  If they were to win through combative means, it would be through skill of the sword.

 

  Three boats materialized out of the mist, slowly approaching the shore.  Each boat was tall, the hull facing them really being a wide gangplank, which would fall when the boat reached shore.  When that happened, the soldiers within would disembark and attack.

 

  Each boat actually had two floors, with fifty soldiers waiting on the bottom floor.  It was on the upper floor that fifty archers stood, their bows primed and ready to rain deadly arrows down upon their foes.

 

  But the King's army was not without archers either, and at Sir Talmid's cry were already armed and ready to fire.  These men were skillful, hopefully more skillful than thedude's.  King Mathias was counting on that.

 

  "Ready!" King Mathias yelled, and the archers raised their bows to the sky.

 

  "Aim!"

 

  "Fire!"

 

  The distance between the boats and the King's army was far, but not too far for the archers.  Thirty arrows launched swiftly on arcs that took more than 80% of them to their targets.

 

  "Arm!" the King repeated, and the archers quickly reloaded.  "Ready, aim, fire!"

 

  Another volley of thirty arrows flew from the skillful hands of King Mathias's army, but those sixty projectiles were al for naught in taking down the boats.  While a third of thedude's archers fell in the first volley, there were still one hundred and fifty soldiers in the lower decks, ready to disembark when the made it to the shore.

~ Chapter 39 ~

Part 4

 

 

  "We must stop those boats!" Sir Luke cried.  "If those soldiers reach the shore we will be overrun!  Have we any cannons?"

 

  The soldier next to Sir Luke shook his sweaty head.  "We have no cannons, sir."

 

  "Then we must board the boats and destroy them from within!" Sir Luke announced.  "We require a boat!"

 

  Sir Sharp stood next to Sir Luke, and told him, "I have a boat in my backpack, but alas it is rubber!"

 

  "It will have to do," Sir Luke said.  He turned around and waved to King Mathias's men who stood with him and Sir Sharp, three archers and six swordsmen.  "To the strait!" he yelled, pointing to a portion of the strait which was a distance to the right from thedude's rightmost boat.

 

  Sir Luke, Sir Sharp, and the nine soldiers broke formation from the army and dashed towards the river.  thedude's remaining two thirds of his archers sighted them, and launched arrows upon the men.  One of King Mathias's archers, trained in the art of the bow, but not in maneuverability, fell.

 

  "Spread out!" Sir Sharp shouted, but then they arrived at the river.  The mist shrouded them, and because of the distance they put between them, they lost sight of even each other.

 

  It was silent within the mist.  "Seek out my voice!" Sir Luke called as a beacon.  "Make your way to me!"

 

  The surviving ten eventually grouped together again, and Sir Sharp proceeded to inflate his rubber boat.  It was large enough to seat them all, and Sir Luke, Sir Sharp, and King Mathias's men boarded.  Sir Sharp took the oars while a swordsman pushed them away from the shore.

 

  The current was in their favor, and they required little effort in closing in on the rightmost of thedude's boats.  As they lacked visibility, they found the boat by literally bumping into it, but because of their boat's rubber design, it was a soft bump, and was unheard by thedude's soldiers within.

 

  The noisy part came next.
