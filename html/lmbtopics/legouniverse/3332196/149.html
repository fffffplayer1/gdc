~ Chapter 39 ~

Part 1

 

  After several days of travel through the farmland plains, across Great Desert of Shrubs - in which they had a run in with the fearsome *gasp* Snrkeling Cactus Weasels - King Mathias, his army, and the Knights minus the Enchanter finally arrived at the Ocean Strait, a long, winding, and very wide river that stretched around the planet's entire southern hemisphere.  From a satellite camera directly under the South Pole, the Ocean Strait would appear to be a blue ring circling a great green plain circling a snowy tundra that was the South Pole.

 

  And at the Northern Shore of the Ocean Strait was an enchanting sight that rivaled even the aforementioned Enchanter's enchanting powers of enchantment.

 

  "For sooth!" Sir Talmid exclaimed, dismounting from his steed - which promptly left him to graze - and raising his eyes to stare upon the sight that was the SKY RAINBOWS!

 

  The planet of Morcia was not without its moons.  The planet actually had two moons, one of which was tidally locked on a low orbit around the planet's Southern Hemisphere.  Whenever the moon passed over the Ocean Strait, its gravity would influence the tides of the Ocean Strait so strongly that they waters would actually rise into the sky.  When sunlight met the suspended particles of water, a rainbow would appear.

 

  "It is beautiful indeed," King Mathias agreed, guiding his horse to trot alongside Sir Talmid, "but we shant busy ourself with sightseeing.  We must make haste to reach the Elephanters, as we are already behind schedule!"

  "Actually," Sir Talmid said, "I think we're right where we want to be.  I happen to bear knowledge that it is an Elephanter holiday to celebrate the Sky Rainbows.  Perhaps, behind this wall of mist, the Elephanters are there right now!"

 

  King Mathias nodded in agreement, and called to his men, "Dispatch scouts across the river!  Carry no weapons, we aim to make a peaceful impression on the Elephanters!"

~ Chapter 39 ~

Part 2

 

  For the next few minutes, the Knights and King Mathias heard nothing from the scouts after they disappeared through the mist as they crossed the river.  The King wondered what the Elephanters would think of his soldiers appearing out of the mist in the middle of a holiday.

 

  Finally, the scouts returned, with their leader, a man named Barclay, approaching the king with his report, and an interesting finding.

 

  "Your majesty!" Barclay saluted, and with the king's permission proceeded to relay his interesting find.  "It is an intersting occurance, but as my men stood in our boat within the mist, we found that we could see very clearly out of the mist, but simultaenously could not be seen!  This is an excellent location to put a surveillance tower."

 

  "Perhaps," the King said.  "Now what of the Elephanters?"

 

  Barclay's face darkened.  "We are too late, we spied thedude's army on the opposite shore."

 

  "But that's impossible!" Sir Talmid exclaimed.  "We came here as fast as we could."

 

  "Evidently thedude was faster," the King grumbled.  To Barclay, he questioned, "We know thedude is there, but what of the Elephanters?  Have they joined him, or are they resisting?  Are they there at all, or have they been vanquished?"

 

  "We did not see the Elephanters."

 

  "Perhaps they have fallen back." the King surmised.  "They are our only hope to defeating thedude, we must attack thedude and liberate the Elephanters!"
