‎01-30-2014 03:41 AM

If there is no beginning,
Can there be an end?
Or will this battle be the one
That time cannot mend?

  All good stories must come to an end... except this one. This is a story of prophesies fulfilled and repeated across the fabric of time. There is no beginning, there is no end. There are those who live and there are those who die. Evil is fought, again and again; and is defeated, again and again. Every time a person walks, they walk in the path of people who came before them, and others will follow step-for-step on to infinity.
  There are always slight differences of course. The Universe isn’t quite that predictable. Or is it? Perhaps if you laid out a great map with all events and prophesies stretching far into the past you could see even the smaller events repeating.
  I cannot see that far... but I know one who can. They can see far into the past and far into the future. They have seen more than everyone in the Universe combined. They have seen the creation of all worlds and the destruction of many. They have seen the darkness of the greatest of evils and the light of the brightest of hopes. They have seen the strongest of heroes fall and the weakest of minifigures rise. They have even seen the one time the prophesies failed, broke, and almost vanish forever.
  If the pattern holds true, the story will never end. But if the prophesies failed once, who’s to say it won’t happen again? And how can you tell that there will never be an ending?
I’m about to tell you a tale, a piece of the endless story. A tale that you could say has a beginning and an end. But while the tale can have a beginning and end, the story it is a piece of cannot. For it is about a thing that is limitless, timeless, and ever-flowing.
  This is a story about Imagination.

Author of Imagination Restored and winner of the 2014 Ninjago story contest drama category!

“And the universe said the darkness you fight is within you. And the universe said the light you seek is within you.”

Prologue One, part 1

‎02-03-2014 10:03 PM

Chaos returns.
It begins again.
The fight to save
All Imagination.

  In the beginning there wasn’t any maelstrom. There were no infected bricks or minifigures. There was no storm and no creatures. All there was was a legend. A legend that told of the Imagination Nexus, a source of pure Imagination used by the first builders. The Imagination Nexus was said to have the power to bring any idea to life.
  Our tale begins with four explorers: Duke Exeter, Baron Typhonus, Hael Storm, and Dr. Overbuild.
  Duke Exeter was the unofficial leader of the group. Even though he was by far the youngest of the four, they all held him in high respect. He was widely known to be a master swordsman, surpassing even his father’s skill. On his deathbed, Duke’s father entrusted him with a box, saying that it contained a key to finding the Imagination Nexus. Most thought the stories of the Imagination Nexus were only myths. But Duke was the most loyal minifigure anyone could ever meet. He believed his father’s words, every last one. And in honor of his father, he decided to go on a quest to find Imagination Nexus. It had been Duke who found and brought the explorers together in his search for the Imagination Nexus.
  Baron Typhonus had been a close friend of Duke’s father. Together, they went on many adventures exploring the Universe. Duke’s father became known as a defender of the innocent and a protector of good. Baron became rich as they made new discoveries and traveled between different lands. Duke asked Baron if he would join him in his search for the Imagination Nexus. Baron was willing and proposed that he fund the whole adventure.

Prologue One, part 2

‎02-04-2014 08:17 PM

  Hael Storm was a pirate, though he didn’t plunder treasure from any minifigures, with the exception of ninja. An old rivalry had grown between the ninja and pirates for years and there were often clashes between them. Hael himself said, “Arr! We be better off without them ninja!” Hael Storm had earned the title ‘Navigator of Worlds’ as a result of finding and exploring more planets than any other minifigure known. For this reason, Duke asked him to come and join them in their adventure. "Arr! Adventure be good, but will there be treasure to be had, laddie?” To which Duke replied, “If you’ve heard of Imagination, then you should know that Imagination is worth more than all the treasure you’ve ever discovered. And even if we don’t find it, I will gladly pay you in gold and precious gems.” Hael Storm never even considered leaving after that.
  Dr. Overbuild was a researcher, historian, and scientist. He had designed mechanical robot arms that worked like an extra pair of limbs. His studies focused mostly on Imagination and he was probably the only minifigure to ever have enough Imagination in one place to experiment with. After reading one of his books on the legend of the Imagination Nexus, Duke quickly got into contact with him. Dr. Overbuild quickly agreed to come along on the quest for a legend that he had dedicated his life to.
  Each of these explorers, with the exception of Baron, carried a key to finding the Imagination Nexus. They called these keys the Golden Corners. When put together on a special stand in their space ship, a blue energy would begin to glow where the fourth corner would be, if one had existed. Ancient texts Dr. Overbuild studied said that when they were near enough to the Imagination Nexus, the Golden Corners would send out a beam of light leading to the temple where the Imagination Nexus was kept.

Prologue One, part 3

Options
  • Mark as New
  • Bookmark
  • Subscribe to RSS Feed
  • Highlight
  • Print
  • REPORT POST

‎02-05-2014 01:28 AM

  The explorers had no idea where in the Universe it could be. All they knew was that the temple was located on a planet called Crux. It was said to have the shape of an almost perfect cube. No such planet was known or heard of.
  So they roamed the Universe, putting the Golden Corners together in different areas, habited and uninhabited. The Golden Corners would levitate and the empty corner would glow blue. It would point this way and that like a broken compass. Then the glow would dim and it would land back onto its stand.
  Duke Exeter never lost faith in the legend. His memory of his father kept him determined, despite the lack of things to do while they were drifting through space. Dr. Overbuild was always studying his scrolls and ancient books, looking for clues and hints that might tell them where the Imagination Nexus lay. Hael Storm was often steering the ship and would keep them entertained with his stories of his days as a kid pirate. Baron Typhonus was often sketching in a notebook. Duke remembered his father mentioned how good of an artist Baron was. But no matter how many times he asked to see Baron’s drawings, Baron wouldn’t let him. “My father always said your drawings were amazing,” Duke would say. Baron would turn away slightly and answer, “My art style has changed since your father last saw any of my drawings."

  Finally, after a long time of fruitless searching, Dr. Overbuild made a discovery. Along the side of the Golden Corners, there were small markings. On closer inspection they saw that it was writing. It looked like their own alphabet, but it appeared different, more ancient, and looked like it was from a highly advanced culture.
  Dr. Overbuild read the first part aloud:

“If Imagination
Is what you seek,
Beware the destruction
You could wreak."
