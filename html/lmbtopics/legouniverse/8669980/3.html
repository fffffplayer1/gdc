Prologue Two, part 1

Options
  • Mark as New
  • Bookmark
  • Subscribe to RSS Feed
  • Highlight
  • Print
  • REPORT POST

‎02-24-2014 01:36 AM

  Duke Exeter examined the hologram of Avant Gardens in front of him. Nimbus Station was one of the first Crux pieces to be cleared of all Maelstrom and Avant Gardens was next on the list. Of course, Maelstrom was being fought everywhere. Ever since they sent out the call three months before, people had been coming in from all over the Universe. Vanda had been one of the first…
  Duke shook his head. Vanda Darkflame was suspicious to say the least. Her past was shrouded in mystery, but she claimed to be the protégé of Baron Typhonus. While Duke believed her, he wasn’t sure about the fact that she still believed that Baron was a good person. He hadn’t told her the whole story himself, but Hael Storm had explained it to her. Except… Hael Storm being a pirate… Vanda probably didn’t believe him. Vanda was a ninja. The ninja were great fighters, but major enemies of pirates. Hael and Vanda had clashed over certain issues and their followers, pirates and ninja respectively, sometimes fought with each other instead of the Maelstrom.
  And when Dr. Overbuild proposed that she lead the Nexus Force along with the three of them, Hael Storm almost stormed away and blasted off in his ship before Duke Exeter managed to convince him that it might be for the best. Vanda had an understanding of the Maelstrom that no one else had. While this was one of the reasons Duke was suspicious of her, he was willing to give her a chance. She also was widely respected through all ninja forces. None of them would say why, but this was typical ninja behavior.

Prologue Two, part 2

‎02-24-2014 01:41 AM

  Hael agreed that she become a leader on the terms that she didn’t command all of the Nexus Force, rather only a fourth. Each of them agreed and the entire force was divided into four groups. Vanda’s force focused on understanding and using the Maelstrom. Unfortunately this started a number of rumors about her and her force. Rumors such as experiments on minifigures and infecting Imagination and turning it into Maelstrom. In order to appease nervous minifigures, Duke had a weekly checkup on all laboratories experimenting with Maelstrom.
  After discovering a slight leak of Maelstrom in a lab, Duke Exeter banned all Maelstrom from Nimbus Station and said there would be a future ban on all planet chunks completely cleared of Maelstrom.
  “Duke Exeter!”
  Duke’s attention snapped to a soldier who came running through the doorway. “What is it Strongheart?”
  Beck Strongheart halted in front of him and saluted. “Maelstrom has returned to Nimbus Station, sir!”
  Duke Exeter hastily pushed the buttons on the hologram display. “Are they invading?”
  “I- We’re not sure, sir.”
  Duke turned to look at him while the hologram began loading information from Nimbus Station. “What do you mean?”
  “We can’t tell where the Maelstrom is coming from. Stromlings and Spiderlings-”
  Duke had turned back to the hologram, but he whirled around. “Spiderlings!?!”
  “Yes, sir.”

Prologue Two, part 3

‎02-24-2014 01:43 AM

  “We have people living on Nimbus Station! And most of them aren’t soldiers either!” Duke was worried. Spiderlings didn’t usually appear except in areas with high levels of Maelstrom. And the people on Nimbus Station... they were only ordinary citizens or family of Nexus Force soldiers. None of them had any fighting experience. The hologram finished downloading the information and the hologram of Nimbus Station appeared above it. Duke stared at it. “What the-?”

  The hologram image showed Nimbus Station… a half destroyed Nimbus Station. Violet and black patches covered most of the city, buildings were destroyed or completely gone, and red dots representing Stromlings were everywhere.
  “Naomi, connect me to all speakers in Nexus Tower!” Duke said.
  “Connecting now.”
  The hologram shuddered and some parts of it turned to static before clearing up and turning to static again. “Maelstrom interference,” Duke muttered under his breath. Soon there wouldn’t be any connection at all.
  “Speakers connected. Push the blue button next to the speakers when you are ready to speak.”
  “Thank you, Naomi.”
  “You are welcome, sir.”
  Duke pushed the button and spoke, his voice echoing up and down the long hallways and large rooms of the Nexus Tower. “Attention Nexus Force! This is Duke Exeter. We have a situation on Nimbus Station. Maelstrom has returned and is threatening to take over completely! This is Code Purple! I repeat, this is Code Purple! Everyone available please get to Nimbus Station as soon as possible!”
  Duke pushed the blue button again, cutting off the speaker’s connection.
  “Sir,” Beck Strongheart said, “What are my orders?"

Prologue Two, part 4

‎02-24-2014 01:48 AM

  The hologram flickered and half of it shut down.
  “Same orders as everyone else,” Duke replied. “Get to Nimbus station as soon as possible and do what you can until I come and give you further orders.”
  “Sir?”

  Duke glanced at Beck who was looking slightly shocked. “What’s the matter? Is there something wrong?"

  Beck shook his head. “No, no sir. I just didn’t think you would come yourself.” He saluted and turned and ran out of the room.

  Duke Exeter lifted his sword and shield and started to hurry out of the room. He had to talk to the other Nexus Force leaders. He needed to make sure he had their and their faction’s support.

  “What’s happening, Duke?” A voice asked behind him.
  Duke whirled around and saw Vanda Darkflame only a few studs away. “How- how did you get in here?” he said, looking around for another entrance to the room.
  “That’s my secret.”
  “I feel like you have far too many secrets.”
  “Perhaps.” Vanda glanced at the hologram which shuddered one last time and then shut down completely. “There’s trouble at Nimbus Station?”
  “Yes. I need to speak with the other leaders and then I’ll be heading there myself. Speaking of which…” He met Vanda’s eyes. “I want to make sure I have the support of the Paradox Force.”
  “Of course.”
  “I want you to send as many as you can to assist in taking back Nimbus Station.”
  “Of course.”
  “Good.” Duke turned and ran from the room. When he looked back, she was gone. The room was empty. But he had more important things to think about than a disappearing ninja. He hurried to where Dr. Overbuild was often drawing out blueprints. When Duke Exeter found him, Dr. Overbuild was lost in thought, pencil in hand, as usual.
  “Overbuild! Nimbus Station is under attack!”

‎02-24-2014 01:49 AM

  Dr. Overbuild jumped slightly. “What? Oh, yes. I heard that. Is there something I can do for you, Duke?”
  “I need every extra soldier you can assemble.”
  “We’re not really soldiers, Duke.”
  “You can still help though."

  Dr. Overbuild nodded. “I know. Truth be told, most have already left for Nimbus Station.”
  “Thanks, Overbuild,” Duke said. He ran towards the Venture League area of the tower and met Hael Storm coming out of his faction’s observation room with his parrot perched on his shoulder.

  “What be the problem, Duke?” Hael asked.

  “Maelstrom has taken over Nimbus Station again.”

  “Aye, I heard.” Hael Storm eyed Paradox minifigures who ran past them and down the hallway leading to the rocket launch. He leaned forward and spoke lowly. “Do ye think it were the Paradox and them ninja?”
  Duke Exeter stared at him confusedly. “What?"

  “The Paradox faction. I be suspicious of them. They be havin’ too many secrets.”
  “You think… they’re the reason Maelstrom has returned to Nimbus Station?”
  “What other explanation be there?”
  Duke heard rockets blasting off for Nimbus Station in the distance. “We can’t afford to be busy pointing fingers at anyone right now. Will you make sure to send as many of your faction as you can?”
  “Aye, that I will do.”
  “And keep an eye on the Imagination Nexus. The tower is going to have very little protection with everyone being sent to Nimbus Station.”
  A shadow passed over Hael’s face. “Or what be remainin’ of it.” He said quietly. Then he looked up quickly. “Aren’t ye goin’ to watch it yerself?”

Prologue Two, part 6

‎02-24-2014 01:49 AM

  Duke shook his head. “No, I’m going to help out on Nimbus Station.”
  “Are ye sure about that?”
  Duke gave him a strange look. “Yes… why?"

  “What if-” Hael hesitated. “Duke, we be needin’ ye. If… if somethin’ happened to ye…”
  “I’ll be fine. I’m more worried about everyone living on Nimbus Station.” Duke turned to leave. “Besides, what kind of leader would I be if I wasn’t willing to put myself in the same amount of danger as my men?”
  “But ye are willing! Ye don’t need to prove it."

  Duke hesitated. Then his face became more determined. “No, it is a person’s actions that truly define who they are. I’m not staying behind. I’ve made up my mind, Hael.”
  Hael Storm nodded in understanding. “If ye be sure, then I won’t be stoppin’ ye. But wait.” He pulled out a small device. “Take this.” He handed it to Duke.
  Duke examined it. It had three small light bulbs on it: one was lit up green and the other two were off. “What is it?"

  “It be a trackin’ device. Thar be a green light which means the trackin’ system be workin’, thar be the light that glows yellow when ye be near Maelstrom, and thar be the light that glows red when ye be in trouble and it be sendin’ out a distress signal. It works even when it be in high levels of Maelstrom."  “Thanks, Hael."

  “Arr! Don’t mention it.” Hael Storm slapped Duke on the back. “Go smash some of them Maelstrom!”
  “Good luck,” a voice said. Duke turned and saw Vanda a little ways off. Hael Storm gave her a small glare. Her face was calm. “You’ll need it."

‎02-24-2014 01:50 AM

  Duke Exeter nodded and quickly sped to the rocket launch just outside of the tower. The wind howled quietly and clouds drifted below him. It was long way down, but Duke couldn’t see the ground through the thick cloud cover. Above the clouds it felt calm, while below, the Maelstrom were doing everything they could to get into the Nexus Tower. But the Imagination here was safe. But at Nimbus Station it was not.
  Duke walked forward and pulled out his rocket. It was a royal blue color with yellow highlights. Minifigures made way for him and let him prepare his rocket for launch first. He climbed in his rocket and before he blasted off he raised his sword and yelled, “For Imagination!”
  Everyone cheered and they repeated the cry. Duke put in the coordinates for Nimbus Station and shot off into the star-filled sky, on his way to confront the enemy that awaited him there.
