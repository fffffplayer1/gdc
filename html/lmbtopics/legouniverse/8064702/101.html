Name: Luthak

 

Species: Skakdi

 

Mask: None

 

Element: Light (Can only be used in conjunction with another Piraka of Light/Shadow. Fat chance, huh?)

 

Weapons/Tools/Defenses/Abilities: Twin-bladed Spear, Impact Vision, Shadow Resistance (Vunerable to Light), Ability to detect nearby Light/Shadow-based lifeforms (Such as Toa or Makuta).

 

Personality: Rather humbled after being exiled from his clan, his goal is no longer his own self-interests but to protect the universe itself from those who would wish to harm it. In battle, his savagery can take hold of him if he isn't careful.

 

Appearance: Clad in silvery/white armor, he is slightly larger than the average Skakdi, though interestingly enough his spiked spine is much smaller than most other Skakdi, although they're much more numerous. Because of this, he is allowed to wear a cloak which easily conceals his full form. Physically, he is much stronger than a Toa or even some of the smaller Makuta, but would not be able to hold up well against someone such as Axonn.

 

Backstory: Once a champion of a Skakdi tribe on the peaceful island of Zakaz, he was one of the first Skakdi to be experimented on, but something had gone wrong with one of the tests, and he had been transformed into a Skakdi of Light. For unknown reasons, he was let go and cast out back into the jungle. Shunned by his tribe due to his new transformation, he was forced off of the island before the Visorak were deployed, cast out unto the sea to wander aimlessly until he would be able to find a new land. Traveling for many, many long years, he eventually set foot on Xia, whose inhabitants had sold him a protosteel twin-bladed spear as well as a warm cloak for his journeys. Paying up and thanking the Vortixx, he began to venture out back into the sea before he was stopped by a strange individual who told him of the Order of Mata Nui. Suspicious and confused, Luthak realized that he had nowhere else to go and decided to pursue the stranger's offer, rowing out to seek destiny

"Oh, his food is fine. Just...make sure to get the 'boneless' meat."

Dinner on the rails! Or...off the rails...whichever works.

Fendrak's only job was to inspect the ore that onu-matoran would frequently trade with his people, which were generally relied on minmerals and allows for construction, rather than wood, which the village shunned. No toa resided on this island, but even so Fendrak's village was believed to contain at least one matoran destined to become a toa, thus, the raid. Fendrak had just completed his shift when he heard screaming. He knew that as a matoran he wouldn't be very helpful in such a situation, and indeed he actually hid inside the onu matoran village, which was then blocked off by an almost natural looking boulder.
