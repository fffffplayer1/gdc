LONFEU (Legacy of Night Flash Extended Universe) Sneak Peek!

The Mythran Saga

Chapter 1: A Powerful Darkness on the Horizon

Theed Faine sat up in his luxurious sleeping pallet, panting and in a cold sweat. He'd seen it again, just as before.. and it still scared him. The Nightmare. A shining night sky, over the city of Pandora, then temporarily lit by a flash of brightest light. The flash illuminated a lava flow running into the city, and unspeakable monsters roaming the streets. It ALWAYS meant poor fortune would find him. And soon.

His quarters were a pleasing suite, with silk and satin abundant. A miniature replica of the Nexus Core sat humming in the center, lighting up several couches, a table, jaded walls, vases, protraits, and other trifles. But the rewal gem was in the underground chambers, where he kept the sword - stolen, of course - that had once pierced the armor of the Darkitect. And it had not been wielded by a Mythran.

He stood, still shaking, for his thoughts and the dream had disturbed him, and walked softly to the thick oak door., clad in synthetic nightclothes. He opened it, ans stepped out side. His four guards were standing sentinel outside. "Do you see anything? Do you hear ANYTHING?" he almost screamed at them.

"Nothing, my lord. just like the last six times," the closest guardsman told him in a bored voice.

"You shoulld rest, my lord," said another. "The Nightmare again?"

"What would you know about it?" Faine growled. "I tell you, someone is trying to gat past you and attack me!"

As he spoke those very words, a figure in a white gi, brown pants, and a black ninja mask swung up onto the balcony, from where he'd been hanging for the past hour, out of sight. The balcony overlooked Pandora, so it could be anybody... but this attacker moved differently, like one of his legs was disabled. Yet he still nimbly flurry-kicked the first guard, cartwheeled into the next, drew the former's dagger and blocked the newest arrival's sword-strike, disarmed him and grbbed his shoulder. Faine could tell the nin ja was pressuring a nerve, and the gyard passed out, having had ten seconds of firm pushing on his carotid artery. The last guard, and the smartest, put himself between the attacker and Faine, but the ninja merely punched him in the nose, through his helmet visor, and delvered a devastating uppercut. The guard fell to the grouhd.

The ninja had successfully put Faine's guards out of the fight without smashing them. Who WAS he? Faine pressed a remote button on the wall outside his door to signal more guards, and the mysterious figure grbbed his wrist too late. Faine dsrove his elbow into the ninja's stomach, but painfully connected with some kind of under-armor. His opponent retaliated by pushing Faine back and letting go, sending the Mythran to the ground.

Voices carried around the corner. Faine rolled over to the edge of the balcony, where the guards would arrive. When he looked up, the visitor was preparing to leap off the balcony. "Who are you?" Faine yelled.

The warrior looked back. "The one that got away," he said in a familiar voice. Then he jumped. Fine ran to the end of the overlook and saw only a hang-glider with the Paradox symbol on it flying away. He knew who it was. The one who got away, indeed.

"Good to see you, too, Dwight." he said with a grim smile.
