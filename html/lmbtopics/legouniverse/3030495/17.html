The Edge of Darkness- EXCERPT

I ran across the floor of the Dominion V2, looking for Seven. The Dominion V2 was shaped like the original ship; circular with a watchtower. But the paint and everything else was totally different. Green and white stripes littered the floor and armor of the ship, and the watchtower was expanded. The ship now had a kitchen; there was a stairwell leading to the "basement" of the Dominion, and after that basement, a large kitchen. I was in the Den of it, frantically searching for Seven. I finally ran over to the TV, and found him laying on the couch. "Seven-" "We're under attack?" "Eeyup." The assassin jumped up, ran over to me, and gripped my shoulders. His hair had grown back- it was originally a buzz cut- and had lost its normal camouflage color. His hair had grown over part of his face, and was a reddish-brownish color. Seven wore a bullet-proof vest, and blue shorts. "Night, who's attacking? Wait. I should know." Seven's face was grim in the moonlight. Suddenly, Ned and Jakk ran out of the blue and said: "Maelstrom. Faine. No time." The Dominion shook, as of on cue, and the door was suddenly busted down. A seven foot tall figure, wearing a purple robe and a torn shirt, ran inside. His face had a purple scar, right under his eyes- which were green slits, by the way- and a top hat, followed by tons of other purple figures. "Well, well. It's the band of misfits, once again."

The Legacy of Night Flash- Chapter 3.5

"Wha-?!" I was suddenly standing in the middle of Avant Gardens with the group. No Lore Master. No freaky, weird Nalia Umbraros. The group was in a tight circle around me, whispering quietly, but then saw me "awake." 'Night! You fell asleep. For some weird reason... ANYWAY. You're up! You were mumbling about Basin. The Lore Master dissappeared and made you... pass out. Well, we're all here now.'' Said Jakk with a queasy look. I turned to my right, and I ran out of the circle the group had made. Then I looked back at them, with a puzzled look. "So...'' I didn't need to say anything else. "Okay, well, since the whole 'Lore Master' ordeal was just a trick, we should probably find a way OFF this planet. Then, we can at least go somewhere else; that way we have a better chance of finding something to destroy the Spider Queen. We still have four whole days left before Faine comes here. If we can't find anything to destroy the Queen, we need to distract Faine. There might be a ship-shop somewhere around here. Let's go find it!" Well, that made sense. Seven ran off to the left, and me and everyone else followed. It was another long, boring walk. The clothes made me extra hot, so I was sweating like crazy. The grass waved, seemingly mocking us. After quite awhile, I spotted something to my right. I freezed in my tracks, but everyone else kept walking. I suddenly passed out.
***

"Ugh... Where AM I?" I rubbed my head, and I realized I was near a hyper, purple, twenty-feet tall Spider Queen. I jumped up, and saw a hazy figure by it. Great. Dreaming AGAIN. The figure had a top hat and held a purple staff. His face was masked by, well, a black mask with a faint purple/blue glow behind it. "Heh heh... Ah! Hello, Night. I don't have the time to visit you right now, but, I CAN see you in your dreams. You see, my friend here is glorious!" Faine pointed to the Queen with his staff. "The Maelstrom will free the Universe." I was puzzled. "Free it from WHAT?!" "From freedom, of course. You were made to be ruled. Ruled by me. The Builders of the Universe had the worst idea in the Universe: freedom. But, if you choose to help me, you will be spared!" He chuckled. 'NEVER." "Oh, I thought so." Suddenly, he snapped his fingers, and my vision went red.
"It appears the game is over before it even begins."
