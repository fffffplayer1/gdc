Survival of the Fittest
(Rough Draft 1.2)
Chapter 1 excerpt
WARNING: SPOILERS

Jake ran through the dense jungle around him, the wind whipping in his face. Amidst the trees and foliage, the forest was teeming with life. All of that would change when night fell. It always did. As he sprinted down the footfall-beaten path to his camp, he had only two things on his mind: his brother, Rory, and a chicken leg. He burst into the clearing ahead, panting and out of breath. He didn't stop. Rory was sitting by a small campfire, roasting a leg of Narjay on a sharpened stick. "Meat! Now!" Jake yelled as he barreled past his brother, snatching the leg and throwing it over his shoulder. The creeping black mist behind him recoiled and stayed put when the drumstick pierced its vapory walls. It paused only for a moment, then the leg was thrown back out, stripped to the bone and gnawed ferociously. The mist kept coming. Rory screamed something incoherent and grabbed a flaming log from the fire, hurling it into the shadow's midst. It shrank back, a hissing sound resonated within its depths. Then another. Rory took up two more burning sticks, handing one to Jake. They held them at arm's length, like swords, hoping the light would repel their dark enemies. "How many?" Rory asked Jake. He was bravely keeping his composure, but behind his confident voice Jake knew he was shaking. Couldn't blame him. Jake was scared, too. A whole lot. "At least thirty Unknown, maybe more," he said. "I can't tell with that blasted mist around them." Rory nodded and hefted his log. Jake looked up for a second. The sky was growing dark. He looked down. The shadows were closer, ever so slowly inching toward him and his brother. This was going to be one heck of a fight.

I know my writing style's a little archaic sometimes, but that's from reading too much J.R.R. Tolkien. Do you like it?
Again, Jake and Rory are just sample names.
