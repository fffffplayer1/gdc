Well, I got caught up with reading the chapters, and I have but one thing to say: I am blown away by this story.

I really like how it dives into detail right away. Your descriptions of the characters are phenomenal, and the way that you get to show the protagonist's feelings in a unique sort of way... It's a way I used to use, but not so much anymore. It's a really good technique to have when you start writing.

Also, the antagonists, their hideouts, and of course, themselves, are also amazing. Even some good authors here on the Message Boards probably could not have came up with this great setting you have here. It's structurally strong and definitely does have the potential to stay popular for a long time.

I really have few cons to say, right now. Just one. While it's not a big problem, and I don't mind it at all, some people might get uncomfortable with the speed of the story. At times it seems like it's going fast-paced or too slow in the wrong times. Let's say if it's an intense part, like an excited chase from the antagonists, try to make it fast-paced to leave the audience on the edge of their seats and begging for more. If it's a part when something might be slow, like walking through a wheat field, then just make it slow (I don't mean slow as in several chapters, but slow as in taking time in one chapter).

I might post something more detailed later, but for now, I can only say that this is excellent work I'm looking at, and it's an amazing story for anyone here on the MBs to read. So, without further a do....

The Legacy of Night Flash- Chapter 2.4

"Yes. Your experiments surprised us, Nalia." Said Dwight with sarcasm. "Okay, now, to business. Why are you here? I don't get visitors often... The only reason I let you come in here is because I KNOW you. So, why are you here?" She asked, her face hardening. "We're here... for help, Doctor. The Mythran Order has finally set the Plot in motion, and are trying to capture us. We came here for help, also so they couldn't track us. Anything you have to help?" I asked. "Well, boy, I have some technology that could make you untrackable anywhere. I don't know what else you want." Said Nalia with a bad attitude. "Advice! What should we do? I mean, how are we supposed to-" The Doctor put a finger to my lips, and said: "I have an idea of where you should go. Your friend, Seven, does too." How did she know Cattivo wanted us to go to Avant Gardens? "You should go to Avant Gardens with the untrackable technology I have. You can at least rest there and get some idea of what you need to do. I have some speeders I can lend you to get back to your ship quickly, too." Yes! We had all the stuff we needed. "Uh, wait, Nalia. Do you think we could have some of that Camofigure formula?" Dwight asked suspiciously. "Well... Maybe you could. Let me go get some." She walked over into another section of the office, and closed the door behind her. "Dwight, why do you want the Camofigure formula?" Jakk asked. "I wanna see what it does. You'll see.'' He said. Umbraros walked in with a grave expression on her face. "H-here. I have some of the-" She quickly pressed a red button on her desk, and I immediately heard a siren. I quickly saw what Nalia described as the untrackable tech, stuffed it in my backpack, and screamed "RUN!'' I barged down the glass door and heard Nalia yelling: "YOU'LL NEVER GET MY SECRETS!" Everyone got in the glass elevator and hurriedly punched the button that said 1, but the elevator didn't move. Everyone stood perfectly still, and heard the "Tick. Tick. TICK." "EAHHH! LOOK!" Yelped Jakk. A single claw scratched the surface of the glass, making an excruciating sound. "Ahhhhhhh! KEEP PRESSING ONE!" I yelled. The glass elevator suddenly dropped, as we screamed. We ran out of the elevator and saw some speeders, but they suddenly dissappeared from sight as several Camofigures, Mugs, and Peds started scuffling towards us. Jakk suddenly got in front of them. "GO! Get the speeders. It's the only way. I'll distract them!" "JAKK! NO!" Yelled Rean. "Reany. Let him. IT'S THE ONLY WAY." We both cried as we got on the speeders. Before we zoomed out the glass building, I saw Jakk salute us. "REAN! LET'S GO!" We blasted out of the building, not looking back.
"It appears the game is over before it even begins."
