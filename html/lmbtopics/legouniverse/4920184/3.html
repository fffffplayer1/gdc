I skimmed over this, and here's what I noticed: your plot is good, but you really need to work on your spelling, grammar, and punctuation.   So here's your first lesson:

 

ALWAYS start a new paragraph when the speaker changes in a dialogue.  For example, you would not say:

 

Person 1: "Hey dude, what's up?" Person 2: "Not much."

 

Instead you would say:

 

Person 1: "Hey dude, what's up?"

 

Person 2: "Not much."

 

Or to be precise:

 

"Hey dude, what's up?" said Person 1.

 

"Not much," replied Person 2.

 

See the difference?  This can really change how well readers understand what's happening.

 

2nd lesson:

 

Use appropriate spaces with punctuation.  A comma does not substitute for a space.  Example:

 

Incorrect: "I had eggs,toast,and orange juice,"I said.

 

Correct: "I had eggs, toast, and orange juice," I said.

 

See the difference?  This is an essential part of making your story look good.

 

 

Okay, I've probably bored you enough already, but try to follow this advice.  Trust me, the most popular stories all tend to have at least decent grammar, and these tip are an all-too-often overlooked part of the basics.
