The glow grew brighter and brighter, it was dark purple though, almost like the color of the Maelstrom.

"Oh... This is going to end badly isn't it..." X said, he had learned that when people started glowing, things get blown up, and the only things to blow up around here were him, the mountains, and Nexus City...

As the glow faded, X staired at Nemisis. His eyes had turned a dark purple, and were glowing, his hair was also purple, and standing straight up, with energy crackling through it. His armor suit was glowing, and had energy crackling through it. And X could sense the same energy he had felt when fighting the monsters, only Nemsis was now ten times stronger than them.

"Great, just what I needed to top-off my day! A super-powered Nemsis! I should have stayed a mercenary..."

Suddenly, Nemisis teleported directly behind X, and blasted him with two energy balls. As X fell, he got a distinct sense of Deja-vu... And teleported just in-time to avoid a huge blast of energy, which slammend into one of the mountains, completely obliberating it.

X staired at the crater that had a few seconds ago been a mountain, and groaned.

"Great, now Nemisis is copying my powers. Only problem is his versions are stronger than mine!"

X dodged a few more huge blasts of energy, each one hitting the ground and causing a semi-earthquake, and of course a huge crater. X teleported behind Nemisis, and firied an energy blast of his own, it hit dead-on, but when the smoke cleared, Nemisis wasn't even scratched.

"Wait, I remeber something about my fight with Layab. The last thing I did...."

X teleported as far as he could from Nemisis, and waited. Nemisis teleported just a few yards away from his, and charged, firing a beam of energy. X didn't even move, and the attack hit him head-on.

As X clutched his chest, he looked up at his opponet, then started laughing. As Nemisi got closer, he started firirng more energy blasts, each one hitting X directly. X's vision started flashing red, he heard energy crackling. And in a flash of light, everything was gone.


Everyone in a one-hundered mile radius saw the huge blast of red energy that lit up the sky, in grew larger and brighter. But even more shocking was a huge blast of purple energy that seemend to counter it. Each growign larger until the overlapped, and in an explosion of light, sent a shockwave across the area. The light grew even brighter, then in a loud, thunder like sound, faded.

When X could see again, the area below him was a crater, energy crackled through the sky, and a figure floated in the sky, not ten yards away.

"No...... It... Didn't... Work..." X said, the last attack had worn him out, but surprisingly, it had seemend to heal his wounds.

Nemisis started laughing, the first sound he had made since he had powered up. Then spoke.

"Nice try X, you impressed me. But don't be surprised your attack didn't work. I've been holding back, just using your moves. Now see my real power!"

"Shadows on the ceiling, shadows on the wall,

Malevolent laughter running through my head."

Swarm followed the sounds of the chant until he came to a large cave. There he beheld a strange sight. In the center of the cave, a perfectly circular pit stretched down into darkness. Over this pit, a wrinkled old man sat cross-legged in the air. The chanting voice belonged to him. As Swarm entered the cave, the man turned towards him. "Welcome, bold one, to my home. You stand before the Abyss. It was into this Abyss that Deathshade was cast, so long ago. And it is out of this Abyss he will rise again." He pointed at one huge wall of the cave, which was covered with strange markings. "There is much you do not know. Before one can defeat his enemy, one must know what he faces. Sit and I will tell you all."

Swarm listened intently to the old mans story...

"Deathshade first appeared centuries ago, as a young boy. Even as a boy though, he had a sense of darkness about him. He was found, and taken in, by a group called The Guardians of Life. We saw the great power in him, and hoped to help him suppress his internal darkness. How blind we were. It soon became clear that Deathshade did not possess internal darkness, rather he was darkness. His powers allowed him to manipulate much of the physical world, but his greatest strength was in the manipulation of minds. He was able to place his own thoughts into another persons mind, but so concealed in their subconscious that they believed those thoughts to be their own. By the time we realized the evil that he was, it was to late. He, through his mental twisting, caused one of the Guardians to attack another, then fled. We hunted him for years, though our only way of tracking him was the destruction he left behind. Finally, we caught up to him, prepared to deal justice. We offered him mercy if he would surrender peacefully. His response was to attack us. In the ensuing battle more than half the remaining Guardians were killed, but Deathshade was eventually brought down. Rather than executing him, we determined to banish him from the existence of the living. It cost the remaining Guardians much of our power to open the Abyss, a realm composed of pure nothingness. However, as we hurled him in, Deathshade lashed out at us with his power. In our weakened state the Guardians were devastated by the blow, which crippled us, draining much of our remaining powers. As such we were unable to close the Abyss behind Deathshade. This meant that his banishment could not last forever. It had become clear in the battle that Deathshade drew power from death and suffering. And since the Abyss remained open, he was not cut off from this supply of power. The Guardians knew that when he became strong enough, he would return. And if he became strong enough to escape the Abyss, we knew we could not defeat him again. Without our full power, the Guardians slowly began to die. Soon only I remained. I vowed to guard the door to the Abyss until the time when I too pass from this world. That time is now at hand bold one. The people of this universe will soon be in grave danger. I only hope that there are still those with the strength to stand."

Upon finishing, the old man closed his eyes, and left the lands of life forever. Swarm said, sadly, to himself, "I didn't even ask his name."
