The body of Whisper lay motionless on the ground, devoid of both life and soul. However, a carful observer would have noticed serpentine tendrils of darkness approaching from all sides. Strange, whispering sounds seeped from them as they writhed around the fallen form. Soon, the body was completely enveloped by darkness. And then, it began rise. Before long, the shadow shrouded figure stood upright. Then the shadows fell away.

The body no longer bore any resemblance to Whisper. It's entire body below the neck was garbed in sinister black armor. Where it's head had been, there was now only a bare skull, it's eyes gleaming like crimson fire. And in the center of it's chest, there gaped a jagged hole, revealing a dark void.

The new form raised it's clawed gauntlets to the sky and spoke.
"I am Soulless. I am Forsaken. I am Darkened. I am Vengeance. I am Swam."

And another voice whispered, "As it should be."

Suddenly, a bright, purple glow shot out of the hole. The minifigures surrounding it were forced to squint their eyes. A glowing minifigure then rose out of the hole and blasted up into the air.

For a moment, Vanda gazed in awe and confusion. Then she realized... it was Yonder!



Yonder tackled Whisper with unmerciful anger. With his inner Maelstrom power that he had contained for so many years, now completely unleashed, his strength was now equally matched with his opponent's.

"Fight me!" Yonder demanded, "Let us end this now!"
<br/> Whisper laughed. "End? You cannot end me fool. For every light there is a shadow. I am the shadow. You may destroy this physical form, but I will take on another. As long as there is light, there will be darkness. As long as there is life, there will be death. As long as there is joy, there will be those who work to bring misery."



Dark energies swirled around Whispers hands as he continued. "But all of that is rather irrelevant, because soon, you will no longer draw breath."



With that, he unleashed a massive wave of shadow energy, which smashed into his foe with devastating force.
<br/>Yonder appeared to be critically wounded. Shadow energy arose from his body unnaturally. However, a couple seconds later, he stood up holding the wave.

"RAAAH!!!" Yonder let out a war cry as he chucked the deadly energy back at his shocked opponent.

"Stop this madness, now, Whisper!" Yonder begged.
<br/>"You just don't get it, do you?" Whisper sighed in boredom. He pointed his finger, and a skyscraper crumbled to dust. "Even if I wanted to, I could no more stop this madness than I can resist keeping you alive long enough for you to watch your friends die. Speaking of which..."



Leaping into the air, Whisper seized Briana by the throat, hurling her to the ground with bone-shattering force. He turned back towards Yonder, his eyes gleaming with pure evil.



"... Lets get started with that, shall we?"
<br/>Yonder's eyes gleamed red with hatred. It was a side effect in his morphing process that he had.

He ran across the building and jumped into the air above the destroyed and literred streets of Nexus City. Whisper was shocked once more as Yonder grabbed him and pushed him onto the skyscraper behind him.

Several Estrinlan helicopters surrounded the skyscraper full of men shooting at Yonder with powerful rifles. The bullets bounced off him as he approached Whisper who was lying on the ground just ahead of him.

Once he reached the powerful being, he snatched his shirt and tossed him into one of the helicopters which instantly caught on fire, and started to crash land in a dark alley.

Yonder hopped down into the alley after it. He landed in a puddle of fish carcasses, rotten food, and grease stained newspapers.

The helicopter was quickly spotted in his keen eye. He walked towards it in swift, cocky strides. However, once he got there, he searched the wreckage for Whisper, but found only the bodies of the Estrinlan.

Yonder was not confused for long. Suddenly, a familiar force tackled him.

He thought of the words Whisper had said earlier, "You cannot end me fool... You may destroy the physical form, but I will take on another..."

Was it impossible to defeat him?

"Νο!" Wiz said as he approached Yonder.

That was the only spark of hope Yonder needed. He kicked Whisper off of him and hopped on top of him.
"You may be invincible," Yonder said through his gritted teeth, "But you can be imprisoned."
Yonder squeezed Whisper shoulder with all his remaining strength. Tentacles of energy started to wrap around Whisper draining his energy... and his soul.
Yonder screamed out of pain. His body began to be controlled by his enemy's spirit inside. However, he quickly regained control of it.
He coughed up a bit of Imagination and realized it was Infected. Instantly, he realized how dangerous he had become.
"Yonder!" a voice screeched.
Vanda embraced him before he turned.
"Vanda, please," Yonder pleaded nudging her off, "Get away from me!"
"But, why...?" she asked, "What's wrong?"
"Whisper..." he replied, "He's... inside of me."
"What?" she asked with a confused expression.
"Go back and help the Agency," he ordered, "I need to leave. I need to get away from all minifigure population," he turned away to leave, "Good bye, Vanda."
"Wait!" she screamed.
Yonder turned back to embrace her once more. But this time it was not only their arms, but their lips too. After several seconds, they let go of each other.
"A few years ago," Vanda said in a dreamy voice, "I would have never guessed I would ever do that.
Yonder smiled slightly, "Goodbye, Vanda. I love you."
And with that, he blasted off towards the sunset.

Purple, smoke, ground, yellow, purple.

Strange things to see when you first wake up.

The figure pushed himself slowly to his feet, trying to ignore the pain that shot through him as he did. His vision was nothing but a blur, and the ground beneath him trembled.

What exactly had happened to him?

X rubbed his head, trying to ease the headache. Of course, every single part of him hurt, but his head would be a good place to start.

Wait, something wasn't right.

X quickly glanced at his hand, and his eyes widened. His hand, and arm, were normal, just like a regular minifigure. Last thing he remebered, he had been a werewolf hadn't he?

After quickly going over himself, he realized that he was, indeed, just a regular minifigure. He recognized the robes on him as the ones his Dark Form wore, only now they were torn and badly scorched. This again raised the question of what had happened to him.

He remebered fighting that idiot Nemisis, but everything after that was a blur.

Speaking of blurs, a very nasty looking blur was slowly walking towards him. Maelstrom, just what he needed.

He was too hurt to run, and he was too tired to fight. And apparently, he had lost his powers. Just great.

And oh the irony, after all he had done, he was going to be smashed by a stromling.

Just wonderful.

Fate, however, decided to give the confused fighter a break, as a blast of blue energy suddenly reduced the approaching stromling to dust. X, his vision finally clearing, turned to face whoever had just saved him.

A solid second passed, and then X closed his eyes, and groaned.

'Oh, why couldn't the stromling just have killed me?'

"Hello X. What's the matter? Not happy to see me?"

The mercenary opened his eyes, and glared at the golden figure across from him.

"Hello, Layab."

((Does anyone even remeber who Layab is?))

Layab clicked his tounge, and smirked.

"Why, X. Is that distaste I hear in you voice? Aren't you happy to see me?"

X was tempted to punch his old enemy, but decided against it. At least, not yet.

"How are you back? I destroyed you."

The figure in battered golden armor chuckled, and shook his head.

"Sorry, but no, you did not. You see, the final blast of energy you unleashed, let's just say it combined with the magical and kinetic energies our battle had unleashed, and... Relocated me. Since then, I've been absorbing the Maelstrom energy here, waiting for my chance to escape."

X narrowed his eyes, but couldn't keep the confused look off his face.

"Then how did I get here?"

At this, Layab shrugged, and turned around.

"I honestly don't know, but if you follow me, I'll show you the way out."

As Layab started walking off, X stared. Should he follow the strongest foe he had ever faced? Or just stay where he was and wait for the next stromling to come along?

Quickly, he hurried after his former employer.

The trip was made in silence, with X staggering along behind, and Layab waiting patiently for him to catch up, before walking further.

X also noticed a surprisngly lack of Maelstrom, espically considerin there was Maelstrom energy all throughtout this barren wasteland. Where ever he was.

"And here it is." Layab said suddenly, stopping in his tracks. X looked around, but didn't notice anything.

"Try looking up." Layab recommended, in a mocking tone. As X glanced into the air, his eyes widened.

"What is..."

"That, my old friend," Layab interrupted, "Is the end."

And they both stared, at a giant, glowing, portal, hanging just over their heads.
