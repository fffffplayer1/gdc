Swarm had finally regained his strength. Granted it had required draining the energy from 258 innocent people, which transformed them into mindless slaves, but that didn't bother him very much.

He was currently standing on the roof of the tallest building he could find, arms spread wide, facing toward the sun. The reason for this was simple. Swarm's new powers allowed him to consume any kind of light or energy, either from natural sources, like the sun, or by draining it from living beings. All light which touched him was instantly consumed, and this resulted in his body being wreathed in shadows. This served to make the already sinister appearance of his new natural form even more intimidating.

All in all, Swarm liked his new power. He liked his new look. He was even beginning to like the madness that filled his mind. However, he didn't like the shining white figure who was even now walking towards him.

Slowly Swarm turned to face the newcomer, greeting him with a voice laced with scorn.

"Greetings Arcolo. What brings a member of the White Knights, the universes greatest hunters of Black Knights, to my part of the universe?"

The purple eagle looked Buck over as the creatures charged towards them.

"Who...what are you?" Buck stammered as he started to crawl away.

The voice appeared in Buck's head again.

"I am your beast, your eagle. I have spent years trying to find someone that is skilled enough to fight along with me. There is a more important battle to be fought than this".

"No wait. You're saying that you need to take me to a more important battle" Buck said as he got up.

"Yes, it is a war between the dark and the light. The legend of the dark says that my companion is destined to make the dark win the war".

"You do know I have friends!!. I'm occupied as it is, you can't just take me away".

"I'm sorry but it is your destiny".

The eagle immediatly zommed at Buck and caught him. The eagle pulled Buck up onto it's back and flew off towards the clouds. The eagle whispered to Buck.

"Look Buck, this is your destiny and you can't decline it. I know it's hard, I didn't want to go around the world and find my rider when I started but it's something you have to face. Now, all we have to do is defeat the light with our power and hope that the light eagle does not find it's rider before we destroy the light".

"Why?" Buck asked as he began to take this all in.

"If the light eagle finds it's rider then both of us will have to destroy the rider and the eagle. Well, whatever comes first, we get destroyed or they get destroyed. Currently the light eagle is here in Nexus City searching for it's rider so we can destroy it".

"Isn't dark evil?".

"It's complicated. It's in another dimension where the war is making that dimension unstable. If we don't stop the war soon then that dimension will fall. If one dimension falls that disrupts the stream of time and stops everyting. There will be no future, no present and no past. Nothing will exist".

Buck sighed.

"Fine, let's do this other battle".

He was currently standing on the roof of the tallest building he could find, arms spread wide, facing toward the sun. The reason for this was simple. "Greetings Arcolo. What brings a member of the White Knights, the universes greatest hunters of Black Knights, to my part of the universe?"

Arcolo stared at Swarm, his face completely devoid of expression. "You have changed a great deal since we last met, Proto-soldier 17."

Swarm stiffened. After a moment he replied, his voice shaking with hate. "NEVER call me that. Never again."

Arcolo's thin lips curved upward in a cold smile. "I did not come here to argue. I came to investigate why you have been in contact with the enemy. Through the scanning equipment installed in your body we detected multiple beings infused with that... filth known as the Element of Black." He made no attempt to hide his disgust. "However, before we had time to take appropriate action, you disappeared. I never imagined that when we found you, you would be... re-corrupted."

Swarm stared at the White Knight in bewilderment for a moment, than realization dawned on him. "You know what happened to me. You know what happened, and I think you know why as well." Swarm stepped toward Arcolo, a long, shadowy blade forming in his hand. "You're going to tell me what's going on. Or else we'll see how fast I can cut you apart."
