The Wise Old Man sighed as he looked up at the perfectly clear sky.  He took a sip of his smoothie as he reclined in a lawn chair by his outdoor pool, which just happened to be thousands of feet in the air attached to the main tower.  The wind carried the smell of various exotic fruits from his orchard.  However, he had an ominous feeling that his retirement was going to be interrupted again.

 

“Dang it, not again!” he groaned.

 

Raising his hand, the air wavered, and then his crystal ball appeared suddenly in his hand.  He gazed into it, and while he was doing this, despite there being no clouds, the sky appeared to darken for just a moment.


“Oh, it’s one of these problems again.  Pesky upstarts.  You’d think they would get the hint from what happened to the others that interfered.”

 

He motioned at his attendant.


Prepare the Phantom for launch.  Also, unlike last time, I want there to be some real physical therapist, not dopey junior year wizards who were needing extra credit!  Just thinking about those baboons makes my back hurt.

Hov nods. "That sounds positively excellent. I have been bored rather recently, and this should make for a nice diversion!" He waved his hands, muttered something under his breath, then vaulted off the balcony. A soft glow encompassed his form, and he floated gently to the ground.

 

Say, Jagin. Look after the place while I'm gone, will ya'? It'll only be for a jiffy, I'm sure, but it never hurts to be safe... And make sure that the lass we found earlier with all the lightning doesn't try to name the girl, alright?

 

He received a sense of mental confirmation. Now, back to the matter at hand. It was a rather beautiful car, nice speaker systems, cushy seats... and no room. The only open seat was in the back, and Hov certainly wasn't going to sit squished between the other two. Well, there was a simple solution.

 

Hov walked around to the back of the car and popped open the trunk. It appeared that he could fit in there easily enough. Still, it wouldn't quite be comfortable.

 

He scraped his hand around the sides, his hands burning with a brilliant energy, runes flaring to life on the surfaces he touched. As he watched, the space seemed to grow, the walls pulling away from each other and stretching. Perfect. From the outside, the car appeared normal. But from the inside, it was at least the size of a good living room.

 

Hov stepped in, shut the trunk behind him, and started the opening of a portal. He could feel the room shudder as the car started up once more. It would be a decently lengthy trip. Might as well make it more pleasant.

 

A few minutes later, he pulled a nice leather recliner through his newly-made portal, along with various gadgets and gewgaws. No need to stop experimenting, after all!

Having no family or friends with her, Miranda decides the best and most direct course of action is to approach Ira and ask him about the current situation. Arround her, vortexes pop into existence as bricks and supplies flow calmly out of them and into the lush, if absolutely artificial, landscape. It seems as if this exact scenario was practiced over and over by the resident of Giovanni, as no one is panicking and everyting progresses almost just as Ira had explained. Sure, there were minor jostles in the crowd and the shouts of people looking for those they knew. But it was more orderly than any post-planetary annihilation this universe had ever seen.

 

Miranda cut her way through the crowd delicately. No one bumped in to her, despite the thickness of the crowd. Her posture clearly displayed confidence and direction, leading anyone in her who saw that she was walking straight ahead to move aside. A few times Miranda stopped to let others pass, but she moved quickly.

 

"Presence is king," she remembered from her classes, "Never let anyone doubt that you are in total control of the situation. Your average bystander looks for posture, body language, and, of course, good looks, when deciding who to follow." As one of few female students at her college, that last part was never difficult. She wasn't stunningly beautiful, but her clear sense of authority honed as she fought her way to becoming one of the Nexus Force's top prosecutors lent an air of perfection to her. She was, as it were, a self made woman. 

 

"Mr. Ira." Se called upon reaching the man. "Miranda Harris. I have a few questions for you, please."
