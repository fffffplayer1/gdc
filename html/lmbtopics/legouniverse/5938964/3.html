Chapter 10

I see about 20 Diseased heading straight towards us from the gate. I quickly pull out my Nexus pistol, shooting the Diseased. I jump over one and shoot it. "AUSTIN! GRAB THIS, REPAIR THE ELECTRIC FENCE!" I toss him the little device with string that can repair fences and other devices. "I'LL STOP UP THE HORDES. EMILY AND AMY, TRY TO REPAIR THE GENERATOR TO MAKE THE FENCE RUN! DYLAN, YOU STAY HERE WITH ME!" Dylan glares at me. "I don't take orders from you." "Well, orders keep us alive, bud." He reluctantly nods. Dylan tosses me an energy spike rifle. "Whoah..." "Yeah, I know." We jump in front of the gate, screaming for the Diseased to come to us. "YOU WANT SOME?" "DINNER TIME!" We scream, as the rest of the group is repairing the security breach. BLAM! Chic-chic! BLAM! Chic-chic! Goes the sound of the energy rifle. I hear a "wum-wum" sound from the generator. The electric fence was working! And... Austin had repaired the fence! "WOO HOO!" I scream. Dylan, for some reason, looks dissapointed... Suddenly, I hear an electric sound.  A yell. I turn around, and see Austin on the ground. Imagi-Electrix weapons? I hear more high pitched sounds, and soon, the only members of the group that aren't injured are me and Dylan. "Hello, Indy."

Chapter 11.1: Aggresive Negotiations

Dwight

 

Dwight glared across the table. He had arrived at the First Builder Temple for the Mythran Conclave 30 minutes ago, and he was already angry with the rest of the council. He sat at the head of the rectangular jungle wood slab. A tousled stack of papers and a quill pen lay in front of him. To his right was an empty seat. He scowled. This was where his father, Eoghan, would have sat, but he was away on business. "The winds of the past blow strongly," he had told Dwight before leaving. "I must listen harder if I am to divine the future." Without his father's guidance, Dwight was inexperienced in Mythran politics and found it hard to keep a rein on his impatience. He was used to the efficiency of a military unit, not a squabbling group trying to reach their goals without making a compromise.

 To his left was Houdin Krell, a heavy, blustery man with a prodigious appetite. He wasn't so bad, but he was impossible to argue with. Next to Houdin was Vera, a thin-faced woman who fervently disagreed with EVERYTHING Dwight and his father proposed for no reason. Across from her was Uthar Seowyn, a bulky Mythran dressed in full armor. He was sympathetic to Dwight and Eoghan, but solved problems with some form of yelling or violence. He was difficult to negotiate with at the best of times.

At the other head of the table sat Theed Faine. A tall man with fair hair and cold blue eyes, Faine presided over the council. He was given power by those he had power over, in a democratic fashion, but he bordered on despotic.. Dwight disliked the other Council members, but he hated Theed Faine without restraint. Faine was arrogant, cruel, and power hungry-standards the Mythrans did not often tolerate. In return to Dwight's opinion, Faine thought of him as an insolent, over-promoted, self-righteous upstart. It was Faine whom Dwight was glaring at, and he who returned the glare in full force. Theirs was not a friendly relationship.

Chapter 11.2

 

Houdin was in the middle of a long-winded tirade about the Pandora situation. "...find a way to quarantine or eliminate the Diseased as soon as possible! The minifigures are trapped and need assisstance, and young master SArun-" he nodded to Dwight. "-was the only one to escape the slaughter of our relief company. We...must...act.!" The other Council members were listening rather disinterestedly. They'd heard this speech before.

"I have heard rumors," said Uthar, who was paring his nails with a jeweled dagger. "Rumors that eat away the minifigures' confidence in us. They speak in hushed voices that the Disease is a Mythran concoction, fabricated to wipe them out in one fell swoop. Why," he surveyed the room with beady eyes. "Why do they say such things. Faine, what do you know of this?" Dwight inhaled sharply. The minifigures supported Dwight, and through him, his father, and Faine was inconvenienced by their tremendous influence. He was also willing to do anything to get what he wanted. This would be the kind of brazen power play he needed to undermine his opposition.

Faine smirked. "I wouldn't call it that. More like a distraction, so they won't interfere."

He paused, and Vera cut in. "Interfere with what, Faine? Justify your actions. Many innocent people suffered from this!" Faine was silent.

"So it was you," Dwight growled. "I don't care how important you are. If what you say in the next two minutes doesn't impress me, which it won't, I promise you'll come to identify with those affected by the Disease. Most specifically, their pain." It was no idle threat.

Now Faine smiled wider. "Listen, friends, and listen well. I shall explain all. You'll have to put a hold on that vengeance, though, Dwight, for I may take longer than two minutes. It is, as they say, a long story."

 Chapter 11.3

 

Faine continued. "We all know the saying thet history tends to repeat itself. Some of us have lived long enough to prove it. I believe that chaos and order must coexist for peace. If the balance is tipped, sooner or later one will return. I am talking, of course, about the Maelstrom." The room seemed to darken as he spoke the last words. "To prevent mass destruction and another Great War, I would propose a plan of action."

"Wait,"" Dwight held up a hand. "You never really answered Uthar's question. Did you authorize the release of the disease?" The tension in the room thickened. Dwight stared at Faine, daring him to deny it.

"Of course," the Mythran shrugged. "What of it?"

"I can't believe you!" Dwight shouted. "That epidemic destroyed Pandora and infected thousands of innocent people! Mow you're insinuating-"

"That I'm going to control the Maelstrom?" Faine cut in. "Indeed. With it under our control..."

"The Maelstrom cannot be controlled," Houdin puffed.

Faine spread his hands. "Typhonus did it. Why not us?"

At those words, Dwight drew his wakizashi sword out of its sheath to fast for even the Mythran eye to follow. He leaned forward over the table and leveled its blue blade at Faine's throat. "Recognize this?" He said calmly. He turned the blade so it caught the light. "It was this sword that smashed the Darkitect. If you think to compare yourself to Typhonus, I'll bet it can very well do the same in your case. Comprende?"

For the first time, Faine looked nervous. "Th-that won't be neccessary," he stuttered. Dwight sheathed his weapon, staisfied.
