Chapter 11.4

 

Faine kept talking. "In any case, you are all wrong. We can control the Maelstrom! Typhonus went mad, yes, but he was a mere mortal! A Mythran such as you or I, with a strong will and a fraction of his power, could keep it in line. I knew the minifigures would try to stop us, so I... distracted them. Now we can take full command of the powers of both Chaos and Order! Peace will endure, for with both yin and yang at our fingertips, we cannot be challenged.

"I like your assumption that the Mythran must be male," Vera murmured.

Houdin leaned forward. "Surely you can't be serious," he began. "The Maelstrom would poison your mind the instant you absorbed it! How do you know it won't destroy us all?"

"That's easy," said Faine. "Because I've already obtained some myself."

Suddenly a thicket of blades were pointed at Faine. Dwight leapt onto the tabletop and angled Excelsior at his opponent again, while Uther hefted a menacing pole-ax. Vera drew an elegant poniard from her sash, and even Houdin mustered a weapon, timorously holding a cheese knife.

"Oh, this is all useless," Faine said good-naturedly. "Put those things away - we're all friends here."

"He who consorts with the enemy is no friend of ours," growled Uthar.

"Waht makes you think we'll just let you live?" added Dwight. "Give me one reason not to take you down now."

Faine grinned. "I can give you six reasons. They'rea ll that's left of your relief company, and they're trapped in Pandora. If you wish to save them, I'd run."

Dwight sprang backward with an oath and started for the door. Then he turned back. "you do realize I'll just come back and get you in the end, right? That's always what happens to the overconfident villain."

Faine's smile widened. "I can give you two more reasons not to do that. First, your wife - what was her name, Reyna?" Dwight stumbled like he'd been struck "And second, you newborn daughter. So you see, Mr. Sarun, I have thought of everything."

Chapter 11.5

Dwight, who had long since sheathed Excelsior, raised his hands to waist level with his palms skyward. A spark, then  a glowing nimbus of blue fire erupted above each one. "You stay away from her," he said in a low voice. "You stay away from them both."

Small clouds of indigo mist began to rom around Faine's fingertips. Dwight tensed; even after six year he recognized the Maelstrom when he saw it. The other Council members edged away from the conflict. "Oh, please," Faine's eyes flashed. "You wouldn't have a chance, youngling."

Dwight stepped forward menacingly. "You could never best me." The flames above his hands grew brighter with his anger. "If you touch my family or friends, I'll personally beat you within an inch of your life in front of the entire Order. That's a promise, not a threat." Silence settled over the room. Faine and Dwight held each others' gaze without blinking. Whoever broke eye contact first would lose an unestaclished contest to no cost but his pride. Uthar and Vera watched warily, and Houdin squirmed in his seat.

Theed Faine was the first to look away. "I tire of this," he said nonchalantly. "Go save your little friends. See if I care."

Dwight didn't move. "How'd you do it?"

Faine's eyes flicked upward exasperatedly. "Do what?"

"'Obtain' the Maelstrom. The minifigures destroyed it, I took out Typhonus myself! So... how?"

Faine shrugged. "The shards of Baron Typhonus's staff were placed at the location where you defeated him. They still conatined residue from the original Maelstrom spore. I happened to be around the block, so I... appropriated it."

Dwight's eyes narrowed. "Fine." He started for the door, then turned back and jabbed a finger at Faine. "Try anything funny-"

"And you'll what? I can always make an appointment to infect your family first."

Dwight drew Excelsior again, only three inches out of its sheath so the blade was visible. "Touch them and you'll discover a new type of pain."

Chapter 11.6

As soon as the warning left Dwight's mouth, Faine countered. "Then who knows? Maybe they'll be the ones I send to get rid of you." He smiled, showing impossibly white teeth.

Faster than the speed of thought, Dwight slammed Excelsior back into its sheath, rushed forward, and flipped the table on end. Cups and stacks of paper flew into the air. The Council dove from their chairs and sought cover. Faine moved too slowly and was caught on the shoulder by the heavy slab of wood as it jacknifed across the room. In the moments before impact he felt a modicum of disbelief at Dwight's incredible strength before he was throw backward and pinned to the wall by a crushing weight. Dwight was already on the move, anticipating a counterattack that never came. He ran to an open window and leapt out, alighting on the ledge outside the building. The black void of Rivendark canyon stared back at him.Bright stars of the Crux Nebula winked in the sky. Dwight spread his arms and let himself fall.

Past thestone plateau that formed the foundations of the First Builder Temple, past the vine-covered cliffs into the empty space in between the jungle and still-desolate Aura Mar. He angled himself as he fell so that he was facedown, and brought his fingers to his lips. Allowing himself a breath, he let out a sharp, shrill whistle. A roar sounded from below, and a black object that at first seemed to be a contour in the cliffs took off and flew toward him. Butterscorch, the dragon he'd rescued from Typhonus's hatcheries all those years ago, caught up to him with three beats of her 13-foot wingspan. Dwight landed perfectly on her back, between two of the biggest spikes at the base of her neck. "Take us home, girl," he said in a hollow voice. He patted her flank. "Yah!" Butterscorch twirled into a corkscrew dive, then rose above the lip of Aura Mar and flew like the wind in the direction of Gnarled Forest.
