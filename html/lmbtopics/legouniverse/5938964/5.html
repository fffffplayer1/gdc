Chapter 12.1: The Dark is Rising

Back at the First Builder Temple, the inhabitants of the conference room were just now recovering from the recent kerfuffle. Council members picked themselves up off the floor, castring furtive glances at the overturned table in the back to the room. AAS they stood, Vera opened her mouth. Before she could say anything, there was a noise like a thunderclap from behind them, they turned back to find the table spilt in two; Faine had blasted his way out. He was clutching his shoulder with one hand, the other was aflame with indigo. "Blasted boy," he wheezed. "He'll not escape me again." He blinked, then peered at the others, as if just noticing them. "Almost forgot you," he admitted, raising his infected hand. Uthar whipped out his axe with a snarl, but the others backed away. Houdin spoke: "Now, Faine, let's talk about this..." A tendril of mist snaked out of Faine's palm, splitting into three beams that lashed about and speared each of the council members. They were not harmed, but the Maelstrom infection process bega and ended faster than it ever had during the Great War. One might take a moment to realize that this mixture of Maelstrom and Mythran was more efficient than either alon, before also remembering that it was controlled by an evil maniac. "I've had enough of talk," Faine growled. "Now you are all mine. Isn't this peace? Isn't this cooperation?" None of them answered, of course, because he hadn't ordered them to. "Ha! Silence, blessed silence! I like that! You have no idea how long I've planned for this! All that's missing..." he paused, then said more quietly, to himself, "All that's missing is the boy and the senile fortuneteller. Can't infect them, best get rid of them as fast as possible. But that will come, in time." He stood and then he laughed. His era had begun. Theed Faine did not stop laughing for a long time.

"Seamus.... I need our best men in the Room. Now.'" I grabbed a pistol on my desk and started running to the ship docks. Seamus was a Follower. "NED! I NEED YOU TO GET IN YOUR SHIP, NOW. GET TO THE ROOM. I'LL MEET YOU THERE." I spoke through the intercom. "Night? Why-" "JUST GET OVER THERE, NOW! THERE'S NO TIME!"  I jumped down the stairs of the hotel we were staying in (Our "HQ") right after we had finished the Assassin Case. I started running out the glass doors that seperated the Hotel from Pandora... "Seamus?!" Great. Just what I needed. The peanut gallery, Dwight Lavamonster. "Bricks... Night, you need to hurry-" "Shut up, Dwight." I finally reached the huge ship docks, and noticed the Dominion Mk 2. I lowered the ramp, and ran inside as fast as I could.

***

The Room was on an abandoned asteroid, in the Outer Space. No one had ever found it... Until Seamus. Seamus... How had I been so gullible? Him noticing the particles on the Lead Assassin,... The ship suddenly stopped, so I hopped out and noticed tons of soldiers were outside waiting for me. The soldiers smiled. "Seamus's inside." I looked up, and saw the huge prison, known as the Room. It had a Crack in the Universe, the perfect prison, which held the most dangerous being in the entire Universe; Baron Typhonus, The Darkitect. Seamus Gold, our most trusted police task force member, was Typhonus's pawn. Me and the company of soldiers started running towards the prison. Put in the password, got through the series of near impossible questions, went through trials, and finally found ourselves on a white marble floor, with a huge wall with a crack in it in front of it. Next to that wall, was a tall, thin, slender minifigure. "I've been waiting for you, Night. Now. Shall we begin?"
