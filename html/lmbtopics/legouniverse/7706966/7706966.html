FOREWORD

 

A few years ago on the Creation Lab, a few friends created a Star Trek-Themed roleplay universe called the Phoenix Command Group, or PCG. Other builders and roleplayers quickly gravitated to the PCG to be awesome and have a good time. I am one of those people.

 

A brief history of me is that I was Captain Thingguy of the Federation before I was convicted of blowing up a Federation Starbase. I was expelled from Starfleet and started my own faction called the UIS, or Union of Independent Systems. My ship is the Thingguyian, a Thaddeous-Class Battleship, and I am the Commander-In-Chief of UIS forces. Any other questions regarding my or the PCG’s history I will happily answer to the best of my ability.

 

This story is not strictly related to LEGO Universe. This is a story that I started on the CL related to the awesome role-play universe of the PCG, but never finished. I am reposting what I have written and will give this story a real ending. I respectfully request that the mods let this up, as it is abstractly related to LU, and that people who read this on the CL don’t give the ending away.

 

And now, the story.

PROLOGUE -- Part One

With the formation of the UIS and Admiral Thingguy’s decision to declare war on the DranCor having been passed by the Senate, the militia of the UIS was vastly expanded in number and weaponry, and was given the name Spartan Force. The planet Nuri, previously uninhabited (or so we guessed) for its harsh environment and terrain, became the Spartan Force training ground. When the DranCor still seemed to be superior to the Spartan Force, Admiral Thingguy (who was at this point considered an eccentric but ingenious person) brought the idea of the Spartan Commandos before the UIS Senate.

The idea was approved, but Chancellor Santor insisted that the first squad trained be called the Santorum force, and placed under direct control of the Chancellor alone. Admiral Thingguy agreed, and began Operation Elite.

Sixteen Spartan Soldiers, who showed both exceptional military techniques and no close relatives, were chosen by Admiral Thingguy to be Spartan Commandos. They were all sent on a textbook suicide mission, but were abducted by Admiral Thingguy’s guard before their mission was complete. According to the Galactic Database, they were now just another statistic.

Prologue -- Part Two

The sixteen soldiers were taken back to Nuri, and placed in an underground facility. In that facility, their bodies were genetically altered for increased physical abilities, tactical decisions, and damage resistance. The commandos also had their memories erased, and a fake history was given to each one. The Galactic Alliance was never made aware of Operation Elite, in violation of the treaty signed between the UIS and Galactic Alliance.

Four of the commandos were chosen for the Santorum Force, hand-picked by Chancellor Santor himself: Soldier 22, nicknamed Beast; Soldier 70, nicknamed Dart; Soldier 34, nicknamed Leopard; and Soldier 01, nicknamed Reff. Beast was put in command of the squad, and the other three were tasked with whatever their specialties were. Dart was an amazingly accurate sniper, Leopard was handy with electronics, and Reff was fond of explosives.

This is the story of me, Soldier 22, and my command of the most elite squad in the galaxy.

Chapter One--Part One

Flanked by Dart and Reff, Beast entered the office of Chancellor Santor. The Chancellor, who was sitting at his desk, looked up at the three commandos, was puzzled for a moment, and then smiled. Signaling for his guards to leave him, he stood up from his chair, walked around his desk, and took Beast by the arm.

“You must be 22,” said the Chancellor, “and this must be your squad. But I thought there were four of you?”

“Soldier 34 is out with the shuttle,” said Beast, “This is 70, and this is 01.”

“Nice to meet you,” said Santor, examining the squad. Twenty-two looked enough like a soldier; tough, muscular, and with strong armor. Seven-oh was the same, but slightly smaller than Twenty-two, but that automatic sniper rifle of his looked like it meant business. As for Oh-One, he looked more like a comic relief than a demolitionist. He had no helmet, either on his head or under his arm, and he had a seemingly permanent sarcastic smile on his face. But a smile wasn’t necessarily something bad for a demolitionist. “Anyway, would you so kindly ask Three-Four to come in here?”

Beast gestured to Reff, and he nodded and smiled. Reff turned and left the room and Dart made to go with him. Beast stopped the commando and told him to remove his helmet in the presence of the Chancellor. He grimaced, and slowly took off his helmet, but left his balaclava on.

Beast turned his attention back to the chancellor, and saw Santor open a drawer in his desk and pull out a holodisk. Beast guessed it was their first assignment.

Chapter One - Part Two

Reff and Leopard came into the room, and the Chancellor inserted the holodisk into a projector, and a DranCor Wolverine Battleship popped up.

“Do you know what this is?” asked the Chancellor.

“A DranCor Wolverine Battleship,” answered Reff.

“Good,” said the Chancellor. He pressed a button, and second, identical projection popped up, “Do you know what this is?”

“A DranCor Wolverine Battleship,” said Reff again.

“Wrong,” said the Chancellor.

“But it looks just like the other one,” said Reff.

“No, it doesn’t,” said Leopard, “look; it has those black markings on the front.”

Santor looked at Three-Four approvingly. The commando looked quite tough as well, with a thick helmet and large jetpack to go with it. He also carried a large electro-blaster that looked like it could take out a Brog.

“Right, Three-Four,” said Santor, “it’s the DranCor’s new Wolverine II class. It’s at least ten times more powerful and three times tougher. Those black markings are more of a recognition symbol than a technological advancement. Your mission is to destroy it, before it reaches any critical UIS systems. So far it’s just been pestering the outlying planets.”

“How large a fleet are we going to lead against it?” asked Dart.

Santor shook his head, “the four of you are going in alone.”

Chapter Two – Part One

“Wait, wha--” said Reff, then quickly corrected himself. “I mean, excuse me, sir, did I hear you right? Alone?”

“You did hear my right, Oh-One,” said Santor.

“With all due respect, however much that is,” Beast gave Reff a hard elbow in the gut, but Santor seemed to find it amusing. “Didn’t you just say this thing is impregnable? As in invincible?”

“I do recall saying something to that effect, yes,” said Santor calmly.

“Then what in the name of Elvis are you--”

“I think what Oh-One’s trying to say is that he doesn’t think the four of us can take out a Supercruiser ourselves, regardless of our Elite training,” Beast interrupted. “Do we have any intel on this ship?”

“Pretty much none,” said Santor. “You are to infiltrate the ship, via the exhaust jets, and collect some Intel. And then I will assign you critical systems to sabotage.”

“How are we going in?” asked Leopard, “I mean, how are we going to get close enough to get into the Exhaust jets? Our helmets only have so much oxygen. And Reff’s rebreather has even less.”

“Reff?”

“Sorry, sir, I meant Oh-One’s rebreather.”

“A Willard-Class Gunship is going to pose as a Pizza Shuttle, and it’s going to pass by the Wolverine II,” said Santor. “Your first objective is to get out before it shoots you.”

Chapter Two - Part Two

“What about the pilot?” asked Dart.

“It’ll be a droid,” said Santor. “It’s always a shame to lose a good droid, but it’s worth it in this case.”

“I’m happy to hear you consider the UIS to be more important than a pilot droid,” said Reff.

Beast elbowed Reff hard in the side, and gave him a stern glance.

“I’m sorry about Oh-One’s behavior, Chancellor,” said Beast. “Be sure I’m going to have a talk with him.”

“That’s quite all right,” said Santor humorously. “We’re very likely to be working together for a long time. Might as well put some humor between us.”

Santor waved them their dismissal, and they turned to leave. But Leopard turned back and asked one final question.

“Sir, how are we going to Evac from the DranCor ship?”

“You’re going to get creative with that one,” said Santor, “your advisor will guide you through the ship, and will be helping you Evac.”

“I see, sir,” said Leopard, and Santorum Force left the room.

Operation Elite -- Chapter Three Part One

Inside the disguised Pizza shuttle, Santorum Force braced for their upcoming mission. Their training officer, Thaddeous Thingguy, had said that first deployments always made you feel like you’re knee-deep in peanut butter. Beast had never been knee-deep in peanut butter, but he assumed this is what it felt like. His stomach was churning horribly, and his feet felt numb.

“Santorum, this is your Advisor, you’re coming up on Target Alpha,” said a voice in Beast’s helmet. Beast wondered what safe place their advisor was monitoring them from, then shook the thought out of his head. He was here to destroy an enemy warship, not dwell on the unfairness of war.

“Roger, advisor,” said Beast. “Squad, secure helmets and rebreathers.”

Beast, Dart, and Leopard turned on their air filters, and Reff took a rebreather out of his pack and secured it on his head. It was a simple helmet-like device that protected the wearer from space conditions, water conditions, and other situations that required oxygen.

“Reff, open cargo doors,” said Beast.

Reff nodded and pressed a glowing red button on a dashboard console. The port-side cargo doors hissed open and Beast held his hand up, preparing to give the order to bail out.

Chapter Three Part Two

“Everyone grab a piece of scrap metal,” said Leopard. The plan was to float in, posing as pieces of metal that were blown away from the Willard-Class, so that someone didn’t notice a squad of commandos floating towards them.

Beast saw a Disruptor turret on the Wolverine turn to face the gunship.

“Everyone out!” yelled Beast, and pushed Santorum Force through the open hatch. The Disruptor blasts hit the shuttle and the explosion threw Beast spinning through space towards the Wolverine II.

“Look, ma, no hands,” came Reff’s voice over the communicators built into their helmets, and in Reff’s case, his ear.

“You haven’t got a ma,” said Dart.

“I’m sure some nice old lady will adopt me, I’m very lovable…”

“Squad, stay focused.”

“Yes, Twenty-two.”

“Sorry, Tutu.”

Beast gritted his teeth and did some mental calculations. They were all going to land in separate vents. Evidently the advisor noticed too.

“Santorum, you’ll be split up once you get inside, do you all remember RV point Alpha?”

“Yes, sir.”

“Good, prepare to infiltrate.”

CHAPTER FOUR - Part One


“Santorum, this Beast, did everyone make the infiltration?” Beast was somewhere in the Engine housing, and wasn’t sure how to get out.

“Beast, this Leopard, that’s affirmative. Three-four out.”

“Roger, Lead. Seven-Oh out.”

“I’m here, Tutu. Oh-One out.”

The squad had made it. First successful infiltration.

“Squad, make your way to RV Alpha,” said Beast. “Uploading coordinates now.”

“Roger, Tutu,” said Reff. Beast wondered how Reff had the HUDs that the rest of them had without a helmet. He’d have to ask him sometime.

Beast wandered around the massive engines, looking for a maintenance shaft or doorway. He couldn’t find anything. He continued to look under pipes and boxes of various kinds of technology, and finally gave up.

“Advisor, can you scan the engine housing and find a way out?” asked Beast.

“Negative, Twenty-two,” said the Advisor, “They have a frequency scanner that would sense my scans and give away the element of surprise. I recognize the radiation signatures.”

“Wouldn’t they detect our communicators?” asked Beast.

CHAPTER FOUR - Part Two


“Our communicators are specially encrypted. Nothing can detect them.”

“Understood, sir,” said Beast, and he continued to search the Engine housing.

After about five more minutes of searching, Beast finally found a large door in the wall of the housing. The door wouldn’t open by itself, so Beast coaxed it into submission using his sidearm. Finally leaving the housing, he turned down a wide hallway and jogged in the direction of RV Alpha.

“Oh-One at RV Alpha,” said Reff into his communicator. A few seconds later, Dart repeated the statement.

“Ha, I beat you,” Reff said to Dart.

“Don’t get cocky.”

“Three-Four at RV Alpha.”

“This is Twenty-two, I’m coming up on the RV point.”

Beast rounded a corner, and scanned the area through the Ironsights of his sidearm. Convinced he was clear, Beast ran a few more feet and came upon the three other Santorums, all behind cover.

“Twenty-two at RV Alpha,” said Beast. “Santorum Force ready.”
