*Another paper airplane comes cruising out of the portal*

 

(Last chapter was page 297)

 

~Chapter 25~

~Part 2~

 

 

The woman rang up the order before announcing the grand total for the coffees. Thankfully the Mythrans didn't know enough about the Minifigure economy to fully comprehend how disgustingly expensive it was, so much so that the cashier didn't even blink an eye when they paid with gold.

 

"And your names?" she said as she slid the paltry amount of change to them.

 

Yithion looked at her confusedly. "Names?"

 

She rolled her eyes, obviously insulted by the lack of intelligence of the world around her. "Yeah, names. So we can call you over when your order is ready."

 

"Oh, I see. Yithion and Anmithrayy." Satisfied, the two of them took their change and went to go find a table, leaving the cashier holding two cups and a Sharpie, wondering how to spell their names.

 

 

"I have to say," Anmithrayy admitted as he adjusted his sunglasses and leaned out of his chair to stare through the window, "I could never have imagined a place like this. Everything is so strange!" He turned and began curiously fiddling with the salt and pepper shakers. He started in surprise when little black grains fell out of one. Hastily, he put the shakers back and swept the mess under the table, glancing around. "Has it changed much since you first came here?"

 

Yithion's eyes got a far-away look in them. "In some ways, no. But this trip has felt as though I've never been here before. Or maybe I just couldn't appreciate it then like I can now," he murmured reflectively. He shook his head as if to rid himself of a bad thought and said suddenly, "I'll tell you the story of my first visit. It was an awful long time ago, when I was in my twenties..." He went on, fondly describing his first adventures encountering Minifigures.
