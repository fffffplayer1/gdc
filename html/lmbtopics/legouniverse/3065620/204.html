Jammers

(remember, this is a luxery yacht )  I added three more holographic displays, two for the armrests of the chairs and one large one for the front of the room.  All three are connected to state of the art computers.  I replaced the standard glass windows with military grade laser resistant glass (though not even close to laser proof of course).  I also upgraded the standard civilian deflector shield to two standard Ranger starfighter deflector shields. I also updraded/added thrusters and upgraded the engines for increased mobility.  And last, I added two ion cannons, four light laser cannons, and  two heavy starfighter grade laser cannons.  For all external updates I recieved the help of Rander mechanics+technicians.  Remember, this is a luxery ship, so although it's small, me and my crew fly in style.

JAMMERS ON!!! RANGERS ONLY!!!

 

Jammers
Everyone, please, before you write this off as more angry yelling or a declaration of war, listen. A few months ago, an argument of what became of the Lieutenant ignited a civil war within the Galactic Nexus, one of the last lines of defense against the slowly-growing Maelstrom and Void forces. I found myself under fire as a minifig trapped for crimes I had not done, and, out of self-defense, I fled with violence and rage. I first attacked out of survival, then vengeance, widening the gap and creating hatred. But what has this done? All we have left is a trail of flaming wreckage between the Balancers and GN across the galaxy, and allowed the Void and Maelstrom enough free time to gain strength again. With Krill back, and now wielding DI, we are all outmatched. Not one ship in any fleet can match the Oblivion, a relic of the wars from last summer. Clearly, if we continue to war with each other, then we will both be eliminated by the Void, who already has a large number of forces. I cannot stand alone, though I have the Cubes that are among the few weapons that can purge unshielded DI. You guys can fight them head-on, but cannot permanently destroy DI. I propose we reforge the alliance that once was the spearpoint of the Rebellion: The Rangers and Bane, fighting as one. We did so much good against the Void, driving them back plenty. I know I have wrongfully been rude and unwise, but now I see that we must bridge the old gap. Neither of us can win this war; I will just keep running and hiding low, guerrila-style, while you and the GN are too large to defeat alone. Our only chance of fighting the Void is to work together. I am asking you,Rangers, to take this first step, and remember our friendship of old to help bring the Balancers back into the GN so we can fight them. However, keep this secret from our Void opponents. If the enemy finds out my intent, then I cannot use the fact they believe I am at war with you. Good luck.

 -Bane
