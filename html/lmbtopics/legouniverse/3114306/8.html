Chapter 2.2 – A team

“Come on, let’s get out of the tent,” the boy coaxed. I didn’t really want to leave, what if Master was looking for me? “You can’t sit here all day; our unit is waiting on us.” When I refused to budge, the boy muttered something about untrained and pulled me outside by my collar.

It wasn’t the same spot I had fainted in; in fact it had to be far away. There wasn’t any grass on the ground, except for a few sprigs here and there; it was all rocks. Although the land around us was flat, off in the distance we could see some cliffs. There wasn’t any Maelstrom here, but somehow I felt that had to be a ‘not yet’ sort of thing.

A round of twenty five minifigs invaded the place, including the boy; each one was bustling around as if their life depended on being busy. “Black, line up!” A bass voice shouted from behind a small building.

(These small buildings are actually tents, but Aurora doesn’t know that.)

“Come on, we’re black team,” the boy said, looking at me with his black irises. A group of minifigs assembled in an orderly line, and the boy led me to them. Every step sent little waves of pain up and down my back. My side still hurt.

The boy got last into line, dragging me by my collar next to him. A man, the same one with the deep voice, came out and stood in front of the minifigs. “Mallof, Skill, Wesley, Ron, Jake,” he started, naming each one in line, “You are all already in the Nexus Force and will be sent to defend Avant Gardens. You are Black Team; you will treat each other as a team and work together. You all leave tomorrow, get to know each other. Ron, you will be this dog’s handler,” he said pointing to me. No, no not him. I recognized him from the Sentinel camp…

“Aw, not the mutt!” Ron complained, eyeing me with loathing.

“What I say goes.” The man said simply and left.

I observed my ‘team’. It was a measly group of just five. The boy was the first I noticed, his name was Jake. Well, so was said. He was wearing simple blue and white clothes. Ron was wearing a peculiar tri tipped brown hat with green clothes. He carried a small gun, but kept it hidden on his belt. Wesley wasn’t as interesting. He and Mallof wore almost the same exact thing. A black helmet with black and red shoulder pads with thick black gear. Mallof was the same person I had seen earlier, and I found it disturbing that I hadn’t seen, or heard him say a word. Skill reminded me of Lilly, at least a little bit. Skill bore blue and light gray armor, he had taken his helmet off, but it looked futuristic.

“Does anyone feel the lacking of Assembly? I mean we have a Sentinel, a Venture Leaguer and two Paradoxes!” Jake said, the only one here who doesn’t have a faction. “One day I’ll be Assembly.”

“Yeah…wait how are you even in the Nexus Force anyways? I don’t even think you’re ten.” Wesley said through his helmet.

“You’re right, I’m not ten.” Jake smirked wickedly and sat down.

Wesley was going to say something, but held his tongue. “Never mind. Also what are we going to do about the dog? We can’t can her ‘dog’ the whole time.”

“Eh…just call her Mutt.” Ron mumbled. I started snarl at him; no way was I going to stand for him. Wesley’s eyes grew wide and he yelped noisily. Next thing we knew he was hiding behind a small building.

“Wesley? What might ya be doing back there?” Skill said, trying to find Wesley.

“Look…look at those teeth!” Wesley stuttered.

“This little thing?” Skill patted me on the head, trying to get me to stop snarling.

“But they’re so sharp!” Wesley whimpered; his voice was becoming rather pathetic. Why those were just my teeth, they are supposed to be sharp. If they were dull what use would they be? I couldn’t understand.

Skill fell to his knees laughing hysterically. “Even the Maelstrom isn’t that scared. And they have a reason to be scared.”

Wesley’s head poked out. “Well…” He slowly approached back to the other minifigs. “What will we call her?” He said calming his voice once I stopped growling.

A heavy silence settled over the group for a long time. This proceeded until the point where I thought Jake was asleep, and then Wesley spoke up, “What about Ulani?” Five heads turned all to me at once as if on cue. “I’m sure she’s had a name before, but since we don’t know it, Ulani will be her name.”

Ulani? What an interesting name.

Chapter 2.1 – Split with broken hearts



I shook my head back and forth, fighting to keep consciousness. Strange smells filled my nose; several were normal, grass, minifigs, trees, and squirrels. Others, however, were unidentifiable. Blurred vision made it impossible for me to see exactly where I was. More important than that was: why wasn’t I smashed?



I recalled the last moment up to where I had been hit. Master getting out of danger, me fighting the spider, getting whacked by one of the spider’s legs, and finally being surrounded by Maelstrom. I blinked rapidly a few times as I tried to get my vision strait. Was I still next to the Maelstrom?



I could see now. I was in a small gray, drab building, the walls shook and flapped about, and the sides were held up by poles.



“She’s sort of awake. I think, let’s see.” A boy’s voice said. Suddenly somebody slopped a bucket of freezing water onto me. “Wake up time!” A boy said, a little too loud for being in such a small room. I practically flew to my feet. My fur was completely drenched, and little flakes of ice stuck to my fur. Wide awake now, I looked up at the two minifigs in the room. One was a young boy; he had shadowy, short, unkempt hair and black piercing eyes. To me it was disturbing, even his bright white clothes couldn’t contrast the black, evil look to his face. The other one was a tall man; he wore black shoulder pads, which swirled with red at the edges. I couldn’t see his face though, he wore black helmet with a shaded visor. I looked down at my paw; it was golden yellow. This guy’s gear has too much black for me.



The mini patted the boy on the shoulder. He stared at me for a moment, and then was somehow gone from the room.



“Sorry for soaking you like that,” the boy said as soon as the man left, “Welcome to the Nexus Force.”



The Nexus Force? Suddenly it hit me, like a rock would. Where was Master? He had to have gotten away, but where was he now? Was he just outside, or…where? Was he in the Nexus Force now? Did…did he forget about me?



………………………………………………………………………………………………………....



Kale stood inside the Venture Explorer, arguing with one of the ship’s workers. “You can’t leave! Aurora is still out there, fighting Maelstrom!”



“These recruits are ready to leave, and what if those Spiderlings come here? We have the bricks we need. We are not going to risk the lives of everyone on here for a dog. Just a simple little useless dog.” One of the workers grumbled. Then pointing towards the girl Kale had saved, he continued, “We’re already allowing you an extra passenger. We don’t have time to search for a stupid dog, which may, or may not be alive.”



With this, Kale reached up and shoved the guy down to the ground. “Stupid you say?” Kale dove in on top, slamming the man into the flooring, and making the whole ship shake.



“Get off of me!” The mini shouted crossly while pushing up on Kale’s shoulders, desperately trying to stand up.



Another worker heard the rumble and dashed into the room where the two were found fighting on the floor. “Whoa, whoa, whoa!” He shouted and tried to pry Kale off. Kale’s fingertips were like glue and were stuck onto the other worker. “Captain!” The second worker cried loudly.



Outside the ship Kale could hear the windup charge power of a weapon; then the door creaked open. “Scatter. Now!” the captain shouted. She was rather short, but also imposing with the gun she held pointed at Kale.



Kale obediently stepped of the worker and took a few steps in reverse. He gulped hard, but didn’t speak.



“Now, are you going to act civilized, or will you two continue to beat each other like your possessed with Maelstrom?” The captain asked calmly, letting her gun cool down. When neither of them replied, she continued. “Good, now you, Kale, take a seat. You, John and Ben, you’re my workers. So work.” With a strange smile, Captain scurried off to another part of the ship.



Kale was quickly ushered back to where all of the other recruits were waiting for take-off. All of the seats were taken, the last one had the little girl in it, and so Kale sat cross-legged on the floor. It was sure going to be a bumpy ride.



“There are Nexus Force troops down in Avant Gardens. Your dog, she’ll be alright.” The captain walked into the room, and stood next to Kale.



Kale wasn’t so sure, but he still nodded in agreement; he was aware she had a gun.



“Or maybe, she won’t”

Although the land around us was flat, off in the distance we could see some cliffs. There wasn’t any Maelstrom here, but somehow I felt that had to be a ‘not yet’ sort of thing.

A round of twenty five minifigs invaded the place, including the boy; each one was bustling around as if their life depended on being busy. “Black, line up!” A bass voice shouted from behind a small building.

(These small buildings are actually tents, but Aurora doesn’t know that.)

“Come on, we’re black team,” the boy said, looking at me with his black irises. A group of minifigs assembled in an orderly line, and the boy led me to them. Every step sent little waves of pain up and down my back. My side still hurt.

The boy got last into line, dragging me by my collar next to him. A man, the same one with the deep voice, came out and stood in front of the minifigs. “Mallof, Skill, Wesley, Ron, Jake,” he started, naming each one in line, “You are all already in the Nexus Force and will be sent to defend Avant Gardens. You are Black Team; you will treat each other as a team and work together. You all leave tomorrow, get to know each other. Ron, you will be this dog’s handler,” he said pointing to me. No, no not him. I recognized him from the Sentinel camp…

“Aw, not the mutt!” Ron complained, eyeing me with loathing.

“What I say goes.” The man said simply and left.

I observed my ‘team’. It was a measly group of just five. The boy was the first I noticed, his name was Jake. Well, so was said. He was wearing simple blue and white clothes. Ron was wearing a peculiar tri tipped brown hat with green clothes. He carried a small gun, but kept it hidden on his belt. Wesley wasn’t as interesting. He and Mallof wore almost the same exact thing. A black helmet with black and red shoulder pads with thick black gear. Mallof was the same person I had seen earlier, and I found it disturbing that I hadn’t seen, or heard him say a word. Skill reminded me of Lilly, at least a little bit. Skill bore blue and light gray armor, he had taken his helmet off, but it looked futuristic.

“Does anyone feel the lacking of Assembly? I mean we have a Sentinel, a Venture Leaguer and two Paradoxes!” Jake said, the only one here who doesn’t have a faction. “One day I’ll be Assembly.”

“Yeah…wait how are you even in the Nexus Force anyways? I don’t even think you’re ten.” Wesley said through his helmet.

“You’re right, I’m not ten.” Jake smirked wickedly and sat down.

Wesley was going to say something, but held his tongue. “Never mind. Also what are we going to do about the dog? We can’t can her ‘dog’ the whole time.”

“Eh…just call her Mutt.” Ron mumbled. I started snarl at him; no way was I going to stand for him. Wesley’s eyes grew wide and he yelped noisily. Next thing we knew he was hiding behind a small building.

“Wesley? What might ya be doing back there?” Skill said, trying to find Wesley.

“Look…look at those teeth!” Wesley stuttered.

“This little thing?” Skill patted me on the head, trying to get me to stop snarling.

“But they’re so sharp!” Wesley whimpered; his voice was becoming rather pathetic. Why those were just my teeth, they are supposed to be sharp. If they were dull what use would they be? I couldn’t understand.

Skill fell to his knees laughing hysterically. “Even the Maelstrom isn’t that scared. And they have a reason to be scared.”

Wesley’s head poked out. “Well…” He slowly approached back to the other minifigs. “What will we call her?” He said calming his voice once I stopped growling.

A heavy silence settled over the group for a long time. This proceeded until the point where I thought Jake was asleep, and then Wesley spoke up, “What about Ulani?” Five heads turned all to me at once as if on cue. “I’m sure she’s had a name before, but since we don’t know it, Ulani will be her name.”

Ulani? What an interesting name.

Chapter 2.3 – Morning holds new things

Morning came faster than I thought it would. The night had been spent with Black Team fooling with each other; it ended with Skill being pushed into an icy bucket by Ron. I hadn’t slept a wink; I was afraid if I closed my eyes I would miss Master coming to find me. Jake had fallen asleep on me as if I were a pillow, which I didn’t mind, much. It was something Master ended up doing all the time on cold nights when chilly air seeped through the walls of our house.

The sun popped out over the horizon just before everyone was jolted awake by the sound a bullhorn. “Get up, get moving, use your map, and be outa my sight in an hour. You know where to go.”

“Ugh, mornings,” Jake complained, rolling off of me and hitting his head on the floor.

“Mornings,” Mallof agreed and went straight to gathering a few things.

I got up and stretched like a cat. I was dismayed to have no food, as I could hear my stomach complain. I tilted my head and sniffed the air as a soft breeze rolled by. Nothing, not a hint of food. Or Master. No Master there either.

Ron was still asleep lying sprawled on the ground; not even the bullhorn woke him. “How hardworking,” Skill said nudging him with his foot. Ron moaned and turned over onto his side. “Let’s get the engines rolling!” Skill shouted, leaping onto Ron and continuing to stand on him.

“Go away,” Ron said. He then tried to swat Skill away as if he were a fly.

“Wake up!” Skill jumped up and down.

Ron stood up and knocked Skill over in the process. “Alright, alright, I’m up.”

“Good.” Skill said, leaving to grab some stuff.

“Ron?” The man with the bullhorn called.

“Yes?” Ron replied, not quite sure where he was.

“You’re the scout of this team, you have a dog, and you’re Venture League. But seeing how a scout needs to move fast, we are giving you a horse to work with.” The mini said walking up to Ron with a horse’s reins in his hands. The horse was an old steed, grizzled and weary from what looked like a long life. The horse was black with a bit of gray on her head, but it faded into a soft blackish-brown on her back. Her back was curved unnaturally, but her eyes still had a wild look to them.

“I get a horse?” Ron turned and snickered to the rest of the team. “Sure.”

“Can’t we all get a horse, I mean…” Wesley started but was interrupted.

“No, we don’t have enough horses for that, only the scout gets one.” The mini said, handed the horse over, turned and left. “Oh and her name’s Nightmare.” He called then was gone.

“Nightmare? What am I like the animal master?” Ron said. Of course not, I wanted to say. You’re not Master and you never will be. I looked up at Nightmare, and she looked at me. Wide eyes full of fear were all I saw. Nightmare nickered thoughtfully and took a few steps away from me.

“Alright, we had better be going soon. We had an hour to leave. We should head west.” Wesley said, grabbing his pack. “Come on Ulani! Let’s go!”

With nowhere else to go, I trotted after him.

…………………………………………………………………………………………………………………………

Kale’s face paled; he was looking out a window just to the side of him on the floor. Space was like a black vortex. He had reframed himself from looking out for hours, but when he couldn’t help himself he wished he didn’t look out. Flight absolutely terrified him. He sat on the floor, being jostled in every which way, unable to find a more stable position to hold himself at.

“In a few minutes we will land in Nimbus Station, brace yourselves,” The captain’s loud voice bellowed over the speaker.

Kale’s face blanched even further. He could see a green chunk of land just out the window. It was getting closer and closer by the second. “We’re going to crash; we’re going to crash…” Kale muttered over and over.

Suddenly the loud boom of thrusters startled everybody. A couple of times they had heard this during the trip, but each time it had shut off instants later. This time it persisted.

“Diffi-“The speaker started, but never finished.

Something banged into the exterior of the ship and the whole thing was sent spiraling in every direction. “Ah!” Kale screamed, his nightmares coming true. He was thrown into the flank of the ship, slamming into it hard with his shoulder. “Ouch!” He screeched.

Several other recruits were sent flying around the room, while others clutched their seat for their lives. Suddenly the ship ceased churning and flipped over to a stop. Minifigs lay sprawling on the floor; one was even on the ceiling, clasping one of the light fixtures.

“Hand tight.” There was a pause in the speaker for a second, then, “Grab your things, we’re launching you outta here."
