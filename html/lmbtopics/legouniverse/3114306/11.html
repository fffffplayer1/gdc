Chapter 2.6 – Nimbus

Space whizzed by Kale as the rocket blasted him towards Nimbus Station. He kept his eyes squeezed shut; he didn’t want to see what he was flying over.

“Landing on point 10-1, Nimbus Side Field, will land in one minute.” The voice said from inside the rocket.

One minute flew by faster then Kale thought it might, the rocket righted, and gently lowered itself to the ground. Kale opened his eyes. Nimbus was bustling with recruits; they were an arrangement of all different ages, stuffed together in a small plaza. There were four stands on the side, each one of a different color.

“Recruit! We’re having a raffle, give me your name.” A man called out to Kale.

“Uh, a raffle for what?”

“We have a limited amount of Faction gear to go around, we are using a raffle to decide who gets what faction and who gets regular gear.”

“Oh, my name’s Kale.” Kale said, studying the uproar of minifigs behind the man.

Without a word the man left and took a stand in front of the group of minifigures. He had a small computer in his hands which was displaying names. “If your name is listed, you will receive Faction gear. If not, everyone else will get a set of Nexus Force equipment. No complaining what you get, here we go. James, Susan, Katie, John...” The names of people getting faction gear ran on and on, and Kale listened to every word he said. “Robin, Kale, Jimmy…” Kale? Yes, he was sure he heard it, he was getting Faction gear!

After all the names were listed, Kale came up to man and asked him about the gear. “What faction will I get? Or can I choose?” Truthfully he wanted Venture League so he could look for Aurora.

“You are assigned to be a Sentinel knight.” The man said.

Kale turned and left. Sentinel, was a good faction, he thought, but he would be stuck in the heat of battle the whole time. Not hunting for Aurora like he wished.

Chapter 2.7

I could hear the calling of my name in shouts coming from the rest of the Black Team, but I ignored them. They would follow me, and I knew it. I could smell the Maelstrom quite strongly now. I was sure of it. I stopped, as I was sure I could hear the sound here. I gazed around me; there wasn’t anything there, but the sound still reverberated in my ears. I cocked my head from side to side, checking to see where the sound was coming from. A lot of minifigs interpret that as a look of confusion, but it’s really me listening for noises. Below me. Right under my feet was the sound of scratching and digging.

I barked loudly, it was one of those barks that meant business. Nightmare pawed at the ground angrily, which, in the end, gave me an idea. I put my feet to the ground and started to uproot the earth. Above me, it was started to rain. Each droplet pounded onto me, being unnaturally big. I shook my ears to clear them, but continued to dig to the Maelstrom I knew was right below me.

I raked my paw against something just underneath the surface. It yowled and the ground around it loosened. What is it? I poked my head closer, as I could hear the rest of Black Team arriving. Suddenly a purple eyelid opened wide. Its orange eye stared at me, intensive, and not blinking.

“Ulani has got something!” Wesley shouted. No, I didn’t have just ‘something.’ I had what completely terrified me and what separated me from Master. Those spider-mutant-things are here. They’re everywhere. Oh, please don’t let them be everywhere.

I backed away from the hole, which, for a reason I was mystified of, the Black Team huddled around it looking in. “Ah, the Maelstrom is here. They’re underground. How many of them do you think there is?” Wesley asked, fixing his helmet on the top of his head. Mallof raised his chainsaw weapon and pointed it at the small fraction of the Maelstrom beast that was visible. He poked at it.

“Eh, is it smashed?”

“Not sure.” Several of them debated what to do, should they dig up some of the earth to see what’s down there? I would say leave it alone, don’t go picking a fight, but, sadly, minifigs have such a bizarre language that I can’t speak to them.
Jake was finally the one who decided on what the whole team should do. He swept the black hair out of his face that had grown unnaturally long in a short amount of time. “We dig around it, pull it up, and bring the Maelstrom back to the Paradox scientists to study it.” Mallof, who himself was Paradox, silently agreed.

At first they all tried to coax me into digging, but in the end they all ended up slashing at the ground with their various weapons. The eye, which was just below the surface, blinked. No one else noticed, but I was sure the spider was going to pop out. It wasn’t the cold rain that sent chills up my spine; I knew it was the Maelstrom.

Suddenly, as if there was an earthquake, the ground shook beneath my paws. Oh, now they made it angry. I could feel the fur rise on the back of my neck; I knew it was going to attack us. Ron looked around, unsure of exactly where it was coming from. The ground bulged underneath his shoes, rocks skittered beneath him. Behind him a blocky head popped out of the ground, like a daisy. Next a leg that was pulled out of the ground; six eyes; then a spider. He stood to his full length before any of the minis could tell he was there. He towered over Ron and let out an ear-shattering cry.
