The Journey Of Vanda Darkflame- Chapter 63
For a split second, Vanda saw the smooth orb hit the ground. The liquid shattered like glass, and sprayed in all directions. Before it could reach her, she felt a tugging feeling in her legs, and everything around her disappeared. She was now in complete darkness, with nothing to see but her two purple hands.
Then, everything came back to her. Why did I join the Maelstrom? she thought to herself. Almost instantly, she was thrown hardly onto a solid rock. Her back ached, and her neck gave a crack every time she turned it.

"Now I know that I am not doing that again", she muttered grouchily.

Caldera Mar looked awfully different. The fire Butterscorch had caused that one fateful night had grown larger ever since, some turning into molten lava. There wasn't only one rock now; there were hundreds floating above what appeared to be a pit. Several Maelstrom enemies swarmed below her. She counted them to look for Bob, but no sign. Then, she saw it. A Maelstrom ape. Those gave her bad memories of her past, but they flushed through her like a scent of a pine leave.

Vanda's mother had just baked her and her brother a batch of freshly baked cookies. They hugged her and smiled. But she didn't smile back. Something had gone from her eyes, something important; her happiness. She looked sad as if something terrible was about to happen. "Children", she said, about to cry, "You are young, but you must understand. My debt paid to Baron Typhonus has ended. Vanda, you are assistant to him, but he is unhappy with our family. Do you understand?" Twelve-year-old Vanda clutched her six-year-old brother in fear. Suddenly, their front door burst open, and a group of Stromlings came and took their mother, led by an ape. The last thing she said to them was, "Vanda, take care of your brother. Be safe until your father comes home!" And then she vanished into the Forbidden Valley's mist. They sobbed for what seemed like forever. Their father never came back. At sixteen, when her brother was ten, he ran away. She searched everywhere, but it was no use. Then, she had gotten the letter asking her if she would make a new faction for the Nexus Force.

A tear strode down her cheek as she sprinted as fast as she could to the ape, but when she was about to slash it, its eyes were not angry, but they were fearful. Vanda had forgotten that she was still Maelstrom. She sheathed her swords as a blast of sadness ran through her entire body.
