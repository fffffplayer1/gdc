The Battle for LEGO Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 71
Short Greetings

Thick Maelstrom fog blanketed all of Nimbus Plaza. But far away, there was the faintest trace of light… a bright, blue glow in the depths of the shadowy violet mist. The orb came closer and closer, until one could notice it was connected to a stick, which, in turn, was connected to an arm. An arm that was connected to a body in patched, orange robes and greyish, burnt beard long enough to pass the man’s belt.
It was Mardolf the Orange. And he wasn’t happy.
“Maelstrom… Maelstrom, Maelstrom, Maelstromaelstromaelstrom! MAELSTROM! Maelstrom… Maelstromaelstrom… Maelstrom…”
The wizard trotted along, occasionally tripping on his robes, and muttered to himself, casting Imagination at any fog that got too close. He didn’t even look nervously behind or beside him…. He just stared forward and mumbled the word “Maelstrom” over and over again.
He soon arrived at the entrance to Brick Annex. “Hmm… the Maelstrom, Maelstrom, Maelstromaelstrom’s lost its touch, eh? He-he-he…”
The wizard had reason to be hesitant. Nothing had attacked him, and from what he had heard from some other Minifigures… over a thousand Stromling were obliterating the place. This was clearly an exaggeration, but at least a hundred should have been in the place…
But he continued down anyways. After all, nothing could really harm a wizard, and… the shields! Of course! The Nimbus Plaza Shields had been malfunctioning lately… that must have been why the Maelstrom attacked at this moment! The shields must have still been up at the Annex!
With a little more optimism, Mardolf scuttled forward, his hand on his hat to keep it from drifting away into the swirling, evil shadows. And to his delight, the Maelstrom in the area receded rapidly, until there was nothing left. The Annex was untouched!
And a little ways away were the exact people he had been hoping to see: Bruno Underbite, Autumn Helix and Burke Barrelchest, all sitting on the stairs to the Assembly Factory whispering amongst themselves. Then Autumn looked up.
“Mardolf! Oh Mardolf, we thought you were dead! Boy, am I glad to see you!” The Summoner leaped to her feet and hugged the approaching wizard.
Mardolf laughed, “M-Maelstro- oh, I’m glad to see you as well!” He shoved the delighted Minifigures off him, “Make sure the others don’t hug me.”
Bruno and Burke sat back down onto the stairs glumly. Mardolf smiled to himself, congratulating his quick thinking.
Sadly, Burke and Bruno decided to tackle him anyways.
“It’s great to see you, old man!”
“Yeah! I really did miss you… other than the constant old people smell…”
After Mardolf finally managed to crawl back to his feet, he turned to face Autumn. “Mae- err… the shields are functioning well, correct?”
The Summoner bowed, then nodded, “Yes. I charged up some extra Blue Imaginite just for some extra protection.”
“Good…”
“Why?”
“You saw Whack Bliddo, correct?”
“Uhh… yes…”
“Were you shocked?”
“Well, yeah… I mean, he’s supposed to be dead.”
“Correct. That’s why I asked about the shields.”
“Umm… okaaay?”
Mardolf sighed, “I’m afraid something worse than a brick-sucking Spider will come back from the dead.”
Autumn shuddered, “Like… who, exactly?”
Then, all of a sudden, a kabuto skidded across the cobbled ground at stopped at the wizard’s feet. Mardolf shook his head in disbelief. “Charge the shields more… a lot more…”
Autumn Helix nodded and rushed away.
Mardolf then turned to Bruno and Burke. “If you value your lives, I suggest you duck.”
“Wait, what?” They asked in unison.
“No time for hesitation!” The wizard yelled, then knocked both of them to the ground.
Just as three, sizzling violet shurikens whizzed mere centimeters from their heads.
Mardolf the Orange stood up and pointed his staff at the kabuto. “Seibatsu, you coward! Reveal yourself!”
Someone replied. Replied in a voice like the gagging of a drowning zebra being dragged to the abyss by a crocodile, a voice like the whizzing of a triggered guillotine…
“Gladly.” Seibatsu snickered, and the kabuto slowly hovered upwards. “It will be an honor to avenge the Riders…”

The Battle for LEGO Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 72
Revenge

Xeno Blueblade’s katana clashed with Murgle Blotch’s hammer, causing sparks to fly in every direction and sizzle out of existence in the swirling mud below. High above, lightning flashed and rain cascaded down from the all-seeing glare of the Maelstrom.
The Samurai then spun around, slashing at the Hammerling General in a sideways cut. Murgle quickly leaped out of the way and slide-tackled Xeno, knocking him to the oozing ground.
But Xeno Blueblade wasn’t going to be defeated that easily.
He flipped backward, spun on his head, and then leaped into the air, slamming his feet into Murgle’s face three times before roundhouse kicking him into the mud.
“This is for Cog!” He yelled, and stabbed Murgle in the chest. The katana cleaved right through the General’s armor and pierced his body.
Murgle screamed, swinging his hammer-hand wildly to no avail.
“This is for Velocity!” Xeno continued, stabbing the Stromling again. “And this if for the Nexus Force!!!”
The Samurai lifted his blade up and swung it downwards to end Murgle’s life once and for all… for good.
“ENOUGH!” Murgle Blotch roared, rolling out of the path of the cutting edge and slamming his head into Xeno’s chest.
The Samurai’s breath was cut off, and he fell to the ground, gagging for some air. His blood-shot eyes just managed to look up to see Murgle Blotch… floating above the ground, his eyes sparkling with a malevolent scarlet.
“Now you die. In honor or whatever, I really don’t care. All that will happen is you will die.” He pointed his hammer at the immobile figure of Xeno, and lightning coursed through the handle and crackled on the top. “Any last words?”
Then, all of a sudden, he was knocked to the ground by a massive force, and the bolt of lightning that was charging in his hammer flew into the sky, evaporating a few hundred raindrops.
Then someone spoke- spoke in a voice like the shattering of glass, like the last breaths of a dying man.
“Stop. We need him… alive…”

The Battle for LEGO Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 73
Crashing at Point Rain

“What just happened?” Dirk Manleigh stared at the direction where the scream had come from.
“I have absolutely no idea…” Ellgren Stackwell sounded nervous… which made sense in this kind of situation.
“Aye, I have a baaaad feelin’ ‘bout this…” Logan Moonshot yanked open the dashboard of his rocket and pulled out his Broadsider (Don’t ask me how it fitted in there).
Dirk turned to face Ellgren, “Warn Silas and Darby. Things are not going as planned.”
“Gladly!” The Engineer replied without hesitation, “I’ll tell them immediately!”
Ellgren pulled at his Solarblast’s joystick controls and spun around and flew away, vanishing in the dark, swirling clouds behind them.
“I hope nothin’ ‘appens to ‘em.” Logan said, more to himself than anyone else before the two Nexus Force warriors went full speed to the Avant Gardens Launchpad, which was somewhere in the gloom below.
Then, all of a sudden, a massive, Maelstrom boulder flew past Dirk’s head, leaving a hole in the cloud where it came from and slightly bending one of the golden leaf adornments that stuck out from his helmet. “Mata Nui!” He gasped, rubbing his eyes in disbelief.
“What did ye say?” Logan asked. He had clearly not noticed the boulder.
Dirk looked at him in fear. “A massive rock just attacked me!”
“No, I could have sworn that you said ‘Mata Nui’. Isn’t that from a movie? Or those really small toys that people call LEGO?”
“I don’t care what I said! I just got attacked by a rock!”
“No, no, you said ‘Mata Nui’. Wasn’t he that Great Spirit guy in some random fantasy that wen-?” He was cut off by a massive boulder that knocked his hat clear from his head.
Logan Moonshot turned to face Dirk very slowly. Then, all of a sudden, he leaped to his feet, “I JUST GOT ATTACKED BY A BOULDER!!!!”
Dirk Manleigh followed suit, “ME TOO! THIS IS CRAZY!!!”””
“I KNOW!!!”
“I’M SCARED!!”
“ME TO- wait, what! Ha! You’re scared! A scared Sentinel, just imagi- OH MY GOD, I GOT ATTACKED BY A ROCK!!!”
“AHHHHH!”
“AAAAHHHHHHHHH!”
“I’M PANICKING!!!”
“WE’RE PANICKING!!!”
“AHHHH!”
“AAAHHHHHHH!”
“WHY ARE WE SCREAMING!!!???”
“I FORGOT!!!!”
“WAS IT SOMETHING ABOUT…”
“A ROCK!”
“AHHHH! THE ROCK!!!”
“AAAHHHHHHHHHH!”
Luckily, their constant screams were off before the two of them could go insane. Two Maelstrom boulders flew out of the shadows and perfectly crushed both their nosecones, forcing them to make their very last yells and come crashing to the ground.

*********************************************************************************************

Dirk Manleigh’s eyes fluttered open, ashes floating off his eyelashes and slowly gliding to the burnt ground. The place was covered in mud that carried the remains of armor and rockets away into the gloom of Nimbus Plaza. Eerie shadows rose from the ground into the murk of the Maelstrom fog, forming monstrous shapes: gnarled hands, sharpened scimitars, dragon wings, and gorillas…
Strangely realistic gorillas.
A pair of blood red eyes revealed themselves from the shadows and glowered down at the cowering Sentinel Knight. A massive, Maelstorm infected fist appeared from the gloom and slammed into the ground right next to Dirk’s head, causing the ground to rumble and a small crack to appear on the planet’s surface. A soft, sucking noice was made as thick, muddy water was drained into the small crack in the ground, and soon, another fist impaled itself on the other side of Dirk.
The Sentinel gulped as the Maelstrom Silverback Ape revealed itself.
“K-K-Kinga? Kinga… Hurl? Oh gosh…”
The monster in front of him smiled, then raised his demonic hand menacingly.
