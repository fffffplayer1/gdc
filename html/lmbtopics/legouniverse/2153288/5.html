The First Builders

Chapter 8

Gallant 5

Sector 360

Omnibrick Trench

That night was a sad one, a night of tears that shined upon the two moons of Gallant 5. Figmatrix sat in the middle of the brick-filled remains of the Zorrillo Beast, the empty cloak of Brickore, the Builder. She looked to the limp, Ninja-like figure that lay upon an outcrop of jagged rocks. Right next to him was the Second in Command of Imagineering: Crystale. And for their leader… she had no idea where he was…
Figmatrix lifted the cloak up, taking it to the side of the trench and covering it in jagged, dagger-like rocks. She then closed her unseen eyes, and wept more.
A strong hand grabbed her shivering shoulder, causing her to bounce upwards in fright. With some unexplainable power, she blasted multicolored bricks from her palms, and the whizzing parts clicked together to make a giant sword atop an engraved staff. She grabbed the weapon and landed, spinning around and pointing the blade at… her master.
“Oh, sorry, my lord!” She whimpered, the spear smashing and the bricks floating back into her body.
But her leader didn’t notice; he was busy staring at the mess. “Good, the monster’s been smashed…”
“GOOD?!” Figmatrix shrieked incredulously. “WHAT DO YOU MEAN, GOOD!”
Her leader looked at her, surprised. No one had ever back talked at him. “Anything that attacks us deserves to be smashed, and you know it. It’s in the code, is it not? Any being that dares defy the power of Imagination must go.”
Figmatrix gawked. “Now you listen to me, Creatra, that “Monster”, was a protector of Imagination!”
Creatra, the leader, almost did a back flip. “You DARE say my name without even a Lord or Sir on front of it??!!”
“As a matter of fact, I do.” Figmatrix stalked away, a tear dripping down her cheek as she looked at the remains of the Zorrillo Beast. She flew to the unconscious figures of Crystale and the cloaked figure, “kneeling” down by them, checking their heartbeats. The pilot, acknowledging that everything was OK, stood up and turned, floating back to Creatra. She then grabbed an object from his belt and yanked it out.
“HEY!” Creatra, who had had enough, leaped up and, with ease, built some sort of hybrid between a lance, a punching glove, and a giraffe. He pointed the machine at Figmatrix. But the pilot took no notice, instead, she held the two Imagimeter Shards tightly in her plastic hands, gliding towards the bricks of the Beast. And there, just as she had expected, was the third Shard. The Zorrillo Beast obviously must have swallowed it, and it must have fallen out when it was smashed. She threw all three of the Shards back at Creatra, who hastily whirled around to catch the precious objects. “There, you have yet another Shard, we just need one more, and I suggest you try not to kill me… I’m the only one who can pilot the “Shard Seeker.”
“She is right, put down your weapon.” A strange, dreamy voice whispered. The sound seemed to ring like a gong in the two Minifigure’s heads. They spun around, to see the Ninja-like figure sitting in lotus position atop a rock, a small fire nearby and a frying pan in its hand. “Dinner?”

The First Builders

Chapter 9

Keepers and Seekers

Gallant 5

Sector 360

Omnibrick Trench

Figmatrix lay floating in the cockpit of the Shard Seeker, the Book of Aruii in her plastic, cloak covered hands. Wisps of purple substance floated out from her robes like violet, shadowy smoke, and her unseen eyes gazed at the worn pages of the book. Next to her, propped up against her joy-stick like controls, were the other two books that belonged to the Conduuraii Chronicles, which consisted of the Book of Aruii, the Scriptures of Odmaarxq, and the Wirgkmrei Codex. The three almost mythic books were the life, culture, religion, and government of the distant world of Shaakri, a planet so far away that only the great Civilization of Imæ knew of it. But this was obvious, because the First Builders if Imæ came from Shaakri, and had lived there for centuries. The great civilization hardly ever left their planet, even with the wondrous gift of being the Children of the Nriisha (Or, in modern terms, the Nexus), and being made out of the magical element of Inriisha (Also known as Imagination).
Figmatrix sighed. She was only a beginner, everyone else had already read the Conduuraii Chronicles multiple times… probably the one who had read them the most in all of Shaakri was Brickore… Brickore the Builder. Forget about it! That is the past! But the pilot couldn’t forget about the old Druid, the Shrine Lord of Imæ.
“Figma! The madman wants you!” Creatra’s sudden voice made Figmatrix jump, causing her to drop and begin coughing on some saliva that went down the wrong pipe.
“Uh… okay…” She got up, picking up the three massive books and floating off in a ghostly fashion.
The strange, Ninja-like figure was not at all normal. Its hood covered everything of its face except one eye… and that eye was completely unrealistic. It was massive and round, and looked like the back of a spinning disc… whirling colors that glowed in a metallic fashion. But the eye also had a strange fell to it… as if you were looking through a swirling mass of… time. Snapshots of death, destruction, happiness and prosperity smacked you in the face just by looking at them. The things robes where red and black, and emitted the same purple aura as the hooded cloaks of the Builders.
“Sit.” It said in the kind of dreamy voice that sends shivers down your spine.
Obediently, Figmatrix built a stool, and, with a little difficulty, sat. This, she had never done before… all the Builders floated, being “higher” than any other being.
“I must say, I am truly excited!” It suddenly said, standing up on its… legs. How did that work?
Figmatrix was taken aback at the sudden statement, and rapidly built a hovering question mark above her head.
“Ah, you don’t understand! No wonder! Us Keepers were told that you Seekers wouldn’t know a thing about the prophecy!”
Figmatrix magically colored the question mark from white to black, turning it “bold” for more emphasis.
“Ah, you don’t understand! No wonder! Us Keepers were told that you Seekers wouldn’t know a thing about the prophecy!”
Figmatrix built three more question marks.
“Ah, you don’t understand! No wonder! Us Keepers were told that you Seekers wouldn’t know a thing about the prophecy!”
“WHAT IS THIS??!!” She roared, hugging the Chronicles of Conduraii closely to her chest.
“I think I should explain more clearly…” The figure said, clearly smiling from under its ninja-hood. “A long time ago, in a galaxy far, far… oh wait… that’s plagiarism… let me start again. Sooo… I long time back… a few centuries, I think, the Universe was created. It was magnificent… a sight to behold! Of course, life didn’t exist back then… never mind! Our astronomers and Tock Lords have found that all that time ago, the universe was created by billions of bricks that floated around, soon snapping together and creating planets. Soon enough, they created life. Some of the first life forms were the inhabitants of Khaakri (Or Gallant 5, as you call it) and of Shaakri, your home world.”
“Wait… how do you know where I come from?!” Figmatrix hollered incredulously.
“Never you mind! Now, as I was saying, some of the first living being were the Seekers, and the Keepers.
“Wha-“
“And so, that is the story… oh wait, I forgot the main part! These two species were the Children of the Inriisha Nriisha, or Imagination Nexus. But the Keepers were granted the duty of protecting the Inriisha Ecffra, or Imaginite Shards… and if they lost it, the Seekers -who were made to constantly look for the Nexus, which disappeared after the Creation- would find them, and return them to the Keepers, and soon after, find the Inriisha Nriisha!”
The only thing that Figmatrix could think of was: Run on sentence…

The First Builders

Chapter 10

Uncharted Lands

Gallant 5

Sector 134

Inriisha Khaakri

Creatra floated around grumpily, following the strange, ninja-person. He hated following people… it… it was against his “I’m better than you” vows. But, no matter how many insults he barraged the figure with, the abnormal Minifig just didn’t notice. It just kept walking forward, to a place that even Crystale, with his amazing vision and cartography skills, hadn’t discovered. Gallant 5 had been stripped of all its secrets with the Shard Seeker’s elite planetary scanners, and those gadgets had NEVER failed… until now. Gallant 5 was thought to be insignificant, the only life forms being the scarce, century year old plants that had been long thought to be dead. This was before their run-in with the unreal, glowing cave flowers, the transparent insects, and the Zorrillo Beast. And now they where stomping casually through mountains… un-volcanic mountains. This high rising structures of land where filled with plants, completely filled. Trees twice the size of red-woods stood almost everywhere with their ancient, powerful roots. Shrubs and bushes filled with thorns and blood-red berries lined the scarce, almost impossible to see paths. Thick vines hung from the massive trees, winding down to the flourishing ground and meandering through the forest floor. Multicolored birds squawked from their perches, taking off in a flurry of blinding feathers. Once or twice, the Builders even spotted some animals, scaly, hog like creatures with patches of dirty hair growing from their filthy, nature worn bricks.
“Welcome to Inriisha Khaakri!” The figure stopped abruptly, spinning around and opening its arms with a welcoming gesture.
“What are you? A savage? Nothing escapes our eyes with the scanners!” Creatra floated upwards, towering over the rest, over shadowing the group below.
But the figure just smiled with his fantasy-like eyes, and pointed upwards. Separating the mountains from the sky was a glowing, light-blue dome… an Imagination Shield. “If that wasn’t there, your people would have charged in and converted us to the Conduraii Order a LONG time ago, and the prophecy would never come true…” The figure trailed off.
“What’s wrong?” Figmatrix stepped to the figure, holding it tightly. Creatra sent her an obscured but clearly dirty look.
“It’s… it’s just… he’s gone…”
“Who?” Figmatrix whispered quietly, the pain of losing Brickore suddenly hitting her hard once again.
“Zorrillo… he was our protector… we knew… we knew that he would die in the pr-pr-prophe-phecy but…” The figure suddenly fell, sobbing intensely. Figmatrix kneeled next to the figure, comforting it but crying at the same time. Creatra just scowled in a corner, building a dagger and chipping away at one of the oversized trees.
“WHOOSH!”
One of the thick vines whipped at him, tying around his waist and heaving him upwards.
“Put me down you… you… you VINE!” He roared, straining both physically and mentally.
“Put him down Sasha.” The ninja-like figure had gotten up, wiping its rainbow colored tears from its hood.
“Awwwww!” Came a strange, girly voice form inside the tree. After a few seconds, Creatra was on the floor, headfirst in a puddle of fly covered mud. Then, suddenly, the giant tree fell into pieces, and in its place was a beautiful girl with a green tinted skin, vine-like, tangled hair, and large leaves as cloths. “Meany!” She spat, before rebuilding the tree around her.
“Imagination sure has its ways… that would be Sasha… a tree Nymph.” The strange figure spun around and kept walking as if nothing had happened; leaving Figmatrix you heave Creatra from the puddle. “Coming?”

The Battle for Lego Universe

Book One: The Beginning of the End

Chapter 18

When Spiders get Arachnophobia

Wisp Lee awoke rather slowly, taking his time to yawn, stretch, and rub his one blurred eye. He sat up, scratching his back and blinking, smacking his lips from under his red bandana.
“Sshhhrrrrrraaashhhhh! Shhiiiaaaathssss! Rrraaatthhhhhhhhh!!!”
The Paradox Researcher leaped up onto his feet, waving his infected hands wildly, screaming and yelling and threatening everything that was in a ten meter radius… but no one (Errr… noTHING) was there. Strange…
But then he heard a far off voice, muffled over the constant chatter of the Dark Spiderlings. But he could make it out just fine: “Run, insolent Minifigures, or face the wrath of the Spider Boss!”
“Oh no, oh no, oh no! This can’t be right… that things a myth! It’s… the first Stromling! It doesn’t exist! Ohhhhh NO!” The Shinobi, vanished in a puff of purple smoke, whizzing for action…

The Sentinel flipped and skidded over the rocks that separated her from the monster, spinning out of the way of the thousands of Dark Spiderlings whizzing, purple orbs. The strange purple, ever-morphing smoke seemed to float towards her, grabbing at every part in reach. Suddenly, one of her daggers where yanked from her hands, grazing her plastic palms painfully.
“OUCH!” She squealed, spinning around just to see her dagger, now infected, floating in midair, held up by the smoke. “No, no, no, no, no!” She just managed to dodge the dagger as it flew past her, cutting of her yellow and red ponytail. Shocked, she grabbed onto a Spiderling, pivoted in midair, and whizzed out the mouth of the cave.
Outside, Kit was already getting to work building the nearest Outpost Beacon… but he was too late to save Melodie. A spinning ray of pure Maelstrom energy came shooting from inside the cave, probably what the Spider Boss had been charging up with the strange weapon. Melodie was about half the way to the Sentinel Encampment when the ray hit her, sending her sprawling to the ground. The ray disappeared as fast as it began, and one of the Sentinel Guards fainted once again, falling of Fitz’s wall and smashing it.
“OH YO-“ Fitz Vanderbuilt trailed off as he saw what had happened to the Sentinel Agent. “Oh my…”
Beck Strongheart skidded right under the truck barricade to see what was going on. “Oh no…”
Kit forgot about the beacon and put himself on fire, racing to Melodie’s side, practically flying right past a crowd off bamboozled Stromlings and Mechs. “Melodie!? Melodie are you okay!!??” He extinguished the flames and shook he shoulder, but she didn’t move. “At least she hasn’t been smashed…” But then something horrible happened: She began to emit a purple aura that grew darker and darker until she was completely infected. Her eyes were glowing a blood red, and her mouth was now no longer visible, her hair dyed black and light purple.
Melodie Foxtrot, Avant Gardens Agent of the Sentinels, was a Stromling…

The Battle for Lego Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 19
More Yuki than Yaki

Rocco Sirocco sat by his post, bored half to the smash with a bowl of old sushi in one hand and his Scythe of Quakes in the other. He yawned in his ninja hood, and then began coughing as his stale breath was sucked back into his maw, causing him to drop his bowl of ancient rice and practically mythic fish.
“Oh well… that’s yet another inedible delicacy gone… Johnny Umami won’t be pleased…” The Earth Spinjitzu, unable to move onto Lightning because of sheer clumsiness, had been left to gather ingredients from different Crux chunks for the stingy cook. Luckily for him, though, when the Spider Queens destination was uncovered, he was sent to Avant Gardens to help out Paradox in finding its weakness, possibly adding a little soil in their bubbling vats and cauldrons. However, after ruining one of their experiments by putting a pebble instead of a Mech eye into a vial, he had been kicked in the behind and sent right back to his small Shrine in the Sentinel Encampment. And sadly, on the way, while flying through the air, he dropped all the Flame-Roasted Popsicles that were supposed to be delivered to Paradox Agent Hu Where… or in Rocco’s mind, himself. The partially melted soda ice’s blew up on contact with the grassy ground, sending shocked Stromlings whizzing in all possible directions… and hitting Klaus Zett’s vending booth, smashing it along with Fitz’s newly made wall. All in all, Rocco’s life wasn’t going that well. And just when things got even worse (Choking on his own breath, for example), thing got… well… hornswoggling…
Kit stepped back in horror, raising his spear in defense. But “Melodie” just laughed maniacally, swinging her daggers in a rather sloppy, un-Sentinelish manner. The Venture scout, seeing her terrible skills, brightened up considerately, oscillating and jabbing with his spear as if all his dreams had just come true. The hits damaged Melodie quite a lot, sending her sprawling to the ground every few seconds. But she just kept muahahaha’ing like a deranged jester with dynamite instead of juggling balls. Kit sidestepped one of her childish, slashing pivots. Kit almost barfed at the madness… it was just unreal! And it was about to become a LOT more like something from a fairytale…
Melodie’s eyes suddenly grew purple, and her monstrous tint turned a lot lighter. Soon, she was the same color as a Chainsaw Stromling. And she had begun running and slashing and flipping and screeching in a manner at least twice as powerful as her normal self. She had been infected… permanently…
“BANG!”
A Imagination tipped bullet hit her straight in the chest, sending her blasting back into the grass. She left a purple, sizzling path in the green and she spun away. But sadly, she was up as fast as she was down, but this time she was weary. She spun her head, her now exceptionally short ponytail flapping against her infected cheeks. And there it was, standing under the truck barricade, was a Sentinel Guard, his rifle trained on her. Next to him was Fitz Vanderbuild, shaking the unconscious Guard, trying to wake him.
Melodie charged at Kit, knocking the spear from his hand and kicking him in the face, sending him flying backwards, causing him to crash into one of Epsilon’s remaining, scorched barricades. The infected Sentinel Agent raised her spear and shouted.
“WE WILL HAVE VICTORY, THE UNIVERSE WILL BE OURS!!!!” All the Stromling behind her raised their bladed arms in triumph.
Kit sat up, rubbing his head. The visor on his helmet had been completely shattered, and his yellow, scarred face was now fully visible, apart from his black, matted hair. He raised his singe Daredevil Flaregun and put himself of fire, racing for the Encampment with incredible speed.
Rocco ran to the truck barricade just in time to see the army of Maelstrom before the conscious guard lowered the vehicle. He knew that he had to do something… he had to save Avant Gardens...
He transformed into a spinning tornado of pure Earth, rocketing out of the hole in the wall just as Fitz had rebuilt it.
“OI! YOU-“ He roared before a wad of mud flew into his face, knocking him back. He spun around for a while, knocking puzzled Stromlings on every direction, before slamming down with the crushing force of a falling cave roof. A “shocked” Mech was blasted backwards, smashing into Melodie. Sadly, with a flick of her daggers, she had cut a hole in the robot, letting it pass right through her, but not getting herself hurt.
“YOU FOOL, GET BACK IN HERE!” Beck Strongheart roared, climbing up to the top of the wall of the Camp. “GET OUTTA THERE!”
But dizzy Rocco didn’t hear him, instead, his wobbling legs gave way and he fell to the ground. Melodie laughed, stalking forward, her daggers held in a menacing way.
“CRASH!”
The infected Sentinel fell to the ground under the weight of a meteor.
“HA!” Rocco smirked, standing up and waving his Scythe triumphantly.
But with unnatural strength, Melodie lifted the asteroid up and tossed it away, squishing a nearby Stromling. Then, in a flash, she began hitting his Scythe with her daggers constantly, until there was nothing but a pile of bricks in Rocco’s hands.
“You.. you… you smashed it…” He whispered incrediously, shaking his head in disbelief. “You… you… YOU SUSHI SNIFFING, MISU LICKING, CHILLED MONKEY BRAIN!!!” He roared, grabbing a bowl of Yuki Yaki from his pocket (It was made by him, and instead of making Johnny sicker, it almost killed him) and smashing it into the Sentinels face. She fell to the ground instantly, spasms zapping through her legs.
“HA! BEAT THAT!” He yelled, turning and waving around madly at all the confused Stromlings. That is, until the Spider Cave blew up…
