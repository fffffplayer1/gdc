The Battle for LEGO Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 49
Shady Business

Meyer Muckrake winced as he was smashed. I was a terrible feeling, like getting fifty one injections at the same time… getting stung my hundreds of bees simultaneously. Then one would lose all their breath, unable to breathe more into their lungs. Then your head began to spin, you feel as if you stab all your funny bones with swords, and… blackness.
The feeling of being rebuilt, however, is wonderful. Imagination surges through your body and you feel as if you are rocketing into the sky, flying, flipping through the air. You get a tingly feeling all over, and begin to hiccup. But you hiccup in a beautiful, soothing way that makes you feel like you tucked into bed, but wide awake…
These are the two feelings that every Paradox member must get used to. Paradox teleporters are wonderfully made, able to completely and utterly kill you, and then bring you back to life in a matter of seconds. One of the most amazing ways to teleport.
And then Meyer was in a small cabinet. It looked like it was filled with brooms and dirty rags… and it was very confined. He had no idea where Mr. Ree was.
“Uhhh… hello?” He kicked the wall, but nothing happened other than his sudden urge to hold tightly to his hurting foot and hop around. While doing this, he bumped his head on the low ceiling, yelped, and slipped the small space of the floor. He was followed by a row of mops, which fell to him with a crash.
Then, all of a sudden, the wall gave way, and he came tumbling out like a bowling ball half covered in dusty rags. “What the!!??” he coughed, holding his lurching stomach gravely. He gave a pained expression as a washing machine (He had no idea where it had come from or how it could have even fit in the small closet) fell into his paining foot. He finished off the grimace with a scream.
But Mr. Ree had already run to Vanda Darkflame, and held her in his arms. She looked terrible, with a grey face that looked as if she couldn’t breathe, and a constant spasmodic twitching in her left foot. She was also mumbling to herself, in a voice that was definitely not her own. Occasionally, her eyelids would twitch, revealing glowing red eyes.
Mr. Ree pulled a sparkling blue vial from one of his jacket pockets (He had over twenty). It was undoubtedly Imagination.
“Stay by the door, yes? Just in case some customers arrive, yes?”
Meyer Muckrake looked puzzled. “Wh-what? There are no doors in this place! You ought to know that! The Paradox Laboratory isn’t completely connected to the Tower, that’s why we use tele-.”
Mr. Ree pointed to what was usually a blank space of wall. There was an open door, with a bunch of brooms and a washing machine sticking out from its tiny space. The very same closet he had fallen out of.
“Quicklyest! Close the door before anyone comes in, yes?!” The Shady Vendor growled, his sun-glasses reflecting the light of the Shadow Orb. He then put the vial to Vanda’s shaky, pale lips.
Meyer, utterly bamboozled, did what he was told, just as a scrawny-looking Minifigure in a lot of racing gear appeared from a teleporter. He shuddered violently when he arrived… he was definitely not from Paradox. Probably Assembly.
The Minifigure glanced at Vanda and gasped. “Is she okay???”
The Paradox Guard scratched his helmeted head. “Uhhh… yeah! Just… errr… passed out! The lot of us do… all these… uhhh… polluted fumes from Nexus City… hehe…”
The Racer didn’t looked convinced, but dismissed the matter anyway. “Mr. Ree? What do have for sale at this hour?”
Mr. Ree, who has concentrating on pouring all the contents of the vial into Vanda’s mouth, gave the Minifigure a angry glance. “I tell you all the time, it’s so randomer even I don’t know, yes?! And anyway, Meyer here is acting for me while I do this…yes?”
Mayer Muckrake looked baffled. “Wha-?”
Mr. Ree gave him a menacing glance.
The Paradox Guard then, turned to the door. But there was just bare wall.
“Door’s run off again.”
Mr. Ree once again gave him a murderous glance. And when the Guard turned back… there was the door!
“Right! Alright… uhhh… racer person! I got the door…” Then it hit Meyer. “Hey… uhh… Ree? This is the room with all the mops and stuff…”
The Minifigure in the racing gear laughed as Mr. Ree, outraged, slammed the half empty vial to the ground, stomped to the door, and flung it open. There, right in front of them all, was a rack holding a Black Cape, a White Cape, and a Fur De Lis Shield.
“What the…” Meyer gasped.
“That’s it? Are you out of stock of cool stuff? Like Launchers or Double Blasters? Perhaps a Shard Armor?” The douncast Minifigure walked towards the teleporter. “It took hours for me to find you…”
He was then hit on the head by a Double Blaster and fell onto the teleporter. “YOU OWE ME!!! YES???” Ree roared at the now empty Paradox Teleporter. “Kids these days… yes?”
“Yes, no, yes, no, what am I supposed to say?” Meyer spat back.

The Battle for LEGO Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 50
The Departure of the Faction Leaders

~Nexus Tower~

~Paradox Laboratory~

There was a flash in the Paradox Laboratory as Billy Ganges’ bricks tumbled from a teleporter, soon rebuilding into his normal, adventurous self. He had been held up by a desperate Olivia Nightshade who couldn’t sell any Man-Eating Seeds (And had accidently dropped a bag, growing half a dozen before he could escape from her begs). He was covered in bright green ooze that dripped from his sticky fedora. Half of his face and body was also slobbered in the emerald goo from the maw of six Minifigure-Eating Plants.
After he had shaken off his hat and wiped his face clean, he scanned the room for Vanda Darkflame.
But she was nowhere to be seen…
*********************************************************************************************

~Nexus Tower~

~Venture League Map Room~

~Observatory~

Notorious Pirate Captain Hael Storm sat by the massive gold telescope atop his tower, shaking madly. It was a terrible sight: seeing the Spider Boss wreak havoc. And it had gotten worse the more he waited. The Sentinel Encampment was now completely obliterated, an infected Beck Strongheart ordering Stromling troops towards the monument. And there, a hooded figure was ordering Dark Spiderlings up, and was ordering a few Stromlings to slice down the fans that lined the three paths up the monument. The fans were being strapped to the backs of Stromling Mechs, who were being handed multiple nasty looking guns.
But the worst thing was the Launchpad: Everything had been obliterated save for three launchpads. Even the only Launchpad with permanent coordinates to the Venture Explorer had been destroyed. Almost every single buffalo pet had been smashed… except the ones that were kept in the conservatory under the ground.
That armory, that conservatory, the brave Minifigures within… they were all that stood in the way of the massive army of Maelstrom.
So far, the Nexus Force had only won one battle: The battle against the Spider Boss.
Hael Storm looked through the telescope once again. The side of the Monument that represented a newbie smashing a fully armored minion of the Maelstrom had fallen, its blade piercing the Spider Boss, followed by the entire Monument.
The Nexus Force needed the Four Faction Leaders to help them. But where were Duke, Albert, and Vanda? They had been called a long time ago…
Suddenly, the sound of a cannon blast made the entire Map Room shudder, and Duke Exeter, Leader of the Sentinel Faction, landed next to Hael head first.
“Youch!” He bellowed in the low, proud voice of a man who had never lost a battle before, but had a habit of falling on his head.
The pirate helped the knight up (Which was hard, since Duke’s armor weighed a ton).
“Duke, ye got the mess’ge, c’rrect?”
The knight stretched for a moment before answering. “Of course I did! Why else would I be in this terrible disorganized, easily smashed junk tower?” He kicked the Imagimeter, and regretted it.
“Least that’ll proves this place ain’t easily smashed.” Hael growled, then, in a louder voice, continued. “As I was saying, the Spider Boss has returned. It was thought ter be smashed, but has now led a full assault on Avant Gardens: Its target? Launchpad ter Nimbus Station. From there, it’ll be’able ter attack any world with ease.”
“Yes, I understood that much…” Duke sighed. He was a clumsy, but we has smart and war hardened, and knew what was at stake.”
“Luckily”, Hael continued, “The Spider Boss has bin smashed rath’rr unexpectedly, by two members o’ the Paradox Faction and itself.”
Duke spun around from glaring at the Imagimeter and stared at the pirate. “It’s been smashed??? By PARADOX?? Gee, those Minifigures are nutty, but they sure know how to turn the tides of a battle… and how could the Spider Boss smash itself?”
“Simply clumsy. Spiders r’mind me o’ you.”
“Veeeeery funny, you filthy buccaneer.” Duke said, just as there was another loud “BANG!” and Doctor Albert Overbuild was tossed into the air by the Cannon Teleporter, skillfully landing on top of the massive telescope on his mechanical arms before touching down with his trembling feet. He was still devastated with the news.
“See?” Hael smirked, “Ev’n mad scientists who ne’er work out land proper’y.”
“Veeeeery funny, you filthy buc-.” Duke Exeter was cut off by Dr. Overbuild’s, slick, worried voice.
“I can’t believe it! It’s like 934 Core Planetary Computers hanging from the right shoelace on the left foot! Impossible! In fact, it is far more that than even the simile I have just stated!” He began mumbling something about buttered bandicoot teeth, asparagus, and something about a 6 meter snail before he looked into the telescope (Without being invited, of course).
In moments, he stepped back in terror, removed his goggles and revealing milky blue eyes, whipped them with a cross between a handkerchief and a feather, and replaced his lenses. “It’s worse than I expected. But I don’t see the Spider Boss anywhere, so what could be so bad?”
“It’s been smashed.” Came a voice that definitely belonged to a woman.
Everyone spun around to face Vanda Darkflame, who was sitting on the Imagimeter.
“When did you… where did you… how did you???” Duke stuttered, as Hael rolled his eyes and Albert yawned.
“I came just after you did, am a bit too wounded to arrive by cannon, and teleported right here. A Couple of seconds later, you kicked the Imagimeter, luckily where my foot wasn’t. I thought you were deliberately trying to hurt me until now.”
Duke just stared at her as Hael patted him on the back. “Ye’d be’er get s’m glasses, Duke me boy!”
Dr. Overbuild giggled, but Vanda interrupted them all.
“The Baron is planning something far more complex and disastrous. I saw him release the Spider Boss in the Shadow Orb… it was a terrible sight. If it wasn’t for Meyer and Ree, I’d be dead right now.”
Everyone gasped.
“But that isn’t all.” Vanda continued. “You have obviously seen what is happening far more than I have. But I got glimpses of what was to come… and what had happened. I saw Epsilon Starcracker fall under the fiery fist of one we only know as Bob. I also saw a massive tree… its roots snapping. I saw a boiling ocean with tentacles blasting from the waves. I saw a massive, infected robot, and four Maelstrom Horsemen. I saw the very fabric of time tear before me… I saw the Nexus Force fall!”
Everyone gasped and blinked.

Wisp leaped towards it, his palms flickering with violet flame. But he was too slow, even with the reflexes of a Ninja.
The Mech leapt to its feet and spun around, kicking Wisp straight in the face and sending him sprawling with a black eye.
But then PRDX-4, who was too busy trying to find a place to hide, saw the Mech’s serial number: PRDX-7.
The Nexus Force Mech spun around and shot the Maelstrom Mech, who dodged the projectile with ease. PRDX-7 then back flipped and landed at the edge of the cliff. He un-slung the Jet Pack/Fan thing, and opened it so that he had the two sections covering the fan in each hand, and the actual fan’s blade stuck in the ground next to him.
Sofia Amalgam charged with Scarlet Scythe in one hand and a Handsome Mug in the other. “Bring it, you pile of scrap metal!”
But she was no match for the Mech General, who glanced at her curiously with a glowing red eye and tossed a half of the fan at her.
The concave side slammed into her, knocking the scythe and the drink far away and flinging her into the rocky surface of the nearest hill. The fan covering slammed into the soil with so much power that it stuck to the surface of the hill… with Sofia imprisoned inside!
PRDX-7 then yanked the actual propeller from the ground and brandished it like it was a weapon, holding the other side of the fan covering like a shield. Then, all of a sudden, he dropped the “shield”, yanked something from his back, slammed the whirring black object into the ground, and picked the shield up again. He did this so fast that no one attacked.
He then kicked the object… and a grappling hook shot from it. “NO!” Wisp roared, vanishing in a puff of smoke once again. But the Mech was far too cunning, and spun around, intercepting the magical teleportation field and knocking Wisp into the air before he could do anything.
Ten more Mechs arrived at the battle, all with fans on their backs. They were followed by five more, and only about 4 were prevented from planting the hooks.
More Mechs climbed up the metal ropes with ease, and soon a massive army was arriving atop the cliff.
The few Nexus Force Minifigures were smashing them like mad, but the defense was in vain.
Dark Spiderlings began to crawl up the steep slope, chattering in their skin-crawling language as their blasters whirred with energy.
In a way, this constant surging of enemies was an even more terrible sight than the Spider Boss.
Wisp Lee watched as his friends were smashed, one by one, by the constant army. Oliver Sudden and Theo Balfour had already sunk to the ground for the last time as their bricks were scattered by the waves and waves of troops.
They had lost. They had to be evacuated. They had to destroy the Launchpad to Nimbus Station…
Or that’s what Wisp thought, until her heard the continuous shots of a Wormholer and the eerie swings of a Doomslicer.
And then, slowly, he heard what sounded like a chainsaw. The slashing of a bladed lance. Even the firing of cannons. Reinforcements had arrived.

The Knight flipped into the back of yet another Spiderling, but it easily tossed him of, causing him to roll around on the dead grass below. “I can see that!” He snarled, in now almost the same voice as Wisp as he tossed the lance the arachnid, slicing off two of its legs.
But even with the Knight, the small band of the Nexus Force knew they couldn’t win. But that soon changed, for if you remember, I said that there were not only two Avant Guardians, but four…
The Maelstrom troops soon overran their small perimeter, driving them backwards towards the Launchpad to Nimbus Station. Three Stromlings leaped onto Brutus Speck, knocking the Spanner from his grasp and causing it to tumble off the nearby ledge.
The Rank Two Engineer spun around and began crawling away, but he was stopped by the nasty, ice-cold feeling of having a Maelstrom blade pressed against the back of your throat.
“Awww, sca-aared, is it, no-oow? Well, no-oo ne-eed to be sca-aared, we-ee’ll ta-aake ca-aar of it! Tu-uuck it in be-eed! Le-eet it re-est!” The Stromling’s terrible voice rang right next to his ear, and Brutus could smell the terrible reek of its infected breath.
He felt the blade leave his neck, and he knew that it would come hacking away at him, when-
BANG!
Three, large, flaming cannon-balls slammed into the Stromling, reducing it to a pile of bricks in seconds. The two other Stromlings leaped back in fear, but soon gathered their courage and rushed towards the defenseless Engineer…
Just to be sliced to bits by a Broadsider they never knew was coming their way.
The Valiant Buccaneer fired another shot at an incoming Mech, then sliced a few more Stromling cleanly in half.
“’Ello thar, mate! Shiver-me-timbers, cold out ‘ere, ain’t it?” And he charged into the fray with a now helmet-less Brutus scratching his head in amazement.
This Minifigure, was, of course, Logan Moonshot, the former Venture League Faction Representative and Booth Director. And he was closely followed by another familiar face: That of yet another Engineer, but this one far more skilled than Brutus.
What looked like an orange whirlwind smashed almost a dozen Stromling, and, as the Minifigure landed on the ground, he smashed some more with two saws: The Serratorizers.
In moments, Imagination blasted from the Engineer (Who was wearing orange and dark blue armor, with a black visor and what looked like sparkling blue bulbs on his shoulders) and bricks spun around him. In about three seconds, a hovering “Cleaner-Bot” was next to him… with blasters as hands instead of the usual mops and brooms.
This was obviously Ellgren Stackwell of Assembly, and now, with these two extra Minifigures, a tiny sliver of hope was making the small band of fighters fight harder and better, and smash far, far more.
That was, at least, until the Maelstrom Dragon landed at the edge of the cliff-face, spurting purple flame and flashing menacing, blood-red glances and the Minifigures who were fighting for their lives.
