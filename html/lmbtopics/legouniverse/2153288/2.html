The First Builders

Chapter 2

Gallant 5

Sector 363

Hingehurl Pass

The hooded leader picked up a chunk of brick with a few studs attached, and turned it in his plastic, two finger hand.
“Strange…” He trailed off, turning his head and looking at the treacherous path ahead, tossing the bricks to the ground.
“What is it?” The other figure asked, floating to its master’s side and staring quizzically at the hunk of bricks.
“This is a power capsule from our ship… but it’s useless to us.”
“Why is that?”
“Pick it up with Imagination… don’t touch it. And if you are gonna do it anyways, put on gloves.”
The other figure concentrated on the object, and suddenly, a swirling wisp if smoky blue twirled out of hits palm, curling around the bricks and lifting them into the stale air. The hooded Minifigure looked closely and scowled, tossing the power capsule to one side. “Why’s it all melted and smoky on one side? And what’s with the green goo?” It asked, dusting of its hands for no apparent reason.
“It was destroyed by a local who obviously has a habit of tasting almost everything; I think the goo would be its saliva.” The leader floated ahead, losing interest. “Keep your eyes out for any sign of blue!”
The other minifigures gulped, taking one last look at the severed console before floating away rather hurriedly. But after a few seconds, it bumped into its leader, who had frozen in place.
“Oh my…” It said, noticing why its master was shocked. Ahead, there was a bridge of floating rocks much like the (At this time period) nonexistent Caldera Mar, but with much wider spacing, making it almost impossible to jump… and this was excluding the almost constant spurts of lava blasting up from the bubbling, flaming depths.
“Oh my indeed…” The hooded leader shook out of its shock, “Well, we’ll just have to carefully “float” across!” It said optimistically, rearing back to get a “running” start. The figure blasted forward like a vent of wind, just to come “hopping” back, its behind on fire.
“Oh my.” The second in command thought, scratching its obscured head.

The First Builders

Chapter 3

Gallant 5

Sector 363

Hingehurl Pass

After about an hour of hopping, floating, and flying up with their plasticized behinds on fire, the two figures with infected hoods finally got… a quarter of the way through the fiery horror.
“Ugh! This is a complete waste of time!” The second in command growled, just managing to jump past yet another jet of flame.
Its master didn’t answer, instead, it sat down cross legged and concentrated.
“Uh… sir?”
The leader turned. “I agree that this is a waste of time, so I think we use Imagination to guide us.”
“Um… but just building almost smashes us… I don’t think this would be constructive…”
“That’s because it’s under construction.” The master suddenly raised its hands and slapped them together, causing every single rock to vibrate.
The second in command jerked backwards and fell off the platform…
“OW!” It shrieked as it splatted face first onto a silky, blue substance that now covered the entire surface under the rocks like a glass floor… very much like one, because the bubbling lava and spouts where still visible. “What in the WORLD!”
“An Imagination Shield, we should hurry up before it disappears.” The leader said matter-of-factly
Hurriedly, the Minifigure “resting” on the shield leaped up and began to float to the other side. “SIR YES SIR!!!!!”

About 2 minutes later, the two Minifigures were on the other side, panting.
“Well, that was fu…” The second in command trailed off, pointing excitedly to a glowing cylinder half covered by a slate like rock.
The leader floated over to the objects, picking up the cylinder.
“Well, we have found the first Imagimeter Shard! Two more to go!”
It then looked at the slate. It was covered in pictures and inscriptions of another language:
Eraweb’ eht Olliroz Tsaet
“What does that mean?”
“Shhhh!” The leader barked, looking at the pictures.
There were tribal looking warriors, battling a massive creature with spines running down its scaly back, four, great bat-like wings, gigantic claws that looked like they could carve a self portrait out of diamond, jagged teeth, and six, evilly squinting eyes. Around it, there lay bones… and the bricks of fallen tribal warriors.
“Hmmm… I wonder…”
“Uh… sir? I think I found something.” The second in command whimpered uneasily.
“What?”
“Read the inscriptions- Eraweb’ eht Olliroz Tsaet- backwards…”
The leader did so… and shook its head in disbelief.

“Beware, the Zorrillo Beast…”

Chapter 8
(1 day later, after the Spiderling eradication turned out a success)
Epsilon Starcracker was back at his post, scouting, smashing, and guiding newbies. Wisp gave him a plastic heart attack about thrice, pooping up when the Ranger least expected it, and vanishing before he could throw a punch.
“Would you please STOP THAT! Wisp, you know it’s a bad idea startling a fully armed Sentinel!” Epsilon roared, stomping his feet as if he was a greedy two year old.
“Unless you’re a Paradox agent!” Lee snorted, once again appearing and disappearing behind the Space Ranger in a puff of purple smoke.
“STOP! STOP, STOP, STOP, STOP!” Epsilon mowed down about twenty, terrified Stromling before powering his jetpacks and rocketing for the Shinobi, slamming into Wisp and sending him sprawling.
“Whoa! You don’t have to go berserk! What can you expect, I’m half infected?!”
“JUST STOP, OKAY? OR THAT SPIDERLING’S GONNA GET A MEAL!”
The four-legged arachnid looked up longingly from its cell, slamming against the bars and spraying Maelstrom goo in all directions.
Then, all of a sudden, a newbies Classic Rocket began its decent from the Nexus Koi, the new transport ship captained by Peppermint Lane, who had been recently saved from freezing for yet another year in the miniscule world called Frostivus. The small village of Frostburg was very dangerous at winter, because if you’re frozen for a year… without food… you’re smashed. And you can’t really be rebuilt by your Spark when you’re… well… frozen. OK, back to the story.
A bald figure with a plain, red shirt and a pair of plain, blue pants hopped out from the rocket and looked around, apparently terrified. No one could blame him. Even Epsilon felt like that when he had first landed on Avant Gardens.
The two Minifigures raced back to their posts to greet the new comer.
“Oh my”, Wisp thought to himself, “Now that’s a new one! A newbie trying to look like Bob! Well… he was a good lad… until he was smashed…” The Shinobi snapped out of it to look at the “Bob” like figure.
“You probably have had a great life before this, but here there is Maelstrom! Talk to Epsilon Starcracker over there, he will give you a weapon!” Wisp waved his infected hands around madly as he spoke.
But what happened next, he did not expect.
“You’re wrong”, the newbie growled. He looked almost exactly like Bob, but his face was not smiley… it was almost upside down with… hatred. “Yes, I once was happy, but now I’m not… and I have definitely had more Maelstrom than even Vanda Darkflame, your pathetic, wimp of a leader!”
Wisp almost leaped back in surprise. “WHO DO YOU THINK YOU AR.”
Epsilon gawked from his perch as the Minifigure socked the Researcher in the face, sending Wisp teacup over tea kettle, knocking him out.
Then the figure turned to Epsilon…

Chapter 9

The figure stalked towards Epsilon Starcracker, clenching his fists.
“Y-y-y-you!!!!” The figure growled. He looked like he was going to explode.
Epsilon back flipped for the console next to him, slamming on the ON button and roaring frantically into the speaker.
“Air Strike 17! Air Strike 17! Come in, come in, come in!!!!!!”
“Yeah?” A bored, sleepy voice yawned through the speaker. You could hear him scratching his head.
“COME TO SECTOR 2 IMMEDIATELY!!!”
“Awww, do I have to? Vanda’s Panda is on! It’s my favorite show!!!”
“JUST COME! THAT’S AN ORDER!!!!!!!!!!!”
“Boy, you’re talking like Sir Strongheart! Ok, ok, I’m coming!”
Three jets soared into view, blue smoke trailing from behind.
“WHAT! You want me to smash a newbie!!!” The pilot came in from the radio.
“Well, look at Wisp, that might answer your question!”
The jet tilted slightly. “Oh.”
The deranged Minifigure was getting closer by the second.
Then a piercing whistle could be heard screaming through the air. The bomb were dropping, and Air Strike 17 has hit their target.
But when the smoke cleared, the figure was still there, madder than ever.
“What the!” The pilot said, shocked.
Epsilon was completely freaked out.
Then, suddenly, the Minifigures eyes glowed a violent red, and Maelstrom began to engulf him until he was completely infected, one get skeletal and half his rib cage showing. It was just another Stromling… a bald Stromling without a sword as a hand.
The jets instantly took action, rapidly firing with their forward laser cannons. One lucky shot hit the Stromling in the chest, smashing it.
Epsilon wiped the sweat from his forehead and sighed, happy to be in one piece
“Woohoo! Way to blue ray that infection injection!”
The jets landed, and the captains hopped out, almost dancing with joy.
“That was some Stromling!” One said, pulling off his head and performing a Fire Dance.
“Yeah…” The Space Ranger looked at the unconscious figure of Wisp Lee.
But just as he was about to run towards his “friend”, a hand grabbed his foot tightly. Epsilon, shocked, looked down to see what had interrupted him. It was an infected arm from the crazy Stromling.
“What the!” The bounced back, kicking of the arm. But then, all of a sudden, the Stromling legs hopped up from the ground and walked over to the body. The arm Epsilon and flung off crawled to the body and picked it up, putting it in place on the pair of legs. It then attached itself and the half made Stromling and the legs guided the rest of the body to the other arm, which had hit the newly made PRDX-7 in the face, smashing it. When the two arms were ready, the body limped over to the infected head, picking it up and placing it on the neck socket. The eyes glowed red once again.
“Celebrating so soon?”

Chapter 11
High above the battle, on the remains of the Assembly highway, Vector Longview was sitting by the Monument Finish Line, gnawing on a bun. She was obviously bored… extra ordinarily bored. But then she heard a sound that made her drop the bread roll and hop to her feet, activating her super-sonic electro-binoculars: The explosion of a Sentinel Air Squad.
Vector loved the sound, it always gave in a show to behold: Shocked Mechs looking up at a faint whistle, Stromling screaming madly and scuttling away, and then there was the moment when the explosives hit the ground… it was like fireworks to the short-sighted Venture scout. But the sight was not at all a sight to behold, not a single stud of it…
Epsilon leaped into the air, powering up his rocket pack and readying his plastic hands on his plastic zipguns.
“You don’t have to do this Bob!”
“Really? I really don’t see another… realistic way!” His palms seemed to spark into balls of purple flames, growing and growing until it looked like he was holding a pair of infected bonfires.
One of the jets rocketed towards Bob, firing a stream shiny blue bullets as if it was part Wormholer. But the bullets just absorbed into his body and he sent a fire-ball whooshing towards the plane, slamming into one of its wings. The jet spun wildly in the air and crashed into the remains of the Paradox research facility, sending fiery bricks in every direction. Sadly, the cage was also destroyed, and the Dark Spiderling tentatively crawled out. In joy, it hopped around, and then scuttled to Wisp Lee, dragging the limp figure to the Spider Cave.
“NO!” Epsilon roared, firing rapidly at Bob in hatred. The “plain” Minifigure flung a rocketing ball of flame in the direction of the Sentinel.
The Space Ranger went into evasive action, spinning upwards like a torpedo, constantly firing with his zipguns at the same time.
Bob laughed evilly, raising his now flame deprived hand. “Fool! You Sentinels keep thinking with your weaponry, never learning! Well, how about I give you a lesson!” Bob’s palm suddenly glowed purple, and a purple bullet shot out from it, spinning through the air, hitting Epsilon’s pack and sending him flying in all directions.
When the scout finally slammed into the ground, Bob called out. “You see? That was one of YOUR bullets! Now, wanna see what ability the jet gave me?” After he had finished his Muahaha’ing, he pointed both his hands straight at the dazed and dizzy Sentinel. His palms glowed once again, and a stream of infected bullets suddenly blasted out, firing rapidly as if his arms had morphed into machine guns.
Just in time, Epsilon produced a plasma shield form his two zipguns. The shadowy studs just bounced off harmlessly.
Bob had finally been tested, and it looked as if he had “raised an eyebrow.”
“Sooooo, you wanna play hard, do you now?”
“I will fight to the smash!”
Bob laughed insanely, swinging his bald, single stud topped head back and forth. “Fine!”
Suddenly, it looked as if infected bricks had begun to fly out of his hands and mouth, all building something around him. Then, all of a sudden, he was smashed, and his bricks joined the other ones until… until he had been replaced by a Maelstrom Ape…
“I’m ready… how about you?” The Ape said in a rumbling voice that sounded like a cross between a tuba and a dinosaur burp.
“I suddenly have the sudden urge to suddenly surrender… but, you know me… I won’t.”

Chapter 12
And so, Epsilon Starcracker, the greatest Sentinel Space Ranger scout to ever live, blasted headfirst into the battle of his life… literally.
The head butt sent the Ape sliding back a couple of feet. But the gorilla just looked down at its chest quizzically, looked up, and tossed an infected boulder at the Ranger. Epsilon flew out of the way, and his already fire ball affected post got a new makeup treatment.
“You traitor!” The Sentinel roared calling down an orbital strike on the Ape, smashing him.
Epsilon landed, panting, and a tear trickled down his sweaty face. “Why?” He thought.
“You know, you are REALLY boring me. Let’s just FINISH this!” Bob was intact, the Ape bricks slowly dissolving around him.
Epsilon didn’t say a thing, he just stood up fully, back straight, and eyes filled with tears. With shaky hands, he lifted his two zipguns, and began to fire rapidly, slowly lifting up into the air simultaneously.
“Traitor…” He whispered, flinging a small device behind his old friend. It burst open with a spark, releasing a black hole.
“What th-“ Bob, shocked, was pulled into the dark, swirling oblivion. After a few seconds, the device spat him back out and disappeared. But it had done what it had been meant to do: It had subdued him, and sent stars spinning around his head.
Epsilon called down an orbital strike on the stunned figure, causing Bob to slam into the ground.
Bob stood up, rubbed his head, and “smiled with an unseen maw.”
“Fool.”
But Epsilon didn’t care for anything. He just had to make sure Bob didn’t wreak havoc on the Nexus Force. He sent a missile at the Stromling, but Bob just fell apart and rebuilt as a Dark Ronin. The shadowy, legless samurai flew towards the Sentinel, slashing with its glowing blade. But quick reflexes and years of training had taught him how to block a Ronin. Rapidly, he raised his arm and the infected katana slammed hard onto a razor blade of one of Epsilon’s zipgun bayonets.
The resounding clang brought Vector Longview to her senses. The Venture League scout jumped into running position… and fainted before she could say a word.
Epsilon kicked the Ronin in the chest… and ended up dropping his weapons and hopping around, clutching his plastic foot.
The Ronin’s eyes glowed yellow for a second, then it flew back at the Sentinel. Epsilon, unarmed, dodged the blazing katana, yanked the helmet off the infected samurai, and slammed it at the Ronin. Sadly, Bob pulled out yet another trick, and the flying bricks came together as an Admiral. The pirate captain hurled a heavy anchor at Epsilon, and the sharp part lodged itself to him helmet, pulling it off and almost crushing him plastic skull. His black, sweaty hair fell of his head.
“Oops!” He dived for the Lego hair and plopped it back on just as the Admiral sent a cannon at him. It slammed into Epsilon, sending him spinning backwards, causing him to slam into one of his makeshift barricades. “Ouch!”
The crash crushed one of his rocket boosters, and sent the other one whirling away into the perfect, cloudless sky.
Bob, looking like his normal, un-infected self, stalked towards the Space Ranger and yanked him up by the shirt.
Epsilon was just muttering the same words over and over again: “Sky, Nexus, Sky, Nexus, Sky…” Then he started to sob incessantly, the tears coming out in big blobs.
He looked up, and spat in Bobs face just before he was smashed…
Nautilus, Starless Night of the Swords of Light
