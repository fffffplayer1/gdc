The Battle for LEGO Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 74
A Series of Unfortunate Events

Whack Bliddo looked up from the crater in the ground, her eyes blazing a bright crimson that twisted and curled eerily on the blanketing light of the Maelstrom.
“Uhh… erhm… hi…” Magnus managed, his Rutcarver slipping from his shocked fingers.
He ended up hopping around left and right clutching his foot.
“YesssSSSssSss…” The spider hissed, lifting one of her many legs up and slamming it into the ground, causing a small crack to meander through the sloshing ground.
Magnus rubbed his sore toes with his unharmed foot, causing it to go numb with pain before he limply made a motion to pick up his Rutcarver. He spun the wheel experimentally, but it hardly turned. The Daredevil sighed, pointed one eye in Whack’s direction and one on the inside of the tire. “Great. Maelstrom Goo in the axel… Meldric, can you keep an eye on that spider, my eyes are hurting.”
No one replied.
Magnus looked up from his tire and looked in every direction… but the Summoner was nowhere to be seen.
And neither was Whack Bliddo.
“Meldric! Meldric, where are you!? Meldric!!!”
Then someone screamed… it must have been Meldric, even though it was a little too high pitched! But what could have happened? As quick as he could, he followed the voice.
*********************************************************************************************
Meldric Steamvalve couldn’t see a thing. Too often, he bumped into hysterical Minifigures who were darting around in the Maelstrom fog blindly, causing him to completely and utterly loose is sense of direction.
Luckily, it looked as if Whack Bliddo couldn’t see anything either. Meldric was quite sure he had tripped over the massive arachnid a few times already.
He stumbled forward, rain cascading down into him and draining right through his hood. The Minifigure held his cloak tightly to himself, trying to keep warm and squeeze some water away at the same time. His feet sloshed uncertainly through the murk, slipping and sliding when they met a rock spinning through the gloom.
But then he heard a familiar voice…
“I’ll kill you!” Someone yelled, his voice resonating through the smoke, rain, and fog.
Meldric couldn’t place a finger on who had said that, so, instead, he decided to follow the yell.
*********************************************************************************************
Dirk Manleigh leaped out of the way as Kinga Hurl’s massive fist came crashing down towards him at unrealistic speeds. As the hand came in contact with the ground, steam vented into the air and rocks flew in every direction. A massive, rigid boulder slammed into the wall next to the Sentinels head, and, before Dirk knew what was happening, mud cascaded onto his head and forced him to his knees. The boulder in the wall tilted downwards slightly, threatening to crush the gagging figure of Dirk.
The Sentinel looked up, drops of mud rushing down his forehead and pouring into his already stinging eyes. Two, blurred red dots appeared in front of him, and then he began to focus a bit more. He felt the hot breath of Kinga Hurl on his face as the enormous Silverback stooped down to face its victim.
Dirk Manleigh stared into the beasts eyes stubbornly. “You may tear be to pieces, but you shan’t even slice a brick from the Nexus Force! I fought for Avant Gardens, and, if it was meant to end like this, so be it!”
Kinga smiled, raised a massive fist, and-
“It won’t end like this!”
Logan Moonshot leaped onto Kinga’s back and imbedded his Broadsider into the monster’s spine. The Ape roared in pain, leaping onto his hind legs before stumbling into the mud in agony.
The Buccaneer tore his Valiant Weapon from Kinga’s back and slid to the ground in front of him. “You won’t hurt Dirk or the Nexus Force. The Maelstrom and its filthy, mindless monsters and tactics will never defeat us. Not even the Darkitect’s complete wrath could harm us!”
And Kinga Hurl slumped to the churning ground, his chest heaving and his crimson eyes slowly dimming.
But then a voice that rebounded off everything, that came from every direction- a voice so powerful it knocked Logan and Dirk to the ground- spoke.
“Ha! Ignorant fools! You know nothing of my power! Rise, my child, all show these imbeciles the wrath of the Maelstrom!”
Dirk got to his feet painfully and looked down to see Logan rubbing his head: The voice had caused the boulder to fall onto him.
Then, all of a sudden, dozens of Spiderlings burst from the ground, spewing mud in every direction.
And that’s when Kinga Hurl’s eyes suddenly turned bright scarlet once again.

The Battle for LEGO Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 75
Old Enemies

Not even the bright light of the moon could be seen through the swirling masses of the Maelstrom. Legions of Stromlings marched through the thick fogs and sheets of rain on the world of Nimbus Station, stealthily wiping out the many stray Minifigures in the area. After all, Nimbus Station was a very easy target.
But deep in the heart of the planet chunk, two Minifigures had just had a rather unsettling reunion.
“I knew you’d return… return. After She killed you… I knew you would come back.”
Seibatsu smiled a hidden smiled in his shadowy face, “You can blame your colleague for that.”
Mardolf blinked, stepping back and clutching his staff in both his hands, “What?”
The Ronin in front of him laughed, the smoky wisps that he used as legs winding and meandering across the ground like massive, greying, glassy tentacles. “A certain Minifigure busted his Time Machine an unleashed an army of dinosaurs!” Saibatsu laughed at his own corny joke.
“Wenn… Wenn… Wennwenn…” Mardolf mumbled, looking down at the ground, which had darkened, cracked, and wilted considerably when the Ronin had arrived.
“Yep. And just imagine, he ordered me to smash random, screaming citizens the moment I was released! ORDERED! And didn’t even say hi! Filthy, cowardly Stromling, Pathetic, really. I’m amazed my Master appointed him to General…”
“Your… master? You mean the Darkitect?”
“Baron Typhonus? Ha! He’s hardly worthy of my presence! My true master is chaos…”
“Chaos has no master.”
Seibatsu glanced at Mardolf momentarily, his eyes blazing a bright, lamp-like yellow that slightly blinded the wizard before him, “My Master IS chaos. The very, dark feelings that lurk through everyone’s minds! The sorrow and depression locked up in a dark alcove on everyone’s brains!” There was a shimmering purple light, and a 4 foot katana with engravings of koi appeared in Seibatsu’s left hand, “And the thing is… when your master is sadness, is anger, is hate, if fear and is suffering… you always have the key to unlock the deepest and darkest recesses of the mind.”
“Hardly worth your presence… the Darkitect if far more powerful than you! He’s the reason we are in this massive mess! He’s the reason you’re here! The reason you were created…created!!!”
“I have told you enough.” Seibatsu growled, and then lunged, his katana raised above his head for and underhand strike.
Mardolf sidestepped the blow, yelped as he cracked his back, and then spun around, causing his cloak to swirl around him like an orange tornado. The wizard then pointed his staff at the Ronin and fired a bolt of Imagination towards his foe.
Seibatsu parried the blow with his shadowy katana, which instantly caught fire as if it had been covered in oil and had been hit by a matchstick. In moments, the Ronin was firing blazing spheres of flame at Mardolf, who was frantically knocking the balls back with blasts of Imagination. One orb of fire slammed into the wizard’s chest, knocking him back and setting his orange cloak on a monstrous blaze.
Mardolf screamed, then dived down and began to roll around in the mud, frantically attempting to put out the flames.
“NO!” It was Burke Barrelchest.
The Property Launchpad Guard leapt forward, pounding his chest with one hand and waving a spear around with the other. A small rod of laser flashed past him and hit Seibatsu in the face, causing the Stromling to flinch slightly.
“Ha-ha!” Bruno Underbite laughed, firing another projectile from his turret as Burke’s spearhead clashed with Seibatsu’s katana.
Mardolf looked up from the ground, his burnt face dripping with mud and rain. “Wait! Burke! You can’t fight him! Seibatsu’s too pow-!”
He didn’t finish his sentence in time.
The Dark Ronin spun around, conjuring a wave of Maelstrom energy that knocked Burke face-first into the mud. The Guard attempted to get back onto his feet, but on smoky tentacle grabbed him by the throat and throttled him as it lifted him of his feet.
“It’s bad luck to stumble on a Sorcerer of Evil when he’s battling an old friend.” Seibatsu growled, before hovering farther into the air than he usually did.
Suddenly, his eyes seemed to catch fire and he blasted forward at impossible speeds, his katana pointed in front of him.
Burke Barrelchest emitted a guttural scream that was quickly choked out of his mouth as Seibatsu pulled his blade from the Guards belly.
Burke’s limp body fell to the slushing ground in pieces.
