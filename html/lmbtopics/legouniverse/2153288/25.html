The First Builders
Chapter 18
Flight from Gallant 5

Figmatrix felt as if she was being slowly compressed, her legs shrinking, her head shriveling up, her body deflating. Then she got the terrible feeling that she was smashing.
It was a nasty feeling. First, you couldn’t breathe. Then you got the horrid, complex feeling of your body parts being painfully unscrewed from their places. In moments, she noticed her hand slip from its socket and vanish in the velvety, bright light that was enveloping Ssaii and herself.
Then her leg went away.
Then her arms.
Then her other leg.
Figmatrix began to scream, but nothing came out of her open mouth…
Then her head slipped off her body and vanished in the golden light.

*********************************************************************************************

She awoke incredibly far away from where she had vanished. Massive rocky slopes loomed above her, only scarcely covered in withered and leafless trees. The air smelled slightly of sulfur… sulfur with a hint of smoke.
They had arrived in Omnibrick Trench.
Figmatrix turned to her side to see Ssaii kicking the Shard Seeker quizzically. Her turned to face her. “Ah! Awake, are you now! You took your time! The Keepers shall soon be with us!” He picked up a small wrench, inspected it, then tossed it behind him, “Well? Can you fix this thing in time?”
Figmatrix stood up and dusted herself off, slightly hovering over the ground. “How much time do we have, exactly?”
“Oh… about fifteen minutes.”
“WHAT!?”
“What do you mean?”
“I can only get this thing into the air in…” She speculated the half smashed starship, “Minimum, if I work hard, a day.”
Ssaii sighed, “That won’t do us much good. Thought of trying Imagination?”
“I haven’t yet mastered it!”
“It has the power to make something out of nothing, or to repair any damage. You must try.” He looked around nervously, then jumped as his eyes met the skeleton of the Zorrillo Beast. “Goodness me. I’m more scared than sad when I see that thing dead!”
“You should have seen it when it was alive.”
Ssaii looked at Figmatrix sadly. “Back when the Keepers were bent on destroying the Beast, Gallant 5 was in pain. The entire world was attempting to kill it… my parents were part of one of those fighting regiments who were trained on exterminating it. They were killed by the Beast of the Zorrillo Caverns… and the Beast took me in. It raised me, and slowly, the Keepers learnt that it was their guardian. It was… it was my parent…”
Figmatrix spun around and stared at him. “What!? But… no… I… I killed it…”
Ssaii seemed to smile at her. “True. And we’re going to join it if we don’t escape.”
Figmatrix nodded, and then turned the face the sizzling rubble. She imagined the starship in its former glory, jetting across the sky like a fish in water. She imagined it as a mere speck in the sky, the monstrous regiments of the Keepers cursing beneath the stars…
Tentacles of pure Imagination flew from her gloved hands, flinging bricks into the air and rebuilding the Shard Seeker. Small, glowing blue orbs spun around the mess, and bricks began swirling in a tornado of metal and plastic. Then, as fast as it began, it ended.
Exhausted, Figmatrix fell to the ground. When she looked up again, the Shard Seeker was right in front of her, fully built. “Wow…”
“Amazing!” Ssaii laughed, then stepped forward. There was a clonk as he hit his head into the ship and fell to the ground. “Err…” He began, rather embarrassed, “Where’s the door?”
Figmatrix snickered, pulled a remote from her robes, and clicked a button. There was a slight beeping sound, like the twanging of a guitar that was both too flat and too sharp at the same time, and the shining glass cockpit opened up.
“Even more amazing!” Ssaii whispered, more to himself than anyone else. He then thought of something that he had been wanting to ask Figmatrix for a while. “Figmatrix… you’re a Builder, correct? Or, as we would call you, a Seeker?”
Figmatrix shook her head. “The Builders were everyone’s ancestors. I have reason to believe that we are both their descendants. I am a Seeker, and nothing more.”
“Oh… but do you call yourselves Seekers where you come from?”
“No. We don’t have prophecies and Keepers and Seekers… where we come from, our species would be called”, Figmatrix removed her chaotic hood, causing a bright blue and white light to flood in every direction, “Mythrans”, she finished her bright blue eyes smiling at the Keeper before her.

The Battle for LEGO Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 61
One Down, Many More to Go

~The Maelstrom~

~Crux Core~

The Seeing Stone sparked and crackled, and shadowy, violet mist within retracting to reveal the smoking, scattered terrain of what remained of Avant Gardens. Beautiful trees now sat at their posts like skeletal guardians protecting nothing specific. Hills were practically leveled, and swarms of chanting Stromlings were pouring through the half-smashed Sentinel Encampment, led by an infected Knight of the Maelstrom. The massive Monument and Bridge, artifacts the Assembly Faction had once held in their proud minds as wondrous buildings now crumbled in ruin, many of their pieces simply wiped from existence.
All the grass was dead, the ground littered with the bricks of shattered walls and smashed Minifigures. Imbedded in the ground close to what once was the entrance to the Spider Cave (It had now caved in and doubled in size) was a dagger, with a golden hilt and a blade carved from Blue Imaginite.
It had once belonged to Melodie Foxtrot. And, a little while away, you would be able to find Brutus Speck’s battered wrench, half buried under ground from its fall down the cliff that led to the Venture League Picnic and Launch Area. That place was too utterly destroyed, probably suffering the most damage of anything else on the once majestic world. And then, close to the Paradox Research Facility, was a helmet. A blue helmet with a shattered visor. A Space Ranger’s Helmet… but there was more. Next to it was a scimitar made from the Maelstrom. Epsilon Starcracker and Bob had some to an agreement, and had fallen under the same evil.
The horrid visions vanished in the swirling smoke, and soon the Seeing Stone looked like nothing but a dark and shadowy mirror. A mirror reflecting evilly scarlet eyes and a murderous, skeletal grin.
“Avant Gardens has fallen. Next, I shall deal a blow so terrible and hard that the entire Nexus Force will shudder. They have now lost their main planet for newbies… and next, they will lose their second most important world…”
The face shining off the stony glass of the orb grinned even more, its mouth opening to show jagged and sizzling fangs.
“Next, I shall bring down the fiery fist of the Maelstrom onto Nimbus Station… and I will insure that there are no survivors… myself…”
The Darkitect stood up from his throne, his cloak swirling around him like the wings of a raven who was just shot down from the sky with an arrow. “The Nexus Force will fall.”
And so Baron Typhonus laughed, his voice ringing from his monstrous chunk of Crux through the swirling power of the Maelstrom like a megaphone, resonating through every planet chunk in the Nimbus System and rettling the skulls of their inhabitants.
War was coming.

The Battle for LEGO Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 62
Peace… for Now

It was time for the Winter Rushapalooza Festival. This amazing race would test the wits, skills, maneuverability, and, most importantly, the reflexes of even the most skilled racers in the Universe. It would start at Nimbus Station’s famous Vertigo Loop (Which, I’m sorry to say, struck Minifigures with vertigo far too often), then would speed up to Gnarled Forest’s Keelhaul Canyon. Afterwards, Minifigures would have to step on the gas to rocket through Dragonmaw Chasm of Forbidden Valley, just to skid to a halt (And slide forward considerably, no matter how good the brakes), in Nausea Tundra, the wonderful, thrilling, and exceptionally cold racetrack in Frostburgh.
It was November… everyone’s favorite month. Why? Because the exhilarating world of Frostburgh would once again orbit the Nimbus System’s sun, and, after a long and tedious year, it would melt from its annual deep freeze.
And it was time to have fun.
Minifigures from everywhere, let it be rain or shine, ninja or pirate, just to arrive at the miniscule world and toss a snowball at a friend or get free presents off the streets. It was simply one of the best times of the year. Almost everyone stopped battling the Maelstrom to have a few weeks of talking to snowmen or training Reindeers.
Simply the perfect season for a Rushapalooza.
So it was that slightly chilly winter afternoon, with Shouty McBullhorn’s voice reverberating off everyone’s rattling skulls, that hundreds of Minifigures arrived at the Nimbus Station Race Place, buying tickets from a multitasking Nexus Jay and testing out new cars purchased from a hectic Ludwig Clutchburn. Velocity Lane was sorting out who was going to participate in the race at the same time as chatting with Peppermint Lane on the mobile about cannon balls, bowling alleys, fuses and paint colors to color the walls of their house.
More and more Minifigures were pouring in, buying snacks and walking onto the Race Track to get their seats. All this was really tiring Velocity. The hated Rushapaloozas, mainly because she always won and never had competition, but also because of the amount of people she had to take care of.
“Oi! Ludwig!” She yelled, throwing a tightly packed bunch of almost thirty tickets into the face of what looked like a teacher with a hoard of screaming preschoolers running around him. “Take over for me, will ya?”
The Car Module Vendor spun around as if he had seen the grim reaper, his visor falling over and covering his face with a soft clunk. “What!? You know how hard I’m working?”
“I need a break!” Velocity snapped back, tossing a checklist and a box of tickets at him like a pair of Frisbees and vanishing into the crowd before he could do anything.
Ludwig scowled, raising his visor irritably. The box and the list flew right past his gaze and into his booth, rebounding off the wall behind him and hitting his head. Ludwig’s visor fell back over his face. “Tipical”. He growled, pushing a chassis into a customer’s stomach and sending the Minifigure gagging to the ground yelling, “I want a refund!” As best he could.
It didn’t work as far as Clutchburn was concerned.

*********************************************************************************************

Velocity Lane sighed and clicked the “Redial” button on her Brickberry. Sky still didn’t pick up.
The race conductor had been calling her sister for a while now, but Sky just wouldn’t pick up the phone. This worried Velocity… Sky usually picked up before the first ring! She had never done this before.
Then something terrible happened: Far, far away… something noticed a mobile ringing on a ground that was now nothing but scorched grass.
Sky Lane picked up the phone.
“Oh, thank goodness! I thought something had happened to you! The Rushapalooza is driving me crazy, and I was wondering if you could come over and help a bit!” Velocity gasped, standing up from the steps of Brick Annex that she had been seated on in relief.
“Grah?” The voice that came through sounded like a screech of a nail being scraped against tarnished bronze (Not to mention it spat a lot into the receiver).
“Umm… Sky? I think the reception is bad… shall I call you back?”
“Sh-Sh-Shky? Griaaah?”
Velocity opened her mouth to ask another question, but then realization dawned on her. “A Stromling…” She whispered, falling back to the steps in shock. Velocity quickly hung up and phone in her pocket in a rather absent minded state. “Sky… she’s in trouble! Something’s gone wrong… really wrong! Stromlings never get up to the Launch Area!”
In mere moments, Velocity Lane was speeding towards Nimbus Plaza. She had to find out what had happened… she was going to Avant Gardens.
