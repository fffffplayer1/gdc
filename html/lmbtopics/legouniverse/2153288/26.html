One of my favorite chapter yet... Sorry if it's long!

The Battle for LEGO Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 63
A Rip in the Fabrics of Time

Nimbus Station. It was a disgusting place. Filled with the rank reek of happy people, walking around happily, talking happily, buying happily, selling happily, travelling happily, and generally just being happy. It was gruesome, and Baron Typhonus had to put an end to the joy.
He scowled at the world on top of a hill, the trees that were once at the summit now nothing but withering, half-smashed objects lying in the dust (Which was long and sweet smelling grass mere second before).
The Darkitect then noticed what he wanted to do. His sickening frown became a monstrous grin, and his eyes glowed a violet crimson as he lifted his crystal-topped, obsidian staff above his head. Thin wisps of violet smoke curled from the sizzling gem and floated down the hill, causing all grass of leaves that they passed to wilt and dry out. The Darkitect then smiled even wider, causing the very air around him to shiver, and slowly faded away into nothingness. Soon, only his grinning, skeletal mouth and his lamp-like eyes were visible, and, in moments, they too had vanished.

*********************************************************************************************

A lonely, Second Rank Knight from the Sentinel Faction walked towards the Avant Gardens Laughpad glumly, dragging his plastic feet along behind him. He hated the Winter Rushapalooza… mainly because he hated snowballs. Many of them hurt him, even when he had with extremely though armor equipped. He hated it.
Then he got the perfect idea. The Knight quickened his pace, whipping through the tall grasses like a cricket. He was going to go back in time with the help of Wenn Wuzzit and fight in the Battle for Nimbus Station. The Sentinel liked soloing it… it was one of the few places he could think straight.
Sadly, he had no idea that he was going to witness more than a simple “Back in
Time Skirmish”. Far more.

*********************************************************************************************

“Where am I? WHEN am I?”
That was always the question lurking in the depths of Wenn Wuzzit’s complex, over IQ’d mind. Sadly, no matter how much he thought, how often his helmet malfunctioned, no matter how many of his monocles fell from his eyes and shattered on the ground below, he couldn’t find the answer.
He was thinking so hard, in fact, that he didn’t notice the Second Rank Knight walking up to him, panting through his helmet, and ask him for a short ride.
“Uhh… sir? I would like to go back in time a wee bit… you now, when Nimbus Station was still called Zorrillo Station and it was completely and utterly under siege by the Maelstrom?”
There was no answer.
“Hello?” The Knight nudged the Time Traveler. “Yoo-hoo?”
There was still no answer.
And that was bad. Knights have a tendency of being stubborn when no one replies when they are speaking to them (Which never happens, so you can understand why), and, as keen in ear, eye, and sixth sense as a fox, they shut down all internal alarms when stubborn.
That is why the Rank Two Sentinel Knight never noticed three, dull purple, shadowy wisps of pure evil, curled around his head and under his legs, floating past him like slightly colored air. Like a drowning snake, they wriggled and knotted until they touched the massive, shuddering, bronze clock that served as Wenn’s Time Machine.
“HEL-!” The Knight was cut by Wenn Wuzzit’s monocle, which, in the Inventor’s concentration, had popped from his eye and hit the Knight on the head with a clang. “Whoa!”
Then Wenn suddenly grinning. “I’ve got it!” He leaped almost six feet in the air, landed on his rump, then hopped back to his feet. “I’m in Zorrillo Station!”
“Er… NIMBUS Station, sir.”
Wenn looked crestfallen. “When’s that?”
“When’s that, sir? It’s now, sir.”
“When’s now?”
“The present, sir.”
“You got me a present? How delightful!”
“No no no! We are in the present!”
“Oh, you mean we are now?”
“Yes, we are now!”
“That means we’re in Zorrillo Station! Ha-ha!”
“Ugh.”
Then, all of a sudden, Wenn looked queasy. He spun around to look at the Time Machine, then screamed. The Sentinel looked at it, but saw nothing new.
“What happened?” He asked, rubbing his helmet in a bamboozled fashion.
“More radiation that normal in being emitted from the Machine! And there’s a quiver in the fabrics of time that isn’t supposed to be there… like the ear-splitting twang emitted when a slight breeze touches the string of a banjo that doesn’t exist! It’s a terrible anthropomorphic ruck of telecommunications!”
“What?”
But an answer never came. At that very moment, the face of the Time Machine glowed purple, and the hands of the massive, golden clock spun around like the propellers on a chopper being chased by a Maelstrom Dragon. Electricity (Well, more-than-usual-amounts of it) sparked from every nut and bolt, setting the lush grass around it on fire.
“RUN!” Wenn yelled, pushing the Knight out of the way just as the thing blew up, sending a massive cloud of Maelstrom swirling all around the place.

*********************************************************************************************

The Sentinel Knight coughed, pushing a piece of rubble from his legs and wiping soot from his face. He had a nasty pain in his right arm, possibly a break, and his cheek was severely burnt, not to mention all the… Wenn! Where was he? He must have taken most of the blast!
The Knight painfully got to his feet, holding his arm tightly at his chest. He kicked pieces of burnt metal out of the way, searching for the Inventor.
Then, all of a sudden, two red lights appeared a few yard before him. Everything else was covered by the rank Maelstrom fog.
“W-ach, ergh, ech! Wenn? ACRGHH!” A wad of half-phlegm spewed from his jaws, catching onto his helmet and splatting back into his face. It was bad being in Maelstrom fog for too long… you could turn into a Stromling.
“Yes?”
That wasn’t his voice. Well, it was, but it didn’t speak like him. It had a tint of satisfaction, darkness and the fact that whoever was speaking was definitely not spaced out in any way whatsoever.
“Wenn! Is that you?”
“Why, of course it is!” Wenn Wuzzit stepped from the fog… infected. His helmet was now dripping with Maelstrom slime, and he had the face of a Stromling. Half of his armor was gone to reveal shadowy ribs also dripping with the goo, along with skeletal legs and arms. The small clock that served as his shoulderpads resembled a smaller replica of the Time Machine before it had blown up… except for the fact that half of it had lost its front and was nothing but half-severed clockwork.
“W-W-Wenn! What… what happened?”
“I’ve been freed from the iron shackles of the Nexus Force! I no longer have to listen to orders! I can be ME!”
“You’re wrong! The Darkitect is controlling you! Snap out of it! You’re not a Stromling!”
“Smart for one who has only just begun battling in Forbidden Valley! Second Rank Minifigures are always the funniest to watch! Ha-ha!”
“No!” The Knight drew his greatsword, “You’re not an evil Minion of Chaos!”
“Oh? Well, say goodbye to the present, by boy!”
The Knight laughed. “’Say goodbye’, that’s what they all say!” Then he stepped back, looking rather puzzled, “Say goodbye… to the present? What do you mean, to the present…?”
“Wenn Wuzzit” laughed evilly, his voice lashing The Sentinel like a whip, “You shall see!” He stepped forward, and the clock on his chest glowed even brighter, “You shall see…”


Nautilus, Starless Night of the Swords of Light

The Battle for LEGO Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 64
Tick-Tock

The blast knocked Velocity Lane to the ground. Smoke tinted with the shadowy purple that only Maelstrom had billowed from the grassy, uphill road to the Avant Gardens Launchpad.
“Oh no…” Velocity whispered to herself, standing up and wiping grass from her racing uniform. “Something isn’t right here…”
Velocity Lane waved her hands, and in a flash of Imagination, a revolver and a spanner materialized in her hands. She spun the drum, added a few projectiles into it, and then ran off for the smoke. So far, she had been the only one who had noticed what was happening, for everyone else was far too preoccupied at Vertigo Loop.
The racer halted when she noticed the grass around her withering before her eyes. Only one thing could do that…
Velocity looked up and was met by a thick Maelstrom fog. She couldn’t go through that! Luckily for her, she didn’t have to.
All of a sudden, a body appeared from the fog, spiraling in the air, and slammed into her, knocking the racer a few feet back. When she got up again, the body was completely and utterly limp, and she could vaguely make out the battered and half-infected armor of a Second Rank Sentinel Knight, the bright blue plume on his helmet now nothing but shriveled, violet string-like remains.
“Are you alright?” She yelled, running towards him. She put her hand by his armored neck, and couldn’t feel a pulse. “That’s probably because of his helmet!” Velocity assured herself, and removed the fragmenting remains of the metal mask.
What awaited her on the other side made her scream and fall back, scuttling away in fear.
It was a skeleton.
Velocity peaked over the Minifigure’s armored shoulder again, just to make sure.
It was still a skeleton.
“W-w-what happened?” Velocity Lane thought out loud, eyeing the skeleton in a frightened way. She could take dragons and steep drops, even Maelstrom pigs, but she hated skeletons.
“Dead men tell no tales, didn’t you know? I couldn’t tell you what happened even if I wanted to! Which I kinda do…”
Velocity leaped up into the air screeching like a crow being run over by a rocket in its hyperdrive. “WHO SAID THAT!?”
“Me, of course!” The skeleton stood up, dusted itself off, and yawned, picking up the mostly severed helmet and plopping it back on his head. The skeleton then smiled, showing off dozens of blazing white teeth.
Velocity’s eyes rolled to the back of her head and she slumped to the ground.

*********************************************************************************************

“Everything is going according to plan, my lord.” Wenn Wuzzit’s voice had changed since he had become a Stromling. It now sounded like the burning of a damp spider web and three badly tuned violins falling down a mountain.
“Good… very good. And the Vortex through Space and Time?” The voice slammed into Wenn, causing him to sag to his knees and groan.
“It-it’s coming along p-p-per-perfectly, my lord. Soon I will be able to completely reanimate the Battle for Zorrillo Station… reanimate it as the Battle for Nimbus Station.”
“Good… I look forward to seeing your progress… I look forward to seeing Minifigures scream and smash… seeing Nimbus Station be crushed by the iron fist of the Maelstrom and watch its remains wither, rot and burn in the fires of doom.”
“Yes, master. The fabrics of time are already tearing.”

The Battle for LEGO Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 65
Return of the Past

“Magnus! Magnus!” Velocity pushed around the roaring crowd outside, searching in vain for the tell-tale helmet of a Third Rank Daredevil. She had already bumped into thirteen of them, but none were who she was seeking. “Magnus!?”
The race conductor slammed into a large piece of metallic plastic, the impact knocking her to the ground. She looked up, and found the person she was looking for: A massive, Third Rank Venture League Daredevil with an extremely bulky shape, along with a helmet with a crack running through it and armor with the paint peeling off.
“Mmhm?” He looked down at Velocity, who was smiling as if seeing him had just made her day.
“Boy am I glad to see you! There’s something I have do show you… something bad…”
“What?”
“Bring all the Faction Vendors to Nimbus Plaza, and I’ll show all four of you.”
“Oh c’mon. I’m a Daredevil, I ride on Bone Wolves, I don’t send messages-“
“You’re not sending messages! You’re bringing people!”
“Even worse.” The Daredevil grumbled, watching Velocity vanish into the bustling crowd.

*********************************************************************************************

Velocity Lane was already waiting for the Vendors by the time they had arrived. First came Magnus Bumperdent, the Daredevil that I introduced earlier. Then a man in flourishing white robes hustled in from the Race Place, a golden-rimmed hood over his head. He had bright blue gloves and a small, salt-and-pepper beard. He had a long staff tipped with a blue orb and a golden wing slung onto his back.
“Ah! Meldric Steamvalve! Good to see you!” Velocity waved at the robed man.
“You too!” The Summoner gasped, putting his hands on his cloaked knees for a quick breath.
Next came a bulky Space Marauder, who was wearing far more armor than Madison Growl. The visor on his helmet was slightly cracked, and was missing the tell-tale cross-eye that most had. He also had a slight limp, and his shoulderpads looked slightly burnt.
“Cog! Cog Blammo! Great to see you!”
“Yeah, yeah. What did you want to show me?” Cog snarled, causing inside his helmet for a few seconds, and then shook his head, attempting to get the saliva in the mask out.
“All in good time, we just have to wait for… ah! And here comes Xeno Blueblade!”
Sure enough, a Sentinel Samurai clad in black sped in, back-flipping and landing at the plaza with extreme skill “What did I miss?” He smiled, wiping his gloved hand on his shadowy kabuto lightly, simply out of habit.
“Absolutely nothing!” Velocity said back. “Now, for what I wanted to show you…”
She moved out of the way, and, sitting where she had been standing just seconds before, was a Second Rank Sentinel Knight covered in a blanked with tape on his helmet.
Magnus Bumperdent blinked under his motorcycle helmet. Cog Blammo laughed. Meldric Steamvalve pushed back his hood to inspect the Minifigure more closely, and Xeno Blueblade just sighed under his breath, mumbling that Venture Leaguers were always so obsessed when they found something, even if it passed them every day. That didn’t go well with Magnus, and soon, all three of them were quarreling over random things like what color their bedroom walls were.
That was, at least, until Velocity kicked the visor out of the way. Her face brightened considerably when the four Vendors gawked at the figure, falling to their rumps in fright and confusion in a heap of legs, arms, and… well… gawking faces.
“WHAT happened to him?” Meldric shouted, pulling his hood back over his head in fear.
The four were even more shocked when the skeleton replied. “I think I died, to tell you the truth.”
The Vendors, who had just gotten to their feet, fell flat onto their faces. “W-w-why’s he talking!!??” They murmured in unison, their teeth chattering in the way you chatter when you meet a Spinjitzu Ninja of Ice playing snowball in Frostburgh.
“Because”, Velocity replied, “He’s dead, but, according to the fabrics of time, he shouldn’t be. He isn’t SUPPOSED to be dead, so he is, but he isn’t.”
“Sounds like a zombie to me!” Xeno whispered, more to himself than to anyone else.
“Well, not exactly.”
“Then what?”
“Zombies move but are still dead. He’s dead but he’s alive.”
“Oh. Like a Stromling?”
“No, Stromlings are fully living.”
“Ah.”
“You didn’t know that?”
“I cut off their heads, I don’t examine them.”
Cog Blammo snickered slightly. “You Sentinels, always thinking with your blades.”
“Tell that to a Space Ranger, and you’ll sure have a blast!” Xeno laughed, wiping his kabuto again.
Cog sighed. “You Sentinels, always thinking with your feet.”
“That’s even better! It makes us fast!”
“You Sentinels, always thinking like Stromlings with their heads cut off.”
The Samurai opened his mouth to object but thought otherwise. Cog Blammo slimed under his helmet before turning to Velocity. “Where did you find him?”
The racing conductor pointed to the path that led to the Avant Gardens Launchpad. Cog could slightly spot some grass covered in Maelstrom goo a little ways away.
“What happened there?” He asked.
Velocity grimaced. “According to this Knight, the Time Machine blew up and infected Wenn Wuzzit.”
The Space Marauder scowled under his helmet. “Then what?”
“Somehow, Wenn has found some sort of control over time. He turned this poor Knight extraordinarily old, so old even for his plastic skin to vanish… all in a few seconds.”
“Control over time… oh goodness me... no, it can’t be…”
“What?” Velocity asked, but was quickly knocked to the ground by a small earthquake, “Wha-?”
Cog Blammo helped her up and then turned to face the path to the Launchpad. Meldric, Magnus, and Xeno followed suit after dusting sparse grass from their clothing.
Maelstrom fog was billowing from the entrance to the path, and there was a loud “CLANG!” as the metal, lattice-work arch above the entrance came crashing to the ground, its posts sizzling and dripping with the power of corruption. The mist curled towards the six Minifigures, and then halted, swirling in every direction but forward.
Then they heard footsteps. The sounds as whoever’s feet hit the ground sent a chill down the Minifigure’s spines, and, as if on cue, the sky darkened slightly as greyish clouds covered the sun.
Then the Minifigure revealed himself… or shall I say itself.
For, sure enough, it was a Stromling.
A Stromling clad in a space¬-suit covered in thick, Maelstrom infected armor. Half of its body, like most Stromlings, was nothing but a ribcage dripping with corrupted, violet slime. It had long spikes on its left shoulder, which was padded with more armor, unlike the right, skeletal arm. The Stromling also sported a large, infected Lock-Jaw Helm that blazed in what little light was shining from the darkened sky.
But the most prominent part of the creature was at the end if it’s shadowy, bone arm: A hammer with a spiked hilt slightly covered by sizzling, hanging cords of string. The actual head of the hammer was slightly destroyed, a quarter of it being nothing but crackling bricks protruding from the bulking weapon. And all over the hammer was electricity, zigzagging up and down the head and the hilt and sparking from it in small flashes that sent little flames into the air.
That Stromling was very well known. It had lead the first Maelstrom hoards in the Battle for Zorrillo Station, a terrible Hammerling General who had wreaked havoc for almost three days before he had been… smashed.
“He’s supposed to be dead…” Cog whispered, pulling a Maelstrom Ball from his pocket and switching it on and rubbing the red surface with his gloved hand.
Velocity opened her mouth several times before she could get the name out. “Murgle Blotch…”
Meldric Steamvalve drew the Wingreaper from his back. “He’s… he’s alive!”
The Hammerling General looked up, his bright red eyes vaguely suggesting that he was smiling. “Alive, yes. And soon you’ll see me kicking… kicking hard…”
Murgle Blotch laughed, raising his head to the sky, which darkened even more. A flash of lightning zipped by and it began to rain, and, high above, the clouds began to swirl round and round and catch on a purple tint. In mere moments, the Maelstrom was visible in the sky, crackling with evil energy. Laughter blasted from its depths to meet with Murgle’s.
Then, all of a sudden, hundreds of pairs of eyes began to glow in the Maelstrom fog. “The Sacking of Nimbus Station begins now…” Murgle snarled as the first Stromling leaped from the shadows, its blade glinting menacingly in the rain…

The Battle for LEGO Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 66
The Battle for Nimbus Station: Recreated

“Someone! Warn everyone else!” Velocity Lane punched the oncoming Stromling in the face, sending it crashing to the ground before she slammed her spanner into its face.
“I’ll go!” Magnus Bumperdent yelled back, waving his hands over his head to see the others over the thick veil of rain.
However, the Maelstrom wasn’t keen on reinforcements from the Nexus Force just yet… and they definitely didn’t want anyone escaping from their reach. “Block their escape!” Murgle Blotch roared, “Don’t let them get away!”
Magnus spun around to rush to the Race Place, but was quickly met by a group of Stromlings blocking his way.
“Ooohh, itzy bitzy Nexus Force wantings to runnings? Oooohhh, hehe!” One of them sneered in its usual, gagging and stupid voice. It raised its arm, the glinting, Maelstrom blade attached to the end flashing in the storm-light over the Stromlings head. “Ssstay here! I deal with bad boy!”
The Stromling charged forward, the five others behind it cheering nastily. Magnus raised unsheathed a Flaregun from a holster at his waist and aimed it at the Stromlings head, his gloved, plastic hand trained on the trigger.
He never got to shoot.
From what seemed like nowhere, a large anchor slammed into the side of the Daredevil’s helmet, sending him sprawling to the ground with a severe headache. A glowing purple cannonball whizzed past Magnus’ head just as he got back to his feet, missing him by inches and hitting the charging Stromling in the chest. “Talk about friendly fire…” Magnus groaned, getting shakily to his feet and pulling out another Flaregun.
He couldn’t see anything… anyone… he was completely surrounded by the Maelstrom mist and the constant blanket of rain. He could do nothing more but hear distant gun-fire, screaming, and the crack of the lightning in the swirling sky.
“Arrrgh!”
Magnus turned just in time to dodge another cannon-shot, barrel rolling out of the way in the last second. He then hopped to his feet and pointed his Flareguns at the charging… the charging Dark Admiral.
“Oh goodness me, either I’m dreaming or sitting at a stall all day wasn’t good for me!” Magnus mumbled to himself, firing two projectiles at the Stromling simultaneously.
The Admiral ducked down, causing the bullets to merely knock its large hat from its head. It then leaped into the air, the long chain attached to an anchor that served as its right hand oscillating around it.
Before Magnus knew what was going on, he felt a terrible pain in his hands as the anchor knocked both his Flareguns out of his grasp. He hardly recovered before the Dark Admiral, roaring some sort of pirate jig to himself, slide tackled the Daredevil and kicked him hard in the stomach.
Magnus fell to his knees, unable to scream because of his sudden loss of air. He looked up to see the massive barrel of the cannon that served as the Admiral’s left arm pointed at his face.
“Say g’dbye te yerself, her dog, and yer bonnie lass, me lad! I’ll meet ya in the Locker… if I ever get there, I mean! Ha-ha!” A small flame was ignited on the small string on top of the weapon.
Mugnus looked up. “Not… not if I have anything to say about it!” He spun around on the muddy ground, tripping the Admiral and rolling on top of him. Mud and murky water sprayed in every direction as the two Minifigures fought on the ground, and soon both of them were far wetter than they were before (Which was saying something).
The Admiral hollered in pain as Magnus punched it in the face, and, in turn, it swing its anchor at him, the chain tying the Daredevil up and making him almost completely defenseless. The Pirate Captain roared his approval, got to his feet, and began dragging Magnus around. Dirty water flushed down the Daredevil’s mouth, causing him to gag and cough continuously… but the Admiral had no mercy.
“I’ll drown ye on yer own turf, ha-ha!”
“Not if I can help it!”
That wasn’t Magnus, it was…
The first owl swooped overhead, then dive bombed into the Admiral’s hat-less head. Without its hat, the Stromling had long, matted dreadlocks that smoked with Maelstrom energy… well, at least it was matted until the bird pulled the Admirals hair off its head.
“Arrgh! I’ll get ye!”
Or at least it thought it would, for, before the Admiral could raise its cannon to shoot the owl, two more flew towards its face, their talons outstretched and glinting in the light of the Maelstrom high above them. The Pirate swiped at one of them, but missed, slipping over the pouring water and falling flat on his face. However, when it got up, and enraged Stromling caught one of the owls by the throat and threw it to the ground, smashing it. The Admiral then snatched its hair from the mud and slapped up back onto its head unceremoniously.
“NO!” It was that voice again… the voice of…
Meldric Steamvalve leaped from the fog, his Wingreaper in one hand and a Ronin’s katana in the other. “Magnus, duck!”
“A little hard when I’m being dragged around on a chain!” The Daredevil snapped back, spitting a fountain of murky water from his mouth.
The Summoner kicked the Admiral in the face, sending the Stromling sliding in the mud. When it tried to get up again, it was knocked back by a gust of wind and hit in the chest with Meldric’s Wingreaper.
“Yraarrr…!” The Dark Admiral groaned, raising its cannon arm feebly before its head was sliced off by the Ronin katana.
Meldric freed Magnus from the chains and helped him back up, shaking water from his hood and imbedding the katana into the now soft ground. “I was attacked by a squad of Ronin led by Murgle. I managed to hold him off for a while, but he knocked me to the ground. When I got back up, he wasn’t in sight. The coward.”
The Daredevil shook his head lamely. “Oh?”
“Gather yourself up and dust yourself off! We have to warn the others!” Meldric took two steps forward before turning, “Oh, and I think you should use your Rutcarver. Flareguns are out of style.”
