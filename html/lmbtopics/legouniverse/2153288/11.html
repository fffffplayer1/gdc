The Battle for Lego Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 33
Halt! Thou shall not pass!

Theo Balfour smiled, leaning on his spear. Finally, he was back on guard duty and didn’t have to battle crazy Maelstrom beings any longer. But then, all of a sudden, what looked like two red lights entered the cave, getting bigger and bigger. He could hear some whizzing sound, like the screech of wheels. Then he saw it: The back of the truck barricade.
“HALT! You have no permission to enter!” The Sentinel Grunt ordered at the incoming part of the vehicle. Of course, it didn’t stop. “DID YA HEAR ME!?”
It just kept flying forward. “Uhhh… halt? Thou shall not pass! I mean it! I’ll torture you with more Shakespearian if you continue!” Obviously, it didn’t freeze in its tracks.
“Uhhh… AHHH! DON’T HIT ME!” Theo leaped out of the way just as the rear bumper crashed into the area he had been standing just seconds before. It spun around, and then skidded to the bouncer. Suddenly, it flew upwards with such force that it embedded itself onto the caverns ceiling above. “Ouch, that must have hurt! Badly…”
But then, at the mouth of the cave, he saw a flash of glowing light; red, yellow and orange, with a tint of blue. The light flickered, then Wisp Lee, screaming so loud his head could have unscrewed, came running into the opening.
“HALT! You are not permitted to pass unless Mr. Penumbra instructs you to!”
Wisp was followed by Kit Wanderare, Fitz Vanderbuilt, Madison Growl, and the two Sentinel Guards, all also shouting and yelling. “You are not allowed here! GET OUT!” Theo stomped his foot on the ground like a three year old.
“WE HAVE TO GET TO THE MONUMENT, NOW!” Wisp roared, shoving the Grunt out of the way. Theo shoved him back.
“No entry!”
Then Silas skidded into the cave. “EVERYONE CAN GO!” He roared, a dozen Stromlings chasing after him, waving their blades malevolently.
Theo saluted, and then moved out of the way. But everyone was too angry at the Grunt to move, and they all talked him. “STOP YOU SAVAGES!” Silas shouted in a high pitched voice, not noticing a Stromling passing him. It crept for the bouncer, and in no time, was flying for the next ledge… just to slam into the still whirring wheels of the rear of the truck. The creature was flung back down, and was soon trampled by Silas, Wisp, Fitz, Madison, and the two Guards, who, with more experience, flew past the menacing vehicle like a piece of cake.
*********************************************************************************************
Rusty Steele, not understanding what was going on, ushered the last few Recruits up a small, well hidden pat bouncer, the fastest way up the monument. Panting, he walked over a few repair bricks for the Assembly Monument, dragging his massive wrench to his boring post. A small bird fluttered to his head and sat.
“GERROF!” He snarled swatting at it with his over-large weapon. The animal squawked, flapping away in a flurry of white feather bricks. “I hate birds.”
But then his non-existent ears caught a sound… screaming and the crackling of… flames. He then saw Madison Growl - a Minifigure who had been lost for over a year- leap over the small trench separating the cave from the Monument, not bothering to assemble the quick-build bridge.
“M-M-M-Madison!? Where’ve you be-.”
“THE SPIDER BOSS HAS A FLAME THROWER!”
“Spider Boss? Ha! That thing’s probably been smashed of old age!”
“NO! IT IS ATTACKING US RIGHT HERE, RIGHT NOW YOU IMBACILE, AND IT HAS MULTIPLE WEAPONS THAT COULD BRING THIS WHOLE PLACE DOWN IN A JIFFY!” She roared.
Rusty could see the shapes of more Nexus Force Agents leaping over the pit, also not bothering to build the easy way past. “The… Spider Boss? I thought you weren’t serio-.” He was cut off once again, but this time not by a sound, but by a sight. Hundreds of Stromling and Mech Invaders were pouring from the cave, Spiderlings crawling upside down and skittering of the boulder filled roof.
But the thing that actually cut him off was ten times more terrible: A massive shadow that emitted a purple aura with a head that sported half a dozen glowing red eyes. It had four ginormus legs, and the sides and top of its head were covered in nasty looking weaponry, including what looked like a cannon shaped as the head of a… dragon.
The cannon’s “maw” opened wide, causing a sulfuric stench to leak from it. A small but bright spark ignited in the metallic throat, and…
The party screamed and rushed for the pet bouncer as everything green flammable went up in smoke. The Spider Boss’s flame thrower blasted a constant column of red hot, blue tinged and ever-licking fire onto every surface in range. In no time, it had begun using its double barreled, rapid firing gun, and used both juggernauting machines to wreck simultaneous havoc.
“Get your pets out, now!” The Assembly Foreman roared, blocking a bullet with his metal hardhat. The blow sent him into the ground. “GO!”
Soon, everyone had launched themselves to the Monumental Finish Line. Everyone except Rusty Steele. Quickly, he summoned his Saber Toothed Tiger pet, and it rushed to the bouncer. He stepped onto it too, but a volley of bullets from the Spider Boss instantly smashed the entire contraption.
“Ohhhhh no!” He yelped turning to the massive arachnid. There was only one way out of this mess: the long way.

The Battle for Lego Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 34
The Monument Race
Rusty Steele stood stock still for a moment, staring at the Spider Boss. The creature raised its flamethrower once again… and the foreman didn’t know what to do…
“AHHHHHHHHHH!!!!!” Rusty, wrench in his hand, bulleted up the Monument’s Green Path, running right through a laser. After a few yelps and a weak smile at the on-looking army, he ran on. The Spider Boss “raised and eyebrow” before screeching at the hoard. About a dozen Stromlings and six Sawlings nodded, proceeded to chase Rusty up the Monument. A batch of Mechs loaded their blasters, and then shot grappling hooks up to the Orange Path, climbing slowly upwards. Lastly, the Spiderlings crawled up the massive statue of the plain, bald, red shirted and blue panted Minifigure.
Panting, the Assembly Minifigure rushed up the next slope… just to be tossed back like a rag doll by a massive wind. He looked up, still flat on the ground from the force of the blow. “Ach, I hate those fans! Why did I ever design this thing!”
He slowly stood up… just to get a glowing, purple blade pointed at his throat. Another stuck uncomfortably at his back. “Up put hands!” A shadowy voice rasped evilly.
The foreman obeyed, lifting both his arms upward slowly, but steadily. From his peripheral vision, he saw the dark, infected, mouth-less face of a Stromling. He could hear more scrabbling behind him, Stromlings panting in their oily, croaking breaths. Rusty closed his eyes and smirked, then, with lightning fast reflexes, he barreled into the Stromling in front of it, whacking it into the fan. The howling creature stopped in midair, looking about in surprise. Then its eyes widened. A blast of air from the propelling machine sent it spinning back at the Assembly Foreman, but Rusty ducked, and the Stromling hit the rest of its kind like a bowling ball, knocking then down like dominoes.
While the dazzled Maelstrom creatures looked about with bamboozled expressions, Rusty Steele dug his hands into the small bags he had strapped to his badly strapped belt. He pulled a handful (Errr… two-finger full) of their contents: shining brass balls that glistened in the eerie light of the Maelstrom. With a single stroke of his hands, he tossed the gadgets at the confused crowd. The spheres with the road with a soft, tinkling sound, then, for some reason, “opened”. By opened, I mean that a couple of hatches appeared at the top, revealing small pipes. The things also grew four, metallic, insect-like legs. Then, all of a sudden, horns or woodwind instrument’s bells popped from the sides, honking with nasty, deafening, tune-less music. The pipes suddenly vented multicolored smoke that covered the entire area, the “music” making it ten times worse. Mardolf the Orange had helped Rusty make these droids… he took special pride in adding a few “Skunk Emergency Defense Mechanisms” to the smoke.
The machinery took effect almost instantly. At first, the Stromlings screeched, their shadowy hands slapping at their faces, searching for nonexistent ears. Their plastic palms then began grabbing for their throats, causing them to cough even more. Eyes watering, the Stromlings fell to the ground wheezing in pain. Rusty was sure their diaphragms had popped.
Rusty took a moment to taunt the gagging creatures, sadly allowing some of the smoke into his lungs.
“Aachh!” The foreman coughed, spinning around and flinging the overweight wrench into the fan. It slammed into the things body, sending the blades spinning in all directions.
But he kept running, yanking the tool from the obliterated remains of the sparking fan and raced ahead. Rusty Steele looked up at the looming figures that consisted of the Monument and sighed. That was one thing that couldn’t be destroyed, no matter what. One of the greatest Assembly creations was resting in his hands… all he had to do was carry the immense weight.
“Zzzreeessshhhh!” An electrical purple projectile hit the foreman in the chest, sending his sprawling.
Dizzily, he sat up, rubbing his sore ribcage painfully. Above, about three Stromling Mechs, eyes glowing a menacing yellow, sat, their Maelstrom powered blasters trained on the poor Assembly Minifigure.
“Ahhh… hehehe…” Rusty raised his hands, causing him to lose balance of his sitting position and fall back onto his back.
One of the Mechs looked at the droid in the middle, the only one to strangely have red eyes. The red eyed Mech stood up on its metallic legs, fitting its gun onto its back. “1…” It started in a voice like claws on glass. “1 PRDX-7… 1 smash y0u… l1ke… smash1ng…”
The name shocked Rusty so much that he leaped to his feet in an instant. “PRDX-7!? You… you’ve been INFECTED!? Ohhhh no, PRDX-4’s not gonna like this…”
“F… f… f1re all… all g-g-g-guns!” The two Mechs beside PRDX-7 shot two projectiles simultaneously at the Minifigure, but he had already run away screaming.
“F0ll0w h1m! D0n’t l…l…let h1m get… away!”
The infected droids clanked along the trench above him, skidding for the Orange Path. Rusty, panting, dragged his wrench along the Green Path. Then he saw it: A laser beam. The foreman smiled, leaping over the red ray, then holding the machine and turning it with all his might. He directed the laser’s glowing crimson beam at the marching Mechs above, and, in seconds, they had been reduced to a pile of bricks.
Shocked, PRDX-7 stomped away, screeching behind him in a voice that now resembled the flushing of a lavatory. “1 w1ll get y0u! Even 1f 1t’s the last th1ng 1 d0!”
“How classical!” Rusty laughed, and then began walking towards the buildable elevator with his own pace. In a blur of his arms and a few bricks, the model had been built and was ready to ride. He stepped onto the platform and it began rising slowly upwards. But when it hit the ledge that it was supposed to stop, it kept going up. “What the-!?”
The foreman looked out the elevator and saw the glowing red eyes of hundreds of Dark Spiderlings, heaving Rusty and the elevator to the top of the Monument with their shadowy webs.
“Ohhhhh no!” Rusty grasped his wrench tightly, whipping a bead of sweat from his brow. He was going to be smashed, and there was no doubt about it.
