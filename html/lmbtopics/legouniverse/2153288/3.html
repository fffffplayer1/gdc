The First Builders

Chapter 4

Gallant 5

Sector 364

The Zorrillo Caverns

The two hooded figures looked quizzically at the fork in the cave. They had absolutely no idea where to go. One side seemed to be full of glowing plants with blood red flowers that swayed in some non-existent breeze. The other size was just full of stalactites and stalagmites, and had no light what so ever.
“I think we’d have a better bet going through the one full of fauna.” The leader whispered.
The other figure nodded, and the two Minifigures with infected cloaks proceeded. The plants seemed to turn to the two figures as they passed them, the bulgy, worm-like leaved wriggling strangely. As they went farther into the caverns, more flowers seemed to appear, until they were in what looked like a blinding tunnel, covered completely in wriggling flowers.
Then the leader stopped in his tracked, and this time, the Second in Command made sure not to bump into its master.
“What is it?” It said, peeping over its leader’s shoulder.
The master floated into a position that made it look like it was lying on its stomach in mid-air, and looked more closely at the plants. The red flowers seemed to look at it, but that’s not what had grabbed its attention. It was this: A neon blue ant-like insect was crawling up one of the bulbous, wriggling leaves. Then the leader saw two more, crawling up different plants. The master floated back up.
“Don’t touch the plants.” It said in a bored tone.
The other figure, flummoxed, looked down and saw the insects. “EWWW!!!!” It spat and zoomed ahead… just to slam headfirst into a dead end.
Its master floated up next to it and looked closely at the rocks that blocked their path. “Hmmm… this was recent, there are no plant growing on it.” It placed its obscured hand on the blockage, and for a second it looked like a ripple of blue energy had waved through it. Then, all of a sudden, the wall blew up in a spark of whirling imagination, and the left-over bricks were absorbed into the leader.
“WHAT THE!” The Second in command shrieked, “bouncing” backwards.
“That solves our problem, lets continue!” The leader said cheerfully.
“Suuure…”
But when the two hooded Minifigures turned around, they saw the most terrible thing they had ever seen. They had arrived in a room where the beautiful flowers had been replaced by bones, scattered and broken weaponry, and battered helmets, torn up clothing and crushed bits of artifacts. Stalactites and stalactites stuck out in off angles with skeletons hanging from them. In one side of the room, a blazing fire burned, spitting out coals and emitting smoke. And in the shadows, there was a limp figure in robes, being held by a bionic (This is Lego!) hand with dagger-like claws. A silhouette of a massive, wings monster with a dozen glowing eyes was attached to the hand by a long, shadowy arm.
The beast roared, the sound knocking the two figures to the ground and extinguishing the fire, turning off all light other than the glow of the monster’s eyes and the distant shimmering of flowers.
The Zorrillo Beast was angry, and was ready to smash…

The First Builders

Chapter 5

Sector 364

The Zorrillo Caverns

The hooded leader was the first to leap to his unseen feet, and floated out of the way of the monsters swinging, jagged tail. The master then rocketed for the other, unconscious figure, and dragged it to a “safe” spot by a grinning skeleton.
The Zorrillo Beast flung its unconscious prey into a wall and spread its four, massive wings. The creature then roared, breathing out a jet of greenish blue, sizzling goo that disintegrated everything in its path.
The leader floated, rigid, staring in horror at the smoking, oozing, and rapidly melting path that had been made right before its unseen eyes, just to be smacked in the back by the monstrosity’s dagger-like, horny tail.
The figure suddenly turned slack, dropping to the ground in a groaning heap.
And just as the Zorrillo Beast was going to finish the figure off, it heard a-
“AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA!!!!!” The Second in Command had woken up just in time. “WHAT ARE YOU THINKING? BRINGING ME TO A MONSTER’S HOUSE, THEN MAKING ME SIT BY A SKELETON LIKE A DOLL AT TEA-TIME!!!!”
The Zorrillo Beast turned to the shuddering figure, raising a scaly eyebrow. The hooded figure turned, jumped down to the skeleton, and began hugging the marrow out of it. “SAVE ME!!!! SAVE ME!!!!”
The Beast roared spreading its four, massive, bat-like wings and took to the air, soaring up to the top of the incredibly high ceilinged cavern. Then, all of a sudden, the monster stopped in midair, turned, facing downwards, and began spinning for the ground like a rocket, sending massive balls of sizzling acid whizzing in all directions.
The hooded figure, still bear-hugging the body of bricks, was surprised to see the skeleton replaced by a bubbling puddle of greenish-blue goo. “AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA! WHAT KIND OF TEA PARTY IS THIS!!!” It floated away in fright, crashing into a boulder and falling to the ground unconscious.
The Beast landed gently, crawling towards the two new bodies. It picked them up. It then scraped one of its massive claws into the rocky surface, causing sparks to fly out and hit the ground, starting a small fire.
“Musssssssssst ssssssstop zzzzzzze propppppppppphhhhhhhhhecy ! Mussssst sssssstop…” The Zorrillo Beast started more fires until the whole room was lit up by a fiery blaze. The shadows left the dark walls, revealing inscriptions and artifacts such as smashed pottery and glazed tusks. The Beast looked to the cavern walls, looking at all the paintings. They showed two figures in dark, swirling, purple hoods with glowing , a ninja-like figure in a black cloak, and a monster just like the one looking at all the inscriptions, standing above them all. But in the next scene, it was lying on the ground by a metal bird, a hooded figure standing on it, flickering flames in the background. They could not be allowed to reach the source… to reach life. It would render them indestructible, only to be confronted by something as invincible as them… ending the Universe, and the last few sources.
The Zorrillo Beast would fight to the death to keep it safe…

The First Builders

Chapter 6

Gallant 5

Sector 364

The Zorrillo Caverns

The leader slowly awoke from his slumber, rubbing his eyes. His head hurt with an excruciating pain that made him shudder violently… he was not used to being at the bottom of the food-chain. Next to him, the Second in Command Minifig was still on the ground, stiff as a plank. And then there was the strange cloaked person. The leader turned, seeing the Zorrillo Beast curled into a ball, snoring. The coast was clear. The hooded Minifigure crawled towards the other figure, taking a close look. The body looked as if it was a ninja, the cloth a reddish black with strange markings. It had a long, shadowy cape with light blue slivers running through it, moving every few seconds like and electrical current. Like most ninjas, its face was covered, but only one eye showed… the other one was covered by the cloth, like the rest of the body. And the eye that could be seen seemed to be blurred; constantly changing color and morphing about like another dimension.
“Yooooou can loooooooook at iiitsssss, but if you eaaaaaat iiitsssss, you’ll joooooin the skeeeeletons.” The Zorrillo Beast growled. It had obviously pretended to sleep, and the leader almost jumped out of his cloak… well, just enough for the Beast to see his floating legs. “Itsssssss YOOOOU!!!!! Zzze proppphhhecy issssss truuuueeeee!!!!” Suddenly, the monster shot up, spread its wings, and grabbed the limp ninja and the Second in Command, then, with a roar that sent more discolored acid hurtling towards the leader, and took off, rocketing for the top of the cave.
The leader flew out of the way, the acid whizzing past, and looked up. The Beast had already flown out of sight… with two un-smashed life forms. Their futures were in his pseudo- hands… great. He always had to save the day!
The master floated into a starfish position, and in a few seconds, Imagination was blasting out of his feet and hand in torrents, sending him spinning to the top.
“CRASH!” The leader slammed his head into the ceiling, and fell back to the ground with a thump. Luckily, on the second try, he stopped in midair, and blasted into yet another tunnel. But after a few minutes of whizzing past jagged rocks, he was blinded by thousand and thousand more of those strange plants. All their flowers turned to face him, their fat, tube-like leaves wriggling incessantly. Then, all of a sudden, the transparent, blue insects buzzed into the air with rapid wings, and rocketed for a hole at the end of the tunnel. The leader, puzzled, floated after the whizzing bags, to see a beautiful sight by the hole. The insects where flying out in a stream of light, kind of like miniscule, glowing bats with frilly antennae. Bugs from other rocky mountains where also zooming out, and when all the streams met, the insects blasted upward, just before they could crash, making something that looked like a massive, blue cylinder… a connection… a Nexus…
Then, as fast as it began, the bugs dispersed, flying in billions of different directions, into the tri-moonlit night, and disappearing, probably to hunt. And yes, I said tri-moonlit; The planet of Gallant 5 was decorated by three, blazing moons with hundreds of craters.
But then the beauty vanished, for far away, past the flaming fireworks of Hingehurl Pass, he hear gunshots, a roar, and then say a massive explosion… The Zorrillo Beast had gotten to camp… and their only way off the planet…


Nautilus, Starless Night of the Swords of Light

Chapter 7

Gallant 5

Sector 360

Omnibrick Trench

The hooded pilot literally dragged himself out of the ship. Yet again, the engine had been fried, and it was up to him to save the rest. The Second in Command, and a strange looking Ninja where lying on the scorched ground, and the Builder was bracing himself for a deadly blast from the Monster’s maw.
The creature had appeared suddenly from the roof of the cavern, dropped the two helpless bodies all 100 feet, and began firing some sort of acid. The ship had just been fixed, but just before it could take off, the monster had come, and the pilot was forced to begin firing rapidly with the ships attached machine guns. But the beast took to the air, slamming into the ground and sending the grounded ship into the rocky surface of the wall, smashing the windshield and obliterating the engine. And now the pilot had to fix it and smash the monster before the monster smashed him and his friends.
He limped towards the ship’s flaming behind, and took a close look at it. Unlike the others, who’s hoods completely shadowed their faces, the pilot’s red, glazed goggled could be seen. Sadly, the rest was pretty much shadows.
He picked up the Builders wrench and began hacking away at the machine, producing bricks from his hood and sleeved to rebuild whatever had completely melted. He was just about to finish when he heard a scream: The monster had sent a ball of acid at the Builder, and he hadn’t dodged fast enough… his hand had been hit and was slowly dissolving, the flames burning his cloak up as if he was made of oil.
“NO!” The pilot tossed the last brick onto the ship and hopped in through the hole in the window. He slammed a red button and held tightly to a joystick like steering console. Blue flames rocketed from the back of the “Shard Seeker”, and if took to the night sky, firing rapidly at the monster. The screaming Builder ran around in all directions, but the creature’s attention was turned to the firing ship.
“Ssssssssarrrd Sssssssseekerrrrrr! Ze meeeetaaaal biiirrrrd!!! The beast shot into the air, wasting no time. In the prophecy, it was dying in front the flying, metal bird known as the “Shard Seeker.” The ship had to go; it was the only way to keep the element safe. The Zorrillo Beast blew a constant stream of sizzling liquid at the “Seeker”, but the ship was fast, and it dodged the blows, keeping the guns on the creature. “RRRRAAAAAAAAAAAAGHTTTHHH!” It roared, rocketing for the ship, all four wings practically buzzing. A well aimed slash with the monsters claws carved to front half off, sending gas spraying into the night sky. Then the Beast snapped with its jaws, missing the ship maneuvering ship by a millimeter. The pilot sent half a dozen missiles at the Zorrillo Beast, causing homing rockets to slam into the monsters mouth and eyes. It screamed and crashed into the bottom of the cavern, smoke trailing from its jaws and three of its eyes.
The pilot landed, quickly leaping from the hole to aid the Builder.
“Brickore! Don’t leave me, please!”
Brickore, the Builder, held out a hidden hand for the pilot.
“Figmatrix… oh, I’ll miss being with you guys… but I will always be here…” Brickore touched the right side of the pilot’s cloaked chest. “In your heart…”
“No… please! Don’t go, don’t go!” Figmatrix could feel that Brickore was smiling at him, and that was even worse. He pulled the Builder over and cradled him in his arms… and after a few seconds, a blue mist floated out of Brickore’s hood is wisps, spiraling into the sky. And soon, only the cloak was left, and Brickore’s body had joined Imagination…
“Yyyooouuuu caaan’t coontiiinue down thiiis path.” The wounded Zorrillo Beast had dragged itself to Figmatrix, and was looking sadly at the cloak with its remaining eyes. The pilot looked over to the monster, tears water-falling out of his hood. And just before he said that he would never forgive it, he saw tears trickling out of the monsters eyes. “Thiiis path, yoouur deestiiination… wiiiill briiing total destruuctiioon…”
“W-W-Wh-What d-do you m-m-mean?”
“Iiii meeaaan whaat Iii saaay…” And with that, the Zorrillo Beast limped towards the center of the Trench, nursing its right wings, and lay down. Before Fitmatrix could ask another question, the Zorrillo Beast of Gallant 5… had smashed…

The Battle for Lego Universe

Chapter 13
Vector Longview woke with a start, hopping up and looking around. Then she remembered: The binoculars. She ran over to them and looked at Epsilons post. The Ranger wasn’t present… he was probably on another eradication mission. She must have dreamt… er… nightmare’d Epsilon being attacked by a bald Stromling. Now that she thought back on it, she laughed. “Boy, am I silly or what?”
She turned, noticing that no one was there to listen to here. “Yep, I’m silly.”

A while away, in the Sentinel Encampment, things were incredibly hectic. “I WANT A THOROUGH ANSWER TO WHY EPSILON IS GONE!” Beck Strongheart was waving his map of battle strategies around like a baby with a blanket. “HE SHOULD HAVE REPORTED TO ME!!!”
“Ummm… sir?” Theo Balfour stepped tentatively forward, addressing his leader.
“SALUTE!!!!”
The Sentinel Grunt hurriedly did so, and then proceeded with his report. “Rocket Jet 3 of Air Squad 17 has crashed into the remains of the Paradox Research Facility, doing maximum damage, smashing the pilot, and allowing the last Spiderling held in captivity to escape. Apparently, it hauled Prime Maelstrom Cube and Arachnid Researcher Wisp Lee into the Spider cave, unnoticed by us… this was recently found out because we found infected hand marks in the ground, and a trail through the Maelstrom goo.” The Grunt then looked down, avoiding Beck’s eyes.
“What, soldier?”
Theo lifted up Epsilons helmet. “I’m afraid Sir Starcracker has been-“
“SMASHED? EPSILON SMASHED!!! HOW CAN THIS BE!!!!!????”
“We… don’t know… “
Fitz Vanderbuild then piped up. “I could swear that I heard the roar of an Ape, along with gunfire and missiles!”
“You were asleep, on the ground with the Guard!” Beck snapped, and Fitz practically curled into a ball. I haven’t seen you this angry since that Stromling came into camp and started riding on the Dobermans…”
“AGH! Don’t remind me of that time!!! And anyway, of course I’m mad; we have practically been completely wreaked by whoever smashed dear Epsilon!”
Fitz managed a small salute before fainting, falling onto the grounded guard.
“Melodie, Kit! I want you two to keep an eye out in the Spider Cave!” Beck roared, and the Sentinel Woman nodded, running towards the entrance of the cavern immediately. Kit Wanderware, on the other hand, grumbled that there was “Too much noise”, but when the Sentinel General gave him “The Look”, the Venture League Scout hurried away, rolling his eyes under the windshield of his helmet.
