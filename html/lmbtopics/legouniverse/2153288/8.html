The Battle for Lego Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 25
Hide and Seek

“Where IS he!?” Meyer Muckrake roared, attracting the attention of some Sentinels. A Knight among them snickered and whispered something to a nearby Space Ranger. The Ranger and yet another Knight began laughing. A Samurai, sitting by the stairs of the Imagination Nexus, giggled lightly. “At least they have a bit of honor”, He mumbled distastefully.
“What did you say?” The Space Ranger yelled, sending his Sentinel gang hollering.
Meyer just walked past. “And Exeter loves Vanda, how ironic. No wonder she calls his an imbecile!” He thought, stepping away from the Assembly Chamber and walking up the neon lit Venture League stairs. And there he was, Mr. Ree, inspecting the hologram of Planet Crux.
Suddenly, the ground shook and the Paradox guard was sent sprawling to the ground. This was a downside of the Crux Map Room: the Venture Cartographers were terrible architects, and made the mechanism that blew the map up badly. So when it showed the planet exploding, the whole floor began shaking.
Meyer stood back up, but the shady vendor was nowhere to be seen. “Oh Crux!” He snapped, running for the archway to the Venture “Teleportation Cannon”, the only way to Captain Hael Storm. He pushed past a snoring Trek Furino, who instantly leapt up and began firing with his flairguns. This caused one of Ace Warprider’s Nexus Dropship Nosecone Modules to smash, making the Space Man wave his hand angrily. And the Paradox Guard was already panting next to the Venture Leader, asking the pirate if he had seen Mr. Ree.
“Yarr! I havn’t seen runnin’ like that since Trek Furino got chased off by Doc Overcoat’s mechanical mangy dog! Ye’ve given me hope fer me te see more humor in them star filled seas!” Hael bellowed in his hearty, and, well… “pirate” accent.
“Umm… ye… ain’t… happened te… see a man named… Mr. Ree anynowhohow, have ye?” Meyer Muckrake tried, failing miserably.
Hael Storm raised an eyebrow. “Errrr… Mr. Ree’d be at the Paradox Floor by now, yarrr.”
The Paradox Guard almost leaped backwards, off the ledge that kept him and the Venture Leader up. “WHAAAAAT!?”
“Aye, he’d definitely be there be now, yarrrr.” Hael twiddles one of his dreadlock braids impatiently. “I’d like te be lookin’ through me telescope now, if ye don’ mind.”
Meyer nodded vigorously, leaping off the edge of the ledge and landing on his behind on the ground. “Ouch!” He shrieked, waking Trek Furino once again. The daredevil waved his Flareguns around wildly, before seeing his fellow guard.
“Ahhh, Mayer! Mind findin’ my lost compasses? I had to deliver them to the Capt’n, but… I lost em’.”
“Again!?” Meyer said hurriedly, running through a purple holographic chunk of Crux. “Sorry, but I’m a little tight on schedule! You know, gotta find Mr. Ree the Mystery!”
“Ahhh… awww, Hael’l keelhaul me if I don’ fend em’!”
“Sorry to hear that! But I’d better be gittin’ back to the Paradox floor before that crackpot vendor shifts again!”
“Okaaaay.” Trek whimpered back, almost crying as Meyer disappeared around the corner.

The Battle for Lego Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 26
A Cobwebby Vengeance

Nexus Tower
The Venture League Floor

Captain Hael Storm stared lovingly at the Imagimeter Shards. The four artifacts had recently been recovered from the debris of the Venture Koi’s wreckage in Crux Prime. They were, after all, his pride and joy. Greater than any priceless treasure, better even than entire temple made completely of gold, the Shards were… everything.
“Oi ye tentacle tongued, oyster licking, rancid goatfish!” A squawk came from behind him, startling the brave Venture League Leader so badly that he fell to his behind in fright. Then he realized what had done it.
“Ach! Shut it ye scurvy bottomed starfish climber, yarr!” The Space Pirate snapped at the parrot perched on the entrance to Lego Club Alpha.
“Ach! Shut it ye scurvy bottomed starfish climber, yarr!” It chirped back, flapping its white and black wings, causing feathers to float to the ground lightly.
Hael leapt to his feet, his dreadlocks slapping him in the face, and pointed his blunderbuss at the tropical bird in anger.
“Ach! Shut it ye scurvy bottomed starfish climber, yarr!” It shrieked, taking to the air and fluttering out of it master’s sight just before the hologram of Crux once again “exploded”.
“Them parrots from me homeland, crazy they are, crazy! Especially that one!”
He turned, his yellow face now boiling red, and placed his hands on the telescope, closing one eye and looking through the lens. He saw Gnarled Forest, with it’s beautiful trees, colorful and well-mannered parrots, swashbuckling buccaneers, Admirals constantly being smashed by Nexus Force recruits. “There be the good life.” He murmured to no one in particular. He pushed the observatory telescope a little to the left, starind distastefully at Forbidden Valley. “Yarrr, all them Ninjas! They’re everywhere, them rascals! Luckily Vanda’s got them under ‘er control, yarr.”
After scowling at Crux Prime and admiring the Ninjago Monastery, he pushed the scope down to stare at Avant Gardens. The Monument was good as new, Vector Longview was unconscious with a Hardtack Biscuit in her hand: Not abnormal. He turned the scope to look at the newly finished Assembly Bridge, glistening in the sun… but there was a small dent. And infected dent. “Weird.” He thought, “Assembly ain’t gonna like that.”
He then turned the telescope so that it would look at the plains in front of the Sentinel Encampment, but instead he saw was a massive explosion. “WHAT THE!?” He yelled out loud, taken aback by the explosion. Almost instantly, he heard the blast of the Teleportation Cannon and Billy Ganges, Nexus Tower Venture Vendor and professional Adventurer came blasting to his leader to see what had happened.
“Wha’s goin’ on up ‘ere!?” He yelled over the sound of Trek Furino blasting for Hael as well.
“It’s Avant Gard’ns… looks like ‘em Maelstrom’ses are attackin’!” Hael whispered, looking back through the telescope. This time all he could see was smoke and dust rising into the purple, swirling sky. When all the disrupting elements had vanished, he saw a massive battle. Full Rank Sentinel Knights charged bravely into battle, waving their Greatswords, led by a Valiant Dirk Manleigh, spinning his Powerjouster gracefully, plowing through Stromling and Sawling Invaders. High above, Airstrike jets and planes were spinning around, maneuvering away from deadly Spiderling blasts and getting time to fire Imagination tipped bullets rapidly from barrels upon the wings. Maelstrom Mechs took to shooting vunerable, beaten down Sentinels, occasionally punching them away if they would get too close. “This ain’t good…”
Billy looked through next and almost screamed. And that was saying something, because he had seen a lot of nasty things in his life, like that time a yak yakked of him when he had gone yak hunting, or when he rode a crocodile as big as a boat… ahhh, let’s not get into that.
“May I go?” Trek asked, moving into his most innocent body position, his eyes bulging.
“Not until ye get me them compasses, yarr!”
“Whaaaat!?”
“Well, ye can, if ye really wanna. But the ticket’ll be a quadruple keelhaul and yer back account… yer mommies one too. And yer daddies. And all yer siblings an’ uncles an’ aunts. An’ yer entire extended families, yarrr”
“Forget it.” Trek mumbled, hunching his shoulders.
“Forget it!” Hael’s parrot squawked as it landed on the Daredevils head.
“Don’t do your business on me.”
“Great idea!” The bird chirped, making a “pushing” sound.
“EWWWWWW! GROOOOOOSS!” Trek Furnino hollered, performing a back flip and forcing the parrot to fly off before it used his helmet as a lavatory.
Then Billy Ganges fainted. Hael looked at him, a worried expression on his face. “An’ ‘e was th’ only one who nows how t’ work with th medicines!”
The Space Pirate looked at the telescope, glancing through the lens. “Nooooo WAY!”
“What?” Trek asked, looking up.
“Ye gotta see fo’ yerself, yarr.”
“But I thought… sir yes sir!” The Daredevil said happily, running for the scope. But the moment he looked through, he fainted too.
A massive, infected leg had stepped out of what was left of the cave, crushing a Knight instantly, and sending the ones around his flying sky high. A strange, ever morphing smoke was floating around the leg that seemed to make the Stromlings shiver in fright. Then another leg stepped out, then another, then another. Soon, four legs were out of the shouldering cave, completely covered by the smoke. And on top of the legs was a gigantic, ugly head with six, massive, gleaming red eyes. On one side of the Spider’s body was looked like a double barreled gun as big as a truck, on the other side was a machine that resembled a cement container. And all along the two sides of its back were pipes, ten of them, five on the left, and five on the right. It looked almost exactly like the Spider Queen… but it was almost ten times larger. It was something Hael had only seen once before, when Typhonus was still one of his best friends… the first Stromling, pretty much the cause the Maelstrom was even there: The Spider Boss…

The Battle for Lego Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 26
Hostages

Wisp Lee woke in the deepest depths of the Spider Cave. He tried to move, but his body was stiff as a board, and was tied from top to bottom in webs that covered everything except his eyes and the top of his head, not to mention he was upside down, hanging from a stalactite filled ceiling. Nausea swept through his body, pulsing through his skull, which was already trying to survive a splitting headache. All around him, on the rocky walls, Dark Spiderlings crawled, all Invaders. Hah, there’s even more, is there? This is outrageous! He thought. Then he noticed a Spiderling crawling on the almost finished cocoon he was stuck in.
“Shrrrraaaaiiitthhhsss! Iiiinffffeeect, yooooouuu beeeeee!” It hissed, making more web around what was left of him.
Infect!? He thought, not being able to move his pail, frozen lips. No, no, no, no! Let’s deal with this like gentlemen!
But the Dark Spiderling just continued its dastardly deed. But then something chopped down the cobweb cord holding them to the ceiling, something that looked like… a chainsaw. The cocoon plummeted, along with Wisp and the arachnid. They hit the wet ground with a CLUNK that sent dirty water splashing everywhere. The Spiderling, who had just been tossed upside down, flipped over and charged, its eyes glowing malevolently. But before it could get close to the paralyzed Paradox Scientist, purple marking began to glow in front of it. In no time, a red and black version of a Mech was pulling itself from the jagged rocks that made up the floor. Its single, glowing yellow eye glared at the surprised Spiderling, casting a luminescent, lemony glow on the infected creature. The Paradox Mech then drew a Plasma Blaster from its metallic waist and began shooting at the Dark Spiderling, making it to scuttle away shrieking in its unintelligible Spiderlingual Tongue. The many arachnids on the dark surfaces of the surrounding walls looked down, dozens of evilly glowing red eyes opened wide. The Mech stepped back, its gun trained in front of it.
Then Wisp heard something cutting at his bonds. He tried to look up, but his curious eyeballs were frozen in place. He was as useful as a block of marble. Then he saw a head looking down at him. Well, not necessarily. It was a head covered by a black and grey space helmet. Its windshield had a glowing red aimer on the left, and it had a breathing mask that made whoever it was in it sound really weird. It was a Space Marauder, and a highly skilled one by the looks of it. Instead of a massive pair of shoulderpads, he had a long purple cape that shifted right slightly because of the stale, underground wind. And instead of a Maelstrom Charged Blaster and a rocket launcher with a pair of small homing missiles, he had a massive chain-saw like machine, its body filled with steam venting pipes and spinning cogs, the blade at the end pulsing with chaotic energy. A Wormholer, one of the most deadly weapons ever created by the Paradox Faction, so massive that it took two arms to bear.
“Looks like you got stuck in a bad state, be happy you got me to save your always-ready-to-be-smashed-bricks.” Said a voice that could not be anything but a woman’s.
What the!? Wisp thought, longing to talk.
“Ahh, I see! Been poisoned by Spiderling Venom, have you now! I’m sorry to say that there is no cure, it only wears off… after a few years.
“Whaaaat!?” Wisp yelled, jumping to his feet in terror.
“That wore off quick!” She laughed back in astonishment. “Well, at least I have someone else to save my bricks from all these disgusting spiders!” She gestured to the Mech, it nodded, and clanked to them, taking a patrolling stance. “This’ll be good!”
“W-w-who ARE you, exactly?” Wisp asked, flicking his infected arms up and down.
The Marauder took off her helmet, and her black hair fell down onto her face. “I might have been gone for a while, but you may remember that I am called-“
“Madison! Madison Growl!”
“Uhhh… yeah, now, do ya know how to get out of this sour smelling place? Reeks like my grandpa’s old boot when he been gone feeding the horses… when they’ve got constipation, that is.”
Wisp grimaced, scrunching up his nonexistent nose and making an ugly face.

The Battle for Lego Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 27
Skirmish at the Encampment

Beck Strongheart leaped back in horror at the massive arachnid stepped out from the smoldering cave. Instantaneously, two of the four remaining flying machines spin for the creature, dropping bombs that just glanced off its hide, instantly defusing the would-be-deadly explosives. “No… no way! That’s… unnatural!” After his momentary shock, he leaped to the top of the truck barricade, pushing the Guard who, at the moment, was unnerved by the charging Chainsaw Stromling. The creature instantly spun around, took one look at its master, and fainted.
“GET BACK IN’ERE!” He yelled for the Rank Three Knights who were battling valiantly, but losing. Dirk Manleigh looked up momentarily, knocking a Mech on the head with his Powerjouster absent mindedly. He then, finally, noticed the giant, four legged spider peering down at him with half a dozen blood read, malevolent eyes. He turned to the Commander, nodded, pulled a Quicksicle from his invisible backpack, devoured it, and then bolted back to the camp, followed by his now whimpering gang of Knights.
The dazed Guard rushed for the mechanism to raise the truck, twisting a steaming crank, allowing the warriors in. He then let go of the plastic handle, causing it to spin till the vehicle hit solid ground again, and making Beck teeter to one side frighteningly.
Fitz managed to finally wake the unconscious Guard, who looked around strangely. When he noticed the spider, however, he fainted once again. “He’s a disgrace to us hard working Sentinels!” The Engineer spat, standing up and pulling his spear from the ground, holding it in both hands shakily before he too passed out.
Outside, a rapidly coughing and incessantly vomiting Melodie Foxtrot yanked the bowl of Yuki Yaki from her head. After wiping her trembling and constantly barfing mouth from the rancid, green guck, the infected Sentinel drew here two, now purple, glistening daggers. They shone menacingly in the light of the Maelstrom above, and her reddish yellow eyes put a sharp, scarlet tint to them. She them “smiled”, before another torrent of eye watering, rotted liquid blasted from her unseen jaws. Coughing hoarsely, she raised one dagger.
“CHARG- blrasssch!” She almost ordered. But the dim witted Stromlings, after a few seconds hesitation, got the hint and charged.
But the Jets, led by the Crystal Corona, swooped downwards, firing rapidly, smashing a few more surprised Maelstrom creatures and catching Melodie in a deadly crossfire. The bullets rammed into her, sending her flying backwards, causing her to slam into the ground, which was now dark brown with lots of shimmering purple goo. But sadly, the Spider Boss noticed them, and, screeching in some unintelligible dialect that made the Spiderlings jump, she pointed her over-large, double barreled blaster at the remainders of the Airstrike. Infected projectiles rained on the jets, shattering the windshields and chopping the wings clean off. Soon, three of them had joined the wreckage below, the only one being piloted by a grim-faced Silas Penumbra. He spun out of the way of spinning pellets, rushing down, rocketing up, and zipping from side to side. But the Spider Boss, to his surprise, knew the element of surprise. This was all the beginning… the beginning of the end.
The ten thick pipes glowed a radiant violet. Silas thought back. The normal Spider Queen had six pipes, three on each side. These pipes fired bombs into the air, so that they could smash unknowing recruits from above. This was an over-large version of the Queen… possibly it grew longer? Nah, there was only one Spider Queen… that had been smashed more than a thousand times… which was unnatural. It was just like the named enemies of Crux Prime. Now, what could these pipes hold? Bombs? Nah…
Ten homing missiles blasted from the ten tubes, all aimed for the Crystal Corona…
Below, things weren’t going well either.
Theo Balfour had come into the encampment, and seeing that Fitz was sound asleep, his head lolling back and forth on the unconscious Guard’s shoulder, he took to assembling barricades and fences of barbed wire around the camp, being the second Sentinel in Avant Gardens to be good at building. Dirk’s highly trained guild of Knights spun their Greatswords, practicing, as he sniffed his cape and ruffled the flag atop the Powerjouster, sharpening the blade with a fellow’s sword once in a while. The Knights surrounded a weary Beck Strongheart, Dirk being right in front of the Minifigure circle. Beck was kneeling, studying his tactics scroll, his Greatsword buried in the ground next to him. The Sentinel Encampment was a green haven in the midst if a surging, twisted army of Maelstrom. Even the sky shone properly above them. Imagination was at their side.
Melodie, after yet another icky situation, pointed her dagger at the camp. “Why not…we charge!?” She shrieked. Becks stared. Her mind was completely under control of the Maelstrom, now that was sure. The oversized spider seemed to nod, the twisting aura around it seeming to morph into a thousand “thumbs ups”. The multiple Stromlings raised their blades, blasters, and… in the Spiderling’s condition, legs. And they charge, smashing into the wall with a crushing blow. The conscious Guard fired a few projectiles, and then leaped off the truck just before the Spider Boss blew it up with its multiple infected bullets. Maelstrom poured in, and was met fiercely by Dirk Manleigh and the Knights in the front of the circle. They slashed at Stromlings, and jabbed at firing Mechs. One did a flip, landing on a Chainsawling with a deadly uppercut, pivoting once he landed and smacking his shield into its face with a crushing blow that sent it flying over the now almost completely smashed and smoking wall. Dirk flung the Powerjouster into a Mechs eye, rushing for it, punching two Stromlings simultaneously with his gauntlets, sending them teacup over tea-kettle. He yanked the Valiant Weapon from the remains of the smashed Mech, spinning around in a wild circle and sending three other Stromlings and a Sawlings sprawling to the ground.
But a wave of Spiderlings got past their defenses, faced by Beck, Theo, and the other half of the Knights.




Nautilus, Starless Night of the Swords of Light
