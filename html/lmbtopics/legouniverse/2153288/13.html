The Battle for Lego Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 37
A Temporary Shelter

The first Spiderling web hit Rusty Steele in the face, causing him to shriek in pain as it began to sizzle, slowly smashing him. The infected arachnid hopped into the elevator, bringing a clawed leg down of the foreman’s stomach, making him lose his breath.
Rusty screamed in a ragged, tortured manner, writhing like a fish tossed to the banks of a river. The Dark Spiderling hissed in satisfaction, pointing a glinting, dagger-like javelin at him for the final blow…
*********************************************************************************************

“Wake up, for goodness sake! They’re coming!”
The feminine voice roused Rusty from his painful sleep. “Wha-?” He murmured in a dry voice, his dark circled eyes trying not to fall shut again.
“Look, I’ll tend to your wounds after we’re smashed, okay? Now get up or I’ll use you as a baton!” Sky Lane slapped in a couple in times, finally resorting to spitting a massive glob of saliva into his ashen face.
“ACH! What IS that!?” The foreman leaped to his feat in an instant, wiping the slobber from his face frantically.
“The result of falling asleep in a battle, now get your spanner, the Stromlings are catching up!”
Rusty Steele limped to a small boulder that had his wrench leaning on it. “Where are we?”
“You made this place, you should know!”
Suddenly, it hit him: On the way up to the monument, there was a large rock. If it was smashed, you would have the shelter of a small cave. “Oh! This pla-!”
Sky Lane’s trained Venture League hand slapped over his mouth causing him to fall onto his behind.
“OW! What was-!?”
He was slapped in mid sentence again. “Shut up! We have company!”
She rubbed one of her flareguns with a small, scorched handkerchief a couple of times, cleaning it. Then, all of a sudden, an unnerving sound echoed into the small cave.
“Where are they? 1 want them smashed! 0therwise y0u’ll be a class1c example of scrap metal!” Came PRDX-7’s familiar, rusted, mechanical voice.
“Shhhhh…” Sky whispered, pointing her flareguns at the rock separating them from the Stromling Mechs.
“What 1s that? Rem0ve that b0ulder!” The named Mech squeaked monotonously, followed by the shuffling clanks of fellow Mechs taking their large strides towards the boulder.
The massive rock moved slightly, causing small flakes of dirt to crumble from the ceiling. Sky Lane smiled under her skull-like helmet, her plastic fingers holding the triggers to her guns carefully. The rock moved a little more… rolling slowly across the rocky ground…
“BANG!” Two sizzling projectiles knocked the first Mech’s arms off, causing it to stumble, eventually tumbling down the steep fall down the Monument.
“1ntruders! They’re here! The M1n1f1gures are here!” Cried PRDX-7, grabbing his blaster from his back and aiming it for the two Nexus Force warriors.
Rusty Steele charged right into the crossfire between Sky and the Mechs, crushing the head on two of them with his massive spanner.
Suddenly, Sky put herself in fire, rushing towards her enemies. The flames licked at their metal bodies as she blasted their stud-like eyes into oblivion.
But then a metallic hand hit her on the back of her head, instantly knocking her cold.
“Sky!” Rusty roared, rushing towards the assailant. But PRDX-7 was far more skilled than the rickety, forgetful droid everyone knew. He sidestepped, tripping Rusty with a metal foot.
The foreman slammed head first into the rocky ground, his head millimeters from the massive fall to the bottom of the Monument. Rusty gulped in horror.
“N0w, you w1ll be smashed!” Rusty could feel the barrel of the blaster of his back, the head charging up inside it stating that it would fire a fizzling Maelstrom projectile in any second.

A Badly Planned Surprise

“Sir! Sir! We1rd M1n1f1gure com1ng!”
PRDX-7 stood up in a rusty fashion. “Take care of 1t! 1’m try1ng t0 smash a Nexus F0rce member, for h1nge sake!”
The Mech pointed its gun at the Assembly Foreman once again, its plastic finger moving towards the trigger slowly.
“OI! Mech! I’m going to attack you from behind without you noticing, okay!? Don’t tell yourself that I just said that!” A voice resonated up and down the giant Monument.
“What?” PRDX-7 droned monotonously. “1d10t M1n1f1gure!”
The Mech spun around in a roundhouse kick that sent Dusty Holster flying into the rocky wall.
“Ehy, I told’ya not to tell yourself! Filthy cheat! Rat’s nest! Macrophage! Xenophobe! Moldy apple!” The cowboy roared, pulling a bright red revolver from his bandolier. “Y’all robo’s need’a learn ‘s’m manners! ‘Ands up!”
PRDX-7’s eyes glowed a menacing red. “Sh00, underl1ng!”
“Oh handkerchiefs! Fiddlesticks, I say! Y’all hammers’n’bolts glug down too much oil, I say! Yer’d bett’a drink more rootbeer, I say!” Dusty Holster aimed his gun and fired… but nothing came out.
“Ha! 1mbec1le M1n1f1gure!”
The cowboy looked down at the gun is amazement.
“Wha-? Sabotage, I say! Y’all messin’ with mah stuff! T-Cells! Fresh water gorillas! Tarnished plugs! Plumbers!”
“D1sarm the madman, he needs to be taken to an asylum!”
Two Mechs grabbed the red faced cowboy by the arms and a third yanked the pistol from his grasp. But the struggling figure suddenly dig a massive jerk that pulled the two Mech’s arms straight off.
“Eh!? Term1nate h1m! Term1nate!!!”
The third Mech aimed the scarlet revolver at the cowboy… but the barrel was the wrong way around when it pullet the trigger.
A bright red plunder blasted out of the bright red gun, slamming into the Mech’s face and causing it to drop off the edge of the Monument.
Bewildered, PRDX-7 rushed towards Dusty, punching him in the stomach and grabbing him by the throat, throttling him.
“Ech…erp… p-p-phago-gocite! For- ach… foreigner! Errp!!! Ghlurrgh!!!!” He gasped, pulling against the Mech’s iron grasp.
“CLANG!!!”
The sound vibrated in the cowboys ears as PRDX-7 fell to the ground, crackling slightly with loose electricity, and anvil by his head.
“If you think Dusty’s taunting is overwhelming, wait till you meet Captain Jack Knife!” Rusty Steele laughed, a Thinking Cap upon his head.
“Yep, he’s a killer, that pirate.” Came Sky Lane’s voice.
“SKY! You’re alive!”
“For now.” She whispered, wide eyed, pointing downwards.
Rusty turned and fainted at almost exactly the same time:
The Spider Boss had decided that it’s forces needed help… and it was climbing the Monument…

The Battle for Lego Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 39
There’s always another way
“That’s not good…. at all…” Silas Penumbra whispered as the massive, infected Spider sped up the legs of the massive monument.
“Agreed.” Madison Growl tossed the handkerchief that she had been polishing her Wormholer with to the ground, standing up. “If that infect gets up here, there won’t be anything to stop it from easily taking over Nimbus Station… which would mean the end of every single planet that we own in this Universe. Every single one.”
Silas bit his lip slightly, beads of sweat dripping from his hidden brow. “But how will we stop it?”
“Easy.” It was Fitz Vanderbuilt.
“Ah, you’re up! And what makes this predicament easy!? Especially with your drowsy Sentinel attitude?” Madison snarled, heaving the Wormholer from the ground and balancing it on her shoulder.
The Engineer scowled, but quickly became happy again as he explained his plan. “You see that sword the statue of Bob’s holding? Well, we just have to obliterate “Bob’s” hand, and it will fall… stabbing the spider and smashing it once and for all! If that doesn’t work, we’ll bring down the entire Monument on it!”
The two Paradox members stared at him as if it was the happiest day of their lives.
“Well?” Fitz snorted, staring back at them quizzically… that was, before he was knocked to the ground with the force of Madison’s hug. “Wha-!?”
In seconds, the Space Marauder was back on her feet. “Perfect! I’ll blow the place up right now!” She spun around, but when she got her first look at the monument, her shoulders slumped.
“What is it?” Silas asked, putting an arm around her downcast body. “What’s wrong?”
“What’s wrong? Well, the only way up the monument from here is by jumping down this cliff and walking up with the path. And that way… we will never outrun the Spider Boss…”
“I can fix that!” Came a gruff voice from behind her.
Madison spun around, her finger on the Wormholder trigger. It was Trek Furino.
“Ah, Venture League, think they have the answer to everything that has something to do with swinging across a deep chasm and flying to the other side, eh?”
Trek smiled, holding up two flareguns. “Exactly.”
“Okaaaaay…” Madison growled back. “Two flareguns, note the word: flareguns. That means they don’t have really damaging bullets and projectiles. How will those work? Do they fire so rapidly that they let you fly? I doubt it, because even my Wormholer can’t do that. So? Then what? To they have hidden telepor-?” A loud click and a resonating ZING shut her up instantly.
“And voila! There’s our ride!” Trek Furino made a sort of “magnificent” gesture at the flareguns that had been dug deeply into the ground. But something was sticking out of the ground… a metal cord…
“Are those…?”
“Yes, they’re grappling hooks! Now hop aboard the “Know-it-all, Awesome, Super, Epic Sauce, Brave, Knight in Shining Armor, Proud Venture Leaguer Trek Furino Express!”
“Ohh, don’t push it! Proud nincompoop…”
“OI! Just because I’m better than you, you start defending your dignity! Your honor! HA-HA! I hit the jack-pot, I did!”
“Perhaps. But I assure you, will never defend my attitude. My “agitation”.”
“What do you me-?”
“LADIES FIRST YOU OBNOXIOUS, WISECRACKING, BROCCOLLI BRAINED, DOUBLE BRICK CHINNED IDIOT!” The Space Marauder roared before whacking the Daredevil in the rump, sending him racing down the rope in a rather badly balanced fashion.
“What do you mean, ladies first!? I’m a man and I’m prou-” He yelled just before he slammed face first into a wall on the other side of the trench.
“Alright! To the other side!” Madison leaped onto the rope, holding on with one hand. She then put her Wormholer on top of the cord, holding it with her hands, and kicked off, sliding down to the arm of the monument. She was closely followed by Silas Penumbra who had decided it would be safer to summon a Spiderling and let it do all the hard work.
“C’mon! Everyone else afraid?”
The active Guard leaped onto the rope, performing the same technique as Madison but with his rifle.
“My sleepy friend and Fitz are in a deep slumber already!” He cried back at the Sorcerer. “And Wisp Lee decided to buy a couple of Crocodile Eggs from Shouty McBullhorn, and is staying behind!”
“Good!” Then, to himself, Silas whispered. “But where could Sky Lane be? I sure to hope nothing has happened to her and Rusty… and what about that fool Dusty?” He shook terrible visions from his head as he neared the other side.
“DUCK! FOR BRICK’S SAKE, DUCK!!!” Came Madison’s shrill voice.
Silas looked up at her and saw her jumping up and down, waving her arms madly. “How am I supposed to duck? I’m on a stinking spider on a stinking rope!”
“THAT’S NOT THE ONLY SPIDER HERE! JUST GET UNDER YOUR SPIDERLING!!!!”
Puzzled, the Sorcerer obeyed, hanging onto the cord right under the Paradox Spiderling. And just in time too: Because the moment he did it, the arachnid was blasted into pieces. “What the!?”
But then he looked up, to the head of the bald statue… and saw a terrible sight: The head had been burnt so madly that a third was gone, and on the place where hair was never built… was the Spider Boss, smoke puffing in rings from the flamethrower and its rapid-firing gun’s bullet holder spinning madly.
“Tsk, tsk, tsk.” Came a voice that seemed to come from every single direction. “The Nexus Force has fallen already… I’ll miss a Universe without any freedom… when we are free and the Nexus Force are our slaves, that is!”

“What?” PRDX-7 droned monotonously. “1d10t M1n1f1gure!”
The Mech spun around in a roundhouse kick that sent Dusty Holster flying into the rocky wall.
“Ehy, I told’ya not to tell yourself! Filthy cheat! Rat’s nest! Macrophage! Xenophobe! Moldy apple!” The cowboy roared, pulling a bright red revolver from his bandolier. “Y’all robo’s need’a learn ‘s’m manners! ‘Ands up!”
PRDX-7’s eyes glowed a menacing red. “Sh00, underl1ng!”

“Ha! 1mbec1le M1n1f1gure!”
The cowboy looked down at the gun is amazement.
“Wha-? Sabotage, I say! Y’all messin’ with mah stuff! T-Cells! Fresh water gorillas! Tarnished plugs! Plumbers!”
“D1sarm the madman, he needs to be taken to an asylum!”

“Eh!? Term1nate h1m! Term1nate!!!”
The third Mech aimed the scarlet revolver at the cowboy… but the barrel was the wrong way around when it pullet the trigger.
A bright red plunder blasted out of the bright red gun, slamming into the Mech’s face and causing it to drop off the edge of the Monument.
Bewildered, PRDX-7 rushed towards Dusty, punching him in the stomach and grabbing him by the throat, throttling him.
“Ech…erp… p-p-phago-gocite! For- ach… foreigner! Errp!!! Ghlurrgh!!!!” He gasped, pulling against the Mech’s iron grasp.
“CLANG!!!”
The sound vibrated in the cowboys ears as PRDX-7 fell to the ground, crackling slightly with loose electricity, and anvil by his head.
“If you think Dusty’s taunting is overwhelming, wait till you meet Captain Jack Knife!” Rusty Steele laughed, a Thinking Cap upon his head.
“Yep, he’s a killer, that pirate.” Came Sky Lane’s voice.
“SKY! You’re alive!”
“For now.” She whispered, wide eyed, pointing downwards.
Rusty turned and fainted at almost exactly the same time:
The Spider Boss had decided that it’s forces needed help… and it was climbing the Monument…
