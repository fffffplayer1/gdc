The Battle for LEGO Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 67
A Fateful Blow

“Xeno! Quick! Tackle him!” Cog Blammo fired a pair of Maelstrom Charged Missiles at Murgle Blotch, but two lines of pure electricity destroyed them before they flew halfway towards him. The impact caused Cog to fall backwards, and his Maelstrom Blaster skidded away and into the gloom.
“On it!” The Samurai yelled back, leaping to the air and kicking the Stromling General in the face.
“Grrraarggh!” Murgle Blotch roared, stumbled over his own feet and crashing down to the wet and muddy grass below.
Xeno Blueblade then unsheathed his glowing blue and gold katana and swung it at the Hammerling. The blade collided with Murgle’s helmet, and then spiraled off into the night, leaving hardly a scratch.
Murgle Blotch grumbled to himself lightly, and then punched Xeno in the face with his “free” hand. The Samurai stumbled back a few feet, wiped his kabuto in a rather confused fashion, then preformed an uppercut to the Stromling’s round, plastic chin.
Murgle howled in pain, then fell right back into the wet soil below. “Graargh. I’ll get you, Sentinel!”
“I don’t think so!” Xeno spat back, smiling over his victory. He was tripped and whacked over the head with a hammer a few seconds later.
“I love Minifigure humor.” The Hammerling snarled, nudging Xeno’s body with his foot in a rather unceremonious manner.
“Alright, you win…Stromling.”
Murgle Blotch raised his hammer to the Maelstrom above him, causing lightning to flash all over the dark and cloudy sky. “You‘ve lost your touch, Samurai.”
“Yes, it is rather shocking… especially since he has been training so hard lately.” Someone snickered, and before Murgle could register all the words in his head, he lost a few brain cells as a rock hit him rather hard.
Cog Blammo laughed, but his victory was also rather short lived. A Dark Ronin leaped towards him, its katana raised high above his head, its eyes blazing a bright, malevolent yellow. The creature sliced at Cog with its blade, knocking his helmet from his head and bruising his left eye. His skin was a slightly darker, more olive-tinted color than the normal yellow of a Minifigure. He had wild blue eyes and dark brown hair that was rather singed in some areas.
It then spun around in the strange way you spin around when your legs are made of smoke, and stabbed him in the stomach.
“Gachh!” The Space Marauder groaned, slumping to his knees in pain. He opened and closed him mouth several times, his tongue flapping from cheek to cheek before he fell to his hands, looking down in the swirling, muddy ground, the horrid reflection of the Maelstrom laughing at him from above.
Then the reflection was replaced by a Ronin, looking down (Or, in Cog’s place, looking up) at him nastily, its kabuto flashing in the light of the Maelstrom. It raised its infected katana, the menacing blade shimmering in the flashes caused by the lightning, and swung it towards Cog’s head.
But Cog wasn’t going down without a final fight. He rolled out of the way, hardly hearing the katana sloshing into the ground where neck was a few seconds before, picked up a Maelstrom Ball from the wet ground, and rushed towards the Dark Ronin.
The Stromling snarled, pulling at its grounded katana in vain. It soon gave up and raised its hands for the coming fight.
Cog Blammo leaped towards it, punching it in its shadowy face and then swinging the Ball towards it. The Ronin caught both his hands, and they had a slight arm wrestle before everything went crazy.
The Space Marauder turned his head to face Xeno Blueblade and winked. “It was great working with ya!” He pressed a button on the Maelstrom Ball. “It was amazing working with you…”
“You t- wait, NO!” Xeno leaped to his feet, but was struck by a torrent of lighting as Murgle Blotch got to his feet. The Samurai groaned as he stepped up from the slowly eroding plaza, but was too late.
The Maelstrom Ball blew up, destroying the Ronin… and taking Cog away with it.
“NO!” Xeno wailed, falling to his shuddering knees in sorrow. A burnt Space Marauder helmet floated towards him, its visor shattered and the breather half melted.
The Samurai stood to his feet, his tears quickly being washed away by the rain. “Murgle.” Xeno Blueblade turned, tossing his katana to the ground and unsheathing a Samuraizer. “You’re not going to walk away from this battle alive. Even if I have to destroy Nimbus Station to smash you.”

The Battle for LEGO Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 68
A Spidery Disaster

No one would listen.
“Please! This is serious! Everyone! It’s a matter of life and death!” Meldric Steamvalve cried at the top of his lungs, pulling at Minifigures shirts for attention.
But too much was happening, and no one, absolutely no one, would listen.
“Hello!? So all of you are just gonna cheer at a bunch of racers while hundreds of thousands of Stromlings that are supposed to be dead decapitate you? Sweet, simply sweet.” Magnus Bumperdent sighed, knocking a random Minifigure over the head with his Rutcarver in agitation, causing the man to fall to the ground, unconscious with a smile on his fat face. The man was wearing a chef’s hat, which rolled of his head and down one of the recently made streams created by the rain. It was Farnham Spoon.
“If only we could do something do get their attention…” Meldric whispered into Magnus’ ear.
“Yeah… should I drive through them with my Valiant?”
“Nah, they’ll just get back up, charge you a fine, and keep watching.”
“You could summon something big… like Scratch!”
The Summoner shook his head, causing water to pray from his hood. “That won’t work, look over there.” Meldric pointed to the right, and there, almost twenty Third Rank Summoners were chilling out on the heads of their Scratches, taunting a far larger crowd of envious Second and First Ranks, who were trying to ride on their Bulpers and Thwoks… and failing.
“Ah.”
“Sooo… what do you suggest?”
“I don’t know about you, but driving through that crowd would really be a thrill…”
“And my Summoning skills have gotten a little rusty just lying around in a booth…”
“You are so hypocritical.”
“And you are so predictable”
“Daredevils take pride in that. It’s what makes us unique.”
“Oh? And why’s that?”
“Everyone always looks for the unpredictable parts… and never see the predictable things coming.”
“You’re talking like a demigod.”
“I am a demigod.” Magnus flexed his muscles and growled.
“Well, at least I know you have fun in your sleep!” Meldric laughed, slightly shifting his position to lean on his Wingreaper.
“Oh? And what about you? Ya have cheesy dreams about your next pet b-?”
He was cut off by a blast that knocked almost everyone in the Race Place to the ground. When the two Nexus Force Minifigures got back up, there was a massive hole in the ground. Fragments of obliterated cars were everywhere, and a chassis had imbedded itself right next to the Daredevils head.
“My next pet what, exactly?” Meldric murmured, yanking his Wingreaper from the ground and stepping back a few steps.
“I think that can wait till next time.” Magnus whispered back as violet smoke billowed from the hole. Muddy water sloshed into the pit, and six, blood red eyes appeared in the darkness. Everyone who wasn’t smashed or unconscious screamed and ran in every direction.
“They never listed to you when you warn them, but when it happens; they go ballistic and yell at you because you never told them. Minifigures these days.” Meldric sighed, waving his staff in an arc. Imagination shimmered around him, and then, all of a sudden, three owl materialized above his head, hooting and flapping madly.
“Well, just like old times, eh?” Magnus lifted his Rutcarver from the wet ground and started the engines, his voice almost completely drained by the rain and the screams.
“Just like old times.”
“Yessss… jussssst like old timessssss.” Whoever owned those eyes hissed back.
“Yikes, that was unexpected…” Magnus shook his head to make sure he had heard right… and…
The Dark Spiderling leaped from the smoke, smashing three frantic Minifigures in midair and landing in the Daredevil, knocking him to the ground.
“Magnus! Watch out!” All three of the owls swooped forward, clawing at the monster with their glinting talons. In moments, a lizard-like creature with scarlet horns, a mouth as bug as its body, and skin a mixture of black and red burst from the ground in a flash on Imagination. It was a Bulper, and it quickly began spitting acidic globs of saliva at the arachnid.
Magnus Bumperdent whacked the Stromling Spider in the belly with his Rutcarver, causing it to roll around in the mud before hopping back to its four, infected legs. The Spiderling shrieked in anger, and, in moments, a sizzling orange web had shot towards the Daredevil and disarmed him. Luckily, it was hit in the face by a Wingreaper a few seconds later, and then quickly knocked back by a gust of wind before it could recover.
Magnus picked up his Rutcarver and examined if for any damage. However, before he could make a full survey of the weapon, he was nudged by Meldric.
“Look closely at that Spiderling…”
The Daredevil looked up, and then stepped back in horror.
It was Whack Bliddo.

The Battle for LEGO Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 69
Duel of Thunder

Murgle Blotch blinked. No one had ever spoken to him like that, and he didn’t exactly like it. He raised his hammer of a hand in self-defense before he thought it would be a better idea to run.
The Stromling general spun around, sending mud spewing in every direction, and bolted.
But Xeno Blueblade wasn’t going to let him go that easily. He slammed his Samuraizer into the sloshing ground and waved his hands. Imagination swirled around him, and, in moments, he was holding a majestic bow colored dark blue and emblazoned with dragons. In another sapphire flash, an arrow appeared in his hand- Xeno drew the string and fired.
The arrow whizzed towards his opponent, slamming into Murgle’s armored back in an explosion of fire that sent the Stromling three meters forward, with his head in the mud.
Xeno Blueblade tanked his Valiant Weapon from the swirling ground- sending more dirty, grass-filled water into the air- and charged. He leaped into the air, the blade on his Samuraizer glinting in the light of the Maelstrom, and brought it down hard on his foe.
The weapon missed by millimeters as Murgle pulled his head from the wet remains of the plaza and rolled out of the way. The Hammerling then kicked Xeno with a skeletal leg dripping with corruption, tripping him once again into the murk. Xeno’s kabuto skidded off in the rain and sunk into the churning ground.
Murgle Blotch then stood up, picking up the grounded Samuraizer with his free hand in triumph. Electricity zigzagged through it, setting a few strings that were tied to it on fire.
“Now, Samurai, you will die like your kind loves to die: By your own weapon… except it isn’t you who is killing you… ha-ha!”
“Not this time, Blotch.” That wasn’t Xeno.
It was Velocity Lane.
Murgle Blotch froze, and then gathered up a bit of bravery. “Oh? And… and why’s that?”
There was a click as she loaded her revolver. “Mainly because I’m going to smash you.”
The Hammerling laughed. “If you kill me, my master will release things far more worse.”
“Oh really now? And who’s this ‘master’?” Velocity smiled, her “finger” on the trigger.
“Grand Infectionist Wenn Wuzzit, of course!”
“Oh goodness me, you have positions in your nasty civilization? And Wenn… why did he have to turn into a Stromling…?”
“Well, aren’t you going to kill me?” Murgle asked contemptuously.
“Gladly.” Velocity pulled the trigger, and the Imagination projectile within shoot from the barrel, whizzing towards Murgle’s turned head.
Lightning shot from the sky and destroyed the bullet in midair.
Murgle Blotch stood up and smiled (If he could) under his Lockjaw Helm. “Point blank range? I little… strange for a member of the Nexus Force. If you worthless Minifigures are anything, you’re not cowards. Point blank is the sign of someone who still believes in monsters under the bed…”
“How did you-?!”
Murgle kicked Velocity in the chest, knocking her to the muddy ground as he yanked the Samuraizer from it. “It was nice meeting you. Any last words?”
“Tipical. ‘Any last words?’… ha!” Velocity Lane snarled.
But the Hammerling wasn’t listening. A slight glint had caught his eyes…
Murgle bent down and picked up Velocity’s Brickberry. “Ah, look what we have here! Well, now you won’t be able to resist a few last words…”
The Stromling skimmed through her contacts until he found what he wanted. He clicked “Call”, and then threw the phone into the mud next to Velocity’s head. He put his foot on her chest to prevent her from moving, and then laughed evilly, causing lighting to streak all around him. “I’m sure Sky will love to hear your voice… rasping and croaking for your very last breaths…”
Murgle Blotch stepped away from the racer, raised the Samuraizer above his head, and stabbed Velocity Lane in the stomach.
Velocity opened her mouth to scream, but nothing came out. She gasped for breath and turned, noticing the kneeling figure of Xeno for the first time. His neon blue hair covered his bowed head, dripping with water from the raging skies. “He’ll be the last real person I ever see…” She thought. It was a terrible thing to think.
Then, to her surprise, the phone stopped ringing and someone picked up. “Vel? Vel? Velocity, oh, you’re okay! Tell me, has anything happened?”
“Y-y-es…. Wh-ere a-a-are you?”
“Vel? Are you okay? I’m almost to Nimbus Station! In hyperspace right now! You’re only three parsecs away! Listen, Avant Gardens has been destroyed. I’m one of the only survivors-.”
“Avant Gardens too, eh? I thought so…”
“And then- wait, what? Avant Gardens… TOO? What do you mean, TOO?”
Murgle Blotch picked up the Brickberry before Velocity could speak again. “What she’s saying is that we’ve almost completely obliterated this place. We have taken Nimbus Plaza and the Avant Gardens Launchpad, and are going for the rest. We’ve almost taken the Race Place.”
“What!? Who are you!!??”
“Murgle Blotch.”
“Wait… WHAT!? You… you’re DEAD!”
“Not any more, thanks to Grand Infectionist Wenn Wuzzit!”
“Grand- WENN WUZZIT!?”
“Yep.”
“GIVE THE PHONE BACK TO MY SISTER, STROMLING! What have you done with her??”
“Oh, she’s on the ground… dying.”
“No… no, she can’t be… NO!”
“You want her to verify it for you? I doubt she’s in the condition to speak. She needs to try to breathe more than chat, you know. A Samuraizer through the belly can be fatal…”
“No… this can’t be happening…”
“Well, it is.”
“Hand me over to her!”
“As I said, she’s in no condition! Good day to you, hope to see you soon… smashed.”
And with that, Murgle dropped the phone to the ground and crushed it with his hammer. “Good day to you too.” He turned to Xeno, “And you.”
Xeno looked up. “You… filthy… MURDERER!” He leaped to his feet and, in a flash of Imagination, was swinging his katana left and right.
“You still want to fight me, then? Alright… bring it on.”
