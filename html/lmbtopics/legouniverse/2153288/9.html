The Battle for Lego Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 28
Surprise!

“FIRE!!!” Beck pointed his Greatsword at a ready-to-pounce Dark Spiderling. The Guard, balancing himself on the remains of the fallen truck, blasted a flaming blue projectile from his gun barrel, the kickback causing him to fall flat on his face. The bullet seemed to, as if in slow motion, whiz past Stromlings and Knights, and slammed into the Arachnids abdomen. It shrieked, spinning around just to be whacked with a Knights crushing shield blow. The Spiderling looked puzzled for a second, and then toppled over, landing on its back. The other half of the Knights charged forwards, spinning their blades, their shields in front of their faces, covering the weak spot of their helms. A Mech trained its blaster on one of the Sentinel warriors, the spinning purple orb just managing to glance off the quick Minifig’s sword. But there were too many, and soon, the Knights were starting to lose. One slashed at a Chainsawling, cutting its blade in half. The surprised Stromling was then kicked into a nearby, smoldering make-shift barricade. But six more Stromlings caught his attention, racing for him, taking leaps, and pinning him to the grassy ground. The Sawling, rubbing its head, walked from the wall and stepped onto the Knight. It reached down to the grounded blade, put it back onto its place, and clicked a button. The saw began rotating, the jagged, dagger-like spikes oscillating, emitting a nasty aura. The creature looked at the terrified Knight, and “smiled.”
But that was only one, and in no time, only a few were left.
********************************************************************************************
Silas Penumbra stared into the rear mirror and grimaced. In his speed, several flies had been smashed on his window, but he just kept accelerating. But the missiles had a nasty habit on gaining, and soon, he knew, he would have to give in to the arachnid’s wrath. But for now, he was still un-smashed and kicking, and all he had to do was warn the obviously oblivious Minifigures at the picnic area… hopefully before the newbies got to the top of the complicated and winding paths and bouncers of The Assembly Monument.
He spun around, and then noticed the Assembly Bridge. “Ahh, Assembly’s not gonna like this… ah well!” He murmured grimly to himself.
He sped for the bridge, looming beautifully over the surging sky. The picnic area was still an amazing, blue skied place. The Darkitect really knew the definition of surprise, and even worse, he knew how to use it. But of course, that’s what Paradox was for.
The missiles, with their menacing blinking lights, were almost touching him now, and he was losing power. But just a few millimeters from the side of the structure, he pulled up, the Crystal Corona narrowly missing the edge and the explosive monstrosities. And luckily, the missiles didn’t have a mind like his, and almost all of them slammed into the Bridge, blowing it to bits, damaging it more than a small Spiderling skirmish 32 years ago that had originally smashed it.
Silas smiled, his eyes glinting in the light of the Maelstrom. He spun around, almost laughing as the picnickers jumped up in surprise. “Well, there’s our reinforcements!”
*********************************************************************************************
The explosion shook the world.
Sky Lane, former pilot of the famed Venture Explorer, fearless warrior, faithful servant of notorious space pirate Captain Hael Storm, almost leaped from her pantaloons in fright. All the surrounding picnickers also looked up from their meals in terror, their head darting to and fro.
Crash Helmut ran for Sky, skidding to a halt almost a millimeter from collision point and spitting in her face. “WA’S GOIN’ ON!?”
The Venture pilot stepped back, wiping the gooey substance from her face. “Gross… eh? Oh, the mighty BOOM? Ahhh… I dunno.” He chattered, trying to hide her fright.
“Whaaaa-!” Crash began before making a circle on one foot and falling to the ground, obviously hyperventilating.
Sky looked at the rest of the picnickers. Sofia Amalgam had ducked under her booth, Darby Snapwish frantically pushing to go in with her partner… rather in vain… or so the constant stream of thrown Handsome Mugs said. Brutus Speck was trying to fit his muscular body into the nosecone of a Classic, Pink Rocket. Hu Where had, strangely, managed to leap into a full trash can and close the lid over him, PRDX-4 had shut himself down, Eekums was happily munching away at a banana chugging down a jug of… banana smoothie (With a banana on top), and a puzzled Dusty Holster, not noticing what had just happened, had been “accidentally” locked in the ladies lavatory.
Sky Lane blinked. “Now… where should I hide?”
*********************************************************************************************
The Spider Boss fixed all six of its eyes on the explosion at the Assembly Bridge. It squinted, the glowing red lenses scanning the area for signs of movement in or around the billowing remnants of a dozen mushroom clouds. Seeing nothing alarming, it turned its attention to the worthless, flimsy defenses of the “insentient” Nexus Force. It began charging it infection ray, then locked it guns on multiple targets. Time for it to join the battle for Avant Gardens…

The Battle for Lego Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 29
Surrender or Die

Water dripped from the stalactites above, the droplets whistling downwards, through the dark caverns, to fall to the ground with a wet PLOP. But one drop turned into steam in midair, being hit by a purple ball on pure Maelstrom energy.
Madison Growl put her Space Marauder helmet back on, then swung the deadly chainsaw at the end of the Wormholer at a prying Dark Spiderling. The arachnid screeched, leaping backwards to join its stalking friends. The Paradox Agent then pulled a glowing trigger, and the blade, pulsing with chaotic power, fired a constants stream of corrupted projectiles. In seconds, she had cleared a path in the spiders. “WISP! WHAT ARE YA WAINTIN’ FOR!?” She shouted, rushing forward and grabbing him by the collar, dragging the scientist along behind her.
The red Mech, creaking with every step, punched one of the creatures in “a” eye. The Spiderling, rather surprised, fell to the ground, stunned. But another one fired a ball of energy that knocked the blaster from the robot’s clip-like hands. It then fired a glowing violet web at the Mech, and almost instantly shutting the struggling droid down. Three more arachnids leaped at it, tearing it apart until it was just a pile of smoldering, infected bricks. But the two Paradox Members were already out of sight.
*********************************************************************************************
Beck Strongheart stared at the explosion at the Assembly Bridge, his eyes bulging from their plastic sockets. The Maelstrom had been waiting for this moment, when it was at the peak of its power. All of battles that the Nexus Force had almost lost… they were all just raids. The fall of the Venture Explorer was an accident, the Skeletons managing to get into the caves under the Ninjago Monastery was just a small skirmish… constant battles with Butterscorch and power of Minifigure backs in the Battle for Nimbus Station were just tricks. This… this was the real deal. Completely hard core. Mad.
But he had a battle to fight, no matter in he was smashed.
Dirk Manleigh, along with what was left of his Knights, fought valiantly, slashing at Stromlings of every shape. Beck was standing on a barricade, cutting at any that got close, and dealing crushing blows with his shield. On the other side of the camp, Rocco Sirocco munching on a few Rockolate Bars. None of the Stromlings took notice on him, but what made Beck angry was the fact that the ninja wasn’t helping at all. “ROCCO! COME HERE AND FIGHT!” He roared, just before he was punched off the makeshift barricade by a Mech. The droid then aimed its blaster at him…
Theo Balfour’s spear stabbed the robot, smashing it. But, almost instantly, he was attacked by a charging Stromling. The creature slashed with its infected blade, the Sentinel only just parrying the deadly blow. The corrupted scientist, dazzled by the block, stepped back, then, growling menacingly, it leaped up and jabbed, its sword-like arm piercing the glass windshield on the grunts helmet. The Sentinel fell, and the Stromling hopped onto him, making sure he didn’t get up. But Beck hated debts, and decided to get rid of the one he owed Theo. The Commander charged, his shield in front of him, smashing into the Stromling with a blow that sent it spinning to the smoking grass below. He then slashed at it, but it blocked the blow with its own blade, then kicked the general.
“OI!” Beck hollered, dropping his greatsword and hopping up and down, clutching his foot. The Stromling slowly stood up, then limped towards the vulnerable Sentinel. But Beck did a flip, landed on the creatures back, his weight forcing it down. He then raced for his sword, and before the Stromling could react, it was reduced to a pile of bricks.
“Oh, there’s a war going on! How exciting!” Beck heard Rocco laugh, pieces of the hard chocolate streaming from his mouth. Beck sighed.
Then, all of a sudden, he heard the distinct cracking sound of a Gatling gun being fired. Clouds of purple smoke shot up from the ground as purple bullets hit the Sentinel Encampment. Beck looked up, and, in horror, he saw the Spider Boss shooting with its massive, double barreled machine gun. Knights, even with their amazing blocking skills, went down like ants in a forest fire. Then, the Maelstrom poured from the camp, leaving the place in ruins, but with survivors. Then, a dark, disembodied voice that seemed to come from everywhere reverberated through everyone’s eyes, a voice that even made the Spider Boss slightly cringe.
“Surrender now, let your men live! Let them survive! Do you really want all this pain? This suffering? This seemingly never-ending smashing!? Surrender, join us, you’ll never have to think, ever again. It with be like peace everywhere, forever, all around everything! Wouldn’t you like that? To relax every seconds of your worthless life, instead of fighting for an even more worthless life? Join me!!!” All the now leafless trees seemed to bend, a subtle breeze blew, and every single minion of the maelstrom curled into a ball.
The Darkitect.
Beck turned to his men, most of who were tending for the wounded. Some were crying, Rocco was eyeing a Dirt Sandwich, flicking off the occasional earthworm. He looked to Dirk, who was kneeling over one of his men’s bricks. The Valiant Sentinel nodded at his commander, and Beck turned to the sky.
“We will never join you.” He bluntly whispered, his voice filled with hatred.
It began to rain. “So be it.” The monstrous voice of the Darekitect almost laughed back.

The Battle for Lego Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 30
One on One

The concord-like Crystal Corona spun from the dust clouds of the explosion at the Assembly Bridge, rocketing for the picnic area. It was now once again covered by a shadowy, Maelstrom fog, just like the rest of Avant Gardens. All the picnickers seemed to be hiding.
The Paradox jet hovered by the Nimbus Station launch-pad for a second, causing leaves to swirl from trees bent by the whizzing air venting from the ship. It then lowered slowly, landing lightly on the ground. Silas opened the cockpit and hopped out, inspecting the area. Thick drops of rain plummeted from the violet clouds, splattering onto the now almost barren and grassless ground. He then noticed Sky Lane running towards him.
“Silas! Silas! What in the name of Crux is going on down there!?” She skidded to a halt by the Corona.
“It seems like the Darkitect has been waiting for this moment to strike. He has hit us with full force, and we are losing terribly. It seems like he has awakened the First Stromling, and it is wrecking havoc, transforming every Minion of the Maelstrom in its path into an Invader.” Silas whispered grimly, the æeaves that had blown off trees fluttering to the ground around him.
“Th-th-the Spider… Spider Boss?” The Venture pilot whispered in a tone of utter terror. It was obvious that she didn’t want to believe.
“Yes.”
“Well, we must help!” She drew two massive, triple barreled flareguns. They looked as if they could deal deadly damage to enemy ranks. Under her arm, she cradled a skull-like helmet that shone in the shadowy, purple light.
“No.”
“What!?”
He put a gloved hand on Sky’s shoulder and smiled sadly. “If something goes terribly wrong, you have to hold the fort. They can’t get to Nimbus Station; it would serve as the downfall to all we hold dear.”
“I am getting the feeling that something very bad has happened.”
“Happening would be more precise.” Silas stepped away, and in a moment, Imagination was swirling around him. Soon, he wore dark red and black robes with Paradox insignias on the chest and arm. A purple cape with a painting of a tiger’s eye in a tornado materialized around his neck, and what looked like a witches hat flew to his head, causing his representative helmet to vanish. Then what looked like a part scythe, part dragon was build from thin air, and it fell to his hands. He was a fully fledged Sorcerer with a Doomslicer.
Silas nodded, and then ran up the road. Sky watched him as he leaped, spinning, from the remains of the Assembly Bridge.
*********************************************************************************************
Some sort of magical force pushed everyone around Beck Strongheart away, sending them spinning like tops. Then, all of a sudden, purple smoke vented from the ground, surrounding the Sentinel and separating him from help.
“Let’s do this… the old fashioned way.” The reverberating, evil voice laughed. Suddenly, a dark leg stuck out from the fog, followed by the body of a woman. Melodie Foxtrot. “By ‘old fashioned’, I would mean, one… on… one…”
“No…” Beck stared at his “colleague” in horror. “No, no, never… I can’t!”
“You have to.” The Darkitect said monotonously, causing even more rain to fall.
Melodie “smiled”, twiddling her daggers evilly in her plastic palms. Behind her, the Spider Boos began charging the infection ray. It was not smash or be smashed. It was smash… or join the Maelstrom. There was only one thing to do…

The Battle for Lego Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 31
The Duel of a Lifetime

A tear rolled down Beck’s dirty cheek, making a wet track along his face. He pointed his Greatsword forward and charged, his shield held tightly in front of him. The first infected dagger slammed into the Knight’s defense, the impact knocking him down and sending the knife spiraling away. In seconds, however, Melodie had caught it once again and charged, both weapons ready for a deadly toss or slash.
Beck leaped up and slammed into the infected Sentinel with a massive uppercut, dazing her for a few seconds, But the effect was soon over, and he spun around, cutting at his with the daggers. Beck ducked, stabbing upwards at her. But with her bullet like reflexes, she did a flick, narrowly missing the Greatsword and landing hard on Beck’s chest, smashing his breastplate and taking his breath away. The Commander gasped for air and stood up slowly on his wobbling feet. But the pain took him back to the ground, groaning. His former soldier “smiled” insanely above her “master”, practically juggling the glistening blades.
But then, all of a sudden, golden light circled Beck in rings, and he leapt upwards, performing a majestic spinning shield slam that knocked Melodie almost 5 yards away. Almost instantly, however, she was back on her feet. Yet another dagger flew at Beck, slamming into his helmet. The Knight was knocked to the ground, and the knife flew into the smoking ground. Kit Wanderware, separated from the Sentinel, gasped. The Spider Boss’s eyes brightened.
Beck Strongheart slowly got up, just to be slammed in his now vulnerable chest by a lightning fast kick from Melodie. He flew backwards, into the chaotic mist made by the Spider Boss, but corrupted wisps of Maelstrom wrapped around his and threw him back into the battle. He got up weakly, narrowly blocking yet another deadly slash. Melodie then leaped, performing an acrobatic arc and landing onto Beck’s shoulders. Just before his toppled over, she yanked his helmet off with skull crushing force, smashing it with her blades. “You do know, my young and naïve Commander, that you will lose. There is no space for you in this world. Join us, and live, not needing to move, not even think. Just relax, sleep, forever. All we need is your body, and the destruction of your spark!”
“I… I’ll never join you!” Beck panted, closing his eyes, trying not to be tempted.
“Ha! That’s what they all say! Now, join us!”
The Sentinel turned, taking to looking Melodie straight in her blood red eyes. “Remember all those times we had together? All those moments? In battle, while guarding, serving under the Sentinels as normal soldiers until we were promoted? When you were not infected? When you have a heart of gold? All those times you came crying to me, thinking that you have seen a flying Maelstrom pig? Remember?”
Melodie’s eyes fluttered, the glowing scarlet reseeding just a bit. “No. It never happened.” She growled, her eyes going red once again as she kneeled on his battered body and held a dagger at his throat.
“Yes, yes it did. The time I charged unknowingly at Burnsnout, the dragon? He blasted me off the cliff, but you caught me. I was just a simple Knight, you were a Samurai who love wearing denim instead of your breastplate.”
Melodie stood up as if in pain, then fell to the ground. “No, no, no, NO! NO! NEVER! NOOO!” She screamed, wriggling and rolling as if someone had poured molten lave on her. “NO!!! PLEASE! I DON’T WANT IMAGINATION! NEVER, NEVER, NOOOO!!!”
Beck hopped to his feet, worried. “Wha-?”
Suddenly, a glowing blue ball lowered from the dark sky. Melodie, for some reason, became strangely still, and her red eyes rolled to the back of her head, making them… red.
The Spider Boss screeched as the crackling sphere touched Melodie’s limp body, instantly becoming completely absorbed. Almost instantaneously, the Maelstrom lifted from her body, and she was herself again. Her spark had returned.
She gasped, opening her now normal eyes. “I…I’m back!” She whispered. “What’s happened to this place?”
But before Beck could answer, the Spider Boss roared something in some unintelligible language.
“She’s saying that she’s angry, and wants revenge on you for taking Melodie away from her, the majestic and all powerful Spider Boss, of course.” A voice came from behind them.
Beck turned and was met by the murderous gaze of…
“Bob!?”
“Say goodbye to the world as you know it, for it’s gonna change a whole lot!” The bald Stromling laughed in what must have been supposed to be a jolly voice.
The Sentinel Commander turned, and the last this he saw was a purple blast of Maelstrom from the Spider Boss’s Ray before we was infected. He fell to the ground.
“BECK!” Melodie shouted running towards him. The flash of bullets, also shot by the Spider Boss, made sure that she didn’t get to the Sentinel intact.
All the surrounding Nexus Force recruits jumped as everything flashed past their eyes, and a single yell, shouted by three people, resonated through the air.
“NO!”
A stream of purple projectiles, a couple of plasmatic, Maelstrom balls, and a red Dark Spiderling interrupted the shock (Or triumph, for most) that the spectators had had just seconds before.
Beck Strongheart was infected, Melodie Foxtrot smashed, and Wisp Lee, Madison Growl, and Silas Penumbra were not happy about it.

The Battle for Lego Universe
Book One: The Beginning of the End
Chapter 32
From Friend to Enemy

“BOB YOU MONSTER!” Madison screamed, not caring if the Minifigure had once been a great… acquaintance. In seconds, a purple pattern had carved itself under him, the ground cracking to let a red Paradox Mech through. She then pulled the trigger on her Wormholer, causing streams of purple projectiles to slam into Bob’s chest.
Silas Penumbra, an uninfected Spiderling scuttling behind him charged at the dazzled array of Stromlings, hacking away and sending bricks flying in all directions. The shocked Nexus Force spectators suddenly sprang to life, drawing their weapons and charging at the befuzzled army. Wisp Lee was standing, his back hunched, leering at the mess of Maelstrom. His corrupted hands sparked menacingly as he raised them, swirling balls of pure Maelstrom energy swirling in his palms. He began throwing violent, fizzling plasmatic balls at the other side of the army, smashing as many Stromlings at possible.
Then, all of a sudden, he turned and blasted a massive projectile at Bob, causing the traitor to go teacup over teakettle. Bob landed hard on the ground, making his imprint of the surface. He sat up, red eyes bulging and looked at Wisp. “So, you’ve learnt how to use the Maelstrom without weapons, have you now? Ha! Well, let’s see how you go with the master.”
Bob made a flip, then, suddenly… evaporated in wisps of smoke. Lee raised an eyebrow just before someone kicked him from behind. Bon had, somehow, teleported behind him. “OUCH!”
The traitor then threw two plasmatic balls of Maelstrom energy at his former friend, sending Wisp straight into Kit Wanderware, who was busy jabbing at a Chainsawling with his spear. The two Nexus Force Agents fell to the ground, a jumble of legs and arms. The Sawling stalked around them, and then leaped for the battered Kit.
“BANG!” The Venture Scout’s Flaregun made sure the creature didn’t get any closer.
But Bob was still alive and, yes, kicking. This time, a burning mass of purple flames hit Wisp and Kit. Wisp began hopping around, his behind on fire, but Kit’s armor just absorbed it and he was set on fire, an uncanny ability all Daredevils had. He then charged at the bald Stromling, his spear glinting the light of the sky. Bob laughed.
“Fool! Your daring is going to get you smashed one day! Ahhh, let’s make that NOW!” A glowing violet whip appeared in his hand, and he slashed at Kit. The rope tied itself around his “neck” and pulled him off his feet, throttling him.
“NO!” Wisp roared, but Madison was there before he could get up. She fired at Bob with her Valiant Weapon, forcing him to drop the gasping Venture League Scout. But in moments, she too had been knocked a couple of meters away.
Wisp Lee fired another ball from his hand, but it was easily deflected by the twisted, mutated aberration before him. Bob concentrated, and, suddenly, particle of Maelstrom came together and built two, massive, two fingered…. hands. They were connected to his hands by cords, like puppets… ginormus puppets with only one aim: to smash. He raised his arms, and the hands floated up into the air. He punched, his fist swiping through nothingness, and his monstrous creation brought itself down of Wisp Lee with a crushing blow that shook all of Avant Gardens. The scientist screamed is pain, falling flat of his face. And Bob just began walking towards him, his merciless eyes gleaming so vibrantly that they could have served as headlamps.
Wisp stood up painfully panting. He looked at remains the truck barricade and weakly smiled under his bandana. He limped for it, and to his surprise, Bob didn’t do anything. The rest of the Maelstrom was a little preoccupied with the rest of the small party of Nexus Force warriors. The Spider Boss was just watching everything with mild interest, its oversized eyes darting left and right.
The Paradox researcher raised both his infected arms, bringing them down heavily. Two electrical waves of Maelstrom cut through waves of Stromlings, slicing the truck into three pieces and shattering its windows. He then lifted two of the chunks with some invisible force, concentrating so hard that he shuddered violently.

The Paradox Representative leaped backwards, ramming into a Stromling, narrowly missing a deadly swipe from Bob, who had re-build into a Dark Ronin. Silas summoned his own Spiderling. “Find him.” He snarled, and then took to smashing weaker enemies.
Rocco Sirocco Seemed to have just noticed what was going on. “Oh my! I’m outta here!” He took one last bite of a Rockolate Bat, threw it at a Mech, then turned to his shrine. His hands began to glow blue with Imagination and he traced a sparkling door from thin air. “To the Monastery!” He yelled, hopping into his portal just before his Earth Shrine was destroyed by a shot from the Spider Boss.
