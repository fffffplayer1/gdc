The First Builders
Chapter 12
Reminiscence and the Start of a Quest

Figmatrix sat by the campfire, her eyes reflecting the flames under her swirling, violet hood. Her mind was set in decoding the message of the Prophecy that the strange, Ninja-like figure had told her.
A lot of it made sense now. “They will come, in a bird of metal, falling from the sky like a flaming petal” was when they had crashed into Gallant 5 while flying the “Shard Seeker” to their destination. “They’ll march out like locusts into the private domain, of the might Zorrillo, demon of pain” depicted their battle between the Beast. That section of verses ended in “Two lives will be lost, at a very high cost”, its meaning being far too easy to discern.
Figmatrix looked up at the starry night sky- which was slightly hazy because of the Imagination Shield surrounding Nrriisha Khaakrii- and thought of Brickore. A hidden tear trickled down her unseen face, and then she remembered the Zorrillo Beast’s final words: “Yyyooouuuu caaan’t coontiiinue down thiiis path. Thiiis path, yoouur deestiiination… wiiiill briiing total destruuctiioon.”
This, Figmatrix didn’t understand at all. According to the Prophecy, they would succeed in their venture, and something would go wrong. But what? It must have been major, since the rest of the Prophecy spoke of a massive war that would change everything. And the Zorrillo Beast spoke of abandoning the mission completely… was that to stop what was to come? Could you unwind the knotted threads of fate? The Prophecy said that that was impossible! And who were “The others”? Who was “The fifth”? And what was to happen to somebody’s mind? And what about that giant insect??? Figmatrix had far too many questions bubbling from her overflowing cauldron of a hooded head.
She turned to look at the Ninja-like figure to see if he was awake so that she could ask him to answer what was in his mind. His swirling, rainbow-like eyes were wide open, but Figmatrix could see that he was asleep from the rising and lowering of his heaving chest, and, of course, his snores.
Figmatrix sighed, kicked a small pebble into the fire, and got up from her perch. She slowly edged towards her small bed of leaves, twigs, and some comfortable fluff from a Couch-Potato they small band of Minifigures had found nearby. She tossed and turned for a long time, thoughts and questions spinning around her head and bouncing off her skull making ringing sounds like a percussion band falling off the side of a cliff, until she finally fell asleep…
“Wake up! Breakfast’s ready! And once we’ve eaten, we’ve gotta get outta here is we are to make it to the Shrine before noon!” It was the stranger’s voice.
Figmatrix sat bolt upright, but the fragrances of their morning meal knocked her right back down. Like dinner, it smelt amazing.
When she finally got back up, Crystale and Creatra were already digging into a plate of strange fruit, an egg with a blue yoke and a grey white, some strange root that looks suspiciously of a voodoo doll, and a slab of yellowish meat garnished in what looked like spiky oregano.
“Pruck ing! It’ps grate!” Crystale spluttered enthusiastically, spitting plenty of the mushy remains of a three fingered fruit in every direction from his stuffed maw.
“Great.” Figmatrix mumbled, wiping her face of the slop before coming to join them. Their chairs were some exceptionally large snail shells (Figmatrix worried that they might still be occupied), and their table was an over-sized leaf that had been bent to the ground and tied there by a long piece of grass. It was pretty hard for a leaf, feeling a lot like wood that could be completely folded (This also worried her, for she couldn’t imagine the hard smack she’d get if the cord holding it in place snapped).
When they finally finished their satisfying meals (With Figmatrix feeling even more content than the others knowing that nothing crazy had happened), the small group packed up, exchanged glances, and set of for the Shrine that the ninja-like person had spoken of. Creatra gave a cautious tap on a tree to make sure there weren’t any Nymphs, and then quickly hurried after the party.
Little did they know that the moment they left, their “chairs” slowly slid away, leaving a trail of slime, the “table’s” cord snapped and the massive leaf flung back to its original position, and of heads poked out of the trees to see what had woken the Nymph’s from their slumber…

The First Builders
Chapter 13
The Gate

The forests of Inriisha Khakrii were dense. Very dense. The Seekers would hardly get anywhere, even though they could levitate around. The Keeper, however, was hopping from tree to tree, flipping over bushes and sliding over roots with the ease tearing up a Report Car filled to the brim with bad grades.
“C’mon!” The ninja-like Minifigure snickered, scuttling up a thorny vine and preforming a backflip onto a jagged ledge a few meters above. “We’re almost at… the Gate!”
“Oh great. Not another gate!” Crystale snarled, slapping a branch away from him an annoyance. “We’ve gone through about a dozen by now!” The branch swerved back and slammed into his face, knocking him to the ground.
“No no, but this is THE Gate, with a capital G!” He flashed an amused glance at the dazed Seeker on the ground (Or as amused as you can look when your eyes are rainbow whirlpools and your face is almost completely covered). “And why do you foreigners keep getting on the Nymphs’ nerve! They can be rather… fun when woken up. Thinking like a tree is very luxurious, being a tree even better. But when one awakens from being a tree, they always feel mischievous.”
Crystale scowled at the tree’s bark. He was greeted by a mossy tongue that stuck right out of the rough wood and spat at him. “I hate this place already.”
“A little late” Creatra snickered back as Figmatrix pondered how that could possibly be funny.

*********************************************************************************************

It took about an hour for the small party to climb up the scraggly and sharp vine, most of it just being a mixture of howling and screaming and waking up Nymphs (Which probably wasted the most time).
When they were all safe on the rocky outcrop, all of them except the “ninja” were covered in thorns, mushrooms, and lots of bruises (The bruises couldn’t be seen, of course).
“We are close! Very close!”
“Just HOW many times are you gonna say that!?” Crystale complained, trying him best to use his weak Imagination abilities to build a shower and a bar of soap.
The Keeper looked at him, trying to make an exasperated face. “Just a little longer.”
“Knew it.”
But, very soon, Crystale was wishing they were still walking through that terrible forest, with the Imagination Shield and six eyed birds with dragonfly wings.
In mere moments, there were absolutely no trees in the vicinity… the only ones in sight being far below them, looking much like an army of ants carrying the remains of asparagus after one has used them in a science experiment. They were in the mountains.
Ice covered most of the rocky land, and it began to snow. Not just those little blizzards you experience in the Poles, but sheets and sheets of frozen water barreling down from the sky (Much like hail, but each piece at thick as an airplane’s window pane and as big as a small computer. They were forced to hold e’s hands and try to use their small Imagination powers of deflect the blows as the Keeper led the way. He, for one, hardly felt the rock-like projectiles pelting against him, and he could see everything as if it wasn’t hailing at all.
Then, all of a sudden, Figmatrix slipped. She lost her grasp of Creatra’s arm and skidded across a completely smooth surface that was cold enough to give ones very organs frostbite. She screamed in pain as the freezing power of the ground surged through her body, and she though that she was going to smash.
“Ah!” Came the Keeper’s voice, “Here’s the Gate!”
“W-w-w-whe-ere?” Figmatrix chattered, her very teeth and eyes going as numb as a rag doll in vinegar.
“You’re standing on it!” The “ninja” laughed, kicking the icy ground as if it was obvious. Then, all of a sudden, there was a burst of red and golden light, and something appeared in his hand. Figmatrix could hardly see it, much less him, in the sleet and haze, but she could make it out to be something pointed…
The Keeper then slammed it into the ground, and Figmatrix felt cracks opening all around her. “Oh no…”
That was the last thing she said before the ice shattered and she fell into a whirlpool that seemed to be made of the very essence of cold. Then her head ducked under the water… and didn’t resurface.

The First Builders
Chapter 14
A Meal Fit for Kings

Everything went completely quiet. It was as if the entire Universe had gone into “mute”. Figmatrix sunk deeper and deeper into the abyss, not even her constant gagging or the spiraling of bubbles jetting from her mouth making the slightest sound. She thrashed around in the water, her chest heaving and her head throbbing with a monstrous pain.
The Builder tried to scream, but nothing happened, other than the fact that a torrent of clammy water jetted into her numb and spasmodic maw like the slick tentacle of a murderous squid.
There was nothing to it: She was dying.

*********************************************************************************************

Figmatrix’s eyes shot open, causing small drops of water to spray from her eyelashes (If she had any). The entire world was blurred, but, even though she was extraordinarily damp, she knew that she was on dry land. She smiled under her hood, but the effort pained her jaws and sent shockwaves through her body, almost instantly causing her to be overcome by nausea. It felt terrible.
“Don’t move.” She knew that voice… it was the voice of the Keeper that had acted as their guide! So he survived too… did the others?
Figmatrix sat bolt upright. “Where is everyone else? Are they okay???”
She instantly regretted her actions, for she had a strange feeling that her heart was swelling up and her brain was being inflated with helium. Figmatrix fell to the ground, hardly able to do something as simple as think.
“They are fine. I wasn’t expecting the gate to be so harsh to you… the rest of us simply walked down a staircase and saw you spinning around in the air screaming and gagging. I knew then that the gate was trying to kill you by sending you though another dimension of thought than everyone else went through. I saved you just in time, and told off the Guardian quite severely.”
Figmatrix, finally learning her lesson, didn’t reply.

*********************************************************************************************

Hours later, Figmatrix awoke from her slumber to the sound of people laughing and music blaring. Her eyes slowly adjusted, and she managed to inspect her surroundings, which was near impossible before. She was in what looked like a cross between a cave and a hall of stone. Walls covered in strange carvings rose high above her, stopping half-way and becoming a jumble of slick and mossy rocks, finally ending in stalactites far above.
Stalactites sticking out of water.
There was no doubt about it. Far above her, the ceiling was a churning mass of water. It was an amazing sight… she had never seen a world so full of surprises as Gallant 5.
And, according to the music and jolly voices a little distance away, there was still much to go through. Painfully, Figmatrix got to her feet and began to walk towards the voices and sounds that reverberated across the cave-like hall, echoing past her like the roaring of a cyclone of happiness.
She quickly floated towards the sounds, and soon came into an opening. She arrived in a room far taller than the halls she had been through before, with a ceiling like the sky (Still with stalactites sticking from it). The ground was cobbled with beautiful crystals that shone in the light of multiple torches roaring with flame all around the room.
And right in the center was a massive stone table littered with platters upon platters of food, an entire legion of smells lifting Figmatrix’s senses and tossing them away in delight. All around it were dozens of the strange, ninja-like “Keepers”, drinking from goblets and digging through large plates of wondrous delicacies. And there, far off at the end of the table, where Creatra and Crystale, nervously whispering amongst each other.
Then, all of a sudden, Crystale looked up and laughed. “Fig! Good to see you!”
Figmatrix smiled under her hood as he rushed towards her, while Creatra merely looked up and waved. Their guide glanced away from a golden cub filled with some thick, red liquid and seemed to wink, his rainbow-like eyes flickering slightly.
Then another of the Keepers stepped up. He looked exactly like all the others, except for a wreath of golden leaves wrapped around his head. “Welcome to the stronghold of the Keepers of Inriisha Khakrii! We have been expecting you!”
“Oh?” Figmatrix said, preforming a curtsy. “Figmatrix, at your service!”
The Keeper with the wreath seemed to smile, and then he burst into a shower of blue flame.
“Wha-?” Figmatrix stepped back, but was cut off by a dagger at her throat.
“We have been expecting you for a long, long time.”

The First Builders
Chapter 15
Escape

The doors of the dungeon shut with a clang that reverberated throughout the entire stone room. Figmatrix sat on the clammy floor, her head in her hands. How could they have though it was her? Could it be her? Just because the Gate had tried to kill her?
Figmatrix thought back to the scenario that had happened in the massive dining room. The King of the Keepers had vanished in blue flame, just to appear behind her, pointing a nasty, curved dagger forged from Red Imaginite. Crystale had rushed towards her, but was knocked onto the table by a torrent of crimson light, causing him to slide across it and smash many a plate and goblet.
She still heard the King’s voice rattling in her brain, “Take Ssaii and this… thing to the Dungeons!” Ssaii had been their guide, and he was probably being jailed because he had saved her from the Gate. And the most chilling thing, even colder than the swirling waters she had gone through hours before, was the King’s whisper in her ear, “But one shall change in the depths of the mind, and terrible things, the one shall find, for when others come, one shall be corrupted, by the power of the One, who can’t be confronted.”
It was the part of the Prophecy that had unnerved her the most. It foretold that one of the Builders… or Seekers, as the Keepers called them, would turn evil when their journey ended.
And this “One”… it was her. Or at least that’s what the Keepers had said… for the Gate had been made to find the One who would end everything. But how could it be her? It was… impossible! Figmatrix couldn’t imagine how.
The cells here weren’t just metal bars and keys. They were massive slabs of enchanted stone rising right above you on all sides, insuring that absolutely no light would get in. Skeletons littered the ground, with was completely smooth covered in carvings made by Minifigures that had gone mad in their isolation. It was terrible to be in there.
Figmatrix thought of Ssaii, who was probably also in a cell in the same Dungeon. And his cell was probably worse than hers, for he was far more powerful that she was.
She quickly stopped worrying about him when she pondered exactly how any food would be able to get into the jail.
*********************************************************************************************
Hours passed, but absolutely nothing changed. Did they want her dead? Did they want to stop the Prophecy from becoming a reality? That was fair enough, especially since her change would cause the end of so many… but… that wasn’t fair! She wasn’t that evil mass murderer! She wasn’t going to corrupt anyone! The Keepers had the wrong person!
Figmatrix thought of who could possibly do something so terrible. Crystale? No… he couldn’t. It wasn’t in him. Creatra? Even less of a chance. He was an amazing leader, and had a pure soul. Nothing could change him from his path for peace and creativity.
Now that she thought of it, she was probably the easiest to turn evil. She was far kinder and far more loving than the other two, but, even though she was bright enough, her mind wasn’t exactly strong. She could be tricked very easily… no… it couldn’t be her!
Figmatrix’s train of thought was suddenly broken when she heard someone yelling. Suddenly, there was a thud against the stone wall of her chamber that sent her to her feet immediately, trying to conjure a dagger from her small resources of Imagination. The yelling continued, and suddenly Figmatrix heart the shuffling of feet running down the stairs from far away: Reinforcements coming to aid whoever was yelling.
She heard the clang of swords and a soft but distinct skittle as one of the men dueling outside was disarmed. “Traitor!” One yelled, she recognized his voice… it was one of the prison guards.
“There he is! Get him!” The reinforcements had arrived, but there was a loud bang as many fell to the floor after the guard screamed again. That probably meant that their assailant had tossed the poor man straight into the oncoming Minifigures. There was another bang, then a whiz, then a very sharp crack as someone casted one of those rainbow attacks at the strange Minifigure. Even though Figmatrix couldn’t see anyone, she knew that sound anywhere.
Then, all of a sudden, a crack appeared in the wall. In moments, the stone looked like it was a massive, egg-like boulder with a demon chicken hatching from it. The wall burst into thousands of fragments, and Ssaii leaped into the cell as ten spherical, scarlet orbs whizzed past him and exploded in the far end of the dungeon.
“Quick!” He half yelled, half whispered. “We have to escape. I know you aren’t the One that the Prophecy tells og. Don’t ask me why.”
“How did you escape!?”
“Later!” He snapped, “Now escape! Or they’ll send us to places that no one can escape!”
And with that he grabbed her and rushed out of the cell and a feiry blast shaped as the head of an eagle screeched past them and turned the place to rubble. Small fragments of sharp rock flew in every direction, imbedding themselves in the walls opposite them. Many almost smashed the frantic Keepers, who hastily created red shields to protect them and sent sizzling snake-like spectral abortions after the two escapees.
“I’m not the One…” Figmatrix whispered to herself as Ssaii pulled her along, sending bubbling piranhas and crackling falcons back at the spells shot by the Keepers. “I’m not the One…”
