OOC: It doesn't really matter at this point, but my teleportation works not by disassembling peoples' molecular structures and reassembling them somewhere else, but by altering the shape of space, pulling some bits closer and pushing some bits further away (I think it's sort of like how it works in A Wrinkle in Time, actually, but it's been a long time since I read that, so I could be wrong.).

Anyways... I drift through space, a tiny, incredibly thin stream of distorted space linking the insides of my lungs with the atmosphere of a semi-nearby planet and allowing me to breathe.

"And so the war grows ever harder for the unnamed, insignificant ones on both sides. I pity them. Their suffering cannot go on much longer..."

I expand the air flowing into my lungs into a large bubble, and begin to search for survivors...
