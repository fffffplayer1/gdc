*the Peregrine, cloaked, exits a wormhole outside one of the more populated Void planets*

~Eris Redbeam~

"Those who can hide should leave hiding to those who know about it"


Oh, just saw this move.

*The motion sensor detectors placed throughout the Void Empire pick up your ship and lock onto its position*

~Emperor Zolmar

Would my opponent please explain his method of tracking my vessel? Many thanks...

~Eris Redbeam~

The Void Empire placed cloaked motion sensors along all our borders and entrances, as soon as you ship entered through the wormhole, they detected you.

In the case that you created the wormhole youself, well, I think our sensors would've picked up a wormhole being created. They then, would have promptly scaned the area in a similar way to the motion sensors.

Now, I don't know if our motion sensors can track your ship dirrectly, but we know the general area your ship is in.

*A small convey of ships is sent to investigate the area we sensed a ship in*

~Emperor Zolmar

Then I will have to null your move. Sorry It is not technologically feasible to place reliable accurate sensors within a cloaked area and get accurate results. That's the way it's spun in every cloaking device system I can think of. Cloaking is a double blind system of hiding. If it wasn't, there would be no point in being cloaked because your sensors could just be traced back to the location they are.



~Eris Redbeam~

No clue what you're talking about. First off, these aren't sensors in that they can track your ships movement directly. There laser beams going from device to device, if the beam is blocked, it sends the data back to our defence squad. We have cameras on the sensors, so when they go off, we can watch the video feed. If we see absolutely nothing, we know it is a cloaked ship, if we see a piece of derbis has passed through them, then we know nothing wrong has happened.

I also haven't even gotten into the self replicating cloaked landmines, our main borders are protected by them as well as our populated planets. Meaning if you go near our populated planets, your ship would be destroyed by them.

~Emperor Zolmar

OOC: Okay, lemme explain this better.

Point one, what does a Cloaking Device do? It blocks electromagnetic and all view of the object. This also extends to the outside in. That's the basic and pillar principle of a cloaking device.

If you send a laser to a cloaked object, the laser will just be absorbed if it is like the non-powerful kind you're referring to. So you wouldn't be able to send the laser to a cloaked object, nor send that camera feed.

~Eris Redbeam

Oh okay, I see what you mean.

*We decloak the sensors*

And there is that tiny problem of the self replicating cloaked mines closing off all entraces to our HQ...

~Emperor Zolmar

OOC: Once again, Wishy, mines couldn't detect objects if they were cloaked either. Even if they weren't cloaked and passed into the cloaking device, the Particle shield would just bounce them back.

~Eris Redbeam~

OOC: That's not the way our mines work. If something touches them, they blow up. And they are in very tightly packed rows. There isn't any "bouncing them back" you touch them, they go boom.

~Icaroth


Fine by me. I still have particle shields. And you still gotta have interstellar travel within your planets. Why you're alienating your most populated planets with minefields is beyond me

IC: Anyway, I have to go fulfill my extra good (though sorta might be evil to you) plan. So if you'll excuse me. No, wait, don't! Ninjas don't need to be excused

*the Peregrine leaves, but leaves behind a small package as well*

~Eris Redbeam~

*Several space stations go out to the package and place a 50 mile shield around it that will stop anything from coming out or going in*

I'm not trusting any packages at this moment.

~Emperor Zolmar
~Watchful Eye of the Swords of Light
