There’s no glory in War- The story of a Nexus Force Veteran.

Well, I’m writing this because it sounded like a good idea in my head, so here I go. Oh, the main character is a Senteninal Knight named John. I will tell it like I normally do, but sometimes stopping to comment on things, as John is telling his story to his Grandchildren. I hope you enjoy it!

Chapter one:

There’s no glory in war. It’s a lie they tell everyone, so they can have more solders to fight for them. Everyone. Not even the smartest know it’s a lie. None. Only a select few know, and they are sworn to secrecy. But after so long, you began to realize what it really is. So here is my tale, a long story, but a true one. Now just sit back and relax, and listen closely, because I won’t repeat myself.

”It was when I was twenty, I was finally ready to join the Nexus Force. I was so excited I nearly ran off the bus because I had so much adrenaline that I could have ran faster than it. I was impatiently waiting to get to-“
“To get to where, Grandpa?” said Sally, John’s 4-year-old granddaughter.
“That’s what I was getting to, now as I was saying,”
“I was waiting to get to the Nexus Force Training Camp. The Nexus Force Training Camp was build in the exact spot the Nimbus Center is at now, but that’s a different story entirely. I wasn’t sure which faction I wanted to join, but I knew I wanted to be in the front lines, where all the action was. I barley knew anything about the factions, but I thought I wanted to join the Sentinels.”
“Yes, Henry?” John sighed, noticing that his 11-year-old grandson, Henry, had his hand raised.
“Why Sentinel? Why not Assembly? That’s the best faction,” Henry said proudly.
“Is not!” Said Alex, the 9-year-old Venture League lover.
“Is too!” Said Henry.
“Is not!” Said Alex.
“ENOUGH!” John yelled, startling all three of them. “Didn’t your mom make you watch the horrible, seemingly never ending sing-along videos with the cheesy chip tune that get’s stuck in your head for days about how all factions are equal?”
“Um… no?” said Alex.
“It sounds… Um…” Henry paused, searching for the right word.
“AWESOME!!!” Sally yelled, finishing his sentence for him.
“I was thinking more along the lines of horrifying, terrible, life-scaring.” Henry mumbled.
“No more interruptions! You ASKED me to tell this story to you!”
They all fell silent, waiting for him to continue.
“Anyways, as I was saying, I wanted to join the Sentinels. I was excited, as was everyone else. When the Nexus Force Training Center finally came into view, a nervous chatter started up amongst all the soon-to-be faction members. Most were worriedly talking to their friends, and questions like, “I am not sure if I want to join Venture League,” or, “Are you sure that Assembly is right for me? Were heard among the talk. I asked my friend Samuel, “Do you know what faction you are going to chose?”
“No. I want to be Assembly, but I don’t know…”
“You always told me you wanted to be Sentential! Well, that’s what I am going to be. See, look up there,” John pointed out of the front window, and a huge tower stood right in the middle of the Nexus Force Training Camp. A faction insignia hung on each side. The Sentinel symbol was facing them.
“See? That symbol there represents all that you ever told me you wanted. To command an elite team, to be on the front lines, and to be a hero. That’s what you wanted. I will support you on whatever decision you make, but Sentinel is the one I think is right for you.”
“Are you sure?” Sam asked.
“I’m positive.”
“Ok. I’ll join Sentential with you.”
“It’s your decision, you know.”
“I know.”
Right when they finished, the bus driver said, “We are nearing the Nexus Force Training Camp. You better decide soon, because we will arrive in around ten minutes. But don’t rush yourselves, because this decision will change the course of your lives drastically.”
Exactly ten minutes later, they hit the bus stop at the Nexus Force Training Camp.
“Are you ready?” John asked.
“I’m more than ready. Let’s go.”
Then together, they alighted down the bus stairs, heading towards a man with a clipboard, going to make the decision that would change their lives forever.
“Is that all your going to tell us?” Henry asked.
“Well, if you hadn’t interrupted so many times, then we wouldn’t have to stop so soon.” John chuckled.
“But do we HAVE to go to bed?” Cried Alex.
Just then, John’s wife, Clara, called in from the dining room, “No, your not going to bed until you come and get the cookies I made for you!”
“Cookies!” They all yelled, running into the Dining Room.
“I’ll continue tomorrow, that is if you still want me to.” John said.
“Of COURSE we do, said Alex.
“Yes! You have to tell us more tomorrow!” Henry said.
“Ok. Tomorrow, I will tell you more. The earlier you get up, the more time I have to tell the story!”
And with that, they all finished their cookies, brushed their teeth, and went to bed.
Clara walked up to John and said, “They seem interested in this. Some of this I’ve never actually heard, you know.”
“Oh, you will need to listen more than, because there is way more from where that came from.” John teased.
Clara laughed.
“Well, that is if I don’t go hoarse before the end of the day tomorrow.”
