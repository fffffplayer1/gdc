Sane Me 2: Just wait, you’ll see.

*Everyone sits down and waits.*

Sane Me 2: No! It shall happen! Just wait some more!

*Everyone waits for another half an hours.*

Clutch Powers: What is it that we’re waiting for?

Sane Me 2: We’re waiting for YOU!!!! Just wait some more, you’ll see!

*A few days later*

Sane Me 2 *drowsily*: Just wait…just wait a little *YAWN* more.

Sane Me 1: I’m starting to thing “Sane Me 2” should be renamed “Insane Me 2.”

(In)sane Me 2: But…but…it’ll happen.

Clutch Powers: Look, I think it’s becoming increasingly apparent that whatever it is you’re waiting for is obviously not going to happen.

(In)Sane Me 2: But I remember it all so vividly.

 

*(In)Sane Me 2 has a vivid flashback.*

Sane Me 2: But if it is as Clutch Powers said, that this change is part of the past, how come I remember the old past perfectly fine?

Sane Me 2: A different time stream?

Clutch Powers: Yes yes yes, you see, the moment you went back in time and altered something, you were no longer in you time stream. Think of it like this: There are two parallel roads going off into the distance. A car driving down one of the roads swerves to the side and does a little off roading. He intends to leave the road, circle around, and come back onto the road a little farther back, but instead he ends up leaving the road, circling around next to the parallel road, and entering that one. Make sense?

Everybody in the Ship: NO!!!
Insane Me: Yep, it made perfect sense!

Clutch Powers: I’m glad someone here has their sanity.

Sane Me: About that—

Clutch Powers: Here, let me make it easier for you to understand:

                                         ||↴ ||

Original Timestream    || ↓ ||     Alternate Timestream

                                         || ↳||

                                         ||    ||

 

Sane Me *Looking around at the other people in the ship*: Are you seeing what I’m seeing?

Sane Me 2: Did Clutch Powers just make those lines come out of thin air?

Clutch Powers: Just part of the job.

Sane Me: The job…?

Clutch Powers: Oh, nothing. My point is, you couldn’t go back in time to change something, because the mere act of changing something makes a time paradox.

Clutch Powers: Oh, nothing. My point is, you couldn’t go back in time to change something, because the mere act of changing something makes a time paradox.

Sane Me 2: I don’t get it.

Clutch Powers: Well you see, let’s say someone was smashed. You go back in time to make it so that he wasn’t smashed. But then since he wasn’t smashed, you would have never gone back in time to make it so he wasn’t smashed.

Insane Me: Ah, got it.

Clutch Powers: Since this would be impossible, it’s obvious that instead you merge into and alternate time stream. But I wouldn’t time travel like this too much; every time you travel through time there is a chance that you will create a time paradox. This is bad. Very bad. Every time a time paradox is created, entire universes are destroyed! Timestreams are ripped apart! It is common knowledge that time traveling should not be taken lightly, and should only be done in the most extreme circumstances. Thankfully everyone knows this and I’m pretty confident that the universe is safe.

Sane Me 2: What do you mean the universe is safe?
Clutch Powers: Well, if there was some idiot who time traveled a hundred time a day then it would be very likely that the universe is coming apart, but luckily there is no one like that. At least to my knowledge.

Sane Me 2: *A worried look crosses my face, and I look down at my portable time machine. I look down at the electronic information screen which reads

 

Average Number of Times Used per day: 286

 

Sane Me 2: :Heh heh, well, good thing there isn’t anyone like that.

 

Clutch Powers: So, don’t use the time machine, and whatever you do, DO NOT CREATE A TIME PARADOX!!! THAT WOULD BE DESASTEROUS!!!

 

Sane Me 2: *I look down at the information screen and see the words:
