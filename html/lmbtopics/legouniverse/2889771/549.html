“Wait!” some random soldier called out. “If Barry truly is Larry’s ancestors, than we dare not smash him!

 

“Why not?” Bobert asked. “It’ll make things so much easier for our future selves if we smash Larry’s great-great-great-great-great-great-great-great-great grandparents right now.”

 

“But don’t you know the standardized rules of time travel?” The random guy objected.

 

“Um...maybe?”

 

“Everyone is accustomed to them! How could you not know? The rules are: don’t smash butterflies in the past, don’t smash yourself in the past, and don’t smash someone's great-great-great-great-great-great-great-great-great grandparents in the past.”

 

“What are you implying?” Bobert asked.

 

“Doing any of the above things could create a time paradox in which your actions are perpetuated in a time loop for all eternity!” the random soldier cried emphatically.

 

“Whatever. Fire the tank!” Bobert ordered.

 

The tank fired and the castle EXPLODED!!!

 

All the random soldiers who had traveled with Bobert yelled and cheered! The battle was finally over! And what’s more, they had ended it before it had even begun!

 

“Woo hoo!” Bobert cried. “We blew stuff up!”

Bobert and his random soldiers lived happily ever after.

 

UNTIL they tried to return to the year 2140, that is. For Bobert had made one serious mistake. He had assumed that because “Larry” rhymed with “Barry,” Barry was Larry’s ancestor. But he forgot that Larry was a fictitious name that we assigned to the Evil Emperor!!! If Bobert had delved a little deeper in ancient history, or if he had cell phone service and had asked Siri, he would have realized that Barry was really his great-great-great-great-great-great-great-great-great grandfather! And so Bobert was caught in an infinite time loop that perpetuated for all eternity.

 

The End
