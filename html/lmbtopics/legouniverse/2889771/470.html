Today my family and I went sledding on a sledding hill!

 

The snow was all fresh powder, and there was two feet of it.  Powdered snow, which I think I rage-posted about last year, is the most useless type of snow! Every snow particle is so light and dry that they build up on each other, but they don't compress naturally without some weight, so that two feet of powdered snow equates to some 8 inches of wet snow.  So trying to walk on powdered snow results in a lot of sinking into the snow.  Sleds can sort of slide on the surface of the powder without sinking but there is still friction and it doesn't catch much speed.  The sledding hill we went to is a big hill and at best we got halfway down because of a short level area in the middle, but we had fun and that's what matters!

 

Thanks for reading.
