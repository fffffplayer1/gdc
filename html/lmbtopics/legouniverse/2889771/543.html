I was thinking of a method of filming between 1 to 5 seconds of interesting space action sequence stuff LDD / Talmid Show style.

 

First I'd need a backdrop of course.  I use that free image editing program called Gimp version 2.8, hardly to its full potential but it gets my simple needs done, and just by dragging files into the program it will overlay them onto the displayed picture.

 

So, I'd put in a background picture, probably of space - then comes the tricky part.

 

For some of the scenes I've done before I've had just many frames of a single object overlayed over a custom background in this manner - but what if I had more than one moving object?  What if I had, say, 50 screenshots of 4 different moving objects each, and I wanted to animate all of them moving over the same background at the same time?

 

It's quite simple.  I 'just' take 50 screenshots of each object doing its motions - then put the nth image of each slideshow over the background, save that as "1", then put the next nth image of each over the background, save that as "2", and keep going.  Also Gimp's user interface lets the user change what image, or layer, goes over and under others somehow, I haven't used this by my graphics designer family relative tipped me off to that feature.  Makes life easier just knowing about it.

 

Well, I dunno what future episode of Talmid Show might incorporate such an animated scene animated in the aforementioned manner, but that's one way to get lots of ships moving at the same time. Would be interesting to test, though, all typically my "tests" are the same things as final scenes.

 

Talmid out!
