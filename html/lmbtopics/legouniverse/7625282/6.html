I hear a noise in the distance, subtle and stealthy. I reach for my knife to prepare myself, but before I can, a hand comes over my mouth.

 

My breathing slows down as I am being deprived of oxygen, and my eyes circle around, trying to take a look at my attacker. He drags me over to the window, struggling to have a grip on me as I squirm around. When we near the broken glass of the windows, I manage to turn my head around and see my attacker for the first time.

 

He’s scraggly, and has a beard that seems like it has been uncut for decades. It’s all I can see before I am knocked to the ground again. I roll over, and the man has his elbow around my throat, putting me in a headlock. I grasp the air weakly, trying to free myself, when I pull on his beard.

 

The person yelped in pain, and seeing the opportunity, I pushed him to the ground. His head falls to the ground with a thud. I inch back from him, cautiously watching for any signs of movement from the man. As I walk back to regroup with Hugo and Elle, the man regains his consciousness and charges at my legs.

 

I fall down to the ground, and I can see the look in the man eyes as I turn my head around. The look of anger and madness dances around in his eyes as he walks slowly to me. 

 

“Let’s make you squeal!” he says malevolently, as he pulled back his shirt cuffs. My hand reaches for my knife in my pocket. Just before he comes to pick me up off the ground, I kicked at him hard, sending him to the wall. When he recovers from the kick, I throw the knife.

 

I let it go.

 

The man looks closely at his chest, and then emotions of anger, pain, and fear register on his face.

 

Then, it’s just me, and the man, now with a shocked expression. He falls quietly.

 

Hugo and Elle come in just as I retrieve my knife from the man. They both have shocked expressions on their faces, but Hugo seems more accustomed this kind of behavior from me than Elle.

 

“It’s time to pack up.”, I say.

Elle glances at the now lifeless man on the ground, then at me.

 

"So, you're saying we're moving because of this?"

 

I shrug.

 

"Yeah."

 

She pouts, and becomes defiant to my orders.

 

"But can't we stay here, just for this night?" she argues. "Who knows what other weirdos are out in the street?"

 

Her words are idotic, yet have the ring of truth in them.

 

"Fine, I suppose." I say. "We'll stay here for the night, but we're leaving first thing in the morning. I'm not going to drag dead weight with me."

 

Elle smiles victoriously, then collapses on the ground to sleep.

 

Hugo and I exchange quizzical looks.

 

"Poke it with a stick and see if it moves?" Hugo says faintly. "She probably ain't one of those Ronin..."

 

"Whatever." I reply. "I'm taking first watch."

 

~A~

 

A soft touch on my shoulder jolts me back to reality. Everything comes bcak in color as I wake up, and the sun has set. When I look behind me, Hugo lies comatose,, while Elle is wide awake, the complete opposite of what she was just hours ago.

 

"So," she says. "We might as well get to know each other a bit before we get mauled in the streets."

 

"And just what makes you think I'll do so?" I retort.

 

"If you're too lazy to tell me, fine. I'll start with my side." she says.


She describes her life from the moment she was born, up until the present. Often, she interjects with "Oh, you should've been there!" or "It's a shame you didn't see that." When she finally finishes, she looks out of the windows, as if telling her experiences triggered wistful memories.

 

"I'm done talking for now. Mind telling me your story now?" she says.

 

I wave my hand in dismissal.

 

"There's nothing more you need to know, other than the fact that I'm only supposed to escort you. Not to become a best friend." I say.

 

I put my head down on the ruined recliner, tired.

 

"Get some sleep. We'll be moving early in the morning." I say.

 

Elle nods in agreement.

 

Before I close my eyes, I still see Elle staring out the window, with moonlight streaming onto her face.

Harsh light brings itself upon Hugo, Elle, and me as we wake up. I try to squint my eyes in order to take a look at my surroundings. Elle yawns, and smacks her lips together, in anticipation of something to eat. She looks at me pleadingly with wide eyes, but I dismiss her beseeching.

 

“You’re not a cat.” I say. “I’m not going to feed you every time your stomach growls. Get moving.”

 

Elle tries to rub her eyes together as if to make herself look more mournful, but as Hugo and I head near the door, she yells “Wait up!”

 

When we open the door, the smell of rust and decay moves into the apartment. We try to make as little noise as possible when closing the door to prevent Infected from finding us.

 

Or even worse, other survivors.

 

The terrain is rough, and hard to navigate. The ground is cracked from many years of fighting and civil strife, and numerous buildings have fallen down, weary of being in a living nightmare for years.

 

“So, Hugo” I say. “How far are we from the extraction point?”

 

Hugo tries to make sense of his map, but throws it down in frustration.

 

“I don’t know. A long time, maybe?” he says.

 

Elle’s eyes widen when we finish talking, and tries to get our attention.

 

“Look over in the horizon, and you’ll see a building with an onion-shaped dome. I – I think that’s the House of Law.”, she says.

 

Hugo and I squint our eyes to get a better sight, and when that doesn’t work, Hugo gets out his binoculars and peeks into them.

 

“Yup,” Hugo confirms. “The girl’s right. That piece of stinking ol’ garbage is the House of Law, indeed.”

 

“And how do you know that?” I challenge.

 

“Trust me.” Hugo explains. “I used to work there as a lawyer. I know my stuff, man.”

 

I sigh.

 

“Alright. Let’s make our way towards the House.”

Hugo and Elle follow me past ruined cars, overturned buses, and bombed-out houses. Everywhere we go, scenes of destruction and fear show themselves.

 

We pass by a ruined temple, with only the faithful symbol standing, among pieces of rubble, Infected, and people.

It’s funny how we give offerings and rub the feet of a supreme deity that sees all, but is blind to our suffering, and hears all, but is deaf to our pleas.

 

Yet the cycle continues. We repeat our actions over and over again, all for the sake of nothing. Not even a single crumb of appreciation.

 

“I’m unsure about what it would say when it sees all the damage and misery in the world.” I wonder aloud. “Would it look the other way and demand more for itself, or actually lend a hand?”

 

“Whoa there, Captain Nihilist.” Hugo says. “It’s okay for you to ramble on and on about your philosophy and what not, but we’ve rested here in this temple long enough.”

 

“How much time do we have left until sundown?” I ask.

 

Hugo stares at the sun, and tries to estimate a time.

 

“Probably 3 hours, more or less.” he replies.

 

We start heading towards the House again. When we come across a slumped over skyscraper, Hugo frowns.

 

“I don’t think there’s any other way around this thing…” he notes. “The only way to get to the House is through this monstrosity.”

 

“I agree.” Elle adds. “Besides, my shoes are starting to feel flimsy. Can we please get to the House soon?”

A brusque pause then follows, and I make my decision.

 

“We’re moving through this place, that’s for sure.” I say. “But we don’t have much time to lose. Let’s go.”

There isn’t even an entrance which we walk through. Hugo and I squeeze our way through a broken window, and we then pull Elle into the room.

 

A sea of cubicles and offices lies ahead of us. We move through it, scavenging what we can. When we finally break free, we go down the stairs. That’s when we stop.

 

Particulates float through the air, lazily, prompting us to go through them. But I know better. They’re spores. One breath of them will gradually turn you into one of the Infected.

 

“Gas masks on, now.” I order quietly.

 

“But I don’t wanna.” whines Elle.

 

“Look, do you want to turn into an Infected?”

 

Elle turns pale when hearing this question.

 

“I thought so. Put this on.”

 

I toss a gas mask to her, and it takes her a few minutes to clasp it onto her face.

 

My slow and steady breathing repeats itself again and again in my ears as we make our way through the spore-infested hallways.

 

Suddenly, I hear a noise.

 

Metal cans are overturned, and something, or someone, is moving across the floor with swift feet.

 

“Hugo, do you hear anything strange?” I ask quietly.

 

“No, why?” he replies just as softly.

 

“Because something sounds like it’s coming towards us and-“

 

I’m not able to finish my sentence since something knocks into me from the side. I lay on the ground, and I’m staring into the face of an Infected.

 

It’s a grotesque sight, being right in front of the jaws of death. Saliva drips onto my mask as I try to push it off me, to keep it from biting me and making me its own.

 

It feels like time slows down when I finally push the Infected off of me, and when it tries to attack me again, Hugo kicks it towards the ground again. He smashes it by stepping on its face, hard.

 

Sadly, that Infected wasn’t the last of them.

Three more come barreling down the stairs, teeth bared and arms outstretched to attack us. They all resemble teenagers who might have just had a little too much fun in this region. Their eyes are covered in a thin red layer, and have not seen the light of day for years.

 

I bring down the first one with a well-placed bullet, and Hugo throws his knife at another one, taking down another target. However, the third one goes for Elle. It lunges at her quickly, and strafes Hugo’s bullets expertly. It then pushes Elle to the ground, but before it can grab a quick, lethal bite in, Elle kicks it off of her. The switchblade produced from her pockets comes to life and she makes short work of the thing, destroying it easily.

 

“Do you think there’s more of them?” Elle asks.

 

“Probably.” I answer.

 

After the attack, we tread the floor cautiously, on the lookout for more Infected. We’re about to move across another hallway, when the floor shakes.

 

“What the-?!” Hugo exclaims through his gas mask. He doesn’t get to finish his sentence however, as I step on a weak spot in the wooden floor.

 

Everything comes crashing down, like Nexus Tower! James once said that.

 

It couldn’t have been truer at this moment. The fragile glue holding the pieces of the floor tears and comes apart. I fall through two stories, and land on a solid wooden table.

 

I feel like cursing the whole world for this injury, and my back aches immensely.

 

“Jowe, are you alright?!” Hugo almost yells.

 

I smile.

 

“Couldn’t have been better.” I sharply reply.

 

Hugo sighs. “We’ll meet at the lobby, which is two stories above for you. Don’t shoot at us when you see us arriving!”

 

I give him a weak nod, and there they go.

 

Whoosh.

 

They’re gone as fast as I know it.
