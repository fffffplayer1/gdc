Its body is littered with freshly formed wounds, numerous scars and Maelstrom growths. The skin looks withered, malnutritioned. We won't be the first victims this Spitter has caught in its hands. Fortunately, its reaction time is slow, slow enough for us to get a move on.

 

"Move, if you don't want to be dead!", Marie hisses in a hushed tone.

 

Hugo, Marie, and I run across the decaying wooden floor, while the Spitter grasps the air with its hands, trying to locate where we are with its crude echolocation.

 

Hugo and I duck behind a bookshelf, while Marie hides under a desk.


"We have to get rid of the darn thing if we want to live!", Marie says. Frankly, I can't see how that would happen, but Hugo shushes me off.

 

"We'll try Fire Blast, Marie." he says, and already I know where we are going at. Hugo sees my expression, and nods. He gets out a jar of seemingly emptiness. Marie raises her eyebrows in confusion, but then Hugo grabs a lighter and sets the insides of the jar ablaze. I look at the insides of my backpack and curse. There's only one cartridge left, and I have to make this shot count.

 

I grab my blaster out, but the Spitter's in close proximity.

 

"Now!", shouts Marie. The spitter turns its head towards Marie's voice, before it lurches towards her. Just before it moves a single step, Hugo throws the jar at the Spitter. The flames immediately jump onto the spitter, consuming the many Maelstrom growths on its body. Before the flames wear off, I load my blaster, and aim steadily towards its head.

 

......

 

Bam!

 


The Spitter, now with a gaping hole in its body, falls down heavily onto the wood, and crashes into the lower levels of the building.

 

Hugo and I wipe the sweat off our foreheads, but we then look all around us. Hungry beings, wanting to feed after being deprived of food for so long.

 

"Well," Marie says, her voice dripping with sarcasm. "I guess if we want to live, you guys better get moving."

She lets her voice ring for a few seconds, as if waiting for Hugo and I to make a dramatic escape.

 

Truth be told, after fighting the Spitter, I'm too tired to do anything "dramatic".

 

But we have to run. Hungry mouths lie agape around us, waiting for the ringleader to screech, beginning the assault upon us. We have only a few seconds left before they swarm us.

 

I desperately try to think of plan to hold the Infected off, and then I look at the floor.

 

The floor.

 


It's decaying, rotten and old. Just a few steps and it will-

 

"Jowe!", Hugo whispers. "They're stirring!"

 

The Infected sense my uncertainty and prepare to run at us, at full speed.

 

"You dolt," Marie says to Hugo angrily. "Can't you see Jowe wants us to shoot at the floor? It's almost near the breaking point-"

 

The sound of thunder pervades throughout the building.

 


One Infected lets loose a guttural, ear-splitting scream, and the horde rushes toward us.

 

"Shoot the floor!", yells Marie, her voice being drenched by fear. Together, the three of us shoot at the floor while running, until one Infected falls down through the floor.

 

This creates a chain effect, as more and more Infected drop into the bowels of the earth, until the floor collapses entirely.

 

We sit down at the stump of what once was a flourishing tree, panting and sweating. There's not much left of this building, as multiple hallways are collapsed, and arches have fallen. When I kick down a door, it crumbles into dust, and is scattered by the wind from outside.

 

I try to make a mental note of my surroundings, when suddenly, I get attacked.

 

A teenage girl comes barrelling at me with a switchknife, and my instincts jump in.

 

I dodge her slashes and strikes, when I grab her elbow, and fling her towards Hugo. He catches her by both arms, and holds her tight.

 

"You donkeys!", she screams.  She could said a lot of more colorful language, when Marie shut her mouth.

 

"Gentlemen", she says.

 

"This is the task I was talking about earlier, remember?"

Without meaning to, my voice works it way up through my throat, and comes out of my mouth as a garbled mess of words.

 

The girl, now being restrainted by Hugo's grasp, looks at me in confusion.

 

"What?", she says.

 

This time, after twenty five years, my voice flows out of my mouth, no longer strained.

 

"Hello, hello.", I say. "What's your name?

 

The girl looks at me as if she can't believe she's dealing with people like Hugo and I, and replies. "My name is Elle. Nothing more, nothing less."

 

The commanding features of her face, her fiery and hardened gaze. She looks as though she could be related to Marie.

 

But I still don't know what to do with this girl, and I have no idea what task Hugo and I have to complete. It's odd enough that we aren't ending this girl's life after she attacked me, but now she is of important value to Marie, and therefore precious?

 

I find it hard to believe, but if Hugo and I want our weapons back, this may be our only chance.

 

"So," I say. "What do we have to do with this...thing over here?" I gesture towards Elle. She gazed furiously at me, and her stare felt like knives poking into my chest.

 

Marie smiles. "Elle is a very important asset.", she says. Funny how she doesn't refer to Elle as a human being, as she had seemed so compassionate to her.

 

"It's very imperative that you keep her alive." Elle rolls her eyes at this statement.

 

"So," Marie says. "Take her to the House of Law, and drop her off. RISE members will meet you there, and will extract her."

 

Hugo raises his hand in protest. "The House of Law?!" he says. "Marie, that's days away from here!"

 

Marie waves her hand away in dismissal. "No buts. Deliver her there, and you'll get your weapons. I promise."

 

A promise. Such a funny little thing, that can have serious consequences.

 

With that, Marie goes.

 

"Wait!", I yell. "What's so important about Elle?"

 

Marie smirks. "You'll find out.", she adds playfully.

 

She walks off into the distance, and melts into the shadows, leaving us with uncertainty.

We walk out of the library, unsure of what lies in front of us. Hugo eyes his surroundings while keeping his gun trained on Elle, in case she tries anything dangerous. As we come near the library's entrance, we come across a corpse.

 


Its features do not seem like a regular Infected. Bone tusks emerge from its head, and its face is entirely red and purple. Maelstrom has transformed this monster into a bastion, as its chest is littered with growths and spikes. But its arms...those are the most dynamic feature. They have been transformed into weapons of destruction, razor sharp and serrated.

 

Elle's eyes light up when she sees the Infected.

 

"Oh my god, is that really a Ronin?", she asks excitedly. Strangely, the name is familiar, but I only have a vague memory of this name.

 

When Elle sees my blank expression, she huffs exasperatedly, and hands me a comic book. I take it and flip through the pages. Inside, the survivors are triumphant after defeating hordes and hordes of Infected. They look victorious, as they set up a new government, and peace reigns over the world.

 

But that's not how things are. I look through more of the book, and I come across the "Ronin". It looks exactly like the thing in front of me.

 

"Um, Jowe," Hugo says. "Are we supposed to poke this thing with a stick?"

 

Elle takes this literally, as she grabs a wooden rod and whacks the Ronin on the head. The Ronin, now provoked, wakes up, snarling and gnashing its teeth, trying to break free from its restraints.

 

Elle screams and drops the wooden rod.

 

When the rod hits the floor, the Ronin senses this and its arms begin elongating into swords, trying to cut loose of hardened Maelstrom. It's about to break free when Hugo hits the Ronin in the back of the head, smashing it. For good measure, Hugo stomps on the Ronin's remains, to make sure they won't reform.

 

I look over to Elle, and I see panic on her face. I toss the comic book over to her, and put my gloves on.

 

"Gear up. We're moving.", I say.

A gentle breeze welcomes me as we walk out of the lobby and into the world. It's soundless as we trudge past wrecked automobiles, their hoods covered with rust. The barren road evokes memories of a more optimistic time, a time when we actually cared.

 

But all that's gone now. We're left with a dystopia, a world without dreams, without hope.

 

Without life.

 

Yet, how do we survive in such a bleak future?

 

We can only hope that tomorrow won't be the last day we set foot upon the Earth. Only, most of us aren't lucky.

 

The ones that are no longer with us got out the easy way. The rest of us, though,

 

We just have to keep fighting.

 

Nature embraces the city with its long, verdant fingers, as it has reclaimed what once belonged to it. Buildings are covered in green, giving us a sight of the past.

 

Vines swallow up edifices, which once standed proud among a city of greats. Now, the giants among us have fallen from the skies, lieing in shame.

 


It's getting close to night. Howls pierce through the night, and croaks follow. They're coming soon, but we still have time.

 

We need to find shelter, solitude. An abandoned apartment complex works perfectly for that.

 

Going up several flights of stairs, we arrive at the only room that's still standing. Hugo kicks down the door, and nobody is here. Or so it seems.

 

"Help me..." comes out of an unseen person. I whip around, blaster ready. We're low on ammunition, and every shot must find its target.

 

I expect to find a recording of a voice, but I found a person sprawled out on the ground, wearing a mask.

 

"Please...help.", is what comes out of his mouth. Hugo raises his eyebrows in question, but I know what I have to do.

 

I fire directly at the person, and no words come out of his closed mouth. His eyes are closed, lost in some other world.

 

Elle, who seems confused by my actions, tugs at my shirt.

 

"Jowe, why did you have to do that?", she asks. "The guy didn't mean any harm."

 

I reload my blaster.

 

"All people are dangerous in some way."
