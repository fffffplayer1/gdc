Elle seems dissatisfied by my response, as she looks at me with a bored face. She rolls her eyes, and scratches the back of her head.

 

"Well, since we're all here now," she says, "What are we going to do?"

 

Hugo responds before I can. "We'll scavenge this dumpster for supplies. You, go with me to the right side. Jowe can take care of himself." He grins.

 

I return to gesture to Hugo.

 

Elle, seems contented by this proposition, smiles. "We're all going to have a lovely time, aren't we?", she says with a lopsided smile.

 

I shake my head, and move away from Hugo and his naive partner. I'm going to get us some supplies, and I better hurry. The sun's setting fast.

 

I lumber over to the dining room, where it's evident age has taken its toll on the building. A huge chunk of the wall has been boarded up with wood planks, although they too, are rotting. Plaster dominates the room, and it is the unquestioned ruler. The floor, the ceiling, the table...everything. It's all covered in that cheap material.

 

I find nothing but halves of scissors and knives on the table. They can be used to make makeshift weapons, but I have no time for that. I need a new kind of weapon, something that will be reliable.

 

My answer has been found in another room. I kick down the door, and a hunting bow, along with a sheath of arrows, lie on the bed. I eagerly grab them, and try to get a feel of these new weapons. They're a bit small for my size, but I can work with that. To test the bow out, I pluck the string, and it vibrates richly. It's durable yet lightweight. Perfect.

 

I try to aim at a painting. I keep missing, but eventually I get the hold of it. Turning my head back, I laugh, as this is the most fun I've had in years, ever since James and I-

 

I drop the bow.

 

I can't remember James, his face and all the memories that come with it. Not now.

 

I sigh in dissapointment, and then I hear a floorboard creak. It's as if someone's trying to be stealthy.

 

And then I suddenly realize that I'm the unwelcome visitor.

The nine most terrifying words in the English language are, ‘I’m from the government and I’m here to help.
