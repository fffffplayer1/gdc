     The doorbell rang. Gwen rushed down the hall to answer it. “Hello there!” A mailman greeted, handing her the mail. “Big news today, huh?”

     “What news?” Gwen asked. The mailman looked puzzled.

“You haven’t heard yet? We got new word from the Crux Expedition! They actually found it! Can you believe that? It’s all over the news. The whole system is bustling about it!”

     “Hold on! Found what?” The mailman sighed, a pang of slight disappointment in his tone.

“Here, you’d better just read it up for yourself. Check the TV too.” He handed her a thick newspaper.

     “Yeah, thanks. I will.” She glanced down at the headlines. “Hey, since when do mailmen deliver to apartments?” She inquired.

“Huh? Oh, I’m not really a mailman. I’m just a volunteer. These were to be specifically hand-delivered to every household.” He reached into his bag and pulled out a moderately sized package. “It’s information on joining the Nexus Force.” He grinned, giving it to her. “I don’t know about you, but I’m definitely signing up. I’ve already been to Plasterra and Decigon just by volunteering to deliver these packages!”  He adjusted his hat. “It’ll be quite the adventure, no doubt. I can’t wait! Have a nice day, Miss! Don’t forget to open that package!” The mailman left, hurrying on to the next recipient.

     Her eyes widened as she read the article. “Imagination? Planet Crux?” She picked out all the important details as she went along. "Converting mere ideas into physical form?” This was incredible, impossible even! How could any such thing exist? How much power did this Nexus have? Wait... “Power?” A thought came to her mind as she rushed the newspaper to her room, seizing the typewriter from its spot on the floor. “Royale!” She slapped his side. “Royale! Wake up! This is important!” She shook him again. “You need to see this!” Gwen held up the paper and pointed to the front headline. “Imagination! Is this one of the powers you were talking about??” He didn’t respond. “Oh, come on, Royale!! Why won’t you talk to me?!” She slumped on the floor beside him, frustrated, waiting for an answer. “I want to know Royale…. Don’t tell me all you said really was a dream….” She frowned disappointedly, but continued talking, in case he could still hear all she had to say. “We’ll…I think I’m going to join this ‘Nexus Force,’ even if it’s just to get close. At least I can learn more about imagination…and what it’s capable of.” She sighed. “Anyway…I’ll read the rest of this and that package…and I’ll tell Uncle Otto about it. Oh, I just remembered…I need to pick up some things at the library before it closes…” She checked her watch. “Oh! I’d better go!” Gwen jumped up and hurriedly left, dropping the paper on top of the typewriter. The door slammed behind her.
