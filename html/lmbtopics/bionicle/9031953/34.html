Trust me, this arguement will make Kratark seem as average as possible. Mrcqm, I hiiiiiighly reccomend you don't reply to this, as you'll most likely rant about how I'm wrong about a LOT of things here, which will kind of null the whole point of this, making it more likely Kratark will either get heavily modified or nulled. So, now then...let's get to work and try to prove Kratark is NOT OP, shall we?

 

  1. One could argue that, due to only having 9 Kraata Powers and not a lot else in canon terms, that the incredibly strong powers Kratark has are to make up for the loss of so many other ones.
  2. Kratark's incredibly weak to light. Like, REALLY weak. Seriously, he recently FAINTED due to exposure of a rainbow that wasn't even elemental light. Thus, it makes sence that the "magic mcguffin" is actually a giant lightstone, as it will pretty much smash Kratark in one blow. And you could potentially REPLACE that lightstone with a large flashlight, and Kratark will be smashed just by being in range.
  3. OK, judging by Kratark's previous battle history agaisnt Blarkrok, he hasn't been able to corrupt Blarkrok's weapon. According to Mrcqm, a morally aligned weapon is used by either a righteous hero who has battled many evil beings, or by a completely corrupt user. Now then, Blarkrok isn't exactly what I'd call "righteous". Sure, he has LIGHT POWERS, but, no offence, he seems pretty much like the majority of the Toa in terms of righteousness: Not following the Toa Code whatsoever, instead trying to smash the opponent. Multiple times. The only Toa RPC who'd I truely call righteous is Ajtazt's RPC, Vierhl(correct spelling?), but she doesn't use a weapon. So, by that logic, pretty much any Toa could face Kratark and not have their weapon corrupted.
  4. His phantasmic Rahi aren't much of a problem.

...Wow. I ran out of space quickly. I guess the arguement is TBC...
