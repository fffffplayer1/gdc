I'd like, since there is now essentially no more discussion, propose a completed plan for the treatment of the transformation of Spherus Magna:

 

  • Spherus Magna will be divided into five biomes, each one based on a group of related Elements.
  • Each biome will be based in one area of Spherus Magna and adjacent to every other biome.
  • Each biome will have its own topic.  Major locations within those biomes can be given their own topics.
  • The Elements that the biomes will possess will be the result of a combination of the Spherus Magnan and Matoran Universe Elements (essentially the Matoran Universe Elements plus Sand, which has been given the prefix 'Ov').

The proposed makeup of biomes will be as shown below.  (Note that the names are in Matoran.  They would also be given in Agori if we had any idea what 'Region', 'Plasma' or 'Psionics' were in that language.)  Credit to RocketRacer7235 for most of the ideas.

 

  • Av-Ta-Su-Wahi: This region is mostly flat plains, but anything normal about it ends there.  The landscape is dominated by a single vast volcano which constantly belches out smoke and pools of lava and geysers of lava and Plasma can be found frequently throughout; not only that but the soil itself is crystalline, reflecting the light both from the sun and from the light sources on the ground, which in turn superheats much of the upper atmosphere.
  • Fe-Vo-Fa-Wahi: This region's ground is also reasonably flat and made up of various metals, making the whole thing look almost akin to the surface of Mars.  However, these metals are also very frequently Magnetic and often powerfully so, resulting in huge chunks of metal being suspended in the air through repulsion.  Huge, constantly-raging electric storms mean that those huge chunks of metal are liable to suddenly either change or lose their charge, making traversing the region for one who has no control over them very risky.

Le-Bo-Ga-Wahi: This region is essentially one vast jungle - appropriate given its location, covering what was previously known as Bota Magna.  Fast flowing rivers weave through the soil, forming the occasional pool or small lake.  In addition, the region produces regular thermals.

 

Ko-De-Ce-Kra-Wahi: This region is mountainous and cold.  Blizzards are commonplace and a thick blanket of snow covers almost every exposed surface beyond a certain altitude.  The mountains are known for their property of reflecting sound, but most quieter noises are muffled by the wind, making for a generally silent and peaceful location.  The mountains also possess many caves that seem to block out all light.

 

Po-Onu-Ov-Ba-Wahi: This region is comprised of a large desert of many dunes, dotted with mesas and canyons.  The canyons frequently lead down into cave systems with mining opportunities in plenty.  The region's underlying rock is particularly dense, resulting in increased Gravity throughout; in addition, Gravitational anomalies are occasionally present, resulting in sideways and even upward Gravity in some of the aforementioned caves or canyons.
