I've glanced through a few of these posts, and I think I can tell what the core of this issue is.

Mass armies.

 

There was a time when armies were rather rare; those who possessed them kept them limited, in both size, and powers. This was, in a large part, I believe, because the RP was more story/character driven. We simply didn't give so much attention, or power, to nonplayer characters. Our main characters were our focus, and, compared to our nonplayer characters, were far more powerful.

 

This began changing when users (mainly myself) within the Brother-Hood began fully taking advantage of the cannonical weapons and armies of the Makuta. As soon as we began focusing attention on our armies, our opposition did either one of two things. They either drastically increased the individual powers of their characters in relation to the Brother-Hood's armies, or they built their own armies. Today, we are voting on whether to allow metals that are tougher then protosteel. This is a direct result of the conquest, army focus of the RP. Everyday, we try to find new ways to use cannonical powers and units, in some cases, even developing completely noncannonical forces to give us an "edge" on the other users using armies. I'm not being critical of this situation, I'm just trying to share some background information of where we've been, and where we are now.

Tougher than protosteel metal is a sign of things to come. With the noncannonical creation of space stations and space ships, we can expect some user, at some time down the road to weaponize it, and convince others to do the same. That time might not be now, but it will come. When we had a charcater/story driven RP we closely adhered to cannonical material, but now, that we have moved away from that we will find it harder and harder to truely say where the limits lie. I saw a profile posted on a massive cannon, which several of you moved in to criticize. If I tried, I could make a very good case for such a weapon. You might ask me where it gets its power from, I could tell you it possesses an equally massive generator with built in regeneration energies which constantly keep the supply of energy from running out. 

 

What I'm saying, is that almost any weapon that we might currently think of as OP, could be argued, and made to seem completely feasible, and if a reasonable argument was all we needed to affirm its creation, it would be passed,  and if not now, then some time in the future.

 

Ultimately, we have a choice. We can either continue where we are now, unlinked from cannon, and deciding wether new technology seems reasonable, or we can return to only allowing cannonical material. Anything else we do, such as putting limits, or caps on things, will only work as temporary caps, because at some time, someone is going to push the limits again, and convince others to do the same.

 

Allowing tougher than protosteel armor is in keeping with everything else we have been doing up to this point, and the feasibility of its creation can not be argued against now that cannon is not our standard. 

 

I would rather not continue any further down this road, and will vote no on this issue.

Since it looks like we're going to end up with a ton of stronger than protosteel alloys, I'd like to suggest some guidelines to prevent us from going in any super crazy directions.

 

1. Any alloy tougher than protosteel must be incredibly hard to make, have a stated process by which it is created, a way to destroy it that already exists, and be limited to vehicles or even just fortress walls and buildings.

 

2. Keeping in mind the protosteel could only canonically be destroyed by Bohrok, plasma nuva blasts, swords using the material, any alloy tougher than protosteel should have very limited use and only be createable by somebody with lot's of resources. e.g. Xia, The Brotherhood, or any other major group with access to high grade production facilities.

 

3. Weight should be considered. A matoran is not likely to bear the weight of protosteel armor, let alone something tougher.

 

All in all, I still don't believe tougher than protosteel alloys (besides GSR plating, and Karunax's armor) should be allowed. I can already picture a massive war of attrition where each side is progressively making a stronger alloy. Why worry about and regulate such things when we can just forget about it altogether?

 

I also state the fact that protosteel has become severely underrated, even though it is standard for Makuta to have armor made of the stuff. Should we really worsen the situation by having people making new alloys?

 

Also remember: Tons of Brotherhood vehicles have hau shielding. Think of how hard they'll be to combat if they have tougher than protosteel armor plating?

 

All in all, these are dangerous waters we're stepping into. I highly suggest everyone reconsider their views on this matter and wether we should allow the creation of so many fanon alloys, none of which existed in the original Bionicle.
