*Karunax and Nadika just seem to stand stock-still for several long moments, with no exchange of words or even a twitch. It's even hard to tell if Nadika is still breathing. Then all of a sudden, a brilliant display of blue fire-like energy explodes around Karunax as he lets out an otherworldly, scraping roar. He stumbles around, slamming himself across the mask with his own hands, beating upon his chest, grasping the sides of his head, and throwing wild punches at the air, all the while uttering strange metal noises unlike anything ever heard before.

 

But as he continues to do this, Charix can notice that his sporadic and bizarre movements are causing him to get closer and closer to Nadika's still form, even though he doesn't even seem to acknowledge her presence. The blue energies swirling around him have become like a miniature storm, with random bolts of blue lightning lashing out all over the place, tearing across the ground and traveling short distances in the sky. The vapors then begin to pulse and compact around Karunax, and he falls to his knees several yards away from Nadika, still convulsing and writhing with no control whatsoever. With one last bestial roar, he falls flat upon the ground and lays unmoving, the energies still engulfing him like a parasite that won't let go. 

 

But then something else happens: the blue vapors extend out away from him and towards Nadika. They appear to take the shape of two incredibly long and spindly arms that end in almost equally as long and thin fingers. But they never make it, and lose their shape. The rest of the vapors appear to "catch up" to the extension, slowly but surely ripping away from Karunax and coming to a swirling rest in the space between him and Nadika. Then, Nadika suddenly snaps out of her trance, her eyes reverting to normal. She looks as if she'd just woken up from a long sleep.*

INHARAX LANDS POWERFLLY ON DESTRAL, CRACKING THE GROUND AND ENDING RUBBLE UP. HE DISINTEGRATES THE FIRST FORCE TO APPROACH HIM: Exo-suits of his own design.

 

Upon this realization, he remembers why he came. His Johun, the Mask of Guilt – the only of its kind – was gone. He takes off his Kanohi and inspects it for the first time. Slitted, black, and pitted with the results of hundreds of blows: Jalkron, Kaatonga, Tredixs, Tazzuk, Vortaxx, and most recently Demaron and his accursed Hauden.

 

The Johun was Inharax's signature, a sign of the fate he had been condemned: to forever live with the results of the past chasing him, vying to end him first, never ending.

The past was over. Let those mistakes be done. He drops the mask and stomps on it. The protosteel shatters to countless shards. Inharax selects a new Kanohi. It melds to black on his face.

 

"Excellllent..."

 

Inharax went to collect the Makuta army.
