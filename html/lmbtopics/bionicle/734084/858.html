Nicholas749 wrote:

The "Ikirro incident" was a huge blow against Karunax's ideals, and seeing all the bickering and fighting nowadays among the Makuta is only making it worse.

And it's hard to tell how he'd react if Ikirro returned. On one hand, he could unleash such fury the likes of which the universe has never seen, and on the other, he could be too disturbed to bother with anger and would simply treat Ikirro like a lifelong friend that suddenly left him to rot one day. 

OOC: I rarely have my posts rejected. And aside from maybe one or two, they've been rejected for very good reasons as of late.

 

IC: Ikirro was obsessed with his own warrior's creed, which heavily involved unity, duty, and destiny, but only to the extent that it wouldn't invite weakness.

Ikirro saw that an obsession with science usually led to a lack of morality and a great deal of disorder. Given that he was assigned the island where all out-of-control Rahi projects were dumped, it's understandable that he'd think that. Thus, almost all full on scientists invited disunity and were unnacceptable.

Many other Makuta were also untrustworthy, making him paranoid, or chose to experiment with sciend or simply be lazy instead of providing order. Thus, those who failed to consistently do their duty were unnacceptable.

As for destiny, Ikirro believes it is the destiny of those who bring stability and naturaleness to the world to always reign victorious. And he was really good at not losing.

 

In the Altronia continuity, Ikirro made a pact with some other Makuta, based around a simple ideal of "our way of order before Teridax's way of deceit."

In the RP, this pact was mainly in Ikirro's mind. Anranok and Karunax were most certainly a part of it.

However, Ikirro realized something. The Toa were not paranoid of each other. The Order always did their duty. Neither ever seemed to stay defeated. They were holding to those ideals in a way that his paranoia of his brethren prevented him from doing.

Ikirro couldn't stand that the Toa and Order not only held to his same ideals, but actually held to it sort-of-better. Excessive violence, obsessions with science, and constant spite for the Toa, who were also trying to bring order, seemed far too common in the Makuta.

Clearly they were corrupted, and as Anranok led the brotherhood to greater heights, Ikirro decided that it needed to be slowed. Smashing Anranok was far from an easy decision for him. Betraying the brotherhood was no big deal, betraying his brethren and greatest allies was another thing entirely.

 

Ikirro's first choice would be to try and convince Karunax of the problem.
