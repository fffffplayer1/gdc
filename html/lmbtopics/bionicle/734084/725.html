 "Genius friend" here

 

Salutations!

 

I have one major caviat with your patented process, and it deals with the relationship between a Makuta's actual mass and its power level.

 

First, it is logical to assume that there is a "normal" density a Makuta or Rahkshi would have to fall back on. Citing Icarax and the Rahkshi from Adventures 3: TDB, the user does not change power level based on their mass change. This means that, if Nexus could reach the size of a dust particle, he would retain his normal size power, but if he were to grow to the size of the Tahtorak, he would still retain his normal-size power. Clearly, mass and power level are not directly proportional.

 

Citing Teridax and Miserix (who took thousands of years of eating Klakk to become a 40-foot dragon), achieving ANY kind of shape change at all requires a huge amount of power and effort, and increasing mass WITH power level even more so. So that energy has to come from somewhere. While it apears Nexus could reach a tiny size, getting there (or back to normal) would require so much power he'd have to have a feast before attempting it, and certainly could not do it under battle conditions, which would be a major bummer.

 

Normally, you would only need shapeshifting and density powers to sizeshift, but Makuta have a serious stipulation on their shapeshifting abilites. They may be able to change into anything they can imagine, but there is a big price to pay to do so.

 

Actually, when writing for Author's Hangout years ago, this very idea had ocurred to me, come to think of it, and I began to wonder why Makuta never used this ability. I think Farshtey put the stipulation on the Makuta's shapeshifting to rule out rapid shape changes as a possible plot device in future storyline.

 

JAMMERS ON! LEGO613MASTER ONLY!

I choose Evergreen.
