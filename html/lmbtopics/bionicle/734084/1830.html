Zakaku begins to work on Rahori's memories. Firstly, he alters it so that Rahori wasn't disguised as a Ta-Matoran, he was disguised as a Le-Matoran, had a different mask, and an introvert that was only really friends with a snarky Plantlife Toa who just popped up after the reformation of Spherus Magna. This gave a reason as to why no one would know him and a point where he showed up. He made it so that the Plantlife Toa was also an introvert, as well, thus explaining why no one would know his disguised form. He also makes it so that the capture happened a few hours ago in a completely isolated house, rather than a few minutes in an an alley, and that he had tested if Rahori was able to swap masks, explaining the Mask of Growth, when he recieved the mental message to help against the Kaita, so, he left Rahkshi of Confusion and Fear to guard Rahori, and then left. A few moments later, a mysterious force crumpled up the Rahkshi, and Rahori then took the opportunity to get the Karz out of dodge. And for the finishing touch, he makes it so that Rahori's name is Magoni. And with that, he was done. No one except an incredibly skilled Psionics Toa would be able to tell that these memories were fake, and, going by Destiny's previous laughs at Zakaku, it was incredibly likely that he would run into the Kateman first, who would probably use their own Psionics Toa to scan his memories and fail to catch on.

 

Zakaku then teleports Magoni away to a completely different point than the one he came out of, away from that other Makuta. He would have to bribe that Makuta later, to keep up the illusion. But for now, Zakaku just lifts up two empty Rahkshi of Fear and Confusion via Magnetism and then crumples them up with Magnetism. 

 

He didn't know what would happen when he presented what looked like sabotage, but the results would be interesting.
