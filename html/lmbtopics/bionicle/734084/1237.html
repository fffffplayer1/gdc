OOC: Yes, but then you could say that of every powerful ranged weapon and call them all OP.

The sanok means your aim is 100% perfect but the projectile you use cannot home in on your target(s). That is, using a sanok on a an already moving target means you have the calculations to not miss it. Using a sanok on a target that isn't moving but later dodges means that the projectile hits where your enemy would have been.

 

Makes sense?

OOC: Indeed.  I don't see your piont, though.  And I am not sure that that is how the mask works.  Quote from the Bionicle Wiki: 'The Kanohi Sanok was the Mask of Accuracy. It gave its user the power to use almost any inanimate object as a projectile weapon and hit almost any target that he or she aimed for.'

If you're aiming at a moving jet for example, even if your aim is perfect, your projectile would miss because the jet is constantly moving forward, and the bullet needs time to travel out. If you aim where a moving object is going, slightly ahead of it, then your projectile would hit the target as it moves into where you're aiming.

 

The wiki said ALMOST any target. Having perfect aim doesn't mean anything if your target has perfect dodge.
