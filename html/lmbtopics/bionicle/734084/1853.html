tuvok9597 wrote:
keplers wrote:

This battle between Koval and Artidax is riddled with metagaming.

Oh realy?

Give me an example.

  • Koval is "mentally stronger" than Artidax --despite there being no indication of this in the past and direct proof against in the present.
  • Koval's "miracle evolution" into plasma just to counter Artidax.
  • Koval did not have a bunker until it became necessary; at that point, the bunker gained strange features: It is just barely weaker than GSR shell.
  • One Rahkshi happens to set up a teleport jammer... despite that fact that such technology is very rare and only owned by a few.
  • The teleport jammer happens to be immune to Makuta powers... somehow.
  • Koval successfully shields his own mind, despite Confusion being an instant power and having no knowledge it was coming.
  • Koval happens to have stood next to Artidax the whole time he was in stasis.
  • Koval's attack happens before Artidax was put in stasis... despite clearly happening afterward.

 

I would count summoning alternates of Koval with the Olmak to be cheating, though not metagaming. There is no guarantee the alts would ally with Koval, and that's assuming Koval knows where to look for them. On top of that, it would take some time to happen to open a portal and catch an alternate Koval, and only one Olmak portal can be open at a time, so to summon more than one would be to open oneself to attack.

 

I spent forty-five minutes compiling instances of metagaming from eleven pages of work. I would appreciate they be addressed in full or acknowledged as metagaming.
