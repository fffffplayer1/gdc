He stopped moving his fingers and pulled himself up into a sitting position. He appeared to be in an icy cave, with a small shaft of light streaming through the ceiling and bouncing off the walls, casting an eerie glow on him. It was a goldeny, almost unnatural color, so Kamma guessed it was early morning. He stood painfully, stretching his sore muscles and trying to resist a yawn. With a flick of his hand, the wall of ice reformed itself into some steps leading up out of the cave. At least that still works. He thought to himself as he pulled himself out of the cave.

 

His estimate of the time of day was correct; A golden/orange sun was slowly rising to the east of the mountain, casting it's beams into the valley below. The bright light coupled with their lack of exercise cause Kamma's eyes to water, and he shielded them with a hand as they got used to their new surroundings. 

 

The battle for the fortress.

 

Slicer, lost in the fray.

 

That weird sensation as he got near the turret, almost as though there was a strong power from within...

 

And the memories that had rushed back as he lay in a coma.

 

He sank his head into his armored hands, numb with disbelief. Was I really like that?  He thought. Was I a monster, betraying my friends, endangering my people? Nearly killing Tanidru? If Vaxis hadn't have stepped in...

 

He sighed quietly, looking up at the peaks above him. This was where he, as a matoran,  had learned to fight, to stay alive in combat. He surprised himself by smiling slightly at the thought of his matoran self laughing in glee as he dodges the whirling blades before getting smacked back down by the punchbag. He absent mindedly rubbed the back of his head, a reminder of the tough training program he'd had to endure.

 

And the big question still remained; What was he going to do now?-
