If one could somehow see into the upper atmosphere, far beyond all clouds, he would spy a very large, flying landmass.

 

Atop the island are hundreds of interceptor and bomber squadrons, staffed by many Rahkshi and troops (not RMS). There is a complete lack of any heavy ground equipment such as tanks.

Batudon notices the slight change in Gravity and flies upwards to investigate.

The island is flat and only thick enough to hold together; beyond this it is simply a rounded platform many miles long.

Batudon lands and begins to investigate.

It is covered with interceptors and bombers in neat rows, with enough room between each craft for personnel to pass through, carrying supplies or doing maintenance on the craft. There is a base in the center of the island, with a prominent tower rising from it. The tower appears to be an air control tower.

 

The personnel, while not unfriendly, inform him that this is Brotherhood private property and unauthorized personnel are not allowed. He is hereby instructed to leave.

"You're hovering above the land that we've claimed for our city," Batudon rejoins.  "Technically, you're trespassing above our property; you're lucky that I'm not currently flying this thing away.  I'm just performing an inspection that I expect will become routine if you intend to stay here."

Without any real argument against that point, and the fact that he is a Toa Mangai, they leave him alone, with a warning not to mess with anything.

Satisfied at having managed to bluff his way out of the situation, Batudon begins a genuine inspection.  He first flies over to the control tower; landing on top of it, he performs a count of the different types of craft present.

"So. More inventions, or improving existing -"

Thanks to Gamma's probability field, Crimax is extremely lucky, causing him to accidentally trigger a new power, in turn sending a bolt of energy into Alpha.

"- ones?"

Alpha's form proceeds to ripple from it's blocky shape into a more natural/smooth shape as energies which are basically a tech equivalent to those of a mask of mutation surge through him.

"...Eh?"

"...So I do have new powers? Interesting..."

*This is where Epsilon walks in with their floating globe-map and stop at the oddness.*

"Who's the new guy and why does Alpha look so different now?"

"I am Delta. If my databanks are correct, you are Epsilon?"

 

 

"I need to train with this power, if I canaffect the structure of technology that easily..."

*Crimax begins doing so.*

Crimax decides to try upgrading the existing robots a bit:

Alpha's emulation abilities are sped ever-so-slightly and given a sort of memory; They can swap between the current emulated power and the last emulated power rapidly.

Beta's armor is strengthened and so are his regenerative abilities.

Gamma is given more firepower to take down his foes with.

Delta's elemental energy systems are made a little more efficient.

Epsilon is given sharper senses, but that's about it.

"This is really useful..."

"Alright. The toa Mangai are a huge potential threat... I need a contingency plan in case they decide I'm evil or some other attack-worthy non-virtue. And that fits into the next logical robot, Zeta. A previous machine named Zetan was built to resist elemental powers, so it's only fitting that Zeta should as well... And perhaps lead some others... I already have ideas."

*Crimax puts himself and his robots to work building Zeta.*
