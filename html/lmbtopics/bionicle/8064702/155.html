Members: Krunad (Leader, Toa of Gravity and Shadow), Vuulta (Deputy, Toa of Lightning), Tholot (Toa of Ice), Rinoh (Toa of Iron), Aukum (Toa of Magnetism), and Nuera (Toa of Psionics)

 

 

Name: Krunad

 

Gender: Male

 

Mask: Shelek

 

Element: Gravity/Shadow

 

Weapon: 2 Spring loaded triple swords (Sword spilts into 3 blades)

 

Backround: Krunad, the leader of the Toa Team, was the leader of a Hagah team under Makuta Kolokvar. When the Hagah betrayed the Makuta, kolokvar personally smashed all five other members of the team. But to Krunad, his once loyal deputy, he wanted to perform an experiment. He exposed the Toa to the powers of a Shadow leech, but the process was disrupted by Vuulta, a Toa tasked with saving any Hagah survivors. Thus, Krunad now walks the line between the world of light and shadow, but even more so then Takanuva. He has both gravity and Shadow powers available to him. 

 

With his new, darker, way of thinking, Krunad formed a team of some of the most hated, hunted, and otherwise unwanted Toa to join him on his quest to destroy the Makuta, and get in charge of the universe, and bring it to order. Despite this, the Kranok all follow the Toa code, other then Krunad, who has denounced it.

So. You guys remember me mentioning the Toa Kateman? Well, here's the profile of the last guy...

 

Name: Katea

Species: Toa

Element: Earth

Kanohi: Mask of Seismic Sense: Allows user to send seismic waves through the ground. Doesn't give them an idea of the location, but does tell them if there are any beings in the area.

Weapon: Katea isn't one for fighting...however, his right arm has been replaced with a prosthetic one of his own design; and it has multiple features that can be used for combat (though these features were meant for other things).

1. Fist fomration: Armor shifts to form a giant hand. Original intention was to carry heavy objects. Increases strength in right arm.

2. Drill formation: Armor forms a giant drill. Original intention was to help with mining operations. Katea's primary choice in melee combat.

3. Multi-tool formation: The normal claw at the end is replaced with dozens of little tools. Original intention was to quickly create devices. Can cause excruciating pain if used at close range.

4. Charge formation: Turns the end into a cannon containing several Onu-Matoran mining charges. Original intention was to safely place charges without any risk of injury. Useful in ranged combat.

5. De-fog formation: End of arm turns into a device that sucks in air and filters it back out. Original intention was to help clear dust.

6. Shield formation: All armor on the arm turns into a shield-like dome able to contain Katea and a few others. Original intention was to protect Matoran during avalanches.

7. Radar formation: Turns the end of the prosthetic into a scanner that can give Katea an idea of what the area looks like. However, it cannot detect beings. Original intention was to map out areas underground.

8. Flare formation: End of hand turns into a bright light. Original intention was to help see in the mines. Can blind enemies.

9. Sound formation: Turns into a large speaker that can create an incredibly loud sound. Original intention was to drive off Rahi.

Cont.

Personality: Katea, as stated before, isn't one for combat. He's usually fascinated by technology, architectual structures, or species he hasn't seen before, and will mentally take notes on them or, more preferrably, write notes down if he has any material. He is a bit timid; however, he can still converse with people normally, Katea just doesn't talk as much. However, he wasn't always like he was now; and still retains a bit of his old, daredevil self.

Backstory: Katea used to be a Onu-Matoran in an underground village. In his spare time, he designed and invented things. However, Katea came across a Toa Stone one day, and he was transformed into a Toa. Surprised, and utterly shocked, he began to train himself, and soon, he had decent control over his element. And he also got a bit more cocky. While he still invented things, Katea became more daring, taking more risks for the fun of it, and overall having a good time.

Then he made a mistake.

One day, he was patrolling the village, when he spotted something...a Makuta. He had heard about them, and he didn't like the fact that one of them was there, so he yelled at the Makuta to explain what he was doing. When the Makuta didn't respond, Katea immediately assumed he was hostile, and charged. However, he didn't expect the Makuta to be so...powerful. The fight was over in a matter of minutes, and Katea lost his arm in said battle. And then, as if to make Katea feel worse, the Makuta then explained he was only down there to mine some protosteel. The Makuta then left, taking Katea's arm with him, and his pride. Hobbling back to the village incredibly wounded, he ended up creating a prosthetic arm with the help of the local Matoran, and decided that he should dedicate his life to helping his village peacefully.

Alright, I'm going to post the masks of each member of the Toa Kateman now (including secondary masks), since I didn't exactly state if they had any extra, and I only came up with this idea now. Keps, several of these masks are fanon, so I'll need you to judge them.

 

Cloaked Figure's Masks:

Primary: Great Olmak carved to look like its Nuva form.

Secondary: Mask of Time Perception: When active, causes user to see things in slow motion. This can be particularly useful in dire moments and the user needs time to plan. However, the mask does not actually slow down time, nor does it give the user incredible speed.

Mask of Morale: Exactly what it says on the tin. It can either boost the spirits of a group or dampen them.

Mask of Emulation

Rode

Mask of Chance: This has a confusing power. It can tip the favors for the user and his/her companions...or against them. It's entirely up to chance (OOC I'll be rolling a die to decide weither or not the Mask of Chance's results are positive or negative); and it can do both tasks very efficiently. If it ends up tipping the odds in the user, it can quickly disable hordes of enemies, give a power boost to the user and their allies, summon powerful allies, ETC. However, it can easily do just the opposite, strengthen hordes of enemies, disable the powers of the user and their allies, summon powerful opponents, ETC.

 

Fereain's Mask:

As the Mask of Vocal Control is fused to his head, Fereain can only wear that mask unless one is forced onto him, which is extremely painful for Fereain. The Mask of Vocal Control enables the user to mind control Rahi, completely overriding other mental forces, such as a Komau, without much effort through an unusual method: singing. The more complicated the task, the bigger the creature is, and the more intellegent it is, the more energy is required to mind control it. Unfortunately for Fereain, due to the Mask being fused to him, his energy is drained faster, meaning taking control of large Rahi is a large risk.

Lierins Masks:

Primary Mask: Mask of Power Amplification: Amplifies power output to extreme levels (ex: a shower of pebbles becomes a shower of average-sized boulders) for five minutes. Requires twenty minute recharge.

Secondary Masks: Mask of Probing: Allows user to seperate part of his/her conscious to insert into another beings mind. However, if user does not concentrate on said probe, it will dissapate in thirty minutes (IC, probably around two turns). 'Probe' allows user to see, hear, touch, ETC from the targets point of view.

Mask of Tracking: Allows user to track a target as long as they have something that belongs to said target, a Kanohi mask, a weapon, a small piece of their armor, ETC.

Mask of Possibilities

Felnas

Mask of Insanity: Allows user to conjure frightening hallucinations, that, if exposed to them for too long, can make a target become insane, hence the name. However, this mask has several downsides. For one, it requires eye contact, and should the user try to use the mask when a target has broken eye contact or attempts to use it on those who cannot be affected, they will get severe mental whiplash from the Mask. Aside from that, the user must have some idea of what the hallucinations are about; they must have a strong will; and this Mask quickly exhasts power. This mask cannot affect already insane people, Rahi, machines, and mind-controlled people.

 

Boyks Masks:

Primary Mask: Mask of Reverse: Allows user to turn into the opposite of their element. Fire into Ice, Stone into Earth, Psionics into Strength, ETC.  Transformation will revert if the mask is taken off.

Secondary: Pakari

Kakama

Mask of Growth

Mask of Adhesion: Allows user to stick to any non-wet/slippery surface.

Mask of Combo: When active, if the user scores multiple hits, their strength and speed increases. However, they have to keep them up, or suffer massive recoil.
