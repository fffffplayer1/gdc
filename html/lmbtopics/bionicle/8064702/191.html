Going to be retconning all of my other characters. Again.

 

Name: Lupar

 

Species: Skakdi

 

Mask: Kakama

 

Element: Earth and Lightning

 

Weapons/Tools/Defenses/Abilities: Lupar's strength is somewhat greater than that of the average Skakdi due to extensive experimenting, both in physical and power capacity, though his greatest attribute is his agility and stamina.

 

Like most Skakdi, he has been granted a vision power – in his case, he can focus on a specific object to lift it up telekinetically, the larger the object the more focus it requires. For instance, if he were to lift up a book, he would be able to do it easily, but if he were to lift up an entire building, he would be completely incapacitated for likely an entire day. Unlike most Skakdi, however, is that he has two elements which are both represented in his weapons.

 

Lupar dual-wields two axes, each with their own unique design yet remaining quite similar. The weapon in his right hand is a sleek, precise, and quick axe. It bears three razor-sharp spikes on the end of the blade, with indents that made the weapon likely usable for climbing. The axe also had a blueish tint to it.

 

The axe in his left hand is slightly larger and seems much heavier, borderlining a mace. This weapon seems like it would be more adept at heavy, crushing blows rather than swift and precise ones. It too has the spikes on the blade, however they seem to be much more dull and round, though it seems to be good for climbing similarly to its twin.

 

He also chooses to use a kanohi.

 

Appearance: Lupar, despite his excess experimentation, didn't look too far off from the usual archetype of a Skakdi, sporting the usual grin. However, it would be a bit hard to see due to the fact that he wore a Kakama that was specifically molded to fit around his face. The Kakama's shape was somewhat lupine, and seemed to have been forged from a mix of Protosteel and Rahi bones.

Descending down from his mask, his spine started on his temples, jutting out in a thick, singular cylinder that narrowed into a sharpened point resembling a Kane-Ra bull's horn (yes, he has minotaur horns). The 'horns' decreased dramatically in size following the two large ones on the sides of his head as they seemed to fuse into a singular, upward-curved horn shape that continued down his spine.

 

His muscular yet agile physique was clad in an armor that was a mix of primitive and protosteel. Resting on his left shoulder was one, large protosteel pauldron which had a thick, boiled Kane-Ra leather strap that went all the way down to his belt. He also had a thick cloak for warmth that was clipped to the pauldron as well as a piece of his armor lower down on his back. This piece of armor interconnected into the slings for his dual-axes, which he could quickly retrieve out from under his cloak should the need for combat arise. The cloak also hid the axes, making them concealed weapons.

 

Back to the front of his torso, instead of opting for a chestpiece, he merely had two Rahi bones coated in Protosteel to block weapon strikes from specific angles. Further down, his leggings were made of Kanohi Dragon scales and had protosteel kneepads. His clawed feet remained uncovered.

 

Backstory: Lupar, as with most Skakdi, hails from the island of Zakaz. Once a peaceful seer of sorts, he was amongst the first of the Skakdi to notice that they were being experimented on. Eventually convincing his Warlord to allow him to bring a small group of warriors to find the source of the strange corruption that was taking hold of their people, he finally managed to discover the labratory of the Makuta of Zakaz. The entire force was quickly decimated by the Makuta, though out of curiousity the Makuta decided to use Lupar as a test subject as he had had the mental acuity to discover where the Makuta's lair was.

-Continued from Backstory-

 

Due to having been from completely seperate tests from the other Skakdi, he was not affected by whatever the Makuta used to make the species a race of raging maniacs, however he had undergone similar changes, such as elemental and vision powers. After a while, the Makuta eventually had the audacity to free Lupar from his bonds so that he would be able to test his powers on a target dummy. Lupar, however, instantly siezed this opportunity. Blasting the Makuta with a bolt of lightning, he quickly ran over to the Makuta's armory to discover that there had been two axes that the Makuta had specifically made for him, despite the fact that he never intended to hand them over to the Skakdi. He took the axes just as the Makuta had recovered from the lightning blast, and quickly engaged him in battle. While the Makuta possessed infintely more power, Lupar managed to overpower the Makuta with strength and sheer force of will. After smashing him, he left the Makuta's lair and burned it to the ground with a lightning storm.

 

When he finally returned to his people, he attempted to rally them to leave Zakaz and use their newfound abilities to establish a new life, insisting that a perfect society could only be found elsewhere. None of them bought it, however, and Lupar angrily left Zakaz on his own to seek a new path.

 

Docking upon the shores of Stelt, he decided to sell himself into a team of gladiators to earn some currency. In his first match, the other two members of his team were smashed, however Lupar managed to single-handedly defeat his opponents. Lupar eventually became the champion of the arena he was in, and later on the champion of Stelt. He also recieved a Kakama that was custom-forged to Lupar's design.

 

After doing numerous other things, his world literally fell apart around him as the GSR was defeated. Escaping, he now seeks to forge a new life in the harsh world of Sphereus Magna.
