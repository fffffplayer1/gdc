"Fifty-seven clicks per call, each sequencing in pairs of three, rising in pitch every three sets."  +He takes his eyes off the statue and looks at the gorund.+ "...it's some form of recognition of the enviroment, or..."

+Deloth takes of his Rode, leaving his mask of clairvoyance on his face. Then he sonic-phases the mask of truth into the base of the statue.+  "My kanohi is my oath to protect the Ignika and Mata-Nui inside it, for as long as I live." +The toa nuva turns and plants his hyper lance in the ground.+

Kapronan nods.  Turning away to look out at the darkening sky, he says, "This universe needs more people like you.  The Mangai are good, but I think that sometimes they forget that their experience and powers don't give them any authority.  I suppose that they're just used to leading."

 

He turns back.  "Perhaps we should expand this idea to others.  I wouldn't mind having a Toa Team that I can properly belong to."

"Aye. I could do that."  Deloth notes the red star. "A new constellation... I suppose we've entered a new age."

+He seems to be tuned into something, then breaks the silence.+ "You're not an element-less toa, you are wise, full of valor... You're a toa of spirit."

Kapronan chuckles.  "Not an Element, but alright.  It's odd - no more than a month or two ago, nobody would have called me those things.  I have changed."

 

Meanwhile, Click has jumped from Kapronan's arm onto the statue of Mata Nui.  He scuttles towards the Ignika.  Before Kapronan can stop him, Click touches the mask, triggering a strange transformation.

 

"What in Mata Nui..."  Kapronan shakes his head in disbelief as he examines the shield - with eyes, no less - lying on the floor.

An older Glatorian of fire raises a hand. "Being that there are many races represented here, a government designed for just that would be ideal. However, it must be noted that an executive should be elected that will make the final decision, for surely if all opinions are voiced at once, nothing can be done."

A nearby Vo-Matoran crosses her arms.  "Well, maybe, but there are more Matoran than Toa and more Agori than Glatorian!  Why do ethnic groups with fewer members get the same number of representatives as those with more?"  There are murmurs of agreement within the Matoran and Agori elements of the crowd.

"Their opinion is less valuable? I don't know, shorty. I'm just throwing out options."

"Shorty?  You have no right to -"

 

"Peace," Demaron calls out.  "Let's discuss this cordially.  I agree that everybody should have a voice, but perhaps we should weight it according to numbers."

Ignu chuckles. "The only way this will be solved if you have both representation by numbers along with single representatives for all groups. And that, simply, leads to chaos. But if you have only one, then the other group will start factions and disensions."

Demaron thinks.  "You are right.  We need something to bind us together."  Thinking for a moment, he comes up with something: "The Turaga.  Agori, Glatorian: what form did your elders take?"

Write into a tablet any changes in
