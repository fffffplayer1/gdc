The Mangai begin getting things done.

 

Jammers for the allies of Atero Nui only:

Jammers
Fesur, Vonai, Fanos and Sukan begin setting up underground mechanisms.

Name: Atero Nui Adaptive Shielding and Anti-Teleportation Field Projector
Purpose: Prevent those hostile to Atero Nui from entering the city.
Description: This mechanism can be activated from Atero Nui's governmental complex and is automatically activated if long-range Biomechanics- and Rode-based sensors detect teleportation or portal opening from evilly-intentioned beings.  The mechanism records previous bypassing of the system and adapts via Adaptation Kanoka to fix the loophole in the system; it also uses Teleportation Kanoka to teleport the being, beings, object or objects who/which bypassed the system away from Atero Nui.

Name: Atero Nui Precision Regenerative and Healing Projector
Purpose: Heal beings and reconstruct objects within Atero Nui.
Description: This system automatically heals and/or regenerates damaged buildings or beings within Atero Nui.  A similar system to the Shielding and Anti-Teleportation Field Projector prevents those beings and objects who/which are morally-dubious from being repaired/healed; it also records official decisions to allow for such things as construction, demolition and infrastructure work to be carried out.

Name: Precision Telepathic Relay
Purpose: Provide a method for the government of Atero Nui to contact those within the city.
Description: Telepathy and Accuracy Kanoka project telepathic messages to beings in the city from the governmental complex.  The system currently has no mechanism for two-way conversation.

Jammers
Proposed system of government:
Each of the sixteen outer Elemental Metru districts will have five representatives in a governmental body known as the 'Assembly of the Great Spirit', with the five candidates with the most votes in a 'one being one vote' election every two years being given offices.  Any Atero Nuian may stand for office.  Those elected to the Assembly may raise, debate on and vote on motions, which, to be passed, require a simple majority.  All decisions made by the Assembly must be passed onto a second body known as the 'Conclave of Elders' who may either accept the decision or suggest changes; these are then voted on by the Assembly and the process is repeated.
