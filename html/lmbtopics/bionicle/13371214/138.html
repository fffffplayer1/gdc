@BIONICHARGE

 

OOC: The Voidwalker's mind used to be a fragment of The Uncreator's mind, which he left behind in the Void to guard his ultimate power and invention, the Red Flame. So while the first half of TU's mind went through the collapsing gateway (left behind in floating structures called Void Towers by one of those extinct super-advanced races) and returned to existence, the other half was left in the Void. Spending a great deal of time there threatened to tear apart all he ever knew and drive him insane (again), and he eventually just "gave in" and came to realize just how peaceful it was in nothingness. It started out as just an appreciation for the quiet, and given his nature, he went on to try and understand it as if it were just another device for him to conquer.

 

Obviously, something cannot truly understand nothing, so he settled for the next best thing. He took his own mind and tore it to shreds, destroying vast portions of himself. While the facts about the nature of existence and everything inside it remained, he destroyed the part where he knew it's where he truly belonged. Personas have the ability to fragment their minds almost infinitely, but doing so means that each fragment will rapidly generate into a whole. What remained of this fragment of The Uncreator ended up regenerating within the Void, with plenty of new blank spaces opened up in his mind. Nothing became his everything, his belonging. Now free of the mental constraints of reality-bound logic, he was liberated in his own mind. And then he started meddling with what he could see of existence all around him.

 

To answer your question directly, he already made the sacrifice, except on a greater scale than anyone can make. His entire mindset is bound to the Void, and by extension, his Domain. He has conditioned himself to have a different experience from those who are bound by existence, which leads us to the ultimate, undeniable truth that would propel you to victory... but I will not tell.
