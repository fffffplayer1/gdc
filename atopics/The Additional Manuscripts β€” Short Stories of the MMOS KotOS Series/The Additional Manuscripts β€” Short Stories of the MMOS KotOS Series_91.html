Time for a flashback.

 

39

Part Unknown

 

 

October 2010


  "So you're leaving now?" his sister asked.  The twenty-six year old woman stood at the stairs, looking over the banister into the entrance hall.  The front door was opened to the morning air and her brother stood at the treshold, suitcase in hand.  "You came back... just to leave us again."


  Killian's face was tilted to the floor.  He did not look up, and when he spoke it was with the subtle hint of unease.  "Marie," he said, "you're not making this easier for me."


  "And you're not making anything easy for us."


  "Marie-!"


  His sister let go of the banister and sighed.  "I'm sorry, that was selfish to say." she said.  She straightened her skirt and looked up.  "I'm not ignoring the points you made last night.  What you are doing, I understand it is for us.  We all do."


  Killian nodded, and he turned around to face her.  "In time, perhaps.  And you don't need to apologize, because you're not being selfish... I am.  There are things I haven't told the rest of the family.  Joining the Nexus Force and fighting the Maelstrom are not the only reasons I'm going to Nimbus."


  Marie looked at him hard.  For the moment, neither of them spoke.  The whooshes of cars outside on the main road permeated the otherwise still atmosphere.  Killian closed the door.


  Then Marie smiled.  "You and your secrets.  I'd always hear you and Abe confiding, and then I'd just walk by your room and suddenly, silence."


  Killian smiled, reminiscing.  "Abe doesn't know about this one." he said.


  "And I don't suppose I will know, either?"


  "No." Killian said surely, but not too regrettedly.  "But," he said, "you have reminded me of the importance of family.  Of our family."  He set his suitcase down and stepped up the stairs to give his sister a hug.  "I'll stay for breakfast."


  "Or lunch." Marie said hopefully.


  Killian chuckled.  "Don't get your hopes up."

* * *

  Killian did the right thing, and four hours later he bid his farewells to his assembled family.  He said goodbye to Marie, to their parents, to Abe, and to his young children, Aiden, Chloe, Evelyne, and little Alex.  The Talmid family was small, but they had spread apart over the years.  It was Marie who organized this reunion, unbeknownst of Killian's answer to the Nexus Force's call.  That night he had told everyone.


  Last night's arguments were not forgotten, but today they were ignored, as they did not matter and no one's minds would be changed.


  Now aboard the Venture Explorer, a Nexus Force spaceship assigned to shuttle minifigures to the Nimbus System, Killian did look back at this morning positively.  He was thankful to Marie.  He was thankful to his brother for joining them.  He was glad to see his family again, especially before departing for the Nexus Force.


  Service could be had in increments of a month, six months, or a full year unrenewed.  But service was risky.  Little was yet to be told and known about this mysterious "Maelstrom" threat.  Killian knew it was dangerous.  Many people might give their lives to stop it.  Other people would return home one day.  He did not know which group he would be part of.


  Saving imagination was a noble fight.  Fighting the good fight was what Killian signed up for.  But, as he had hinted to Marie, he had other reasons for embarking on this route of life.


  Not reasons.  One reason.  He had one other reason, and Killian could remember a time when this reason was very important to him.

 

  That reason was a she, and fifteen years later she was still important to him.
