Into the Storm and out of Darkness

 

        Flashes of red and light blue shone in the dark night sky as two minifigures crossed through it. No they were not minifigures! They were beings shaped like minifigures, but made of a different substance; a substance that did not look entirely physical and shone. One was light blue, the other was red. They were Mythrans. And they were fighting.

         Each moment they would collide and wrestle with each other, conjure flames and lightning, each of their respective colours. And every time they did any of that, the sky would be coloured blue and red, purple in the midst. They were dueling for long and neither of them tired. No one could predict how such battle would end, though no one watched it anyway.

         Then, suddenly everything changed when the Blue Mythran was hit by lightning. It struck on his orb-heart and red electricity branched all over his plasma-like body, visible through his half-transparent exterior. The Mythran spasmed mid-air until the flow of electricity stopped at which moment the Mythran fell from the skies and landed in the wilderness below.

       The Red Mythran dove to find his prey and finish it off. As the dark Mythran reached the other, he was getting up.

       “You have no power over me, Dark Mythran!” the Blur one spoke in a forgotten ancient language “You cannot defeat me!”

        “Oh! But I am not a simple Dark Mythran” the other replied with a malicious voice.

Indeed at that moment, the Blue Mythran managed to see the Red one clearly for the first time and he could see he was not the standard Red Mythran. There were flames on his head, neck, shoulders and arms, not as if he was on fire, but as an extension of these parts, made by the same essence. Electricity would crackle through his body, originating from his orb-heart and finally his eyes were not the simple generic round all Mythrans had. They were shaped to look evilly upon others.

      “What? Now?” Deofan replied.

        “I don’t see why we should wait. Besides, we don’t have any time to lose”

        “Let’s go then!” Findros said.

         “Wait, I have to wake Beolin! I do not wish to leave him here”

        “Are you sure you want him in such a dangerous mission?”

        “It would not be right to leave him out of this. As my apprentice, he is meant to be with me all the time and learn from studying all of my activities”

         Ten minutes later, they were all at Findros’s ship, which he’d parked just next to Deofan and Athena’s house. Yorkanton had built his home on the Avintderos, the Garden Hill, some time ago to be near the Nexus. Athena and Deofan had built their Crux house next to the hill in order to be near their friend. It was there that Findros had landed his ship, too.

         “I practically already know where Malfros is” Findros was saying while pressing buttons rapidly “You’ll see what I’m talking about”

        A screen turned on and a map of Galaz –one of the faces of Crux– appeared on it. It indicated Imagination and Chaos concentrations with blue and red respectively. Findros zoomed in to the centre of Galaz. East of the Mountains surrounding the Nexus, there was the Vale of the Storm, where the Blue and Red Mythrans fought each other. Between those was a little area where they were located currently. In the centre of the Vale of the Storm, there was a circle of very dense red, where blue was almost non-existent.

        “Do you see that area of dense Chaos?” Findros asked “This is where Malfros is. Only he could cause something like it. We’ll have to manoeuver our way through the Stormvale and into the Storm’s centre. That’s we’re going to need a fast and small vessel. We’ll take one of the ship’s shuttles. I think it will do the job. Probably! Anyway, Athena, you can take the ship to the Galitros Temple. You have a long journey ahead of you, so you’re going to need it. Any questions before we begin?”

        He waited for a moment “Good! Let’s roll!”

       Beolin was amazed at everything he saw and heard. He’d never been on a spaceship before, nor had he taken part in any real adventures. Now, his master was taking him to one of the most dangerous ventures of his time. He was scared, but he was equally excited.

         They entered the shuttle in a hurry and took off with it. Behind them, Findros’s ship parted with the ground and started flying, too. They were on their way.

         “How's Nirwella doing?” Yorkanton asked Findros a little afterwards.

         “She's well. A little shaken, though, by how bad things are. Last I talked to her -it was three days ago- she was on her way for Kingston. I hope to see her in person soon. It's been a long time!”

        It wasn’t long before they reached the Vale of the Storm. Red Mythrans and Blue Mythrans were everywhere on and above it fighting each other. Lightning, flames, rays of light were everywhere colouring the area in red and blue. Dark clouds were always spreading for many years from the centre of the Vale. They covered the whole Vale and beyond depriving the area of light. They were about to immerse themselves into the most intense and fast battle the Figorani had ever known. Surviving was dubious, but Findros was an excellent pilot.
