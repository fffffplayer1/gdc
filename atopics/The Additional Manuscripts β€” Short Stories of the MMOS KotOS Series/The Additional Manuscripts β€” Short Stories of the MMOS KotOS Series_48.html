PART 13

 

Jonna urged Hwin forward, and the horse began to gallop up the mountain path as arrows whizzed past them. Risking a glance behind her, she saw Chase turn around and yell obscenities at the trees, which shuddered as an armour-clad figure began to step out of them, wielding a massive sword. Jonna’s eyes widened.

 

“Faster, Hwin, faster.” She urged.

 

“What do you think I’m TRYING to do?” Her horse replied, exasperated, but she sped up nonetheless.

 

Jonna looked back again. Vladek’s knights began to emerge from the trees, but their leader hung back. Chase turned back to the front and saw Jonna looking behind them.

 

“Eyes on the road!” He exclaimed.

 

Jonna whipped her head around and began to steer Hwin up the mountain. She tried to keep her cool as arrows flew by her. More exasperated than frightened at this point, she rolled her eyes.

 

“I don’t suppose you have any way of defending us?” She asked Chase, who shook his head.

 

“I was never really the fighter amongst us.” He admitted.

 

“Great.” Jonna sighed, before unsheathing her sword. “Hold this.”

 

“Wait, WHAT?” Chase yelled in surprise as Jonna tossed her sword to him. “What am I supposed to do with this?”

 

“Hit anyone that comes close!” Jonna replied. “I need to concentrate!”

 

“Great.” Chase muttered.

Lady of Calibus

Part Three

 


The woman led thedude to the dining room. This was not the formal banquet hall- rather it was a smaller version that she held in reserve for when she had only a few guests staying at her estate, and the one she personally used when there were no guests at all. It was a medium sized room with an elaborate oak table in the middle, surrounded by matching chairs. Family portraits and tapestries containing her family's history were hanging up on the walls, the rows of immortalized, poker-faced nobles only broken by the huge double door entrance that they were coming in through now.

 

thedude immediately sat at the head of the table without being offered the seat, the gestured for his hostess to sit as well.

 

“tel ur survents 2 bring brekfust. Im hungri” he said.

 

The woman nodded to a nearby attendant, who bowed and left to alert the kitchen.

 

“noaw,” thedude said, “u r probs wundring y I came 2 vizit u, ladie sharlette. I am heer 2 qwestyon ur loyultys.”

 

Lady Charlotte lifted her chin in alarm. “Question my loyalties?” she repeated, astonished. “Your Grace, I have shown nothing but abject obedience to your reign!”

 

“troo,” king thedude admitted, “but u come frum reebellius stok. I need 2 mak shur u dont git any ideas.”

 

“I would never dream of rebelling against you, Your Grace. Trust me,” her eyes darted to the portrait of her father on the wall, “I know very well how you treat those who oppose you.”

The Lady of Calibus

Part Four

 

 

King thedude noticed her glance at the painting. "I see u reemember wen ur fathur batled aganst meh" he said smugly.

 

Lady Charlotte averted her gaze. "Of course, Your Grace."

 

"let dat bee a reemindur 2 u. i wil smash evry1 woo trys 2 halt meh advanse, just liek i did 2 ur fathur."

 

Charlotte twisted a handful of her dress. "It is a reminder every day, I assure you. I have no wish to commit such foolishness against you as he did, and will behave accordingly."

 

"guud." As thedude spoke the great double doors opened, and the servants came in with trays of food. The mood was very grim throughout the meal.

 

After the food had been finished thedude announced that he was departing immediately to visit a different estate nearby. Charlotte rose to show him out, acting very subdued. As they were passing through the outer courtyard they found that a line of laborers who where carrying a long timber beam blocked their path.

 

"owt ov deh whay slayves" thedude ordered loudly, "i am deh king! nobudie gets in meh whay, espeshially not symple pawns liek u!"

 

The line of workers hurried out of the way, casting anxious glances backwards when they could spare it.

 

"I apologize," Lady Charlotte said profusely. "I ordered for repairs to be made on the eastern wing; they ought to have known not to be bringing unsightly supplies through the courtyard when your esteemed self is present."

 

"watevr" the kind muttered, then pulled out his Ibrick to text his next unsuspecting host.

 

Without so much as a farewell from thedude, the king's footmen helped their master back into the royal tank. It started with a huge growl and tugged itself away, looking very menacing as it rolled out of the driveway.

 

Charlotte watched until it was out of sight. As soon as it was gone she raised a handkerchief to her forehead and exclaimed, "I thought that would never end!"

Lady of Calibus

Part Five

 


The maid escorted Charlotte into her personal rooms and shut the door. The Lady of Calibus sat down with an exhausted sigh, tossing her handkerchief onto the desk.

 

“How do you think I did? I hope I didn't give anything away,” she said.

 

“Not at all, Ma’am,” the maid assured, “You were splendid! I don't think the thought ever even crossed his mind.”

 

“I'm glad you think that,” Charlotte breathed. “I was terrified that he was here to confront me about the mines we are building! Please, fetch Austin.” The maid curtsied and rushed off.

 

Ten minutes later she returned with a man who looked to be in his late twenties or so. He pulled his hat off and inclined his head tersely. “My Lady,” he greeted shortly, “So he's left then?” Charlotte nodded soberly.

 

“Curse him!” Austin growled under his breath, looking away darkly.

Lady of Calibus

Part Six

 

 


Lady Charlotte gave the younger man a sad glance. “Watch that tongue of yours, sir. I already know too well how you feel about our king.”

 

“You should have let me go out there! I could have avenged... avenged....” His knuckles turned white as he cruelly gripped his hat.

 

“Avenged your family? How can you avenge those whose fates are a mystery? They may yet live. Besides,” she shook her head, “thedude is powerful. He would have destroyed you, and then out plot would have been found out.”

 

Austin clearly did not agree, but merely straightened out his hat with harsh movements then changed the subject. “Did he suspect anything?”

 

“I don't think so. Looks like it was nothing but a routine scare tactic, going around and threatening all the nobles to stay loyal to him. As if the constant taxes, patrols and inspections weren't enough already! But I am getting ahead of myself- our secret is safe. Soon we can begin quietly recruiting. Then the rest of my plan can slowly be brought into action.” Charlotte stood up and went to the window thoughtfully. “I'm certain we can muster an adequate token of troops within the next few years. My plan will take many, many years; yet it is better to plan carefully and silently than to rush into outright denial, as my dear father did.”

Lady of Calibus

Part Seven

 

 

 


The maid was still standing by the door. She had served in Calibus Tower for countless years and knew its inhabitants as one would know a dear friend. Even so, she had her reservations about Charlotte's scheme, yet had not voiced her doubts out of respect. Now she said, “If I may speak openly, I'm surprised you're still planning to fight against him, after your father's death and the oath of loyalty you made!”

 

Lady Charlotte plucked the bejeweled comb out of her carefully pinned hair, suddenly allowing it to unravel in long, silky, graying curls. “Some oaths are made to be broken,” she replied coyly, looking back at them with a small smile on her face.

 

“Never thought I’d hear you say that!” the maid said with a fond grin.

 

“Perhaps you are more like your sister than people say,” Austin added with some amusement.

 

Charlotte's smile faded. She turned to the window quietly.

 

“We did have our similarities,” she agreed softly. Placing the comb on the sill, she pushed a lock of hair from her sober face. “We used to play chess, you know. Amelia always got upset and stormed off whenever I won.” She looked out over the courtyard, watching her dutiful vassals scurrying across the cobbles, calling out to each other as they labored to construct the mine. Pawns, thedude had called them.

 

“I always won.”
