My review of the story

 

Pros:

  • Despite only having the span of a short story to develop the character for 10 different heroes, you did an amazing job characterizing them and giving each of them a distinct personality. You also characterized the faction wars very well in the limited span of a short story.
  • The battle was epic and for the most part flowed very smoothly
  • The cliffhanger at the end of the story leaves one wanting moar.
  • I actually was thinking to myself "this battle seems a bit too easy" during the fight description, which you were probably intending. Good job on that.
  • The smashing of two of the heroes at the end of the story was startling and quite effective.

 

Cons:

  • It seems kinda anticlimactic that at least two of the ten heroes were summoned by the prophecy only to be smashed at the end of the mission. There was all this hype about them being one of the ten, but then it turns out their only purpose was to get smashed. If this was Star Trek, Malia, Selis, Amanda, and Carter would all be wearing red shirts. Maybe even Xeno too.
  • The whole scorpion prophecy for Bob didn't make sense.  The other prophecies had to do with the character's backstory, whereas Bob's seems like it was randomly chosen and assigned to Bob. When I first read the prophecy, I thought that it had something to do with Vladek, because Vladek's symbol is the scorpion, but then Bob appeared out of the blue. Also, there is the fact that it is a self fulfilling prophecy. Bob went to the Hills of Binding because the prophecy said the hero would be found there. Regardless of where the prophecy said the first hero would be found, that's where Bob would have been sent and that's where the hero would be found.
  • The whole thing with Duke Exiter forgetting Shadow's name seemed forced.

 

As a whole, I think that this was an amazing short story; I give it 7 out of 10. (7 out of 10 is a high score on my strict grading scale )

SEGMENT 1 – THE EASY LIFE

 

“Each friend represents a world in us, a world possibly not born until they arrive, and it is only by this meeting that a new world is born.”

 

1

 

It was, on the whole, a sight not entirely unwelcome that awoke Iamos Wilder from his slumber. It admittedly could have been more comfortable – his ribs felt like they were being crushed – but this did little to dull his mood as he caught sight of the inquisitive eyes of his younger sister, Azura Wilder, turned on him. As he showed signs of consciousness, a beaming smile appeared on her face.

 

“Good morning, big brother!” She practically sang, leaning close to Iamos’ face as she announced her words.

 

“Your breath smells.” Iamos deadpanned in response.

 

“How rude!” His sister blanched as his statement and leaned back. “I’ve already brushed my teeth this morning!”

 

“I was kidding, Azura. Don’t worry about it.” Iamos rubbed his sister’s head affectionately as he sat up. “And how might you be this fine day?”

 

“I’m doing well, thank you very much.” Grinned Azura. “Just be careful with how you speak, brother – that sounded close to olde speech.”

 

“And so what if it does?” Rolling his eyes, Iamos slid his legs out of the bed covers as he continued to converse. “It’s not my vault my vocabulary is extensive, I read a lot.”

 

“Yeah, but they say that the Eeeevil one known as “TehDewd” will come all the way from Morcia to get you if you keep speaking it.”

 

“Considering we live on the other side of the continent and in a separate nation, I don’t think I have much to worry about.” Iamos laughingly acknowledged, beginning to dress himself; starting with pants, as most do. “So, what’s happening today?”
