39


  Sir Talmid watched the sun set as he made his way to a mountaintop pass.  Along the way he found Ember, who joined him at his side.  They walked in silence, but the knight's mind was active - he was playing with time, an amount of which he did not know if he had.  He hoped he had enough time for one, final, plan.


  When they reached the mountaintop, a small, open mesa overlooking the Athelas Mounts, with the great plain of Aquila in the northern horizon and a hint of Morcia to the south, Sir Talmid had his plan sorted out, and he turned to Ember.  He knelt to her height and put his hands on her shoulders assuringly.


  "The gist of this is, we're turning ourselves over to thedude." Sir Talmid told her.  "But I shant forsake you.  So, I need you to create an illusion of yourself.  Create the illusion of that purple smoke, as well, rising around the mountain.  Put the illusion of yourself up here, next to me, and keep it up for as long as you can.  You can do it."


  The girl did not stir, but as Sir Talmid watched, a field of the disabling gas, an illusion of it, appeared behind her.  He turned to watch her handiwork.  It surrounded the mountaintop, fading at the higher altitudes, but creeping over the rocks as well, across the ground and around their feet.


  And then, when Sir Talmid turned back to Ember and his hands, that rested on air, dropped to his sides, he realized that for all of this time he had been staring at an illusion as well.
