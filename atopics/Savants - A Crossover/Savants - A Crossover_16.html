Chapter 13 Part 1:

 

"So glad we could see eye to eye." Crooned Norik.

 

"Where... where should I start?" Cadmus hissed, doubtlessly with all the venom in his voice he could muster.

 

Norik pretended to ponder the question. "Well, there are a few things I'm interested in. Firstly, what is the overall goal of The Twelve of Infinite Sight?"

 

"The security and peacekeeping of the universe." Replied Cadmus.

 

Well.

 

That had certainly come out of left field.

 

"Is that so?" Norik couldn't help but laugh. "Yeah, you're doing a real fine job so far. Okay, who's it led by?"

 

"A man known as Damien Ironsides."

 

"Can't say I've heard of him. Should I have?"

 

"No. His general existence is anonymous."

 

"Once again, doing a real fine job. Why are you so interested in the gate, anyway? The dimensional gate, I presume?"

 

"It is an anomaly." Cadmus' voice grew stronger as he spoke, as if he was being empowered by his own words. "Left unchecked, it could potentially cause devastation."

 

"And why didn't you get it beforehand, I wonder? I can only guess you can't detect the gate itself, but rather when it's turned on. I guess... it's energy, or something?"

 

Cadmus said nothing.

 

"Ah well. Suppose that's not too important. Alrighty then, answer me this. If you're really trying to keep the peace, why did you start attacking us when you got here, as opposed to knocking on our front door and asking if we could spare a moment to talk about interdimensional gates."

 

"Aside from the fact you don't have a designated "front door"?"

 

"Yeah, aside from that."

Chapter 13 Part 2:

 

"People can be posessive of dangerous objects, potentially even corrupted by them, and will rarely be willing to relinquish them without a fight. We were already aware of your identities as troublemakers... though we didn't know you existed."

 

"Yeah, well, we're full of surprises." Snarked Norik. "Also, as a side note, how much of that was stuff that you WEREN'T programmed to say."

 

Cadmus raised an eyebrow. "I don't know what you mean."

 

Norik threw his hands up in the air in exasperation.

 

"Oh, come ON, man! I know brainwashing when I see it. Somebody has NOT been telling you the full truth. I..." He sighed. "I don't even care, right now. I'm not in the mood to argue. But - butbutbutbut - is there any method of identification we can use to figure out who's a member of the twelve and who isn't?"

 

Cadmus bit his lip.

 

"Unless you want your friend to... I don't know, suffer in some way, you should probably answer." Reminded Norik. Sighing, Cadmus rolled up the bodysuit sleeve and brandished the underside of his forearm. Squinting, Norik saw the words A CLASS - 016 tattooed onto the intruder's skin.

 

"Every member will have one of these." He confessed.

 

"I see." Norik peered closer. "Exactly that?"

 

"The number'll be different for everyone." Amended Cadmus. "And... some will have an "S" instead of an "A"."

 

"I see. Which should we be more weary of?"

 

""S"."
