Chapter 8 Part 2:

 

"Much appreciated." Cooed Jonna as she sauntered forwards, past Dante. Talia followed suit, offering a much less voluable "thanks" as she did so.

 

Jonna turned her attention back to the room they had entered, and her eyes widened as she took in the sight. An large portion of cave had been hollowed out, leaving an enormous room in the shape of a dome constructed entirely out of glistening, transluscent crystal that sparkled in the faint light produced by the electrical lights hanging from the ceiling. Hanging off the wall to the left was a large screen - currently deactivated and lightless. Below it was a crimson recliner chair with what appeared to be control panels just to the sides of the armrests. On the other side of the room was a bed next to a desk which had a laptop on it, screen fllickering and relaying information. Beside it was a chair that looked a lot more worn than the other, complete with a rickety set of wheels enabling mobility. The ground was smooth, if carpetless, and gave little indication of the less-than-formal setting the room was based in.

 

Dante pressed forward, Ivy over his shoulder, and approached the wall directly parallel to the door, which he pressed again. A small new cavern opened up, and a control panel sprung out of the wall. Without hesistation, Dante tossed the cotamose woman into the opening before pressing a button on the control panel. A thin, azure beam of light flickered into existence over the hole, like a window.

 

"That should hold her." He hypothesised with satisfaction as Jonna shook herself out of her awe.

 

"Do you... live down here?"

 

"Oh, yeah." Dante shrugged. "Sorry about the lack of seperate rooms. It's normally just me, so I didn't bother with all of that."

 

"How does your tech even work down here?" Queried Jonna.

 

"Well, it's actually these crystals." Explained Dante, tapping the wall. "These things act as a power supply, so I can just set stuff down here for an indefinite amount of time."

Chapter 8 Part 3:

 

"That seems... convenient." Jonna placed her hands on her hips. "Anyway, not to be antisocial, but we came here with a purpose."

 

"Pray, tell me your purpose." Dante sank into the wheeled chair, keeping his eyes on Jonna the whole time. "I'm intrigued. This is the first time I've seen a Mythran before - and one who was once a stromling, no less... it's quite the parallel to my fall from grace."

 

"Well, this is the first time I've seen someone be able to be in such close proximity to maelstrom and not be infected." Pointed out Talia.

 

"Touche." Acknowledged Dante. "Man, now I really want to introduce you to Gaia."

 

"Who?" Jonna asked.

 

"A friend of mine. I can explain later. So, your purpose?"

 

"Ah - yes. We came here looking for two friends of ours." Talia walked towards Dante and looked into his eyes, light blue meeting dark blue. "They're called Peragrine and Percevel. Have you-"

 

"I'm just going to stop you there." Dante raised a hand as he spoke. "The answer's no, I haven't seen them. As a matter of fact, you two are the first people I've met in person in over a month and a half."

 

"Wait, what?" Jonna gaped at him, but Talia remained determined; making Jonna feel uneasy once again.

 

"You said you have friends. Could you get in contact with them?" She pressed. Dante shrugged.

 

"I can try, but we're pretty busy as is. I don't know if they'll appreciate pooling our resources into finding a couple of missing persons."

 

"Well, could you at least show us a way out?" Jonna stepped forward, putting her misgivings aside. "We'd probably be able to look for our friends a lot better if we were out of these caves."

 

"I could show you away, but that would mean I'd have to go with you. And frankly..." Dante's eyes trailed across the room to where Ivy lay, still unconcious, in the small cell.

 

"...I'd rather not leave her alone inside my home." He finished. Jonna could see his logic.

Chapter 8 Part 4:

 

"Besides," he continued, "would you really be comfortable with leaving the woman who attacked you unsupervised?"

 

"I guess not." Admitted Jonna.

 

"Exactly." Dante nodded. "I don't know about you, but I think that we should get some answers pronto. The sooner we know what's going on, the sooner we can act and get word out to my team."

 

"Why do we need to know, though?" Jonna put forward. "Couldn't we just... I don't know, keep her locked up, or something?"

 

"Jonna, was it? I know you want to get your friends and get out of here as soon as possible, but we need to face the facts. Someone is after your friend for what she is. Not only that, but they were able to detect her arrival in this dimension and send a force as soon as they could to apprehend her for their presumably nefarious purposes."

 

"That doesn't explain why you care."

 

"This happened on my watch, which means it's my problem. Something tells me Ivy isn't acting alone, and even if you manage to leave safely I'll still be stuck here with whoever sent her. What if they decide I know to much? What if one of my crew comes into their interest. No, the best thing I can do is track the problem to it's source and stop it now, before everything gets out of hand." Dante looked directly at Jonna. "I'd appreciate any help you could give me."

 

"What? Look, Dante, we don't WANT the trouble! What we want is-"

 

The man banged his fist on the desk behind him. "The trouble's already found you, darn it!" He snapped, glowering at Jonna, who gave an uneasy start as she realised that his eyes were purple once again. "Dante layed it out for you, I think, perfectly reasonably, and your best hope for safety is-"

 

Raelyn was cut off by a moan from Ivy, who was coming round in the cell.

 

"Perfect timing." Raelyn stood up, and glared at Jonna. "This conversation isn't over."

 

With that, she stormed over to the cell and crouched down, meeting a startled Ivy eye to eye.

 

She growled. "Tell me everything about the Twelve of Infinite Sight."

Chapter 9 Part 1:

 

"Found... him..." huffed Percevel, holding a grinning Peragrine by the collar. Plue and New Guy exchanged glances as they looked at the dysfunctional duo, before shrugging.

 

"Good to see. We managed to subdue that Cadmus bloke." Plue told him.

 

"Good to hear." Acknowledged Percevel. "What's next?"

 

New Guy folded his arms. "Answers, obviously. I don't know about the rest of you, but I want to find out why this jerk attacked us."

 

"He mentioned the gate as a motivation." Mused Plue. "Could he have detected that it was activated, somehow?"

 

"What gate?" New Guy raised an eyebrow.

 

"The dimensional gate that Peragrine and Percevel came from, obviously." Plue deadpanned, ignoring the confunded look on New Guy's face. Taking pity, Percevel walked forward whilst dragging Peragrine behind him and placed a comforting hand on the young man's shoulder.

 

"I'll explain everything at a more convenient time." He said. New Guy still wore a look of confusion, but managed to nod nonetheless.

 

"No, it's fine." He murmered quietly. "I've seen wierder..."

 

"If you're sure." Percevel turned back to Peragrine, who was subtly trying to worm his way out of his friend's grip. Growling, Percevel wrapped his arm around his younger's neck and put him in a headlock.

 

"As for you, where were you when we were fighting for our lives?" He snapped. Peragrine cocked his head, curious at the words, as he strained to look Percevel in the eyes.

 

"I don't know what you mean by that." He stated. "All I was doing was laying a trap with Gaia - oh, yeah, it was BRILLIANT, by the way, hopefully someone'll trip that wire soon enough - when she perked up and said something about danger and then ran away, leaving little old me to fend for myself. I mean seriously!" He stretched his hands out as wide as he could, considering he was still being restrained. "I have no idea where anything is in this place! This isn't even my world! For all I know, the universe could comprise of boring metal corridors!"
