"Jonna?!" Talia Sneeko, stromling-turned-mythran, exclaimed as she caught sight of her old friend. "I-I-I-"

 

"We have elected to send Mythran Talia as a representative of our kind in this other dimension." The former mythran filled in.

 

"Did you know about this?!" Gaped Jonna. Talia shook her head violently.

 

"N-no! All I knew was that I was going to be sent somewhere because of my... um... mythran/civic duty, or something! They gave me instructions, but - why, what's happened?!"

 

"Peragrine and Percevel have gone missing!" Blurted Jonna, struggling to remain whelmed by an influx of emotions, and proceeded to relay to Talia what Gambit and Thomas had told her.

 

"Oh, no! Is that why-"

 

"We felt as though, due to your personal connection with the missing persons, you would be more efficient and motivated than anyone else."

 

"Well, you're not wrong there." Talia began to calm down, and her eyes flashed full of power. "Now that I know they're missing, I don't plan on resting until I get them back!"

 

"Admirable motivation." Declared the mythran.

 

"So... how are we going to get to this other world?" Jonna asked. Seemingly in response to this, the mythran tossed a cube-shaped device to Jonna, who caught it in her hands before clumsily fumbling with the object.

 

"That is a method we ourselves have devised of interdimensional transportation." The mythran said. "The frequency has been set to match the one that the gate was emitting. You shouldn't arrive inconveniently far away from them - say, on the other side of the universe - but you will have to figure out the details of their rescue yourselves. If you want more detail, Jonna Fig, mythran Talia has been given more specific directions."

 

"Well, that's convenient." Jonna mumbled as Talia ran to her side. "Man, you mythrans love your cubes, don't you?"

 

"May fortune be in your favour." The mythran declared as Talia pressed a button on the cube. The next second, the room spun and Jonna found herself in another whirlwind.

Chapter 3 Part 2:

 

"If she's anything like Peragrine, I shudder to think what the two of them might have already done." Percevel mumbled to himself, shaking his head in dismay. Plue nodded.

 

"Be that as it may, we definitely have more important things to worry about at present." Her blonde hair fell in front of her face as she lowered her head, resting her chin on her knuckle as she walked. "Such as the question of how you're supposed to get home."

 

"Indeed." Percevel nodded his agreement. "We just have to hope that, in the meantime, nothing too disasterous-"

 

At that point, he was cut off by the sound of a rending and a crashing, followed by a hissing noise. As the two wheeled around, smoke began to pour around the corridor towards them.

 

"What the heck?!" Gaped Plue as Percevel grabbed his powerjouster off his back. Pointing the axe-spear at the cloud, he bellowed, "Whoever's there, come out with your hands raised!"

 

Instead of recieving the reply he had hoped for, there was a ripple of energy across the room and a silvery construct in the shape of an enormous hand slammed into Percevel, sending him sprawling onto the floor.

 

"Percevel!" Plue called helplessly, before growling and raising her katana in front of her face. Out of the smoke stomped a heavily armoured figure, almost as brawny as Vector. He was clad in shiny metal armour, a dark silver with crimson decals around the edges. In one hand he held a spiked mace, and in the other he held a shield made of a completely silver metal. His brown hair bounced slightly in the wind created by the steam, and when he opened his eyes were the colour of his shield; a deep, haunting, almost threatening silver. Plue's primary instinct was to back away, and only her training prevented her from doing so.

 

"My name," spoke the knight, "is Cadmus of the Silver Shield." He held the mace in front of him, threateningly.

 

"I have come for the gate."

Chapter 4 Part 1:

 

The scream died in Jonna's throat as her eyes began to see properly again. Glancing around, she saw that Talia and her had been transported to what looked like an enormous cavern, hollowed out unevenly. Crystals of different colours were jutting out of all the walls, their surfaces gleaming with an almost polished sheen. There was silence once the noise of their transportation died down, save the occasional drip of moisture from the ceiling. Talia breathed an uneasy sigh.

 

"Great." She murmered. "We arrived. Now we just need to conserve the power of the olmak-"

 

"Olmak?" Questioned Jonna. Talia nodded, and held out the cube.

 

"Yes. The olmak is the portable dimension-travelling device we have been given. As I was saying, we need to conserve it's power if we want to get back with Peragrine and Percevel."

 

"Uh... sure. I'd like to be able to return." Mumbled Jonna to herself as Talia scanned their surroundings.

 

"No sign of anything dangerous." She murmered. "The question is how we're supposed to locate Perce and Perry... I don't have any idea where they could be..." She turned on her heel and began to march up a pathway leading to another segment of the cavern. "We should probably start by getting out of this cave first."

 

"Okay, hold up!" Jonna clambered, uneasily, to her feet and sprinted over to Talia. "What's happened to you?"

 

Talia cocked her head to one side, bemused. "What do you mean? Nothing has happened."

 

"Yes, it has!" Protested Jonna. "You... you've changed, Talia. Last time I saw you, you were all... scared, and socially awkward, and kind, and adorable, and all these other different things! And now... you're just... stoic."

 

"And I thought I was the only person who'd been through such a dramatic shift of personality type." A voice remarked. Wheeling around, the girls saw the silhouette of a man approach from another tunnel.

 

"Let me guess... you two aren't from around here, are you?"
