"I don't know."

"I don't know."

 

"Where did it come from?"

 

"I DON'T KNOW."

"I know that you're a literal pop-up book from hell."

 

"Vector! Gaia!" Plue Abernathy meandered into the storage room, which currenly had an empty space in the middle in order to make room for the strange construct that the away team had discovered. It was a very large sepherically-shaped, hollow device that could easily fit a group of people within it's center. The exterior was lined with runes and mismatched hunks of metal, and according to Gaia it was emitting a very suspicious aura. Granted, she then immediately said that was because of the rubber aliens and that Pandemonium's Bane should surrender while it had the chance, but there was still something off-putting about the object that made Plue eye it with suspicion as she walked over to the quibbling pair.

 

"Yes boss?" Gaia saluted, sanding tall. Her dark brown hair, dyed with purple streaks, flowed down her back and her lime green eyes glinted with their usual eagerness as she stretched to her full impressive height in order to respect the Sentinel faction leader.

 

"Hey, how come you don't treat me like that?" The Venture league's commander, Vector Jadewing, glowered at his companion. Despite Gaia's unatural height, Vector was still at least a couple of inches taller than her due to his muscular build. His black stubble clung to his face, desperatly trying and failing to form a beard, and numerous weapons clattered on his back as he shifted his position.

 

"Because you don't deserve my respect." Answered Gaia.

 

"Why not? I'm the same rank as Plue!" Vector protested.

 

"Good sir, you're no leader of mine." Gaia declared.

 

"I doubt anyone would want a soldier like you, all things considered." Admitted Vector. "Scratch that - do whatever you want, just don't "help" me in any way."

 

"Ah, sir!" Gaia turned her salute on him. "What can I do for you? Lick your boots? Inflate your ego, sir?"

Gambit lowered his wormholer as he stared at the now lifeless metal circle, then turned to the Assembly.

Chapter 1 Part 2:

 

"Now I KNOW you're just trying to annoy me!" Snapped Vector, growing impatient with Gaia's taunts. For her part, Gaia simply shrugged and appeared nonchalant.

 

"Oh, stop it Vector. I'm struggling for breath because your ego is taking up all the oxygen."

 

"What is it with you and my "ego"?! I don't have an inflated ego at all!"

 

"You once called your face "proof of the existence of a divine entity"-"

 

"Guys! That's enough!" Plue held up her hand, and her friends fell silent. "I hope you still realise that we're faced with the issue of what the heck this thing is?!"

 

"Beats me. All I know is that SOME of us put themselves - and me - through hell to get it." Vector muttered, pouting and folding his arms.

 

Gaia glared at him. "Hey, I stole nothing, broke nothing, and stabbed nothing - you should be proud of me!"

 

"That's about all you DIDN'T do..." Reminded Vector. Plue pinched the bridge of her nose as the two continued to argue. It was bad enough that the leadership of the Sentinel faction, and consequently the Nexus Force, had fallen to her after the battle of Crux Prime, but did every headache she seemed to gain have to be multiplied by the quibbling of the leader of one of the other factions and the deputy of an elite organisation?

 

The answer was an obvious "no".

 

"Look, can we just-" She was cut off by a low humming sound.

 

"I don't even... hey, do you hear that?" Vector tilted his head.

 

"Yup." Confirmed Plue.

 

"I don't know what that is, but it's getting on my nerves." Gaia shook her head from side to side, reminiscant of a dog. "Can someone shut it off, please?"

 

At that moment, the humming turned to a screech and a crash. Shifting her gaze upwards, Plue discoverd the source of the secondary noise: a piece of the device's exterior had slidden along the outside and fallen into a slot with a loud ca-chunk.

 

Plue, Vector and Gaia stepped backwards and watched as the machine continued to whir and hum, centuries-old mechanics being reactivated seemingly at random.

Chapter 1 Part 3:

 

"Okay, that's moving." Gaia muttered. "That's moving, that's moving... is it supposed to do that?"

 

"Looks like that's what it was designed for." Mused Plue. "The question is what's going to happen-?"

 

"If you ask me, we shouldn't be staring at it unless from a distance." The Pandemonium's Bane deputy interrupted, her eyes showing a mixture of anticipation, nervousness and excitement as she continued to watch the contraption move. "All in favour of using Vector as a battering ram to escape from this room, say aye."

 

"Once again, the door's open." The buccaneer retorted, proving his words by opening said door. "And I'm never letting you throw me at a door again."

 

"You weren't greviously injured!" Protested Gaia as she and Plue headed for the aforementioned door. She had to raise her voice to be heard over the ever-increasing hum and whirr of machinery.

 

Vector looked like he was about to say something back, but the noise died in his throat as he indicated with his hand. The girls turned around and saw a blue energy field flickering into existance within the structure. The roar became almost unbearable and the other items in storage started to tremble. Plue could only stare in horrified anticipation with her hands over her ears. Just when it seemed that the noise could not get any higher, two objects were flung out of the gate. One landed on the floor nearby with a loud clatter, and the other slammed into a thankfully empty crate, initiating a *cruch* sound.

 

The energy field died down, and the noise faded. Plue felt the hairs on the back of her neck begin to die down, until an unknown voice rang out.

 

"Peragrine... PLEASE... don't every do that again."

 

Plue stared as the object near the gate as it rose, and realised with a jolt that it was an armoured person. They rose, and she saw blonde hair so light it could be compared to white. The new arrival adjusted his glasses and glowered at the other person.

 

"Now, what sort of trouble have you gotten us into now?"
