Chapter 9 Part 2:

 

"Just so you know, it doesn't." New Guy filled Peragrine in. "Look, can we ignore the kid's unobservance for now? We have an interrogation to get done."

 

"Right. Come on you three." Plue ordered, and marched to where they were keepng Cadmus, the cell block, with the three men in tow behind her.

 

As a stylistic choice, the cell block had been made to look more run-down than the rest of Pandemonium's Bane HQ, with a greenish hue to some of the metal and lights that flickered every now and then. Plue stopped outside a door labelled 03.

 

"Okay, he's in here." She declared. "Volunteers for interrogation?"

 

"I'll do it." New Guy stepped forward.

 

Plue raised an eyebrow at him. "You sure?" She queried.

 

"Look, as much as I hate to admit it, I'm not entirely sure what's going on right now." New Guy glanced around at the visitors. "All this talk of gates and stuff. But I have experience with getting into people's heads. Let me do something I'm good at, okay?"

 

"How old are you, anyway?" Plue asked him, wondering about his specific skill set and how he could have gained it so early on in life.

 

"Seventeen, give or take." New Guy shrugged. "I'm not entirely sure when my birthday is, so..."

 

"Pretty rich of you to call me a kid." Choked Peragrine, Percevel's arm still wrapped around his neck.

 

"You say that." New Guy leaned down to glare at the hyperactive youngster. "Not only are you shorter than me, which is quite the achievement considering I'm smaller than average, but I can't tell if your voice has even broken yet-"

 

"Okay, that's enough." Plue remarked, holding out a doorkey. "You can compete for the position of alpha male later. Right now, didn't you VOLUNTEER to take the interrogation."

 

"Right." New Guy nodded apologetically, took the key, and sauntered to the door; pausing before his hand reached the handle.

 

"A word of warning." He deadpanned. "Don't come in until I come out."

 

With that, he swung the door open and walked in. The door shut behind him with a sense of finality.

Chapter 9 part 3:

 

"What an ominous fellow." Mused Percevel.

 

"Yeah, I'm going to have to ask Dante some questions about him later..." Addressed Plue, stroking her chin slightly.

 

The silence that followed was remarkably awkward.

 

"Soooo..." Peragrine eventually uttered after a while. "Mind letting me go, Perce? I value being able to breath as much as the next guy, after all."

 

With a disinterested grunt, Percevel released him.

 

"Woo!" Peragrine stretched his neck and arms. "Man, that was cramped. Got one heck of a grip on you there, big guy."

 

"I'm not "big guy"." Rumbled Percevel.

 

"Yeah, yeah, you're the smart one too. Man, whoever handed out genes was totally biased towards you. Man, you've got everything! Muscles, a brain, lovely blonde hair..."

 

"I wear glasses."

 

"Which only increase your cool factor! You can push them up your face whenever you're making a point!"

 

"Do we not have more serious matters to discuss?" Percevel sent a meaningful look towards Plue, but before she could say anything Peragrine continued.

 

"Oh, lighten up, Perce! Look, I know this looks pretty bad, not knowing how to get back and all, but the fact of the matter is we're in another dimension! This stuff only happens in the net dramas! Shouldn't we take the opportunity to hang snake and have some fun, or learn something, or... I don't know, just take advantage of the situation in some way?"

 

"Return is our first priority." Percevel told him, sternly. "And the fact is we have no idea how to do that. Maybe you haven't remembered the fact that we don't even know how TIME works relative to our dimension. For all we know, the people we left behind might live out their whole lives in the time it takes for us to eat a meal! And don't forget the people themselves! Gambit, Thomas, Jonna, Talia-"

 

His voice broke.

 

"Trinity." He murmered. Peragrine's face fell.

 

"Shoot." The boy cursed. "Perce, I'm sorry."

 

"It's fine." Percevel hissed softly. "It'll... it'll be alright."

Chapter 9 Part 4:

 

Plue was about to speak works of sympathy, but before she could do so she heard voices coming from a turn-off in the corner. Turning her head, she caught sight of Gaia and Vector meandering towards them, quibbling as they walked.

 

"You're insane."

 

"I know."

 

"That is literally the stupidest idea you have ever had."

 

"How would you know that?"

 

"You tell me all your ideas, and then expect me to be proud of you."

 

"Aren't you partial to chaos?"

 

"No, not really, I'm not a huge fan. I prefer things with limits."

 

"Oh. Well, why didn't you tell me you appreciated my ideas?"

 

"Would you have listened?"

 

"..."

 

"Exactly."

 

The two stopped walking and arrived in front of Plue. Vetor jerked his thumb over his shoulder to Gaia, who was pulling a face at him. "Plue, can you please tell this literal five-year-old that hanging on moneky bars from the bottom of Eaglestriker whilst the jet is flying is NOT a good idea?"

 

"What he said, Gaia." Plue snapped before Gaia herself could interject. "Now isn't the time."

 

"What's it time for, then?" Gaia shot back, seemingly agitated that Plue had not taken her side in whatever argument she and Vector had been having.

 

"Time for thinking."

 

"My least favourite time."

 

"Is it because you find thinking hard?" Snarked Vector. Gaia wheeled around and glowered at him.

 

"At least I... I... I..." She trailed off.

 

Plue raised an eyebrow. It was unlike Gaia to be without a comeback, and she expressed as much. Gaia turned to her.

 

"Well, I don't know... maybe I'm just not in the mood right now."

 

"Weren't you and Vector arguing just now, though?" Peragrine wondered out loud.

 

"Y-yeah?" Gaia's words faltered.

 

Plue rolled her eyes.

 

"Look, we don't have time for character drama. Right now we have a situation to deal with, ok? You four, don't antagonise each other. I don't want to be the mom friend."

 

"Loud and clear, mom!" Peragrine gave a mock salute.

Chapter 10 Part 1:

 

Annoyance threatened to engulf Raelyn. Not only had she gone and injured Dante's body during her first stint of control for a year, leading to threats of a lecture on responsiblility and less time in control in the future, but these insolent other-dimensioners were being a nusance with their one-note topics of conversation. They wanted to go home? Well, she wanted her family back. Dante wanted his best friend back. Everyone wanted something, and she had learned that complaining never lead to getting what one might want.

 

Whatever, she thought, hunkering down to meet eye-to-eye with the startled agent in front of her. At least she'd be able to have some fun with this one.

 

"What?" Ivy gasped, subtly flinching away from Raelyn's malevolant indiego stare.

 

"You heard me." Raelyn spat. "Twelve of Infinite Sight. Info, if you please. I'd like to know more about the people who sent someone to roam around on my turf and kidnap an innocent Mythran."

 

"Do you not know what you've just said?" Ivy looked more incredulous than anything else as her gaze flittered from Raelyn to Talia and back to Raelyn again. "Mythrans are supposed to be extinct, and yet there's one standing behind you!"

 

"I know she's behind me, this isn't a panto performance." Curling her lip in disgust, Raelyn spared Talia - who seemed someone disturbed at the mention of all Mytrhans being extinct - what she hoped was a sympathetic glance. She seemed to be taking to Talia. Probably the appreciation of someone unassertive. Raelyn considered herself relatively cool so long as nobody gave her orders or made demands of her. "And, yeah, I know Mythrans are supposed to be extinct, but Talia here seems to be from another dimension. She and this other girl here came to our dimension looking for a couple o' friends of theirs... I don't know the details, and frankly I don't care." She glared at Ivy again. "My adgenda first. And don't skimp on the details. The last thing you want...

 

...is me annoyed."
