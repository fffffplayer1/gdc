Chapter 4 Part 4:

 

"And you were talking about me, after all."

 

Jonna thought she heard a low rumbling noise, but dismissed it in favour of speaking to the stranger.

 

"Ugh... fine. So you say you're not going to kill us. How can we believe you?"

 

The man stood silently for a moment, as if pondering the question, before replying.

 

"Well, there's no sure-fire way that you can trust me. But you look slightly lost in here, so the way I see it is you can either follow me or stay here and wander around. And let me tell you, these caves are pretty extensive. You could be here for a while."

 

"Come on, Jonna." Talia said. "We need to go with him."

 

"Only if you're less formal." Declared Jonna, folding her arms. "I'm not used to this new you, who acts just like the other Mythrans and seems to have lost touch with the everyday life the rest of us lead."

 

"That's what happens when you spend a while with another culture, or experience another way of living for a long time." The man butted in. "You pick up their habits through osmosis."

 

"And how would you know?" Jonna argued.

 

The rumbling grew louder, and it occured to Jonna that ignoring it may not have been the best strategy.

 

"Let's just say I'm not-"

 

At this point, however, the man was cut off by the crumbling of rock from the ceiling above.

 

"Get down!" He roared, leaping forwards and knocking both Jonna and Talia to the ground as a hunk of rock crashed into the area in which the trio had been standing previously. The rumbling grew unbearable, and Jonna looked up to see a strange object with a drill attatched fall down and sink into the ground where they stood, slowly dying down.

 

The shaking stopped.

 

The compartment flipped open with a loud hiss, and out stepped a woman clad in regal robes that shimmered alternating shades of orange and yellow. As she stepped forward, she pulled a dangerous-looking scythe off her back.

 

"My name is Ivy of the fire heart." She held the scythe in front of her, threateningly.

 

"I have come for the Mythran."

Chapter 5 Part 1:

 

"Not good, not good, not good..." Plue muttered as she ran.

 

"So?" Panted Percevel. "What's the plan at this point?"

 

"Well, this guy Cadmus wants to get his mitts on the gate - for SOME reason." Huffed Plue, throwing her arms out to express her disenchantment with the current situation. "Heck, all I know is that he attacked us and now I'm offended. I guess we need to-"

 

These were the last words that Plue spoke before she turned a corner and collided with a figure running in the opposite direction to her. The pair fell to the ground, and Percevel whipped out a freshly loaded pistol before either could react.

 

"Another one?" He spat.

 

"Wait, wait, wait, no! I'm not an intruder! I was on my way to deal with it!" The new arrival protested, rising and brushing themselves down. His eyes were a brillaint shade of gold, and his hair was a light brown. As he shrugged the dust off his trench coat, which was a mud brown in colour, he realised that he was recieving quizzical looks from the other people in the vicinity. Rolling his eyes, he reached into his pocket and pulled out a name badge. Plue barely had time to look at it before he stashed it away, but it was verification enough.

 

"See? I'm a member of Pandemonium's Bane!" He protested.

 

"I didn't even know we had ID cards, in all honesty." Plue muttered. "Who gave that to you?"

 

"The ginger guy in the glasses." The man responded.

 

"That'd be Jacen, then. He does like his neatness and tidyness... listen, I'm going to call you New Guy for now. Is that ok?"

 

"My name's-"

 

"New Guy, I'm really sorry, but we're in kind of a dire situation at the moment. Can you just hang snake until all this craziness is sorted out?"

 

"I... fine." The newly christened new guy rolled his eyes. "What exactly is going on, anyway?"

Chapter 5 Part 2:

 

"Some crazy strong dude called Cadmus is attacking us." Plue explained, as New Guy helped her to her feet. "He's a deviant, and a pretty strong one at that. Last I saw he was doing battle with Gaia."

 

"Will that be enough? I mean, Gaia's pretty strong, right?" New Guy queried.

 

"Not exactly. Her powers are dependent on the concentration of maelstrom in the area, and we can only afford to have so much inside the base without becoming infected. It doesn't help that she wastes a lot of her power on being flashy."

 

"Like when she arrived and covered her hands in fire and lighting for no specific reason?" Percevel cut in.

 

"Exactly." Plue nodded, while New Guy craned his neck to look at Percevel.

 

"Speaking of unfamiliar faces, I haven't seen him around." He said. "Is he a member of Pandemonium's Bane?"

"No, he's... he's a guest." Plue told him, which was technically correct. "I'll explain the details later."

 

"Fine." New Guy didn't look sated with the answer, but nodded nonetheless.

 

"So. You a devaint?" Asked Plue. New Guy nodded.

 

"Yeah, but I don't have a power that'll be particularily useful in here."

 

"What is it?"

 

"I can, like, talk to and control creatures. Not that it's not handy, but I'm not seeing any wild animals in here."

 

"Fair enough. Is there anything else you can do?"

 

Here, New Guy smiled a slighly wicked smile as he pulled his trench coat away to reveal a pair of swords in a large scabbard, conjoined by what looked like a steel cable, attatched to his waist.

 

"Fortunately for you, I also happen to be a dab hand with these." He revealed.

 

"Perfect." Plue declared. "I-"

 

"Uh-oh." Percevel muttered.

 

"What is it?" Plue asked, praying that something wasn't greivously wrong.

 

"Peragrine. I didn't see him with Gaia." Muttered Percevel.

 

"Ugh, you're right... Okay!" Plue turned to the two men. "New Guy, you're with me. We're going to make sure that Gaia doesn't get herself killed. Percevel, you're on "find Perry" duty. Comprende?"

 

Both men saluted.

 

"Sir!" They declared.

Chapter 5 Part 3:

 

"Okay, you don't have to be so formal. Geez guys." Plue rubbed her head as the others began to look slightly bashful.

 

"Sorry. Force of habit." Percevel muttered.

 

"Same here... I think I'll prefer this to the last people I served under." Muttered New Guy. Making a metal note to ask New Guy what he meant by such a comment later, Plue wheeled around and began to run back down the hallway. "Percevel, go and find your friend!"

 

"But how am I supposed to find you afterwards?" He called after the backs of her and New Guy.

 

"Follow the noise!" Called Plue, before rounding a corner; she saw Perceval nod before he disappeared from sight.

 

"It's been a while since I've been in a proper fight!" Grinned New Guy, cracking his knuckes as he ran.

 

"Well, don't get overexcited. Like I said, this guy's a deviant." Plue reminded him. New Guy rolled his eyes.

 

"Please. I've been trained to tackle people like him."

 

Plue raised an eyebrow. "You're serious?"

 

"Deadly. Let's just say I wasn't always that nice a person-"

 

At that moment, however, New Guy was cut off as the wall the pair were running beside caved in and Gaia was blasted through it, slamming into the opposite wall and sliding down to the floor, groaning. Whipping her head back around, Plue's eyes widened and she barely had time to duck another silver construct, in the shape of a scythe, whisking towards her. Rising, she stepped backward hastily and fired an arrow, which bounced off Cadmus' shield.

 

"So this is the guy?" New Guy spat, glaring at him.

 

"Ugh... sorry Plue." Muttered Gaia. "Not enough... maelstrom."

 

"This is why you don't go rushing into confrontations." Plue chastised her. "Now take some time out. We'll handle it from here."

 

"The... the two of you?" Panted Gaia, shifting herself upright.

 

"The THREE of us." A voice corrected her. Looking round, Plue saw Vector approach the scene, pulling a cutlass off his back.

 

"I thought I heard something noisy around here. Gaia, can you TRY to not get involved for once?"

"Aye sir." Mumbled Gaia, angrily. Vector breathed heavily, calming himself.

 

"I don't know if it's clear, but we do appreciate what you've done so far." He said. Gaia raised her head in surprise.

 

"Huh?"

 

"This staying out of it business is for your own benefit, you need to understand that. How can you hope to be a hero if you can't let yourself rest when you don't win?"

 

Chastised yet complimented, Gaia seemed to have nothing more to say, and instead leaned back against the wall with a small smile on her face. Plue looked at her and almost did a double take, for the smile Gaia was wearing contained no trages of vanity, sarcasm, or mockingness. It was one hundred percent genuine.

 

"Now, as for me..." Vector began to stalk towards Cadmus. "... I could use some exercise."

 

"Hey, save some work for me." New Guy drew his swords.
