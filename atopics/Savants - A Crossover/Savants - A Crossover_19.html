Chapter 16 Part 2:

 

"Acting?" Echoed Norik.

 

"Yes, acting." Cadmus hissed as he rubbed his forearm. "Thanks for the knife, by the way."

 

"Can't say I expected you to give it back." His younger admitted.

 

"And I can't say that surprises me." Acknowledged Cadmus. "But it all worked out."

 

"What worked out?" Norik pressed. "Stop playing the pronoun game with me!"

 

"My plan." Explained Cadmus. "What I just destroyed was a chip. A chip that surved two purposes. To monitor my location... and to record audio."

 

"You... you destroyed it?" Norik furrowed his brow. "Why?"

 

"Because I'm resigning." Cadmus told him, folding his arms and staring the boy down as if challenging him to disagree. "I've had my suspicions about the Twelve of Infinite Sight for a while now, to tell you the truth. But I saw an opportunity and, after some deliberation, I took it." He spread his arms out wide, now. "So here I am. A free man, one who has no qualms with telling you anything you might want to know."

 

"Well that seems somewhat abrupt." Norik deadpanned.

 

"Belive me, it isn't. Like I said, I've been considering it for a while. But my suspicions were unconfirmed, and the opportunity never arose."

 

"And have your suspicions been confirmed now?"

 

"I know that by going against you, the Twelve are going against the Nexus Force, which is something we were told we WEREN'T doing. And I know that you guys aren't necessarily in the wrong."

 

"Look at you, questioning the system." Norik grinned. "You're better than me. But anyways, I don't suppose we could count on support from anyone else, could you?"

 

"There's someone I need to talk to." Cadmus admitted. "Everyone else... no. Ironsides has got them in his clutches. Look, could you let me out of this cell?"

 

"Not so fast." Norik frowned. "I need you to tell me about how this "Ironsides" came to have a bunch of deviants working for him before the Nexus Overcharge."

 

"Gladly." Cadmus stroked his chin. "This might take a while."

Chapter 16 Part 3:

 

"S'not like I have anything better to do."

 

"Okay, then I'll begin." The knight rested his hands behind his head. "I guess it all started a while ago. A few years after the Maelstrom War had started, Overbuild - you know who that is, right?"

 

"Yep."

 

"Right. Overbuild set up a laboratory so that he could find a way to defeat the maelstrom, because regular battling wasn't going to be enough."

 

"I was told that Overbuild was in league with the Darkitecht." Norik interjected. Cadmus raised an eyebrow.

 

"Personally, I wouldn't know. Maybe he had a master plan, or something. But, in any case, the laboratory was set up and, away from the prying eyes of the Nexus Force, he was able to try and create the ultimate weapon. Time passed, and along came Damien Ironsides, who was in the market for some... helpers. He teamed up with Overbuild, and convinced him to set up a program that revolved around the artificial creation of special abilities within people. The only trouble was, nobody volunteered to be experimented on, which led the two to take some drastic measures."

 

"What sort of measures are we talking about?"

 

"I'm talking about people disappearing. Children, especially, because it was important to have soldiers that were loyal as well as powerful. Families would lose their younger generations within the space of hours. And these subjects were experimented on and put through testing. Each one..." He help up his arm, showing off the tattoo again. "...given a brand, to let them know that it was all permenant."

 

"Well, long story short, Ironsides eventually selected candidates - twelve of them - to be his lackeys, and thus, the Twelve of Infinite Sight were born, created shortly before the end of the war. We were his chosen ones, and I can't remember being more relieved than when he took us out of that facility and put us to work. After all, we had some degree of freedom. The rest... well, I guess you'd have to ask the psycho."

Chapter 16 Part 4:

 

"Who - oh. What does she have to do with it?" Norik asked.

 

"She didn't show you?" Cadmus grimaced. "When we were fighting, she struck me as familiar. And, well... I saw a tattoo like mine on her arm."

 

"What?"

 

"Looks like she's a former S-class test subject."

 

"Oh no." Norik muttered. "Dante... Dante told me that he found her inside a facility that Overbuild had run. Are you saying..."

 

"That's where it was all happening? Yes." Cadmus nodded.

 

Norik furrowed his brows. "How many S-class members of the Twelve are there?"

 

"Four."

 

"That's bad."

 

"It's very bad."

 

"We're up against the brunette, times four, with eight - sorry, seven - other deviants. I'm beginning to wonder if we shouldn't just given them the gate and be done with it."

 

"No." Cadmus glowered at Norik. "That is precisely what you should NOT do."

 

"Why?"

 

"That's my main suspicion. Since we were founded, we have been made to collect anything of importance or power, and we've been told that it is for the system's benefit, to keep this power out of the wrong hands. But... I don't know... something just seemed off about the whole setup. Ironsides is a recluse - mostly keeps himself to himself. I haven't met him in person more than a couple of times. And I don't really see any of the stuff I bring back once I've brought it back. You think I'd at least be able to check on it."

 

"You think he's hoarding it for himself?"

 

"That what I've been suspecting, yes. And considering all the shady business Overbuild and he were conducting... well, it can't be for anything good."

 

"Wait, how do you know that Ironsides was involved with Overbuild?"

 

"The reason I was taken," Cadmus revealed, "is because I overheard something I shouldn't have. I don't think Ironsides recognized me later down the line, but..."

 

"Say no more." Norik fished in his pocket for the key to Cadmus' cell. "We need to do something about this."

 

"I agree." Cadmus affirmed as Norik pulled the door open. "We can't waste any more time."

Chapter 17 Part 1:

 

"I'll go." Perceval declared.

 

Vector and Plue tore their eyes away from each other to stare at their guest instead.

 

"Huh?" Vector grunted, as if the knight had just made a speech in another language.

 

"I said, I'll go." Perceval repeaed. "You don't want Vector to go with her because they'll just argue, right?" He directed his words at Plue. "Then how about someone who won't necessarily annoy her, but someone who won't just encourage her to be annoying either."

 

"I take it that means you're not inviting me on this expedition of yours?" Peragrine postulated.

 

"Sorry." Shrugged Perceval.

 

"Nah, it's cool. Just bring me back a souvenir if you find one."

 

"Will do."

 

"Perceval." Plue broke out of her stupor, folding her arms. "You don't have to go with. You're a guest."

 

"I know." Shrugged the knight. "But someone's got to do it, right?" He eyed Vector. "I can keep her safe."

 

"I..." Vector's shoulders slumped, and the anger and tension seemed to fade away as Perceval watched. "Sure. Just... look after her."

 

"Hey. Don't mention it." Perceval shrugged. "Just keep an eye on Perry, okay? We can trade eccentric personalities."

 

"Please." Peragrine scoffed. "They don't need to keep an eye on me."

 

"You're biased." Perceval deadpanned, rising from the chair he was sitting in and walking to the door. "Plue, do you want to come with, and give us the mission?"

 

"I... uh... yeah, sure." Plue surrendered herself to what seemed to be the inevitable, and let herself be led out of the room by Perceval, leaving Vector and Peragrine to themselves.

 

There was a brief silence, then - 

 

"You liiiiiiike her." Drawled Peragrine.

 

"Don't test me, kid." Snapped Vector. "In this world, I lead the Venture League. That means that I have the authority to inflict corporal punishment on you if necessary."

 

"Whaddya gonna do, sir?" Peragrine grinned, sitting upright. "Find work for me in this base that basically takes care of itself?"

 

"You'd wish, by the time I'd be through with you."

"I know." Shrugged the knight. "But someone's got to do it, right?" He eyed Vector. "I can keep her safe."

 

"I..." Vector's shoulders slumped, and the anger and tension seemed to fade away as Perceval watched. "Sure. Just... look after her."

 

"Hey. Don't mention it." Perceval shrugged. "Just keep an eye on Perry, okay? We can trade eccentric personalities."

 

"Please." Peragrine scoffed. "They don't need to keep an eye on me."

 

"You're biased." Perceval deadpanned, rising from the chair he was sitting in and walking to the door. "Plue, do you want to come with, and give us the mission?"

 

"I... uh... yeah, sure." Plue surrendered herself to what seemed to be the inevitable, and let herself be led out of the room by Perceval, leaving Vector and Peragrine to themselves.

 

There was a brief silence, then - 

 

"You liiiiiiike her." Drawled Peragrine.

 

"Don't test me, kid." Snapped Vector. "In this world, I lead the Venture League. That means that I have the authority to inflict corporal punishment on you if necessary."

 

"Whaddya gonna do, sir?" Peragrine grinned, sitting upright. "Find work for me in this base that basically takes care of itself?"

 

"You'd wish, by the time I'd be through with you."
