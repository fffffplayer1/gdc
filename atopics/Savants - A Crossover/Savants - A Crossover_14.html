Chapter 12 Part 4:

 

Gaia breathed deeply, in and out, trying to calm herself down. The smile that had been so hastily been plastered to her face now slid off as she stared at the ceiling, almost afraid to move. Vector's words echoed in her head.

 

"I just want to help you", he had said.

 

He hadn't been the first person to say that.

 

Grimacing, she miserably rubbed her forehead. "Stop that", she told herself inwardly. It wouldn't do, she had decided years ago, to dwell on the matter. Besides, Pandemonium's Bane was DIFFERENT. They were the good guys. She should know; she had helped found the organisation, after all.

 

Grimacing, she lowered her appendage and began absent-mindedly fiddling with the bandages on her left forearm; the ones she had been consistently replacing for over three years. As she did so, she stared at the lamp placed on her bedside table. At her request, it spawned a bright fuscia glow. Generic, sterile white lights brought back too many bad memories, and red lights just made her feel uneasy.

 

So lost in these thoughts was she, she didn't even realise the bandage had completely come off her arm when her fingers rubbed against skin against fabric. Tilting it so she could view the underside, her eyes were greeted with the tattoo she had been given what felt like a lifetime ago; as jet black and stark as the day it had been inked.

 

S CLASS - 004
