Chapter 11 Part 4:

 

Song Used: When Your Middle Name Is Danger (Trocadero)

 

"And, uh... she's being stubborn as well." Selecting the gender was a gamble on Norik's part, but he could easily salvage the interrogation if he had messed up. Fortunately, Cadmus didn't react as if he had said anything wrong. At leas the Twelve of Infinite Sight weren't traditionalists. Besides, it sounded worse if a she was captured. Norik could only hope that some protective, testosterone-y instincts would kick in.

 

"I'm not surprised."

 

Norik raised an eyebrow.

 

"You believe you are... aware of this second agent's identity?"

 

Unfortunately, Cadmus clammed up once again.

 

"Well, whatever." Smiled Norik. "Doesn't matter, as long as she tells us who you guys are."

 

"She won't."

 

"She will. They've got the psycho brunette grilling her for info. The one with the purple highlights."

 

"Didn't strike me as the interrogating type."

 

"Oh, she can get people to talk, she's just... not as refined as me. Has a bad habit of shoving things up people's noses..."

 

"What?"

 

"I mean normally that big guy, with the beard, is there to keep her in check, but he's not in the room with her right NOW because he had to go do something else. For all I know, she's blasting rock music into your lady friend's ears, full blast. Probably yelling... maybe a little bit of sleep deprivation..."

 

"But- why-"

 

"Well, I said that these aren't desperate times, but think of it from our point of view." Norik leaned forward, eyeing Cadmus directly. "We've just been attacked by a couple of agents, who have managed to do numbers on a couple of OUR agents, and we have no idea where you're coming from or how to stop the flow of people breaking our walls. Don't you think we might be eager to get this all over with as quickly as possible? We have things to do, you know."

 

Cadmus grimaced.

 

"It'll only get worse for HER if you don't talk." Norik continued to exploit the vunerability he had found.

 

Cadmus opened his mouth, gritting his teeth.

 

"I'll talk."

Chapter 12 Part 1:

 

"So what has you so antsy?"

 

"Nothing."

 

"Nothing, shmuthing. You're bothered."

 

"How can you tell?"

 

"You think I'd know how to read you after being your co-worker for over a year and a half."

 

Gaia threw her hands up in the air in exasperation, a contrast to her normally chipper self. "I don't like being read... or reading... or books in general."

 

Vector stroked his beard. "Is Dante's idea of adding a library to the base bothering you?"

 

"No." Gaia snorted. "I can always just, uh, not GO there."

 

"Well then, what IS bothering you?"

 

"Okay, stop." Gaia ceased clomping through the hallway on the way to her room and turned around, Vector also stopping in his tracks.

 

"First of all, why do you care?" She pointed an accusatory finger at him.

 

"Well, uh... it's wierd."

 

"Huh?"

 

"You being in a bad mood. It's wierding me out."

 

"So I'm wierding you out?"

 

"YOU'RE not, your mood is."

 

"Right." Frowned Gaia. "Secondly, don't you have anything better to do?"

 

"No. That simple enough for you?"

 

There was the sound of teeth grinding.

 

"Yes, I would say so-"

 

"Okay, what IS it?!" Snapped Vector, irritation getting the better of him as well. "Don't start biting my head off just because I'm trying to understand-"

 

"I don't want to be understood!" Protested Gaia, taking an agitated step backwards. "D-Don't try and get in my head!"

 

"No-one's getting in anyone's head!" Vector grabbed Gaia's arm before she could move any further away. "Gaia, I just want to help you!"

 

"I..." Gaia faltered, obviously thinking. Then, with a visible effort, she calmed herself down.

 

"It's about earlier." She confessed. "I... I know I'm not, uh... not really smart, but I just..."

 

"You're kidding." Gaped Vector. "That's what had you so agitated? My saying that you found thinking hard?"

 

"It's a sensetive topic!" Protested Gaia. Vector reared backwards.

 

"Okay, okay! Sorry, I just... I didn't know."

 

Gaia sighed again. "It's fine. I overreacted. I just..."

 

Vector leaned forward to hear what she had to say.

Chapter 12 Part 2:

 

"It's fustrating." She confessed. "It's fustrating, being the dumbest out of everyone."

 

"But you're not-" A finger placed itself on Vector's lips, silencing him.

 

"I said out of everyone. And, I mean, have you seen the rest of this organisation? Dante's, like, a genius, Jacen is the most tech-savvy person I've ever seen in my life. And even New Guy is apparently an interrogator. And then... and then there's me. No-one else in Pandemonium's Bane has a ban on social interaction, do they?"

 

"I think Raelyn was joking."

 

Gaia ignored him.

 

"I'm just fed up of being the lovable idiot stereotype! Whenever something comes up that requires strategy, I'm useless! That's why I enjoy fighting so much. It's... it's the one place I can really shine. Where I can be helpful. I mean, if it weren't for Dante and Plue and... and you..."

 

She trailed off, and stared into his eyes.

 

"If it weren't for the three of you, I'd still be in... in Overbuild's facility. I'd still be in suspended animation. I'd still be in a nightmare. You all saved me from that, and I can't do anything. Can't do anything to repay you, or say thanks, or..."

 

She stopped speaking, breaking eye contact with Vector and choosing to focus her attention to the ground.

 

"Anyone can throw a punch." She mumbled. "Any idiot can throw a punch."

 

For a few seconds, nothing happened. Then Gaia felt a pair of strong arms wrap themseles around her.

 

"You're not useless." Hissed Vector determinedly. "You're my friend. And that alone is thanks enough."

 

Gaia could do nought but listen as he continued.

 

"Every day, you cheer us up when the rest of us are feeling down. Yeah, you're annoying, but at the same time, you're so endearing. You've been through a lot, and you can still smile brighter than anyone else I know. And when you throw a punch? You could beat any of us, I'll bet. And not because you're strong. You'd never give up. You're not one to walk away - not when there are people relying on you."

 

"Vector..."

Chapter 12 Part 3:

 

"You should never feel that way about yourself. You're just as important a link in the chain as everyone else. And I know that everyone else values you as well." Vector finished, drawing out of the hug and smiling towards Gaia. "It's ok, honestly. I don't know if you've noticed, but I'm not the sharpest tool in the toolbox either."

 

"Please." Gaia was smiling now. "I doubt you've got anything on me."

 

"Whatever you say." Shrugged Vector, also grinning. Then he raised an eyebrow. "Feeling better?"

 

"Yeah." Gaia nodded. "Yeah, I just... I just needed a moment."

 

"You needed someone to listen." Vector amended. "We all need that sometimes, trust me."

 

"Well, uh... thanks."

 

"Hey, it's fine. If you ever need to pour your heart out to anyone again, I'll be right here."

 

"Yeah, yeah. Don't get used to it, Jadewing. You caught me in a moment of weakness, is all. Give it another year and a half and then I'll let you hug me again."

 

"You mean, I'll let you cry on my shoulder again."

 

"I wasn't crying!"

 

"Weren't you?"  Vector craned his neck around, trying to look for any signs of the contrary. Gaia laughed.

 

"No, I wasn't!" She smiled.

 

"Were you planning to?"

 

"No comment."

 

Shrugging, Vector laughed. "That's good enough for me."

 

"It should be." Gaia grumbled, though she was still beaming. After another moment or two of relative mirth had passed, she turned on her heel. 

 

"Well, I'm gonna check out for the day." She declared. "Between one thing and another, I'm pretty trashed. I need to recharge."

 

"On maelstrom?"

 

"Yep."

 

"Wierdo."

 

"Whatever." Shrugged Gaia, though she was relieved that the pair were back to their usual formula - and she had a feeling that Vector felt the same way. "Seeya later, nice guy."

 

"Same to you." Called Vector as Gaia rounded a corner, blocking her view of him. Opening a nearby door - the door that led to her humble abode - Gaia wasted no time in gliding across the carpet and flopping onto the bed of her room.
