Chapter 10 Part 2:

 

"You're incorrect." Ivy snarled. "The last thing I want is for you to know anything."

 

"You don't intend to talk?"

 

"Nope."

 

"Why am I not surprised?" Raelyn leaned back and yawn, blinking sleep out of her eyes. "Ugh. I only woke up an hour ago, so our brain is not working as well as it should. Gimmie a moment."

 

""Our"?" Echoed the prisoner, raising an eyebrow.

 

"Oh, yeah, our." Raelyn confirmed. "What, did whoever sent you not tell you exactly what the deal is with me and him? Oh, that's a good point, actually. Who sent you?"

 

"Are you not Dante?"

 

"My questions first!"

 

The two glared at one another for several seconds. The Raelyn snapped her fingers.

 

"Oh, right! Um... it SHOULD work."

 

Before anyone else in the room had time to ask her what she was on about, Raelyn had slammed a hand into the wall, deactivating the film of laser that seperated them from Ivy. Without pause, she grabbed the warrior, standing her up, and then proceeded to violently ram their foreheads together.

 

Blinking, Raelyn twisted her head around.

 

"Woo-hoo!" She cheered. "It worked!"

 

Instead of the cavern, Raelyn now found herself in a pure white, seemingly infinite expanse. Below, above and around her was nothing. Except for two people. One was a man lying right beside Raelyn's feet, clutching his forehead. Dante groaned out loud as he began to drag himself off the floor.

 

"My forehead..." He gasped, rising. "I - wait, where even are we?"

 

"The mindscape." Raelyn shrugged. "I brought our prisoner her for a thorough interrogation."

 

Dante tilted his head to the side. "You look... different." He declared. Raelyn quickly viewed herself. In the mindscape, her form was significantly shorter then Dante, with long purple hair and eyes of the same colour. Except at this moment in time she wasn't shorter then Dante at all. They were the same height.

 

"Intimidation purposes." She shrugged. "You're a tall guy. Height equates to intimidation."

Chapter 10 Part 3:

 

Dante opened his mouth to speak, presumably some buzzkilling words about how intimidation did not equal height, but Raelyn waved her hand in his face and strode over to the other figure in the infinite white expanse along with them. Ivy's eyes fluttered open just in time for Raelyn to glare down at her in what she hoped what a most threatening manner.

 

"Wha - what?" Ivy jumped up and backed several steps away from the pair before stumbling backwards, noticing where she was for seemingly the first time.

 

"What is this place?" She spat.

 

"Uh..." Dante began, only to Raelyn to snap her fingers in his face and speak for herself.

 

"It's my domain." She declared. This wasn't exactly, true, but Ivy didn't need to know that.

 

"What's that supposed to mean?" Having recovered from the shock of her surroundings, Ivy was returning to her more prickly self. This was not what Raelyn wanted.

 

"You have your plane of existance, and I have mine." She continued, hurriedly. "Whatever! The point is I control everything around her. Like, literally. I'm basically the god of this world."

 

She extended her hand, and a bottle of some kind was conjured up.

 

"Mustard." She declared. "Now, are you going to tell me what I want to know, or am I going to have to shove this up your nose?"

 

"Raelyn-" Dante began.

 

"Shush, my pedestrian friend." Snapped Raelyn. "Desperate times call for desperate measures." She extended her other hand and mauve flames licked her palm. "Oh, yeah, this stuff'll probably hurt in here too."

 

"I see." Ivy glared at Raelyn. "This is all an illusion, is it? Some kind of psychological torture?"

 

She eyeballed the other woman. "Well, I won't give in." She declared. "If you want information from me, you're going to have to do a whole lot worse than that."

Chapter 11 Part 1:

 

Song Used: When Your Middle Name Is Danger (Trocadero)

 

Norik stepped into the cramped space, squinting as his eyes adjusted to the room's lower light levels. The only light source was a single ceiling light that emitted a bottle green glow across the room, giving it an otherworldly atmosphere. Blinking once, Norik's eyes scanned the man, Cadmus. His silver armour and weapons had been confiscated upon his defeat, and he now stood in the furthest corner of the room; clad in an undersuit of fabric and neglecting both of the chairs placed upon the floor. With any luck, he'd be feeling the absence of his armour most keenly.

 

"Hello, Cadmus." Norik crooned, pulling up one of the chairs and reclining in it, taking the chance to view his surroundings. Minimal decoration on the walls, no table, and no easy access to the lights or air conditioning. Perfect setting. No secondary interrogator, but he didn't need one, anyway.

 

"Have a seat. I insist." He continued. Cadmus begrudgingly sat down, and Norik too the chance to straighten his coat, giving the other man a glance at both the swords attatched to his hip and the handgun in his inner pocket.

 

"So how old are you, Cadmus?" Norik queried casually, leaning back in his chair. In contrast, Cadmus leaned forward.

 

"I could be asking you the same thing." He spoke for the first time.

 

Norik idly shrugged. "My question first, if you don't mind."

 

"Why am I obliged to answer your questions?"

 

"Honestly? You aren't. But there are some things we're curious about, and it'll probably be easier for everyone if you tell us what we want to know of your own free will."

 

It was Cadmus' turn to shrug. "You make it sound like you have ways of making me talk. Why not just go ahead and use them?"

 

"Because we're not the bad guys." Norik declared. "Sure, desperate times call for desperate measures, but now that we're in control of the situation... we don't need to worry about those desperate measures, do we?"

 

*line break*

Chapter 11 Part 2:

 

Song Used: When Your Middle Name Is Danger (Trocadero)

 

"Now, I'll ask again. How old are you, Cadmus?"

 

"I'm twenty-one. Why does it matter to you?"

 

"To me? It doesn't, really." Confessed Norik. "I'm just trying to make polite conversation. We have all the time in the world, after all."

 

"Maybe you do." Grimaced Cadmus. "But I have a feeling that I'll be free soon enough."

 

"Are you that confident in your own abilities?" Norik pressed, innocently. Cadmus gave no reply.

 

"I see." He murmered. And he did, as he quickly withdrew a notepad and pencil from his pocket and scribbled down C is not alone.

 

"I thought you said you were just trying to make polite conversation?" Observed Cadmus.

 

"What can I say? I like getting things down for furute reference." Norik finished writing and stowed the pad away again. "STill, if you want to get straight to the nitty-gritty, I won't stop you. Now..." He leaned forward for the first time.

 

"Who are the Twelve of Infinite Sight?"

 

The reaction was instantaneous and expected.

 

"I'm not telling you." Spat Cadmus.

 

"I would have assumed as much." Norik admitted without missing a beat. "it's a shame, we know everything else already. First of all, the name. Twelve of INFINITE Sight. Sounds like some false advertising right there, wouldn't you agree?"

 

Cadmus said nothing.

 

"There's also the fact that you were after the gate." Norik continued. "I can only assume you mean the dimenisonal gate we apparently have in the basement, because you seemed to only show up after it was activated. And, I mean, who wouldn't want it? A gate that can send people between dimensions? How frickin' useful woudl that be, right?"

 

Norik then glared at Cadmus.

 

"The problem is your entry. You didn't exactly knock on the door and politely ask about the gate, did you? No, it was more like "boom!" "Imma take the gate and kick all the butts!" "Look at me, I manipulat energy!" Very flashy."

 

Standing up, the teen began to pace around the room, speaking as he did so.
