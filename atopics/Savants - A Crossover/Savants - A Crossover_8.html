Chapter 7 Part 4:

 

"Just the action scene, don't worry." Vector told her. "Just make sure that this guy can't run off again."

 

"Speaking of running off..." Mused Gaia, looking round the hall as she walked towards Cadmus, "...where are our guests?"

 

"Stirling went to find Peragrine." Filled in Plue. "I guess he's not back yet?"

 

"Well, I guess he missed all the fun. Shame." New Guy strolled over to Cadmus with a look of disdain on his face. "Now, the question is, what are we going to do with this waste of space? Do we have holding cells in this base of ours?"

 

"Not officially, but some of the rooms we have should be strong enough to hold him. We could put him in my room!" Offered Gaia.

 

"No, because your room is full of maelstrom." Vector deadpanned. "It doesn't empower everyone, Gaia. In fact, it's toxic to most."

 

"Well, ex-cuuuuse me for being a special snowflake." Groaned Gaia, rolling her eyes.

 

"You don't ever stop and think that you might be unnecessarily putting yourself in danger with your actions?"

 

"I'm not IN danger, I AM the danger."

 

"In any case, you probably shouldn't just dump him in your room, he could turn out to be trouble."

 

"Maybe I don't want to be saved the trouble? Maybe I am the trouble."

 

Plue rolled her eyes and turned to New Guy.

 

"Hey, New Guy, how about we find somewhere to put this guy? While we're at it, we can keep an eye out for Perceval and Peragrine..."

Chapter 8 Part 1:

 

Jonna Fig had been through her fair share of wierd days in her time. It was normal, given the life she had chosen to lead. But there were still moments when she mentally stepped back, looked at her life and began to dispair. And this was one of them - in another universe, looking for a couple of friends with hers, with her and Talia being lead through caves made entirely of crystal by some REALLY shady bloke with an unconcious assailant slung over his shoulders.

 

"Try to keep up." Dante called. "Like I said earlier, you wouldn't want to get lost."

 

"Excuse us for being suspicious." Retorted Jonna.

 

Dante snorted. "I don't blame you. Raelyn can be really wierd sometimes."

 

"Raelyn...? Is that the name of the, um... other persona?" Talia pressed.

 

"Yeah. Quite the handful, right?"

 

"She said that you "held her back". Does that mean you have to deal with her every day?" Jonna wasn't entirely sure why she wanted to know such information, but she asked nonetheless.

 

"Well, it's complicated. Seeing as how, for all we know, this bonding is permenant, we try to avoid arguments and stuff, and I think we've got things going quite well when you consider the circumstances. I don't give her much free reign, though. The last time I didn't keep her in check, someone's life paid the price."

 

He said these words so casually, as if the someone in question had ventured to the shops.

 

"That's horrible." Talia murmered, reflecting Jonna's inner monologue.

 

"Yeah. Well, it's difficult. She has my best interests at heart, but I can't really trust her around other people. She'll always use my mouth to insult someone or something. Plue's the only person I can stay in the same room with for longer than half an hour before some awkward situation arises. Ah, here we go."

 

Before Jonna or Talia could press him for any further details, Dante placed a hand on the wall of crystal and a segment of it swung open, reminiscant of a door. Dante caught it and held it open.

 

"Ladies first." He grinned.
