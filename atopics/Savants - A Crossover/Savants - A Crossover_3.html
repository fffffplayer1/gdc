Chapter 1 Part 4:

 

"You know better than to trouble trouble until trouble troubles you, Perce!" The other figure stated, rising and casually bending their limbs back into their correct positions.

 

"That wasn't an answer to my question, you insubortinate brickolorum!" "Perce" snapped. "Besides, I'd say by this point we're well and truly troubled anyhow!"

 

"Now what trouble would this trouble be?"

 

"The trouble of temporal dislocation, of course?"

 

"Uh... is anyone else getting a wierd sense of deja-vu?" Plue mumbled, watching the scene unfold. Turning around, she saw Gaia with stars in her eyes.

 

"I think I've just found my soulmate." She squeaked. "Vector, catch me." She then mad a big show of falling backwards, only to smack into the ground as Vector neglected her catching-related needs due to being in a shocked stupor.

 

"We're doomed. There's two of them... we're so doomed." He moaned, quietly.

 

"Say, where are the-" The eccentric figure was interrupted by the sound of feet tapping as Gaia sprinted towards him.

 

"Hell-oooo!" She chirped. "Can I say right now, I don't know who you are or where you come from, but I feel like this meeting was totally destined."

 

"You're kidding! That's what I thought when I saw you ungracefully run towards me!" The stranger replied, doffing his helmet to reveal light brown hair and a face covered in freckles. "Stranger, I know we met each other seconds ago, but I get the impression that you like pranks. Do you want to set one up whilst we get to know each other?"

 

"Oh, you read my mind!" Cheered Gaia, and within seconds the pair were gone, sprinting out the room like their lives depended on it whilst shouting something about a whiteboard rubber.

 

All was quiet for a few moments.

 

"I take it we have a lot of talking to do?" Plue eventually said. The other man turned to her and nodded courteously.

 

"My name is Percevel." He introduced himself. "And yes, I would say that fair amount of talking is in order right about now..."

Chapter 2 Part 1:

 

"What do you mean, they're gone!?"

 

"We mean what we say!" Thomas protested. "One minute, they were there. The next, they got sucked into that gate thing and they vanished."

 

...

 

"Okay." Sighed Jonna. "Thanks for telling me."

 

"Hey, no problem." Gambit's voice sounded tinny over the phone. "We'll call you if we get any more news on them, alright."

 

"Thanks guys." Hanging up the phone, Jonna leaned back on her sofa and groaned, putting her hands over her face.  It was bad enough that she hadn't been in contact with her now-Mythran friend Talia since the fall of the Darkitecht, but now her two other closest friends were missing? This was worse than losing any business, or... anything else, for that matter. The knowledge that Peragrine and Percevel could be literally anywhere made her feel far more anxious than she would have liked, considering that she knew the pair could easily handle themselves when it came to dangerous situations. Peragrine practially was one, after all.

 

In fact, it was probably a good job that he hadn't been offered a seat on the Nexus Force council like the other two had been, because if he had accepted, it would have been about as dangerous as allowing a toddler access to nuclear devices. Thinking about it, however, just filled Jonna with more regret. What if she had joined the council, anyway? Would she have the authority to organize people to search for her friends? What about the apparent gate thing that made them vanish anyway? What was happening to that? The lack of answers was beginning to drive her insane - 

 

Oh no. Percevel's wife. Jonna bit her lip.

 

"She's not going to be taking this well." She muttered.

 

Doing an about turn, Jonna slapped her hands over her face as she meandered down her stairs and into the ground floor of her house. So busy was she lamenting current events and rubbing her eyes that at first, she failed to noticed the Mythran standing before her.

Chapter 2 Part 2:

 

"Jonna Fig."

 

The otherwordly voice echoed through the air, and Jonna gave a start as she saw her visitor. Whilst there was a brief period of time in which she was unable to speak completely, but eventually she managed to respond sufficiently eloquently:

 

"Um, hi?"

 

"You have been summoned." The translucent figure grabbed Jonna's hand, and the landscape blurred around her, disappearing into a void of blue and white. Jonna's head began to spin as she watched the whirling surroundings until nothing remained but her, the mythran, and the void. Just when it seemed like the white noise would become unbearable, the world reconstructed itself and Jonna fell onto her knees, gasping for breath. Such was her disorientation that it took her a few moments to realise that she was no longer in her home.

 

Instead, Jonna found herself inside a sterile white environment, lit with aqua neon lights. The room was enormous, with a ceiling that stretched many meters above their heads, and several corridors lead down different paths. Holographic arrows and posters flashed and then disappeared only to be replaced by something else, and Jonna felt so overwhelmed that she sunk to her knees - only for the Mythran to help her rise again.

 

"It's alright." It calmly told her. "You are in no danger."

 

"Then... why am I here?" She gaped.

 

"Your appearance here concerns the fates that have befallen two of your comrades, known as Peragrine and Percival."

 

These works seemed to snap Jonna out of her trance, and she looked at the Mythran slightly desperately.

 

"You mean you know what happened to them? They can be saved?"

 

"Not quite. It is... complicated."

Chapter 2 Part 3:

 

"Uh... I'm listening."

 

"We belive that the missing persons you know have disappeared as a result of a Mythran device."

 

 

"...huh?!"

 

"Have you ever considered the possibility of parallel universes?" The mythran asked Jonna, beginning to walk towards one of the corridors. Not wanting to be left behind in an unfamiliar environment, Jonna hastened after them.

 

"Um... yeah, actually."

 

"Well then, what if I were to tell you that the concept of parallel universes is not just a simple concept, but reality?"

 

"I wouldn't be too suprise - oh no. Oh no. Don't tell me this is relevant to...?"

 

"We believe it is."

 

"So... so my friends are in a parallel universe?"

 

"This is the case. But there is no reason for you to fear."

 

Jonna stopped rubbing her head, and raised an eyebrow.

 

"Oh? You sound like you have some kind of solution-y plan sort of thing, and those are good. Please continue."

 

"First, a question. How far are you willing to go to get your friends back?"

 

"What kind of...? As far as it takes, I guess."

 

"I see."

 

The mythran led Jonna to a grey sliding door near the end of the hallway, where he turned around to face Jonna.

 

"Normally, we would not interfere in your affairs. However, we Mythrans are essentially responsible for the predicament you face - even if it was not US, perse, who created the gate."

 

"Gate?"

 

"This is the reason that we are sending you to the dimension after your friends, along with a means to return. Also with you will by a Mythran representative, to take care of affairs should you encounter our parallel kin, as well as defend you from any dangers you might face in another world. Do you find these conditions acceptable?"

 

"Uh... yeah. Sure."

 

"Very well." The Mythran turned around and clapped their hands, and the door swung open. Jonna walked inside and promptly did a double take, forced to rub her eyes. Standing in the center of the room, staring back at her, was a person with blonde hair and luminous, light cobalt eyes.

 

"Talia?"
