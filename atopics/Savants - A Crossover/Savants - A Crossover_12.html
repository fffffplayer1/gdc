Chapter 11 Part 3:

 

Song Used: When Your Middle Name Is Danger (Trocadero)

 

"Well, let's see here. The Twelve of Infinite sight can't be a branch of the Nexus Force, because two of our number have... very direct ties to that particular orgnaisation. It also can't be the maelstrom, because this isn't the maelstrom's style, like, at all."

 

"Are you trying to insinuate that the maelstrom has a style?" Cadmus spoke, words painfully squeezed out from behind his lips.

 

Norik smiled. Cadmus had started talking.

 

"Take it from me, pal." He stopped pacing, and folded his arms. "The maelstrom can actually be really subtle when it wants to be. All this breaking through walls and stuff? A few years ago, if the maelstrom had wanted that gate, there's a good chance it'd be gone and we'd all be dead already."

 

"And how would you know all that?"

 

"I have more, uh, experience than you might believe." Norik shrugged. "In any case, that leads me to assume that the Twelve of Infinite Sight are an independent organisation, kinda like Pandemonium's Bane - except instead of watching out for deviants, holding back the maelstrom, and generally acting as the Nexus Force's long arm, you guys just take what you want, when you want, because whatever."

 

"That's not true." Snarled Cadmus, indicnancy wrought upon his features.

 

"Then enlighten me." Shot back Norik.

 

Cadmus said nothing, which Norik was okay with. Now that the knight was rattled, he was more likely to let something slip.

 

"Alright, fine. Be like that. We'll find the answer, anyhow."

 

"And how will you find your answers if I refused to speak?" Cadmus demanded.

 

Time for Norik's favourite part.

 

"Oh? Oh, you don't know?" Norik feigned surprise. "Well, I guess whoever you're working for doesn't trust you. They've actually sent someone ELSE after us, which was lovely. Another bid for the gate? An attempt at a rescue mission? I'm not sure. Either way... we've defeated your little friend, too."

 

Something flickered in Cadmus' eyes for no longer than a split-second.
