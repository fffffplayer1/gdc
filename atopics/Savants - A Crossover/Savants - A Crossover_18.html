Chapter 14 Part 4:

 

"You're lying."

 

"Am I?" Raelyn leaned forward and slammed her head against Ivy's. Within that moment, Raelyn focused on manipulating the mindscape and copied a batch of her memories into Ivy's. Nothing too specific, but enough to convey the fact that she had lived, sort of, for thousands of years. There was a smack and both women reeled backwards.

 

"Ah! Ow!" Ivy winced and furrowed her brow. "Wha... what just happened...?"

 

"That, my dear," Raelyn tried to sound chipper whilst also nursing her own smarting forehead, "was a memory transferral. You know, proof that I'm as old as I say I am. I know I've aged REALLY well, but... you know."

 

"Why can I see Mythrans?"

 

"Probably a memory."

 

"And someone... uh... freezing time?"

 

"Also a memory."

 

"And why can I see... is that the Darkitecht?"

 

Raelyn's blood ran cold.

 

"Don't think about that. Ignore that one."

 

"What's he doing?"

 

"Ivy, focus. Forget about the Darkitecht, ok?"

 

"I..." The young warrior refocused and glared at the trio in front of her, though with less strength in her gaze than previously. "You're telling the truth."

 

"Don't get too excited." Muttered Dante.

 

"What can you expect? It's probably a matter of pride." Reasoned Talia, before turning to Ivy. "Look, Ivy, I don't intend to hurt anyone. Honestly. But... what gave you the idea that Mythrans were as bad as you said they were?"

 

"I..." Ivy faltered, folding her arms. "It's what I've been told."

 

"By whom?" Pressed Dante, but to no avail.

 

"Dante." Talia spoke softly, and in a considered fashion. "I think Ivy needs to work through some stuff. Her perspective has been forcably proven wrong, and that could call a lot of stuff into question... I, uh..."

 

"It's okay." Dante nodded. "I get it." He turned to Raelyn. "Release everyone, Rae. We're done here."

 

"Got it."

 

Everything fluctuated once again, and Raelyn once again found herself alone in the mindscape, Dante back in control of his own body.

 

Great.

"Alone?" The buccaneer demanded.

because of your feelings for her?"

"Watch your tongue, Abernathy."

Chapter 16 Part 4:

 

"Kid?"

 

The call sent a shiver down Norik's spine.

 

He had been walking past the cells, deep in thought - some of it more serious, like the whole Twelve of Infinite Sight business, and some of it less so, like the fact that the doorknob had fallen off the door to his bedroom. It was a new bedroom, too. He had missed having one. In any case, the voice had called out to him, causing him to halt in his tracks.

 

He sighed.

 

"Yes, Cadmus?"

 

The muscular blonde rose from where he was seated in his cell, and walked deliberatly over to the bars, staring at Norik.

 

"What are you doing?" Cadmus' voice quivered slightly, which puzzled Norik. He opened his mouth to reply, but Cadmus raised a finger to his lips, before lifting up his forearm.

 

Knife, he mouthed.

 

Norik titled his head to the side to express confusion, as Cadmus rolled his eyes. Unexpectedly, he suddenly slammed his hand onto the bars, which clattered noisily, and let out a cry. Norik stumbled backwards, unsure how to react.

 

No time, Cadmus continued to communicate without making noise, moving his lips only. Knife! Now!

 

Norik grimaced. If the prisoner was bluffing, he would be handing him a weapon, and giving him a way to break out. His first response was to ignore him...

 

however, the teen also knew, perhaps better than any other member of Pandemonium's Bane, that it was sometimes necessary to give people a chance. That was why he reached into his boot, unsheathing a small blade, and tossed to Cadmus, who wasted no time in turning around and doing something, letting out a final cry as he did so.

 

Before Norik could ask, the blonde turned back around with a glistening piece of plastic held between his finger and thumb, which he almost immediately snapped in half, tossing the pieces over his shoulder. An uncharacteristic grin spread across his features as he tossed the knife back to Norik, before beginning to inspect what looked like a recent wound on his arm.

 

"Good acting, right?" He managed.
