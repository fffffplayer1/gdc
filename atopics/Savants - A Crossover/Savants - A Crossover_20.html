Chapter 17 Part 3:

 

"Fair enough." Sighed Vector, which led to another drawn-out silence between the two.

 

"Did you believe in parallel worlds before this?" Peragrine eventually ventured.

 

"Well..." Vector thought for a moment, leaning back. "I never really gave it much thought, to be honest. Never had the time."

 

"What do you mean?"

 

"I grew up outside the system, in the wastelands. My parents are scavangers out there, just like pretty much everyone else. It's a pretty tough place, actually. "You can't fight, you can't survive" sort of place."

 

Peragrine raised an eyebrow. "Enlighten me. I don't know if we have these "wastelands" where I'm from."

 

"Ok." Vector nodded. "Well, there's the Nimbus System, but nearby, there are a set of planets collectively known as the wastelands - at least, that's what the inhabitants called them. They're mostly made up of deserts, or volcanoes, or other undesirable landscapes. Many of us, we joined the Nexus Force just for a chance to get off those rocks. A chance to see colours other than earth tones for once in our lives." He laughed. "Even now, I don't think my parents have seen an ocean. Anyways, the point is I was busy from a very young age. Our main source of income was finding junk that meant something to someone in the desert, and selling it. Kinda like archeologists, except we weren't giving it to a museum for no charge once it was all over."

 

"Find any traps?"

 

"A few. My mom once had to save my dad from this one trap where the ceiling of the room was slowly lowering onto him."

 

"I think I've heard of that one."

 

"Yeah, like that. But that wasn't often, to be honest. Most of the time, it was just hard work. Trekking. Manual labour. The usual. It didn't exactly leave much time to ponder the universe." He sighed. "That's probably why I don't do much deep thinking. It's a force of habit."

 

Having said his part, the man turned on the sofa he had been reclining on to face his younger.

 

"So? Did YOU ever believe in parallel worlds before all of this?"

Chapter 17 Part 4:

 

"I'll give that a resounding yes!" Chirped Peragrine. "I've always loved that idea, you know? The idea of there being parallel worlds, where everything's the same but a little bit different. I have to say, it's uh... it's cool to actually be in one. Wish I had met another version of myself. I had always hoped that I could just meet myself. That would have been cool."

 

"For all I know, there could be." Reasoned Vector. "Besides, you met Gaia."

 

Peragrine's gaze brightened. "Yeah!" He agreed raising a fist into the air. "But yeah, it's cool. I'd like to get out of this base, at some point. You know, see most of the universe. Right now, I've just seen this base. It's a bit like just being in any other place, except I won't be able to come back."

 

"Mmh."

 

"Yeah... I'll miss you. And Gaia. And, if I stay longer and get to know everyone else, I'll probably miss them too."

 

"Wow. That was... surprisingly profound."

 

"Well, what can I say? Guess I'm just full of surprises."

 

"Fair enough... hey, Perry?"

 

"Yeah?"

 

"How'd we both end up in the Venture League? We're pretty different."

 

"Well, it could be that the factions have different quota in this dimension... but I dunno. The Venture League's all about exploration, right?"

 

"Right."

 

"Right. Well, that's what you were doing when you were a kid, right? So... I guess that's why you ended up in Venture League."

 

"Makes sense."

 

"Yeah, and me... I've always been a thrill-seeker. I'm a daredevil. If I don't flip around or do enough dares in a day, I get annoyed with myself. It's... it's part of me, you know?"

 

"So basically all we have in common is that we look for stuff?"

 

"Yeah, that didn't really go the way I wanted it to."

 

"Well, maybe, finding stuff is the only real Venture League, uh... quota."

 

"Oh? That... that makes sense."

 

"I guess so."

 

"Hey Vector."

 

"Yeah?"

 

"Good talk, man."

 

"Thanks."

Chapter 18 Part 1:

 

"Finally! I was really worried, you know!"

 

Groaning, Talia forced her eyes open to be met with the dusty brown ground, and a twinge of pain. Rubbing her forehead, she sat up and glanced around, blurry vision eventually focusing on the concerned Jonna standing over her.

 

"J... Jonna?" She muttered.

 

"Who else?" Jonna snarked. "What happened, anyway? Some kinda fainting epidemic I should know about?"

 

"I... uh... no, no. Nothing like that."

 

"Good to hear." Stretching out a hand, she pulled Talia to her feet, before turning to where Dante and Ivy were both regaining conciousness as well.

 

"Shut up." Dante muttered to himself - no, to Raelyn - as he rose, before turning to Jonna and Talia with a grimace. "She's still salty about how Ivy insulted the Mythrans."

 

"Wait, what?" Jonna turned to Talia questioninly, who twisted her fingers together.

 

"It's a long story." The latter confessed. Jonna rolled her eyes.

 

"Do you trust them?"

 

A brief silence.

 

"I do." Talia eventually answered. Jonna nodded with satisfaction.

 

"Well, that's good enough for me, at any rate." She held her hand out to Dante. "Jonna fig, at your service."

 

"Dante Wilder." Dante responded, clasping her hand in his and shaking gently. "Charmed."
