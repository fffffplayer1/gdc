Chapter 24! Part Four!

 

Barry: Hey, Agent Scotty. We've got some questions, do you have a moment?

 

Mardolf: A moment? A moment?! *Throws his hands in the air and waves his staff around in distress, accidently casting a spell onto a nearby robotic dog and causing it to change into a robot cat* *It looks down at itself in confusion then shrugs* No, I do NOT have a moment! Do you have any idea how busy I am?!?! Every literal second I'm receiving a dozen requests for a teleportation, far more than average, so much so that there was a 0.004 second delay during your teleportation here! Unacceptable!!! Add to that getting constant, nagging crystal messages from Gandalf, Dumbldorf and Vitruvius about how upset they are that I missed the annual InterGalactic Wizards Conference, FOR THE FIFTH LIGHTYEAR IN A ROW, and trying to go through all the psychic fan mail I'm getting from my fellow Plutonians! *Pulls out some of his beard in agony* All while having to pretend I'm just a simple NPC who has all the time in the world to smile at and approve a bunch of minifigures!!! NO! I do NOT have time for you!!!

 

Opal: O_o Okaaaay, then... we still need to ask you a question.

 

Mardolf: AUGH! Make it quick!

 

Barry: Have you noticed any unusual activity going through the MPATS? As in any minifigures asking for a teleportation?

 

Mardolf: Of course not! No minifigure can enter the queue for teleportation. It requires for more Imagination than a minifigure can contain to engage the actual teleportation itself, which is taken from the individual who placed the request. It's literally impossible for anyone besides a Mythran to ask for a teleportation. However, a Mythran can place a request to teleport himself and up to several minifigures at once, though it is somewhat taxing for the average Mythran. Would you like to place such a request?

 

Barry: Uh, no, but thanks a bunch, Agent Scotty! Bye!
