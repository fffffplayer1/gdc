“Well?” Santis exclaimed after several long moments had passed. She shrugged and pocketed the map.

 

“Sure, I guess I believe you.”

 

“Much appreciated.” Danju nodded.

 

“So why did you come here, anyway?” She continued.

 

“We need to fix the map.” Explained Danju. “If we have any hope of standing against TheDude-“

 

“So, you want to fight TheDude?”

 

“That is the idea.” Admitted Talmid. The girl grinned.

 

“Well, finally!” She exclaimed. “We’ve been waiting ten years for this!” Without explaining anything further, she shoved past the knights and slammed a clenched fist against the door.

 

“Yo! Al!” She hammered on the metal again. “They’re cool! Open the door, already!”

 

The door creaked on its hinges and began to swing open, prompting the girl to push the knights backwards. Inside, Talmid could see the beginnings of what looked like a huge cavern.

 

“What even IS this?” Santis gasped, echoing Talmid’s thoughts.

 

“Just our little hidey-hole.” The girl grinned and began to march through a final tube of rock before stepping out onto a metal platform, followed by the others. She flung her arms out wide.

 

“Welcome!” She proclaimed. “To the guild!”

 

Behind her was definitely one of the more unconventional hideouts Talmid had seen in his time on Militiregneum. An enormous gulfing cavern opened up behind their apparent guide, the blackened earth illuminated by the golden light of hundreds of torches and lanterns. Mounds of earth, with what looked to be doors and windows, rose up from the ground and out of the walls, with stairs and ladders connecting them to each other and ensuring that nothing found itself cut off.

 

“What is all this?” He heard Danju exclaim from behind him.

 

“I told you!” The girl exclaimed. “It’s the guild!”

CHAPTER PART SOMETHING

 

“That ain’t a description.” Santis muttered darkly, but she paid him no heed.

 

“Come on, I’ll introduce you to Iamos.” She indicated with her hand and began to walk through the almost maze of structures.

 

“Who?”

 

“You said you wanted the map fixed. He’s probably the only person who can do that. He’s also kinda in charge here, so… you know…”

 

“Is he the alchemist?” Talmid cut in, not wanting to be led on a wild goose chase.

 

“Well, I don’t know about “the”, but he definitely is A alchemist.” The girl revealed, before walking on.

 

Talmid nodded. “Good a place as any to start.” He turned to Santis and Danju. “Come on.”

 

**--**--**

 

Stones and clods of dirt crunched underneath Talmid’s feet as he walked, with the red-headed girl leading him and Santis and Danju at his back. Groups of muscle-bound men kept passing them by, deep in their own conversations. Talmid himself couldn’t stop looking around, puzzled by one question.

 

“So… how was this place built?” He ventured.

 

“Alchemy.” Came the answer.

 

“And how long, exactly, did that take?” Picked up Danju.

 

“I don’t know… I guess it was a month before everything was finished.”

 

“A month?” Santis repeated in surprise as Talmid surveyed the enormous cavern in a new light. “No way.”

CHAPTER PART SOMETHING PART II

 

“Yes way.” The girl declared. “Not a lot of people know that alchemy is actually really useful for a lot of stuff.”

 

“Not tha’ we’d hear anything.” Santis pointed out. “Everyone I know thinks that alchemy’s cursed, or somethin’. Certainly, they wouldn’ like ta learn more about it.”

 

“Well, that’s their problem. Alchemy’s just a thing, like science, or bioelectrical manipulation, or… magic.”

 

Talmid noted the hesitation in her voice as she said this final word. “What was with that hesitancy?”

 

She shrugged. “Everything has a dark side. Magic isn’t an exception.”

 

Only then did Talmid notice that she was unconsciously clutching her shoulder with her free hand as she was speaking, fingernails digging into the leather of her clothing. A brief time passed before she seemed to shake herself out of the depressive state. When she spoke again the energy to her voice had returned, albeit slightly forced.

 

“Anyways, we should be just about here.”

 

She stopped in front of a door build into the wall at the far end of the cavern, knocking six times in a pattern.

 

A hoarse voice called from within – “Akane?”
