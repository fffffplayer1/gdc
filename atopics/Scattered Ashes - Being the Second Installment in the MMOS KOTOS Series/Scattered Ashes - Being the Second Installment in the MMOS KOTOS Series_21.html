‎09-20-2016 03:25 PM


  "In there!" Talmid heard a voice yell out, and then a squad of Shadow Knights ran past his hiding place, their flurry of footsteps headed down the way he came, for the observation room.  Talmid heard a shattering of glass followed by several thuds; no doubt Sir Rascus was putting Shadow Knights on the floor.


  The knight shook his head.  This wasn't his idea of a stealth mission, but at least the force field was down, and so were the lights.  Steeling himself, he got up and ran around the pillar.  He entered the nearest aisle, the darkness hiding him.  He almost brushed against a standing Shadow Knight at an intersection, but his back was to him so he made it past unnoticed.


  Then he saw the platoon.  Sir Talmid barely ducked behind cover before they could see him.  Then he peeked out again.


  Ten Shadow Knights and a rotating Barney Bot stood in the center of the warehouse, where everything was cleared out.  They stood in a circle around a pedestal, with a piece of parchment atop it.  Whatever they were guarding, they knew it was important.  It had to be the map piece.  Since they were looking in all directions, there was no secret point of entry, except...


  Glancing back at the observation room, Talmid could tell that Rascus and Marty were no longer there.  Then when panning back, he happened to still be looking up, and he saw a green shape climbing along the underside of the roof.


  Sir Talmid smiled.  Rascus was getting the drop on the Shadow Knights.

  The green knight dropped suddenly, soundlessly, right into the center of the group to take the parchment.  Not even the Barney Bot detected the movement, but it was about to turn on Rascus, who was effectively trapped now, so Sir Talmid chose that moment to run out with a battle cry.


  "Aaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaghhhh!!!" Sir Talmid screamed, charging the group.  The Barney Bot swung away from him, since Marty also ran out from nowhere, and began to shoot rockets at Marty, while the Shadow Knights spun in all directions, confused by the echoing of Sir Talmid's voice in the warehouse.


  Sir Talmid bodyslammed the first Shadow Knight into one of his comerades, then engaged another in a sword duel.  Things were blowing up all around the warehouse as the Barney Bot continued to target Marty, who ran as fast as his legs could wherever there was an opening.  The rockets made high pitched whining sounds and detonated on contact with everything they hit, and the Barney Bot was laughing too.  It spun suddenly and Sir Talmid barely jumped over its metal tail, which tripped a few Shadow Knights instead.  Rascus was nowhere to be seen.


  "RASCUS!" Sir Talmid called out.  "I hope thou art escaping!"


  "Pity me!" a voice called back, and then Sir Talmid saw the green knight lying on the ground, halfway to the nearest aisle entrance, prone and unmoving.  "I appear to have been caught!" he moaned.  A Shadow Knight stood above him with a tazer.


  The Barney Bot decided to stop shooting at Marty and hit Sir Talmid on the head instead.

‎09-20-2016 03:30 PM

 

  "This was supposed to be a stealth mission." Sir Talmid groaned when he awoke in the observation / control room.


  A Shadow Knight, the only one in the room, grabbed the knight's chin.  "You can make this easy for yourself," the dark knight threatened.  "Tell us everything and we won't torture you."  It was the same knight who had captured Rascus, since he held a tazer menacingly.  Or tazers were now standard equipment for Shadow Knights.


  Talmid eyed the weapon.  "I am not keen to tell thou anything." he said.


  "Good thing you don't have to tell us anything," the guard said, "just give us what you stole."


  Talmid frowned.  "Whateth?"


  A familiar voice spoke up behind Sir Talmid's head, and he had to crane his neck to see that it was Rascus.  "Give him the map, man!" Rascus said.  "It's hopeless!"


  "I don't have-"


  "I told them you have the map." Rascus hissed.


  "Oh." Talmid looked at the guard again.  He got an idea.  "It's in my pocket."


  The guard aimed the tazer at him.  "The green man said you had booby traps.  Take it out yourself."


  "You must untie me first." Talmid reminded him.

 
  Glaring at him, the Shadow Knight slowly untied Talmid's right arm.  Once him arm was free, Talmid began to slowly reach into the right pocket of his trousers, while maintaining eye contact with the Shadow Knight, who stared back menacingly.  Talmid's eyes narrowed, so did the Shadow Knight's.


  "Hurry up." the guard growled.


  "Remember," Talmid explained, "I have to bypass the booby traps."


  The staring contest continued until all of a sudden Rascus stood up.  "Thank you!" he exclaimed to the guard with a bow.  "The entire time you have been focused on Sir Talmid, I have been sawing out of the ropes restraining me with the sharp points on my armor.  Now I am free."


  The Shadow Knight angrily rushed Rascus, who bent down lower until the Shadow Knight was practically on top of him, then he grabbed the evil Knight's legs and threw him over his back against the wall, knocking him out.

  After being untied, Sir Talmid asked, "Do you have the map?"


  "I threw it to Marty." Rascus said.


  Talmid didn't wait to ponder what action 'threw it' connoted to.  He went for the door.


  "Where is Marty?" he asked.


  "I don't know."


  The reason only one, also not particularly bright, Shadow Knight was left to guard the two prisoners was because all the other Shadow Knights and the Barney Bot were searching for the third intruder.  Marty, pressing his body as flat as possible, could barely breathe in his hiding spot - not only was he afraid to, he was physically unable to, since he had slid himself under a metal shelving unit in one of the aisles.  Many Shadow Knights had run past his location in the ten minutes since, and he was very, very scared.


  He also gripped a paper airplane that had divebombed him in the head.  He had barely looked at it before hiding.


  Marty had barely begun to relaxed when he heard footsteps approaching again.  These ones were slower.  Methodic.  Systemic.  Getting closer.


  He was too scared to scream about the Barney Bot that was about to get him!


  Then a set of hands grabbed his right arm and he was pulled into the open.  Marty squeezed his eyes shut and would have screamed for real if another hand covered his mouth.  Then he realized these hands were soft.


  "Your arm was sticking out," Rascus chided him.  The green knight and Sir Talmid were there, and Marty breathed out raggedly, relieved that he was not about to be blown up.  "I'll take the map now."


  Marty looked at him, confused.  "I don't have-"


  Rascus grabbed the paper airplane from his clenched hand, and somehow it didn't rip.  Looking at it peculiarly, Rascus unfolded it, refolded it, and then threw it.


  Talmid wanted to yell at him, "WHY DID YOU DO THAT?!"

  But he had thrown it not a moment too soon, since then Shadow Knights appeared at the aisle end.  Sir Talmid and Rascus raised their swords, which they had retrieved, and prepared to run but more Shadow Knights appeared at the other aisle end, blocking them off.  They were surrounded for real.


  Heavy footsteps thudded towards them, and then the Barney Bot joined the first group of Shadow Knights.  Its eyes glowed red, and it levelled a chaingun at them.  "L0RD VL4D3K H4S G1V3N M3 P3RM1SS10N TO D33ZSTR0Y Y0U." it said.


  Now Marty could contain himself no longer and he screamed.


  Rascus threw a wad of sticky putty to cover the Barney Bot's 'eyes', but unfortunately not its mouth.


  "WH4T TH3 BR1CK." the Barney cursed.


  "Hit the deck!" Rascus said and he and Talmid threw themselves to the floor, dragging Marty with them, while the Barney Bot opened fire and blindly smashed all the Shadow Knights that were behind them.  When the Bot ran out of bullets, the three of them turned around and tried not to trip over the smashed Shadow Knights, some of whom were starting to rebuild, and then around the next aisle towards the front of the warehouse.


  A Shadow Knight stepped in front of them but was taken down by a swing of Talmid's sword.  The entrance was in sight, twenty yards away, and the other Shadow Knights were ten paces behind them.


  Suddenly the lights turned on.  Looking up, Talmid saw that the guard who had captured them was in the observation / control room, and grinning evilly while he stood over the controls that would turn on the force field.


  "Turn on the force field!" a chasing Shadow Knight hollered.

  "This might hurt!" Rascus warned.


  Electrical generators whirred to life, red lines lit up on the walls, and then a forcefield began to descend from the ceiling.  Talmid was in the front and ran under it with room to spare, then he turned around, fearing the worst for his teammates.

 

  The forcefield descended quickly and Rascus grabbed Marty, spinning on his heel and using his momentum to punt Marty through the gap.  Then Rascus slid under the forcefield Indiana Jones-style just before it touched the ground.

 

  Talmid sighed and nearly fell to his knees with relief.  It had been close, but now they were seperated from their pursuers.


  "Turn off the force field!" the Shadow Knight screamed.


  That was the last they heard of him since Talmid, Rascus, and Marty immediately ran out of the door, and once they were outside they kept running down the Morcian streets for a very, very long time....
