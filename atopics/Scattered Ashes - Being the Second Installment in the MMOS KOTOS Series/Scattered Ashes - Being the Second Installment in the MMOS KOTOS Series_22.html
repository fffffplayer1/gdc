CHAPTER THIRTY

 

“So now we have the two map pieces?”

 

Mattias glowered at the torn map, disregarding Jakyo’s question for a few moments.

 

“So it would seem.” He eventually said.

 

Rascus rolled his eyes. “I could have told you that.”

 

“Yes, well…” Mattias held the two pieces together, as if waiting for something to happen. “It appears to be broken.”

“Can’t you just hold the pieces together, like you’re doing now?” Asked Joe, hesitatingly.

 

“No, you don’t understand.” Sighed Mattias. “This map has magical properties, and is the only way that we can get the upper hand on TheDude and the advanced technology of his men. But it’s not working…”

 

“Probably because it was torn.” Reasoned Santis. “The question is, how do we fix it?”

 

“I do not know for certain.” Confessed Mattias. “But perhaps the one who gave me the map can help you.”

“Who gave you the map?” Pressed Talmid, anxious to get going.

 

“I’d say… hmm… yes, it was about ten years ago. A young alchemist gave it to me.” Declared Mattias, at which Marty, Joe and Jakyo all gave a start.

 

“Did you… did you say alchemist?” One of them said, but Mattias waved the question off casually.

 

“Yes, yes. He was quite an agreeable fellow, actually. In any case, he gave me this map, at least the main portion, a decade or so ago. He told me that if I needed anything, he’d be here.” He pointed at the map. “This village, between Orlan and the Scary Mountains. It shouldn’t take more than a few hours to get there on foot.” He sighed, gave up trying to piece the map together through willpower alone, and gave the two portions to Talmid, who stowed them away in his pocket.

 

“So who wants to go?”

CONTINUING CHAPTER THIRTY

“How come we ended up having to go?” Grumbled Danju.

 

“It was luck of the draw, Danju.” Pointed out Talmid. “Anyway – “ he coughed, not used to long walks after his stints in prison “- consider yourself lucky. I had to go, again.”

 

“Well, both the Thingguys went off to rescue Jonna, and Lucas is still recovering.” Said Santis, even his neutral tone taking on a hint of irritation. “You’re the only Knight of the Olde Speech available at the moment.”

 

“You make it sound like he was chosen for this, and that we didn’t jus’ draw sticks outta my helmet.” Danju grumbled.

 

“I’m more curious about where we even got all those sticks from, and in such convenient lengths.” Santis countered.

 

“I coulda broken them anyway.”

 

Talmid was content to listen to the knights converse, as they reminded him of himself a decade and over ago. It was as if his and his friends’ youthful optimism had been reborn in the souls of these new four warriors. But, eventually, the trail ended and the trio found themselves…

 

“Where exactly ARE we?” Santis wondered out loud as the knights surveyed what was in front of them.

A cliff, towering high above them into the sky.

 

“Wasn’t there supposed to be a village here?” Talmid pointed out.

 

“Probably moved or somethin’. Wasn’t Mattias given the map, like, a decade ago?” Asked Danju.

 

“Yep.” Confirmed Talmid. Danju sighed.

 

“Whatever was here musta moved. With TheDude n’ his eyes everywhere, it ain’t surprising.”

 

“The question is where to?” Declared Santis, forlornly.

 

“Where too, indeed?” Talmid concurred as he approached the rock face. There seemed to be nothing out of the ordinary about the rock – in fact, it looked too natural. Something about it seemed off…

“They’re here.” Santis stated abruptly, startling his companions.

 

“What makes you so sure?” Nitpicked Danju. Santis indicated the surroundings with his hands.

 

“We were told that there would be a village here, and yet when we arrive there isn’t one. Not a trace. Don’t you get it? Not a trace.” He emphasised the words. “Even when purposefully destroyed, villages can leave remains that stay standing for decades. Here, there’s no proof that a village was ever built in the first place.”

 

“So how d’we know they’re here and not somewhere else?”

 

“I highly doubt this alchemist would have forgotten to make sure his intel was accurate when telling Mattias of all people where he would be situated.” Santis placed his hands on his hips, and began to scan his surrounds. “They’re here, alright.”

 

Talmid frowned, and turned his attention back to the cliff face, absent-mindedly picking at his tattered jacket as he did so.

 

“So how are we supposed t’get their attention, anyway?” Danju grumbled. “Where are we supposed to knock? I’m not seeing any doors.”

 

His question was answered, however, when there was a low grinding sound. A tiny, almost completely inconspicuous hole appeared in the cliff face. Inside it appeared to be just more dirt, but Talmid peered closer nonetheless.

 

“We’re not with TheDude.” He stated. “My name is Talmid, and I am a Knight of the Olde Speech. We wish to speak to the alchemist who gave our ally, Mattias, a map ten years ago.”

 

“Uh, Talmid? Why are you talking to a wall?”
