STILL CONTINUING CHAPTER THIRTY, I WOULDN'T SAY I'M NEARLY DONE YET

 

For a moment, nothing happened. Then, suddenly, a far louder grinding could be heard as a portion of the cliff face first slid back, and then slid open, revealing a tunnel and a pair of stairs that descended into the unknown.

 

“Do we follow?” Danju spoke.

 

“Follow, we do.” Confirmed Talmid and led the way into the darkness.

 

*line break*

 

“You ever wonder how long this stuff takes ta make?” Wondered Santis, out loud, as the trio continued to walk with only the odd flickering wall light preventing them from being in total darkness.

 

“About ten years, perhaps?” Danju said with more than a hint of sarcasm in his voice.

 

“I would never have guessed.” Talmid decided to join in, allowing himself a smile as he did so.

 

“Ain’t a wonder they call you “the smart one”.” Santis tacked on.

 

“At least I can consider myself renowned for my intellect.” Declared Danju, his words haughty but a sly grin on his face nonetheless. “Physical strength is far more common, I have found.”

 

Instantly, the passageway was filled with noise as Santis, outraged, tried to defend himself and his strength against Danju’s words. Laughing, Talmid continued to put one foot forward in front of the other, wondering if perhaps there was still hope after all.

SA SA SA

 

**--**--**

 

Talmid himself wasn’t sure how long he had been walking in the almost-darkness, Santis and Danju as his back. Time seemed to become irrelevant and he couldn’t help but wonder what he was leading the other knights into. His doubts were brought to the forefront of his mind when an engraved metal door began to loom out of the darkness, blocking the bath.

 

“Who wants to knock?” Santis queried. Talmid gave a weary sigh.

 

“We’re not drawing straws.” He declared, before moving forward to knock.

 

It was the voice that prevented him from doing so.

 

“Don’t try to run.” It echoed out around the passageway. “Put your hands above your head.”

 

Talmid saw the glint of steel out of the corner of his vision, and heard a gun cocking behind him, and decided to listen, stretching his tender arms into the air. To his sides, he saw the others doing the same.

 

“You say you’re working with Matthias?” The speaker continued, stepping into the light of the nearest torch. Turning his head, Talmid could see a figure slightly shorter than him, clad in jeans and a waist-length jacket, open to reveal a red shirt similar to his. The girl (for it was a she) also had shoulder-length choppy hair the colour of blood, and a pair of bottle green eyes that were currently staring directly at him. Also pointing in Talmid’s direction was the pistol she held in her hands, a line of sight positioned between his eyes.

 

“Yes.” Talmid’s voice was a course whisper. “We have…” He trailed off, and instead drew the two pieces of map from his pocket. The girl’s eyes widened.

 

“That old thing?” She questioned. “Let me see.”

 

Grimacing, Talmid offered no resistance as she reached forward and plucked the paper from his hand, and watched as she frowned, peering at the torn paper in great detail whilst still aiming her gun at him.
