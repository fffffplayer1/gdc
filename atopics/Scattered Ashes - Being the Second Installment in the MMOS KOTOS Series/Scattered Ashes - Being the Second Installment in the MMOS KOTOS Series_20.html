CHAPTER TWENTY-NINE


  After dragging the incapacitated Mythran around the building's corner behind some barrels, Talmid returned to the door and inspected the doorknob, and seeing no visible locking mechanism or booby traps, gripped the handle.  Interestingly the door opened, unguarded and unlocked, the Dark Mythran being a lock enough to anyone who wasn't a trained knight at least.


  Without looking back, Talmid raised a finger and gestured behind him.  Then he slipped into the warehouse.


  It wasn't discernable through the boarded up windows, but the forcefield containing the map piece and Lord thedude's other secret articles maintained an ominous present within the building's armored walls.  Ten feet away from the door, seperating the warehouse entrance from its aisled goods, the forcefield was tinted red like a Dark Mythran and transparent like glass, like a large window spanning the thirty feet from the ceiling to the floor with a little empty space at the top where the roof arched.


  Noting that gap, Talmid figured that grappling hooks could get someone up and over.  But Talmid was on the ground, so they would have to find the forcefield controls instead.  And he had to be watchful, since Vladek's guard could be anywhere.


  Turning right, Talmid inched along the wall, and he was keen to dart past aisle openings.  The electrical crackle of the forcefield helped smother his footfalls.  A thick metal pillar, decorated in stone, lay ahead, one of four that supported the warehouse at its corners.  Talmid quickly darted behind it, where he found what he was looking for: a hiding place.  He crouched down, steadied his breathing, and there he waited for Rascus's cue.

  On cue, Marty and Rascus left their hiding spot in the bushes and crept towards the warehouse.  Marty couldn't help but feel conspicuous as they ran, their bodies low, across the parking lot.  His feet sounded especially loud slapping on the tarmac, and Marty tried not to run too fast.  Meanwhile Rascus was slipping soundlessly through the air and seemingly much speedier.


  "Rascus!" Marty hissed.  "Please wait!"


  "Speak up, I can't hear you!" the green knight answered back.  "I mean keep it down, you're being very stealthy!  Good work!"  Then Rascus was around the doorway and into the warehouse.


  Five seconds later Marty was inside, and he turned left.  Metal stairs lead up to an observation room, and Marty tried really hard to climb them as noiselessly as possible.  The stairs clanked with each cringeworthy step.  When he reached the top, Marty saw that the door was ajar, and he entered it with bravery.


  In the center of the room was a card table, around which four Shadow Knights sat on Samsonite chairs.  Marty sighed in relief to see that they were slumped over a game of solitaire, knocked unconscious, the work of Sir Rascus.  Shelves and desks lined the room's edge, with the windowed wall side having control panels lined with levers, dials, and buttons.


  "One of these must control the forcefield." Marty said.


  "Disable it then," Rascus said, looking around the small room's dimensions, "and quickly.  Did you know I'm claustrophobic?  This room is too confining for my acrobatics.  I already stubbed my toe."  Radcus gave each Shadow Knight another whack for good measure, while Marty got to work.  He began pulling random levers.


  All the lights turned off.  So did the forcefield.
