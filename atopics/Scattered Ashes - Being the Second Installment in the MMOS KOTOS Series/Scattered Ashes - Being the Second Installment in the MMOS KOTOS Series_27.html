"Don't look down!" Akane chided the trio of knights. "You made progress!"

 

"Precisely." The alchemist agreed. "Now, fixing the enchantment... well, it might not be so simple. You see, from what I've gathered - and feel free to correct me if I muck something up - the map was, ah, created by a man known as the Grey Wizard. When he wrote the Book of Morcia, the map was one of it's pages. The trouble is, that was a long time ago. The main question we now face is who, if anyone, has the magical capabilities to repair the enchantment."

 

"So there's a "we" now?" Talmid voiced. "Does that mean... you're with us?"

 

"That, it does." Voiced the alchemist. "I've been organising this resistance of mine for a while. It's about time I put it to use." He smiled for the first time. "I also have friends in high places."

 

"So what's the plan?" Akane asked.

 

"We're going with them, Akane." The alchemist revealed. He turned back to his table. "Just give me a moment to pack some of my more relevant research, and then we shall convene with the rest of their group."

 

"And what about the rest of the guys here?"

 

"They'll stay here, until I say otherwise. Our men are nothing if not self-sufficient."

 

"I... ok." Akane nodded, before turning to the assembled knights. "Gents, mind giving us just a moment."

 

"Of course." Danju nodded, and the trio stepped outside the room, shutting the door behind them. After hearing the wooden rattle that signalled privacy, Akane turned to the alchemist.

 

"You think this is a good idea?"

 

He nodded. "We've been hiding long enough. It's about time I do what I actually set out to do."

 

"We don't have to go through with this."

 

"Frankly, I think I've contracted cabin fever. This will be good for me, if nothing else."

 

"Yeah, but I..." Akane lowered her head, frowning. "I'd rather stay."

 

The alchemist sighed. "You know as well as I do that we can't compromise. I'm sorry..."

 

"But your word is law."

 

CHAPTER END
