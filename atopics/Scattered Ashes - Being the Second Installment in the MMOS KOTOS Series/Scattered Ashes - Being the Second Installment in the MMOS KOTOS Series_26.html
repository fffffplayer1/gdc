CHAPTER 327

 

“Yo boss.” The girl – Akane – called from without. “Brought some visitors for you.”

 

There was a long sigh.

 

“Show them in.” The alchemist eventually spoke.

 

Akane’s first reaction, apparently, was to kick open the door and gesture with an arm, giving a sarcastically haughty bow as she did so.

 

“After you, gents.” She chirped.

 

“Much appreciated.” Croaked Talmid as he stepped through the doorway, Santis and Danju behind him.

Once inside, they were greeted by…

 

Not whom they had been expecting.

 

The first notable thing about the alchemist was how terrifyingly young he looked – for all he had heard, Talmid had expected a wizened old man to greet them, but this person couldn’t have been older than thirty, some ten years younger than Talmid himself. He carried himself like a young man, too, standing up straight as the trio entered, and staring directly at them. A light brown overcoat was hung on his shoulders like a cape, and trailed down to his shins, flapping slightly as he rose. Underneath that, Talmid caught sight of a worn purple T-shirt and a pair of jeans, with some kind of pendant hung around the alchemist’s neck.

 

A mop of scraggly dark brown hair, grown out over time (perhaps due to a lack of scissors), hung in messy bangs over his forehead, almost completely concealing the long thin line of a scar that marred the skin above his right eyebrow. His eyes, a gunmetal blue, were the only part of him that looked as old as Talmid had expected. There were prominent bags under them, and were partially obscured by a pair of half-moon glasses that sat crooked on his nose.

 

The alchemist’s brow furrowed.

 

“And what business do you have here?”

STILL CHAPTER 2...8? IDEK

 

"Important business."  Danju replied, doffing his helmet.

 

"We've come to talk about fighting against TheDude." Croaked Talmid. "And... about fixing this."

 

Akane looked up, and gave a start.

 

"Oh, right!" She bounded over to the alchemist, who wore a look of mild exasperation as she approached. On the desk he had previously been sitting at, pages and pages of handwritten notes lay in seemingly unorganized piles, rolls of parchment on the ground and piled high on the wooden surface. A single candle lit the surroundings, and without the light shining in from outside the room, it would have been rather dark indeed.

 

"Look what they found!" She exclaimed, holding out the map pieces to the alchemist, who raised an eyebrow.

 

"Both of them?" He mused, looking up at Talmid for an explanation.

 

"We broke into a compound to retrive one piece, and Matthias had the other one."

 

"Mm." The alchemist nodded. "Okay, I see."

 

"We need the map intact. Without it, we won't stand a chance against TheDude's forces. This is the kind of advantage we've been waiting for for fifteen years." Danju urged. "Can... can you fix it?"

 

The alchemist looked up again. "Yes, and also no."

 

There was a brief silence.

 

"Huh?" Barked Santis.

 

"Technically, I CAN fix the map." The alchemist explained, taking the pieces from Akane and handing them back to Talmid, who was shocked to find them now joined seamlessly together.

 

"How did you-"

 

"Alchemy, but don't start celebrating just yet." The alchemist deadpanned. "The map works through a magical enchantment. I can fix the physical map the enchantment is bound to, but I can't actually restart the enchantment."

 

"So... we came all this way fo' nothin'?" Santis growled.

 

"I wouldn't say that." Counted the alchemist. "Personally, I doubt the enchantment could have been repaired without the map being one whole piece, so you didn't come here for nothing."
