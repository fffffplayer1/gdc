He darted across the road and one said, "Hey, shut up for a moment! Did you hear that?" He drew his sword, pointing it in Drumr's general direction.


The other one responded, "Yeah, maybe some of your buddies in the army coming to take us out," drawing his sword as well.


"Don't play innocent, we both know you're the imposter!"


"At least you imposters have a sense of humor these days! You make me laugh so much!"


Drumr had no idea what they could possibly be talking about, but decided to proceed with his attack. He crept up behind them and darted across again, attemping to scare them into rash actions.


"There it was again!" the first one said, pointing with his sword at the spot where Drumr has previously darted across the road.


"I know!! Stop back seat walking!" the other growled, holding his sword up in a defensive position.


"You're back seat walking!"


"But I noticed it first!"


"But I said something first!"


Drumr very nearly groaned to himself for two reasons. For one, they were becoming increasingly annoying by bickering with each other, probably over some petty nonsense. For another, these two weren't getting spooked like normal soldiers would get, which put him at a disadvantage. Deciding that he should move in before they detected his path, he jumped out of the bushes behind them and swung his sword in a wide arc towards them.

Chapter X (Not a number(I don't wanna call it 31 )) Part IV

 

 

Both of them heard him coming and wheeled around, their eyes going wide as the sword came around. They both raised their swords at the same time, managing to deflect the blow. Not wasting a moment, Drumr kicked one of them backwards and brought his sword down on this other one, and there was a loud clang as the apponent raised his sword to deflect once more.


Getting angry at the fact that they refused to just be smashed, Drumr flipped over the first one and drew a secondary sword from his backpack in an instant, attemping to stab at both of them at the same time. And yet again two swords were deflected right back at him. He kicked the legs out from under one and  leaped into battle against the second. He disarmed the second one after a short confrontation, but before he could strike the final blow he had to move his swords to deflect a blow from the rear, as the first had recovered.


Wheeling around, he tried to kick the first, but to his surprize the enemy dodged his attack, using the oppritunity to launch an attack of his own. Drumr barely got his sword up in time, and yet again he was forced to spin around and deflect a blow to the rear, as the second apponent had recovered his weapon. And suddenly he found himself in one of the stickiest situations there was: Holding back two apponents at once on opposite sides.


Suddenly one of his swords dropped as the strain of holding back one of the apponents made it give away. Realising he couldn't win a battle on two sides with just one sword, Drumr flipped backwards, landing out of the mess and grinning.
Brick, he thought to himself, There's no way I can win, they're too good. Against one of them there's a chance, but two?

 

"You two aren't half bad. It's been a while since I've actually had to try."
