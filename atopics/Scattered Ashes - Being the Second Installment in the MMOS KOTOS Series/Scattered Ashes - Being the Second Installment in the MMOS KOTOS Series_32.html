"So, you obviously know who we are," one of the Thingguys said, racing along behind Drumr.
"Yea," Drumr said, not stopping to look back.


"But who are you? We're supposed to work with you, but we don't even know your name," the other Thingguy said.
"My name is Drumr, I'm a solo rebel. Been fighting Vladek's forces here for Brick knows how long."
"Are you from Morcia?" the other Thingguy asked.


"No," Drumr replied, before continuing "I came here chasing a lead across the galaxy, but these clowns blew up my ship. So I ended up stuck here, and over time I learned the story of this land. That's why I do what I do now. But as soon as this nonsense is over, I have to continue on my journey."


The two Thingguys glanced at each other before sprinting even faster after their new ally.

After running for about fifteen minutes, the trio finally came across the camp where Vladek had his forces positioned. Hiding behind a small slope in the mucky terrain, they watched as an eerie fog set in, cloaking the camp.


Drumr eyed the camp from a distance with his spyglass and scowled, ducking back down with the Thingguys.


"What's wrong?" one of them asked, peeking over the hill.


"It isn't a standard camp. It has a completely different layout than the ones I'm used to raiding. In other words, I have no idea how to navigate it."


"And?" the Thingguys said, both of them peeking over the hill now.


"Unless you've seen this camp design before, we're going in blind," Drumr said, scouting out the camp yet again.


"There!" one of the Thingguys said, pointing to a small crevice. "Ambush party, with a patrol behind them!"


Drumr looked over to where Sir Thingguy was pointing and noticed that indeed he was correct.


"Good eyes," Drumr complimented before turning his attention back to the main entrance.


Guarding the entrance were two wooden towers, each holding three archers loaded with arrows. At the base of the towers stood two swordsmen, both heavily armored.

An eiree fog set in and Drumr looked around suspiciously.


Drumr growled and stated, "I don't like this fog."


The two Thingguys looked at him like he was crazy and one said, "What do you mean? It's just fog."


"I don't like fog. It brings back bad times."


"What happened to you because of fog?" one of them asked, peering over the hill.


"I almost lost my two friends to fog."


The two Thingguys looked at each other before turning back to face him as one said, "Either way, we should be going."


"Agreed, if we stay here for too long it's almost certain we'll be spotted," the other replyed.


"I think I've located our way in," Drumr said, pointing to the front gate. "See the two gaurds? We'll have to drop them fast and move on before the archers can get a bead on us."


"What's the plan inside?" one of the Thingguys said, peeking over the mound at the camp.


"We'll need to somehow split into two groups. One will need to hold off the enemy while the other scours the camp for Lady Jonna."


One of the Thingguys replied with a sour look on his face "Splitting up will most likely end poorly, but I guess if it's the plan, I will try to find Jonna."


The other bolted upwards and retorted, "I don't trust you to do it! I shall go with you to make sure you do not betray us!"
The other grumbled, "I don't believe you'll still be pretending to be the real Thingguy by the time we seperate, but I'm prepared to smash you when you betray us."


The two stared at each other with narrowed eyes, yet no more words were spoken.


A light shuffling was heard, and their eyes widened as they realised Drumr wasn't there.


Realising where he must have gone, the two Thingguys peeked over the mound. Drumr yelled a battle cry and charged, taking out the two guards before rushing inside, dodging the arrow fire as he ran into the camp, dissapearing into the fog.


Both Thingguys facepalmed and whispered, "Idiot!" before leaping over the mound to chase after him.

a week ago

Drumr entered the camp, not knowing exactly what he should expect or what he could expect. But he did pray that Vladek was not in the camp, for that would set them back tremendously.


He grinned and thought to himself, Vladek ain't so scary. I've seen worse. A part of him snickered, I've smashed worse...


He hoped that the Thingguys had found their way into the camp relatively easily, because he had not gotten in unnoticed. He grimaced at this as he ran from his pursuers. Far too many to even hold ground against.


He ducked around a small wooden shed, then proceeded to duck inside of a convienient door which he just noticed. Quietly shutting the door, he heard angry soldiers rushing past as they tried to find him.


He sighed and thought to himself, And I thought these things only happened in movies...


After making sure it was safe to leave the small building, Drumr made haste to do so, bolting from the shed to attempt to find the Thingguys, and hopefully Lady Jonna as well.


He narrowed his eyes. He felt an odd sense of power coming from somewhere in the fog, which had thickened so much that he could barely see three feet in front of him.


His worst fears resurfaced. If Vladek was here, that would be more than an issue. That could be that end of their journey.


He looked around in his belt for something to at least aid him in the fog a little bit, but the closest thing he found was a pair of broken night vision goggles.


"What the heck did I do with my flashlight?" he wondered out loud, checking all of his pockets.


He remembered suddenly where he was, and quickly ducked to the side as several soldiers rushed past him, talking to each other trying to triangulate his location.


He knew he hadn't much time, so instead of waiting around, he rushed to the side and continued the search.
