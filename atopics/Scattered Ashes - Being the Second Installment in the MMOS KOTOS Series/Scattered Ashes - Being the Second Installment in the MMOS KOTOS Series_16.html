CHAPTER TWENTY-EIGHT

The Guardian ran out of his house at the sound of the bell. It was the alarm bell, usually only sounded when there was a siege or some other emergency. Talmid, Rascus, and Marty had left them almost five hours ago, and he was beginning to fear the worst.

“Soldier!” The Guardian called to a passing guard. “What’s going on?”

The bell clanged loudly.

“A warehouse just…” the guard was cut off as a surge of panicking people pushed by him. “A warehouse just blew up! Please get indoors and wait for further instructions!”

A sliver of dawn cut through the air as the sun peeked over the roofs of the houses and the Guardian saw smoke coming from the center of the city. He turned back toward his house and called for Jayko.

The bell clanged loudly.

“What is it?” Jayko sleepily emerged from the house to join the Guardian in the street, which was now flooded with chaos as people tried to comprehend what was happening.

“Run and find Talmid and Rascus!” said the Guardian. “Make sure they’re alright!”

Jayko took a second to fully wake up, but then jumped into action. He sprinted off into the crowd and shouted something the Guardian didn’t quite hear.

The bell clanged loudly.

The Guardian’s heart was racing. The bell’s resonating booms crashed around inside his head as painful memories showed their ugly faces once again. He had seen this happen before, only there was a lot more smoke then. The memory was still quite vivid, and the Guardian could almost taste the blood in his mouth from so many years ago. His head swam as he tried to distinguish from the real smoke and the smoke he was imagining. There was a pain in his chest and he fell to the ground.

The bell clanged loudly.
