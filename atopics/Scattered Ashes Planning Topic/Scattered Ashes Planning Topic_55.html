Well, for one thing I'd say do not directly oppose to his choices. That could lead to hurt feelings and even to a fight. Try to be as discreet as you can in whatever you do and if you see that he continues listening to this music even after you state your disagreement and you can't dissuade him, then I think you should let it go. It's no good if you lose him as a friend. Even in the worst case, you will do him more good as a friend than as a... let's say non-friend, for lack of a better word.

 

As for what you should actually do... From what you say, he doesn't sound like the person who would engage in such activities, so the songs probably do not express him. That means they might not affect him. If, though, you realise they do affect him in any way, perhaps you should discuss your opinions on these matters and see that at least he does not consider these matters lightly. In the end, he is a free person and you cannot change his choices, but you can still encourage him to search for music that he likes and expresses him better. In any case, you should stand by his side as a friend and know that he will appreciate it and value your opinion.

 

I hope this helps.
