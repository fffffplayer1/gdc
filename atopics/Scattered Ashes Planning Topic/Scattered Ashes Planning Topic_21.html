I think that in Star Wars the clone troopers had chips or something secretly planted into their heads, which were what caused them to be forced to obey Order 66 involuntary. However, during the Clone Wars series, one trooper's noggin malfunctioned, and Order 66 went off in his brain early, causing him to gun down his own Jedi general in the heat of a battle against the Separatists. Some clones investigated this, and upon finding out about the Order 66 chips, had them surgically remove it from their brains, Captain Rex included. This caused them to be the very few clones to actually survive the Clone Wars without turning on the Jedi, and years later getting their character brought back in "Star Wars Rebels". I'd also like to note "Ragnarok," the cyborg clone of Thor that that Tony Stark, Reed Richards, and Hank Pym whipped up in the comic book "Civil War" storyline: what it be said that when Hercules "smashed 'im around the 'ead" he yelled the words "THOU ART NO THOR!" and, well...: let's just say that you could see the mechanical parts to prove it (and it sure wasn't pretty at that). I like the idea of following a theme of clones that have sadly been forced to have cybernetic implants to force their programming to work (although it's not their faults; they should be liberated, although they'll probably both get their creation sparks extinguished...).
