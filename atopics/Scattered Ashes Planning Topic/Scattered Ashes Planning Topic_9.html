1 year ago…

 

Ninjago_Builders was his name. And, once upon a time, he lived in the LEGO Message boards. More specifically, however, he resided in the LEGO Universe subforum. The Island of the LU subforum, off the coast of the Classics, wasn’t the most lavish place to live, but it had a whimsical charm that appealed to Ninjago_Builders. He lived in a small topic that he had constructed with his own two hands. He christened his topic “LEGO Universe: A Comedy,” and it was his pride and joy. But there was something about that subforum that he loved even more than its quaint charm, even more than his own cherished topic. It was his neighbors. All of them were amazing writers, and each supported the other in their writing. One of Ninjago_Builder’s favorite pastimes was simply walking around the neighborhood, looking at the beautifully crafted topics his neighbors created. Funny topics, dramatic topics, poetic topics, tragic topics, all of them magnificent in their own way. In the subforum, there were numerous town councils, dubbed “talk forums” where a friendly neighbor would be more than happy to lend a helping hand or impart a handy piece of advice. Consequently, when Ninjago_Builders received a letter of invitation from a prestigious school, he was hesitant to accept. Despite his misgivings, however, he resolved to take this academic opportunity presented to him. For the first few weeks at school, he wrote letters back to his friends and neighbors on the MBs, but eventually his all-consuming school life took over, and he became isolated in the pit of inactivity. After a year of arduous studies, he was overjoyed to receive summer leave, allowing him to return home to the Message Boards.

Present day...

 

*I drive along the dusty road, following the road signs toward the LEGO Universe Sub-forum.*

*I swerve to avoid a bone beatle in the middle of the road.*

*I hum to myself the LU Pirate Camp song.*

 

Ninjago_Builders (we’ll calling him N_B for short) paused in his actions, obeying the regulation that no more than three actions can be made in a single post when driving along a roleplay freeway or highway. After driving a little farther (humming the entire way) N_B stopped at a Like-station to refuel his vehicle. To his surprise, the Like-station was empty and abandoned.

“That’s funny,” N_B thought to himself.  “I could have sworn that this like station wasn’t abandoned when I left town.” This seemed odd to him, but N_B didn’t like to worry about things, so he simply ignored it and kept driving. A few more hours of driving/humming later, he finally reached town. As he exited his vehicle, he couldn’t help but break into a huge grin. He was back! He was home!


How ignorant he was.

When he stepped into town, things seemed...different. What had once been an modest, yet elegant, row of LU topics was now a crowded, haphazard mishmash of eclectic topics from various themes. He stumbled around town in a daze. When he looked at the street signs, he recognized the town of his childhood, but when he looked at the topics strewn alongside the street, he gazed at a foreign town. There! Finally he recognized a topic! Imagination Infection, a sprawling topic with hundreds of beautifully designed pages. He rushed in, excited, and saw...two cramped pages.
“What?!” Ninjago_Builders exclaimed.  “What the brick happened here?”

“Pst! Over here!” Someone whispered.

Ninjago_Builders turned toward the voice and was met by an old friend.

“Hey! Quintuple F! How’s it going?”

*I pull out a paint gun and shoot you in the face,* N_B roleplayed.

“Hah, good ol’ paint fighting,” N_B laughed. “Never gets old!”

“Quiet!” FFFFFPlayer hissed as paint dripped down his face. “Do you want to get us all deleted?” 

N_B’s smile slid off his face and shattered on the floor. “What are you talking about?”

FFFFFPlayer mouthed We can’t talk here and motioned to the back of the topic. Brimming with questions, N-B bit his tongue and walked to the back of the topic, where Johna, another old friend and author of Imagination Infection, was waiting. Then he stopped biting his toungue because it hurt a lot more than he thought it would. After a few glances to ensure no one else was around, Johna lifted up a trap door in the wooden floor and descended into a dim tunnel. N_B followed. The tunnel was made completely of earth, with wooden beams every 10 feet propping up the ceiling. On each beam hung a lamp providing faint illumination.

“So what’s all this abou--“

“Shh!” Johna shushed, motioning him to continue walking.

1 month ago…

 

“Attention! Civilians of the LEGO Universe subforum!” the booming voice of a moderator bellowed. “This subforum has become obsolete! Please prepare to be merged with the Classic’s forum!”

“Merged? What did that mean?” Many civilians wondered.
