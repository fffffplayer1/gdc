Did I already write a speech?  I might have.

 

 

I attribute three things to my growth in online personas and as a good person in the past six years.

 

1. A certain MMO game, not LEGO Universe, but I have mentioned it a few times.  It had a chat, a competitive setting, and an amazing community I interacted with daily on their online forum.  When the game ended, and the forum shut down a few months later, I lost many friends, but some I can still reach out to, and some I have neglected to stay in contact with.  But that experience helped grow me more than anything else in my formative years as a teenager, and I am forever thankful and grateful for the experiences that the forumers shared with me.

 

2. The LEGO Messageboards.  By far, there is no better place for a LEGO fan, or a good person to hang out on than the LMBs.  There are so many good people here, of all ages, and there is so much friendship here.  LEGO hosts an amazing website, and a great loss will be felt in March when it closes.  But the loss will not be of our friendships, and our memories, may they be eternal, of the great things that have occured in this place.  We have the Galleries and who knows whatnot.

 

The LEGO Messageboards have fostered me since I was six years old.  I'm a bit over a decade older now.  I read the users' stories, and laughed at their posts.  After I made an account, only with LEGO Universe and the Stories of LU forum did get to achieve the same greatness I so enjoyed reading.  I became a successful writer, and through that writing, and reading, I met my friends.

 

3. My friends have shown me love, patience, compassion, inspiration, loyalty, and all things from goodness.  I have learned so much from you all.  In my worst times, you give me safety.  I know where I belong, and what I must be thanks to you all, and that is as good a person and a friend that I can be.  So I am not saddenned by the LMB's closure, but gladdenned because we will still be together.

 

And I will never let go.

 

Thank you all.
