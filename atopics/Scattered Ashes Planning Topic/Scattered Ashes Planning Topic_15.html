Well, as you can probably ascertain from my username, I do follow the Ninjago storyline. In my own humble opinion, I feel that the Ninjago storyline has declined; it has become less and less of a ninja theme and more and more of a superhero theme. Don't get me wrong, I have nothing against superhero themes, but if a theme is supposed to be about ninjas and it lacks all the elements of a ninja theme, then it failed. I greatly enjoyed the first two ninjago episodes which depicted a whimsical world of ninjas; but once LEGO added the golden weapon vehicals (which were blatantly created for the sole purpose of creating new LEGO sets) and the NRG superpowers, Ninjago lost the whimsical charm that appealed to me when it first came out. The storylines that followed added more "superpowers" at the expense of actual ninja awesomeness. Sorry, I tend to rant about the Ninjago storyline when the topic is broached.

 

When Bionicle G1 existed, my brother and I read all the comics from the Piraka to the ones on Bara Magna. From this, we gained a pretty good idea of the storyline. Interestingly enough, it wasn't until G1 ended that we became really interested in the Bioncle story. We bought the visual novels that correspond to the comics before the Piraka, and we also bought the visual novels for the comics we had read because why not? The old Bionicle storyline is very interesting, although much of it is scattered across comics, videos, and those Bionicle Schoolastic Books that they published. Until I read about it online, I had not idea that in one of said Schoolastic the books the Red Star was revealed to be a facility for resurecting deceased beings in the matoron universe. Quite interesting

 

In short, yes, I do follow ongoing stories.
