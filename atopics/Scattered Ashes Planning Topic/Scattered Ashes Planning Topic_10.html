Most ignored him. But some listened. A multitude of users remained active in popular talk forums, most notably, the Knights of the Old Speech talk forum. Not only did they write awesome stories, but I mean who doesn’t like talking in Olde Timey Speech? Am I right or what? On the fateful day of the merging, thousands of minifigures stood in the KotOS forum in a noble attempt to save the topic, led by Talmid himself, who stood in the middle of them shouted words of encouragement. Many of the thousands of minifigures talked between themselves, idly chit-chatting, but then they stopped, suddenly, by an unseen signal. Know one could completely pinpoint the reason that they stopped talking. Maybe it was because the breeze stopped blowing. Maybe it was because the birds stopped chirping. Maybe it was because of the subtle drop in temperature. No one could discern the changes, but they all felt them. A cloud passed overhead, and everyone held their breath.  The cloud passed, and everyone exhaled. The topic was spared! Of course it was spared! Surely such an active topic could not be deleted! LOL! What a preposterous thought! After a few nervous laughs, the talking started up again. But it was abruptly shut by a loud metal clanging from every door and window in the forum. A panicked minifigure cried “There are metal bars covering the windows!” Another minifigure dashed to the nearest door and shook it repeatedly, to no avail. “So are the doors!” He called out. A sense of dread crept up on Talmid as he realized what had happened.

“The Mods locked the topic!”

Chaos ensued. Panicked minifigures dashed from post to post, up and down stairs from page to page, trying to find an escape. Many minifigures were trampled by their fellow minifigures in the panic to escape.

“The merging of the forums will now begin!”

From the two hundredth page up in her topic, Johna looked down below her at the moving forum. The entire island of the LEGO Universe subform shuddered, then lurched forward. Slowly the forum picked up speed as it races through the ocean waves on a collision course with the mainland of the Classics forum. As she looked out at nearby buildings, she saw metal bards systematically closing around the entrances of some topics. Then her vision was obscured as her own window was barred. She gave a heavy sigh, knowing that Imagination Infection was locked.

~ ~ ~

 

Johna headed for the trapdoor installed in her own topic. She raced down flight after flight of stairs, as the building crumbled around her, cursing how many pages tall the building was. “Why did I have to create so much!” she asked herself as her legs tired after descending countless steps. The beauty of her topic was forgotten; instead, she dreaded each of her own chapters as she passed them, because she knew that it would take her that much longer to reach the bottom floor. Finally, she reached it, leaping down the remaining 5 steps and scramblubg toward the corner of the page, where she opened the wooden hatch and leapt in just before the walls collapsed around her. Inside the tunnel, Johna gave a final, sorrowful glance up into her topic, and saw beams collapsing, lights shattering, wood snapping, chandeliers falling. For a few moments, dust obscured her view, and then...it was gone. Not even debris from the topic remained. Not a even single “Like” remained to betray that Imagination Infection had once existed. Nothing.

 

~ ~ ~

 

Across the former LU subforum, buildings crumbled, crushing the minifigures locked inside. The jolt of the impact flung many minifigures from the tops of high buildings to plummet to their smash. Some buildings had even collapsed before impact due to the movement of the subforum, and causing even more pandemonium.

 

~ ~ ~

 

Majorq10hero, one of the greatest authors in the land, had been sleeping in his mansion when the merge had occurred. His topic disappeared with him still in it, gone from the world as we know it.

Present day…

 

“This is why we must stay silent,” Talmid admonished N_B, finishing his account of the events that had transpired. “If moderators got word of a post that was inconsistent with our pretence of “Scattered Ashes Planning Topic” they would delete us without second thought.”

“No, no.” Ninjago_Builders muttered. “No no no no no.”

“I know it is difficult to comprehend,” Johna comforted softly, “But you must learn to--”

“NO!” N_B shrieked wildly.

“Quiet!” Talmid hissed. 

Ninjago_Builders didn’t care any more. He burst out of the door of the “Scattered Ashes Planning Topic,” emerging on a familiar yet foreign road.

“It will be there.” N_B assured himself. He quickened his step.

“It’s gotta be there. Hah, why wouldn’t it be there?” He laughed nervously, before breaking into a run.

“It will be there, everything, just as it was before!” His foot caught in a rut in the road, and N_B received a face full of dust and dirt as he was sent sprawling. His face was cut and bleeding from the fall, but he ignored it, quickly righted himself, and continued running, fleeing, fleeing from his fears. He turned past an alien house on a familiar corner, and stopped dead in his tracks. He fell to his knees.

“No, no, no, where is it,” he murmured softly to himself.

It was like a blow to the gut. A blow delivered by nothing.

“Let’s think this through,” he told himself in desperation. “I must not on the right street.”

But he knew he was.

“My topic! My home!” He sobbed, kneeling before the desolate lot before him.

A tear fell down his face, mingling with blood and dirt.
