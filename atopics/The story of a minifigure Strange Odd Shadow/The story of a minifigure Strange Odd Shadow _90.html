Chapter 28: Space… wouldn’t it be empty without some dangers in it? Part 5

 

          In their hurry to get out of the Space Maelstrom’s reach they hit a rock on the side of the ship. They were shaken and the spaceship was slightly damaged, but it wasn’t anything serious. They continued ahead. The gap of the Space Maelstrom grid was getting smaller and smaller every second. They were close enough now, but the gap was big enough for the ship to barely pass without being damaged, now. Then, the ship reached the grid and the two young men held their breaths.

          They were on the other side. A screen notified them that there had been damage on several parts of the hull, but no Space Maelstrom had stuck to them. They both sighed in relief. They were out of danger again.

          Shortly afterwards they got out of the area with the rocks, too. At that moment, they managed to see another world from afar. They headed the ship there. Soon, they were near enough to see a big arena that took most of its surface. That didn’t seem very good. Arenas were never good places. Was this part of the Ensee? They would soon find out. . .

          “Captain of Starship JC-378 B, identify yourselves and state your purpose!” a voice spoke through the radio of the ship.

          “This is John Steele and Antony Bandogan! We ask for permission to land. We come wishing to investigate this world!” Shadow replied looking at the roman style arena.

          “What’s with our normal names?” Magical asked.

          “I don’t want them to know we’re Nexus Force, in case they’re Ensee!” Shadow told him “Though if they are, they will probably have heard about us, so I don’t know if it’s any use!”

          “There’s space in Hangar B!” the voice came again.

          “Apparently, they’re not! Or they don’t know it’s us! Otherwise they would have already blasted us out of their atmosphere!” Magical said.

          “Not necessarily! They could want to question us! In any case, be at the ready when we get there!” Shadow argued while he started going round the Arena until he found an opened gate with a large B written over it.

          “Also…” he put his hand inside his backpack and got a blonde fake moustache, a blonde wig, blonde fake eyebrows and fake glasses.

          “I never know what you’ll get out whenever you put your hand in there!” Magical commented while he watched Shadow wear all these and smiled at the odd result.

          “Well, I’m running out of tricks to use soon! And don’t worry!” he said as he saw Magical’s mocking smile “I have something for you, too!”

          Soon, a red beard, wig and fake eyebrows were at Magical’s hands.

          “You’ve got to be kidding me! I’ll look like a Viking!” he protested.

          “The purpose is not to look like yourself, so that’s good!”

Chapter 29: The Tremendous Arena Part 2

 

          Shadow’s “hair” was shoulder-length and it got in his face, so that it hid a good part of it. The glasses had big circular lenses of the kind that fits you only if you’re Harry Potter or John Lennon. They were even bigger than the usual round glasses, because Shadow wished to vanish the clever look of his eyes, which could possibly be characteristic of his. On the other hand, Magical did look like a Viking with the beard, bushy eyebrows and long curly hair also hiding a big part of his face. His already tall and muscular body completed the Viking look. They had also removed any of their Nexus Force gear they had worn again ever since they left the Lonely Islands. However, if someone that knew them well looked at them from close they’d recognise their faces. Shadow wondered how good picture of them the Ensee had.

          By the time Magical had worn his new hair, Shadow had landed. They stood up and walked to the bridge’s door. They got out. Two guards, wearing armour that likened that of Romans, but with futuristic touches like the fact that the shaft of their spears had a laser look in a similar way lightsabers do, greeted them together with another person that wore a suit.

          “Welcome, Mr. Steele, Mr. Bandogan!” the person said “I am Legatus Dulciverbus. I am here to accompany you!”

          “Great! The rest of the crew will be staying in the ship, if you don’t mind!” Shadow said.

          “No problem! Shall we go?” Legatus said.

          They all started walking. Legatus Dulciverbus led them. Shadow looked all around. There were more spaceships around, but not too many. There were many of the roman-like soldiers though. They got to a door and to a hallway through it. More soldiers were there standing guard every now and then, but not too often. The whole place seemed to have an ancient roman style.

Chapter 29: The Tremendous Arena Part 4

 

          By that time they reached another door that led them to the inner part of the Arena, at the seats area. There were a few fighters in the oval sand-covered floor exchanging blows, but without really hurting each other. Some people stood aside and gave tips from time to time. Tranqimilites stood guard in the periphery of the arena by the wall. There were also some spectators, but they were scarce. On the periphery of the whole Colosseum-like building, there stood nine metal poles that Shadow couldn’t see very well from afar. The sun was in the start of dusk from that point of view, but soon Shadow realised the world remained perpetually in dusk time.

          “It is certainly spectacular!” Shadow exclaimed.

          “Yes indeed! It was first built by a Mosaic Jester architect for a gift to the Emperor of Batheia after a war they won together against the Bat Lords and Bone Men! It has often been damaged, repaired and renovated ever since”

          “Its history must go quite back, if it was included in the Old Crux Wars!” Magical commented impressed.

          It was a fact that the Old Crux Wars had occurred about three thousand years ago, but…

          “I believe the particular alliance took place after the fall of the Diephtharmians, Antony, so it actually was built during the Second Crux Wars about fifteen hundred years later!” Shadow corrected him.

          “Quite right so!” Dulciverbus agreed.

          “Still old!” Magical argued.

          “Mr. Dulciverbus, what are these poles all around the coliseum?” Shadow asked.

          “They serve to our communications, but quite frankly I do not know how they work, so I wouldn’t be of much help in this, I am afraid!” the man smiled.

Chapter 29: The Tremendous Arena Part 6

 

          Legatus nodded and left closing the door behind him. Shadow looked around to see an office. Behind a desk, on which there were many papers and books, a man with an aura of confidence and importance was sitting examining Shadow with his clever eyes. He pointed to a chair.

Chapter 29: The Tremendous Arena Part 7

 

          Malidux Nagnus had broad shoulders and a muscular buildup, though he didn’t seem to be too tall. He was wearing some armour similar to the one of the Tranqimilites. He had graying black hair and clever black eyes surrounded by wrinkles. His mouth formed a subtle smile. He would be considered handsome, especially in his younger days.

          “Commander Nagnus, do you know anything about the aims of the Ensee or who’s behind it? I’m sure that if anyone knows, that would be you!”

          “Mr. Steele, you must realise that the Ensee is a secret organisation. I’m sure you already know too much and I don’t think that’s for your good!”

          Shadow remained silent for a while examining the man, while the man examined him. It seemed like they could be keeping something from him. At last, he said “Are you a fan of the Roman Empire, Commander Nagnus?”

          “Indeed I am! I am glad to find another person that knows about it!”

          “I understand that the original resemblance is owed to the Mosaic Jester architect,” When at a time the First Builders had established communications with the Ancient Builders of Earth, people in Crux and other planets had come to know a lot of stuff about it. Mosaic Jesters had been very interested in the Roman Empire and had imitated it in many aspects “but I can see that there are newer touches that show an interest to the Romans, like for instance the Tranqimilites”

          “Indeed! You have a very observant eye, my friend! I learnt most that I have about Romans when I came to this place and found several scrolls that talked of them. There are many things admirable about the Roman Empire, but the one that I find most amusing is that it actually never fell!”
