Chapter 22: Run Shadow Run Part 7

 

          When they were far enough, Shadow led the others back to the central street. Once he considered they were safe enough, he stopped the trick, so that he wouldn’t waste his energy. While they went on along the street, they looked around them to make sure there was no one around. Shadow did not see any signs of life from the buildings, except for one time when he saw two frightened eyes looking at them from between the curtains of a window. When the person realised he had seen them, the eyes disappeared.

          Shadow realised that the darkness was slowly becoming less and less thick while the stars on the sky disappeared one by one. He looked around and saw light emerge from one place. The sun would rise soon. They had to find a hiding place soon.

Then, all of a sudden, a door opened next to them just the tiniest bit. A voice spoke silently. They did not understand what the man looking at them through the opened door said, because he spoke in a different language. Seeing them looking at him quizzically, he just waved them to get inside.

          The fellows didn’t think much about it. It could be a trap, but there wasn’t anything better out there. Besides, the person looked honest. They hurried in. They were safe now. . .
