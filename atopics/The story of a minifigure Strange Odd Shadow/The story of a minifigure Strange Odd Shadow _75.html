Part 2

 

            “I knew the Nexus can cause you some slight fake sensations, but I didn’t know it was capable of creating whole illusions” Shadow suddenly said.

            “What are you talking about?” Kate asked.

            Shadow pointed to something. Sure enough, a minifigure was walking towards them. It was Dracula.

            “Aren’t these things supposed to be personal? Only you can feel them, in this case, see them!” said Sarah.

            “I thought so, but I don’t know about this one!” Shadow answered.

            “Why can’t he be real?” Platinum, who didn’t know, asked.

            “He was the one that exploded the Vortex!” Shadow replied.

            “Oh!”

            At that time, Dracula reached them. There was a smile on his face.

            “I am alive!!!” he announced “I survived it!”

            “But how?!” Shadow enquired.

            “Well, I am a Vampire and though vampires aren’t really immortal, we are a lot harder to smash!” he explained.

            “Then, why didn’t you tell us that?!!!” Shadow bellowed, but he was actually happy.

            “Well, I wasn’t sure myself whether even vampires can survive that!” Dracula responded.

            There was a general wild enthusiasm as all the gathered friends cheered and celebrated this good news.

            Perhaps, things are not so bad! Shadow thought. Some of them seem to be alive after all! Not all of them smashed! I actually still have many friends!

Part 3

 

            “Shadow, would you like to have a walk and talk a little!” Overbuild’s voice was heard behind him.

            “So, you are accusing Mr. Shadow with no proof! I am sorry, my friend, but I cannot punish him without really knowing whether he did it!”

            “So, you’re taking his side!”

            “No, Panagis! I’m taking justice’s side!”

            Panagis seemed to be about to explode. Instead he just turned around sharply and left.

                                          The Story of a Minifigure:

                                            The Nexus Chasers

 

Prologue Part 1

 

          A frightened girl was roaming the dark and dirty alleys of Perith City in the dark of the night. Perith City was a world infamous for gathering the worst people of the Crux System. Criminals, outcasts, smugglers, escaped prisoners, even Dark Sorcerers frequented or lived in that place. And that was not the end of the list.

          The girl, who had every reason to be afraid, was walking quickly trying to find her way to some place better. It was clear that she didn't belong there. Her clothes even though ruined were still colourful. No person of Perith City would ever wear anything in the bright green colour of her clothes. Her age was also giving her away. Few of the people who called Perith their home were so young, not even their teens finished. Finally, a good observer would notice the symbol that appeared on her clothes. While the skull could suggest her being a pirate, the Cruxians knew better. It was unmistakably the symbol of Venture League.

          The girl was in quite a bad state. Apart from her clothes being ragged, her hair seemed not to have been brushed or washed for days, the visible parts of her skin all held injuries and, even though in the dark it wasn't easily seen, her right leg (but not her pants) was infected. Adding to that, she kept looking around at others or places from which people could emerge with bulging eyes.

Prologue Part 2

 

          “Hello, little girl! Are you lost? Let me help you!” the man said with a voice fitting his image.

          “No!” the girl yelped while stepping on his foot with force.

          The man swore, but at the same time his grip loosened. The girl escaped from it and started running while the man swore again. While running, the girl was more or less oblivious to her surroundings, so she bumped into another minifigure, this time a woman. She was getting away from her, before the woman could stop her. However, she didn't realise that as she ran, she got through a doorway and inside some inn or something. Inside, the light, which was bright only compared to the darkness outside, blinded the girl, but she didn't stay still nonetheless. She heard several exclaims like “What are you doing here, girly?!” Suddenly, as her eyes got used to the light someone grasped her arms. The girl saw that she was in a place full of many round tables around which Perithians were sitting, drinking, eating, laughing, gambling. Now, their interest had been drawn to the newcomer. They looked like predators watching their prey. Some of them had gotten up to get to her. One of them had managed to do so. He was a man similar to the other one she had met.

          “Where did you come from, girly? This is no place for little girls! Let me take you somewhere safe!” he said.

          “Hey, Bondevan! She told you to leave her alone!” someone said calmly.

          There came no answer, but suddenly a fist came into the girl's sight. Before she could realise it, Bondevan had been knocked out. The girl felt an arm wrapping around her back. She looked at her saviour. He was wearing what seemed to be Bat Lord gear, though his helm was not on his head, but hanging from his neck. He had black curly hair and black eyes. He was holding his staff in a way to threaten anyone who thought they might want to go against him. He then smiled to the girl. It was a different smile. It was neither creepy nor evil. It was genuine.
