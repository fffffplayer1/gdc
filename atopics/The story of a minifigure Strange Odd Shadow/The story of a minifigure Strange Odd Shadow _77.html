Chapter 2: A phone call in the midst of the day Part 5

 

          Soon, they were off for Avant Gardens. Magical spotted their rockets before they spotted him in the crowd of minifigures gathered in the Launch Pad area. Such places were always crowded, even after the Great Battle when many Nexus Forcers were smashed. When the three friends landed, Magical had already approached the Launch Pad to greet them.

          “Greetings Magical!” Shadow said as he jumped off his rocket, which quickly shrunk and got inside his backpack.

          “Hello! It’s good to see you again, Shadow, Kate!” Magical replied neglecting to notice Sarah. He paid his insult with an ‘accidental’ kick on his shin.

          After greeting each other, the fellowship headed for the Monument. Seeing the huge statue of Bob, Shadow remembered the news of his returning to the Venture Explorer after it was restored. Actually, the news talked of all of the old crew returning –the surviving part of it–, but Shadow knew Bob was among them.

          “It’s insane there, in Castle Kingdom! We drove them off one month ago, but many of the survivors hid in the other castles where they fortified, as you know!” Magical was saying.

          “Then, why do you tell us?” Sarah said with bitterness.

          Magical chose to ignore her “It’s very difficult to get in the castles or get them out! And it’s not like we can leave them, because we can’t let them multiply until they can really fight back! We are on the verge of recapturing one of them, but that’s all we have achieved until now!”

          “Perhaps, they shouldn’t have recruited you! They would have done a lot better without you!” replied Sarah, who seemed to be in a worse mood than her usual when talking to Magical “It would have spared your awful state, too!”

Chapter 2: A phone call in the midst of the day Part 6

 

          Magical was indeed in an awful state. He had wounds everywhere, his clothes were almost ragged, he was dirty everywhere. His eyes were red, perhaps due to many sleepless nights. But not all of that was necessarily caused by the battling.

          “Speaking of which, why don’t you have a shower or something?” Sarah asked making a grimace of disgust.

          Magical seemed ready to explode; he certainly had a vein pulsing on his forehead. The answer to the last question, which he did not want to reveal, certainly was one of the things that outraged him the most at that time. It was that the water running on his back reminded him of the time when the Healers had poured some sort of medicine on his burnt back. It had caused a very acute pain. But for some reason he didn’t let himself explode. The truth was actually that Shadow had told him to go easy on her, because she was in a fragile state.

          “I… I didn’t have time!” he finally said with a frown.

          Sarah frowned, too. Making Magical angry wasn’t as enjoyable as it used to be, for some reason. After that, the atmosphere was tense and no one talked much. Even Shadow, who usually saw the two minifigures’ quarrelling as a silly thing, was now worried.

          They passed the cave and reached the Sentinel Camp. Shadow had a look at the battlefield from behind the gates. It seemed empty compared to the past. The amount of Stromlings and Mechs and thus of Nexus Forcers was much smaller ever since the Great Battle had been won. There were only a few No-faction-yet-recruits waiting for the next Stromling to appear.

Chapter 2: A phone call in the midst of the day Part 7

 

          Shadow did not look for long. They soon had entered the cave that led to the Avant Gardens Survival. Survivals were some of the most dangerous areas in the Nimbus System –at least if you let them be. They were places where there was a special Maelstrom Field that turned all used Imagination to Maelstrom. That was why, the more time you passed in it, the stronger the enemies became. It was also why they didn’t let in more than four people in each of the Survival areas. If too many minifigures entered, the increased amount of Imagination used by the minute would make the Maelstrom far too strong. It is also necessary to carry Green Imaginite Crystals with you when you enter such a field, because when activated it forms an Imagination field that prevents your unused Imagination being turned and thus your being infected. When someone is smashed in a Survival, they are safely rebuilt due to Nexus Force mechanisms in a place where they can wait for the others. After the end of a Survival match, you have to wait an amount of time till the Maelstrom has gotten weak again. There were other Survivals, too, like the Gnarled Forest Survival, but those were only accessed by the elite.

Chapter 3: Another phone call in the midst of the day Part 1

 

          Shadow was approaching the end of the tunnel, when his friends caught up with him.

          “Who was it on the phone?” Kate asked.

          “It was Vlad!” Shadow said, Vlad or Vladimir being the actual first name of Dracula.

          “What did he say?” Sarah enquired.

          “That he found a lost girl in Perith City! He thinks she’s a Nexus-Forcer!” Shadow answered not stopping striding.

          As soon as Vlad had informed him of his having brought a lost girl, who seemed to have gone through something foul, from Perith City to Nexus Tower, Shadow had abandoned the Survival. This could be important and Shadow always wanted to help those harmed by Maelstrom or even from other things. His friends were dumbfounded for a bit, but soon followed him.

          By that time, they had almost reached the monument. As he looked at it, this time, Shadow thought of other things, even in his hurry. It reminded him of his father, the architect of this work. He had been smashed due to an injury caused in the Great Battle. Shadow had been angry at him for ages, but only made up with him in his last moments of life. He would never forgive himself for that. But as they neared the Launch Pad area, having climbed on the monument and jumped from it, his mind was slowly distracted from that.

          It wasn’t long until their rockets shot across the sky of Avant Gardens. Life in the Nexus Force was like that. You always travelled from world to world going to and fro all the time, as if someone was playing pinball and you were the ball. Everyone was used to it and yet it was still strange how Nexus Forcers never stayed for long in one place.

Chapter 4: The lost girl and the searching man Part 1

 

          In the end, the doctor let two of them stay. Shadow and Kate did. They were discussing in whispers about how the girl could have been smashed permanently and then return back to life! Neither could find an answer to the question that was enough on its own.

          Half an hour later and something, Nirwell woke up. She was surprised to see where she was, but then remembered that Dracula had brought her to the Nexus Tower. She looked at him and tried to smile. Neither the doctor nor the sleeping man drew her attention, but her eyes stopped on Shadow and Kate when she surveyed the room.

          “Vlad? Who are they?” she said in a low voice.

          “I am Strange Odd Shadow! That’s Kate Pirate!” Shadow told her coming closer “I am hoping we’ll be of help to your case!” Shadow smiled “We’re also friends of Vladimir!”

          “Strange Odd Shadow and Kate? Perhaps Shadow and Katie?” the girl asked.

          “Yes indeed!”

          “You are the friends of Magical Fire Monkey! I heard he said you were lost somewhere! So, they found you!” the girl continued weakly.

          “Well, that’s a pretty good memory! Surely, not everyone would remember all these details of one month ago!” Shadow commented.

          “They are some of the last things before…” she said with an even lower voice and then hesitated “Befor-”

          “It’s okay, miss Lane! You don’t have to answer that!” said a new voice.

          It was the sleeping psychologist. He had woken up and was looking at Shadow with annoyance.

          “Mr. Shadow, I think it’s time you left now! I am the girl’s doctor and I don’t think you should be talking about this now!” he said and showed him the door.
