Chapter 25: A mission in the dark Part 5

 

          “You could have come earlier!” said one of the two men.

          “We wouldn’t need to be here, if you’d done your job right!”

          “Correct me if I’m wrong, but aren’t you two the ones who were supposed to eliminate them twice before?”

          “At least, we were only two! You are an islandful of people and can’t even find them!”

          “The locals have hidden them!”

          “There’s no need to fight right now! Let’s just do our job, shall we?” another one spoke.

          They did not talk further, they closed the gates and left. However, in the meantime, Shadow and Valihombre had found the chance to sneak in just before the rockets entered. Now, Shadow thanked his luck. Breaking out and notifying people of your presence was so much better than breaking in and notifying people of your presence while you still had to do your mission and break out.

          They looked inside. It looked like a rocket garage where people lay their rockets. Shadow approached the nearest one and scanned with some techie-thingy.

          “Yes, it’s different than our rockets! I’ll have to trust my guess and think the difference has to do with the Zephyr Field!” he mumbled to himself.

          He took the rocket and placed it inside his backpack while it shrank. Meanwhile, Valihombre was bringing another one. He put it into the backpack and while Shadow was about to pick up the next, the alarm went off!

         “Let’s go!” he shouted and started running.

Chapter 25: A mission in the dark Part 6

 

          Fortunately, there was no lack of communication despite the speaking of different languages and Valihombre started running with him. Shadow placed some explosive on the door and it was broken to pieces by it. They got out and didn’t stop running until they reached the other island. The simultaneous running and use of his power had tired Shadow and he slowed down.

          They did not stop going fast, though. Meanwhile, people started appearing out of everywhere and heading towards the garage. Soon, they started searching around. The two of them often had to change direction or just stay still, so that they wouldn’t be discovered. Soon, the helicopter reappeared, too. At some point, Shadow saw something pass through the light of its headlight and he thought it must have been a rocket. That meant that more people were on the sky searching for them.

          Not much distance remained until they reached the hill when the headlight, all of a sudden, looked their way. Suddenly, they were visible for everyone to see. They lost no time and started running again, only this time Shadow was not camouflaging them. They immediately started being fired at. Many rockets were following them and the helicopter’s headlight remained on them making them an easy target for the rockets firing them.

          They reached the hill and started ascending it. Shadow thought that even if they reached the cave that would just give the others in. He did not know what to do. Minifigures started appearing, too, on the base of the hill. Things were getting worse by the second.

         Then, another sudden thing happened. Magical appeared in front of them. He was running frantically as well, but on the opposite direction.

          “They found us!” he shouted. . .

Chapter 26: Departure Part 1

 

          “Where are the others?” Shadow screamed as he did not stop running, but instead grabbed Magical and dragged him towards the opposite direction.

          “They went that way! I delayed them and then came to notify you two!” Magical replied pointing to their left.

          The three of them turned towards that direction. Shadow put his hand into the backpack.

          “Why didn’t you use your F-phone?” Shadow asked.

          “I guess I didn’t think of that!” Magical said almost conversationally, but mixed with that panic of talking when being chased.

          Shadow got three rockets out of his backpack. The one was Magical’s, the other was his and the last one was one of the stolen ones. He gave the first to Magical and the third to Valihombre, while they still increased in size.

          “Ride on! We’ll find them faster and may save our skins!” he said getting into his own.

          The rockets’ engines roared as they started working and they shot forward. They flew around the hill with the helicopter and the hostile rockets after them. The minifigures that were on foot soon got tired of chasing after vehicles that were faster than they were, but soon people inside cars arrived. Some of them tried to enter the hill, while the rest surrounded it, so that no one could leave –at least using their feet or a ground vehicle.

          Suddenly, they noticed two figures running on the slope of the hill in front of them. As they approached them, Shadow noticed pursuers appearing from above, from higher parts of the hill, too. And then he saw it.

          “Oh yeah! They’ve got this giant robot, too!” Magical commented.

          “You are forgetting an awful lot of things lately, Magical!” Shadow said angrily.

Chapter 26: Departure Part 2

 

          Valihombre saw the robot, too, and he muttered something in a panicked tone in his tongue. It was a tall thin machine that could turn around its waist and had a relatively small head, next to which, on its shoulder, there was a minifigure. On the end of its big legs it had wheels. Yet, the upper side of it was more of a cause to worry about. Its left arm consisting of little pieces to be flexible ended up as a great sword electricity coursing through it and a circular saw on the down side of its grip. At the middle of the same arm, there was a gun. The other arm ended in a laser blaster. In addition, the part of the robot that could be considered its upper chest there were three missiles that could be launched. Even though, in the dark, they couldn’t see it very clearly, Shadow had a reason to recognise it and know all of this very well.

          “That’s D.R.O.I.D.! It’s of my design! How can they have one of them?”

          “That’s yours?”

          “Yes! Destructive Robot Of Imagination Defense! They must have stolen the design somehow! This mean war…er! Give these to Kate and Jomadre!” he tossed the other stolen rocket and Kate’s rocket to Magical.

          Then, Shadow turned the other way. He was going to make sure the DROID did not complicate their taking off. He knew that if they turned their backs to it, it could destroy them. As he approached, the DROID turned towards him and fired one of the missiles. Shadow made a maneuver to avoid it, but the missile turned and came after him.

          “Oh brick! They’re homing! Why do I have to be so good?” he exclaimed.

Chapter 26: Departure Part 3

 

          The idea to lead the missile onto the DROID was rejected immediately as he knew he couldn’t come close to it without getting sliced by the sword or the saw. Besides it had a very hard skin. Thus, he made a turn towards the ground and the missile followed. He looked like he was crashing and it was very risky, but the missile was closing in on him. He suddenly changed direction the last second –he actually was slightly late as the back of his rocket did scratch the ground a little. Yet, the result was the desired; the missile did not turn as quickly and exploded on the ground leaving a crater.

          Meanwhile, the DROID, which was nearer now, started firing shells and laser beams at him. He maneuvered to avoid the projectiles, but then being nearer to the destructive machine he noticed some of its details. At that point, Magical came to his aid.

          “Magical! Thank our luck! They seem to have stolen the early design!” he shouted jubilantly.

          “What does that mean for us?” Magical asked as he started firing back, to no avail though.

          “Do you see the heart?”

          There was indeed a part that looked like the DROID’s heart only it was in the middle rather than on the left. It was made of a different material than the rest.

          “That’s its core, but at this design, it’s more fragile! If we destroy that, it will stop working!” he spoke to his comm..

          “Let’s do this then!” Magical replied.

          That moment, the saw scratched his rocket on the side and he was pushed away.

          “Shadow, I’m coming to help!” Kate’s voice was heard from the comm..

          “No! Get Jomadre and Valihombre and get out of here! We’ve got this!” he said.

Chapter 26: Departure Part 4

 

          There was a moment of silence during which Shadow could imagine Kate sighing and then “Ok!”. He looked back and saw two rockets shoot towards the sky. He felt a knot in his stomach.

          His attention was drawn back to the DROID when a laser beam hit the front of his rocket and set it gyrating around its vertical axis. Shadow set it to a course towards the DROID and checked what was happening. Magical’s rocket had been hit a couple of times by the look of it, but he was still trying to shoot the robot’s core. Meanwhile, everyone else had caught up on them, but they didn’t dare draw near DROID. Its projectiles could easily hit them, too. Thus, the rockets landed nearby, the helicopter flew above them lighting the scene and the people on foot had created a wide circle around the DROID watching. The people in cars had stopped trying to get to them where there were no roads a long time ago.

          Another homing missile was launched. However, Magical shot at it immediately and it exploded mid-air. Shadow tried to get nearer and fire at the core, too.

          “Shall we explode the other missile to hurt it?” he suggested.

          “It won’t blow up! It’s protected when not launched!”

          “Then, I’ll shoot it when it fires!”

          Shadow did not answer, because at that point his rocket was hit by one of the shells DROID fired and then by its sword; he had to control his rocket as electricity coursed through it stopping its engines. He punched buttons again and again as it started falling, but it did not respond. At the last moment, he pressed a button and he was ejected out of the rocket which crashed and exploded.

Chapter 26: Departure Part 6

 

          As the rocket flew higher, Magical’s eyes turned towards the DROID’s chest and he noticed the slightest movement and then his quick reflexes did the rest. Before Shadow could realise what had happened there was an explosion on the aforementioned part of the robot. The machine did not seem to collapse as Magical had hoped since the core was near the centre of the explosion, but he noticed it was spasming. Perhaps, something important had been damaged. He did not lose his chance and fired inside the smoke at once. There was another smaller explosion and the DROID stopped moving altogether.

          “That’s it! Let’s go! Good job!” Shadow bellowed jubilantly and Magical changed direction.

          Someone on the helicopter tried to shoot them and the people on the ground started getting inside their rockets, but it was over. Shadow and Magical were already too far away to catch up with. The Ensee people surely had not expected them to defeat the DROID.

          Soon, the rocket was outside the world’s atmosphere and the single rocket set course for the next adventure. . .

You may or may not have noticed that I just posted two chapters. If you have, congratulations your eyes are working correctly and you have mastered the science of counting. If you haven't... I don't know what to say!

 

To the point, though: What that means is that for the first time ever since I started writing Book 4 -do you believe it's actually the fourth one; it's weird every time I remember that- of the series there is no chapter in stock. For all this time, every time I posted a chapter, I had actually just written the next one.

Everything started when I finished the FFFFF Team. It was still summer and I was thinking I should start posting the Great Battle's chapters on September like I had with Strange Odd Shadow and because it felt like each school year was the same with a book year (even though I delayed in finishing SOS). But I did write some chapters anyway to have them ready for September. So, I had a stock of chapters then. Yet, I quickly posted them all this time.

Next year I did the same with the Nexus-Chasers, but this time one chapter was left in the end and it was always replaced with another until now.

I did not have a particular reason for doing it other than the fact I usually only post one chapter at a time. Today, I said "Let's post both!" and I did it! So, hooray! Now, we're finally on the same part of the story.

Here ends my story ramble, which isn't random I suppose since it is related to this story! Nothing official about the rambles by the way!
