Chapter 18: The Rockline Tribes Part 1

 

          The rock boat hit the dark gray rock-made side of the lava river, guided by the Rock creatures. The beings forced them out of the boat after some of them had disembarked. Once they were all on ground, the group started walking on a cave tunnel that they found there. The leader and a crystal creature led the way. Four guards surrounded the three minifigures behind them. Two more creatures were the rearguard.

          The three friends proceeded in anguish. They did not know what fate awaited them at the end of this tunnel or wherever they were being led. On the one hand, the creatures had saved them. Didn’t this mean they did not mean harm to them? On the other hand, they might have just been fighting Maelstrom and saved them accidentally; their behaviour to them wasn’t very friendly until now either. And the communication problem certainly didn’t help.

          They walked for some time. The creatures had short legs and so they were slow. Shadow, who if on his own usually walked fast when he was going somewhere, found it annoying after a while to have to walk so slowly. Yet, eventually they reached a bigger cave. It was taller, wider and longer. It was some sort of natural hall –though it must have been dug more when the rock creatures had found it. There were hexagons shaped all over the ‘walls’ of the cave as if it was a stone beehive; rock creatures were lying in them, apparently sleeping. At any spots where there was magma, the minifigures saw rock creatures relaxing in them as if they were in a Jacuzzi.

          And then when Garutho informed them they were close, they heard a voice echoing through the tunnel “They are escaping! Quickly!”

          Their two pursuers had reached them. It would seem neither the quarreling giants nor the rage of the volcano had stopped them. Lasers started raining on them. Shadow was not sure how exactly they all managed to avoid the fire. Perhaps, it was because everything was shaking. Or because their chasers tried to save their lives, too. Perhaps, they were good at dodging, but Shadow did not think that was enough.

          Then, they saw the exit. There wasn’t much light, because the world was always stuck in a time around dawn, but they could see the dark sky. Shadow got his rocket out while still running. His friends did the same. The vehicle started growing to its proper size on his hand. At the point when he could no longer hold it in his hand, he got outside.

          How good it felt not to be under a ceiling and breath fresh air, feel the cold temperature. Yet, Shadow did not have time to enjoy this. He hopped into his rocket, which was just now finishing growing. He waved his hand to the Rocklines shouting “Thank you!” and started pressing buttons and pulled a lever.
