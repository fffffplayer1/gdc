Chapter 27 Part 3:

 

"I'll keep that in mind." Harold said, stealing a couple of glances over Lux's shoulder into the caves. There was definitely SOME sort of light coming from there, but he wasn't too keen to enter - the last light he had found in a glowing cave had been the gem, after all.

 

"Ok." Lux said. "Uh... what... what are you gonna do now?"

 

Harold shrugged. "There's not a lot I CAN do with two broken limbs, is there?"

 

"I guess not." The boy admitted. "When they're healed, then."

 

"I suppose I'll try and find a way back to Nimbus Station." He said. "My friends and sort-of-family are there, after all."

 

"How would you know something like that?" Lux questioned.

 

"A bit of logic, a bit of reasoning - and a gut feeling." Harold explained.

 

"Oh." Lux said slowly. "What do you think is going to happen now the tower is destroyed?"

 

"I don't know." Harold admitted. "I guess it would be too much to hope the maelstrom gives up and goes away."

 

"Almost definitely too much." Agreed Lux.

 

"Well then," Harold declared, "in that case, I think it's safe to assume that there's going to be some major trouble ahead. For all of us."

"The meeting room wasn’t at all what Skye had been expecting. Her preconception of meeting rooms was that they were where official decisions were made, and as such should look important and plain, so as not to distract those who were trying to make choices that could affect people’s lives. Here, however, was the complete opposite of that. The room was brightly lit with fluorescent lighting which changed colour from blue to red to green every ten seconds. The walls were green, the floor was painted in earthen tones and the ceiling was decorated with a sky blue colour.

 

 In the centre, there was indeed a table, but it looked reminiscent of a ping-pong table with no net. In one corner of the room, there were a number of televisions switched onto various news stations. Chairs were positioned around the entire room, and cups of something were lying on most of them. Despite the multitude of places, there were only three people in the room before the new group had entered. The most normal looking one was a girl of an undetermined age, (Skye would have guessed late teens,) who had glossy black hair that fell to her chest. At present, she was conversing with a humanoid figure which had some kind of metal obsession – an armoured body and a war helm with a visor for a face.

 

The final character in the room seemed to be made entirely of black flames save for his eyes; they were nothing more than crimson slits. Skye couldn’t help but feel slightly afraid, even if this person was an ally. The figure turned towards her, and focused its red eyes on hers.

 

“Hello!” Announced Gaia as the four waltzed inside. “We’re back!”"
