Chapter 2 Part 3:

 

"What..." What IS that?" Asked Will.

 

Harold reached into his pack and pulled out the crystal, holding it in his outstretched hand. It was hypnotically facinating, and he could have stared at if for hours if not for Will.

 

"Can I hold it?" Asked Will. Harold turned around and stared at him.

 

"Will." Harold said slowly. "This isn't show and tell at a school. This is a strange, powerful crystal that has given me maelstrom themed superpowers."

 

"I still wanna hold it!" Protested Will. Sighing, Harold dropped the crystal into Will's hand. Instantly, Will cursed and snatched his hand back. The gem bounced on the floor before Harold dived for it and picked it up, somehow not phasing through the floor again.

 

"What was that?" Harold asked.

 

"Ah! Ah! Ah!" Exclaimed Will. "It started to infect me!" Will held out his hand, and Harold saw the telltale purple mist spreading towards Will's wrist.

 

"Don't panic." Reassured Harold. "I've got this." He held out his hand, and the mist immediatly left Will's hand and travelled to his own. But instead of infecting him, it seemed to disappear. Harold himself suddenly felt a rush of energy, as if he had downed a high-energy drink.

 

Will kept looking blankly around, his eyes flicking from his hand to Harold's.

 

"Ha-Harold... what.. the..."

 

"I don't know." Said Harold mournfully. "I wish I could tell you I knew, but I can't."

 

"So now what?" Asked.Will.

 

Harold shrugged. "We keep testing, to see what else I can do."

Chapter 2 Part 4:

 

"Experimenting?" Asked Will. "You're not a test subject, I'm not sure how wise that is-"

 

"We need to know, Will." Pointed out Harold. "This could be- heck, it IS a game changer. It's important that we at least need to know what exactly my limits and strengths are."

 

"I'm still not really keen on this." Sighed his friend. "But if you want to do it, that's fine, I won't stop you."

 

"Well..." Exclaimed Harold. "I can't exactly do this without you!"

 

"I don't see why." Pointed out Will. "You should be able to write after materializing, right?"

 

"Write?" Repeated Harold.

 

"Right."

 

"Right, or write?"

 

"Right. Or maybe it was write."

 

"Which right is right!?"

 

"Write?"

 

"Right! That's right!" Gasped Harold. Then he composed himself.

 

"Did you purposefully do that?"

 

Will shrugged. "Maybe. But I didn't originally intend to start-"

 

"I don't care." Interuppted Harold. "Are you going to help me, or not."

 

Will looked at him blankly. "No." He said. "The powers stuff is cool and all, but REALLY? You want to make some kinda list to know which one you have?"

 

"Well..." sighed Harold. "I like to be organised."

 

"Too organised." Retorted Will. "You make yourself sound like a test subject, and you're more than that. You're my friend. But if you start treating yourself like..." He trailed off. "Frankly, you're beginning to sound like the side of Doctor Overbuild's personality that Plue warned you about." 

 

"What?" Asked Harold. "Are you serious?"

 

"Bro, I was there almost every day at some point." Declared Will. "The only people who were there more than me were Plue and Overbuild. I'm telling you, Skip was so annoyed. The assembly faction fell behind schedule because their leader was watching you take a 13-month long nap. He's really interested in you, and not necessarily in a good way."

 

With that, Will turned and walked out the door, the wooden item shutting loudly behind him. Harold sighed, and looked at his hands.

 

"This is getting serious." He murmered.

Chapter 3 Part 1:

 

The next day, Harold decided to visit Plue, at her house, to talk to her and see what she thought. There was just one problem. A temporary qaurantine had been set up, due to an increase of maelstrom energy in the area.

 

"Gee, I wonder where that came from." Harold muttered to himself as he tried to think. The Nexus Force had ordered no-one was to leave their homes, and had stationed gaurds around the area. That way, the people knew they were serious. But Harold went by a specific sort of code: If a rule is stupid, ignore it. And he knew that, whatever power he had, it wasn't necessarily dangerous. So there was no need for a quarantine. Thus, it was stupid and he could ignore it. 

 

But he still had to sneak around, undetected. That was where the powers themselves came in very useful. It didn't matter if it was teleporting from one building to another, swinging around on maelstrom webs or phasing into shadow, there were plenty of ways to go around undiscovered. But he had to admit one thing - this who powers thing? It was FUN.

 

 

 

There was a knock on Plue's door. It didn't sound forced or imperious, so it couldn't be the Nexus Force. Wondering to herself, her feet pressed down on the cold wooden floor of her house as she walked to the door. But when she opened the door and nobody was there, she grew suspicious. Reaching behind the door, she silently pulled out a katana and held it in front of her, ready to defend herself. 

 

Glancing round, she looked down just in time to see a shadow on the ground that was not her own. She glanced down suspiciously at it. Suddenly it reared up to take minifigure form. 

 

"Oh." Plue sighed. "It's YOU."

 

Harold arrived at her house around half an hour later, and knocked on the door cautiously.

 

"Hello?" He asked. "Plue? You home?"

 

It was at this point that his tapping on the door knocked it over, slamming on the ground. Inside the house, all Harold could see was a large amount of chaos, damage, and maelstrom.

 

This was bad.

Chapter 3 Part 2:

 

 As Harold ventured into the house, a sense of extreme dread began to subtly overcome him. Admittedly the rooms weren't completely trashed, but there were still signs of a struggle, and remnants of maelstrom around the place. Lights flickered and sparks flew as Harold tentatively stepped across the wooden floor through the house, his shoes creaking with almost every step.

 

There was a snap.

 

Harold wheeled around. Nothing. Cautiously, he looked to either side of him. Nothing. Finally, fearing what he might see, he looked up.

 

Nothing.

 

Trying to calm himself down and figure out what had happened at the same time, he lowered his head to it's normal field of vision. He placed a hand on his chin, and the other in his pocket as he tried to think.

 

Whilst Harold had a good sense of humor, and was by no means a mathematical genius, he did have an impressive tactical mind. No-one could beat him at chess, and he probably would have been promoted several times through the ranks of the Nexus Force if it were not for his non-outspoken nature and his inability to get people he didn't know to listen to him.

 

At this point, the young man thought about all the possible options regarding both the house being trashed and the suspicious "snap" noise he had just heard. 

 

Thinking about the new maelstrom abilities he now possessed, he decided to try and see if they could be any help in the situation. Concentrating, he suddenly felt a flow of energy into his eyes. Upon opening them, he discovered he was currently using some type of X-ray vision. Hurriedly, he looked in all the same directions, now with the ability to see through solid walls.

 

Still nothing. 

 

Sighing, he deactivated the power until he realised something.

 

There was one direction he hadn't looked.

 

Down.

Chapter 3 Part 3:

 

 Using his power again, Harold viewed the ground below him. There were several items of interest for him, but he didn't have long to look at them as the wooden floor below him opened up and he found himself sliding along at an almost vertical angle.

 

As he slid, he contemplated all sorts of things-instincs, irony and traps. But eventually he landed at the bottom of the slide, and found himself in a small stone room. There was little in it, save for shadows. Sighing, Harold sat down and leaned against the wall. As he did so, he leaned against the wall, activating a hidden switch. The wall opened, and out popped a worn Television, playing Star Wars Episode VI.

 

"Now that's just rubbing salt in the wound." Harold complained, as Admiral Ackbar ranted on.

 

Suddenly, something flew out of the shadows. Harold couldn't see it, but the projectile smashed into the Television, sending glass raining everywhere. At the same time, a person stepped out from the shadows. Their identity was unrecognisable, and Harold wasn't sure he knew who they were anyway. In any case, the figure pulled from their back a wicked-looking blade, with spikes all along the metal.

 

"As much as I appreciate you turning off the irony channel," said Harold to the figure, "I wouldn't exactly like to be skewered."

 

The stranger retorted by lunging with the blade at Harold, who dodged and watched as the sharp prongs stuck into the wall instead. 

 

"Whoops." Harold shrugged, and then zapped his assailant with lightning. The attacker flew into a wall.

 

"My goodness!" Exclaimed Harold, and approached the stranger, holding out a hand. "I'm so INCREDIBLY sorry..."

 

The assailant knocked Harold's hand away, only to have their own hand covered in webbing.

 

"Oh." Said Harold emotionlessly, as the shadow tried to free it's hand. "At least you weren't dumb enough to take my hand, but still... you're not the brightest, are you?"

 

The stranger looked at him.

 

"What? Life is too short tot be pretend nice to idiots."

Chapter 3 Part 4:

 

The shadow responded by seemingly looking up, and Harold was taken aback at the crimson eyes that stared at him from the blackness. Suddenly nervous, he back up cautiously as the shadow stood up.

 

"Okay." He said. "Not such an idiot then. Could you please tell me exactly WHAT you are?"

 

There was no worded reply from the shadow, but Harold's cranium suddenly felt cold, and he had to supress a yearning for it to all be over, and for the maelstrom to win.

 

The Maelstrom Minifigure rolled his eyes.

 

"What a predictable response." He sighed, before holding his palms out. It looked like a placating gesture, and the shadow gleefully swept towards him. But suddenly, lasers shot from Harold's palms, going right through the shadow. The figure looked at the holes in itself, which were already starting to close, and crossly back to Harold. 

 

Harold looked from the holes to his hands, and then into the malevolent red eyes.

 

"In all fairness, I wasn't entirely sure what would happen and whether it would work, so why don't we just-"

 

He was interrupted as the shadow dived at him, knocking him to the ground. Harold gasped as the assailant's hand clamped around his throat, and retaliated by making a fist and smacking his attacker on the back of the head. Amazingly, it seemed to work, and the figure reeled backwards. Grinning, Harold kicked out and sent the shadow flying.  Dazed and wheezing, he climbed to his feet. 

 

Suddenly, the wall opened again and a speaker popped out. For one terrible moment, Harold imagined the phrase "It's a trap!" Being played, but instead he heard something almost as bad. There was a terrible, garbled screeching. Harold winced and placed his hands over his ears, so agonizing was the sound. But the shadow stared in... was that horror? The bright red eyes of the figure were widened for the first time in what looked like fear. The speaker stopped emitting noise and retracted back into the wall.

 

Then the celing started to descend.
