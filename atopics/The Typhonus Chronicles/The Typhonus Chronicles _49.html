My hands were over my ears the laughter was so loud. It didn't help that I was adding to the noise with my own laughter. But, the room did eventually calm down once the microphone was turned off. I still chuckled to myself, thinking of Albert and his insufferable addiction to donuts. I then looked down at myself. Raggy clothes, grubby hands and feet, general 'ick'. I needed to spiff myself up a bit.
"Has anybody got a cane?" I yelled out.
"I do." Daniel was suddenly right behind me.
"Ee-yah! Darn you Daniel. Never mind bless you, you have a cane right?"
He nodded.
"Great, good, anything I can trade for it?"
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
After a few minutes of trading with the locals, I had accumulated a white top hat, white trousers and shoes, a white overcoat and black vest, and a white button-down shirt with a black bow-tie. Generally spiffy in my opinion.
Me and Overbuild had agreed to get everyone onboard the airship above us, other than two minifigs who would stay behind and pilot the Cata-tank back to Nexus Tower. We had also agreed on a time, thirty minutes after we conversed. And after a quick look at my watch, I saw that that was right now.
"Alright then, open the upper hatch!"
The pilot turned to me and said, "We don't have one."
I face-palmed.
"Ugh, sure they put in everything but the ceiling hatch! Stupid maelstrom, they are such idiots! Or they didn't get all of the plans before I was rescued. Huh."
I grabbed for the microphone. "Alright then, Overbuild. Turns out we DON'T have a ceiling hatch. So if you could make one..."
Four giant metal fingers punctured the cieling of the Cata-tank, and they quickly tore it off.
"Yeah well, that... works... great."
It was quickly followed by eight rope ladders.
"And that works even better."
