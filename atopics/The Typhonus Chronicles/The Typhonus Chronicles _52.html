Numbah twenty!

The Venture Explorer. If you recognize that name, then you would know it to belong to a ship. Very closely designed to the Venture Koi, many Minifigs were ferried across the universe to come to the Crux Worldlets. You would also know, that on its final mission, it was boarded by the maelstrom. The Venture Explorer was quickly overtaken, and the Minifigs aboard were forced to create their own rockets to fly to safety. But what most don't know, is that it had a sister ship, the Assembly Pathfinder.
The Assembly Pathfinder is Overbuild's special operations ship, made to the same specifications as the cargo ship Venture Explorer, yet with much heavier armor, and a reserve tank of imagination set to blow should the ship be boarded. Albert kept many of his designs in this ship, and often experimented on samples of atmosphere collected from different worldlets.
At that moment, he was showing me one of those experiments.
"It's amazing! We assume the air around is oxygen, breathable. In reality, the atmosphere throughout every one of the worldlets is actually a gaseous form of imagination, and when we breathe it, it becomes what we need!"
I nodded my head, intent on his lecture.
"This gas is stimulative giving us new thoughts with every breath, so it explains why figs that come here gain such a link to imagination."
Clay and Daniel, who had come along, were not as entertained. Daniel's head kept bobbing to his chest, eyes fluttering, while Clay just kept moaning, grumbling, and complaining about dying of boredom. G couldn't enjoy the lecture with all this grumbling.
It among all this, that I had an idea.
"Albert, sniff up this sample."
"Ah, why? I'm breathing the same stuff that's in the tube, it's just more compressed."
"Exactly. Sniff up the compressed imagi-gas."
"Well, alright. And did you come up with that name on the spot?"
I nodded. "Yes, yes I did."

"Good for you." Albert then leaned over, opened the lid to the test tube, and sniffed it up like a child sniffs mothers fresh bread.
Alberts eyes went wide, and a spark flashed from his forehead. He stumbled back, and caught himself on a table occupied by tons of papers. Overbuild was breathing heavliy at this point, and clutching his heart. He put both hands on the table, his head hovering just above it. His eyes widened once more, and he grabbed up a sheet, staring at it in disbelief.
"What the brick is going on!?" He said in a wavering voice.
"Albert," I said as I put my hand on his shoulder, "Did you see something."
He nodded, and pointed to the sheet. It was a roster of known mythrans, each one with specific characteristics that defined them. He was pointing to Spark.
"I saw, him. But, there was something wrong. He was writhing in pain, purple entering his body. What did I see?" He was pleading now, tormented by this vision.
"Albert," I said, quietly, "The Mythran you saw, his name is Spark. He isn't a normal Mythran, a guardian of imagination. He IS imagination. If he dies, so does Imagination. And we're killing him."
