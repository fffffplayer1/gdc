"Wha-" Escaped his lips, as he found himself and G plummeting towards the unforgiving surface. His hand remained outstretched, grasping for the safety that wouldn't come. No expression came to his face, Just a simple, stiff gaze.
There was screaming, he was sure of that, but he paid it no mind. He just kept his current trajectory, towards death. He closed his eyes, and went limp, accepting a cruel fate. Time slowed around him, the wind barely registering, the gut wrenching feeling that always comes from falling, the flapping of his cloak. It was after a few minutes that he realized he wasn't falling anymore.
He dared to open his eyes. Maybe he had been blessed, and died before the horrible smash at the end of the drop...
"...If this is heaven, it is not what it's cracked up to be."
"Albert!' G mocked exasperation, and tapped his shoulder.
"Oh you wonderful machine, you!" He shouted, as soon as he discovered his means of, well, not being a splat on the ground.
"Mech re-activated once we got a fair distance from the ship. I think they got their hands on an EMP dissorter."
They were still far above the ground, though much closer to it than the Pathfinder. Mech had them cradled in his lower arms, the upper ones had come apart to reveal helicopter rotors.
"So, where is everyone else?" G inquired..
"I saw the sillouhettes of Clay and Daniel still in the room, and I'm fairly sure I saw Vernon fall out..."
"Vernon? Who's that?"
"Someone. Someone I hope has changed."

I didn't know it rained on Crux Prime. Not until I had to spend a freezing night underneath a large blue spider. And might I say, it was not in the least pleasant. Tripod, whom I left on guard duty, would not keep quiet. He would chitter at the slightest light or movement, and turn his searchlight I unwittingly gave him on. The Spider Boss, whom I decided to name Phil, because saying Spider Boss all the time was irritating, proceeded to explore these areas that Tripod lit up, as his new curious nature drove him to explore every new thing he saw.
With the rain, which I normally would enjoy, pouring down onto my head, I was nearly driven to my grave. Thus, I stayed awake in fear of me catching pneumonia and never waking. So I would be found drawing in the muddy ground at my feet, unsure of what would happen, or what would come to be. I finished the last detail of my mud drawing, and finally fell asleep when the rain ceased to drown the earth.
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
In the morning, we picked up our feet, and began our trek towards the Nexus Tower.
