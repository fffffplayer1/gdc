"Good for you." Albert then leaned over, opened the lid to the test tube, and sniffed it up like a child sniffs mothers fresh bread.
Alberts eyes went wide, and a spark flashed from his forehead. He stumbled back, and caught himself on a table occupied by tons of papers. Overbuild was breathing heavliy at this point, and clutching his heart. He put both hands on the table, his head hovering just above it. His eyes widened once more, and he grabbed up a sheet, staring at it in disbelief.
"What the brick is going on!?" He said in a wavering voice.
"Albert," I said as I put my hand on his shoulder, "Did you see something."
He nodded, and pointed to the sheet. It was a roster of known mythrans, each one with specific characteristics that defined them. He was pointing to Spark.
"I saw, him. But, there was something wrong. He was writhing in pain, purple entering his body. What did I see?" He was pleading now, tormented by this vision.
"Albert," I said, quietly, "The Mythran you saw, his name is Spark. He isn't a normal Mythran, a guardian of imagination. He IS imagination. If he dies, so does Imagination. And we're killing him."

Albert stared at me, his eyes wide behind his oversized goggles. He was still breathing heavily, so confused.
"Every time we destroy, break, hurt, we kill him just a little more. That is th brilliance behind the Maelstrom. The more we fight it, the stronger it gets, the weaker imagination becomes. You've noticed its been harder to imagine just what you want recently, yes?"
He nodded, still in his shocked, wide-eyed state.
"But, if he dies, we'll still live. Just because if this Mythran dies doesn't mean we will." G threw in.
"No. But, we will be nothing. just machi-"
"Hey!" Mech shouted out offended.
I just stared side to side, quite aware of the mistake I had made.
"Uhm, right. Sorry Mech. Machines are people too. We would be like... Brick it, I don't have a good comparison. Something mindless, no real drive but to survive..."
"Animals?" Daniel said helpfully.
"No, not quite."
"Rocks?" Clay put in helplessly.
I just gave him a look. "No. Not rocks."
"Zombies..." Overbuild breathed out.
"Ah, yes! Thank you Albert!" I exclaimed while throwing my arms into the air.
"Oh, wait, no. We would actually be dead, us on the Crux worldlets anyway. With no imagination, no imagi-gas, no air."

"Life on these worldlets would end as we know it..." Clay said miserably.
"Basically."
"Well what do we do? If we fight the maelstrom, we'll just speed up the process!"
"Not necessarily." G stated.
We all turned to her, each sharing a similar expression; the poker face.
"Look at it this way, whenever we fight, we use weapons, guns, explosions! All kinds of kaboodle."
We remained the way we were.
"Just use imagination! I can't give any good examples, but there have been theories that we could use imagination to cure fully infected Stromlings."
Clay pathetically raised his hand.
"Yes, Clay?"
"I have a good example."
He casually walked over to me, and poked me. And then he poked me again. He poked me again and again, saying nothing, being as casual as if he was reading a book.
"What!?" I released my irritation on him.
"You. This man is a perfect example of being able to cure stromlings. Not only did he do just that, but he was cured himself."
