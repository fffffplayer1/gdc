I needed to talke to Overbuild about Sparky, even if it meant telling him who I was. But then there was the problem of getting there. When I reached the rocket pad, I realized that not only did I not have a rocket, but that this was obviously not the right rocket pad.
"Excuse me," I said to a nearby minifig, "Where is the launch pad to Nexus Tower?"
He looked at me up and down. "You a member of the Nexus Force?"
"Well, not entirely yet."
"You won't even be let near the pad before your a member!" He chuckled, "Go see Johnny Thunder, Mardolf the Orange, Sky Lane, and Commander Beck Strongheart."
"I've seen Mardolf already."
"Good, so Johnny Thunder would be the next on your list."
"Right. Thank you."

Many hours and many rockets through space (after I bought myself a rocket) later, I came back to nimbus station, ready to become a full member of the Nexus Force.
There was Nexus Jay, at the end of the Avant Garden path, greeting new recruits.
"Oh! Hello again! Do you have all the factions approvals?"
"Yes. All, four." I said, pausing between each word.
"Took a long time then?"
"So, so, soooooo very, long." I said, Still pausing.
"Well, your efforts weren't for nothing. You are now officially a member of the Nexus Force." He said, as he jotted down on his paper.
"So, which faction are you going to join?"
I thought for a moment. If I were to join the Sentinel Faction, I could put major devastation on the maelstrom. But, then didn't Spark say to build to defeat the maelstrom and save him? To use imagination? Yes, but which faction would most support that? Assembly, of course. Then again, it wasn't my area of expertise. I should join the faction in which I would be the most use. But then that would be...
Yes, that was the one.
"I would like to join the Paradox." I said.
