Many things in the world are described, as indescribable. This is ironic, in and of itself, as you are describing said object. In my opinion this is a grammatical error, so instead of describing the following situation as indescribable, I will describe it as being beyond comprehension.
It was beyond comprehension.
One moment, I was with the group on the Assembly Trailblazer, the next moment, I was tumbing through the sky, end over end. Up was down, down was left, and I couldn't find right. Everything was dark, the only thing to illuminate the empty night were the flashes of lightning, the bursts of purple, and the laughter. It was mine, but not mine, as if I had suddenly taken evil classes. It rang through the air, crashing into me, tormenting me, threatening me...
These were the only things that made remote sense to me. Everything else had no meaning, shape, or form. To me, it was unreal. And then something very real came. Pain. I crashed into something. It wasn't the ground, it was smoother than the rough surface of Crux, and softer. And it was making a noise, a wierd, chittering sound, like a demented squirrel. I had heard it before...
"Oh dear." Escaped my lips. I looked down, and I was still quite high off of the ground. My eyes continued their descent, and I found myself staring into a large, intense-blue eye. I was on top of the Spider Boss. I did what any perfectly logical scholar would. I ran like the dickens.
