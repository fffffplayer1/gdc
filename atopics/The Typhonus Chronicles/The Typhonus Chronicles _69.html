Chapter 23, part 1:

 

The mountains of Crux Prime were large, and plentiful. Each one soared high into the sky, their rocky ledges and great cliffs daring the bravest and most courageous of Minifigs to climb them. They beckoned all with their seemingly infinite might and unparalleled natural glory. Any who would dare the immense stone mass would be challenged to their limits, having to overcome crevices and precipices that would turn the bravest hearted to whimpering fools. At their bases, the landscape of Crux Prime spread as far as it could, until it reached one of these great stone beasts or a chasm to the maelstrom far below.

Some of these chasms are revealed to be sinkholes, filled to the brim with vile monsters of every shape and size. Purple is the choice color of most as they prowl their hunting grounds, waiting for an unsuspecting minifig, or possibly a foolish one, to fall into their hands. Pits of lava riddle the expanse of the chasm floor along with steam gushing from their vents.

And the dragons! Once great and noble creatures of the elements, they were reduced to mindless monsters that attacked all their infected eyes can see. Their wingspan easily surpassing two cars, they could carry themselves into the air with great speed and agility. And, as with all dragons, they breathe spouts of purple fire from their threatening snouts, which sport their own savage sets of teeth as well.

There was no doubt that Crux Prime was an immense, and perilous place. It made me wonder why the Nexus Force went and built their home base in the middle of it.

"Skrit-chik-ik." The Spider Boss chittered to me.

I looked at it's nearest eye from my position on top of it's head. I couldn't tell what it was saying, or even implying. I didn't like having to rely on a pair of headphones jacked into Tripod so I could listen to him translate it.

"This takes too long." Was the translated complaint. I just nodded to him and rubbed his head, which he seemed to enjoy much.

"Can we stop to eat?" The monotonic synth voice inquired.

I just smiled and rubbed his head a little more. Tripod looked at me  pleadingly, and I knew he wanted some too. So, after balancing myself on top of the spider, I removed my other hand from it's stabilizing position to scratch tripod's back. As soon as I begin, his light dimmed, and he looked like he was in heaven. 'Quite a contrast to my surroundings,' I thought. In the distance, I could barely make out a column of light shining in the sky. It had an angelic glow, contrasting the chaotic black-light of it's surroundings. I knew this was the Nexus Tower.

I sighed. There was still a long way to go. I couldn't make out any more detail than the fact of it's presence. I scratched an itch in my hair that could only have been developed by an anxious feeling of hopelessness, which was followed by a whine from the spider that translated to, "Don't stop!"

I resumed scratching. Tripod eased off gently from my hand, signaling that he was fine with not having his head scratched. I patted him one more time in thanks, and began scratching my itch again.

The spider was an interesting fellow. So calm and benevolent, and somewhat playful. It was so unbelievably different from the spider that dragged him into the Imagination Nexus so long ago.

A thought then occured to me. I kept referring to it, as an it. I didn't even know if it was male or female. I wasn't about to check, so I was faced with the uncomfortable decision of asking it itself.
