Chapter 53

 

Being a Figdroid, Jay didn't feel any pain from the clone's punch to his chest.  He was never really a fighter, always the calmer one, with Katie being the "ZOMG IMAH GET YOU" type of person.

 

He was doing alright though.

 

He just barely caught the clone's next punch in his hands before it reached his face, but a kick sent him into the far side of the glass tube.  And his inertia sent him flying through the glass again into a room full of shocked minifigures who had been watching their descent.

 

Jay stumbled to his feet, but was pulled down again by his clone, who was grasping his ankles.  The familiar purple lightning was back, and Jay quickly smashed his foot onto the clone's hand, stopping the lightning from flying anywhere where it could cause more damage.  He was a little slow though, because he felt an electric shock charge through his foot, and for the second before reserve power kicked in, he lost all control of his right leg.

 

So he kicked out with his left leg.

 

He felt his foot connect with the clone's head.

 

And it disconnected from its body.

 

The moment it happened, the clone vaporized.

 

Everyone around the room wore the same jaw-dropped-wide-eyed collective look.  Jay managed to pull himself to his feet and called, "Nothing to see here, move along!" before darting into an elevator that was just opening.  He could only hope Katie was doing alright-

 

"Sup bro." Katie said, and Jay would have jumped 30 feet into the air if there wasn't a roof in the elevator.  Bump.

 

Jay landed, stumbled, and collapsed on the floor. "Don't do that," he complained.  He eyed the scuff marks all over her shirt. "You don't look very good."


Katie grabbed his arm and hauled him up to face the mirror at the back of the elevator. "Look at yourself first," she retorted.  Jay whinced at the sight of his, well, lack of a face.  A lot of his face was just circuitry....
 
"Where is this elevator going anyway?" he asked, and as if in answer the doors swung open.
