Chapter 22

 

In less than a second Intrepid was standing in front of them, his shield inches from Jay's chest and his staff frozen in mid-poke centimeters from Katie's face. "Congratulations," he told them. "You're dead."

 

...

 

"Try and develop a better reflex, response time, hand-eye-coordination, pretty much don't be slow." Intrepid stated as he tossed piles of bouncing bricks from his backpack across the roof.  After inspecting each one, he announced, "Quick bulid them."

 

The response he got was a blank expression from each of his trainees. "Aw, you can't be serious." Intrepid jeered, but then stopped himself. "Actually come to think of it, you are serious." He held up a picture of a bald minifigure in a red shirt and blue pants. "Have you ever seen this guy?"

 

"No?" Jay answered.

 

"As I thought." Intrepid said. "You can't quickbuild and you're can't fight, hehehe.  It's because you haven't had DA POWAH OF IMAGINATIONNNNNNNNNN!!!!!!!!!!!!!! unlocked for you yet, by that guy who's called Bob."

 

"What's the power of imagination?" Katie asked.

 

"The power of imagination means to use your full imagination potential to do awesome stuff at a massive scale, such as building whole towns, using special abilities that come with gear such as speed hats or shield slams like meh awesome Bat Lord gear, getting pets to become your pets very quickly, and quickbuild small stuff quickly." Intrepid explained. "But I've been thinking, do you guys really need to travel all the way across this star system just to find a guy called Bob, when you're figdroids?

 

"As in, can't I just activate it for you now?  Flip a switch?" he continued.  He held his hand out, and asked, "Can I see your blueprints?"

 

...

 

In the end, Intrepid announced that it had been a long night and as day's first light appeared, they departed for the morning.

 

But they would be back.
