Chapter 52

 

  Quick to respond, Katie dove across the floor, grabbing the legs of her Maelstrom clone and tackling it. "Get the other one, slowpoke!" she said through her teeth, and Jay the slowpoke took off after the runaway Maelstromcopycatdroid.  The one thing Mr. Darkitect Jr. Guy had left out in replicating the figdroid platform was speed....

 

  The clones were agile and fluid in motion, yes, but they had no straight-line speed at all.

 

  Within but a few seconds Jay was at his clone's heels, and was just about to grab its neck and put on the brakes when it darted to the side.  Jay managed to grab its shoulder, but he was still throw off balance and began to slide across the floor....

 

....towards the rapidly being-approached glass windows surrounding the Imagination Nexus.  The service robots that took care of Nexus Tower's appearance and well-being had just polished the tiled floor the night before, so it was especially slippery.

 

  A red light flashed in the edge of Jay's vision, warning him that he was exceeding the 'normal' max speed of, like, 60 miles per hour?  Instead of slowing down, Jay's speed was increasing.  This was going to be very pretty.

 

  Like bullets, Jay and Maelstromcopycatdroid smashed through the glass (pretty glass shards flew all over the place) and began the long straight-line tumble through 200 stories of gaseous.... Imagination.

 

  Its sole purpose being to destroy, damage, infect, or assimilate its surroundings to the service of Master Darkitect, purple lightning began charging around the Jay clone's fingertips.  Despite his rapidly increasing velocity towards the very dark basement of Nexus Tower, Jay had the sense to grab the clone's hands and absorb the incoming lightning burst, momentarily saving the Nexus from doom.  He then punched the clone in the face, ripping off artificial plastic skin and exposing the cheap Maelstrom-engineered circuitry that made up the clone's logic center.

 

  Then the Jay clone counterattacked.
