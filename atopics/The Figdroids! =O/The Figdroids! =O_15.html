~ Chapter 24 ~

 

In the end, Intrepid Fusion Eclipse took leave of his two trainees, telling them his mission was finished and that they were on there own.  Not very comforting, but enough for Jay and Katie to know the most important thing about their lives....

 

There would be more challenges to come....  Or would consequences fit the description better?

 

* * * * * * * * *

 

During the war of fourteen years ago, the Nimbus System was mostly under Martial Law, and with military control came military rules.  Including the rule to NOT contain in your house, your property, your Paradox Research Lab, or your backpack any essense of Maelstrom because it could lead to infection.

 

Of course, whether or not rules are meant to be broken, they usually end up getting broken anyway.  Dark Ronin ended up appearing all over Block Yards, Raven Bluffs, Chanty Shantys, and Avant Groves.  It took years to clean them all up following the war.  Maelstrom infected bricks found their way into innocent minifigures' inventories, numbering in the thousands.  Those also took a long time to be rid of.  And while an official exception was made for a Dark Spiderling to be housed in the Paradox Research Lab for studying, it would lead to a great mistake in itself as well....

 

* * * * * * * * *

 

With Intrepid gone, Jay and Katie and their dad had about two weeks left of having fun on vacation in Avant Gardens, and the first 12 days were spent looking at the monument, going into tourist traps at the Launch Area, exploring the caves, and doing nothing at all.

 

That left the 13th day to do something they hadn't officially done.

 

An official tour of the Paradox Museum.

 

Which was also the Paradox Research Lab once upon a time.

 

Whic had a Dark Spiderling in it.


What did I tell you about mistakes, broken rules, and consequences?

~ Chapter 25 ~

 

It was pretty uncomfortable for Jay to act like he had never seen the basement of the Paradox Museum as the group-of-about-twenty-tourist's tour guide, a red haired lady called Mara, lead them to that controversial location.

 

"This, fellow adventurers used to be the center of all experiments conducted in the Paradox Laboratory." Mara explained. "It was in here that the renowned researcher Wisp Lee explored the concept of the Maelstrom's infecting power, which he got to see first hand, no pun intended, when the Maelstrom escaped and infected all his colleagues, and a bit of him too."

 

She lead them towards a row of shiny metal doors, long cleaned since whatever Maelstrom infected the museum. "Each of these doors used to lead to a containment cell on the ground floor, which housed infected specimens, including the fabled Spider Queen; the cells are still there, but a wall was built in front of them to keep the museum looking cool."

 

"Can we go into them?" a guy in a baseball cap wearing mirrored sunglasses - even though he was in a dark basement - asked.

 

"I don't see why not." Mara answered, and pulled a ring of keys out of her pocket.  She jammed one of the keys into one of the doors, and it swung open, revealing its stained-in-purple other side, and a stairway that lead to the ground floor.

 

Mara walked casually up the stairs, ignoring any trace of Maelstrom that could have been there fourteen years ago. "Don't worry about any Maelstrom traces, they weaken over the years and shouldn't have any effect on anything."

 

The group followed her in, with Jay and Katie taking the rear.  Faded splotches of purple studded the wall, and Jay ran his fingers over them.  They tingled a bit, but nothing else.

 

Then his eyes fell on the Maelstrom Infected Bricks.
