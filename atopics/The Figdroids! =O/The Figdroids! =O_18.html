Chapter 26

 

It was a pile of purple and black bricks hidden mostly under the staircase that caught Jay's eye.  But not just any pile of purple and black bricks.  They were jumping.

 

Jumping bricks.

 

"Oh uncool, a quickbuild site." Katie grumbled. "Looks boring."

 

"Probably, but I wonder what it builds into," Jay said. "It shouldn't take long, since it's a quickbuild."

 

"Alrighty," Katie agreed. "But don't say I didn't warn you that it'd be boring."

 

Once Jay started quickbuilding the thingy, he found that stopping wasn't really possible.  And then he realized how big a mistake it was to try and build a quickbuild that involved not just any jumping purple and black bricks, but jumping purple and black MAELSTROM INFECTED BRICKS which, unlike Maelstrom stains, stayed fresh a loooong time because of the larger volume and depth in an infected brick.

 

Within seconds, the bricks had transformed into a large, oversized, ugly, weapon-wielding SPIDER.

 

Jay and Katie screamed, not because of the disgusting arachnid that stood hissing at them offering no thanks or gratefulness for being revived, but rather because a hand clamped down on each of their shoulders from behind.

 

"Now look what you've done!" a disappointed voice groaned. "I thought you'd be smarter than that.  Now I have to fight Maelstrom again."

 

Jay and Katie whirled around to face the guy with the reflective sunglasses and the baseball cap, except now he wasn't wearing his reflective sunglasses and baseball cap, rather he was instantly wearing a leather jacket, some funny green aviator pants, a brown cape, and a silly looking color changing jester cap.  But in his hands were a serious looking cleaver like Intrepid's and a dragon shield.

 

"Be gone with you, ugly Dark Spiderling thingy!" the guy screamed, and proceeded to trip over his own feet when he made a failed attempt at a battle charge.

 

Despite the situation, Katie facepalmed.
